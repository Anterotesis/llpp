Middlesex To the Worshipfull Sr. John Hawkins Knights and other the Justices at their General Quarter Session of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled

The Humble Petition of Henry Jordan of the Parish of Saint George the Martyr in the said County Taylor

Sheweth That by Indenture of Apprenticeship bearing Date the 23d Day of March [---] 1773 Robert Thomas Bradley Son of Jane Chadley Widow of the Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster became Bound to your Petitioner for the Term of seven years in Consideration of ten Pounds and ten Shillings to Learn the Act of a Taylor

That the said Apprentice refuses to [...] Obey your Petrs Lawfull Command & doth frequently Absent himself from your Petrs Service with his Licence or Consent for many Days and nights together and refuses to be any ways under your Petitioners Contronl

Your Petitioner therefore most Humbly Beseedes your Worsps to Discharge the said Robert Thomas Bradley from your Petitioner And grant your Petr such further and other Relief as to your Worships may seem meet And the Nature of the Case may Require

And your Petitioner will never Pray Etc Henry Jordon
