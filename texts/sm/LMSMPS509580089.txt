To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate in the City of London

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order of Removal or Pass Warrant the Hands and Seals of John Devaynes and John Collick Esquire two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the third day of December one thousand seven hundred and ninety eight Elizabeth Saunders wife of John Saunders (alledged by the said Order to be gone from her) was removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex and delivered to Your Petitioner being the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate in the said City of London (the said Justices adjudging that the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said John Saunders and Elizabeth his wife were in the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate in the City of London by

which said Order of Removal or Pass warrant Your Petitioners Apprehend themselves to be aggrieved.

Wm. Chetham Solr. For the Appellts. Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Appeal against the said Order of Removal or Pass Warrant and pray relief in the Premises. And that the benefit of his their Appeal may be saved unto them and that the Hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County.
