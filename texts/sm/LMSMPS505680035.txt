To the Worshipful John Hawkins Esquire and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their general [...] Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Harding in the County of Oxford.

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Thomas Bulack and Jaspir Arris Borredule Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex bearing Date the Day of January 1767 Mary Smith Widow of Robert Smith lately deceased and her Son Samuel aged about Eighteen Months were removed from and out of the Parish of Kensington in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Harding as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby Yours Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and humbly appeal therefrom.

Your Petitioners therefore humble pray your Worships That a Day may be appointed for the hearing and determining of the said Appeal and that your Worships would be Pleased to make such Order in the Premisses as to your Worships shall seem meet And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Hen: Whitfield Agent for the Appellants
