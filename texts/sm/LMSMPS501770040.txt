To the Rt. Worshipfull his Majestys' Justices of the Peace for the County of Midlx Assembled in their Generall Quarter Sessions

The humble Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poore of the Libertys' of Saffron hill Hatton garden and Ely Rents within the parish of St. Andrew Holborne in the Said County of Midlx

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant bearing date the Eighteenth day of March last Under the hands and Seales' of Thomas Rand and Richard Browne Esqrs. two of his Majestys' Justices of the Peace for the County aforesaid (Quorum Unus) Ann Woodhouse Widdow and her four Children (Vizt) William Aged about thirteen Years' Ralph Aged about Eleven Years Margarett aged about Eighteen Years and Mary aged about Seven Years were removed and brough from the parish of St. Giles without Criplegate in the Said County to the Said Libertys' of Saffron hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents as the place of their last legall Settlement

Your Peticoners think themselves Agrieved by the Judgment of the Said two Justices humbly appeale to this Court and pray that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Said parish of St. Gyles without Criplegate and all other parties concerned may be ordered to Attend this Court Someday [...] Sessions to hear and abide the Judgments and determinacon [...] Your Worships touching this Appeale

And Your Peticoners as in duty bound Shall pray Etc
