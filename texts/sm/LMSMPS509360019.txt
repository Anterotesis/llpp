To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Sessions of the Peace at Hicks's Hall in the said County Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Hampstead Norris in the County of Barks

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of John Spiller and George Reid Esqrs. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one where of being of the Quorum) bearing Date the twentieth day of December 1796 (but not served full the latter End of the February last) William Brown with his Wife Elizabeth and three Infant Children to wit William Martha and Mary are adjudged to be last legally settled in the Parish of Hampstead Norwich in the County of Berks and are ordered to be removed and conveyed from the Parish of Isleworth in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Hampstead Norwich Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order. And pray that your Worships will please to appoint some day in this present Sessions for the hearing and determining this Appeal, And to order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Isleworth and all Persons concerned do then attend this Court And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Fras. Lockey. Solicitor for the Appellants.
