To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex[---] in their General Quarer Sessions Assembled

The Petition and appeal of Albion Cooper of Richmond in the County of Surry Tallow Chandler against the Conviction if Nathaniel Bland Esquir one of his Majesties Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order Judgment or Convict in under the hand and Seal of Nathaniel Bland Esquire one of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the said County bearing date the 13th. day of January 1794 the said Justices Convicted your Petitioner in the penal Sum of five pounds and Costs for that your Petitioner did on the tenth day of said month of January 1794 use or cause to be used a Cart in or upon a certain Highway or Public Turnpike Road in Old Brentford in the said County of Middlesex without having his Surname and place of About painted thereon in large legible letters contrary to the Statue Etc

That your Petitioner did on the Eighteenth day of January last cause the said Nathaniel Bland Esquire to be served with a Notice if Appeal to the said Conviction

That your Petitioner has entered into the usual Recognizance with proper

Security for Abiding the order of this Court to be made in that behalf And that your Petitioner humbly Conceives himself aggrieved in the premises and that he has good cause for the said Appeal

Your Petitioner therefore appeals to your worships against the said Judgement Order accd Conviction did humbly prays relief in the premises and that your worships will appoint some day in the present Sessions for the hearing and determining the said appeal and that all parties concerned may then attend

And your Petitioner shall ever pray
