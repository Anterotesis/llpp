To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of Alexander Layel

Sheweth That by Indentures of Apprenticeship bearing date the twenty ninth day of September One Thousand Seven hundred & fifty three your Petitioner was bound Apprentice to Robert Noyes of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the said County Carver for the space of Seven Years

That Your Petitioners Brother John Layel paid as a Consideration to the said Robert Noyes at the time of the executing of the said Indentures the Sum of Ten Pounds and gave his Bond for payment of the further Sum of Ten Pounds when Your Petitioner should have Served half of the sd. Term

That Your Petitioners said Master Robert Noyes hath very frequently taken him and his Fellow Apprentice into Gardens belonging to other Persons to Pilfer trees that were growing Flower Roots and box for borders and divers other things till at length Your Petitioner began to apprehend the fatal Consequences that might Result from such against proceedings and therefore Your Petitioner applied to his Friends to interpose in his favour to prevent his destruction

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays Your Worships to discharge him from his said Indentures of Apprenticeship and to Order that his said Master may Return part of the said Ten pounds Consideration money and deliver up the said Bond executed by your Petr's. said Bror. as aforesaid in Order to be Cancelled

And Your Petr (as in duty bound) will ever Pray Etc.

Alexr. Layel
