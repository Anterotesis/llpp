To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of John Cox and Stephen Clarke Esquires Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one where of being of the Quorum) bearing date the 30th day of August 1770. Margaret Rawlins Widow of Isaac Rawlins Chocolate maker deceased and their 3 Children namely Sarah aged 8 years and upwards Isaac aged 5 years and upwards and Jane aged 2 years and upwards were Removed from the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above Barrs to the said Parish of Saint Luke as the place of their last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved by the said Order of the said Two Justices of the Peace Appeal to this Court against the same and humbly pray your Worships will be pleased to appoint some day in this sessions for the Hearing and Determining the said Appeal.

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc.

J: Jenings Atty: for the Appellants.
