Middelsex To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Anne Middlesex in the County aforesaid

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of John Staples and George Story Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty third day of January in the year of our Lord and thousand seven hundred and ninety eight John Bird was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint George Middlesex in the said County to the said parish of Saint Anne Middlesex by the description of Sarah Anne Limehouse, as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said John Bird whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly beg Leave to Appeal from the said Order and pray that the said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the Hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter, Sessions of the Peace to be held for the said County and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint George Middlesex and all others concerned may be then ordered to attend and that your Petitioners may have such Relief herein as to the Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc

Smith & Rivers (for Salter) Solicitor for Appellants No 66 Great Prescott Street
