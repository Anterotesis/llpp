The Worshipful

To John Haskins Esqr. Chairman And the nest of the worshipful his Majesty's Justices in their General Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall in St. John Street Assembled

The Humble Petition of Martin Smith of Great Chelsea in the County of Middlesex Baker

Humbly Sheweth That on Saturday the 31st. day of August last your Petitioner was Convicted before Thos. Miller Esqr. one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in the Penalty of Five Pounds for having in his Custody Offerd and Exposed for Sale four half Peck Leaves of Houshold Bread at the Price of Elven Pence half penny P. Loaf and [...] [---] Six Quarter of a Peck [---] Loaves of Henshold Bread at the Price of Six Pence P Loaf And one Quarter of a Peck Loaf of Weather Bread at the Price of [...] Seven Pence farthing Pr. Loaf [---] as not proportioned in respect to the Price each Sort of Bread ought to be sold at according to the direction of the Statute in that Case was and provided whereby your Petr. concerning himself aggrieved And humbly [...] Appeals the same Therefore your Petitioner humbly pray worships to Appoint a Day for the hearing and Determining this his said appeal and your Petitioner as in Duty bound will ever pray Etc

Robert Mason Solicit [...]
