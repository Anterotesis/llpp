To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in the General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of R. Clark and W. Bleamire Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty fifth day of September [...] last Joseph Sessions was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West in the City of London as the Place of the last legal settlement of the said Joseph Session

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order Do humbly Appeal against the same and pray this Court that the benefit of the said Appeal be saved unto them until the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex and that they and all persons concerned may attend to hear and abide the judgment and determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc
