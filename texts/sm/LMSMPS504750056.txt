To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assambled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Kensington in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of George Wrighte and Richard Manley Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westmr bearing date the fifth day of August last Elizabeth Wild Widow is Removed from the said parish of St Margaret Westminster to the said Parish of Kensington as the place of her Legal Settlement

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Concerning them Selves Aggreived by the Judgment of the said Justices Do hereby humbly Appeal to this Court for such Relief in the Premises as to your Worship shall seem Meet

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

At the General Session of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King in and for the County of Middlesex holden at Hicks hall in St John street in the County aforesaid on Monday the Eleventh day of September in the fifty Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord [---] George the Second by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and in the Year of our Lord 1758
