To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the County of Middlesex at their general Quarter Session of the Peace held in & for the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition of John Mason of the Parish of Saint John Wapping in the County aforesaid.

Sheweth That on or about the 12th. day of Decembr. 1759 the said John Mason was by Joseph Hodgskins of the Parish & County aforesaid Sailmaker his present Master persuaded to bind himself to the said Joseph Hodgskins And at that time being very Young and not knowing or being Acquainted with the Custom of the Trade he entred into an Agreement with the said Joseph Hodgskins for the Term of Seven Years. But your Petitioner is now Informed that the said Joseph Hodgskins never Served a regular Apprenticeship to the said Trade & therefore he the said John Mason will not be able to get a livelihood in the said Trade at the End of the Term in the said Agreement made, for it is a Custom in the said Trade for the Masters who have served regular Apprenticeships never to Employ a Person who hath not done the same. And the said Joseph Hodgskins has lately used your Petitioner very ill & of ten threatned to Send your Petitioner on Board one of his Majesty's Ships of War and as your Petitioner is informed & verily believes to be true he the said Joseph Hodgskins hath Spoke to an Officer to Impress your Petitioner as aforesaid.

Therefore Your Petitioner prays that this Court will Appoint a day this present Session for the said Joseph Hodgskins to Attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of this Court touching the same And that your Petitioner may be relieved in the premisses as this Court shall see meet

And Yor Petitioner will ever Pray Etc. John Mason
