To Her Majties: Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in Sessions assembled

The Peticon and Appeal of John Burkell and Thomas Hunt Church wardens and other the Overseers of the poor of the parish of Hackney in the said County

Humbly Sheweth That by an under the hands and Seals of Alexander Pitfeild and Joseph Jory Esqrs two of her Majties Justices of the Peace for the said County (Quor unus) Dated the 21th of May 1707 One Ralph Romball wth. his wife and Children upon Complaint made by the Churchwns. and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Gyles without Cripplegate were passed from the said Parish of St Gyles to the abovenamed parish of Hackney to be exeded for therein The said Justices having adjudged the said Pish of Hackney to be the place of the last legall Settlement of the said Ralph Romball By which Judgemt of the said two Justices the abovesd Churchwdn. and Overseers of Hackney are aggrieved they being ready wth. the leave of this Court to disprove all the matters when induced said Judgmt And further they humbly Informe the Court that the saw Ralph Romball at the time of the said order made rented an house of twelve Pounds p Annum in Hacks house Court in Golden Lane within the said pish of St Gyles without Cripplegate of the demise of cope Jeweller for four Yeares remaining at Lady day 1707 Upon all which makers the Churchwns and Overseers of Hackney aforesaid humbly appeal to this Honble Court for Releif praying that the said Order of the said two Justices may be sett aside and that the said Ralph Romball his wife and Children on any of them may not be the occaon of any Charge to the said parish of Hackney prsent or future, on ptence of Leutent And they pray costs may be awarded them.

Jno Bushell Thomas Hunt

A: Yardley
