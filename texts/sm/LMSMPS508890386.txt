To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Andrew Enfield in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by an Order made under the hands and Seals of John Floud and Daniel Williams Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex bearing date the thirteth day of September 1793 Mary Whiting Henry Whiting and Mary Whiting were removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Andrew Enfield as the place of their last legal Settlement Where by Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore Appeal against the said order and humbly pray relief in the Premises and that Your Worships will be Pleased to appoint same day in this present Session for the hearing and Determining this their said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray [...] .
