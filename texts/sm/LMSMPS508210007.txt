The Worshipful William Mainwaring Esqr. Chairman and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at the General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of Samuel Allen.

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on Tuesday last Tried in this Court for an Assault upon one Thomas Maughfling found Guilty and the Court sentenced him to be Imprisoned in the New Prison at Clerkenwell for six Weeks.

Your Petitioner has hitherto supported himself and Family by his own hard Labor and Industry at Coal heaving with Reputation and was never before in Trouble.

That having Satisfied his Prosecutor and paid him the Expences he has been put to on this Occasion and he Consenting to your Petitioners being discharged.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays the Court will be pleased to reverse his Sentence and restore him to his Liberty

And Your Petitioner as in Duty bound wills our pray Etc.

Samuel Allen

April 21st. 1787
