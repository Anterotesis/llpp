To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Sessions assembled.

The Humble Petition of the ChurchWardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Wandsworth in the County of Surrey.

Sheweth

That by an Order bearing date the 26th. day of August 1795 under the Hands and Seals of Mich. Downs and R. Johnson Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace within the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex grounded on the Complaint of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the said City and County, for that Dessemay Peckford widow of John Peckford had lately intruded and come into the said Parish of Saint James and become chargeable.

That the said Mich. Downs and R. Johnson upon examination of the Premises upon cath and other Circumstances did adjudge the same to be true and did also adjudge the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Dessmay Peckford to be in your Petitioners said Parish of Wandsworth By which said order your Petitioners think themselves Aggrieved

Your Petitioners humbly pray, That your Worships will take th same into consideration, and grant them such relief as the nature of their case May require.

And

Your Petitioners Shall ever pray.
