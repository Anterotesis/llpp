To the Right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Christ Church in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (whereof One is of the Quorum therein mentioned) bearing date the fifteenth day of November One Thousand Seven hundred and Seventy four Mary Stevens Widow and William her Son were removed from the Parish of Saint Pancras in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Christ Church London as the last legal Place of their Settlement By which your Petitioners humbly. conceive themselves Aggrieved and appeal to this Court against the said pass Order

Your Petitioners therefore pray your Worships will be pleased to hear and Determine the Matters touching this Appeal And give them such Relief in the Premises As to your Worships shall seem Just and meet

Rd. Welch Agent for the Appellants And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Middlesex At the General [---] Session of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the County aforesaid on Monday the fifth day of December in fifteenth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith Etc.

It is Ordered by this Court that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Pancras in the County of Middlesex have Notice of this Appeal And that they and all Parties concerned do Attend this Court on Thursday next at Eight of the Clock in the forenoon to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

By the Court Butler
