To the worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in General Session assembled.

The humble Petition of Thomas Chambers.

Sheweth, That on the 19th. day of May instant your Petitioner was taken up a Warrant granted against him by David Wilmot and Charles Friquet Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex upon an Information laid before them for unlawfully entring into an Agreement and Combination with Edward Lane, Benjamin Budd & others Journeymen Workmen in the Flax Trade for the advancing the [...] of them and all other persons employed in the said Trade and your petitioner was Convicted by them thereon

That your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by the Order of Conviction of the said two Justices and therefore appeal to this honorable Court against the same

And prays that this honorable Court [...] please to appoint a Day for hearing and determining the said Appeal and make such Order therein as may be just.

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc.

W: Bennet Solr. for the Appellants 30th. May 1786.
