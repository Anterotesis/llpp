To the Worshipful Willaim Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of the Justices for the County of Middlesex in Session assembled

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamblet of Hammersmith in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of William Bleamire and William Kinnard Esquires two of his Majestys Justice of the Peace acting [...] for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quroum) bearing date the second day of July 1794. Elizabeth Field was removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of St. James at Clerkenwell within the said County to Hammersmith as the place of her last legal Settlement whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved and beg leave to Appeal therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore pray that they may be at liberty to enter their Appeal And that the same may be adjourned to the next Sessions for the hearing thereof.
