To William Mainwaring Esquire Chairman and other his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the Session House on Clerkenwell Green to hold the General Session of the Peace for the said County beginning this Eight day of December 1783

The Petition of Thomas Norton of Artillery Court in the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex Insurance Braker

Sheweth

That the Goods of your Petitioner were distrained upon the 18th. day of October last for the Sum of One Pound Four Shillings for one Years pavement Rate said to be due to the said Parish of Saint Luke Middlesex at Christmas 1782

That your Petitioner being advised of Councel to appeal thereto in as much as Artillery Court wherein your Petitioners House is Situate (for the Assessment of which the said distress has been made) is not specified in the Act of Parliament made in the 11st. Year of the Reign of his present Majesty intilted an Act for paving Etc of the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex (or as he apprehends within the meaning thereof ) gave due Notice to the Clock to the Commissioners appointed under the said Act of Such his Intention to appeal at the present December General Session and entered into a Recognizance as required by the said Act

That your Petitioner therefore deeming himself aggrieved doth appeal unto you his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex and hereby requests that you will appoint a day in this Present General Session wherein you will be pleased to hear and determine the matter of such Appeal and make such Order in the premises as shall seem meet to you therein

Thos. Norton
