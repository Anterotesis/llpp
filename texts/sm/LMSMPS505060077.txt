To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Church Warden and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the Hands & Seals of two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (One being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 30th. Day of October last Hannah Grant therein described to be the Wife of Daniel Grant a Soldier aborad in his Majestys Service with her three Children Vizt. James aged 7 Years last June John Aged 5 Years last Month and Duncan Aged three Years last January were removed from the Parish of St: Mary White Chapel to the Hamlet (in the said Order called the Parish) of Hammersmith the said Justices thereby Adjudging that to be the place of Settlement of the sd. Daniel Grant and Hannah his Wife and their said Children

That Your Petitioners Conceive themselves Aggrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said Justices and do humbly Appeal therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that your Worships would be pleased to Appoint some Day in this present Session to hear this their Appeal and to make an Order that the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish [...] of St. Mary White Chappel and all Partys Concerned in and about the Premisses may then and there attend hear & abide the Determination of your Worships therein

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Robt: Kent Churchwarden

Jos: Newman George Walker} Overseers of the Poor
