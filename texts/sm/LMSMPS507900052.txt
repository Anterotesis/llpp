Middlesex To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said County in their General Session of the peace assembled

The humble Petition and appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Simbury in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth that by Virtue of an Order pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Robert Taylor and John Barnfather Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the peace of the said County of Middlesex bearing date the Twenty Seventh day of October new last past Ann Wilkinson Widow of George Wilkinson a Coachman deceased and her two Children namely Thomas aged near three years and George aged five Weeks ware removed and Convey'd from the parish ye St: Pancras in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Sunbury as the place of Him last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Order or Warrant of the said two Justices Do therefore appeal to yourWorships therefrom & humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the premisses & Owen that the said Churchwardens & Overseers of the born of the said Parish of St: Pancras may attend your at a certain day to be appointed day your Worships to heard and abide the Judgement & determination of your Worships touching the said Appeal

Decr: 6 1784

And your Petitioner shall pray Etc

Rd: Tallemack tollr: for the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Humbly
