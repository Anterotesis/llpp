To His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace assembled.

The Humble Petition of Joseph Walker of Long Acre Baker.

Sheweth That on the 20th. day of May [---] Instant your Petitioner was Convicted before David Walker Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex for unlawfully selling on the 19th. day of the said Month of May at the Parish of St Martin in the Fields[---] in the said County to One Joseph Nicholson One Loaf of Wheaten Bread purporting to be a Quarter Loaf deficient in Weight One Ounce and a half One Loaf of Wheaten Bread purporting to be a Quartern Loaf deficient in Weight Three Ounces One Loaf of Wheaten Bread purporting to be a Quartern Loaf deficient in Weight four Ounces One Loaf of Wheaten bread purporting to be a Quartern Loaf deficient in Weight Two Ounces, One Loaf of Wheaten bread purporting to be a Quartern a Loaf Deficient in Weight One Ounce and an half One Loaf of Wheaten Bread purporting to be a Quartern Loaf deficient in Weight Two Ounces and one other Loaf of Wheaten Bread purporting also to be a Quartern Loaf deficient in Weight Three Ounces according to the Assize then and there set for the said Several Loaves of Wheaten bread against the form of the Statute in such Case made and provided and was then and there adjudged to Pay and forfeit for the said Offence the Sum of Four Pounds five Shillings

That Your Petitioner Conceives himself aggrieved by the said Conviction

Your Petitioner therefore doth appeal against the same and humbly Prays that the said Conviction may be quashed and that Your Petitioner may have such other Relief in the Premises as to Your Worships shall seem meet and that the hearing and determining of this his Appeal may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County

And Your Petitioner Will Ever Pray Etc.
