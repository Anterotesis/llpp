To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the County of Middlesex assembled al the general Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green on the 30th Day of November 1795.

The Humble petition and Appeal of Henry page of this well Street in the said County Pawn broker.

Sheweth that your Petitioners was on or about the 26th of Novr. instant convicted in the Penalty of five Pounds by John Floud Esquire one of the Justice of the Peace for the said County apon an Information exhibited and prosecuted by Patrick Russel charging that your Petitioners did in the Parish of Saint Luke in the said Count in the 29th day of October last demand receive and Late of and from one John Clay on Redcemine the Pawn broker therein [---] in mentioned the sum of four pence by way of profit upon four shilling on the 22th day of July last last upon a Pledge of & manis Coat to the said John Clay contrary to the Statute or

That your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by such correction and to advised to appeal against the same

Your Petitioner Herefore Humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his appeal so that the same may be heard at this present general Session of the Peace in and for the said County and that your Worships will appoint a day for Hearing and determining she same

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
