To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the peace at Guild hall in King Street Westminster in the said County assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the City and Liberty of westminster and County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or order under the hands and Seals of A: Aldrige and Thomas Bishop Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace Acting in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (one whereof being the Quorum) bearing date the 11th. day of February 1779 William Fletcher about the Age of Twelve Years and Apprentice to Thomas Sherrif of the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden aforesaid Trunk Maker was removed from the Parish of Hayes in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint paul covent garden as the Place of his last legal Settlement by which order your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved and do Appeal to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioners therefoe Pray that this Court will be pleased to appoint a Day in this present Session to hear and Determine the said appeal and to order that all persons concerned may be directed to Attend

And your Petitioners shall Pray etc.

John Lane Sollicitor for the said parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden
