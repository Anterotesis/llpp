To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their general Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel in the County aforesaid

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of George Storey and John Staples Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid one whereof being of the Quorum, bearing date the twenty third day of May one thousand seven hundred and ninety six Catherine Carter the widow of Samuel Carter deceased was removed and conveyed from and out of the Hamlet of Ratcliffe in the Parish of Saint Dunstan Stepney in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Catherine Carter Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly beg leave to appeal from the said Order and pray that their said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of the Peace to be held for the said County and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Hamlet of Ratcliffe and all others concerned may be then and there ordered to attend And that your Petitioners may have such Relief therein as to the Court shall seem meet.

And your Petitioners will pray Etc

J: Smith Solicitor for Appellants Great Prescott Street
