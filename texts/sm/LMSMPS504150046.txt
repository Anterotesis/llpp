To the Worshipfull his Maties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in their General Session Assembled

The humble Petition of John Birch of the Parish of St. Mary White Chapel in the said County Carpenter

Sheweth That by Indenture bearing Date the 15th. Day of October 1748 William Jump (then Aged about Fourteen Years) a Poor Child of the said Parish of White Chapel was Put & Bound an Apprentice to your Petr. until the said Apprentice should accomplish his full age of Twenty Four Years

That your Petrs. said Apprentice is now in Custody in the House of Correctiion at Clerkenwell upon Your Petrs. Complaint for being a very idle disorderly Apprentice and frequently absenting himself & lying out of his said Masters Service, doth not Orderly and Obediently demean & behave himself and when your Petr. gives him Work he absolutely refuses to Perform it and with very impudent Provoking Language & menacing Threats tells your Petr. he will not work but do just as In pleases in Spite of whatever yr. Petr. can do to him

That your Petr. said Apprentice is a very Surly Sulky Boy & growing Ripe for all manner of Vice and Wickedness and being conversant with very Bad & Wicked Acquaintance Your Petr. humbly conceives himself & Family to be in very great and Manifest Danger from his sd. Apprentice being justly fearfull that he or some of his Wicked Accomplices will either Robb or do him & his Family some great Mischief

Your Petr. therefore most humbly Prays Your Worps would be pleased to Order that the said Wm. Jump (his said Apprentice) may attend this Honble. Court some day this Present Session there to Answer your Petrs.Complaint, and to Shew Cause why he should not be discharged from Your Petrs. said Service

And your Petr. shall ever Pray Etc

Jno. Birch
