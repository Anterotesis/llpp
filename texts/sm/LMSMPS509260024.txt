To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Enfield in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order made under the hands and Seals of H Jackson and Jno. Robinson Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the Twenty fifth day of May 1796 Richard Goddard and Charlotte his Wife and two of their Children namely Richard Aged Six years and upwards and Sarah aged near two years were removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Tottenham in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Enfield as the place of their last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore Appeal against the said Order and humbly pray Relief in the Premises and that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a day for that purpose

Middlesex to wit At the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex on Monday the Twentieth day of June in the 36 year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith Etc

It is Ordered that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Tottenham in the said County of Middlesex have notice of the above Appeal and that they and all persons Concerned do attend on the day of [---] next at nine of the Clock in the forenoon of the same day at the Sessions House Clerkenwell green to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal
