To the worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in A General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of South Mims in the County of Middlesex Aforesaid

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of William Underwood and [---] Pierce Galliard-Esqrs- two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (Whereof one is of the Quorum) Bearing date the fourth day of August 1748 Whereby William Haydon and Mary his Wife John their Son Aged Seven Years Hannah their Daughter Aged five Years Joseph their Son Aged four Years Were Removed from the Parish of Enfield in the County of Middlesex Aforesaid to the Said Parish of South Mims as their Last Legall Place of Settlement

And Your Petitioners do think themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Warrt of Removal and do Humbly Appeal to this Court Against the Same

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Pray that this Court will be Pleas'd to Appoint A day for the hearing and Determining of the said Appeal

And Your. Petrs shall pray Etc Luke Rudworth John Scott William Wright William Georry} Churchwardens Overseers of the Poor
