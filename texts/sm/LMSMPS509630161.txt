Middlesex To the Worhipful his Majesty's justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church-warders and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of West Ham in the County of Essex

Sheweth That by virture of an Order under the hands and Seals of John Staples and George Story Esquires two f his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Eighteenth day of June one thousand seven hundred and Ninety nine Ann Peacock Window was on the Nineteenth Day of the said Month of June removed from the Parish of Saint John Wapping in the Country of Middlesex unto the said Parish of West Ham in the Country of Essex as the place of her last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order Appeal to this Honorable Court against the same and humbly Pray Your Worships to Appoint a Day hear and Determine the same and your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

Church-Wardens{ Robt. Maggs John Talloh

Overseers{ Edwd Barkers Saml. Pace

At the General Session of the peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex at the Session house for the said Country on Monday the Ninth Day of September one thousand and seven hundred and ninty nine.

It is Ordered that the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint John Wrapping have Notice of this Appeal and that they and all parties Concerned as Attend this Court on Thursday next at Nine of the Clock in the forenoon on the matters of this Petition and Appeal to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal

By the Court
