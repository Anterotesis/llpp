To the Right Worshipfull his Maties Justices of the peace for the County of Middx in General Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition of Thomas Oliver Apprentice to Robert Musset County of Middx aforesaid

Sheweth That Yor Peticon was bound Apprentice by Indenture of Apprenticeshipp bearing date the 2d Day of July 1731 to the said Robert Masset for the term of five years to warn his art Etc That your said Petitioners said Master Did not instruct or Cause him to be instructed in the Art of a Bricklayer according to the true intent and meaning of the Said Indenture whereby Your Peticoner hath been Obliged and is shill Obliged to put himself into other Hands to lea [...] the said Art And hath not found and provided for your said Petition of Meat Drink Apprarell and other necessaries according to the true intent and meaning of the said Indenture

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays His horroble Court (that as he was not bound according to Law the said Indenture being made for the Term of five years only and for the reasons aforesaid) To Discharge him from the said Indenture of Apprenticeshipp

July your Petitioners shall Pray Etc
