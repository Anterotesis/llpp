To the Right Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled in Generall Quarter Sessions

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Thomas Neal of the parish of Aldenham in the Liberty of Saint Albons in the County of Hertford.

Sheweth That by an Order made by William Dobyns and Thomas Inwood Esquires Two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex (Quorum Unus) being therein mentioned Dated the seventeenth day of March 1732/3 Your said Petitioner is thereby adjudged to be the Putative Father of a Female Bastard Child then lately born in the parish of Stanmore magne in the said County of Middlesex on the body of Anne Fern and in the said Order mentioned likely to be chargeable to the said parish of Stanmore magna And it is also by the said Order Ordered that as well for the Relief of the said Parish of Stanmore magna as also for provision and Maintenance of the said Child that Your said Petitioner Thomas Neal shall weekly and every Week from the twentieth day of March and so long as the said Child shall be chargeable to the said [---] parish of Stanmore magna for the time being the Sum of two shillings Per Week for and towards the Maintenance of the said Child And itt is by the said Order further ordered that your said Petitioner Thomas Neal shall immediately pay to the Churchwardens or Overseers of the Poor of the said parish of Stanmore magna the Sum of one pound seven shillings and two pence for and towards the Extraordinary Charges they have been at in maintaining the said Anne Fern during the time of her lying in And Your Petitioner is also by the said Order Ordered to give sufficient Security to the Church- Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Stanmore magna aforesaid for the due performance of the aforesaid Order

Brightwell Smith Agent for the Appellant Your Petitioner thinking himself aggrieved by the said Order and Adjudgement of the said two Justices of the Peace humbly appeals to this Court against the same And Prayes that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Stanmore magna and all other parties concerned may be ordered to attend this Court some day this present Sessions to hear and abide the Determinations of your Worships touching this Appeal

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray
