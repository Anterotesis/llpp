To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex at them General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Kensington in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of John Hatchett and W. Carr Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County Dated the seventeenth day of October Instant Elizh. Dimmock the Wife of Henry Dimmock of the Hamlet of Hammersmith Labourer John the Lawful Son of the said Henry Dimmock aged about seven Years and Mary the Lawful Daughter of the said Henry Dimmock aged about three Years were removed from the said Hamlet of Hammersmith in the said County of, Middlesex to the said Parish of Kensington as the place of the last legal Settlement of them the said Elizabeth Dimmock her Son John and her Daughter Mary whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and do appeal to your Worships against the said Order

Wherefore your Petitioners humbly pray your Worships will be pleased to appoint a day on this Present Session for the hear in and determine the said Appeal and that all parties

concerned may be Ordered to attend and to abide and perform such Order as your Worships shall please to make and that your Worships will please to give your Petitioners such Relief in the Premises as shall be just and your Petitioners shall pray Etc:

William Smith Jon. Hamston} Church Wardens

John Lomas Geo. Tunks} Overseers
