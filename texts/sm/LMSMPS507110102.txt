To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex At their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the City of London

Sheweth Thay by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of David Walkers Esquire and Philip Dyot Esquire two of His Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex(One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the tenth Day of March One Thousand Seven Hundred and Seventy Nine Sarah Lindon Widow of Thomas Linkon (in the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal called Sarah Lincoln Widow of Thomas Lincoln Cooper deceased with her four Children namely Mary Aged Ten Years and upwards Sarah Aged Eight Years Jane aged five Years and Charlotte Aged three Months were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Pancrass in the said County of Middlesex to the said County of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the City of London (called in the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal Saint Botolph by Aldgate London) As the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Sarah Linkcoln in the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal called Sarah Lincoln) and her said Four Children

Your Petitioners conceiveing themselves Aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal of the said Two Justices Do therefore humbly appeal to this Court Against the same Praying such Relief in the Premises as to this Cowet shall seem meet

And Your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc

Jms. Fisherston Sollicitors for the Appellants
