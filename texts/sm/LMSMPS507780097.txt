To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire Chairman and the rest of His Majestiys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex

The Petition of Joseph Wood of Cow Cross Street in the Parish of Saint Sepulchre in the County of Middlesex Carpenter

Humbly Sheweth That Your Petitioner was the Contractor for the performance of the Carpenters and Joiners Smiths and Plumbers Works in the Erection of the New Session house for the County of Middlesex

That the Agreement with the Commissioners for building the said Session house was for the Surveyor to give Your Petitioner a Certificate as soon as he had done Work to the amount of £400 and the Commissioners were immediately to pay Your Petitioner the Sum of £300 or ¾ ths. of the Money do Certified to be due to him and that at the expiration of Six Months after the Surveyor should Certify that Your Petitioner had fully compleated his said Contracts he was to be paid the whole of the Money due to him under the same

That Your Petitioner complied with the Conditions of the said Contracts on his part in every particular and fully expected that the Commissioners would have done the same by making the several Payments as soon as they became due

That Your Petitioner has subjoined a Statement of the time when he received the several Certificates from the Surveyor the time when he received the Money under the said Certificates and the Interest that would have accrued to Your Petitioner had the Commissioners been punctual in their Payments

Your Petitioner therefore Humbley Prays Your Worships to allow him the Sum of £71..9..8½ the amount of the Interest that would have accrued to him upon the several Sums that he received had he received the same in due time a give him such other allowance or relief in the Premises as to Your Worships shall seem meet

And Your Petitioner as in Duty bound will every pray Etc Etc Etc.
