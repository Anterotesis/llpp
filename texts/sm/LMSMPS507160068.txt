To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace Assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace holden by Adjournment at Hicks's Hall in and for the County of Middlesex on Monday the Eighteenth day of October 1779

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Petworth in the County of Sussex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of G. Allcock and Jos: Wilton Esquires two of His Majsety's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty Ninth day of September [...] 1779 Mary Wells Wife of Thomas Wells and their three Children (that is to say) Ann aged Eight Years Richard aged Seven Years and George aged One Year and Six Months were removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Mary-le-Bone in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Petworth in the said County of Suss of as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby the petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray that your Worships will be pleased to grant them time to Lodge an Appeal to the said Order of removal untill the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be held in and for the County of Midd

And your Petitioners will ever pray
