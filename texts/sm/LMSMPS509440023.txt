To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in general quarter Session of the peace for the said County assembled

The Humble petition and Appeal of the churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex-

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of John Scott and Nathaniel- Conant Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof eing of the Quorum) bearing date- the seventh day of October 1797 Maria Glliford (Wife of Joseph Gulliford) and her two Children (Namely) Sarah aged inwards of three Years, and James aged about Cow Months, were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above Bars- in the said County [...] to the said Parish of Saint Anne as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order And Pray that the Benefit of this Appeal may be saved to the Petitioners and that the hearing and determining thereof be adjourned to the next General Session of the Peace to be holden in and for this County to hear and determine

determine the said Appeal according to law And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

Wm Yarnold

William Red Alexr Gordon Osmond Gilles
