To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in the General Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled.

The Humble petition and appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint Michael Crooked lane in the City of London on behalf of the said parish

Sheweth

That by an Order under the Hands and seals of W. Addington and cha Churchchill two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex Made the Wth: Day of January last for and Concenring the settlement of Phillis Edgerley Signlewoman upon the complaint of the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Gardens within the Liberty of Westminster in the county of Middlesex It was adjourned That the Parish of Saint Michael Crooked Lane London was the place where the said Phillis Eagerly was last settled And it was therefore ordered that she should be removed from the said parish of saint Paul Covent Garden to the said parish of saint Michael Crooked Lane and delivered to the Church wardens and Overseer of the poor there who were thereby required to receive and provide for her according to Law

That in Pursuance of the said Order on the said 11th: Day of January the said Phillis Eagerly was removed and delivered to the Church wardens and Overseer of the Poor of the said parish of Saint Michael Crooked Lane Who have ever since provided for her

That Your Petitioners apprehending the said parish of saint Michael Crooked Lane to be aggrieved by the said Order Therefore humbley appeal to

to your Worships against the same And pray relief in the premises And that your Worships will be please to hear and determine this your Petitioners Appeal at the next General Sessions of the Peace Dated 2nd February 1796

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

John David Towse Sollr. to the Appellants}
