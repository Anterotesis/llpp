To the Right Worshifull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex In their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of it Poor of the Parish of St. Botolph without Bishopgate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant Under the hands and Seals of two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (One Whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 8th. Day of January 1747 Mary bryant (Called in the said Order) the wife of Benjamin Bryant Was Passed from the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of St. Botolph without Bishopgate London aforesaid Adjudging the Last Legal Place of Settlement of the said Mary Bryant to be in the said Parish of St. Botolph Without Bishopgate and your Petrs. Concerning themselves Agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the Said two Justices Do humbly Appeal therefrom and humbly Pray your Worships

Burr Atterney for Bishopgates Parish} Would Appoint some Day this Present General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County to hear this Appeal and that your Worshiops will make and Order that the Churchwardens land Overseers of the Poor of the Said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields and all Parties Concerned in and about the Premises may then Attend hear and abide the Final Determination of Your Worships And Your Petrs. Shall ever Pray Etc.
