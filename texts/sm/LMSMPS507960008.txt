To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace assembled.

Sheweth. The humble Petition and Appeal of The churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Acton in the County of Middlesex.

That by Virtue of a certain order or Pass Warrant under the the Hands and Seals of H. R. Courtenay and Jno. Griffiths Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex (one where of being of the Quorum) bearing date the sixth day of April 1785 Elizabeth Garrett was removed from and out of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex as the place of her last legal Settlement whereby Your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved.

Chetham Soliciter for the Appellants } Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said Order or Pass Warrant And pray that Your Worships will be pleased to appoint some day in this present Session for the hearing and determining thereof.

Middlesex (to wit) At the General Session of the Peace holden for the County of Middlesex at the Session house in and for the said County on Tuesday the Tenth day May in the Twenty fifth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third King of Great Britain Etc.

It is Ordered that the churchwardens and OVerseers of the Poor of the Parish of saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of westminster in the County of Middlesex have Notice of this Appeal And that they and all persons concerned do attend this Court at the Session house aforesaid on Thursday the Twelfth day of May instant at Eight of the Clock in the Forenoon to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.
