To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the peace for the said County Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of John Ford

Sheweth That by a certain Conviction under the hands and Seals of Sir Sampson Wright Knight and William Kitchiner Esquire two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the second day of March last Your Petitioner was and stands convicted for that your Petitioner on the twenty seventh day of January one thousand seven hundred and ninety one within the Kingdom of Great Britain (that is to say) in the parish of Saint Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex did sell to William Laudonsack twenty Buttons then and there being bound and each and every of them then And there being bound with certain Stuff called Stocking Kint, the said Stuff called Stocking Knit then and there being Stuff of which Clothes and Wearing Garments were then usually made contrary to the Statue in such Case made and provided And Your Petitioner was adjudged to forfeit and pay for the said offence the Sum of Eight Pounds six shillings and Eight pence.

That your Petitioner apprehends himself to be aggrieved by the said Conviction and therefore humbly Appeals against the same and.

Prays that your Worships will be pleased to appoint some day in this present Session for the hearing and determination thereof.

Middlesex. At the General Quarter Session of the peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex at the Session House for the said County on Clerkenwell Green (by adjournment) on Tuesday the twelfth day of April in the thirty first year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third King of Great Britain Etc.

It is Ordered that Sir Sampson Wright Knight and William Kitchiner Esquire have Notice of this Appeal and that they and all persons concerned do attend this Court at the Session House aforesaid on Thursday the fourteenth day of April instant to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

By the Court
