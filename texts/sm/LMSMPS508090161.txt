To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary le Bone in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Wm. Gascoigne and James Croft Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 17th: day of March 1786 John Jones Lamp lighter and Mary his wife and their five Children namely Charlotte Aged twelve Years Williams Aged Seven Years Ann Aged five Year John Aged four Years and Mary Aged 11 Months were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint Pancras in the County aforesaid to the said Parish of Saint Mary le Bone as the place of their last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved And humbly Appeal there from

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray your worships will be pleased to Hear them in the Premiser And to Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Pancras may appeal before you at a certain Day to be Appointed to Hear and abide the Judgment and determination of your Worships touching this Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall pray Etc

Thos Martin Sollr. for the Appellants
