To the Right Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex In their General Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopgate London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex One whereof being of the Quorum bearing Date [...] the Sixth day of November 1750 Mary Taylor (Wife of William Taylor) was removed from the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden in the Liberty of Westminster aforesaid to the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopgate London aforesaid Alledging the last legal place of Settlement of the said Mary Taylor to be there And your Petitioners Conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices Do humbly Appeal therefrom, and humbly pray Your Worships.

Burr Attorney for Bishopgate Parish.

Would appoint some day this present General Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex to hear this Appeal And that your Worships will make an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Paul And all parties Concerned in and about the Premisses may then and there attend hear and abide the final Determination of Your Worships

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
