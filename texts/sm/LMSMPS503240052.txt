To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx Assembled in Quarter Session

The Humble Petition of Edward Lane of Hammersmith in the Said County

Sheweth That Your Petitioner, by an Indenture bearing Date the 26th of October 1732 was bound Apprentice to John Wheeler of Hammersmith aforesaid Farrier. That his said Master Died upwards of three years Since that Theadocia Widow of his said Master, Carried on the said Trade till about the Month of March last Past, whose She became insolvent her Household Goods and Working Utensils being then Destrain'd and Sold which renders her uncapable of ever following the said Trade.

That Your Petitioner has ever since been unemploy'd and Destitute of the Knowledge of his business

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays that Your Worships would be Pleased to Discharge him from his said Apprenticehood that he may be turn'd over to another in Order to learn his Trade to Procure him a future Subsistance or Such other Relief as to Your Worships Shall Seem Meet

And Your Petitioner as in Duty bound Shall ever Pray Etc.

his Edward [x] Lane Mark
