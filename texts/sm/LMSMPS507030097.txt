To the worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex at their Quarter Session of the peace assembled

The humble Petition of Robert Tew

Sheweth That your Petitioner was bound Apprentice to Thomas Tew of Queen Street Seven Dials in the Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the County of Middlesex Coach maker by Indenture bearing Date the twenty third day of October and thousand seven hundred and sixty six.

That your Petitioner hath a faithfully served the said Thomas Tew for one year and upwards as his apprentice

That soon after your petitioner was bound apprentice his said Master hired himself to work Journey work and offered your Petitioner's Father five pounds if he would take his Son away from him but that your Petitioners Father told his said Master Thomas Tew, if he could not employ his Son your Petitioner that he the said Thomas Tew must fined another Master for him which the said Thomas Tew said he could not do under forty pounds And that he would not learn your petitioner his Trade because he would not hurt his own Family as he could not get Journey work if he kept him as his Apprentice

That your Petitioners said Master hired a Lodging for him when your Petitioner by laying with strange people got the Itch which it cost your Petitioners Father two Guineas to get him cured of and that your Petitioner is totally unable of being instructed in the said Business of a Coachmaker if he continues with the said Thomas Tew his Master

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays your Worships to order him to be discharged from his said apprenticeship or make such further order in the premisses as your Worships shall think fit

And your Petitioner as in Duty bound will ever pray Etc

Robert Tew
