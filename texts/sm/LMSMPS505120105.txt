To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions at Hicks Hall Assembled

She Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth,

That by Virtue of a pass Warrant or Order under the hands and seals of John Upton, and Wm. Rugge Esqrs. two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster (one where of being of the Quorum) bearing date the 30th. day of April 1762, Elizabeth Phillips Widow was removed from the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint James as the place of her last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners think themselves Aggrieved and Appeal to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that the benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto their until the next General Quarter Sessions of the peace to be holden for this County; And that the hearing and determining thereof may be Adjourned until the said next General Quarter Sessions of the peace And that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Saint George Hanover Square and all Persons concerned may be Ordered then to Attend To hear and Abide the Judgment and determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

And your Petitioners shale Pray Etc. Saml. Seddon Solicitor for the said parish of Saint James.
