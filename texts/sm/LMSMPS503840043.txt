To the Rt. Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General [...] Sessions of the Peace at Hicks's hall in [...] St. John Street in and for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Warden & Overseers of th Poor of the Liberty of Saffron hill Hatton Garden & Ely Rents in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands & Seals of Daniel Gach & James Trader Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (one of whom is of the the Quorum for the said County) bearing date the 4th. day of February instant Thomas Whitmell Martha Whitmell his wife and their two Children, to wit, Sarah aged thirteen Years and upwards and Martha aged eleven years and upwards were removed from the Parish of Saint Mar [...] in the Feilds in the said County to the said Liberty of Saffron-hill Hatton Garden & Ely Rents as the place of their last legal Settlemt.

Joshua Cox Sollr for the Appellts. Feb: 22d 1747 That your Petitioners conceiving themselves agreived by the said Warrant of the said two Justices DOE therefore Appeal to your Worships therefrom and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the Premes and order that the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Martin in the Feilds in the said County may appear before you at a Certain day to be appointed to bear & abide the Judgment & Determination of your Worships' touching this Appeale

And yor. Petrs. shall ever pray Etc.
