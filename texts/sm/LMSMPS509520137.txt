To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire Chairman and the next of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of Charles Gillett

Sheweth That on the twelfth day of June last your Petitioner was Convicted before William Bleamire Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex in the Penalty of Five Pounds upon the Information of one James Stallwood for taking on the Sixteenth Day of May last One Penny as and for and by way of Profit upon three Shillings but and advanced by your Petitioner upon a certain Pawn or Pledge of a Gown Pledged with your Petitioner by one Thomas Showell in the Name of William Bryant on the Fifteenth day of the said Month of May Contrary to the Statute

That your Petitioner Conceiving himself aggrieved by such Conviction humbly appeals to this Honourable Court from the same

And your Petitioner will ever Pray Etc
