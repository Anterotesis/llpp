To the Right worshipful William Morinwaring Esquire and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Nuthurst in the County of Sussex

Sheweth That by Virtue of un Order or Pass Warrant under the Hand and Seals of I. Slade and Edward B. Greene Esqr. two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one where of being of the Quorum) Rebecca Smith Widow of Thomas Smith and their three Children Vizt. Thomas Aged about Eight years Edward Aged about Six years and Francis Aged about three years were removed from and out of the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the Parish of Hulham in the said County of Middlesex into the said Parish of Nuthurst as the Place of their lastly at Settlement whereby your Petitioners apprehand themselves to be Aggrived.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Order a pass Warrant and pray that your Worships will appoint a Day in this Present Session for hearing and determining thereof And that your Petitioners may have such other Relief is the premises as to yord Worships shill Seine most

Wm. Eliz So [...] for the Appellants}
