To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for this said County.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Richard Cox of Queen Street in the Parish of Christ Church in the said County Weaver.

Sheweth

That your Petitioner was on the fifteenth day of March last Convicted before Daniel Williams Esquire Rice Davies Esquire and the Reversend Henry Reynett D. D there of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County for having in his Dwelling house alledged to be situate in King Street in the said Parish a Quantity of unwrought Silk suspected to have been purlioned and embezzled not having given any Account how your Petitioner came by the same

That your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by the said Conviction

Your Petitioner therefore Appeals from the said Conviction and humbly prays that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a day in this present Sessions to hear and determine the said Appeal

Richard Cox
