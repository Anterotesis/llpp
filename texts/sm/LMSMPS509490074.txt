To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Michael Crooked Lane in City of London on behalf of the said Parish

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of W Bleamire and A Graham two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex made the 29th Day of January last for and concerning the Settlement of Mary Blackleach upon the complaint of the Churchwardens or Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex It was adjudged That the Parish of Saint Michael Crooked Lane London was the place where the said Mary Blackleach was last settled And it was therefore ordered that she should be removed from the said Parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell to the said Parish of Saint Michael Crooked Lane and delivered to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor there who were thereby required to receive and provide for her according to Law

That in pursuance of the said Order on the said 29th: Day of January the said Mary Blackleach was removed and delivered to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of

of the said Parish of Saint Michael Crooked Lane who have ever since provided for her

That your Petitioners apprehending the said Parish of Saint Michael Crooked Lane to be aggrieved by the said Order Therefore humbly appeal to your Worships against the same And pray relief in the Premises And that your Worships will be pleased to hear and determine this your Petitioners appeal at the next Session of the Peace Dated 17th. April 1798

And Your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc

John David Towse Solr. for the Appellants}
