To the Worshipful the Bench of Justices

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Governors and Guardians of the Hospital for the maintenance and education of reposed and deserted young Children

Sheweth

That by an Order in writing under the Hand and Seal of Samuel Foyster Esqr one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex made on the 14th. day of October instant It was ordered that your Petitoners should writting six days after notice thereof pay the sum of Four pounds, thirteen Shillings and incipience charged upon them for and towards the repair of the Highways of the Parish of Saint Pancras to the Surveyors of the Highways of the said Parish and in case your Petitioners should neglect so to do he the said Samuel Foyster should immediately after issue a Warrant to levy the said sum together with Costs and Charges attending the same by Distress and Sale of your Petitioners Goods & Chattels by which said Order your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships that they may have an Appeal therefrom And your Petitioners shall ever prays
