To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green

The Humble Petition and Appeal of James Collins

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the 3d. day of Augt. last convicted in the Penalty of Five Pounds by Cranley Thos. Kerby Esquire one of the Justices of the Peace for the said County upon an Information exhibited and Prosecuted by Richard Coxford charging that your Petitioner did at the Parish of St. Giles in the said County on the sixteenth day of March last take and receive of one John Gordon on redeeming the Pledge thereinafter mentioned the Sum of two Pence by way of Profit upon three shillings [---] on the twelfth day of February last lent upon a Pledge of a Pair of Boots pledged in the name of [---] Mr. Walls to John Gordon contrary to the Statute Etc

That your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by such Conviction and is advised to appeal against the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays Your Worships will be Pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this present General Sessions of the Peace in and for the said County and the Your Worships will appoint a Day for hearing and determining the same.

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc.
