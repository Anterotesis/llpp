To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition of James Davies of Old Brent ford in the said County Victualler

Sheweth. That by virtue of an Order Judgment or Conviction under the hand and Seal of Nathaniel Bland Esquire one of his Majesties Justices of the peace in and for the said County bearing date the 4th. day of March 1794. the said Justices convicted your Petitioner in the po [...] Sum of five pounds and Corts for that your Petitioner did on the twenty fourth day of February 1794 Use or caused to be used a Cart upon a certain Turnpike Road in the Parish of Ealing in the said County the Sole or bottom of the Fellies of the Wheels of which said Cart being of less breadth or Gauge than six Inches and then and there did pass or was drawn with more than three Horses that is to say at the time and Parish last aforesaid the said Cart did pass or was drawn with four Horses contrary to the Statute.

That in pursuance of such Order Judgment or Conviction a Warrant of Distress was issued under the hand and Seal of the said Nathaniel Bland Esquire to levy the said Penalty of Five pounds in pursuance whereof the same was accordingly levied and paid by distress and Sale of the Goods and Chattles of your Petitioner

That your Petitioner conceives himself to be aggrieved by the said Judgment Order and Conviction and other proceedings aforesaid in as much as that he your Petitioner did not on the said 24th. day of February 1794 unlawfully Use or Cause to be Used any Cart of the description aforesaid upon the Public Highway or Turnpike Road aforesaid in the Parish of Ealing aforesaid or which did there pass or was drawn with more than three that is to say with four Horses as aforesaid at the time and parish last aforesaid as in the

the said [...] Judgment Order and Conviction is alledged

That your Petitioner conceive himself to be further aggrieved in the premises was much as your Petitioner is advised that the Proceedings against him before the said Nathaniel Bland Esquire for the aforesaid alledged Offence are in other respect errors one irregular and informal

Your Petitioner therefore appeal to your Worships against the said Judgment Order and Conviction and humbly prays Relief in the Premises and that your Worships will be pleased to Order that the said Sum of Five pounds so levied upon and paid by Distress and Sale of the Goods and Chattles of your Petitioner may be returned and repaid to him forthwith and that your Worships will be pleaded to appoint some Day in the present Session for the Hearing of this his Appeal

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc
