To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of ye peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition of the John Hope.

Humbly Sheweth That your Petitioner on the Eighth day of January 1761 did put himself Apprentice to Elizabeth Steel Widow of well Street in the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the County of Middlesex Grocer for the Term of Seven years from the Ninth day of November 1760 to learn her art or business of a Grocer She finding the sd Apprentice Meat Drunk Washing Lodging and all other necessares during the sd Term (Clothing expected)

That the sd. Elizabeth Steel died about Christmas last and that John Barrell Sugar Baker being Le cutor of the Will of the sd Elizabeth steel hath taken your Petitioner as Apprentice to himself against his Consent and to another Business which he does not like but would willingly serve out time to a Grocer which he was at the first put to

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays that he may be discharged from the sd John Barrell and turned over to another Master a Grocer or be otherwise releived in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.

And Your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc

John Hope
