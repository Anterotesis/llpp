Middlesex To the Worshipfull John Hawkins Esquire and other his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their present General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Catherine Billson

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the thirtieth Day of August last Convicted before William Kelynge Esquire one of his Maties Justices of the Peace for the said County [...] of Receiving two Ounces of Silh the Property of John Garsed and John Moyrick of Gould Street neer Cheapside in the City of London Weavers and Copartners and by them Delivered to Thomas Makepeace to be Manufactured.

That your Petitioner thinks herself greatly Aggrieved by the said Conviction Therefore beggs leave to Appeal and hereby Appeals against the same and every part thereof

And most humbly Prays that the said Conviction May be Quashed And She Allowed her reasonable Costs and Charges Occasion thereby And that your Petitioner may have Such further and other Relief as to your Worships may seem Meet

And your Petitioner will ever pray Etc

Edwd Denton Sol for the said Catherine Billson
