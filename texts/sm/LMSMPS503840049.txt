To the Right Worshipfull his Maties. Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Gillingham in the County of Kent

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant Under the hands and Seals of Henry Norris and Samuel Tyssen Esqrs. Two of his Maties. Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (One being of the Quorum) bearing date the Nineteenth day of January in the Year of our Lord 1747. William Carey John Carey and Thomas Carey (Called) the lawfull Issue of William Carey and Sarah his Wife were Removed from the Parish of Saint John at Hackney in the County of Middlesex unto the said Parish of Gillingham in the said County of Kent Alledging the said Parish of Gillingham to be the Place of their last Legall Settlement

Thos. Tomlyn: Agent for the Apellants That your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said Two Justices Do therefore Appeal to your Worships therefrom And humbly Pray that you will be Pleased to Hear them in the premisses And Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint John at Hackney in the County of Middlesex and all Persons concerned May Appear before you at a certain day to be Appointed to Hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships Touching the Appeal

And your Petitioners shall Pray Etc

George Dann George Burnes} Churchwardens

John Simmons Thos, Pankhurst-} Overseers
