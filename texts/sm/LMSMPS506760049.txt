Middlesex

To the Worshipful Six John Hawkins Knight and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in their General Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of Thomas Rogers

Sheweth

That your Petitioner has for some time had the honour of being Employed as Surveyor to the several Buildings which have been carryed on under the directions of the Gentlemen in the Commission of the Peace for this County and Presumes to hope that he may alledge without the Imputation of Variety that his endeavours to give satisfaction have not wholly disappointed his intention.

That your Petitioner hath surveyed the Work done pursuant to the last Contract with W Wood at New Prison and the House of Correction at Clerkenwell to the amount of £2302..3..5¾ whence at a charge of £5 plent there is due to your Petitioner the Sum of £115..2..2¾

That your Petitioner has made various designs of different parts of the Work, adjusted the respective Workmen's Bulls and measured and examined their Work; and attended all the Meetings of the Committee under whose immediate view and Control the Buildings were made

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays that your Worships would be pleased to order the said Sum of £ 115..2..2¾ to be paid him out of your Worships Treasury or as your Worships shall [...] meet

And Your Petitioner will ever pray Etcs Thomas Rogers
