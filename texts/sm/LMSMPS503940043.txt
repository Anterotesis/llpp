To the right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their general Quarter Sessions assembled.

1749 The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Anne in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a Warrant or Order bearing date the eighth day of March last past under the hands and Seals of Walter Burry and N Dukinfield Esqrs. one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) George Toogood Fisherman Elizabeth in his Wife and their three Children, to wit Elizabeth Toogood aged Fifteen Mary aged Eleven and Sarah aged four years were removed from the Hamlet of Popular and Blackwall in this County to the Said Parish of Saint Anne as the Place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the Said Order of the Said two Justices do appeal against the Same

And humbly pray that this Court will be pleased to appoint a Day this present Session for all Parties to attend to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of this Court touching the Said Appeal

And your Petrs. will pray Etc.

Joseph Hester Agent for and Churchwarden of Saint Anne
