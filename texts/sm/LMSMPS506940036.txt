To the Right Worll: Hen Mosties Justice of the Peace for the County of Middlesex

The Humble Peticon of John Cox on the behalf of Phillip Grice his Son in Law

Sheweth That on the nineteenth day of December in the Year of our Lord 1701 the said Phillip Grice was Put forth an Apprentice to Thomas Smith of St Clement Danes Poulterer for the Term of Seven Years

That Yor. Petr did Covenant wth: the said Master to find the said Apprentice Necessary Appwell of all fort during the said Term

That about Two Years Since the said Master Died & his widow not Following the Trade the Apprentice went to Sea in Her Maties: Service where he now remaines And the widow of the said Thomas Smith haveing taken no Care to fitt out the said Phillip Grice or to Provide any thing for him

Your Pett there fore humbly Prayes (the Prmisses considered) that this Court will be Pleased to Order Anne the widow of the said Thomas Smith to appeare in this Court four day this Session to shew cause why she should not delivers up the Indenture of Apprentice hood for that the Said Phillip Grice may be discharged

And Yor Pets Shall en Pray Etc.
