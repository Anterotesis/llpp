To the Worshipfull Sir John Hawkins Knight and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden at Hicks's Hall in and for the County of Middlesex on Monday the tenth Day of January 1774.

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Humberstone in the County of Leicester

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of Joseph Girdler and Stephen Clarke Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the thirteenth Day of December last past Thomas West together with Rachael his Child aged Six Months have been removed from the Parish of Saint Pancreas in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Humberstone as the Place of the said Thomas West's last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be injured and aggrieved by the said Order

Your Petitioners therefore appeal against the said Order and humbly pray that the Hearing and Determination of them said Appeal may be adjourned to the next General Sessions of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County of Middlesex

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Stattd: Squire; Baxter Sollr for the said Appellts
