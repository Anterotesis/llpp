To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire Chairman and to the rest of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of Robert Holmes of the parish of Hanworth in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a rate made for the relief of the Poor of the parish of Feltham in the County of Middlesex bearing date the Eleventh day of May One thousand Seven Hundred and Ninety One certain pieces and parcel of Meadow Land Arrable Land and premises Situate in the said parish in the Occupation of Your Petitioner have been Assessed and rated at the Sum of Five pounds and Seventeen Shillings from the first Day of May aforesaid to the end of Sixteen Weeks from thence next ensuing to be Gathered out of the said parish in the Proportion of twelve pence in the pound by which your Petitioner conceives himself agreieved and humbly Apprehends that such rate is Excessive And that such Rate ought not to have been more than Five pounds being One Shilling in the pound on the rent of such Land and premises and that such rate in unequal irregular and Contrary to Law

Your Petitioner therefore Humbly Prays Prays that a day may he appointed for the hearing and determining the said Appeal and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Feltham and all others may attend your Worships to hear and abide the Order and Determination of your worships concerning the said Appeal and that the aforesaid rate may be Quashed

And your Petitioner shall ever pray
