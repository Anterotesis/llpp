To the Right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (whereof one is the Quorum) therein mentioned bearing date the fourteenth day of November one thousand seven hundred and eighty five William Sale was removed from the Parish of Saint Luke Old Street in the said County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate in the City of London as the last Legal Place of his Settlement By which your Petitioners humbly conceive themselves aggrieved and appeal to this Court against the said Pass Order,

Your Petitioners therefore pray your Worships will be pleased to hear and Determine the matters touching this Appeal and give them such relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem just and meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Richard Welch Junior Attorney or Agent for the Appellts.

Middlesex-ss, At the General Session of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex

at the Sessions House for the said County on Monday the Twelfth day of December in the Twenty sixth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith Etc.

It is Ordered by this Court that the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint Luke Old Street in the County of Middlesex have Notice of the this Appeal And that they and all Parties concerned do attend this Court on Thursday next eight of the Clock in the Forenoon to heard and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal,
