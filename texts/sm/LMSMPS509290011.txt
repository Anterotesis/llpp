To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Session of the Peace for the said County at the Session House on Clerkenwell Green on Monday the Twelfth Day of September 1796

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Robert Armstrong of Shorts Gardens in the said County Pawnbroker

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the Seventeenth day of August last Convicted in the Penalty of Five pounds by Thomas Robinson Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex upon an Information Exhibited and Prosecuted by James Tuffield charging that your Petitioner being a Person using and excercising the Trade and business of a Pawnbroker at Shorts Gardens in the said county on the second day of July now last past at Shorts Gardens aforesaid did take and receive of and from one Esther Dale a certain Pledge to wit a Ring upon which your Petitioner did then and there lend the sum of Three Shillings and six pence and at the time of taking the said Pledge did neglect and refuse to give to the said Esther Dale a Note or Memorandum as directed by the Statute in that Case made and provided and did receive and retain the said Pledge without the said Esther Dale accepting or taking the said Note or Memorandum contrary to the form of the said Statute

That your Petitioner conceives himself agrieved by such Conviction and is advised to appeal against the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his appeal so that the same may be heard at this present General Session of the Peace in and for the said County and that you Worships will appoint a day for hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner as in duty bound will ever pray Etc
