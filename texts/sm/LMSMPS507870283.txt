To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in Your General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Islington in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of William Quarrill and John Staples Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the Nineteenth day of October 1784 Elizabeth Lighton Widow of David Lighton deceased and seven of their Children to wit Ann aged twelve years. Eleanor aged eleven Years Charlotte aged nine years Susanna aged Seven Years David aged five years George aged two years and William aged one year were removed and conveyed from and out of the Hamlet of Poplar and Blackwall in the said County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint Mary Islington as the place of their last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships that the benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determination thereof may be adjourned unto the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County

And your Petrs- shall ever pray Etc.
