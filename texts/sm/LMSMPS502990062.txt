To To the Right Worshipfull his majesty's Justices [...] the peace for the County of Middlesex assembled in their Generall Sessions of the peace

The humble Petition and appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Westham in the County of Essex.

Humbly Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of Oliver Lambert: and Miles.Harper Esquires two of his majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex (One whereof is of the Quorum) bearing date the Thirteenth Day of March One thousands seven hundred and thirty three that Ann Hudson Wife of William Hudson and their child James aged about Fifteen weeks were removed from the Said Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex aforesaid to the said parish of Westham as the place of their last legall Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore thinking themselves aggreived by the Order of the said two Justices appeal to this Court and humbly pray that the churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster and all parties concerned may be ordered to attend this Court some day this Sessions to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of this Court touching this Appeal

And Your Petitioner Shall ever pray and so forth

John Hodgkins Agent for the Appellants
