To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Thomas Heming of the Parish of Saint John Hackney in the County of Middlesex Victualler.

Sheweth That by a certain Order and Conviction under the hand and Seal of John Spiller Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the third day of February in the thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord King George the third and in the Year of our Lord one thousand seven hundred and ninety one your Petitioner is Convicted of Knowingly suffering Gaming at a certain Game called Justice Jarvis with half pence in his House by two Apprentices And your Petitioner is by the aforesaid order or Conviction of the said Justice Adjudged to have thereby Forfeited the Sum of Forty Shillings.

Your Petitioner Concieving himself Aggrieved by the said Order and Conviction of the said Justice humbly Appeals again the same And prays that the same may be Quashed And that your Worships w [...] give your Petitioner such further and other Relief in the Premises as your Worships shall think fit.

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
