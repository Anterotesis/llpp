To the Worshipful. His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex, Assembled, in their Quarter Session

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens, And others Gentleman Ancient Inhabitants of the Parish of Chelsea in the Said County.

Sheweth That That Thomas Mills and Danl. Sexton were Appointed Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish for the Year 1732. and having been out of their Office for several Months, The Parishioners being apprehensive that Great Injustice has been done and Committed by some Parish Officers, they Mett in Vesty, and y an Order Nominate and Appointed several Gentleman Inhabitants of the said Parish as as a Committee (amongs other Affairs) To Examine into the Accounts of the said Overseers of the Poor, And sheer finding several Articles there in Charged to be very Extravagant, and others Evedently false and Unjust [...] humbly Think Themselves Greatly Imposes upon, and Agrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly prayes this Court will be Pleased to Referr the Accounts of the said Overseers of the Poor, to some Neighbouring Gentlemen in the County Commission of the peace, to Examine into the Pticulars of the Petitioiners Complaint, [...]

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Agast for the Appellants

Molmes
