To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace the County of Middlesex in their of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of John Sherwood and Jasper Clarke Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Tenth day of April now last past Mary Ponder Wife of Charles Ponder who hath deserted and left her and her three Children Vizt. Charles aged about four years William aged about two Years and Ann aged about Two Months were removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel in the county of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint George as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Ponder and her said three Children

Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the Adjudication of the said Two Justices and therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the same And play your Worships will appoint a Day for the hearing and determining the said Appeal And your Petitioner will ever pray Etc.

Major Wright Sollicitor for the Appellants
