To the Right Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex In their General Quarter Sessions Assembled as

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Over seers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Botolph Without Bishopgate London.

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hand said Seals of two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx (One Whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 24th. Day of Septr. 1748 Alice Lovet Wife of William Lovet, In the Said Order Called Alice Castboult Single woman, Was Passed from the Parish of St. Luke in the County of Middlesex To the Parish of St. Botolph Without Bishopgate London aforesaid Alledging the last legal Place of Settlement of the Said Alice to be in the Said Parish of St. Botolph Without Bishopgate And Your Petitioners Comewing themselves Agrieved by the Said Order and Judgement of the Said two Justices Do humbly Appeal therefrom and humbly pray Your Worships

Burr Attorney for Bishopgate Parish} Would Appoint some Day this Present General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the Said County to hear this Appeal and that your Worships Will make an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor and all of the Said Parish of St. Luke and All Parties Concerned in and About the Premises may then Attend hear and Abide the Final Determination of Your Worships

And Your Petr, Shall ever Pray Etc
