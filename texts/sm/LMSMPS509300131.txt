To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of Mary Buckingham of the Parish of Hendon in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth,

That by a certain Conviction under the Hand and Seal of John Spiller Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the twenty third day of September one thousand seven hundred and ninety Six made on the Information of our William Clarke, your Petitioner was Convicted as therein alledged for that your Petitioner on the fifteenth day of September last at the Parish of Paddington in the said County of Middlesex did use and cause to be used a Cart of which your Petitioner was then Owner without having painted or caused to be painted upon some conspicuous part of the said Cart, your Petitioners Christian and Surname and Place of abode in large legible Letters contrary to the form of the Statute in each Case made and provided and the said Justice did adjudge your Petitioner to forfeit for the Offence supposed to be committed by your Petitioner the Sum of Five pounds.

That your Petitioner thinks herself aggrieved by the Judgement of the said Justice and therefore

Your Petitioner doth Appeal to your Worships at this present General Quarter Session of the Peace against the Judgement of the said Justices and doth pray such Relief in the Premisses as to your Worships shall seem meet

M Buchingham
