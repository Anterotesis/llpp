To the Right Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church- wardens & Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Botolph without Bishop gate London

Sheweth

That by Vertue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Tho: Exelbee Esqr. and- Ri: Farmer Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex One whereof being of the Quorum bearing Date the 9th. Day of April in the Year of our Lord 1737 Thomas Cooper was Passed from the Hamlett of Bethanll Green in the County of Middlesex To the Parish of St. Botolph without Bishopgate London aforesaid Alledging the last Legal Place of Settlement of the said Thomas- Cooper to be there And your Petitioners conceiving themselves Agreived by the said Order and Judgement of the said two Justices Do Humbly Appeal therefrom And Humbly pray your Wroshipps would Appoint-

Some Day the Present General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be held for the said County to- hear this Appeal and that your Worshipps will make an Order that the Churchwarden- and Overseers of the Poor of the Said Hamlett of Bethnall-Green and all partys Concerned- in and about the premisses may then Attend Hear and Abide the Final Determination of Your Worshipps

Burr Attorn for Appellts.

And Your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc
