To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of Thomas Kynaston and Thomas Balack Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster (one of them being of the Quorum) bearing date the 16th. day of June 1760. Judith Roundtree wife of John Roundtree was Removed from the Parish of St. Martin in the feilds in the Liberty of Westminster to the said Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster as the Place of her last Legal Settlement, Whereby Your Petitioners Conceive themselves to be Aggrieved, and against the said Order do Appeal

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray that this Worshippfull Court will be pleased to appoint a Day in this Present Session for hearing and determining this Appeal, and that all Persons concerned may then and their attend

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.

Saml. Peirson Wm. Selfe} Churchwardens of St. Margaret Westmr.

Titus Wilson Jno Burfrod} Overseers of the Poor of [...] Parish [...]
