To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green on the Second day of July 1798.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Mary Minns.

Sheweth That in consequence of a Summons issued under the hand and Seal of Cranley Thomas Kerby Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County and served on Your Petitioner he appeared before the Magistrates at the Public Office in Queen Square Westminster upon an Information exhibited and prosecuted by James Stallwood charging that Your Petitioner did at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the City and Liberty of Westminster in the said County on the fourteenth day of May last take and receive of George Winton in the Name of Mr. Wright on redeeming the Pledge therein mentioned One Penny by way of Profit upon three Shillings on the Eleventh day of May aforesaid lent upon a Pledge of a Waistcoat Contrary to the Statute Etc And Your Petitioner was upon the hearing of the said Information convicted in the Penalty of Five Pounds.

That your Petitioner conceives himself agreived by such Conviction and is advised to appeal against the same.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this present Quarter Session of the Peace in and for the said County And that your Worships will appoint a day for hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
