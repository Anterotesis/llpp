To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Chairman and the rest of His Majestys Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Lewisham in the County of Kent

Sheweth. That by a pass Warrant or order under the Hands and Seals of Richard Clark Esquire and William Bleamire Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the Fifth day of August 1796. Margaret Baker Widow, and her three Children, Vizt. Edward Beckwith, aged about 9 Years, Mary Ann aged about 5 Years and a half, and Thomas aged about 2 Years and a half were removed from and out of the Liberty of the Rolls to the said Parish of Saint Mary at Lewisham whereby your Petitioners Conceive themselves aggrieved and humbly Appeal there from

Your Petitioners, therefore humbly pray Your Worships that a day may be appointed for the hearing and determining their said Appeal, And that the said Order of removal, may be quashed, and that your Worships may make such other order in the premises as to Justice appertain.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray.
