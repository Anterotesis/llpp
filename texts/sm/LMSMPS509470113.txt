To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petitioin and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of Joseph Merceron Esquire and Daniel Williams Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices [...] assigned to keep the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof of the Quorum) bearing date th third day of January one thousand seven hundred and Ninety Eight William Wood Mary his Wife and their three lawful Children namely Eleanor aged Seven Years or thereabouts who in the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal is stated to have gained no legal Settlement in her own right Sarah aged five Years or thereabouts were removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the City of London as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said William Wood Mary his wife and their said three Children namely Eleanor Sarah and Mary

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Pass Warrants order of Removal of the said Two Justices Do therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the same praying such Relief in the Premises as the this Court shall Seven meet

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

[...] Sollicitor for the appellants
