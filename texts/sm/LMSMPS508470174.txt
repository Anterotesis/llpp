To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace at the Sessions House on Clerkenwell Green in the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of John Spiller and Tho. F Cotton two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the 20th. day of June 1789 Samuel Angier Son of Charles Angier was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint John at Hackey in the said County to the said Parish of Saint James as the place of his last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and pray that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day to hear and determine the said Appeal.

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc.

Jas: Dyson

Solicitor for the Appellants
