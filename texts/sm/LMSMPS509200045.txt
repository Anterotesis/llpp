To the Right Worshipful Williams Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Crawley in the County of Sussex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of John Scotland P Neve Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex of (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Seventeenth day of December One thousand Seven hundred and Ninety five John Chettle, Hannah his wife and their two Children (to wit) Martha aged about four Years and nine Months and William aged eleven Months were removed from and out of the parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the said County of Middlesex into the said parish of Crawley as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby Your petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Order or pass Warrant and pray that Your Worships will appoint a Day at the Adjournment of this present Session for hearing and determining thereof and that Your Petitioners May have such other relief in the premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.

W. Ellis Solr. for the Appellants}
