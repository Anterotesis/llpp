To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Now in their Court of General Quarter Sessions of the peace at the Guildhall Westminster in and for the said County Assembled

The Humble petition and appeal of John Busher Waterman

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on the fifth day of March last Summoned (On the Information of One George Patterson) to appear before the Worshipful John Staples and Peter Greens Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County to answer the Complaint of the said George Patterson for unlawfully taking upon himself the Charge of Navigating a Certain Ship or Vessel Called the Hanbury on the River Thomas as Pilot thereof Contrary to the Statute

That in Consequence of such Summons your Petitioner did on the Sixth day of said March last Appear before the said Justices who thought proper on the Oath of Charles Bredie to Convict him in the Penalty of Twenty Pounds

Wherefore Your Petitioner Conceiving himself aggrieved by the said Conviction of the said Justices Humbly appeals to this Court against the same praying such relief in the Premises as to your [---] Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioner is in Duty Bound shall ever pray Etc.

John Busher

B Hyams Sollicitor for the appellant 16th April 1707
