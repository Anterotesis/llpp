To the Worshipfull Thomas Lane Esqr. Chairman and the rest of the Bench of Justices Assembeld at Hick's Hall

The Humble Petition of Richard Peers

Humbly Sheweth That your Worships Petitioner was Hired by Mr John Turner Late keeper of the House of Correction at Clerkenwell as a Weekly Servant at Seven Shillings per Week in which Service he Continues the Whole Time Mr. Turner was Keeper

That during which Time your Worships Petitioner Received but Six pounds nineteen Shillings and Two Pence and Mr Turner is now Indebted to your Worships Petitioner in the sum of Seventeen Pounds Eight a Shilling and Four Pence

That your Worships Petitioner Humbly Relying on Your Worships Goodness, and Hearing there is some Salary due to Mr Turner most Humbly Prays your Worships will take Case into Consideration and Grant him such Relief as your Worships Think most Proper as your Worships Petitioner as been a Considerable Time without his Money

And as in Duty Bound your Worships Petitioner

Wile Ever Pray Etc

Richard Peers

12 Janry 1764
