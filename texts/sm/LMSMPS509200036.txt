To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Michael in the Borough of Saint Allan in the County of Hertford

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of Sir William Gibbons Bart. and Thomas Wood junr. Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one of which being of the quorum) bearing Date the twenty fourth day of October one thousand seven hundred and ninety five Ann Spriggs Wife of John Spriggs and her three Children, Rosannah aged about fourteen years, Mary aged about eleven years and John aged about nine years, were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Staines in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Michael in the said Borough of Saint Alban as the lawful Settlement of the said Ann Spriggs and her said three Children Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved and against the said Order to Appeal

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that a Day may be appointed in the present Session to hear and determine the Matter touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

John Boys, Solicitor for the said Parish of St. Michael
