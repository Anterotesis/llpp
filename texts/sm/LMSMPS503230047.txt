To the Right Worshipfull His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex.

The humble Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Peterle Poor London

Sheweth That by an Order dated the 14th. of May 1737 under the hands and Seals of Clifford william Phillips and Richard Farmer Esquires (two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex whereof one is of the Quorum) One Mary Craggs and Hannah Craggs the Daughter of her the Said Mary and Thomas Craggs her late husband deced were passed from the parish of St. Mary White Chappell in the Said County to the Said Parish of St. Peterle Poor in London as the place of their last legall Settlement

That the Said Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Peter le poor conceive themselves aggrieved by the Said Order

And therefore Pray this Honoble Court to appoint a day for hearing the Said Appeal against the Sd. Order

And your Petr. Shall ever pray Etc

At the Generall Sessions of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks hall in St. John Street in the County aforesaid on Tuesday the twenty fourth day of May Ja the Tenth year of the reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Second King of Great Britain France and Iraland Defender of the Faith.

It is Ordered by this Court that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Mary White Chappell in this County have notice of this Appeal and that they and all persons concerned do attend this Court on Friday next at nine of the Clock in the forenoon to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the Said Appeal

By the Court Walter [x]
