To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for fot the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Parish of Shenley in the County of Hertford

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of J. Girdler and Stephen Clarke Esquires Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty first Day of February last past Ann Dancer Widow and her Four Children namely Mary aged about Ten Years, Rebecca Aged about Six Years, John Thomas Aged about Four Years, and Fanny Aged about Two years, were removed from the Parish of Saint Mary Islington in the said County of Middlesex to the [...] said Parish of Shenley in the said County of Hertford as the last legal Settlement of the said Ann Dancer and of her said Four Children Mary Rebecca John Thomas and Fanny, whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved and Against the said Order do Appeal

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray That a Short Day may be appointed this present Session to hear and determine the matter touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioner shall over Pray.

At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the said County by Adjournment on Tuesday the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third King of Great Britain Etc.

It is Ordered that Notice of this Appeal be given to the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Islington in this County and that they and all Persons concerned do attend this Court on Thursday next at nine of the Clock in the forenoon to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the Court Touching the said Appeal

By the Court Waller
