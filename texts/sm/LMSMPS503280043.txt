To the Worshipfull the Chairman and the rest of the Justices assembled at the Generall Quarter Sessions of the peace at Hicks hall in St. John Street in & for the County of Middlesex

The humble Petition of Thomas Hull Headborough of the Parish of St.Luke

Sheweth That yor. Petr. being Summoned to attend your Worships on Wednesday the Eleventh day of [---] this instant January yor. Petr. did accordingly attend till the adjournment of the Court

That your. Petr. was on the next day Subpened to appear before the Grand Jury to give Evidence against Edward Cundelll and Philip Brown two House breakers and accordingly did attend and give Evidence there and afterwards at the old Baily where they were found Guilty But your Petr being Ignorant of the Practice of this Honble Court did not ask Leave so to do since which your Petr. has been informed yor Worships have laid a Fine on him

Therefore yor. Petr. most humble prays that yor Worships would be pleased in regard he did it thro Ignorance and the Case being as above set forth that you would be pleased to discharge him from such fine your Petr. hereby promising never to offend in the like

And yor. Petr Shall ever pray Tho: Hull
