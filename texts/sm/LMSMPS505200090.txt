To the Right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereby being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Thirteenth day of January 1763 Elizabeth Hambleton Aged About Twenty Eight Years and her Son Joseph Aged About Eighteen Months were Removed from the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham in the said County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the said County of Middlesex Adjudging the last legal place of Settlement of the said Elizabeth Hambleton to he there And Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order and Judgmt: of the said Justices do humbly Appeal therefrom,

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that your Worships would be Pleased to appoint some day in this Present Sessions And that your Worships will make an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Hamlet of Hammersmith and all Parties Concerned in and about the Premisses may then and there hear and abide the final Determination of your Worships therein

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc,

Thomas Old John Longland} Churchwardens

Wm. Killingworth Willm Lacon} Overseers of the Poor
