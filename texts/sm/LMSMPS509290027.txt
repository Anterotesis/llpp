To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Allhallows London Wall in the City of London

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of J. Floud and P. Colquhoun Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 18th. day of August 1796 Sarah Foxwell Widow of Charles Foxwell Deceased and her two Children lawful Issue namely Mary Ann aged four years and upwards and Charles aged ten Months were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the County of Middlesex to the said Perish of Allhallows London Wall London as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and pray that your Worships will be pleased to Appoint a Day to hear and Determine the said Appeal

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc

Willm: Hughes for the Appellants
