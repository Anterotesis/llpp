To the Worshipfull his Majestyes Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of John Abbott

Sheweth That by Indentures of Apprenticeshipp bearing Date the Twenty Eighth day of August One thousand Seven hundred and fifty One Your Petitioner was bound Apprentice to Robert Noyes of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the said County Carver for the Space of Seven Years

That your Petitioners Father Thomas Abbott Paid as a Consideracon to the said Robert Noyes at the time of the Executing of the said Indentures the Sum of Twenty Pounds

That your Petitioners said Master Robert Noyes hath very frequently taken him and his fellow Apprentice into Gardens belonging to other Persons to Pilfer Trees that were growing Flower Roots and Box for Borders and divers other things till at length your Petitioner began to Apprehend the fatal Consequences that might result from such Unjust Proceedings and therefore your Petitioner Applyed to his Friends Interpose in his favour to Prevent his Destruction

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays your Worshipps to Discharge him from his said Indentures of Apprenticeshipp and to Order that his said Master may return Part of the said Twenty Pounds Consideration Money to your Petitioner

And your Petitioner (as in duty bound) will ever Pray Etc

John Abbott.
