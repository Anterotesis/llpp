To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices for the County of Middlesex in Sessions assembled

The Humble Petition of Richard Murthwaite

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on the 3d. day of January 1799 convicted before Henry James Pye and Joseph moser Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex upon the Oath of William Kettle in the Penalty of Five Pounds for demanding and receiving of the said William Kettle one penny as Profit upon Two Shillings lent by your Petitioner upon Pdcon of Waistcoat to the said William Kettle in the name or under the Description of M. Catel one penny being more than at the rate of one half penny for the Loan of any Sum not exceeding the Sum of Two Shillings and Pence by the Calendar Month including the Currant month on which the said Pawn was received contrary to the Statute Etc by which Conviction your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved

And therefore Your Petitioner appeals to this Court against the same and prays that your Worships will hear him thereon and grant him such Relief as his Case requires and that a day may be appointed for the hearing and determining the Same

And Your Petitioner will over pray

Richd. Murthwaite
