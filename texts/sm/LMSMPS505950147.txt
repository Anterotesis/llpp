To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Michael Cornhill London

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of Sr: Robt: Darling Knight and David Wilmot Esquire two of his (Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Eleventh day of April 1769. Honour Page and her three Children to wit John Page of the Age of Four Years or thereabouts Thomas Gregory Page of the Age of Three Years or thereabouts and Paul Francis Page of the Age of Three Months or thereabouts were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint George in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Michael Cornhill London as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners think themselves aggreived and appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray this Court will please to grant them such Relief in the Premisses as shall be thought equitable

Richard Waller Sollicitor for [...] the Appellants }
