To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at the Quarter Session held at the Sessions House Clerkenwell Green in and for the County of Middlesex on Monday the 26th. day of October 1789

The Humble Petition and appeal of Joseph Attersol Maltster.

Sheweth That your Petitioner on or about the seventeenth day of July last was convicted on the Information of one William Boyd before John Slade and Joseph Wilton Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the sd. County of Middlesex in the Sum of hundred and twenty three Pounds fifteen Shillings For that your Petitioner had mixed 495 Burhils of Corn which had been wetted and steeped and making into Malt on the 18th day June last past some part thereof being of one wetting or steeping and other part there of being of a former wetting or Steeping before the same had been puet on the [...] for dressing

That your Petitioner conceiving himself aggrieved by such Conviction is advised to appeal against such Conviction on the sd Information in manner aforesaid

Therefore your Petitioner most humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his appeal before you against such Conviction

And your Petitioner and Appellant will ever pray Etc

Joseph Attersol 7 Octr. 1789
