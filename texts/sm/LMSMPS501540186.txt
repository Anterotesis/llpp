To the Rt: Worsppll his Matys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx

The humble peticon of William Beresford

Humbly Sheweth

That your peticoner by his Indenture of Apprenticehood dated the 24th: day of October Anne Dom 1713 become an Apprentice to one Mathew Musson of the Parish of St James Westminster Plaisterer for the terme of seaven yeares Com during from the Date of the said Indent me As thereby may Appeare.

That your Peticoners said Master by reason of Severall Debld he hath Contracted hath Absconded from his Habitation Ever since February last and is now in Ireland And since his departure all his Goods have beene Seized and disposed on and Your Petitrs. mistresse turned out of doores.

That your peticoners hath great reason to Beheve his sd: Master. Intends to Settle in Ireland Having sent over for his Wife (who as your peticoner is Informed setts out for Ireland this Weeke) Soe that your peticoner ever since February last hath beene oblieged to apply himselfe to his Friends for comon Susteynances and hath beene noe waies Instructed his Trade Soe that your peticoner if not Relieved in the premises by this Court will be utterly ruined.

Your Peticoner therefore humbly prays he may be Discharged from his said Indenture of Apprenticehood If your Worshipps soe think fett.

And Yor. Petr: Shall Ever pray Etc

Wm Beresford
