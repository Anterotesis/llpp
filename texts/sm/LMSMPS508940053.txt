To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace Acting in and for the County of Middlesex at the Court of Quarter Session assembled.

Whereas Joseph Drury of the Parish of Harrow on the hill in the County of Middlesex Gentleman, some time in the Month of July 1789. was by an Order of the said Court Sworn into the office of High Constable for the Hundred of Gore in the County of Middlesex before William Mainwaring Esquire which office he has Executed for the space of four years and upwards.

We his Majestys Justices of the peace acting is and for the said Hundred of Gore Pope your Worships will order the said Joseph Drury to be discharged pray the said Office and Offer to your Worships choice either of the under witten persons every of them being fit and proper persons to Execute the said Office And do Recommend the under named Benjamin We all to Serve the said Office in the stead of the above named Joseph Drury. Witness our hands this 3d. day of February 1794

Jno Rudge Wm. Phillimore

Mr. Benjamen Weall. of Helton Green parish of Harrow Faunts Mr. Henry Greenhill. Mr. William Walker.
