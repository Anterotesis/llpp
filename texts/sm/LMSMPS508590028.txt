To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General [---] Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of Pio Sienesi of the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden in the sd County of Middlesex Pawn broker

Sheweth

That by a certain Conviction in writing under the Hand and Seal of William Hyde Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex bearing Date the Twenty Fourth Day of September last your Petitioner was Convicted before the said Justice For that he your Petitioner being a Person broker on the Tenth Day of August then last fast at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden aforesaid in the County of Middlesex aforesaid did take and receive of and from and Phineas Andrews a certain Person or Pledge that is to say as a Silver Table Spoon and did then and there Advance and lend therein the Sum of Ten Shillings and Six pence of Lawful Money of Great Britain

That being such Person Broker as aforesaid and not regarding the Statute in Such Case made and provided North Hearing the Penalty therein Contained Did not before your Petitioner had Advanced and Cent the said Sum of Ten Shillings and Six Pence upon the said Person or Pledge Enter or Cause to be entered in a Fair and regular Manner in a any Book or Books kept for that Purpose a Description of the sd. Table Spoon which your Petitioner so received in Person and also the said Sum of Money to be advanced and Cent there on with the Day of the Month and Year on which such Table Spoon

was pawned and pledged and the name and place of abode of the Person by whom the same was [...] pawned and Pledged and also the name and place of abode of the Owner thereof according to the Information of the Person P [...] ring or- pledging the same according to the Direction of the Statute in such Case made and provided but wholly neglected to make such Entry as aforesaid contrary to the Form of the Statute in such Case made and provided

And you Petitioner was adjudged to pay and Forfeit For Such Offence the Sum of Ten Pounds Whereby your Petitioner concieves himself Aggrieved

[---] Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays that he may be at liberty a day in this present Session may be appointed for the to Appeal to the said Conviction And that hearing and determining thereof [...] [...]

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Pio Sienesi
