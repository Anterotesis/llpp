To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of Saunders Welch and Bartholomew Hammond Esqr. two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (one of them Being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 6th. day of August 1760. Elizabeth Carsey the Wife of Charles Carsey (who is a Soldier and gone abroad) and their two Children Viz William aged about 6 Years, and Ann aged about 12 Months were Removed from the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster, as the place of their last legal Settlement, Whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggreived, and against the said Order do Appeal.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray that this Worshipfull Court will be pleased to appoint a Day in this present Session for hearing and determining this Appeal, and that all Persons concerned may then and there attend.

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.

Saml. Peirson Wm. Telfe} Churchwardens of St. Margaret Westmr.

Titus Wilson Jno-Burford} Overseers of the Poor of the same Parish
