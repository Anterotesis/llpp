To the Worshipfull Wm. Mainwaring Esqr. the Chairman and the Best of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace held for the said County in the Month of February 1787. Assembled

The Petition of Henry Adams Humbly Sheweth,

That Your Petitioner is the Contractor for Conveying Vagrants in though and out of this County and hath been so over since April Session 1774

That at the General Session of the Peace held for this County in September 1785. your Petitioners applied to the Court and stated that the number of Vagrants Conveyed by him had so much increased that during the Year ending in April 1785 he had removed 4244 Vagrants by virtue of 3329 Passes which was 1038 Vagrants by 954 passes more than the number by him removed during the Year ending in April 1784 and in consequence of the Application the Court were Pleased to for the Extra Expence Trouble and attend brought upon him by the great Increase of Vagrants during that Period

That your Petition hath Conveyd 4002 Vargrants by 3164 passes between April Session 1785 and April Session 1786 which

is nearly Equal to the number removed by him the preceeding Year

Yours Petitioner therefore Humbly Prays your Worships to grant him such further Relief as in your Wisdom I Judgment shall Seem meet

And Your Petitioner will Ever pry Etc

Henery Adams
