Middlesex To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex in their [...] General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary le bone in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of James Larne and William Hyde Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex aforesaid (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 28th. day of November 1781 John Clavery now in a State of Insanity was removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Mary le bone as the place of his last legal Settlement

That your Petirs. conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order or pass warrant of the said two Justices Do therefore Appeal to your Worships there from and humbly pray that you hear them in the premises And that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square may Appear before you

at a certain day to be Appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of your Worships touching this Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.

Thos. Martin Solr. for the Appellants Howman Street
