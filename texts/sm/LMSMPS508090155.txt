To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Chobham in the County of Surrey

Sheweth That W: Bleamire and Thos. Gordon Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex upon Complaint made to them by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Stoke Newington in the County of Middlesex that Jane Gosdon Singlewoman had lately come to reside in their said parish not having obtained any legal Settlement there and was likely to become chargeable thereto did by an Order or Warrant of Removal under their Hands and Seals bearing date the Seventeenth day February last Order the said Jane Gosdon to be removed and conveyed from the said parish of Saint Mary Stoke Newington to the said parish of Chobham as the Place of her had legal Settlement

That the said Jane Gosdon by virtue of the said Order hath been Removed and conveyed from the said parish of Saint Mary Stoke Newington to the said parish of Chobham where who now remains chargeable

That your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved by the said Order

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said Order and pray that the said [---] Merjts of the said Appeal may be heard and such Order [...] [...] made thereon as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

J: Carter Soll: for the Appellants
