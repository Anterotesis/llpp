To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace in and for the said County Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwarden and Overseer of the Poor of the Parish of Bridenbury in the County of Hereford

Sheweth That by an Order of Removal under the hand and Seals of Paul Vaillant Esquire and Stephen Cole Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 23d. day of November 1784 John Lewis and his wife named Elizabeth and their four Children one Names John aged 8 years another named Thomas aged 6 years another named Elizabeth aged 4 years and the other named William aged 2 years were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Isleworth in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Bridenbury in the said County of Hereford by the description of the parish of Bambury in the said County of Hereford as the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved by the said Order and the Appeal against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that a Day may be appointed of this present Session to hear and Determine the Matter of the said Appeal and that all parties concerned may then Attend

And your Petitioners shall were Pray Etc

Lacock Solr. for Appellants
