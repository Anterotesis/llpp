To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace of the County of Middx in their general quarter Session of the peace Assembled

The humble petition and Appeal of Martin Marshall of the parish of Saint Giles in the feilds Baker

Sheweth

That your petitioner on the twelfth day of January 1760 was Convicted before John Fielding Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said County for that your petitioner on the Eleventh day of January instant At the parish aforesaid did sell one quarter Loaf of Bread the same then and there being of an Inferior quality to Wheaten Bread and at a higher price than Households then was set at by the Assize And the said Mr. Fielding did Adjudge your petitioner to pay and forfeit for the same the sum of twenty Shillings

That your petitioner thinks himself aggrieved by the said Judgement of the said Mr. Fielding the Justice before whom he hath been so Convicted And doth therefore Appeal to your Worships At this Court against the Same

[---] Martin Marshall
