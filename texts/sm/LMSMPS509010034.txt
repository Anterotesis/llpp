To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled at their General Sessions of the Peace holden for the said County on the Fifteenth day of September 1794

The Humble Petition of the Church wardens Overseers of the Poor and other Inhabitants of the Parish of St. Benedict in the Town of Cambridge

Sheweth unto your Worships that by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of Thomas Collins and John Scott two Justices of the Peace for the said County beaing Date the Sixteenth day of July 1794 Ann Marrington Wife of William Marrington and her two Children namely William aged Ten years and Robert Aged Seven Years were Removed from the Parish of Hornsey in the said County of Middlesex to St. Benedicts aforesaid.

That your Petitioners Verily believe that the legal Settlement of her the said Ann Marrington and her two Children is in the Parish of St. Mary le bone in the said County of Middlesex and not elsewhere

Your Petitioners therefore humbly hope that your Worships will permit them to enter their Appeal at this present Sessions against the said Order and that the hearing of such Appeal May be Respited until the next General Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County.

And your petitioners Shall ever pray Etc

Miller for the Petitioners
