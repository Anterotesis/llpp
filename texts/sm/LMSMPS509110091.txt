Middlesex to wit To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County at the Session House on Clerkenwell Green on the day of June 1795

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Nicholas Byerley of Holborn Hill in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the County of Middlesex Vender of Medicines

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the 30th. day of May last Convicted by John Floud Esqr. one of the Justices of the Peace for the said County in the Penalty of Five pounds [---] on an Information charging that your petitioner did on the twenty fourth day of March in the Year of our Lord 1795 at Holborn Hill aforesaid Utter be [...] and Sell in Great Britain to one George Hawkins one Box containing a certain preparation called Pectoral Lozanges from the Balsam of Toln at and for the price of one Shilling the said Preparation being used and Applied and to be used and applied as a Medicine or Medicament for the Relief of a certain Disorder and Complaint called [---] Cough and the same disorder and Complaint being a disorder and Complaint Incident to and Affecting the Human Body And the said preparation being subject to the Stamped Duty of one penny half penny by the said act Imposed, the said Box so containing the said preparation then and there not having any Cover Wrapper or Label directed by the said Act to be Pasted Stuck fastened and Affixed to the said Box and Marked and Stamped with the Stamp provided and used pursuant to the said Act by the Commissioners Appointed to Manage the duties charged on Stamped Vellom Parchment and Paper to denote the said duty of one penny half penny as by the said Act is directed Contrary to the form of the Statute in such Case made and provided

That your Petitioner conceives himself agrieved by such Conviction And is advised to Appeal against the same

Therefore Your Petitioner humbly Prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this General Quarter Sessions in and for the said County and that your Worships will appoint a Day for hearing and determining the same.

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc
