To the Worshipful Sir John Hawkins Knt and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex at the [...] General Sessions of the peace Assembled in the Month of December 1774

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Witney in the County of Oxford on behalf of themselves and the rest of the Inhabitants of the said parish

Sheweth. That by a warrant or order under the hands and Seals of William Gascoigne and John Barnfather Esqrs. two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the third day of November one thousand seven hundred and seventy four John Coney a Lunatic was removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Mary Le bone in the County of Middx to the said parish of witney as the last place of his legal Settlement

That the Petitioners apprehending themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or order of the said two Justices Conceiving it not to be the said John Coneys legal Settlement Do therefore appeal against the same and pray that your Worships will be pleased to order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the parish of Saint Mary le bone and all persons Concerned personally to attend the Court upon the hearing and determination of the Court touching the said Appeal & That your Petitioners may have such Relief in the premisses as your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
