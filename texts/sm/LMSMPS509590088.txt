To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and to the rest of the Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of William Gowers of Tanconberg Court in the Parish of St. Anne Westminster in the said County of Middlesex Clumney Sweeper

Sheweth That Your Petitioner and Appeallant in and by a certain Conviction in Writing under the Hand and Seal of [...] [...] Henry Winstead Esquire One of the Justices of our Sovereign Lord the King assigned to keep the Peace and also to hear and determine divers Felonies Trespasses and other Misdemeanors done and committed within the said County of Middlesex bearing date the Eighteenth day of February in the Year of our Lord One thousand seven hundred and ninety Nine was Convicted by the said Henry Winstead [...] For that he the said William Gowers being a Person exercising the business of a Chimney Sweeper did in the Morning of the [...] Sixteenth day of the said Month of February unlawfully came Thomas Brofey his Apprentice Boy to call the Streek before seven o'Clock in the Morning the same being [...] between Michaelmas and Lady-day against the Statute or And the said [...] Henry Winstead did adjudge your Petitioner and Appellant to have forfeited a Sum not exceeding ten Pounds nor less than five Pounds to wit, the Sum of Five Pounds to be distributed as the Low doth direct Whereby your Petitioner thinketh himself aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Appeals to this Court against the said Conviction And prays that your Worships will appoint a day in this Present Session for the Hearing and determining of this Your Petitioners Appeal

Wm. Gowers 2d. April 1799
