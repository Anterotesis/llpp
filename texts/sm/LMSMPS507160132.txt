To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Sessions of the peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Eltham in the County of Kent.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of John Sherwood and Jasper Clarke Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Sixteenth Day of July last past Mary Hall the Wife of William Hall who was then Absent from her at Sea and his place of Settlement unknown was removed from the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Eltham the said Justices thereby adjudging that to be the Place of her last Legal Settlement.

That you Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order and Judgement of the said Justices and do humbly appeal therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that your Worships would be pleased to appoint someday in this present Sessions to hear this their Appeal And to make an Order that the Churhwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate and all Parties concerned in and about the Premisses may then and there hear and abide by the Determination of our Worships therein

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Anthony Russell Churchwarden

Wm Pattenden W Simmans} Overseers
