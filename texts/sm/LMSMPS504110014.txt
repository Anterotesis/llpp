To the Worshipfull the Chairman and others his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx at this Present General Quarter Sessions at Hicks Hall assembled

The Humble Petition of Sarah Plumpton Widow, and Mother of Ann Plumpton, an Infant

Sheweth That your Petrs. Daughter Ann Plumpton an Infant aged about Eleven years was by Indenture of Apprenticeship bound out by the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middx, in the Year One Thousand Seven Hundred and Forty Nine to One William Sumner of St. Leonard Shoreditch in the said County upholesterer

That During the time the Child has been put an Apprentice She has been most Crully, Beat and Barbarously used, Half Starved and not allowed the common necessarys to Support Humane Nature, so that had it not been. for the Neighbours assissting and giving the Infant Charity, it must have Perished

That this Usage has been continually repeated, has been Beat Wounded and Treated in such a manner That when inquired into by your Worshipps, your Petr. humbly presumes you'l releive her

She therefore most humbly begs that you would be Pleased to Consider the Melancholy Scitution the Child is in and that unless your Worships, should be Pleased to make such Order in the Premisses, that the Child should be discharged from the Indre it must still Labour under the Greater Hardships. Humbly submitting to your Directions

And is in Duty bound to pray

Sarah Plumpton
