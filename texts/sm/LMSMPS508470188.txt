To the Right Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Mayor and Commonalty and Citizens of the City of London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a certain Rate or Assessment or Rates and Assessments made for the Relief of the Poor of the Hamlet of Hampton Wick in the County of Middlesex from Lady Day One thousand seven hundred and eighty seven to Lady Day One thousand seven hundred and eighty eight and also a Rate or Assessment or Rates and Assessment made for the Relief of the Poor of the said Hamlet from Lady Day One thousand seven hundred and eighty eight to Lady Day One thousand seven hundred and eighty nine a Claim was made or the Fifth Day of June One thousand seven hundred and eighty eight on your Petitioners by the Collectors of the Poors Rate for the said Hamlet of Hampton Wick in the Following Words "The "Mayor and Commonalty and Citizens of the City "of London Stand assessed to the Poor Rate of "the [...] Hamlet of Hampton Wick for the "Barge Way and Toll Gate for the two Rates of "One thousand seven hundred and eighty seven "Eleven Pounds four shillings and for one Rate for "the Year One thousand seven hundred and eighty "eight Six Pounds thirteen Shillings and Four "Pence

That on the Fifth Day of February last Past the said Collectors of the Poors Rate for the said Hamlet made a Claim on your Petitioners

for two years from Lady Day One thousand seven hundred and eighty seven to Lady Day One thousand Seven hundred and eighty nine including their said Former Demand

That your Petitioners Conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Rates or Assessments and the Demands made thereon in Regard to their not being liable to be Rated in Respect to the said Barge Way and Toll Gate do therefore appeal to your Worships therefore and humbly pray that You will be pleased to hear them in the Premisses and order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Hamlet of Hampton Wick may appear before You at a certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of Your Worships touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall pray and so forth

P W Crowther City Solr
