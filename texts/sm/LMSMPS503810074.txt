The King on the Information of James Winter Against. Leonard Lee.

To the Worshipful Thomas Lane Esquire & the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden by Adjournment on Monday the twelfth day of October One thousand Seven hundred and forty Seven at Hicks's Hall in and for the County of Middlesex.

The humble Petition & Appeal of Leonard Lee.

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the Twentififth day of July last upon the Information of James Winter Convicted before Henry Broadhead Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of selling or exposing some Cloths to Sale Contrary to two Acts of Parliament the One Made in the fourth and the Other made in the Seventh Year of his late Majesty King George the first and Adjudged to a forfeited Certain Penalty Mentioned in the said Conviction

That your Petitioner Apprehends and is Advised that the said Conviction is illegal & wants form

May it Please your Worships to Re-hear the said Cause and to grant to your Petitioner Such relief as in your Judgment Shall seem Adviseable

And Your Petitioner Shall ever pray.

L Lee
