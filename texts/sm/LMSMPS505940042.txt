To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall assembled

The humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of he Parish of Shefford in the County of Berks

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of William Tinbrell and Wm. Mantle Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) being date the 19th. Day of January last Hannah Plumber with her two Children William Aged One year and an half and Jonathan Aged about three months were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Shefford as the [---] place then last legal Settlement Whereby your Parishioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the same and pray that this Court will appoint a day in this present Session for the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square and all persons concerned to Attend the Court to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of this Court touching the said Appeal and your Petitioners shall ever prayer

Geo. Jones Sol for the Parish of Shefford

Middlesex At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the said County by adjournmt. on Tuesday the found day of April in the ninth year of the Reign of on Sovereign Lord George this King of great Britain Etc It is Ordered that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square have Notice of this appeal and that they and all persons concerned do Attend this Court on Thursday next at nine of the Clock in the forenoon of the same day to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of the Court touching the said appeal

By the Court Waller
