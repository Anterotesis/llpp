To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth

That by virtue of an Order of Pass Warrant under the Hanes and Seals of Thomas Lockwood and R. P. Jodrell, Esquires, Two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 16th day of May 1794 John Danells, Ann his Wife and their Two Children namely James aged Six Years and Ann aged Five Years were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Marylebone in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said Two Justices and their said Order and pray that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day to hear and determine the same

And Your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc

Thos: Wallis P Richardson Willm: Morris Thos. Cocking Edwd. Hursley Thos Gleaves Jno. Dutton Robt. Frisby Josh. Smeeton Ferdinand Ballhorn Joseph Whitaker} Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor
