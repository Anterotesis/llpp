To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at the General [---] Session of the peace held at Hicks hall in the County aforesaid assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Margaret Pattons in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of John Upton Elz and William Timbrell Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westmr in the said County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) hearing date the 19th day of January last a poor Woman and Wiltewale Child by the names and descriptions of Mary Nightingale (wife of Wm. Nightingale gone from her) and Mary their child aged four Months were removed from the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the said County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint Margaret Pattens as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be agrieved

Therefore they humbly appeal to your Worship's against the Judgemt. of the said two Justices and pray your worship's will be pleased to appoint some day this present Sessions to hear and determine the matter touching the said appeal

and your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Allam Solicitor for the Appellants
