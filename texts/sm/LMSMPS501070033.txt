To the Worshipfull the Justices of the peace for yr. County of Middlesex in Sessions at Hicks Hall

The humble Petition of Ann Elmes Wife of Henry Elmes

Sheweth

That yor. Petr. sd. Husband hath lately turn'd yor. Petr. out of Doores, and taken in one Mary Wife, with whome he lives Incontinently, having had 2 Children by her, And doth not allow your Petr. any Maintenance so yt. She is fored to lives in service.

That yor. Petr. some Short time agoe going to her sd Husband House (being the Punch House in Henclock) Court he together with the sd. Mary Wife assaulted and beat yor. Petr. in a Barbarous manner, he denying yor. Petr. to be his Wife, & hath caused her to be taken up by the Informers, [---] prtending her to be Lewd Woman, which is utterly false she being new and having for ye. Space of almost 6 yeares last past beene in Service in One place, and from wch. service her sd. Husband marry'd her

Yor. Petr. therefore humbly prays yor. Worships to compell her sd. Husband to allow her some Cornpetent Maintenance and that the sd Mary Wife may be dealt with according to Law

And yor. Petr. shall ever pray Etc
