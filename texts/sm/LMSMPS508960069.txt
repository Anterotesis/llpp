To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green in the Month of April 1794

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Winter of Ford Hook house in the Parish of Ealing in the County of Middlesex aforesaid Esquire

Sheweth That your Petitioner reason or about the Thirty first day of March last convicted by Nathaniel Bland Esquire one of the Justices of the Peace for the said County upon on Information exhibited and prosecuted by George Tilley of Old Brentford in the Parish of Ealing in the County of Middlesex aforesaid Yeoman charging that your Petitioner did on the Twentyninth day March 1794 unlawfully use of cause to be used alert upon a certain Tirnpike Road at Old Brentford in the Parish of Ealing in the County of Middlesex aforesaid the Sole or Bottom of the Fellies of the Whools of which said Cart being of Sess Breadth or Guage than six Inches and then and there did pass or was drawn with more than three Houses (that is to say) with four Horses and was adjudged to forfeit for the said offence the Sum of Five Pounds Whereby your Petitioner conceives himself to be aggrieved

your Petitioner therefore humbly Appeals against the adjudication of the said Justice And prays that your Worships will be pleased to Order that the benefit of this his Appeal may be saved unto him & that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned Until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for this County

And Your Petitioner Etc

John Winter
