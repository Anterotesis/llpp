To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Sessions of the Peace assaulted

The Humble Petition and appeal of John Sampson of the Parish of Christ Church in the said County Seller of Bread

Sheweth That your Petitioner was convicted on the fifteenth day of July one thousand seven hundred and ninety six by the Recerend Henry Reynett D. D. one of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County on the complaint of Richard Osman for having unlawfully exposed to Sale on the twelfth day of July last nine Quarter of a Peck leaves of Wheaten bread deficient in weight Ten three penny loaves of Wheaten bread deficient in weight One two penny loaf of Wheaten bread deficient in weight and three three penny leaves of Wheaten bread deficient in weight according to the Assize set for such bread and your Petitioner was adjudged to pay and forfeit the Sum of Seven Pounds ten Shillings for the same

That your Petitioner conceives himself to be aggrievd by the said Conviction and humbly appeals against the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships to appoint day to hear and determine the said appeal

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc
