To the Worshipfull His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Quarter Sessions Held at the Sessions House One Clerkenwell Green in and for the said County on the month [---] January 1787

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Thomas Gurney of Pinner in the County of Middlesex Chandler and Maker of Candles for sale

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on the 28th. day of December last Convicted before Henry Lambe and Thomas Fellows Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and forth County of Middlesex on the Information of one Mile Rogers in a Penalty of One Hundred Pounds Alledging that your Petitioner had frauduling removed Candles from the Place of making the same before they had been Weighed by the Surveying Officer

That Your Petitioner conceives himself Aggreived by the said Conviction and is Advised that the Ought not to have been Convicted

Therefore your Petitioner most humbly Prays your Worships will be Pleased to Permit him to Enter his Appeal to the said Conviction

And your Petitioner will ever Pray Etc.

Thos. Gurney
