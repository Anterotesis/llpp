To his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session assembled.

The humble petition of Henry Harrison of the Pish of St. Giles in the Fields in the County of Middlex

Sheweth That yor. Peticoner thirteen Years ago by Indenture was by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Giles putt in Apprentice to John Kent of the said parish Chimney Sweeper to serve from thence hit your Peticoner shod. attain to the Age of Twenty four Years

That your Peticoner hath faithfully served the said Kent to this time & behaved & demeaned himself in all things as an hour st Apprentice But the said Kent hath not allowed to your Peticoner sufficient Meat Drink Washing Lodging & Apparell as by his Agreemt. he is obliged to do and hath several times beat yor. Peticoner very immoderately without any just Occasion

Therefore your Peticoner humbly prays that for the Causes aforesaid he may be discharged from his said Apprenticeship and have such other Relief as to this Court shall seem meet

And your Peticoner shall ever pray Etc.

Henry Harrison
