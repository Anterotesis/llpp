To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled in their- Generall Sessions of the Peace

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Samuel Boydon of the parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex Victualler

Sheweth

That Your Petitioner by virtue of a Warrant under the Hand and Seal of the Worshipful John Spencer Colepeper Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex was Convened before the said John Spencer Colepeper Esquire and was convicted (as he apprehends) of Knowingly suffering and Permitting one Thomas Salmon and one Charles Walker to play at a certain Game called Skittles in his skittle Ground in the parish aforesaid against the form of the Statute in that behalf

That Your Petitioner humbly conceiving himself aggrieved by the Judgement and Determination of the said Justice doth Appeal to this Honorable Court from the same and prays such relief in the premisses as to your Worships shall seem meet

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays that all parties concerned may be ordered to attend this Honorable Court to hear and abide the Determination of your Worships in the premisses

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc

Wm. Watts Sollr. for the Appellant.

Middx At the General Sessions of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks's Hall in Saint John street in the County aforesaid on Monday the thirteenth day of September in the second Year of the reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third King of Great Britain France and Ireland Defender of the faith as

It is ordered by this Court that all Parties concerned have notice of this Appeal had that they do attend this Court on [---] Friday next at nine of the Clock in the forenoon to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

By the Court Waller
