To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their general Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Parish of Cheddington in the County of Bucks

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of George Stubbs and W. Kelynge Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of Liberty of Westminster (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 24th. day of May 1773. Ann Shillingford the Widow of William Shillingford deceased with her four Children by her said late Husband, Namely Thomas aged about eighteen Years Jane aged about thirteen Years Mary aged about nine Years and William Aged near four Years were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint Margaret in [...] said City of Westminster and County of Middlsex to the said Parish of Cheddington in the said County of Bucks as the last legal Settlement of the said Ann Shillingford and of her said four Children whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved and against the said Order do appeal

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray That a day may be appointed in the present Session to hear and determine the Matter touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray
