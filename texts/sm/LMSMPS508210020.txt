Middlesex To the Worshipfull William Mainwaring Esquire Chairman and the rest of his Majesty's Justices in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal & Michael Stevens of Stoke Newington

Sheweth That by Virtue of and under a Conviction of Humphrey Jackson Esquire One of the Justices Assigned to keep the peace in the County of Middlesex bearing date on the Thirteenth Day of December last past your Appellant was convicted in a Penalty of Fifty Shillings for that he on the Fifteenth day of November then last past did utter vend and Expose to sale and in Consideration of Seven pence sell unto one William Blogg one pott containing soft pomatum without causing the words"Licensed to deal in Perfumery to be painted or written in large or legible characters either over his Door or in any Visible place in the front of his House Shop or Warehouse contrary to the Form of the Statute in that Case made and provided

By which Conviction your Appellant Conceives herself aggrieved and humbly appeals therefrom

Your Appellant therefore prays your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day for the hearing and Determining thereof

And your appellant will ever pray
