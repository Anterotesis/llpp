To the Right Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Alhallow's Lombard Street in the City of London

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Patrick Colquhoun and Rupert Clarke Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quarum) bearing date the twenty sixth Day of March last 1795 Thomas Collison Ann his Wife and their Seven Children lawful issue Namely Isabella Aged fourteen Years Mary aged twelve Yearann aged Eleven years Sarah aged ten years William aged Seven years Joseph aged four Years and Maria aged Eighteen Months were removed from the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Alhallows Lombard Street London as the last legal Place of their Settlement by which your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and therefore appeal to your Worships against the said Order or Pass Warrant

J: Thorias Solr for the Appellants

Your Petitioners therefore pray relief on the Premises and [...] your Workships will be Pleased to appoint a day in this present Sessions for the hearing and determining of this their Appeal.
