To the Right Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a Certain Pass of Order under the Hands and Seals of Christopher Scott and Walter Berry Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex Bearing Date the Eighth Day of April one thousand Seven Hundred and Sixty two one Mary Rice /mentioned in the said Pass to be the Wife of William Rice who is now abroad in foreign Parts/ and two Illegitimate Children born of the Body of the said Mary Rice to witt Abraham Wilkins aged about two. Years and a Quarter and Henry Wilkins aged about four Months were Removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the said County of Middlesex as Likely to become Chargeable to the same to the said Parish of Saint George in the said County of Middlesex as the Place of the Last Legal Settlement of the said William Rice and his said Wife

Your Petitioners Concerning themselves Aggrieved by the said Pass of the said Justices of the Peace Humbly Appeal to this Court against the same And Pray that the Court will Appoint a Day this Present Session for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate aforesaid and all other Persons Concerned to attend this Court to hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners Shall Ever Pray Etc.
