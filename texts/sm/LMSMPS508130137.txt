To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County assembled

The Humbled Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of that part of the Parish of St. Andrew Holborn which his above the Bars in the County aforesaid

Sheweth That by Virtue of and Order under he Hands and Seals of N. Forster and John Collick Esquires Two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof is of the Quorum) bearing Date the Ninth Day of August Instant Robert Bridger was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of St. martin in the fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County to the said Parish of St. Andrew Holborn above the bars as the place of his last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners humbly conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to [...] against the a [...] of the said Justices and their said Order And pray that your Worships will please to appoint some Day in this present Session for the hearing and Determining the said Appeal and to Order that all Persons concerned Do then attend.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
