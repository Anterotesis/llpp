The Peticon of Ann Miller

To the Worshipfull Bench of Justice

The humble Peticon of Ann Miller of ye Parish of St. John at Hackney Widow Etc

Humbly Sheweth

That whereas ye Peticoner for many Years last past have lived in great Credit and have pd. all Taxes in ye Sd. Parish for many Years last past. But now through great Losses by Thieves (they having Robbed yr Peticoner of all her Linnen and Woollen so yt yr Peticoner had not any thing to Shift her wth: all) and other great misfortunes is now brought to great Poverty in so much yt Your Peticoner hath not a Bed to lye on nor all last Winter but bye on Straw so yt yr Petioconer through hard ship have gotten a great Lameness in her Limbs and yr Peticoner being of a great Age am ways able to get any thing towards my Maintenance your Peticoner therefore humbly Traves yt Your Worships will take it in Consideration her deplorable Condition and Order yr Peticoner speedy relief from Ye Sd Parish

Sept ye 4th 1716

And yr Peticoner as in Duty bound shall ever Pray Etc
