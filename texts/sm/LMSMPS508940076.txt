To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their general Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and appeal of the Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Bishopstone in the County of Wilts.

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of W. Bleamire and Rupt. Clarke Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the sixteenth day of January 1794 Mary Whiting and Henry Whiting aged about five years and Mary Whiting aged about eight Months were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Bishopston in the said County of Wilts as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Whiting Henry Whiting and Mary Whiting whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggreived

Your Petitioners therefore appeal against the said Order and humbly Pray relief in the Premises and that your worships will be pleased to appoint some day in the next Sessions for the hearing and determining this their said appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Ben Price Solr for the appellts.
