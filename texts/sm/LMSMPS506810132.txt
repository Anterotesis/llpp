To the Worshipful the Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General [---] Session of the Peace for the said County assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Overseers of the Liberty of the Rolls in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order Pess Warrant under the Hands and Seals of George Alleged and Richard Aulse Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County (one whreof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 25th. Day of May 1778 Ann Phillips Wife of Thomas Phillips was removed and conveyed by the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary le bone in the County of Middlesex aforesaid from and out of their said Parish of Saint Mary le bone to the said Liberty of the Rolls as the Place of her last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners concerning themselves to be aggrived by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly appeal against the same to your Worships and pray relief in the Premises And that a Day in this present Session may be appointed for the hearing and determining the said Appeal.
