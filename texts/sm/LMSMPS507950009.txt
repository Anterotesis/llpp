To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of Peter Lamb of the Parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green in the County of Middlesex Victualler.

Sheweth That by a certain Order and Conviction under the Hands and Seals of Robert Smith and Samuel Hawley Esquires, two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace, in and for the said County of Middlesex, bearing date the fourth day of December in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord King George the third and in the year One thousand seven hundred and Eighty four, your Petitionerer's convicted, of Knowingly suffering Journeymen Labourers Servants or Apprentices, to Game with Cards in his House And your Petitioner is by the aforesaid Order or Conviction of the said Justices, adjudged to have thereby forfeited the Sum of Forty Shillings.

Your Petitioner conceiving himself aggrieved by the said order and Conviction of the said Justices, humbly appeals against the same and prays that the same may be quashed, and that your Worships will give your Petitioner such other Relief in the Premises as your Worships shall think fit

And your Petitioner shall ever pray etc.
