To William Mainwaring Esquire and other His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of Henry Saunders

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on the seventh Day of November last Convicted by Edward Parker Esquire one of the Justices of the County of Middlesex on the Information and Oath of Dominick Adlain and one Mary Martin in the Sum of Twenty Pounds for a supposed offence of Selling a Pair of Gloves with a Stamp of less Denomination than by the Statute in that Case Made and Provided is Directed

That Your Petitioner humbly States he has a good and Substantial Defence to the said Charge and is Advised that such Conviction was Unlawfully made as he was Convicted Party upon the Testimony of the Informer upon Record who was Intrested therein and viewed his Expectation of a Part of the Penalty

Your Petitioner therefore Conceiveing himself Aggrieved by the Judgment of the sd Justice Humbley begs that Your Worships will be Pleased to appoint a Day to hear Try and Determind the Merits of the said Conviction and that the Same may be quashed or Unquashed

And Your Petitioner as in Duty bound shall Ever Pray Etc

Henry Saunders
