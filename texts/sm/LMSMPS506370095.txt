To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace holden at Hicks's Hall in and for the County of Middlesex.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Carshalton in the County of Surrey

Sheweth That David Wilmot and Charles Digby Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex aforesaid upon Complaint made to them by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green in the County of Middlesex that Elizabeth Whitbourne Wife of Francis Whitebourne who was gone away and left her quite Destitute and unprovided for had come to Inhabit in the said Parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green not having gained a legal Settlement there nor produced any Certificate owning her to be Settled elsewhere and was become chargeable to the same Did by an Order or Warrant of Removal under their Hands and Seals bearing Date the Thirteenth Day of December last Order the said Elizabeth Whitbourne to be removed and conveyed from and out of the said Parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green to the said Parish of Carshalton as the Place of her last legal Settlement.

That the said Elizabeth Whitbourne by virtue of the said Order hath been removed and conveyed from the said Parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green to the said Parish of Carshalton where she now remains Chargeable to the same Parish

Your Petitioners Apprehending themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Warrant of Removal made by the said two Justices as aforesaid do therefore humbly Appeal against the same

John Brewster Sollicitor for the Appellants 6th January 1774.
