To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of William Windsor of High Street in the Parish of Saint Mary Whitechaple in the County of Middlesex Pawnbroker

Sheweth That by a certain Conviction under the Hand and Seal of William Hyde Esquire one of the Justices our Lord the King Assigned to Keep the Peace of our Said Lord the King in and for the County of Middlesex and also to hear and determine divers Felonies Trespasses and other Misdemeanours Commitee within the Said County bearing Date the Twenty Sixth Day of August last

Your Petitioner and Appellant was Convicted by the said Justice for that he then using and exercising the Trade and Business of a Pawnbroker in High Street aforesaid on the Second Day of August Last Past take did and receive of and from one George Robert Manderville by Way of Pawn or Pledge a Dollar and French Crown and did them and there advance and lend thereon to the Said George Robert Manderville a certain Sum of money exceeding five Shillings to wit the Sum of Seven Shillings of Lawful Money of Great Britain Yet that your Petitioner and Appellant being such Pawnbroker as aforesaid did not forthwith and before he advanced and Lent the said Sum of money upon the aforesaid pawn or Pledge enter

or Cause to be Entered in a fair or regular manner in any Book or Books kept by Your Petitioner for that Purpose a Description of the Said Dollar and French Gown which your Petitioner so received in Pawn or Pledge and also the Sum of money to be advanced and Lent thereon with the Day of the Month and Year and the Name and Place of Abode of the Person by Whom the Same was so Pawned or Pledged and the Name and Place of Abode of the Owner thereof according to the Information of the Person Pawning or Pledging the Same according to the Statute in such Case made and Provided bit that Your Petitioner and, Appellant then and there wholly neglected to make Such Entry as aforesaid Contrary to the Statute Ct

And that your Petitioner and Appellant was by the said Justice adjudged to Day and forfeit the Penalty of Ten Pounds for the said supposed Offence whereby your Petitioner conceives himself Aggrived

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Appeals to this Court against the said Conviction and Prays that Your Worships will Appoint a Day in this Present General Quarter Session of the Peace for the hearing and Determining of the Same. And your Petitioner will ever Pray Etc.

John Brem Sollr. for the Appollt.
