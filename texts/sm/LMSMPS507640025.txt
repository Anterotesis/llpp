To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St Botolph without Aldgate in the County aforesd.

Sheweth That by virtue of a warrant or order under the hands & Seals of J. Penleaze & Rice Davies Esqrs. two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex one being of the Quorms bearing Date the twelfth day of Febry instant Ann Glover widow of Charles Glover & her three Children viz. Ann Aged about Five Years Charles Aged about two Years & Martha Aged about four Months were removed from the Parish of St. John Wapping in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of St Botolph without Aldgate as the Place of their last legal Settlement.

That our Petitionrs Conceivg themselves Aggrieved by the sd Warrant or order do humbly Appeal against the Same & Pray that your Worships will be Pleased to Appoint a Day this Present Session that all Parties Concerned may attend to hear & Abide the Judgment & determination of this Court- touching the Said Appeal And your Petitionrs & Shall ever Pray Etc

James Lare & J Bampfield} Sollr for the appellants

Middlesex. At the General Session of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holden for the County of Middx at the Session house in the County aforesd. by Adjournment on Monday the twenty fourth day of February in the twenty [...] Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third King of Great Britain France & Ireland Defenier of the Faith Etc

It is ordered by this Court That the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St John Wapping in the County of Middlesex have Notice of this Appeal & that they & all Persons Concerned do attend this Court on Thursday next at nine of the Clock in the Forenoon to hear & abide the Judgment & determination of this Court touching the Said Appeal

By the Court Selby
