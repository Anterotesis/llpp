Middlesex. ss To his Majesty's Justices of the Peace now in there generall Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Church-Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Somerset London.

Sheweth: That Sarah Ashton Single woman was by Order or Pass Warrl [...] Dated the fifth Day of September last past under the Hands and Seals of Richard Farmer Esquire and Clifford William Phillipps Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (One being of the Quorum) removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapple in the said County unto the said Parish of Saint Mary Somersett London as the place of the legal Settlement of the sd. Sarah Ashton.

That Your Petitioners humbly Conceiving themselves aggreived thereby, appeal to this honourable Court agt the sd Order or pass wart for such Releife in the Premisses as this Court shall think proper.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

John Richardson Solicitor for the said Parish of St. Mary somersett Londen
