To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green on the Second day of July 1798.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Wilson

Sheweth That in consequence of a Summons issued under the hand and Seal of Cranley Thomas Kerby Esquire one of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County and served on your Petitioner he appeared before the Magistrates at the Public Office in Queen Square Westminster Upon an Information exhibited and prosecuted by William Kettle charging that your Petitioner did at the Parish of Saint Mary le bone in the said County on the [---] Thirteeth day of May last take and receive of Martha Bunting in the Name of Martha Sampson on redeeming the Pledge therein mentioned one Penny by way of Profit upon Three Shillings on the Twenty eight day of May aforesaid lent upon a pledge of Two Handkerchiefs contrary to the Statute Etc And your Petitioner was upon the hearing of the said Information convicted in the Penalty of Five Pounds.

That Your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by such Conviction and is advised to appeal against the same.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this present Quarter Session of the peace in and for the said County and that your Worships will appoint a day for hearing and

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
