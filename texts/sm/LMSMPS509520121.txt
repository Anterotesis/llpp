To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in general Quarter Sessions of the Peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Goff of the Parish of Saint Mary Matfellon otherwise Whitechapel in the County of Middlesex Pawnbroker

Sheweth, That by a certain Conviction under the Hand and Seals of Rice Davies Esquire and Daniel Williams Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the Eleventh day of June One Thousand seven hundred and Ninety Eight Your Petitioner was Convicted as therein alledged for that on the fourth day of June then Instant at the Parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel in the County aforesaid Your Petitioner who their and there used and Exercised the Trade and business of a Pawnbroker did then and there demand receive and take of and from one Mary Pais in the name of Mary Brown on her applying and offering to redeem certain Goods and Chattels to wit one Bed Quilt pawned or Pledged with your Petitioner the Sum of one Penny of Lawful Money of Great Britain as and for and by way of Profit upon and over and above the Principal Sum of Three Shillings of like Lawful Money which had been within one Calendar Mouth to wit on the said fourth day of the said Month of June at and in the Parish aforesaid in the County aforesaid tent and advanced by your Petitioner upon the Pawn or Pledge abovementioned to the said Mary Pais in the name of Mary Brown the said sum of three Shillings being an intermediate Sum Exceeding the Sum of two Shillings and six pence and not exceeding the Sum of Forty Shillings and the said Sum of One Penny so demand received and taken by your Petitioner as and for and by way of Profit as aforesaid as therein alledged being more and at and after a greater and higher rate than the rate of Four Pence for the loan of Twenty Shillings by the Calendar Month

Month including the Current Month in which the said Pawn or Pledge was redeemed Contrary to the four of the Statute in such Case made and provided And the said Justices did adjudge your Petitioner to pay and forfeit for the same the Sum of Five Pounds of Lawful Money of Great Britain

That your Petitioner thinks himself aggreived by the Judgment of the said Justices And therefore

Your Petitioner doth appeal to your worships at this present General Quarter Sessions of the Peace against the Judgment of the said Justices and doth pray such Relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall Seem meet

John Goff

B. Bishop. Solr. for the Appellant No 2 Middle Temple Lane

Middlesex At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at the Sessions House on Clerkenwell Green in the said County on Monday the Second day of July in the Thirty Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third King of Great Britain Etc.

It is Ordered that Rice Davies Esquire and Daniel Williams Esquire have Notice of this Appeal and that they and all persons concerned do attend this Court at the Session House aforesaid on Thursday the fifth day of this Instant July at nine o Clock in the forenoon of the same Day to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal
