To the Right Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of John Milner and John Mercer Esqrs. two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said County (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Eighteenth day of June 1734. John Watson and Anne his Wife were removed from the parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said John Watson & Anne his Wife

That your Petitoners Conceive themselves Aggreived by the said Warrant or Order and humbly Appeal against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray that your Worships will be pleased to Appoint a day of this present Sessions that all parties may Attend to hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court for and touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners (as in duty bound) shall ever Pray
