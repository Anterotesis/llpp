April Sessions 1797} To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of the Justices for the County of Middlesex in Session assembled

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order under the hands of Nathaniel Conant and John Scott Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 14th day of February 1797 William Brunsdon & Bett his Wife were removed & conveyed from and out of the Parish of Kensington within the said County to the said Hamlet of Hammersmith as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioner conceive themselves aggrieved and beg leave to Appeal therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore Pray that they may be at liberty to enter their appeal & that the same may be Adjourned to the next Session for the hearing thereof
