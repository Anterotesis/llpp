To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Mitcham in the County of Surry

Sheweth That by Virtue of a certain Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Nicholas Bond and Rupert Clarke Esquires Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex and Liberty of Westminster (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty Sixth Day of September one thousand Seven Hundred and Ninety Seven Elizabeth Beadle Wife of Henry Beadle who had deserted her was removed and Conveyed from and out of the parish of St. Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex and Liberty of Westminster and delivered to your petitioners being the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Mitcham in the County of Surry the said Justices adjudging the last legal Settlement of the said Elizabeth to be in the said Parish of Mitcham whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Agreived

R. Fisher Solicitor for the Appellants Staining Lane And therefore your Petitioners humbly Appeal against the said Order or Pass Warrant and pray that the hearing and determining of the said Appeal may be Adjourned till the next General Session of the peace to be holden for the said County
