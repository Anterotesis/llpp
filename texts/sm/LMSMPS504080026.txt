To the Right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden for the said County at Hicks's Hall Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of Richmond in the County of Surrey

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of John Upton and Francis Burton Esqrs. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace (One of them being of the Quorum) for the Liberty of Westminster bearing date the Second Day of January 1750 Richard Cooper Singlewoman was Removed and conveyed form the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westmr, to Richmond afore as the Place of his last legal Settlement. Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved.

Therefore they Humbly Appeal to your Worships against the said and Judgment of the Said Two Justices of the Peace And pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto your Petitioners And that your Worships will be Pleased to Appoint some Day in this Sessions for the hearing and Determining the said Appeal. And Your Petitioners. Shall ever Pray Etc.

Wm. Clerk agent for Richmond
