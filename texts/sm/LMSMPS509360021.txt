To the Right Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George Southwark in the County of Surrey

Sheweth

That by virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of W. Addington and I. Robinson Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster (one whereof being of the Quourum) bearing date the Tenth day of April instant Donald Macdonell (who is therein stated to be by Lunacy so far disordered in his Senses that it may be dangerous for him to be permitted to go abroad") was removed from the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint George Southwark in the said County of Surrey alledging the said Parish of Saint George Southwark to be the place of his last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices Do therefore appeal to your worships there from and Humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the Premises And order thta the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields may appear before you at a certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of your Worships touching the Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Town Shend & Russell Southwark Sollrs. for the appellants
