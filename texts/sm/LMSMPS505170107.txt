To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace holden for the Said County at Hicks Hall Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Acton in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order Under the Hands and Seals of Philip Dyot and Henry Lambe Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the day of December in the Year of Our Lord One thousand Seven hundred and Sixty two Ann Smith Aged about nine Year And William Smith Aged about Six years in the said Order said To be the Children of the One William Smith were removed from the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Acton- as the last legal place of Settlement of the Said Father of the said [...] Ann Smith and William Smith

That Your Ptitioners Apprehend themselves Aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or Order And the Judgment of the said two Justices and therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that this Court will be pleased to make an Order for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said [...] Hamlett of Hammersmith and all Parties Concerned to Attend this Court on Thursday the ninth day of December Instant to near and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners will ever pray Etc

Mr Clarke Sollr for Appellants
