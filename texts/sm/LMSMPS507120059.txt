To the Right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrants under the Hards and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex one whereof being of the Quorum bearing Date the twenty eighth Day of April last Sarah Cressey aged four years or thereabouts, and Thomas Cressey aged one year and a half or thereabouts, the Case Children born of the Body of Fanny Cressey within the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldergate were removed from the [...] Parish of Saint [...] [...] John at Hackney in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldergate, as the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order of the said two Justices, humbly beg leave to appeal to this Honourable Court against the same

And pray that this Honourable Court will please to take their Case into Consideration, and appoint Day for the hearing and determining the same Appeal and to make such Order therein as shall be just

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.

Robt: Wooder Sol: for the Appellants.
