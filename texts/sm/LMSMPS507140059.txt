To Sir John Hawkins Knight Chairman and the rest of the Worshipful Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in their general Quarter Session assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Peter in the City of Chester.

Sheweth That by a certain Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of William Timbrell and Charles Marsh her of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing late the 9th. Day of June last Francis Froggatt with his two Children to wit Francis aged about six Years and Ann aged about three Years were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Peter in the City of Chester as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved.

Your Petitioners there fore humbly appeal to this Court against the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices and pray that your Worships will appoint some day in the next general Session of the Peace to be holden for this County for hearing and determining the sd. Appeal.

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc. S. Brown (for Ryder) Agent for the Appellants.
