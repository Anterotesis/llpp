To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Robert Johnson of Whitecross Street in the County of Middlesex Pawnbroker

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the sixth day of February instn. convicted in the Penalty of Five Pounds by Williams Esquire one of the Justices of the Peace for the said County upon an information Exhibited and prosecuted by James Bradley Pegler - charging that your Petitioner did in white cross Street in the said County on the Eighteenth day of September lake and receive of Patrick Russell on bending and advancing the Sum of thirteen Shillings on the Pledge hereinafter mentioned the Sum of Two pence as and for the price and value of a note or memorandum delivered for the said Pledge to wit a Black Filk Clock [---] contrary to the Statute &cc

That your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by such conviction and is advised to appeal against the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this present general Sessions of peace in and for the said County and that your worships will appoint a day for hearing and determining the same.

And your Petitioner shall ever pray &c
