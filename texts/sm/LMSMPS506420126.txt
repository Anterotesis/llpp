To the Worshipful Sir John Hawkins Knt Chairman and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in their Gent Quarter Session of the peace at Hicks Hall Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of Charles Cameron of Green Street in the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the County of Middlesex Architect

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order of Adjudication under the Hands and Seals of Six John Fielding Knight and Thomas Miller Esqr. two of his Majestices Justices of the peace for the County of Middx bearing date the 24th day of May 1774 your Petitioner was ordered to pay to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Kensington in the said County or to Some or one of them the Sum of one Pound and Ten Shillings towards the Maintenance of a Male Bastard Child born on the body of one Mary Ware to the time of making this Order And did also further order that your said Petitioner she likewise pay or Cause to be Paid to the said Churchwardens and Overseers or Some of them for the time being the Sum of three Shillings weekly and every week from the true of making the said Order for and towards the keeping Sustentati [...] and Maintenance of the said Bastard Child for and during so long time as the said Bastard Child shod be Chargeable to the said Parish

That your Petitioner went Abroad Some time in April 1768 & returned to England in Septr 1769 and did not See the said Mary Ware untill the Latter End of the said Month of Septr And She being but to Bed of the sd Child on the 28th of [...] April following as Appears by the said Order of Adjudication

Your Petitioner humbly apprehends that he as not the Father of the said Bastard Child being no more than Seven Months from the time of his first knowing her to the time of the Delivery

Your Petitioner Therefore Humbly Prays Your worships to take his Case into your Considert [...] and appoint a day for the hearing and Determining this Petition and appeal

And Your Petitioner as in Duty bound will every pray Etc

Capstacks & Cameron Sol for the Appellant
