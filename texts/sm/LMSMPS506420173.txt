To the Worshipful Sir John Hawkins Knight, Chairman, and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition of Samuel Drake.

Humbly Sheweth That by an Act passed in the last Session of Parliament, For the further and better Regulation of Buildings and party Walls, and for the more effectually preventing Mischiefs by Fire within the Cities of London and Westminster, and other Places in the said Act mentioned certain Persons skilled in the Art of Building are to be Nominated and Appointed Surveyors, to See the Rules and Regulations in the said Act mentioned well and truly observed.

That Your Petitioner Served his Clerkship with, and has been for 24 Years past in the Service of James Paine of Salisbury Street Esqr, Architect, during which time he has been conversant in the Several Branches of Building

That your Petitioner humbly presumes himself Qualified for undertaking and performing the intended Employment of a Surveyor under the said Act.

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays Your Worships will be pleased to Nominate and Appoint him one of the Surveyors to see the Rules and Regulations in the Said Act mentioned wall and truly observed within such District of the County as Your Worships may be pleased to allot to him.

And Your Worships Petitioner Shall ever Pray & Etc. Saml Drake
