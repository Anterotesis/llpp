To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in the General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of William Sims of the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the County of Middlesex Builder

Sheweth

That your Petitioner lately erected four Houses in Long Alley in the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the County of Middlesex aforesaid and your Petitioner humbly apprehends that he hath in all respects in erecting the said four houses Conformed himself to the Laws now in force respecting the Regulations of buildings and in particular to the Act of Parliament made at Westminster in the County aforesaid in the twelfth Year of the reign of our Sovereign Lord George the King now King of Great Britain Etc Intilued An Act for the better Regulation of buildings and Party Walls within the Cities of London and Westminster and the Liberties thereof and other the Parishes Precincts and Places in the weekly Bills of Mortality the Parishes of Saint Mary le Bone and Paddington Saint Pancras and Saint Luke at Chelsea in the County of Middlesex and for the better [...] Presenting of Mischiefs by pre within the said Cities Liberties Parishes Precinct and places and for amending and reducing the laws relating thereto into one Act and for other purposes

That one William Payne on the eighth Day of May last went before Thomas Kynaston Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County- and gave the said Justice information that your Petitioner being owner of the said houses did erect and fix on the First Day of May aforesaid certain Projections of Wood called Cornices to the Bres summer Fronts of the said Houses and that Your Petitioner did not convey and cause to be conveyed the Water from the tops of the said Cornices in Lead or other Pipe or Pipes affixed against or on the Sides or Fronts of the said four Houses reaching into the Publick Street extending downwards to the pavement of the said Street or Areas Contrary to the Statute in such case made and Provided and that by reason of which Your Petitioner had forfeited the Sum of Ten pounds for each of the said four Cornices.

That the said Justice thereupon Proceeded upon the said Information and your Petitioner denying the said Charge was afterwards convicted by the said Justice of the matter charged upon him before the said Justices, but your Petitioner humbly Submits that your Petitioner ought not have been convicted thereof in as much as your Petitioner humbly apprehends he did shew to the said Justice and is now able to shew to your Worships when your Worships shall be pleased to hear the same that your Petitioner hath not in any Manner offended against the said Statute on the occasion abovementioned but hath

fully complied with the same for which reason Your Petitioner thinks aggrieved by the said Conviction and humbly appeals against the [...]

Your Petitioner therefore humbly pray Worships that the said Conviction when returned quashed or that Your Petitioner may have Such re [...] the premises as to your Worships shall Seem meet
