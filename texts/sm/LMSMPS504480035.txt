To the Right Worshipfull the Chairman and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex At their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwards and Overseers of the Poor of The Parish of Christ Church in the said County.

Sheweth.

That by a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Thomas Quarrill and Richd. Ricards Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of Peace for the County of Middlesex (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Sixteenth Day of May 1755 Whereby Susannah Fitcombe (Wife of James Fitcombe who was in Parts beyond the Seas) and their two Children (Vizt.) Susanna Aged about Five Years and an half and Mary Aged about Four Years were removed from the Parish of Saint Mary white Chapel in the said County of Middlesex unto the said Parish of Christ Church in the said County Alledging The Said Parish of Christ Church to be Place of their last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners Concerning themselves Agrieved by the Warrant or Order of the said two Justices of the Peace do therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court Against the same Praying such relief in the Premisses as to this Court shall seem meet.

And your Petitioners Shall over Pray Etc.

William Carters Attorny for the Parish of Christ Church Middx
