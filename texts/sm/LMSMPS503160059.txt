To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Athallows Barkin London

Sheweth That by Vertue of a Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Robert Donnet and Richard Ricards Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace the Said County of Middlesex One whereof being of the Quorum bearing date the Twenty Sixth day of January Last Ephriam Lyle and his three Children, that is to say, Isabella aged fourteen years, Robert aged thirteen years, and Sarah aged Eight years are Passed from the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Athallows Barkin London alledging the last Legal Settlement of the said Ephriam Lyle and his three Children to be there, And your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices of humbly Appeal there from and humbly pray That your Worships would appoint some day the [...] present

General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be held for the said County to hear this Appeal And that your Worships will make an Order That the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate aforesaid And all Partys concerned in and about the Premisses may then Attend hear abide by the final determination of your Worships

And your Petitioners Shall Ever Pray

William Clissold Attorney for the Appellants

Dated 4 May 1736
