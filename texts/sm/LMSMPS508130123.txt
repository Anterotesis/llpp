To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Alhallows Honey Lane in the City of London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant bearing date the tenth Day of May now last past under the Hands and Seals of Charles Friquet and David Wilmot Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one where of being of the Quorum) Ann Hergest Widow was removed from and out of the Hamlet of Mile End New town in the parish of Saint Dunstan Stepney in the County of Middlesex aforesaid to the said parish of Alhallows Honey Lane in the City of London as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Ann Hergest whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly appeal against the said Order or pass Warrant & pray that the benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them And that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County of Middlesex

And Your Petitioners Shall ever pray Etc.

Natl. Nicholls Solr. for the Appellants
