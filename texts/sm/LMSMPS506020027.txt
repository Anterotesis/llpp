To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of John Lane an G: Wrights Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) dated the 3d. day of May 1770. Sarah Hanney, the widow of William Hanney and her two Children William aged Nine Years, and Joseph aged seven Years, were Removed from the Parish of Saint Mary le bone within the said county of Middx to the said Parish of Saint James as the Place of their last legal settlement whereby your Petitioner apprehend themselves aggrieved and appeal to Your Worships against the said Order.

Therefore your Petitioners humbly Pray that this Court will be pleased to appoint some day in the Present Sessions for the Hearing and determining of the said Appeal, and that all Persons concerned may then attend.

And Your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc.

Thos: Thomas Solr. for Appellts.
