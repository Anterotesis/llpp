To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate in the City of London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a certain Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of John Scott and Rupert Clark Esquire two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex and City and Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the eleventh day of June 1795 John Tilling Susannah (his Wife) And two Children (namely) John aged about four years and Susannah aged about Seventeen Months were removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate in the City of London by the description of the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate in the liberty of the City of London as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said John Tilling Susannah (his Wife) and two Children whereby your Petitioners Apprehend themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Order or pass Warrant and Pray that the benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them And that the hearing and determination thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of the peace to be holden in and for the said County.

W L & J Newman Solir. for the Appellants.}
