To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the County of Middlesex In their General Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor, of the Parish of Saint Mary Rotherhithe In the County of Surry

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of Stephen Clarke and Philip Dyot Esquires Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing Date the 19th. Day of April 1763. Mary Stowe wife of John Stowe was Removed from the Parish of Saint Giles In the Fields In the County of Middlesex, unto the parish of Saint Mary Rotherhithe in the said County of Surry, Alledging the said Parish to be the Last Legal Place of Settlement of the said John Stowe and his said wife

That your Petitioners Conceiving Themselves Aggrieved by the said Order of the said Two Justices Do Therefore appeal to your Worships There from, and humbly Pray That your Worships will be pleased to hear them In the premises, and order That the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Giles In the Fields In the County of Middlesex, May appear before your Worships at a Certain Day to be appointed, to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships Touching The Appeal.

And your Petitioners Shall Ever Pray Etc
