To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Joseph Brown an Inhabitant of de Parish of Isleworth in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a certain Rate or Assessment made for the relief of the Poor of the said Parish of Isleworth in the said County of Middlesex on the Twenty ninth day of October One Thousand Seven hundred and Ninety five Your Petitioner and many other Inhabitants and Occupiers of Houses and Lands in the said Parish are in and by the said Rate or Assessment charged or assessed several Sums of Money for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish

That your Petitioner is charged towards the said Relief at the Sum of forty Eight Pounds as and for the Yearly Rent of a certain Messuage or an Tenement and Premises in his occupation situate in Hounslow in the said Parish of Isleworth whereas your Petitioner apprehends he ought not to be charged in the said Rate or Assessment at any greater Rent than the Yearly Rent of Fourteen Pounds for and in respect of the said Messuage or Tenement and Premises being the Rent which your Petitioner now pays and has paid from Lady Day One Thousand Seven hunderd and Nienty two for the said Messuage or Tenament and Premises by Virtue of an Indenture of Lease to him thereof.

That Your Petitioner is charged in and by the said Rate or Assessment much more than several other Persons inhabiting in the said Parish in proportion to the yearly Rent of the Messuages or Tenament and Premises they respectively occupy in the said Parish are charged in and by the said Rate or Assessment and in particular Your Petitioner is charged much more than Thomas Hiscock, John Innwood and Thomas Bristow named in the said Rate and who severally inhabit and occupy

Messuages or Tenements and Premises in the said Parish are severally Charged in and by the said Rate or Assessment in Proportion to the Yearly Rents of the several Messuages or Tenements and Premises for which they are respectively charged in and by the said Rate are Assessment and also for that the said Rate or Assessment is in many respects partial and unequal.

And Your Petitioner hath also a material Objection to the said Rate or Assessment for that the Reverend Mr. Drake who at the time of making the said Rate was and now is Vicar of the said Parish is not charged or Assessed in and by the said Rate of Assessment in any Sum of money whatsoever for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish.

That your Petitioner for the reasons aforesaid hath material Objection to the Said Rate or Assessment and Apprehends himself to be aggrieved thereby and that the said Rate or Assessment is illegal by reason of the manner of making the same and therefore humbly appeals against the said Rate or Assessment-And

Your Petitioner prays that the said Rate or Assessment may be Quashed or that your Petitioenr may have Such Relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.
