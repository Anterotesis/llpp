To The Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace at Hicks Hall in the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Luke in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Philip Dyot and Stephen Clarke Esquires Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 11th. day of January 1775. Sarah Hawthorne Singlewoman Aged about Twenty five years and her Male Child named Thomas aged about three Weeks (for nurture) were Removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Luke as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Sarah Hawthorne Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the said Pass Warrant or Order of the said two Justices and pray your Worships will please to appoint some day in this present Session for the Hearing and Determining the said Appeal and to Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields and all Persons concerned do then Attend this Court.

And your Petitioner Shallever pray. Etc.

J. Jenings Sollr: fro Appellants
