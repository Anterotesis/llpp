To William Manwaring Esquire Chairman and the Rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Session to be held for the said County on Monday the tenth day of September 1798.

The Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Staining London.

Sheweth That by an Order a Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of I Flind and John Nares Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the Sixteenth day of August last Hannah Norman Wife of John Norman and her Child named John aged Seven Months was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the said County to the said Parish of Saint Mary Staining As the Place of their last legal settlement

That Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by such order of Removal and therefore pray your Worships to Examine into hear and determine the circumstances respecting the legal Settlement of the said Hannah Norman and her Child And that the Order of Removal of the said I Flind and John Nares may be quashed and that a day may be appointed in this present Session for hearing and determining this Appeal
