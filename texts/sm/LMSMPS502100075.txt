To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middx in their Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Sepulchres London

Sheweth That one William Hare, Margaret his Wife and Elizabeth William and Margaret their three Children by Vertue of a Warrant under the hands & seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County of Middx one being of the Quorum bearing date the 26th day of March now last past were removed from the Liberty of Glass house Yard in the Parish of St. Bottolph without Aldersgate in the said County of Middx to the parish of St. Sepulchres London aforesaid Adjudgeing the said Parish to be the Place of the last legall Settlement of the said William Hare Margaret his wife and Elizabeth William & margaret their Children

That Your Petitioners doubt not to make it appear that the said William Hare and margaret his wife and their said three Children had no legall Settlement in the said Parish of St. Sepulchres London Whereby they Conceive themselves Aggrieved by the Judgement of the said two Justices and doe humbly Appeal to your Worships there from

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships will be pleased to hear them in the premises And Order the Overseers of the Poor of the said Liberty of Glass house Yard in the said Parish of St. Bottolph without Aldersgate to attend & hear and Abide the determinacon of your Worships touching this Appeal

And your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc

John Blakie Church warden Tho: Joyner: Ch: Warden
