To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the county of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County

That by a pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of William Baker, and James Clitherow Esquires two of this Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said county of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the first day of May 1762 Esther Messenger the Wife of John Messenger was removed from the Parish of Hanwell in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of saint James Westminster, as the place of her last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners thir themselves aggreved and appeal to this Court Against the same.

Therefore your Petitioners pray this Court will be pleased to appoint a day for the hearing and Determining the said Appeal And that ale persons concerned may be Ordered then to Attend

And uour Petitioners shall Pray Etc

Sam: Seddon Solicitor for the parish St James Westminster

[...] Opposite James's Church. 17th. May 1763.
