To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of Jas: Stratten, and William Brodie, two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quarum) bearing date the 26th: day of November last

Amey Goodeve and five Children her lawful Issue to wit, Thomas aged Eight Years, Charles aged seven Years Mary aged six Years, Charlotte aged one Year, and seven Months, and one Child (not yet baptized) aged one Month were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint John at Hackney in the said County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Anne as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two

two Justices and their said Order And pray that the Benefit of this Appeal may be saved to the Petitioners, and that the hearing and determining thereof be adjourned to the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden in and for this County to hear and determine the said Appeal according to Law

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
