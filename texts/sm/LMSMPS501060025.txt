To the Rt Worspll her Matys Justices of ye Peace for ye County of Midds.

The humble Petition of Joseph Kingston

Sheweth

That About the Seaventeenth of September 1705 Your Petr. became an Apprntice to John Tarr of St James Clerkenwell in this County Cabbinett Maker for the Terme of Seaven Yeares comencing from the Day of the date of his Indenture of Apprnticeshipp As there by May Appeare

That the Sd John Tarr is not Capable of Instructing Your Petitr in the said Art of A Cabbinett Maker All though Your Petrs: Friends Gave the sd John Tarr to Take Your Petr: his Apprntice the Sum of Fifteene Pounds And Forasmuch as the said John Tarr having of ten abused Your Petr During the Time be hath beene with him And Did Promise To Make Your Petr: A Freeman of the Citty of London att the Expiration of his Apprnticeshipp when Itt has Since Plainly Appeared that the sd John Tarr neither then was nor now is A Freeman himselfe

P Tho: Nash

Your Petr therefore most humbly Prays your Worshipps to be Pleased to Grant that he may be discharged from his sd Apprnticeshipp And yt Such parte of the sd Fifteene Pounds may be Returned back by the sd John Tarr as unto your Worshipps Shall Seeme Meete

And Yor Petr: Shall Ever Pray Etc

Joseph Kingston
