To the Worshipful His Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace at the new Session House on Clerkenwell Green Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Bar of is the Parish of Saint Luke In the County of Middlesex

Sheweth.

Tha by Virtue of an Over or Pass Warrant under Hands and Seals of Stepten old and William Hick fore Esquires Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one where of being of the Quorum bearing Date the 28th day of October 1783 whereby John Elkins and his Wife Named Elizabeth were Removed and Convey from the Parish of Twickenham. In the County of Middlesex aforesaid to the said Parish of saint Luke as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners Conceive themselves to be Aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and pray that your Worships will please to appoint some Day in this Present Session for the Hearing and Determining this Appeal and to order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Twickenham, and all Persons Concerned to attend this Court

And Your Petitioners Shall ever pray Etc

Rich Jenings Sollr. for Appellants
