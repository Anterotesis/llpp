To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Paul Shadwell in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of John Milner and Philip Dyot Esquires Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (Whereof One is of the Quorum) Dated the Twelfth Day of February 1750 [---] Mary Willson Widow of John Willson deceased and Isabella her Daughter aged about Ten Years were removed from the parish of St. Giles in the fields in the sd. County to the said Parish of St. Paul Shadwell in the said County of Middlesex as the place of their last legal Settlement.

That Your said Petitioners humbly apprehends they are aggrieved by the said Order of Removal and Appeals against the said Order to this Court,

Your Petitioners humbly prays Your Worships to appoint a [...] Day for the [---] Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said parish of St. Giles in the Fields in the said County of Middx and all other Parties concerned to attend the said Court to hear the said Appeal And your petitioners shall ever pray

John Higgs Sollr. for the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Paul Shadwell
