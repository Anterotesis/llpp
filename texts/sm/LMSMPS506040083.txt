To the Worshipfull John Hawkins Esqr. one of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex and other his Fellow Justices Etc

The Humble Petition of Thomas Clarke an Apprentice to Thomas Gibbs of Quaker Buildings in the Parish of Saint Sepulchre without Baker

Sheweth That your Petitioner was by Indenture [---] bearing Date on or about the Year One Thousand Seven hundred and Sixty Seven Bound an Apprentice to the said Thomas Gibbs for the Term of Seven Years from the Date of the said Indenture

That Your Petitioners said Master received the Sum of Ten pounds of and from the Society of Quakers as a Consideration Fee That your Petitioners Master has Obtained the Counterpart of Petitioners Indenture from him

That your Petitioners Master has for Several Months last Past Beat him with any was reasonable Provocation given by your Petitioner

That on or about the Third Day of October Instant Your Petitioners Master turned him out of his House and would not receive Your Petitioner any more nor give him any of his Cloaths altho' Several Applications has been made to him a great many times and over to this Day

Therefore your Petitioner most Humbly Prays your Worships taking the Premises into your Consideration To Order Your Petitioners Master to Attend this Honourable Court to Shew Cause why Your Petitioner should not be Discharged from his said Apprenticeship or otherwise as your Worships shall think proper and Also to Order his said Master to Attend this Honourable Court on some Day in this present Session And your Petitioner as in Duty Bound shall ever pray Etc

Thomas Clark

Middx to wit At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the County aforesaid (by Adjournment) on Tuesday the twenty third Day of October in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third King of Great Britain France and Ireland Etc

It is Ordered by this Court that the said Master have Notice of this Appeal And that he and All persons concerned Do Attend this Court on Thursday the Twenty fifth Day of October Instant at Nine of the Clock in the forenoon to hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

By the Court Butler.
