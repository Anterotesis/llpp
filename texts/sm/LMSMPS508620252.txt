To the worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire Chairman and to the rest of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their general Quarter Session assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of Aubrey Duke of Saint Albans

Sheweth That by a rate made for the relief of the Poor of the Parish of Hanworth in the County of Middlesex A Certian Messuage or Dwelling house Garden and premises situate in the said Parish in the Occupation of the said Duke hath been assessed at the Sum of Seven Pounds ten shillings for the present year by which your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved and humbly conceives that such rate is excessive and that the same ought not to be more than Two Pounds ten shillings being one shilling in the Pound on the rent of such House and Premises and that such rate is unequal irregular and contrary to Law

Your Petitioner therefore prays that a day may be appointed for the hearing and determining his said Appeal and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Hanworth and all others may attend your Worships to hear and abide the Order and determination of your worships concerning the said Appeal
