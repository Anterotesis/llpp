To the Right Worshipfull His Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden for the said County at Hicks Hall Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Stains in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of removal under the Hands and Seals of Sir Joseph Ayloffe Houlding Baronet and Thomas Bennett Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the city and Liberty of Westminster for the[---] of Middlesex aforesaid (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Sixteenth Day of August One Thousand Seven Hundred and Fifty four made for the removal of Elizabeth Collier Spinster in the Said Pass Warrant or Order mentioned from the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminser in the Said County of Middlesex to the Said Parish of Stains as the Place of her last Legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners Apprehend themselves Aggrieved by the Said Pass Warrant or Order and the Judgment of the Said two Justices And Do therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court against the Same

Your Petitioners Therefore Humbly Pray That this Court will be pleased to appoint a Day to hear and Determine the Matter Touching their Said Appeal and that all partys Concerned may then Attend

And Your Petitioners Shall Ever Pray Etc

James Ton Attorney for the Parish of Stains
