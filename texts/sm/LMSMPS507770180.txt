To William Main waring Esquire Chairman and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the said County of Middlesex.

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Sir George Booth Baronet and Willm. Blackborow Esquire two of his Majesty's Justice of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the ninth day of January in the Year of our Lord 1784 Jno Ford (by the Name of George Poor) aged about Twelve Years was removed and Conveyed from and out of the said Parish of St. Sepulchres in the said County to the sd. Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the County assd. as the Place of the last Legal Settlement of the said George Poor.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly appeal against the same to your Worships and pray Relief in the Premises and that a day in this present Session may be appointed for the hearing and determining the said Appeal.

And Your Peors. will pray Etc Jno Skutt Sel for Appellants Goodmans Fields
