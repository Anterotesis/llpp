To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Peticon and appeal of the Churchwarden [...] & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the Bars in the said County

Sheweth That by vertue of an Order or Pass Warrt. under the Hands & Seals of Hamond Crosse & William Withers Esqrs. Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said County (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing date the Twentieth Instant Margaret Rake the Wife of Samuel Rake (who hath deserted her) & her Three Children Elizabeth aged about Twelve Years Thomas Aged about Six Years and Ann Aged about Two Years were removed from the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the said County to the sd. Parish of St. Andrew Holborn above the Bars as the Place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petrs. thinking themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrt. of the sd. Two Justices Appeal to this Court against the same & humbly Pray that your Worships will be pleased to Appoint some day in the next General Session of the Peace to be held at Hicks Hall in & for the said County for the hearing & determining of the same

D. Bolton for ye Appellts.

And yor. Petrs. shall ever Pray Etc.
