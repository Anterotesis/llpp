Middlesex To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of Francis Wood Oldknow of the Parish of Saint Ann Soho in the said County

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the Eleventh day of May now last Part Convicted before Nathaniel Conant and John Scott Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County For that your Petitioner did on the Eighth day of the said Month of May at a Certain House in Conduit Street in the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the City and Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex Set up Maintain and keep a certain fraudulent Game to be determined by the Chance of Cards under the Denomination of Pharash against the Form of the Statute in such Case made and Provided And your Petitioner was Adjudged to Pay and forfeit for the said Offence the Sum of Two Hundred Pounds To be distributed as therein is mentioned

That Your Petitioner Conceiving himself Aggrieved by the Adjudication of the said Two Justices Entered into a Recognizance with Two sufficient Sureties before them to Appeal to the present Quarter Session of the Peace against the said Conviction and Adjudication

Your Petitioner therefore Humbly Appeals against the said Conviction and Prays that the same may be Quashed and the having thereof may be [---] appointed on a doy in this present [...] Determining [---]

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc
