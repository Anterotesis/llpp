To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the county of Middlesex assembled. at a General [---] Sessions for the said County.

The humble Petition of Robert Simpson

Sheweth That your Petitioner is a licensed dealer in Perfumerary residing in Princes Street Cavendish Square in the Parish of Saint Marylebone in the county of Middlesex.

That your Petitioner on the fourteenth day of January last was convicted before Richard Ford Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County for that your Petitioner on the third day of January last did sell to one Elizabeth Dodd a Roll of Pomatum for the hair no stamped cover wrapper or label being pasted stuck fastened or affixed to the same contrary to the form of the statute in that case made and provided And the said Justice did declare that your Petitioner had forfeited the sum of five pounds for the offence aforesaid.

That your Petitioner finding himself aggrieved by the Judgment of the said Justice did give security to the amount of the said penalty and Costs as by the said Statute is directed and appealed to the next General Quarter Sessions against the said Conviction.

Your Petitioner therefore mays that he may be allowed to prosecute his said Appeal before your worships

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.

Robert Simpson
