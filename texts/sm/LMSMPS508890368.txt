To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Sepulcher London

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Edward Read and William Hyde Esquires Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 18th Day of October 1793 one William Ham was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Luke Chelsea to the said Parish of Saint Sepulcher in the City of London as the Place of his legal Settlement

Your Petitioners thinking themselves aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant of the said Two Justices Appeal to this Court against the same and humbly pray that the [...] said Appeal may be adjourned to the next General Sessions of the Peace

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray
