To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Session of the Peace now assembled

The hbe Petition of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlet of Ratcliffe in the Parish of St. Dunstan Stepney in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order Under the Hands & Seals of Samuel Surbey and John Levy Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Surry bearing date the Fourteenth day of August last past Ann Brock the wife of John Brock gone away from her waw removed from the Parish of Saint Saviour Southwark in the said County of Surry to the before mentioned Parish of Saint Dunstan Stepney as the place where the said John Brock with his said Wife was & is last legally setled & that the said Pauper together with the said Order of Removal was delivered to the Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlet of Ratcliffe aforesaid where the said John Brock now & for some time past hath dwelt & gone by the name of Benjamin Buck

That your Petitioners soon after applied to the said John Brock a lies Benjamin Buck to provide forth said Ann his Wife but he absolutely refused whereupon they abtained a Warrant against him and he was thereupon apprehended & on the thirteenth day of November last was comtted to Clerkenwell Bridewell as a Vagabond but on the Sixteenth following was bailed out form thence

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships will appoint a time this present Session for the said John Brock alies [...] for appear before this Court then and there to hear & abide by the Sentence & determination there of as to Your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.

J. Seacome Agent for the Hamlet of Ratcliffe 7th. Decr: 1767
