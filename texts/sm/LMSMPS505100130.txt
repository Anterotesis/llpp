To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions at Hicks Hall Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth, That by Virtue of a pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of Fran: Bedwell and Barth: Hammond Esqrs: two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 22d. day of May 1762 Margaret Hill wife of Samuel Hill (gone from her) with one Child Mary aged about two Months were removed from the parish of Saint Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint James as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners think themselves Aggreived and Appeal to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that the benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto them until the next General Quarter Sessions of the peace to be holden for this County And that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the said next General Quarter Sessions of the peace And that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Saint Clement Danes and all persons concerned may be Ordered then to Attend To hear and abide the Indgment and determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.

Saml. Seddon Solicitor for the said parish of St. James
