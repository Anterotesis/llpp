To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the peace for the said County Assembled

The Humble petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by virtue of a certain Outer or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Danl. Williams and John Horton Esquire Two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the Liberty of his Majesties Tower of London in the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the second day of July One thousand Seven Hundred and Ninety four Elizabeth Mackglashan Singlewoman was removed and conveyed from and out of the Old Artillery Ground in the said Liberty to the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate in the City of London by the description of Saint Botolph Bishopgate in the City of London as the place of the last legal settlement of the said Elizabeth Mackglashan Whereby your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved

W. L. Newman Solicitor for the Appellants} Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Order or Pass Warrant and pray That the benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determination thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County.
