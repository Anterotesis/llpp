To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace for the said County assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Paris of Soberton in the County of Southampton

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Samuel Glase and William Heckford Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 6th day of May 1794 Sarah Oliver Widow and her two Children were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Twickenham in the said County of Middlesex to the Parish of Soberton aforesaid as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that the Benefit of their said Appeal may be reserved to them And that a day may be appointed in the present General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex for the hearing and determining the said Appeal And that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Twickenham be required to attend to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of the Court touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Daill Sollr. for the Appellants
