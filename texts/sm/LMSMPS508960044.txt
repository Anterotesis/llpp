To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The Petition and Appeal of Albion Cooper of Richmond in the County of Surry Tallow Chandler against the Conviction of Nathaniel Bland Esquire One of the his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County of Middlesex.

Sheweth, That by Virtue of an Order Judgment or Conviction under the Hand and Seal of Nathaniel Bland Esquire One of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County bearing date the 13th. January 1794 the said Justice convicted your Petitioner in the Penal Sum of Â£5 and Costs for that your Petitioner did on the 10th. day of the said Month of January 1794 use or cause to be used a Cart in or upon a certain Highway or Public Turnpike Road in old Brentford in the said County of Middlesex without having his Surname and place of Abode painted thereon in large Legible Letters contrary to the staute Etc And

That your Petitioner did on the 18th. day of January last cause the said Nathaniel Bland Esquire to be served with a Notice of Appeal to the said Conviction

That your Petitioner has entered into the usual recognizance with proper Security for the abiding the Order of this Court to be made in that behalf And that your Petitioner humbly conceives himself aggrieved in the Promises And that he has good cause for the said Appeal

Your Petitioner therefore Appeals to your Worships against the said Judgment Order and Conviction and humbly prays relief in the Premises And that you Worships will be pleased to appoint some day in this heresent Sessions for the hearing and determining the said appeal and that all Parties concerned may then attend.

And your Petitioner shall ever pray.

Middlesex At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex at the Session House for the said County by Adjournment on Tuesday the 29th. day of April in the thirty fourth year of the reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third King of Great Britain
