In the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex at their General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex and Liberty of Westminster

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of N Conant Esqr and Philp Neve Esqr two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty second Day of July 1793 Elizabeth Robinson Wife of William Robinson (who is therein Described to have been then at Sea) and her Male Child named Thomas aged two Years and upwards were removed from the Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the said County to the said Parish of St. Clement Danes as the place of their last legal settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore Appeal against the said Order and humbly pray releif [...] in the Premises and that your Worships will be pleased to Order that the benefit of their said Appeal should be saved unto them, that the hearing and determining thereof should be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County and that some Day may be appointed in the same Session for that Purpose

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
