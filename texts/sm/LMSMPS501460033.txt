To the Rt Worsppll his Matys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx

The humble petition of John Wallis

Sheweth That by Indenture Dated the Twenty Sixth day of June Anno Dom 1714 one Jane Wallis a poor Child of the Parish of Hornsey in this County was by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor thereof putt and placed to your Petitr to serve him untill she should accomplish her full Age of Eighteen Yeares

That your Petitr was by agreement with the sd Churchwdns to have five pounds with his said Apprentice But the Churchwardens Did not pay yor Petitr the sd Moneys at the Executing the sd Indentures Alledging they had not then Moneys in their hands But promised him payment soone after

That Your Petitr hath often Demanded the sd Moneys but they refuse to pay him

Your Petitr therefore humbly prays your Worspps will be pleased to order the Churchwdens and Overseers to attend this Court some day this Sessions to shew Cause why your Petitr should not be paid the sd five pounds [...] that they may be other wayes relieved As to this Court Shall Seeme Meete

And Yor Petitr: shall Ever pray etc

John Wallis
