To the Rt. Worspl his Maties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx Assembled at their Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace now holden for the said County at Hicks hall

The Humble Petition of Owen Jones Constable of the parish of Uxbridge in the said County for the Year 1728.

Sheweth, That your said Peticoner in his has Office Disbursed divers and Sundry Sums of Money, for Passing and Relieving of Vagrants, Amounting in the whole to the Sum of Seven Pounds and three Shillings, as may appear by a Certificate, under the hands and Seals of Sr. George Cooke, Knt; and Robert Vyner, and James Rogers, Esqrs:, three of his Maties Justices of the peace for the said County, bearing Date the 29th Day of May, And Dni 1729: which said Sum, your said Peticonr. is out of Pockett

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays this Court, that the said Sums may be Ordered to be paid and Reimbursed to your Petitioner, as to your Widows Shall Seem Meet.

And Your Petitionr. shall Pray Etc

the mark of Owen [x] Jones
