To the Right Worshipfull His Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden for the said County at Hicks Hall Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Fulham in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That By Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honourable The Earl of Kinmoull and John Goodchild Esquire Two of His Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twelfth Day of January One Thousand seven hundred and Fifty Four made for the Removal of William Clarke and Ann his wife in the said Pass Warrant or Order mentioned from the Parish of Shepperton in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Fulham as the Place of their last Legal Settlement She the said Ann (without the said William her Husband was on the Twenty First Day of the said Month of January One Thousand seven hundred and Fifty Four Removed and Conveyed from the said Parish of Shepperton to the said Parish of Fulham.

That your Petitioners Apprehend themselves Aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant Order and the Judgment of the said Two Justices And Do therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners Therefore Humbly Pray That this Court will be Pleased to Appoint some Day in the next General Sessions of the Peace to be held for this County to Hear and Determine the Matter touching their said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall Ever Pray Etc.

Nicoll Hatton Garden Sollr. for Appellts.
