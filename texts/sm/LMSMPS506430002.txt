Newgate Middlesex

the [...] Day of August 177 4

To John Shewood Esqr

One of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County

The Humble PETITION of John Sheppard a Prisoner in the said Prison

SHEWETH, THAT your Petitioner, on the Twenty-eighth Day of April, in the Year of Our Lord 1774, was actually a Prisoner for Debt, confined in the said Prison, and hath continued so ever since, and is willing in all Things to conform himself to the Directions of an Act of Parliament made in the Fourteenth Year of th Reign of his present Majesty King GEORGE III. intituled, An Act for the Relief of Insolvent Debtors, and for the Relief of Bankrupts in certain Cases, to the Benefit of which your Petitioner conceives to is entitled.

That the annexed Schedule (to be sworn unto by me, at the next General or Quarter Session, or any Adjournment thereof) contains a full and true Account of all your Peti- tioner's Real and Personal Estate s of every Kind and Nature whatsoever, in Possession, Reversion, Remainder or Expec- tancy, excepting Wearing Apparel, Bedding for himself and Family, Working Tools, and necessary Implements for h is Occupation and Calling, a Sum of Money not exceeding Forty Shillings, and those in the Whole, not exceeding the Value of Twenty Pounds.

Your Petitioner, therefore, humbly prays your wor- ship to grant your Warrant directed to the Keeper of the said Prison to bring your Peti- tioner, and the Warrant or Warrants of Detainer, together with the Copy or Copies of the Cause o [...] Causes, with which your Petitioner is charged in his said Custody before his Majesty's Justices of the Peace at the General or Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County or any Adjournment thereof, which shall be held next after the Ex- piration of Ten Days from the Date hereof, pur- suant to the Tenor and Direction of the said Act of Parliment.

And your Petitioner, as in Duty bound, shall ever Pray, Etc.

John Sheppard
