To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of John Ford.

Sheweth That by a certain Conviction under the hands and Seals of Sir Sampson Wright Knight and William Kitchiner Esquire two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the fifth day of March last your Petitioner was and stands convicted for that your petitioner on the twenty seventh day of January one thousand seven hundred and ninety one within the Kingdom of Great Britain (that is to say) in the parish of Saint Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex did sell to William Laudonsack twenty two Buttons then and there being bound and each and every of them then and there being bound with certain Stuff called Kerseymere, the said Stuff called Kerseymere then and there being Stuff of which Clothes and Wearing Garments were then usually made contrary to the Statute in such Case made and provided And your Petitioner was adjudged to forfeit and pay for the said Offence the Sum of Nine pounds three shillings and four pence.

That your Petitioner apprehends himself to be aggrieved by the said Conviction and humbly Appeals against the same.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays that the benefit of his said Appeal may be saved unto him and that the hearing and determination thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the peace to be holden in and for the said County of Middlesex.
