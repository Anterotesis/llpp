To The Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Dodson late of the Parish of Enfield in the County of Middlesex Labourer now a Prisoner in the New Prison Clerkenwell in the said County.

Sheweth. That Your Petitioner was on the seventeenth Day of March last convicted before pierce Galliard Esquire one of His Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the said County by the Oath of one Henry Barnes of Enfield aforesaid Sawyer for that Your Petitioner on Monday the third Day of February last past on his Majesty's Chase of Enfield in the said County unlawfully did hunt and a certain fallow Deer the Property of his said Majesty did course and hill with out the consent of any Person who had the Care of the Deer therein or without being otherwise duly Authorized against the Form of the Statute in such Case made and Provided by Reason whereof Your Petitioner had forfeited the Sum of Thirty Pounds.

That Your Petitioner having no Goods or Chattels whereon the said Penalty of thirty Pounds could be levied was by the said Justice by Warrant under his hand and Seal bearing Date the said Seventeenth Day of March committed to the Custody of the Keeper of the said Prison there to be safely kept for the space of one whole Year without Bail or Main prize unless the said Penalty should be sooner Paid.

That Your Petitioner apprehending himself to be aggrieved by the Judgments of the said Justice upon the Conviction aforesaid humbly Appeal from and against the same and prays Releif in the Premises and that Your Worship will be pleased to examine and inquire into the Facts and Circumstances of the Case against Your Petitioner and hear and determine this Your Petitioners Appeal at this Present General Quarter Session of the Peaces

Willm Chetham Selr. for the Appellant}
