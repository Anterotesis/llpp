To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the Bars in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Thos. Edwards and William Hyde Esqrs. Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 13th Day of July last 1779 Jane Whitwell singlewoman aged about Twenty one Years was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the Bars aforesaid in the said County as the Place of her last legal Settlement Whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and appeal to this Court against the same

And therefore humbly pray that the Benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them until the next General Session of the Peace to be held in and for the said County of Middlesex And that Your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day in the said next General Session of the Peace for to hear and determine their said Appeal

Thos. Wade Junr. No. 33 Larnbs Condt. Street Sollr. for the Appellts.

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.
