To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the peace for the said County assembled

The Humble petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the parish of Cholsey by Mistake in the Order of Removal spelt Choseley, in the County of Berks

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order of Removal a pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of John Scott & [...] two of his Majestys Justices of the peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex one whereof being of the Quorum bearing date the Second day of September 1799, Ann Saunders the Wife of John Saunders by the name of Ann Saunders and their three Children name by William aged Seven years, Martha aged three years, and John Aged Eight months were removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Kensington in the County of Middlesex and delivered to your petitioners being the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Chelsey in the said County of Berks the said Justices adjudging that the place of the last legal settlement of the said Ann and William Martha & John her Children was in the said parish [---] of Cholsey, in the said County of Berks, By which said Order of Removal or pass Warrant, your petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggreived

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said Order
