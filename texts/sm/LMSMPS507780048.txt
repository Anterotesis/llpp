To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace at the Session House for the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition & Appeal of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Anne Within the Liberty of Westminster in the County aforesaid

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands & Seals of William [...] & John Staples Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace in & for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the third day of February Instant Mary Clay Wife of Job Clay who hath deserted & left her & their two Children Vizt. Sarah Aged about Twenty One Years and Ann Aged about Twelve Years were Removed & Conveyed from & out of the Parish of Saint Leonard Bromley in the said County to the said Parish of Saint Anne as the place of their last Legal Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order And Pray that the Benefit of this Appeal may be saved to the Petitioners And that the Hearing & Determining thereof may be Adjourned to the next General Session of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County of Middlesex

And your Petitioners shall Pray Etc

Jno. Allison Geo: Js: Seward} Church Wardens

William Thomas James Unsworth} Overseers of the Poor of the Said Parish of St Anne
