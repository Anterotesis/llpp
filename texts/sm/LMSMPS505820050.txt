To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in the their General Quarter Session of the peace assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Church Warden and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Richmond in the County of Surry.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order on pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Rt. Darling and R. Hodgson Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County) of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the thirteenth day of January last past John Nash was removed from the Hamlett of Mile End Old Town in the parish of Saint Dunstans Stepney in the County of Middlesex unto the parish of Richmond in the County of Surry aforesaid Alledging the said parish to be the place of the said John Nash's last Legal settlement,

That your petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices Do therefore appeal to Your Worships therefrom and humbly pray that you will please to hear them in the Premisses and Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlett of Mile End Old Town in the parish of Saint Dunstans Stepney may Appear before you at a Certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships touching this Appeal And your petitioners shall ever Pray

Chas: Jemmett Sollr for the Appellts.

April 12th. 1768
