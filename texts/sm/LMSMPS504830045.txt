To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden for the said County

The Humble Petition of John Miles

Sheweth That your Petitioner was at the Present Session Nominated to Serve the Office of Chief Constable within the Hundred of Elthorne in the said County in the Stead of John Drinkwater

That Your Petition would very Willingly take upon him the Execution of the said Office, but for that he is under an ill state of health being frequently Afflicted with very severe and Tedious Fits of the Rhumatism and other disorders which he is fearful will render him uncapable of his Duty

That there are several Gentlemen of Ability that are his Neighbours ready to Serve the said Office and such as with Submission to your Worships Judgement better Qualified in all Respects than your Petitioner

Your Petr. therefore humbly prays that he may e Dismissed from the said Office and that any One of the under written may be Appointed thereto as to your Worships shall seem meet

I Recommend this Petition and approve of either of the persons undernamed to be appointed in Stead of Mr. Miles We think Howard a very proper Man.} Jo: D: Bickhead

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray

John Miles

Richard Howard of New Brentford Clockmaker Thomas Crosby of the same Draper Christopher Lonsdale of the same Colour man Thomas Tipping of the Same. James

edward Howard Sworn in Court at Hicks Hall the 20th Day of May 1760
