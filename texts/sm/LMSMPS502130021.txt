To the Right Worsppll his Matys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlx:

The humble peticon of Charles Cooke

Humbly Sheweth. That your Peticoners wife Elizabeth Cooke was on the 16th: of this Instant August by the Worshippfull Justice S: Parry Esqr Comitted to the House of Correction in this County at the Complaint & on the Oath of your Peticoner for being a loose Idle Disorderly person To be Putt to hard Labour untill discharged by due course of Law.

That Your Peticoners said Wife lyes so Itt in the said Prison that her Life is Dispaired of

Your Peticoner therefore humbly prays your Worshipps will be pleased. to Order [...] his said wife may be Discharged from her said Imprisonment.

And Yor & titr shall Ever Pray Etc Charles Cooke.
