To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in there Michaelmas Session of the Peace Assembled for the Year 1773.

The humble Petition of William Davis.

Sheweth. That your Petitioners Predecessor John Wilkins deceased was in contract with his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex for keeping the Pavement of Brentford Bridge in Repair for the yearly allowance of Nine Pounds and since his death the same hath been repaired by your Petitioner for the same allowance.

That these [...] therefore due to your Petitioner at Michaelmas last the sum of Nine pounds for keeping the said Bridge in Repair for the space of one Year

Wherefore your Petitioner humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to Order Mr. John Benson the County Treasurer to pay unto Your Petitioner the said sum of [---]

And you Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc.

William Davis
