To the Worshipful Sir John Hawkins Knight Chairman and to the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in this general Sessions assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the said County

Sheweth That by a pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Sir James Esdaile Knight and John Walford Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twentieth day of April 1774: A Male Bastard Child named Thomas aged about four Months born on the Body of Sarah Cooper Singlewoman was removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Lake in the said County to the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Male Bastard Child by which your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and humbly appeal therefrom:

Your Petrs. therefore humbly pray that a day may be appointed for the hearing and determining their said Appeal and that your Worships would be pleased to make such Order in the Premisses as to your Worships Shall seem just and Your Petr. shall ever pray Etc.
