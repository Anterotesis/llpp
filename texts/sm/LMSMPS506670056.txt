Middlesex} To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Sessions of the peace for the said County to be holden at Hicks Hall the fourteenth Day of this Inst. October.

The humble Petition of Joseph Read one of the High Constables for the Hundred of Gore in the said County.

Sheweth, That Your Petitioner was sworn into the office of High Constable on or about the tenth day of October 1773 and has duly served the said Office to the best of his Judgment and ability and is now near sixty four years of Age being a Farmer having a large Family, and your Petitioner has here under nominated four Substantial Persons to succeed him in the said office, and humbly prays to be discharged.

Edgware 7th: Octr. 1776.

and your Petitioner as in duty Bound shall ever pray

Joseph Read

Philip Aldwin Pinner John Ewer of the same Daniel Wilshen of the same John Wilshen of the same

We his Majesty's Justices of the peace Acting for the Hundred of Gore in the County of Middlesex do approve of Daniel Wilshen above Mentioned as a proper Person to serve the said Office.

Edgware 7th: Octr. 1776.

Willm. Hallett John Rudge
