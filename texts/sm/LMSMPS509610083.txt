Middlesex to wit To the Worshipful his Majesy's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Maryle bone in the said County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of Thomas Collins and Thomas Robinson Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the 31st. day of May 1799 Reciting that William Morris and John Hayter Church Wardens of the said Parish of Saint Mary le bone had made complaint unto their the said Thomas Collins and Thomas Robinson that Catherine Lilly then late of the said Parish of Saint Mary le bone had quitted it ever since the Sixteenth day of February then last past leaving her two bastard Children namely Catherine aged three Years and Peter aged Eighteen Months upon the charge of the said Parish and that the said Catherine Lilly had Goods and Chattels in the said County of Middlesex They the said Thomas Collins and Thomas Robinson did by the said Order declare that it did appear Upon the oaths of the said William Morris and John Kayter and they did adjudge that the Premises no alledged were true And the said Thomas Collins and Thomas Robinson did in and by their said order in his Majesty's Name Authorize the said Church Wardens together with the over Seers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Mary le bone to take and Seize so much of the Goods and Chattels of the said Catherine Lilly as should be found within the said County of Middlesex and should amount or be Sufficient to raise or pay the Sum of Â£5 which they the said Thomas Collins and Thomas Robinson did in and by their said Order appoint and Order

the said Church Wardens and overseers to receive towards the discharge of the said Parish for the providing for the said Bastard Children

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that your Worships will be pleased to confirm the said order of the said Thomas Collins [...] Thomas Robinson and order that your Petitioners may impose of as much of the Goods and Chattels of the said Catherine Lelly as shall be Sufficient to cause and pray the Sum of five Pounds according to the form of the Statute in such Case made and Provided
