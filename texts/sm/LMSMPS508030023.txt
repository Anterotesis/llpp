To the Worshipfull This Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace (by Adjournment) at the Sessions House on Clerkenwell Green in the said County assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of N: Forster and D. Walker two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quourm) bearing date the 31st. day of August 1785 Mary Ekins aged about Sixty three Years was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Giles in that Fields in the said County to the said Parish of Saint James as the place of her last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and pray that the benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for said County

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc.

Jas Dyson

Solicitor for the Churchwardens and Overseers of th Poor of the Parish of St. James Westminster.
