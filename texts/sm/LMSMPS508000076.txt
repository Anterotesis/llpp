To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace Assembled

The Humble Petiiton and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary le Bone in the said County [---]

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Rd. Taylor and James Croft Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty first day of July [---] 1785 Mary Smith Single Woman and her Bastard Child named William aged three months were removed from the Parish of Saint Pancras in the said County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint Mary le Bone as the place of their last legal Settlement Whereby your petitioners Conceive themselves aggrieved and humbly appeal therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your worships will be pleased to hear them in the premes And to order that the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Saint [---] Pancras may appear before you at a certain day to be Appointed to Hear and abide the Judgement and determination [---] of your worships touching this appeal

And your Petitioners shall pray us

Thos Martin Ser. for the Appellants
