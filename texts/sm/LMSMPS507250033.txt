To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the said County assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of Jno Walford and Jenkin Jones Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex bearing Date the Twentieth Day of April last whereby Sarah Spicer Widow of Richard Spicer deceased and her two Bastard Children namely Sarah aged about three Years and Mary aged two Years were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint James Westminster as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Sarah Spicer whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the adjudication of the said two Justices and their Said Order and Pray that your Worships will please to appoint same Day in the present Session for the Hearing and determining the said Appeal [---] Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Luke Middlesex and all Persons concerned do then attend this Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray

Jas Dyson Solicitor for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James Westminster
