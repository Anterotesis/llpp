To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Blew of Liquorpond Street in the Parish of St. Andrew Holborn in the said County of Middx Baker

Sheweth

That your Petitioner on the Eighth day of May last was convicted before Thomas Kynaston Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex for that your Petitioner did within three days then last past in the Parish aforesaid expose to Sale & Sell Eight Loaves of Wheaten Bread importing to be three penny and two penny Loaves wanting in weight nine Ounces and fourteen Drams According to the Assize then duly set and in force for of the said Bread that is to Say. one three penny Loaf wanting in Weight one Ounce One other three penny Loaf wanting in Weight two Ounce one other three penny Loaf wanting in Weight two Ounces one two penny Loaf wanting in Weight fourteen Drams one other two penny Loaf wanting in Weight one Ounce one other two penny Loaf wanting in Weight one Ounce one other two penny Loaf wanting in Weight one Ounce and one other two penny Loaf wanting in Weight one Ounce And the said Justice did adjudge your Petitioner to pay and forfeit for the same the Sum of two pounds and Seven Shillings

That your Petitioner conceiving himself agrieved thereby doth humbly Appeal against the same and humbly prays such relief in the premises as to your Worships shall seem meet And that this Court will be pleased to appoint a day at this present Sessions of the Peace for the Complaint Samuel Shiply and all other Persons concerned to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of the said Appeal

John Blew
