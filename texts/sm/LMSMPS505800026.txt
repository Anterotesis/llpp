To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys [...] Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Tenbridge in the County of Rent

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order of Remove under [...] and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Thirtieth day of January last Margaret Aston, aged about Six Years, William Aston aged about four Years and Mary Aston aged about Twelve Months the Children of James Aston by Mary his Wife were removed from the Parish of Saint Giles in the Field in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Tenbridge the said Justices thereby adjudging that the East legal Place of Settlement of the said Margaret A [...] Aston and Mary Aston was in the said Parish [...] [...] conceive themselves [...] by the said Order and Judgement of the said [...] humbly appeal the from

Your Petitioners the [...] humbly Pray that Your Worship [...] ould be pleased to appoint some Day in this present Session to hear this their Appeal and to make an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Giles and all Parties concerned in and about the Premisses may then and there hear and abide the Determination of Year Worships therein

And Your Petitioners Shall ever pray Etc

G Children Robert Chalklin} Churchwardens

Wm: Martin Wm: Mickman} Overseers of the Poor
