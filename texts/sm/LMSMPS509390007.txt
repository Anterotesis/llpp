To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of William Husband of Cockspur Street in the parish of Saint [...] Martin in the Fields in their the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex Postmaster

Sheweth That by a Certain Conviction under the hand and Seal of Henry James Pye Esquire of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the eighth day of June now last past your Petitioner was Convicted in the Penalty of Ten pounds for that he after the first day of August one thousand seven hundred and eighty five to wit on the Sixth Day of April in the Year of our Lord One thousand Seven hundred and Ninety Seven being a person duly Licenced Let Horses to hire agreable to an Act of Parliament passed in the twenty fifth year of the Reign of his present Majesty enfield "An Act for repealing "the duties on Licenses taken out by persons Letting Horses for the "Purpose of travelling post and on Horses let to Hire for travelling "post and by time and on Stage Coaches and for granting other "Duties in Lien thereof and also additional Duties on Horses let "to hire for travelling post and by time" Did within the Kingdom of Great Britain to wit at the parish of Saint Martin in the Fields in this [...] Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex aforesaid let out for hire divers to wit two horses to draw a certain Carring with four wheels called a post & house by the Stage to be used and which were accordingly used in travelling post that is to say from the parish of Saint [...] Martin in the Field within the liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex aforesaid to Woolwich in the County of Kent and did deliver or Cause to be delivered a certain ticket called a Stamp Office ticket to the hirer of the said horses but Did Insert in the said Ticket a less Number of Miles than the number of Miles Charged to such hirer that is to say did insert in the said Ticket Nine Mills but did charge such hirer Twelve Mills for the said Stage whereby the said hirer was Compelled to and did pay for a greater Number of Mills than the Number of Mills Inserted in the said Ticket that

that is to say this said hires was Compelled to and did pay for Twelve miles for the said Stage Whereby Your Petitioner Conceives himself Aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore humbly begs leave to appeal against the said Conviction and that the same may be heard at the Present [---] General Quarter Session of the Peace more holden the Session House for the said County of Middlesex

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc.

Mr. Husband
