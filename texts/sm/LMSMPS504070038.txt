Middlesex To the Worshipfull Thomas lane Esqr and others his Fellow Justices Assigned to Keep the Peace Etc. At Hicks Hall in General Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Peticon of Thomas Camm a Pensioner of Chelsea Colledge in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That on the 9th day of September 1749. One Elizabeth Hall late of the Parish of St. Andrew Holborn in the County of Middx was bound an Apprentice to your Petr. by Indre bearing date the sd. 9th. day of September aforesd. for the space of Seven Years to learn the Art and Mystery of Mantua Making in which Art and Mystery Your petr. and all his have Endeavour'd to teach & Instruct her, Yet the said Elizabeth being quite regardless of their Tuition and her Duty of an Apprentice as aforesaid, And having for several Years past contracted a bad habit of Body from the Venereal Disease Insomuch that three Salivation's since last Christmas have proved ineffectual. And being from time to time since her said Apprenticeship found and Discover'd to be a great Pilferrer and Despoiler of Your Petrs. Goods & Chattells in his Dwelling house Your Petr. was Obliged to Support her in the work house at the rate of 2s.6d.P week for the Space of 3. Months last past; and which he being but a Pensioner as aforsd. is not able to do any longer.

Therefore most hbly Prays yor. Worships would be Pleased to Discharge him from his said Apprentice Or Make such Order in the Promisses as to Your Worships Shall seem meet And Poor Petr. as in Duty bound Shall ever Pray.

Thomas Camm
