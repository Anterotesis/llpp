To the Rt. Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Sessions Assembled

The Humble Peticon of John Horn of the Parish of Saint Martins in the Feilds in the County of Middlesex aforesaid Carpenter.

Sheweth. That Thomas Mawley Son of Mary Mawley of Richmond in the County of Surry Widow was by Indenture bearing Date the Eighth Day of July One Thousand Seven Hundred and Twenty Nine Bound an Apprentice to your Pet. for the Term of Seven Years and that yor. Petr. was (as by the sd. Indenture) to learn him the Art of a Carpenter and was to find him in sufficient meat Drink Washing Lodgeing and all other necessarys dureing the said Term Expect Cloaths in Cons of which yor. Pet. P received the Sume of Twenty one pounds of the Honble Charles Lanoe of Westmr: Esqr

That yor. Petrs. said Apprentice has been very neglectfull of his duty refused to work or learn his Trade absconded frequently and for several Months past from yor. Petrs. business and home and has also very much misused your Pet and evil intreated him and his family to the great loss and damage of your Pet. and this without any Just Cause

Yor Petr. therefore most Humbly prays that your Worships will be pleased to discharge him from the sd. Indre and Apprentice

And yor. Petr. shall ever pray Etc John Horne
