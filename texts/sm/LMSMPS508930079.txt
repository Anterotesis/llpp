To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex assembled at their General Quarter Session of the Peace begun and holden the 13th day of January in the 34th year of King George the third

To the humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex and Liberty of Westminster

Sheweth

That John Whitehead the younger the natural & lawful Son of John Whitehead the elder by Hannah his Wife being Poor [---] Insane and unable to work and maintain himself did on the 28th day of June in the year of our Lord 1791 become chargeable to the said parish of St Clement Danes and was received into the Workhouse belonging to the same

That the said John Whitehead the younger hath ever since been and still is insane unable to maintain himself and supported at the charge of the said parish of St Clement Danes and for the space of foure Months hath been kept at a private House at Floxton for the reception of Lunaties where he still is at the heavy expence of seven shillings per Week

That the said John Whitehead the elder the Pauper's Father is and during all the time the said Pauper hath been chargeable to the said parish hath been of

sufficient ability and competent circumstances to Maintain and provide for his said Son the said John Whitehead the Younger Yet he hath not contributed thereto altho' he hath been often requested by Your Petitioners so to do, but hath wholly neglected and refused to allow any thing in respect thereof whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore humbly pray your Worships to make such Order on the said John Whitehead the elder for the relief of your Petitioners in the premises as in your Wisdom and Judgment shall seem meet and that your Worships will be pleased to appoint same in this present Session for the hearing and determining the Matter of this their [---] Petitcon

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
