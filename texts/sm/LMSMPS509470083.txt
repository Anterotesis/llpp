To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Gregory by Saint Pauls in the City of London.

Sheweth

That by virtue of an Order of Removal or pass Warrant under the hands and seals of William Coleman and William Claxton Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the first day of January One Thousand seven hundred and ninety Eight Arthur Bradley was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Enfield in the County of Middlesex and delivered unto Your Petitioners being the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of saint Gregory by Saint Pauls the said Justices adjudging that the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Arthur Bradley was in the said Parish of Saint Gregory by Saint Pauls in the said City of London by which said Order of Removal or Pass Warrant Your Petitioner apprehend themselves to be aggrieved

Wm Chetham Solr For the Appellants Falcon Square

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said Order of Removal or Pass warrant and pray that the benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determination therefore may be adjourned until the next General Session of the peace to be holden for the said County.
