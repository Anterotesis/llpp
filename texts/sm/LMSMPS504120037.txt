To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in St. John Street in the said County at their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Stephen Walbrook in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Order under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing Date the 5th. Day of June 1751. Dorothy Trueman was removed from the Parish of Saint James within the said Liberty of Westminster in the County aforesd. to the said Parish of St. Stephen Walbrook as the last legal place of her Settlement By which your Petitioners humbly conceive themselves aggrieved and Appeal to this Court against the said Pass Order

Your Petrs. therefore Pray that your Worships will be pleased to hear and Determine the Matters touching this Appeal and give them such Relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem just and meet

And your Petrs. shall ever Pray Etc

William Ivatt of Walbrook Agent for the Appellants
