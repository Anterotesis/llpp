To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of William Husband of Cockspur Street in the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields [...] within the liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex Postmaster

Sheweth That by a Certain Conviction under the Hand and Seal of Henry James Pye Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the eights day of June now last past your Petitioner was Convicted in the Penalty of Ten pounds for that he after the first day of August 1785 to wit on the eighth day of April in the Year of our Lord 1797 being a Person duly Licensed to Let House to Hire agreable to an Act of Parliament passed in the Seventy Fifth year of the Reign of his present Majesty entitled "An Act for Repealing the "duties on Licenses taken out by Persons letting Houses for the "purpose of Travelling post and on Horses let to Hire for Travelling "post and by time and or stage Coaches and for granting other "duties in Liew thereof and also additional duties on Horses let "to hire for Travelling post and by time Did within the Kingdom of Great Britain to wit at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex aforesaid let out for Hire divers to wit two Horses to Draw a certain Carriage with four Wheels called a post Chair by the Stage to be used and which were accordingly used in Travelling post that is to say, from the parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex aforesaid to Woolwich in the County of Rent and did deliver or Cause to be delivered to the Fire of the said Horses a certain Ticket called a Stamp Office Ticket, but did insent in the said Ticket a less number of Mill than number of Miles

Mills charged to such Sliver that is to say, did insert in the said Ticket Nine Miles but did charge the said Sliver Twelve Miles for the said Stage whereby the said Sliver Compelled to and did pay for a greater Number of Miles than the number of Miles inserted in the said Ticket that is to say the said Sliver was Compelled to and did pay for Twelve Miles for the said Stage Whereby your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore humbly begs leave to Appeal against the said Conviction and that the same may be heard at the present General Quarter Session of the Peace now holden at the Sessions House for the said County of Middlesex

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc

W Husband
