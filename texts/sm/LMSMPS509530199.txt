To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by an Order bearing Date the Eleventh day of September in the year of Our Lord One thousand Seven Hundred and ninety eight under the hands and Seals of Robert Baker William Bleamire Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) Stating that Ann Haake the Wife of John Haake (who hath deserted her) and her Infant Daughter by the said John Haake namely Mary aged two years or there abouts came to Inhabit in the [---] Liberty of [---] Saffron Hill [---] Hatton Garden & Ely Rents in the said County of Middlesex not having gained any legal Settlement there nor produced any Certificate acknowledging herself to be settled else where and that they were become chargeable to the said Parish.

That the said Justices upon Examination of the Premises upon Oath and other Circumstances did adjudge the same to be true.

And did also adjudge the last legal Settlemt.

Settlement of the said Ann Haake and her said Infant Daughter Mary to be in the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex

And did Order that the said Ann Haake and here said Infant Daughter Mary to be removed from and out of the said [---] Lib [...] t of Saffon Will Hatton Gader & Ely Hent to your Petitioners said Parish of St. James Clerkenwell

Your Petitioners considering themselves aggrieved by the said Order of Removal do appeal against the same and Humbly pray your Worships will be pleased to take their Case into your Consideration And to make such Order in the Premises as shall be agreeable to Lan and as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

Rhodes Fry & Cook Vestry Clerks of Clerkenwell}
