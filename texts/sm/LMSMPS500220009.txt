To the Right Worll: their Maties: Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex

The Humble Peticon of Thomas Gillingham of the Pish of St Giles in the Feilds in the said County

Sheweth

That about Sixteen months since John Gillingham the Son of your Petr was put an Apprentice to one George Felster of the pish of St Martins in the Feilds for the terme of Seven Years to learn the Art of a Carver

That your Peticonr gave wth his son the Sume of Seven pounds in money & to find him Apparell dureing the said terme

But more may it please this Worshipfull Bench yor peticoner finds himselfe deceived the said George Felster not useing the Trade of a Carver as he alleadged for wth he Stands bound in this Honble: Court

[...] Your Peticonr therefore humbly prays (the prmises considered) that you would be pleased to grant your Order that the said George Felster may appeare in this Court at a day certaine to Shew cause why the said John Gillingham should not be discharged of his Indenture of Apprenticehood & placed wth Some other Pson useing the Trade of a Carver to serve the residue of his time

And yor. Peticonr shall ever pray Etc.
