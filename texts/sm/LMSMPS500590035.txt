To the Right Worll: his Maties: Justices of the Peace of the County of Midd

The humble Peticon of the Church wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Pish of Ealing in the said County

That upon hearing the appeale of the Church wardens & Overseers of the poor of the Pish of Basingstoke in the County of Southton against an Order made by two of his Maties: Justices of the peace of this County where by the Child of one Mary Goffe who dyed at Kensington was removed from thence to Bassing stoke This Court at the last guk Sessions of the peace held for this County die allow of the said Appeal And Ordered that the Said Child should be forgt at Kensington untill they pishoners should free themselves of the charge there of by due Course of Law

P B Tysons But Since that time the Sine Child is left & became Chargable to the Said Pish of Ealing by the Indirect menner of the Church wardens & other Officers of the Said Pish of Kensington (as your peter humbly conceive)

Your Petrs: there fore humbly pray the Justices considered that the Church wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Pish of Kensington [...] other Plies concerned may be ordered to attend this Court some day this Sessions to Shew Cause why they should not receive back & pvide for the Said Child according to the former Order of this Court

And go Petrs: Shall en pray Etc Josephs Parkers Churchwarden John: Anderson Overseer of ye poor
