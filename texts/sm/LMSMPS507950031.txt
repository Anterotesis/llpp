To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the county of Middx at the General Quarter Session of the peace holden in and for the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Peter in Brackles in the County of Northampton

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrt or order of removal dated the 10th day of December 1784 under the Hands and Seals of William Hyde and George Rec'd Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for ye County of middlesex (Quorum unns) upon the Complaint of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Luke Chelsea Susannah minill widow of John Minill aged about thirty nine years and her 5 Children (viz:) Mary aged 13 years Alice aged Thos. aged 8 and Jane aged 4 years and Ann aged lyear who had become chargeable to the said parish of St. Luke he Chelsea and whose Settlement the said [...] Justices did adjudge [...] to be in the said Parish of Brackley in the County of Northampton were removed to the said parish of Brackley

And that your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order

Yours Petitioner therefore humbly pray to appeal to this Court against the adjudication of the said two Justices and their said order and pray that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a day to hear & determine the said Appeal

And your Petitioner Will ever pray etc
