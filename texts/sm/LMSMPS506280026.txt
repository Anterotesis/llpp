To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their general Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Parish of Ivinghoe in the County of Bucks

Sheweth That by a Vagrant Pass under the Hand and Seal of George Stubbs Esquire One of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex bearing date the 21st. day of January 1773. Ann Shillingford the Widow of William Shillingford deceased with her four Children by her said late Husband, namely Thomas aged near 18. Years, Jane aged about 13. Years Mary aged about 9. Years and William aged about 3. Years and an half were Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Margaret in the said City of Westminster to the said Parish of Ivinghoe in the said County of Bucks as the last legal Settlement of the said Ann Shillingford and of her said four Children whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggreived and against the said Order do appeal

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray, That a day may be appointed in the present Session to hear and determine the Matter touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray
