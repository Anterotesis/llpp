To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Paddington in the said County

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of Hy. Pye and J. P. Andrews Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County and the City and Liberty of Westminster in the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty Seventh day of November one thousand Seven hundred and Ninety five Thomas Brown Elizabeth his Wife and their 4 Children Vizt. William aged near thirteen Years James aged eight Years Mary aged five Years and Ebenezer aged about one Month were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster in the said County to the said Parish of Paddington as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and Pray that Your Worships will be Pleased to Appoint a day in the Present Session for the hearing and Determining [---] the said Appeal

And your Petitioners will pray Etc

Herbt Robertson Solr: for the Appellts.}
