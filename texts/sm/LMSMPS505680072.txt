To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace or an Adjournment thereof assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate London Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Joseph Girdler and Stephen Clarke Esquires Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (One being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty first Day of October in the Year of our Lord One thousand Seven hundred and Sixty Six William Turvey was removed from the Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the said County to the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate London as the Place of his last legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners Conceive themselves Aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order and Humbly Appeal against the Same

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly pray that Your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day of this present Sessions that all Parties may attend to hear and abide by the Indgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Thos. Earl Sollr. for the Appellants
