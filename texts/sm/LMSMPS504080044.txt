To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Warden and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Dorking in the County of Surrey.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hand and Seal of Francis Bedwell Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the thirteenth day of November last, Jane Edwards Singlewoman was (as is therein Alledged) Apprehended in the Parish of Saint Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex as a Vagabond (Videlicet) Wandering and begging and by the said Pass, Warrant or Order was removed and Conveyed from the said Parish of Saint Clement Danes to the Parish of Dorking called in the said Warrant or Order the Parish of Darking in the County of Surrey as the Place of her last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners think themselves Aggreived by the Aforesaid Warrant or Order of the said Francis Bedwell Esquire and humbly Appeal against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them and that a day may be Appointed in this present Session of the Peace to hear and Determine the same And to grant Your Petitioners such other Relief in the Premisses as to Your Worships shall seem meet.

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Tho Benn[---] Attorney for the Appellants.
