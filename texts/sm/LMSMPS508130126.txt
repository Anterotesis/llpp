To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in for the County of Middlesex in their general Quarter Session of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwarden & Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of Saffron I Hill Hatton garden and Ely Rents in he Parish of St. Andrew Holborn in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one where of being of the Quorum) bearing date the first day of July instant the five legitimate Children of Thos. Trout, (then a prisoner in Newgate) to wit Robert aged fourteen years & upwards William aged nine years and upwards Edmund aged seven years and upwards Catherine aged four years and upwards and Thomas aged one year and upwards, were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to the said Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton garden and Ely Rents, as the place of their last legal Settlement; Whereby your Petitoners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore appeal against the said Order, and humbly pray Relief in the Premises, & that the benefit of their sd. Appeal may be saved into them, until the next General Session of the Peace to be taken in and for the said County of Middlesex, and that your Workships will be pleased to appoint some day in the said next Session for the hearing and determining this their said Appeal.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Cox Hatton Street, Soll. for Appellants.
