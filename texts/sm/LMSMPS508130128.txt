To the Right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George Southwark in the County of Surrey

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of R: Heavinde and F. Girdler Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum bearing date the 19th. day of July 1786 Amelia Oakshatt Singlewoman was removed from the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the Bars in the County of Middlesex unto the said parish of Saint George Southwark in the County of Surrey alledging the said Parish of Saint George Southwark to be the place of her last legal Settlement

That your petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved by the said Warrant or order of the said two Justices, do therefore appeal to your Worships therefore and humbly Pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the premises and order that the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Andrew Holborn above the Bars in the County of Middlesex may appear before you at a certain day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships touching the Appear

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Vincent Solicitor for the Appellants
