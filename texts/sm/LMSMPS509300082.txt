Middlesex to wit

To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County o Middlesex in Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Chipping Barnet in the Liberty of Saint Alban.

Sheweth

That Edward Marsh and Sarah his Wife and their six Children namely Edward aged about Eleven years George aged eight years and upwards Joseph aged five Years and upwards Rebecca aged about four years John aged two Years and upwards and also a female child aged about two months whose name is not at present known were by an order under the hands and Seals of C.J Kerby and I. Warner Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County bearing date the tenth day of August one thousand seven hundred and ninety six Removed to the said Parish of Chipping Barnet [...] from and out of the Parish of Friern Barnet in the County of Middlesex as being the place of their last legal Settlement

That the legal Settlement of the said Edward Marsh and Sarah his wife and their said Six children being in the said Parish of Friern Barnet by Reason that the said Edward Marsh did rent and Empty upwards of Forty days within the said Parish of Friern Barret Tenements of the yearly Value of Ten Pounds and hath not since gained any legal Settlement elsewhere

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray Your Worships that the said Order of Removal may be Quashed and that they the said Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Chipping Barnet may be Reimbursed the Costs and Expences that they have wrongfully in this case sustained pursuant to the Statute in that Cope made

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Cornt. Cope Josh Rodbard} Churchwardens

Isaac Newton John Kaye} Overseers
