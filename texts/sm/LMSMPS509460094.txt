To William Mainwaring Esquire and others His Fellows Justices of our Lord the King in and for the County of Middlesex.

The Humble Petition of James Crowder

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on the eighth Day of November last convicted by and before Patrick Colqu- houn Esqr. one of his Majesty's Justices of the County of Middlesex for that he did on the first day of November aforesaid play at a certain game under the Denomination of the game of Hazard and adjudged to pray the penalty of Â£50

That your Petitioner feels himself aggrieved by such judgement and submits to your worships he hath a good and substantial Defence to the same and humbly prays

Your Worships will permit your Petitioner to appeal against such conviction and that such Appeal may be heard on the next Day of Appeals.

And your Petitioner as in Duty bound will ever pray etc

James Crowder
