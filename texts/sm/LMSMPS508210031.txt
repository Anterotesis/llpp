To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of that part of the parish of Saint Andrew Holborn which lies above the Barsin the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or pass warrant under the hands and Seals of Sir Sampson Wright Knight and Thomas Gorden Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing date the 7th. Day of February last Ann Franklin wife of William Franklin and their two legitimate Children namely Ann aged near five years and Richard aged Two years and upwards were removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Mary le Strand in the said County to our said part of the said parish of Saint Andrew Holborn as the place of their last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said order or pass Warrant of the said two Justices appeal to your worships therefrom and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the premisses and order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Mary le Strand may appear before you at a certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of your Worships touching the said Appeal
