To the Rt. Worshipfull his Majts: Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx

The Humble Peticon of John Baker.

Sheweth.

That your Petr: about July 1755 was bound Apprentice to Capt. John Taylor of poplar in this County Marriner that your petrs. sailed about twenty Months with his said Master during wch. time he was taught nothing but only waited on his said Master as a Cabin Boy the your petrs. friends gave 20 Guineas to his said Master with yor. Petrs.

That your petrs: said Master has been dead about two years and his Mistress Elizabeth Taylor being uncapable to be learne him his buisiness refuseth to Turne him over and dees otherways very much misuse your petrs. for wch: she is bound by Recognizance to appeare in this Court this present Generall Sessions.

Yor: Petr: therefore humbly prays that your Worships would be pleased to Order that his said Mistress may attend this Court some day this present Sessions to shew cause why she should not turne him over to some Master to learne his Art or be discharged from his said Apprenticeship.

And yor. Petr. shall over pray Etc

John Baker
