To the Worshipful John Hawkins Esqr. and to the rest of the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the peace at Hicks Hall in St. John Street in and for the said County Assembled.

The humble Petition of Wm: Pittman

Sheweth That at the last General Quarter Session of the Peace hold for the County of Middlesex your petitioner was indicted and convicted for an Assault on one William Wood, for which Offence Your Petitioner was fined Â£20 by the Court, and committed to New Prison Clerkenwell till the said fine is paid.

That your Petitioner has been a Prisoner ever since the Tuesday in the last Session, being five Weeks all but one day during which time he has applied to several of his friends & acquaintance to assist him and endeavour to get him released from his present Unhappy situation but they cannot [...]

That your Petitioner has a Motherless Child to maintain by his own labour who must under go many hardships and be obliged to come to the Parish very soon if your Petitioner should be confined much longer, and your Petitioner will [...] be reduced to the greatest distress as the Prosecutor hath been offered Eight Guineas by a friend of your Petitioner, which hath been refused

Your Petitioner therefore relying on the Justices and humanity of this Court most humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to take his Case into consideration and reduce his fine to a Smaller Sum or grant him such other relief in the Premises as to this Worshipful Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioner as in Duly bound will ever pray Etc

May 8th 1769

William Pittman
