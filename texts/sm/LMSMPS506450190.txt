To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

[...] Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and [...] Poor of the Parish of Docking in the County of Norfolk

[...] by Virtue of a Vagrantor Pass Warrant under the [...] Wrighte one of [...] his Majesties Justies of the Peace [...] Middlesex bearing Date the Sixth Day of May [...] Malby in the Parish of Saint James within the Westminster and County of Middlesex was apprehended [...] and Vagabond begging Alms and was removed from [...] Parish of Docking aforesaid alledging the same to be the to the said Thomas Malbys last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Vagrant or Pass Warrant of the said Justices Do Appeal to your Worships therefrom and Humbly Pray

That your Worships will be Pleased to hear them in the Premisses and Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster may appear before you at a certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of of your Worships touching the appeal and your Petitioners shall ever Pray

H Elwin Sollicitor for Appellants
