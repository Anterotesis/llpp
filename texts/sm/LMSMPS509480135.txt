To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace at the Session House on Clerkenwell Green Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of The Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of The Hamlet of Ratcliff in the Parish of Saint Dunstan Stepney in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of The Reverend Herbert Mayo Doctor in Divinity and William Blackmore Esquire Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the Thirteenth day of February Instant John George and Virtuous his Wife were Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex to the said Hamlet of Ratcliff as the Counting Settlement of the said John George and Virtuous his wife Whereby Your Petitoners conceived themselves to be Aggrieved

And therefore they Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said Two Justices and the said Order And Pray that the benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them And that the bearing and determining thereof may be Adjorned Until the Next General Quarter Session of the peace to be holden for the said County And that Your Worships will be pleased to Order that the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint George And able Persons concerned do then attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination thereof touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

H Parnell Sollicitor for the Appellants Church Street Spitalfields 16th February 1798
