To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Present General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of William Oakley

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on the Twenty first Day of this Present Month of October convicted by Jacob Larne Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the Said County of Middlesex in the Sum of Two Pounds for having on the fourteenth day of the Said Month of October Caused permitted and suffered a Waggon (the fallier of the wheels being of less Breadth than Six Inches) to pass on the Turnpike read at Highgate in the Said County of Middlesex Which was drawn with more than four Horses.

That Your Petitioner Concerving himself aggrieved by the Said Conviction Bass Leave to appeal to your Worship against the Same

And humbly Prays Your Worships will that the Benefit of the Said Appeal may be Saved unto your Petitioner untill the next General Session of the Peace and that all Parties Concerned May have Notice to attend

And as in Duty Etc

Mr: Wade Jas Soll for the appellant
