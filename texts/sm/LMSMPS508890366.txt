To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in general Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Katherine Coleman in the City of London

Sheweth That by virtue of a certain order a Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of P Colquhoun and R Davies Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty Second day of October 1793 Joseph Sumner a Lunatic and his four Children Lawful issus (by Mary his Late Wife) namely Sarah aged Fifteen Years Ann aged thirteen years and upwards John aged ten years neither of whom had as alledged in the said over gained any Settlement in their own right and Joseph aged Six years were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Katherine Coleman in the City of London by the description of Saint Catherine Coleman Fenchurch Street London as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Joseph Sumner a Lunatic and four Children whereby your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said order or Pass Warrant and Pray that the benefit of the said Appeal May be saved unto them and that the hearing and determination thereof may be adjourned untill the next general Session of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County of Middlesex

W. L. Newman Sol for Appellants}
