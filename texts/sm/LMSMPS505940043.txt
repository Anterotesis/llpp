To the Right Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Stephen Walbrook in the City London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass order under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing Date the 21st. day of February 1769 Joseph Brown Ann his wife and their two Children Vizt. Nathaniel aged Two Years and half and Joseph aged one Year were removed from the Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the said County to the said Parish of Saint Stephen Walbrook as the last legal place of their Settlement By which Your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved and Appeal to this Court against the said Pass order

Your Petitioners therefore pray that your Worships will be pleased to hear and Determine the Matters touching this Appeal and give them such relief in the premisses as to your Worships shall seem Just and Meet

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

D Webb Agent for the Appellant }
