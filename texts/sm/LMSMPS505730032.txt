To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Richard Saunders late of Totteridge, but now of Shenley Hill in the bounty of Hertford Gentleman.

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of Edmund Byron, and John Brettell Esquires, Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 15th. day of June 1767, They the said Edmund Byron and John Brettell Did Adjudge him the said Richard Saunders to be the Putative Father of a Male Bastard Child begot on the Body of Jame Humfreys Singlewoman and born in the Workhouse in Grays Inn Lane belonging to that part of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holbron in the said County of Middlesex which lies above the Barrs And which said Child is baptized by the Name of William And Did Order and Appoint him the said Richard Saunders forthwith upon Notice of the aforesaid Order to Pay or cause to be Paid to the Overseers of the Poor of that part of the aforesaid Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn which lies above the Barrs or to some or me of them the sum of Six pounds of lawful Money of Great Britain by them Expended for the Relief and Maintenance of the said Male Bastard Child, and for the Charges of the said Jane Humfreys lying in and for other Charges and Expences Attending the same As also that he the said Richard Saunders should Weekly and every Week from and after the Date of the abovementioned Order Pay or cause to be paid to the said Overseers of the Poor of that part of the aforesaid Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn which lies above the Barrs or to some or one of them the sum of Two shillings and Sixpence of like lawful Money for and towards the Relief and Maintenance of the said Male Bastard Child for so long time as the same should remain Chargeable to the said Parish Whereby Your Petitioner thinks himself aggrieved, and Appeals to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioner therefore Prays this Court will be pleased to appoint a day for the Hearing and Determining of the said Appeal And that all persons concerned may then Attend.

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc Thos. Thomas Sollr. for Appellant.
