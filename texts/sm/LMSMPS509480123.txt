To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace at The Session house on Clerkenwell Green assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of The Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of The Hamlet of Ratcliff in the Parish of St Dunstan Stepney in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of John Staples Esquire and George Story Esquire Two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex bearing date the Twenty seventh day of February One Thousand seven hundred and Ninety Eight Mary Huntley the Wife of Thomas Huntley and their three Children namely Elizabeth aged fifteen Years Helen aged Eight Years and Eliza aged six Years or there abouts (neither of which Children have gained any Settlement in their own right as in the said Order is alledged) were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint George in the said County of Middlesex to the said Hamlet of Ratcliff as the lawful Settlement of them the said Mary Huntley and her said three Children Whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved

Therefore they Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said Two Justices and the said Order And Pray That Your Worships Will be pleased to appoint some day in this present Session for the hearing and determination of the said appeal And to Order That the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Saint George and all Persons concerned do then Attend this Court

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

H Sarnell Spitalfields Sollicitor for the Appellants
