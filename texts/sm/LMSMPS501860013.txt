To the Rt. Worspll. his Matys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx

The humble Peticon of Ann Wellbone Widow-

Sheweth

That Yor. Peticoner last Wednesday was unfortunately a little in Liquor and comeing by a person in the Strand Yor. petr. (being like to face) catcht hold of her gown in order to save herselfes from falling and did tare the same- Upon which your petioner was forced by the Mobb wch. was then there- into the River of Thames and was there almost drowned and was at the same time most truelly beaten and bruised.

That the Mobb after that carryed . her before a Justice of the Peace and was comitted to New Prison.

That Yor. petr is avery carefull and industrious woman and is aged almost- 70 years and never was in a gaol before

Yor. petr. therefore most humbly prays Yor. Worspps to be pleased to consider how extreamly she- suffred and to order that she may be discharged from her sd Corfinemt.

And Yor. petr. shall ever pray Etc.
