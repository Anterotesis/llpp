To the Right Worshipfulll his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their [---] Quarter Sessions assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of allhallows Barking London

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex One whereof being of the Quorum bearing Date the 4th: Day of Octr 1749 Durance Blinkman Widow of James Blinkman deceased was passed [---] from the parish of St: Giles in the Fields in the said County to the Parish of all hallow Barking London aforesaid alledging the last legall place of Settlement of the said Durance to be there And your Petrs conceiving themselves aggreived by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices Do humbly appeal therefrom and humbly pray your Worships

Martin Attoney for Barking Parish} Would appoint some Day this present Quarter Sessions of the peace to be holden for the said County to hear this appeal And That your Worships will make an Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields and all Parties concerned in and about the premisses may then attend hear and abide the Finall Determination of your Worships

And Your Petrs shall ever pray Etc
