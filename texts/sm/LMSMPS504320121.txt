To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall Assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James in Bury Saint Edmunds in the County of Suffolk.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the hand and Seal of Philip Dyot Esqr. one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the Eleventh day of September 1754 Mary Weale in the Said Warrant or Order called Mary Weale Widow of Edward Weale Decd with their Two Children in the Said Warrant or Order named George aged Four Years and Jane Aged Two Years were Removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the Said County of Middlesex to the Said Parish of Saint James in Bury St. Edmunds in the Said County of Suffolk as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the Said Mary Weale and her Said Two Children George and Jane in the Said Warrant or order named.

That Your Petitioners Apprehend themselves Aggrieved by the said Warrant or order of Removal.

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court against the same and Pray that this Court will appoint Some day in this Present Session for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Said Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields and all other Persons Concerned to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the Said Appeal.

And Your Petionrs. Etc.
