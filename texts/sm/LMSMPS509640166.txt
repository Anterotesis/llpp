To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County assembled.

The humble Petition of William Kirby Apprentice to Henry Sheldrake.

Sheweth, That your Petitioner was bound Apprentice for the Term of Six Years by Indentures bearing date the 28th. day of March 1795 to Henry Sheldrake of Southgate in the Parish of Edmonton in the County of Middlesex Collar and Harness Maker and that an Apprentice Fee of Ten Pounds was paid to the said Henry Sheldrake with your Petitioner that the said Henry Sheldrake hath frequently misused and evil entreated Your said Petitioner by unmercifully beating him keeping him up to very unseasonable hours and neglecting to instruct him in his said Trade.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly represents his grievances to this Court and earnestly solicits such Relief is the Premises as to your Wisdom shall seem meet.

And Your Petitioner will ever pray Etc.
