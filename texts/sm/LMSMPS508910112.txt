To His Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex

The Humble Petition of Peter Grove late of the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields in the said County of Middlesex but now of Woodford in the County of Essex Gentleman

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the fifth day of January 1791 pleased as an Apprentice with Evan Williams and Thomas Williams of the Strand in the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields aforesaid Booksellers and Stationers to learn their Art and Trade during the space or time of Seven Years from thence next ensuing

That your Petitioner hath served the said Evan Williams and Thomas Williams for the term of Two Years and eleven Months during which time he hath received much ill treatment from the said Evan Williams and Thomas Williams And that the said Evan Williams and Thomas Williams have neglected to teach and instruct Your Petitioner in such their Trade and calling And that Your Petitioner by reason of the want of such Instruction is thereby ignorans of the same or any part thereof

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays your Worships that he may be discharged from the Service of the said Evan Williams and Thomas Williams And that a proportionable may be returned to Your Petitioner or to such other person as Your Worships shall please to appoint In Trust for him Or that Your Worships would make such other Order in the Promises as to your Worships shall seem meet

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc.

P: Grove
