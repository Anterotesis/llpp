To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esqr. and The Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the peace assembled.

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Low Layton in the County of Essex.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seal of R Davies and John Sherwood Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Nineteenth Day of August one thousand seven hundred and eighty two Mary Blake a Child aged about Twelve Years and eight Months was removed from and out of the parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Low Layton in the County of Essex as the place of her last legal Settlement whereby Your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved.

Chetham Solr. for the Appellantt} Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said order or pass Warrant and pray that your Worship will be pleased to appoint a Day in this present Session for the hearing and determining thereof And that your Petitioners may have such other Relief in the premises as to Your Worships shall seem meet.

Middlesex. At the General Session of the peace of our Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at the session house in and for the said County on Monday the Ninth day of September in the Twenty second Year of the Reign of ever Sovereign Lord George the third King of Great Britain Etc

It is Ordered that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the County of Middlesex have Noticed of this Appeal and that they and all persons concerned do attend this Court on Thursday the 12th. Day of September instant to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

By the Court Selby
