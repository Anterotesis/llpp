To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled at their General Quarter Sessions

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Giles Camberwell in the County of Surrey

Sheweth That by an Order bearing Date the fourth day of October instant under the Hands and Seals of Danie William Esquire and Casten Rohde Esquire two of this Majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County of Middlesex by reciting that Complaint had been made unto them by the Overseers of the Poor of the Precinct of Saint Catherines That Mary [...] Medhourst the Widow of Edward Medhourst deceased lately intruded and came into the said Precinct of Saint Catherine and was become chargeable to the same The said Justices upon Examination of the Premises upon Oath and other Circumstances did adjudge the Place of the last [---] legal Settlement of the said Mary Medhourst to be in the Parish of Camberwell in the County of Surrey The said Justices did by the said Order require the Overseers of the Poor of the said Precinct of Saint Catherines to remove and Convey the said Mary Medhourst from and out of the said Precinct of Saint Catherine to the said Parish of Camberwell and her relieves unto the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor

there or to some or one of them together with that there Order on a true Copy thereof and the said Mary Mid to wit was removed and conveyed accordingly

Your Petitioners humbly concerned they have good pounds whereon to appeal against the said Order but from the shortness of time since the making the same Order they Lave not been able to Collect Evidence or can they be prepared to hear such appeal heard at the present Quarter Session

Your Petitioner therefore humbly pray that the said appeal may now be set down and the hearing thereof respited until the next Quarter Sessions

And Your Petitioner as in Duty bound will ever pray

23d. October 1798
