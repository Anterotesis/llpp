To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex At their Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition of Thomas Purden the Elder of Walthamstow in the County of Essex Gardner And of Thomas Purden the Younger Son of the said Thomas Purden

Sheweth That by Indenture bearing date the 28th. day of March 1792 the said Thomas Purden the Younger, did put himself Apprentice to Samuel Player of Tabernacle Square Old Street in the Parish of Saint Leonard in the County of Middlesex Carver and Gilder to learn his Art, and with him after the manner of an Apprentice to serve from the day of the date of the said Indenture, for and during the Term of Seven Years, And the said Master in Consideration of the Sum of Twenty one pounds paid to him by the said Thomas Purden the Elder the Receipt whereof he did thereby acknowledge his said Apprentice in the same Art did Agree to teach and Instruct. And to find unto the said Apprentice sufficient Meat Drink Washing and Lodging during the said Term, As by the said Indenture when referred to will appear

That the said Thomas Purden the Younger hath served near four Years of the said Term, And that for a Considerable time Past, the said Samuel Player, hath paid little or no Attention to his Apprentices, hath kept them from time to time Unemployed. And from the derangement of his Affairs frequently threatne'd to run away and leave them. And the said Samuel Player did actually on or about the 26th. day of November last, from such his Situation. Sell up his Effects, and leave the said Thomas Purden the Younger, and his other Apprentices to shift for themselves, without food or even a bed to lye on, or any him of necessarys whatever

That the Conduct of the said Samuel Player has been extremely Immoral; frequently coming home Intoxicated early in the day and

That it does not appear by any means likely (from the present Insolvent Situation of the said Samuel Player) that the said Thomas Burden will ever attain a Competent knowledge of his Art or Business.

Your Petition's therefore humbly pray Your Worships, that your Petitioner the said Thomas Burden the Younger may be discharged from the said Indentures And that the same may be cancelled and made Void, And that the said Samuel Player may be Ordered to pay back a Proportionable part of the Apprentice Fee given to him with the said Thomas Purden, And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
