To the Rt: Worsppll his Matys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx

The humble Peticon of William Wallis

Sheweth That by Indenture Dated the 14th Day of June Anno Dom 1714 one Henry Wallis a poor Boy of the Parish of Hornsey in this County was by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poore thereof putt and placed to your Petitr: to serve him untill he should accomplish his full Age of Twenty and One yeares That your Petitr: was by Agreement with the said Churchwardens to have five pounds with his said Apprentice But the Churchwardens did not pay Your Petitr: the sd Moneys at the Executing the said Indentures Alledging they had not then Moneys in their hands But promised him Payment soone after

That Yor. Petitr hath often Demanded the said Moneys But they refuse to pay him

Your Petitr therefore humbly prays your Worspps will be pleased to order the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor to attend this Court some day this Sessions to shew Cause why your petitr. should not be paid the said Five pounds or that He may be other wayes Relieved as to this Court Shall Seeme Meete

And Yor. Petitr shall Ever pray etc

Wm. Wallis
