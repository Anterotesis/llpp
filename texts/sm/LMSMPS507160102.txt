Middlesex To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Marylebone in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of John Cox and George Mercer Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the fourth day of August last past John a Child aged three Years and upwards was removed and conveyed from the Parish of Twickenham in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Marylebone as the place of his last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves Agrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices Do therefore Appeal to your Worships therefrom And humbly Pray that you will please to hear them in the Premises And Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Twickenham may appear before you at a certain Day to be appointed to hear and [...] abide the Judgement and determination of your Worships touching this Appeal

And your Petitioners shall Pray etc

Thos Martin-Solicitor for the Appellants
