To the Rt. Worship full his Maties Justices of the peace for County of Middx in their General Quarter Sessions of the peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeale of Walter Wood on the behalfe his Son Samuel Wood now apprentice to James Hely late of the parish of St. James Clerkenwell in this County

Sheweth That Your Petitioner about Three Quarters of a year aged Bound his son Apprentice to the said James Heley to learn the Art of a Gold Smith, but instead of instructing him in that art or the business of a Buckle maker which the said Heyley mostly follows he wholly Employ'd him in drawing path of Drink and lanicing the same oat to his Customers and that about Three Months agoe the said Heyley broke and went into the Mint and hath not only beat his said appren- tice in an inhumane mannor Several times but also hath not found him Comon Necessarys but hath Sent him home in alliserable Condition with the Hich and Your petitioner hath Endeavomed all that possibly he Could to gett him the said Heyley bound over to this present Sessions but by reason of his Sheltoring himselfe in the Mint he cannot obtain the Same

Therefore yor petitioner most humbly prays Your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day this present Sessions for the said Heyley to Attend this Court and all other parties Concerned to skew Cause why the [...] Saml Wood should not be discharged from his apprenticeship on that this Court will grant him such other whose in the promisses as may thought meet

And Yor. Petr Shall ever prays
