To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green on the day of May 1798.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Jeremiah Mangaar of Spitalfields Pawnbroker

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the 18th. day of May instant convicted in the Penalty of Five pounds by Cranley Thomas Kerby Esquire one of the Justices of the peace for the said County upon an Information exhibited and prosecuted by James Stallwood Charging that your Petitioner did in the parish of Christ Church in the said County on the eleventh day of May aforesaid take and receive of our John Smith on redeeming the pledge therein after mentioned the Sum of one penny by way of profit upon three Shillings theretofore to wit on the tenth day of May aforesaid lent upon a pledge that is to say A Waistcoat Contrary to the Statute Etc

That your Petitioner conceives himself agrieved by such Conviction and is advised to Appeal against the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this present General Session of the Peace in and for the said County And that your Worships will appoint a day for hearing and determing the same

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
