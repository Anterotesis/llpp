To the Rt. Worspll. his Matys Justices of the Peace for the County of Midds

The humble Peticon of James Calliday

Sheweth That in the Yeare 1694 This Court being Sattisfyed that yor. Petr. was a Person deserving to be Releived as a poore Maimed Souldier and Marriner of the County and to have a Yearly Pencon Assigned to be paid him out of the moneys raised & to be Raised for the Releife of the poore & [...] maimed Souldiers [---] of this County (haveing Served as a Marriner in the Royall Navy of England in Seuall Engagemts wherein he Lost One of his Eyes & Recd Seuall wound in his Body Your Worspps were then pleased to Order that your Petr. should be admitted as a Manned Souldier & Marriner of this County & Should be allowed Â£3 P ann reward his better Support which your Petr hath hither to recd But in Rec [...] d your Petr haveing now almost lost the sight of both his Eyes & being very ould & lame is uncapeable of getting a Livelywhood & being a Leivetenat. in the Royall Charles when he was wounded.

P Tho: Nash

Yor. Petr. therefore most Humbly prayes your Worspps to be pleased to Augment the sd. Pencon of 3l P ann unto Such Degree as unto your Worspps Shall Seem meet

And Yor. Petr. Shall Ever pray Etc. James Calladay
