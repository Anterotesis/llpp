To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church-Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Great Yeldham in the County of Essex

That by an Order bearing date the Twenty ninth Day of August in the Year of our Lord One Thousand Seven Hundred and Ninety eight under the Hands and Seals of William Kinnard and J Leroux Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) stating that Elizabeth Fuller with her illegitimate Child named William aged Twelve Months lately came to inhabit in the Parish of St: James at Clerkenwell not having gained any legal Settlement there nor produced any Certificate acknowledging herself to be settled elsewhere and that they were become chargeable to the said Parish That the said Justices upon Examination of the Premises upon Oath and other Circumstances did adjudge the same to be true and did also adjudge the last legal Settlement of the said Elizabeth Fuller to be in the Parish of Great Yeldham in the said County and did order the said Elizabeth Fuller and her

illegitimate Child named William to be removed from and out of the said parish of St. James at Clerkenwell to your Petitioners said Parish of Great Yeldham

Your Petitioners considering themselves aggrieved by the said order of Removal do appeal against the same and humbly pray your Worships will be pleased to take their Case into your Consideration and to make such Order in the Premises as shall be agreeable to Law and as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.

Francis Fairbank Solicitor for the Church Wardens and overseers of the poor of the parish of Great Yeldham
