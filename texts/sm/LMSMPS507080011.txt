To the Worshipfull John Hawkins Esgr. and the west of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition of James Wiley of Charter house lane in the parish of Saint Sepulchre in the County of Middlesex Whitesmith

Sheweth That by Indenture here unto annexed one John Young was bound Apprentice to your Petitioner for the true of Seven years therein mentioned That the said John Young hath served your Petition about Six years but has for some time past kept very bad company and been guilty of pilfeuing and is because incorrigible

That the said John Young has of late absented himself from your Petitioners service with Whores and Thieves and other dissolute persons in Black boy Alley and Sharps Alley.

That your Petitioner has several Servants who very much complain of your Petitions said Apprentices [...] Language and dissolute behaviour And your Petitioner has likewise a Son aged Fourteen years who works with him whose Morals are in great danger of being corrupted by his said Apprentice

So that your Petitioner cannot keep the said Apprentice any longer without the most dangerous Consequenaces to himself and his Family

Where fore he humble prays year Worships will appoint a day for hearing the Premisses and that the said Apprentice may be discharged from his said Apprenticeship.

And as in Duly bound Your Petitioner will ever pray Etc

James Wiley
