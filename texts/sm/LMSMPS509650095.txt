To the Right Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of Joseph East of Heath Row an Inhabitant of the Parish of Harmondsworth in the County of Middlesex against a Rate or assessment made for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish and entitled "An Assessment "or Rate made this 4th. day of November 1799 '' for the Relief of the Poor and other Purposes " mentioned relating to the Poor for the Parish " of Harmondsworth aforesaid at one shilling " in the Pound'

Sheweth That Thomas Jarvis, James Tillyer, William Randall and John Guyler and Divers other Persons Parishioners of the said Parish of Harmondsworth and Persons liable to be rated and assessed towards the relief of the Poor of the said Parish of Harmondsworth before and at the time of the making the said Rate or Assessment are greatly undeviated and assessed in the said Rate or Assessment for the Premises in their respective Occupations in the said Parish and that your Petitioner is over rated and assessed in the said Rate or Assessment for the several Premises in your

Petitioners Occupation in the said Parish and that the said Rate is in other respects partial unfair oppressive unjust and unequal

That the said Rate was on the day of last signed and allowed by Thomas Bishop and Edward Hillyard Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex

Your Petitioner therefore conceiving himself aggrieved by the said Rate or Assessment doth appeal to your Worships against the same and humbly prays your Worships well be pleased to hear him in the Premises and that the said Rate or Assessment may be amended or quashed as your Worships shall think fit And that your Worships will order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Harmondsworth and such other Persons as this appeal may concern do appeal before you at a certain day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships touching this Appeal

And your Petitioner as in Duty bound will ever pray Etc
