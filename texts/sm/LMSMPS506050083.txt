To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Sepulchre London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant the hands and Seals of Joseph Girdler Esquires and Stephen Clarke Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty fifth day of October One thousand seven hundred and Seventy Thomas Bayley Alice his Wife and their four Children namely Elizabeth Aged nine Years John aged [...] Years Sarah aged three Years and Ann [...] one Year and three months were removed from the parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Sepulchre London as the Place of the last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore thinking themselves aggrieved by the said Order of the said two Justices of the Peace Appeal to this Court against the same and humbly pray your Worships will be pleased to appoint some Day in the next General Quarter Session [...] hearing and Determining the said Appeal

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc

Chrs Sesthakes Sollr for the parish of St Sepulchre Londn.
