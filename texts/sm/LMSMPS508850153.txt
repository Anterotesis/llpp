To the Worshipful William Mainwarring Esqr and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition of John Ray one of the Constables of the Parish of Finchley in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth that your Petitioner at Easter 1792 was appointed Constable of the Parish aforesaid by a Court Leet then held for the Manner of Finchley at the Castle at Highgate And your Petitioner was sworn in and continued to execute such Office for above a Year

That at Easter last Thomas Taunton was chosen one of the Constables in the room of your Petitioner but in as much as the said Thomas Tainton appeared to be an old and inform Man and past the Age of Seventy your Petitioner hath been informed that the said Thomas Taunton hath [...] procused himself to be discharged from [...] being such Office And the Inhabitants a Court Leet have not perceeded to chuse any other person in your Petitioners slead

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays that as your Petitioner hath served longer than usual that he may be discharged from longer sewing the Office of Constable for the Parish of Finchley [...]

And your Petitioner shall ever pray

The Mark of [x] John Ray
