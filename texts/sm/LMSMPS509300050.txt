To His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex

The Humble Petition of George Ede of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the County of Middlesex Post master

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the Twenty fourth day of June last Past Convicted by John Spiller Esquire one of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County for that he did on the Twenty fourth day of May last at Hammersmith in the parish of Fulham in the Said County unlawfully keep use and Employ a Certain Carriage for the purpose of conveying passengers for hire to and from different places within the Kingdom of Great Britain to wit to and from Hammersmith to London in the said County without being first duly licenced agreable to an Act of parliament made in the Twenty fifth year of the reign of His present Majesty's Intitled "An act for repealing the Duties on " Licences taken out by Persons letting horses for the purpose of travelling " Post and on horses let to hire for travelling post and by time and on " Stage coaches and for granting other Duties in lieu there of, and also " additional Duties on horses let to hire for travelling post " and by Time "And Your Petitioner Conceiving himself aggrieved thereby Doth humbly pray to be permitted to appeal from Such Conviction And that you will please to Appoint a Day for that purpose

And your Petitioner Shall Ever pray Etc

George Ede
