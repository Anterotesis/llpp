To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace for the said County at the Sessions House on Clerkenwell Grew on the twelfth day of January 1795

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Bond of the Strand in the Parish of Saint Mary le Bond in the County of Middlesex Performer

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the 28th. day of November last Convicted by William Bleamire Esquire one of the Justices of the Peace for the said County in the Penalty of Five Pounds man Information Charging that your Peitioner did on the Seventh day of Novemebr last expose to Sale and Sell to one Catharine Stewart one Pounds of Hair Powder for the Price of Nine Pence Subject to the Stamp Duly of one penny without the Stamped Ticket by a certain Act of Parliament directed to be Affixed to the same being affixed and mastred and Stamped with the Stamp no by the said Act is directed and required contrary to the State

That Your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved such Committee and is advised to appeal against the same

[---] Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays your Worshipful will be pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this General Quarter Sessions of the Peace in and for the said County and that your Worships will appoint a day for hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
