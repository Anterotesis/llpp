To the Rt: Worsppful his Majties: Justices of the peace for the County of Midlx at their General [---] Sessions assembled.

11 May 1725

The Humble Peticon and Appeal of William Camden Apprentice to James Horseley of the Hamblet of Bethnal Green in the Parish of Stepney in the sd: County

Sheweth That yr. Petr. was by Indenture bearing date the Twenty third day of September Anno: Dom. 1723. bound Apprentice to the sd: James Horsely to Serve him the Terme of Seven Years to learn ye. Art of a Weaver

That yr. Petr. said [...] hath of [...] beat bruised & Misused yr. Petr. without any Lawful Occassion, in a very barbarous manner

That yr. Petrs. said Master stand bound by Recognizance to appear in this Honble Court this present Sessions to Answr. ye. premises

Yr. Petr: therefore most humbly prays that yr. Worspps would be pleased to Order that yr. Petr: may be discharged from by sd. Apprenticeship

And yr: Petr: shall ever Pray Etc.

William Camden
