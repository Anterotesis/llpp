To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at the General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of The Reverend Francis Willis Doctor of Physick Rector of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth. That by a rate or Assessment made for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish of St. John Wapping on the 28th. day of September last Your Petitioner was Assessed at the Sum of £5. per One Quarter of a Years Rate towards such Relief for and in respect of the Income of the Rectory of the said Parish

That the Income of the said Rectory Consists principally in the Sum of £130 a Year paid by Virtue and under the Direction of a certain Act of Parliament made in the [---] fifth and sixth Years of the Reign of King [---] William and Queen Mary Intituted An Act for [...] [---] a new Parish to be called the parish of Saint John of [---] Wapping within the County of Middlesex [---]

That your Petitioner or any of his Predecesson have never before been charged towards the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish for and in respect of the said Sum of £130.

That your Petitioner apprehends he is not liable to be Assessed towards the said Relief in any Sum of

Money wharsoever for and in respect of the said Sum of £130 and therefore Your Petitioner conceive himself to be aggrieved by the said Rate or Assessment and humbly appeals to your Worships against the same And

Prays such Relief in the premises as to your Worships seem meet

Chetham Solr. for the Appellant}
