Middlesex To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and others his Majesty's Justices of the Peace Assembled at the General Session of the Peace held at the Session House on Clerkenwell Green in and for the County of Middlesex on Monday the ninth Day of September in the thirty third year of the Reign of King George the Third.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of William Dean an Inhabitant of the Parish of Little Stanmore in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by a certain Rate on Assessment made for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish of Little Stanmore in the County of Middlesex on the first Day of August last your Petitioner and Many other Inhabitants and Occupiers of Houses and Lands in the said Parish are in and by such Rate or Assessment charged and assessed in several sum of Money for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish.

That at the time of Making the said Rate or Assessment William Stone and Joseph Stone were Occupiers of a certain Tract of Land in the said Parish of Little Stanmore Commonly called and known by the Name of the School Field, and liable to be charged and rated to the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish for such Land, but the said William Stone and Joseph Stone are not charged or rated in the said Rate or Assessment in any Sum of Money whatsoever as Occupiers of the said Land or for any Part there of.

That the Reverend Henry Poole Rector of the said Parish is not charged or rated in any sum of Money as to a Piece of Land or Garden Ground occupied by him within the said Parish of Little Stan more, although at the time of making the said Rate or Assessment the said Henry Poole was liable to be rated and assessed towards the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish as the Occupier of such Land or Garden Ground as aforesaid.

That James Minall is not charged or rated in any Sum of Money as the Occupier of a House and Piece of Land situate in the said Parish of Little Stanmore, although at the time of making

such Rate or Assessment the said James Minall was liable to be rated for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish as the Occupier of such House and Piece of Land.

That Richard Fitch, William Crate, Joseph Stone, and Thomas Patrick named in the said Rate or Assessment are materially under rated and not charged and Assessed agreeable to their Rents mentioned and inserted in the said Rate or Assessment

That for the Reasons above set forth Your Petitioner objects thereto, and humbly appeals against such Rate or Assessment and

Your Petitioner prays that the said Rate or Assessment may be quashed, or that your Petitioner may have such Relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.

Wm. Dean
