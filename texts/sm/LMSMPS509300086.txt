To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Ann Black friars in the City of London.

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Nathaniel Conant and Philip Neve Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the ninth day of May 1796. George Hook was removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the County of Midldesex and delivered to your Petitioners being the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Ann Black Friar's (the said Justices adjudging that the said Parish of Saint Ann Black Friars in the said City of London was the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said George Hook) Whereby Your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be Aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Order or Pass warrant and Pray Reliefin the Premises and that the Benefit of this their Appeal may be saved into them and that the hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County.

Wm. Chetham Solr: for the Appts.

Falcon Square.
