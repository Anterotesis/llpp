To the Worshipful His Majesty's of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Sessin of the Peace for the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldersgate in the City of London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of William Bleamire and David Dean Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the twelfth day of January One thousand seven hundred and Ninety six A female Child named Mary Richardson aged Seven Years or thereabouts was removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Sepulchre which heth in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Botolph Aldersgate in the City of London and delivered to your Petitioners being the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Botolph Aldersgate the said Justices adjudging that the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Richardson was in the said Parish of Saint Botolph Aldersgate.

That Your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved by the Order of the said two Justices and therefore your Petitioners appeal against the same and pray relief in the premises and

Your Petitioner also pray that the benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County of middlesex

Wm. Chetham Ses. for the Applts} Tale on Square
