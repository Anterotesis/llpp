To the Worshipful his majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace for the Said County Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of John Frears of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of westminster in the County of Middlesex Pawnbroker.

Sheweth That by a certain Conviction under the Hand and Seal of William Hyde Esqr. one of his majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the twenty ninth Day of august one thousand seven hundred and ninety one made on the information of one Thomas Sambridge your Petitioner was Convicted (as therein alledged) for that your Petitioner then using and exercising the Trade and Business of a Pawnbroker did in his Shop inmate Little Pulteney Street in the Parish of Saint James Westminster in the County of Middlesex on the fifteenth Day of July in the Year of our Lord one thousand Seven hundred and ninety one take and receive of and from one Launcelot Knowles by way of Pawn or Pledge a Rumnant of Cloth and did then and there advance and lend Thereon to the Said Launcelot Knowles a certain Sum of money exceeding five Shillings (to wit) the Sum of ten Shillings of lawful Money of Great Britain yet that your Petitioner being such Pawnbroker as aforesaid did not (as therein also alledged ) forthwith and before your petitioner advanced and lent the said Sum of Money upon thee aforesaid pawn or Pledge enter or cause to be entered in fair and regular manner in any Book or Books kept by your Petitioner for that purpose a description of the said Remnant of Cloth which your Petitioner or received in pawn or pledge and also the Sum of money to be advanced and last thereon with thee Day of the Month and year and the Name and place of abode of the person by whom the same was so pawned or pledged

and the Name and place of Abode of the Owner thereof according to the Information of the Person pawning or Pledging the same according to the statute in such case made and provided but that your Petitioner as therein also alledged then and there wholly neglected to make such Entry as aforesaid contrary to the Statute in such Case made and provided and the said Justice did adjudge your Petitioner to pay and forfeit for the Offence supposed to be Committed by your Petitioner the Sum of ten Pounds

That Your Petitioner thinks himself aggrieved by the Judgement of the said Justice and therefore

Chetham Solr. for the Appellant} Your Petitioner doth appeal to your Worships at this Present General Session of the Peace against the Judgement of the said Justice and doth pray such relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.
