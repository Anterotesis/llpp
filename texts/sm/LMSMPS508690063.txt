To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace For the County Middlesex Assembled at the General [---] Sessions of the Peace For the said County [...] at the Session House on Clerkenwell Green

The humble Petition and appeal of Richard Murthwaite of Oxford Street in the Parish of Saint Anns Soho in the County of Middlesex Pawn broker

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the 28th Day of November last Convicted in the Penalty of Five Pounds by William Hyde Esquire One of the Justices of the Peace For the said County upon an Information Stating That the same was Exhibited by William Rudd of Sommers Town in the Parish of Saint Pancras in the said County of Middlesex Painter against Your Petitioner Richard Murthwaite of Oxford Street in the Parish of Saint Ann Soho in the said County of Middlesex Pawnbroker For that Your Petitioner did on the 7th. Day of July in the Year of Our Lord 1791-at and in the Parish and County aforesaid in his Shop in Oxford Street in the Parish and County aforesaid Demand receive and take of and From one John Webb the Sum of Seven pence half penny of lawful Money of Great Britain as and for and by way of profit upon a loan of Six Shillings of like lawful Money being an Intermediate Sum between the Sum of two Shillings and Six pence and the Sum of Forty Shillings theretofore made by You the said Richard Murhtwaite to Wit on the Fifteenth Day of February in the Year 1791. aforesaid upon a certain Pawn or Pledge that is to Say Seventy Numbers to the said John Webb the said Sum of Seven pence half penny being more than at and after the date of Four pence For the Loan of Twenty Shillings by the calander Month including the current Month in which such Pawn or Pledge was redecmed aggrieved by such conviction

That Your Petitioner conceiving himself aggrieved by such conviction and being advised to appeal against the same

Your Petitioner most humbly prays Your Worships will be pleased to permit Him to Enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this present general Sessions of the Peace in and For the said County and that Your Worships with appoint a Day For hearing and Determining the same

And Your Petitioner will ever pray Etc Mr Murthwaite
