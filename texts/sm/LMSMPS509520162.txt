To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Quarter Session of the peace in and for the said County on Clerkenwell Green on the tenth day of September 1798

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Wilson.

Sheweth That in Consequence of a Summons issued under the hand and seal of Henry James Pye Esquire one of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County and served on Your Petitioner he appeared before the Magistrates at the Public Office in Queen Square Westminster Upon an Information exhibited and prosecuted by William Kettle Charging that your Petitioner did at the Parish of Saint Mary le Bone in the said County on the fourteenth day of June last take and receive of Martha Bunting on redeeming the Pledge therein mentioned One Penny by way of Profit upon three shillings on the fourth day of June last lent upon a pledge of Two Handkerchiefs Contrary to the Statute And your Petitioner was upon the hearing of the said Information Convicted in the penalty of Five pounds.

That your Petitioner conceives himself agrieved by such Conviction and is advised to appeal against the Same.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the Same may be heard at this Present General Quarter Session of the Peace in and for the said County And that your Worships will appoint a Day for hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc
