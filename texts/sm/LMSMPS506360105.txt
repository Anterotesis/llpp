To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Sepulchre in the City of London

Sheweth

That by an Order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Charles Dead and Stop Clarke Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (and one of them of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty sixth day of October in the year of our Lord 1773 Marg Woodworth was removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Sepulchre in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Sepulchre in the City of London in the place of her Settlement

Your Petitioners thinking themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant by the said two Justices Appeal to this Court against the same and humbly pray that the benefit of the said Appeal may be saved to your Petitioners and that [---] [...] day may be appointed in this present Session for the hearing and determining thereof [---]

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc

R. Brewer Attorney for Appeal
