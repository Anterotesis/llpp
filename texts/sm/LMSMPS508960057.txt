To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of Jasper Pollecut of Old Brentford in the said County Cordwainer

Sheweth That by Virtue of a certain Adjudication or Conviction under the hand and Seal of Nathaniel Isand Esquire one of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County bearing date the twenty Sixth day of April 1794 the said Justice Convicted your Petitioner in the Penal Sum of Ten Pounds for that your Petitioner did on the 12th. day of April 1794 at the Parish of Caling in the said County of Middlesex unlawfully refuse to Account for or deliver to William Clarke of the Parish of Saint Mary Islington in the said County Gentleman the Sum of Five Pounds said to be due to and directed to be paid to him in and by virtue of a Warrant of Distress Issued under the hand and Seal of the said Nathaniel Island Esquire and levied by the said Jasper Pollecutt (then constable of the said Parish) pursuant to the said Warrant (directed to him for that purpose) on the Goods and Chattles of [---] James Davies of Old Brentford in the Parish of Ealing in the said County Victualler (for the Offence therein stated by him to have been Committed

That your Petitioner Conceives himself to be Aggrieved by the said Adjudication and Conviction in as much that he your Petitioner did not on the said 12th. day of April 1794 unlawfully refuse to Account for or deliver to William Clark of the Parish of Saint Mary Islington in the said County of Middlesex Gentleman the said Sum of Five Pounds said to be due to & directed to so paid to him as aforesaid

as in the said Adjudication or conviction is Alledged and set forth

That your Petitioner Conceives himself to be further Aggrieved in the premises inasmuch that no such person as William Clarke described in the said Warrant of Distress ever made any demand whatever of the said Jasper Pollemtt for Payment of the said Five Pounds so directed to be paid as aforesaid and in as much that your Petitioner is Advised the Proceedings against him by the said Nathaniel Bland Esquire for the aforesaid Alledged Offence are in other respects Crroneous Irregular and Informal

Your Petitioner therefore Appeals to Your Worships against the said Adjudication or Conviction and humbly prays Relief in the premises And that Your Worships will be pleased to Appoint some day in the present Session for the hearing of this his Appeal

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc

Jasper Pollocutt
