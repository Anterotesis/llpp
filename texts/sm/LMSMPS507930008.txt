To the Worshipful William Manwaring Esquire Chairman and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in the Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of Richard Sacheverell of Southampton Building in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn Book binder

Sheweth That by Indenture of Apprentiship bearing date the 15th. day of July 1782 Thomas Harden a Poor Boy of the Parish of Saint Giles Reading in the County of Bucks became bound by the Said Indenture for the term of 7 Years to learn the Art of a Bookbinder.

That in Month of July 1783 the Petitioner from the disorderly behaviour of the Said Thomas Harding his Said apprentice was forced to Complain to the Magistrates who Committed him to Bridewell but out of Humanity and from the Persuasion of his friend he received them back in to his House and forgave him

That Since he was received Back into his House he hath behaved in the most outrageous manner refusing to do his Lawfull Business and Several times Stricking the Petitioners Wife

That at the General Session of the Peace held for the County of Middlesex in July last he was Convicted of Stealing a Handkerchief and underwent a Punishment for the same Crime he hath Since been Committed to Bridewell for assaulting Petitioners Journeyman and attempting the Life of Petitioners Wife.

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays that your worships will be pleased to release him from his said Apprentice or grant him such relief in the Premises as to your worships shall Seem meet. And your Petitioner will for ever pray Etc.

Rd. Sacheverell
