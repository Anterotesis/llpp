Middlesex To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of William Palmer an Apprentice to John Stephens

Sheweth That by Indentures of Apprenticeship bearing date the 9th. day of August 1793. Your Petitioner was found Apprentice for Seven years to John Stephens of Streatham Street in the Parish of Saint George Bloomsbury in the County of Middlesex Plater and Founder and in Consideration of £20. paid to him by your Petitioners Rather he Covenanted to reach and Instruct your Petitioner in the set of a Plater and Founder which he followed as by the said Indenture will appear,

That the said John Stephens hath divers times behaved towards your Petitioner with great bruetly by Violently Assaulting and Beating him without any just Cause and otherwise Ill treated him by refusing and Neglecting to find and provide him with necessary food and by Neglecting to Reach him his Trade

That the said John Stephens having on the 20th. day of June Instant Violently and Maliciously Assaulted Beat and Bruised your Petitioner in and upon his head Neck Shoulders and divers parts of his Body with a Stick without any Cause or Provocation whatsoever on your Petitioners

Petitioners part your Petitioner applied to Nicholas Bond Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex for Protection who thereupon caused the said John Stephens to come before him to answer the Complaint and upon hearing the said Complaint the said Justice ordered and directed the said John Stephen, to find duration to answer the same at the present Session and advised your Petitioner to apply to Your Worships for Redress and directed your Petitioners Father to take him under his protection until the hearing and determining of the said Complaint

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays that your Worships will take his Care into Consideration and discharge him of and from his Apprenticeship to the said John Stephens so that he may be able to Bind himself to another Master for the remainder of the said tern And that your Worships will be pleased to Order the said John Stephens to pay back to your Petitioner such Proportion of the said Sum of £20 given with him as an Apprentice Fee to the said John Stephens as in your Worships Wisdom shall seem Meet

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc

William Palmer
