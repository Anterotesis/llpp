To the Rt. Worpll. her Majts: Justices of the Peace at their Sessions at Hix's Hall

The humble Peticon of Richard Sutton of Finchley in the County of Middx

Sheweth That Your Petr. was on the Eight of Aprill instant 1708. at a Court field for the Mannor of Finchley chosen Constable for East End in the said Parish.

That Your Petr. Served the said Office above Twenty Years agoe, in the Parish of St. Dunstan in the West London.

That he is now incapable of Serving the said Office by reason of his Age, being above Sixty Eight.

That Your Worps at a Session in this Court thought him incapable at Christmas last by reason of his Age, and Infirmity to discharge the Office of Surveyor of the Highwayes, To which he was then Chosen. and Your Petr. is now much more infirme, than he was then, Haveing been above a Fortnight Sick and weak. and under a great Indisposicon of body.

All which did appear by Affidavitts at Westmr.

Your Petr. for the Reasons aforesaid most humbly prayes this Honoble: Court to grant him a Discharge from Serving the said Office; which it is impossible for him to Execute.

And Your Petr. as in duty bound shall ever pray Etc.
