To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace at Hickes's Hall Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Chuchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of George Errington and Gerrard Howard Esqrs: Two of His Majsestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 28th day of February 1769 Philip White aged about ten years was removed and conveyed from the Parish of Hampstead in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Luke in the said County of Middlesex as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Philip White.

Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order of the said two Justices therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the same And pray that this Court will be pleased to appoint a day in this present Session to hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever prey Etc. Etc. Etc.

J:Jenings attorney for the Appellants Bunhill Row. 3d April 1769
