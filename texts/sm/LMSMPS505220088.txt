To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church- Warden and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Luddesdown in the County of Kent

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removed under the Hands and Seals of Walter Berry and John Shakespear Esquires Two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Eighth Day of March last Eleanor Brookes aged about Seven years and Six Months a Bastard born of the Body of Mary Brookes Since deceased was removed from the Hamlett of Ratcliffe in the parish of Stepney in the County of Middlesex to the said parish of Luddesdown as the Place of her last legal Settlement whereby Your Petitioners do conceive them selves aggrieved

John Percival} Churchwarden Thos: Sanders Thos: Stiles} Overseers Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray Relief in the Promises And that your Worships will be pleased to make an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Hamlett of Ratcliffe in the said Parish of Stepney and all Persons concerned may attend the Court some Day in these prevent Session to be appointed by your Worship to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the Court in the Premises.

And Your Petitioners Shall ever pray Etc.
