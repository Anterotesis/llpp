To the Worshipful his Majestys Justice of the peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Exchange in the City of London

Sheweth

That by an Order or pass warrant bearing date the twenty sixth day of August 1789 under the hands and sales of Philip Dyot and David Walker Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the said County (One whereof being of the Quorum) whereby William King Ann his Wife and their two children Vizt. Catherine aged about eleven years and William aged about ton years were removed from the Parish of Saint Clement Danes in the said County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Exchange as the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners apprehending themselves aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant of the said two Justices humbly Appeal against the same

And therefore humbly pray that your Worships will be pleased to Order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the parish of Saint Clement Dane aforesaid and all persons concerned to attend the Court some day this present Session to hear and abide the Judgement of this Court touching the premisses and that your Petitioners may have such relief therein as to your Worships may seem meet.
