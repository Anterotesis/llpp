To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of William Fitts of the Parish of Christ Church in the said County of Middlesex Fruiterer

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of John Tall and Boulton Mainwaring Esqrs. Two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said County one being of the Quorum bearing Date the fourth Day of September One Thousand Seven Hundred and Forty Seven Your Petitioner Stands Adjudged the Father of a Male Bastard Child Born of the Body of Catherine Nicholls[---] Widow in the said parish of Christ Church in the said County [---] on the Twentyeth day of March last past.

That the said Order was Served on Your Petitioner on the fifth Day of the said Month of September[---] the same recites the Birth of the said Male Bastard Child to be on the Twentieth day of March last past) Your Petitioner never was Charged with begetting the said Child nor is he now, nor ever was under any Recognizance whatsoever for the same by which Order yor. Petr. is required to pay Several Sums of Money therein Mentioned

That [...] Your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by the said Order of the said two Justices, [...] gnd humbly Appeals to this Court against the Same and prays the benefit of his Appeal may be saved to him and that a day may be appointed in the next General Quarter Session of the peace to be holden for this County for Vacating the said Order and that all parties concerned may be ordered to Attend

And Your Petitioner as in Duty bound shall Ever paay Etc.

The Mark of William W Fitts
