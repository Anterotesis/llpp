To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of the Middlesex in their Michaelmas Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled 1773.

The Humble Petition of Joseph Maund Churchwarden, Chauncey Davis and Thomas Rees Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary in to the Town and County of Haverfordwest on behalf of themselves and the Rest of the Parishioners of the said Parish.

[---]

That Mary Griffiths of the said Parish of Saint Mary in the Town and County of Haverford West South Wales Widow is a Poor Old and Impotent person being 70 years of Age and not able to Work or get her Livelihood by Labour or otherwise and is now actually Chargeable and relieved by the said Parish

That John Griffiths of New Street in the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden in the County of Middlesex Tinman and Brazier the true Lawful Son of the said Mary Griffiths is a person in good Circumstances and of sufficient ability to Maintain the said Mary Griffiths his said Mother.

Therefore your Petitioners humbly pray your Worships would make such Order for the said John Griffiths to relieve and maintain the said Mary Griffiths his said Mother as to your Worships shall seem meet and also to pay the Costs of this application

And your Petrs. shall ever Pray Etc.

Joseph Maund Chauncey Davies Thos. Rees
