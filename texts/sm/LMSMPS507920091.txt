To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex at the General Quarter Session of the peace holden in and for the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Graves of the parish of Saint John & Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex & Carpenter

Sheweth That by a certain Rate or Assessment made on Your Petitioner and other Inhabitants of the parish of Saint John Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex by the Commissioners appointed for putting in Execution an Act of parliament made in the Eleventh year of the Reign of His present Majesty King George the Third Intituled "An Act for repairing "cleansing lighting and watching the Streets Squares Lanes and other "passages within the parish of Saint John Clerkenwell in the "County of Middlesex and for removing Obstructions and Annoyances "therein" for one whole Year commencing for Midsummer Day One thousand seven hundred and eighty four your petitioner is rated and charged at the Sum of Twenty five pounds as and for the yearly Rent of a certain Messuage or Tenement and premises in the occupation of your petitioner situate in Red Lyon street in the said Parish of Saint John Clerkenwell Whereas your petitioner ought not to have been rated or charged in the said Rate or Assessment at any greater Rent than the yearly Rent of Sixteen pounds ten Shillings for and in respect of the said Messuage or Tenement and premises being the Rent your petitioner now pays and has paid from Lady Day one thousand Seven hundred and eighty two for the said Messuage or Tenement and premises by virtue of an Indenture of Lease made to your petitioner thereof.

That your petitioner is rated and charged in and by the

said Rate or Assessment much more than several other persons Inhabiting in the said Parish in Proportion to the Yearly Rent of the Premises they respectively occupy in the said Parish are charged in and by the said Rate or Assessment and in particular your Petitioner is charged much more than John Brown Thomas playter Philip Booth William Clare Thomas Wallis John Crouch and Benjamin Mountique are severally Charged in and by the said Rate or Assessment in proportion to the yearly Rents of the several and respective premises for which they and your & Petitioner are severally and respectively charged in the said Rate or Assessment.

That your Petitioner Apprehends that the said Rate or Assessment is in many respects partial and unequal and your Petitioner thinking himself aggrieved by the said Rate or Assessment doth humbly appeal to Your Worships against the same and

Your Petitioner prays such Relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.

John Graves
