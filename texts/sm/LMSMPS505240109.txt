To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace of the County of Middx at their general quarter session of the peace holden at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in and for the County of Middx assembled

The humble petition and Appeal of the Church-Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Tottenham in the County of Middx

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant or Order under the hands and seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middx aforesaid (one whereof being of the Quorum) Bearing Date the ninth Day of June 1763 Ann Marsh Singlewoman was removed from and out of the parish of Saint Luke in the County aforesaid to the said parish of Tottenham as the peace of her last legal Settlement

Your petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices of the peace Do therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the same and pray that this Court will appoint a Day in this present general quarter session of the peace for the hearing and Determining of the said Appeal And that the Church-Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Saint Luke and all other persons concerned may then attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

James White, Overseer of the Poor of the said Parish of Tottenham
