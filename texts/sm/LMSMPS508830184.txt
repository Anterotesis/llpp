To His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Court of General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex on behalf of themselves & other the Inhabitants of said Parish

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of John Staples Esqr and John Spiller Esqr two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex bearing date the 5th day of March 1793 one John an Infant Aged about 6 Months the Illegitimate Child of Michael Callahan & Ruth Smith Single woman was Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint George in the said County of Middlesex as the Lawful Settlement of him the said John the Infant

That by Virtue of such Pass Warrant or Order of Removal the said John the Infant has become and is now chargeable to the Parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex.

That your Petitioners caused Notice to be served of their present Petition and Appeal on one of the Church Wardens and also on one of the Overseers of the said Parish of Saint John of Wapping on the 4th day of April 1793

Your Petitioners therefore conceiving themselves aggrieved by such Pass Warrant or Order of Removal humbly Appeal to this Court against the same And Pray Your Worships to appoint

appoint a day for the hearing & determining said Appeal and that such relief may be given and granted to your Petitioners in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem Just and reasonable

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc
