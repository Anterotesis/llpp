To Sir John Hawkins Knight and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition of Joseph Laver one of the Officers of the Sheriff of Middlesex

Sheweth That Your Petitioner is informed he was fined Forty Shillings for departing the Court Yesterday without Licence

That Your Petitioner has been appointed [...] to his Office very lately and this being the first Session he was ever ordered to attend this Court departed Yesterday without leave of this Court intirely through ignorance and not with intent to neglect any part of his duly

That Your Petitioner is extreamly Sorry at meeting with the dis pleasure of this Honourable Court and will make it his utmost Study in future that no such neglect shall happen

Your Petitioner humbly begs pardon of this Honourable Court for the above neglect and prays Your Worships will be pleased to Spare the said fine

And Your Petitioner as in duly bound will every pray Etc.

Sworn in Court 12th Decr 1778 }

W.

Joseph Laver

Reced DH 2s.
