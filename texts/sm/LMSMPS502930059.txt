To the Right Worshipfull His Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex.

The Humble Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Peter le poor London

Sheweth That by an Order dated the Eighth day of May One them sand Seven hundred and thirty three Under the hands and Seals of Thomas Pindar and Robert Tothill Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (Whereof One is of the Quorum By which said Order Elloner Gerry Wife of Peter Gerry and her two children Peter aged between four and five and Elizabeth aged about one year were passed from the Parish of Saint Giles without Criplegate in the said County to the said Parish of Saint Peter le Poor as the Place of their last legall Setlement

That the said Chirchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Peter le Poor Conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order and Pray an Appeal to this Honourable Court

Your Petitioners humbly pray the Court to appoint a day for hearing the said Appeale against the said Order.

Middx At the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace four Sovereign Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the County aforesaid [---] on Monday the twenty fifth day of June in the Seventh Year of the reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Second of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith

It is Ordered by this Court that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate in this County have notice of this Appeal, and that they and all persons concerned do attend this Court on Friday next at ten of the Clock in the forenoon To hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court tone bing the said Appeale

By the Court Walter
