To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the [...] County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peae assembled-

The Humble Petition & Appeal of the Churwarden & Overseers of the Hamlet of Gosford in the Parish of Kidling in the Court of Oxford-

Sheweth.

That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of N. W. Mortimer & Richard Holbrook Esquires 2 of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex (our being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 12th. day of April last John Graves & Elizabeth his [...] Wife were inte [...] to be removed from the Parish of Saint Pancras in the County of Middlesex to the said Hamlet of Gosford as the place of their last legal Settlement.

That by an Indorsement on the back of the said Order [...] Dated the 12th. day of April last the Magistrates suspended the Execution of the said Order till the said Elizabeth could be safely removed she then not being in a State of Health to be Removed.

That by another Indorsement dated the 7th. day of June last the said Magistrates permitted the said Paupers told removed & directed the Churchwardens & overseers of the said Hamlet of Gosford to pay to the said Churchwardens & Overseers of Saint Pan [...] Â£2..5..0 being the Charges proved on Oath to have been in [...] by the Suspension of the said order-

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggreived by the said Warrant or order do humbly Appeal against the same & pray that your Worships will be Pleased to appoint a Day in this present Sessions that Parties concerned may Attend to hear & abide the Judgment & Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Thos. Hy. Saunton Solicitor for the Appellants.

Middlesex (ss) At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex at the Session House for the said County on Monday the 28th. day of October in the fortieth Year of the reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third King of Great Britain Etc.

It is Ordered by the Court that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint Pancras have Notice of the within Appeal & that they and all Persons concerned may attend the Court on Thursday next the thirty frist Instant at the Hour of nine [...] in the fore noon of the same day to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the Court touching the said Appeal.

By the Court Selby.
