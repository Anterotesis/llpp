To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Catherine Coleman in London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hand and Seals of George Garrett and Thomas Smith Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Eighteenth Day of June One Thousand seven hundred and Sixty five Thomas Baker and Elizabeth his wife were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of St. Catherine Coleman as the place of their last legal Settlement

your Petitioners conceiving themselves Aggrieved thereby Do humbly Appeal against the same and pray that the hearing and Determining of the said Appeal may be Adjourn [...] untill the next Session of the Peace to be held in and for the said County and that the Court will be pleased to Appoint a Day in the said next Session for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Saint Mary White Chapel and all others concerned to Attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal.

Fras. Salt Attry for Appellants
