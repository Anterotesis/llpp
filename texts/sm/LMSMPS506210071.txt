To the worshipfull John Hawkins Esqr. and other his Follows Justices of the peace now sitting at Hicks Hall in and for the County of Middlesex

The Humble Petition of John Shea an apprentice

Sheweth That your Petitioner was by and with the Consent of his mother Judith Shea by Indenture tearing date the Twenty Sixth day of November One Thousand seven hundred and Sixty Six bound an Apprentice unto John Marsh of Plough Court in the parish of Saint Clemens Danes in the County of Middlesex Cutler for the Term of Seven Years And That your Petitioners Master in Consideration of the Sum of Twelve Pounds paid by your said Petitioners Mother Judith then was to Instruct Your said Petitioner in the Art or Trade and also to Find your said Petitioner in sufficient Meat Drink and Lodging during the said Term

Saith That your Petitioners Master the said John Marsh abt. Twelve Months ago lost his Habitation and Family without Leaving any support for your said Petitioner or any person to Learn him his Trade and that your Petitioner has since been assisted by his Mother

Saith That sometime ago your Petitioner was Informed that his said Master was gone to Guildford in the County of Surry to reside upon which your Petitioner went to the said Town of Guildford and there Inquired after the said John Marsh his said Master of several Persons but Coud not hear any Thing of the said John Marsh in the said Town

Therefore Your Petitioner most humby prays your Worships taking the Premises into your Consideration to order your Petitioners Masters Wife to attend this Honourable Court on Friday next at this present Sessions to thus Cause why your [...] said Petition

Shoud not be discharged from his apprenticeship

And your Petitioner as in duty bound shall Over pray Etc

John Shea

Middx At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the said County (by Adjournment) on Thursday the Sixteenth day of July in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our sovereign Lord George the Third King of Great Britain Etc

It is Ordered that the within mentioned John Marsh have Notice of this Petition and that he and all Persons Concerned Do Attend this Court on Thursday next the Twenty Third day of July instant at Nine of the Clock in the forenoon of the same day to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of the said Court touching the said Petition

By the Court Butler
