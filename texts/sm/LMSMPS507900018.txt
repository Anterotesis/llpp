To the Worshipful his majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled at the general session of the peace of our lord the king holden in and for the county aforesaid at the Session house Clerkenwell on Monday the sixth day of December 1784.

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Swanbourne in the County of Bucks

Sheweth That by virtue of an order or warrant of Removed under the hands and Seals of Mr Addington and Thos, Gordon Esqrs. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the county of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 15th. day of October 1784 Susannah Minill Widow and her five children (Viz) Mary aged thirteen Alice aged Eleven, Thomas aged Eight Jane aged four and Ann aged One years were removed from the parish of Saint Luke Chelsea in the said County of Middlesex to the parish of Swanbourne aforesaid as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therfore humbly Pray that your Worships will receive this appeal from and against the said warrant of removal and order that the same be heard and determined this present Session.

And your Petitioners shall Ever Pray Etc

Joseph Burnham Solicitor for the said Appellants

Dated [...] 6th. Decr. 1784
