To His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of James Mercer of West Drayton in the County of Middlesex Gamekeeper

Sheweth That by a Distress Warrant under the Hand and Seal of Thomas Bishop Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex bearing date the 20th. day of March now last post it appeared that your Petitioner was that lay Convicted before the said Thomas Bishop upon the Oaths of John Portlock William Booth George Morton and William Wortley For having on the Morning of the Sixth of the said then Month of March in the Night of the said Day used a Not for the Destruction of the Game by Virtue whereof your Petitioner Forfeited the Sum of Twenty Pounds to be distributed as in the said Distress Warrant is mentioned (Viz) one half of the said Penalty to John Newton of the Parish of Bedfort in the said County of Middlesex Labourer (who informed the said thomas Bishop of the said Offence) and the other half of the said Penalty to the Overseers of the Poor of Hillingdon in the said County of Middlesex

where the Offence was Committed for the use of the Poor of the said Parish Whereby your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore Humbly Appeals to this Court against the said Conviction and prays that you will be pleased to appoint a Day in this Present Session for the Hearing and Determining the said Appeal

Rd: Walford Solicitor for the Appellant April 29th.. 1794 }

James Mercer
