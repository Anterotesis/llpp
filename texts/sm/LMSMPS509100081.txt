To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of A. Graham and Rupert Clarke Esquires. Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the second Day of March Instant Ann Harrison was removed and conveyed from and Act of the Parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West in the City of London as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Ann Harrison.

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order Do humbly Appeal against the same and pray the Court that the benefit of the said Appeal be saved unto them until the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex and that they and all Persons concerned may then attend to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

And Your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc
