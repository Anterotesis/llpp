To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of Thomas Rogers

Sheweth That your Petitioner hath surveyed Carpenters Work done by Mr Joseph Wood in the repair of New Prison and Clerkenwell Bridewell to the amount of £658.0.2¾ Smith Work done by Mr. John Streel in the same to the amount of £253.19:10 and Masons Work done by Mr Joseph Brayn to the amount of £177.12.8 making together the Sum of £1089..12.8¾ for which your Petitioner conceives there is due to him a Commission of £3 pcent which amounts to £32..13..9

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays your Worships to Order the said Sum of £32..13..9 to be paid him out of your Worships Treasure or as your Worships shall seem meet & also the sum of eight Guineas for two plans of Alterations designed with intent to the enlargement of Clerkenwell Bridewell &

Yr Petitioner will ever pray Etc

32.18.9 8 8 41.1 9
