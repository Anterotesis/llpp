To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Giles in the Fields in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Edmund Pepys and Samuel Foyster Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty fourth day of January last. Mary Ann (called in the said Order a female Bastard child of Sarah Mansfields) aged near four Years was Removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of St. Pancras in the said County to the said Parish of St Giles in the Fields as the Place of the last legal Settlement of her the said Mary Ann whereby your Petitioners are, as they conceive aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order And pray that Your Worships will be pleased to appoint a day in this present Session for the hearing and determining their said Appeal

And Your Petrs will Pray Etc

Harbt Robertson Solicitor for the Appellants
