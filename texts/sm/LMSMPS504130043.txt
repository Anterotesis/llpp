To the Right Worpll. his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx In their General Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churcwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopgate London.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hand sand Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middsex One whereof being of the Quorum bearing Date the first day of July 1751 Martha the Wife of William Burroughs and her three Children namely James aged about Seven Years William aged about five Years and John aged about Twelve Months were removed from the Parish of Saint Janmes Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopgate London aforesaid alledging the last legal Place of Settlement of the said Martha Burroughs and her said three Children to be there And Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices do humbly Appeal therefrom And humbly Pray Your Worships.

Burr Attorney for Bpsgate Parish. Would appoint some day this present General Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex to hear this Appeal And that Your Worships will make an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell And all Parties Concerned in and about the Premisses may then and there attend hear and abide the Final Determination of Year Worships.

And Yr. Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc
