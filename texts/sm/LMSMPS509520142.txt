To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex at the General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant or Order under the hands and seals of William Brodie and John Nares Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the ninth day of March 1798. John Davis, Sarah his Wife and their five Children namely Ann aged about nine years, John Aged about seven years Sarah aged about five years Daniel aged about three years and a female Infant aged about seven months were removed from the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London as the place of their last legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order and humbly appeal against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray That your Worships will be pleased to great them such relief as shall be thought most equitable.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray

John Mill Wm. Tomlin} Churchwardens

Wm. Jones Wm. Adam} Overseers
