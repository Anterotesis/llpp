Middlesex To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esqr. Chairman and the Rest of the Justices in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The Petition and Appeal of John Baines

Humbly Sheweth

That by an Order of Adjudication bearing date the 5th. day of November 1785 and made by Wm. Blackborow & Thos. Cogan Esqr. Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx Your Appellant was thereby as well on the Complaint of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the said County As upon the Oath of Elizabeth Doweitt Adjudged to be the reputed Father of a Female Bastard Child Baptized by the name of Elizabeth of which she was delivered on the 26th day of May then preceding, And was Ordered to pay the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish the Sum of Â£3,,15,,0 for and towards the lying in of the said Elizabeth Doweitt and the Maintenance of the said Bastard Child to the time of making said Order, And Also the further Sum of Three Shillings and Six pence Weekly So long as the said Child shall Continue Chargeable to the said Parish, As by the said Order doth more fully Appear,

Your Appellant Conceiving himself Aggrieved by such Adjudication humbly Appeals therefrom and Prays a day may be Appointed for the hearing and determining thereof.

And your Appellant will Ever pray Etc

John Bains
