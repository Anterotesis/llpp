To the Right Worsppll her Matys Justices of the Peace for the County of Midds.

The humble peticon of Thomas Minchin on the behalfe of Mathew Minchin his Son

Sheweth That yor. Petrs. said Son by his Indenture of Apprentice this dated the 13 0. July 1704 Did put himselfe Apprentice to John Fox of the parish of St. Gyles in the feilds in the said County Cordweyner for the term of Seven yeares comencing from the date of the Same Indenture as thereby may appeare.

That in Some time after the said John Fox caused yor. Petrs. said Son to leave his Said Service and ordered him to yor. Petr. for his Mainteynance alledging that he would not any longer entertain him in his said Service whereupon yor. Petr. Sent his said son to Sea Yet never the less the said John Fox doth insist to have the benefitt of yor. Petrs. Said Sons wages whereby yor. Petr. conceives himselfe aggreived.

That the said Matter having been heard before the Worsppll. Justices Bucknall who thereupon ordered the said Jno. Fox to deliver up the said Indenture to be concelled and made void which he refused Soe to doe

S Westley

Yor. Petr. therefore humbly Prays yor. Worspps to be pleased to grant that he may be discharged from his said Apprenticeship.

And yor. Petr. shall ever pray Etc.
