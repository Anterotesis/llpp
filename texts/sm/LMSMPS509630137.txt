To the Right Worshipful, His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Owen Paper Hanging Manufacturer and Stationers an Inhabitant of the Parish of saint Leonard Shoreditch in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth

That the Churchwardenes and Overseers of the Poor and also the Parishioners of the said Parish of saint Leonard Shoreditch at a Meeting held on the 5th day of July 1799 in the Vestry Room of the said Parish did make a certain Rate or assessment for and towards the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish and for other the endsand Purposes of a certain act of Parliament made and passed in the fourteenth Year of the Reign of King George the Third for the better Relief and Employment of the Poor within the said Parish of saint Leonard shoreditch and that they the said churchwardens and Overseers and also the parishioners of the said parish of saint Leonard shoreditch have omitted in such Rate or assessement the names of William Humblebee, Thomas Sharp, John Amos, Herbert Garden one of the Overseers, James Sallwell, William Harrison Samuel Leban robert Edward Benjamin Dunnage William Ravenhile John Mill Robert Staff John Willson Robert Armstrong Thomas Scott Joseph Parke Peter Edwards John William James Duthait Richard Edwin Elizabeth Paine Edward Barnes Richard Stuller, James Bumpitead, Robert Bevard Richard Hedgeman, John Lawrence, Henry Barton, The Widow Hatch and Samuel Hill, Inhabitants of the said Parish and hable to be rated and included in the said Rate and to be assessed towards the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish and who so were at the time of making of the said Rate and also the names of several other Inhabitants of the said parish who are and at the time of making the said Rate or assessment were hable to be rated and assesed towards the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish.

That the said Churchwardens and overseers of the Poor and also the Parishioners of the said Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch have by such Rate or assessment wrongly compounded with James Sattwell, Robert Stacey (One other of the overseers) Thomas Herbert and others owners of Houses or other Buildings within the said Parish the annual Rent or Value of each such House or Building not exceeding Ten pounds by having in such Composition remitted more than one third of the Rate by the said Act intended to be laid upon such premises respectively contrary to the Directions of the above named act and have also compounded with James Matter for his House or other Buildings in the said Parish contrary to the directions of the above recited Act the same being respectively above the annual Rent value of Ten pounds

That the said Rate or assessment was on the said 5th day of July last satisfied and confirmed by John nares Esquire and william Brodie Esquire two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex

Your Petitioner conceiving himself to be aggrieved by the said Rate assessment Doth appeal to your worships their from and humbly prays that you will pleased to hear him in the Premises and that the said Rate may be quashed And that your Worships will be pleased to order that the Church wardens and overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch and such others whom it doth or may concern may appear before you at a certain day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of your worships touching the appeal

And Your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc
