To the Right Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Midd in their General Quarter Sessions assembled.

The humble peticon and Appeal of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the poor of the parish of Hambleton in the County of Bucks.

Sheweth That by Vertue of a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Thomas Jones and Henry Dodson Esqrs. two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said County of Midd bearing Date the fifteenth day of April last past Thomas Elberry aged about eight years and William Elberry aged about Five years (mentioned in the said Warrant or Order to be the Lawfull Issue of Thomas Elberry and Anne his Wife then both deced) were removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of St. George in the County of Midd and on the nineteenth day of April last past were brought into the said parish of Hambleton in the County of Bucks as the place of their Settlement.

Your peticoners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the Said Order of the said two Justices of the peace humbly appeal to this Court against the same and pray that [---] Some day [---] may be appointed for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St. George in the said County of Midd and all other parties concerned to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

And your peticoners shall ever pray Etc
