To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at the General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of I Girdler Esquire and William Blackborrow Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorun) bearingdale the Thirteenth day of November One Thousand seven hundred and seventy eight Thomas Martin aged about eighteen Months whose Mather has deserted him was removed from the [...] Parish of Saint Sepulchre in the County of Middlesex unto the said Parish of Saint Botolph adjudged by the said two Justices in the said Pass Warrantor Order of Removal to be the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Thomas Martin

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal of the said two Justices Do therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the same praying such Relief in the Premises as to this Court Shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Jas Fisher & Son Solls. for the Appellants
