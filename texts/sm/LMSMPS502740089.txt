To the Rt. Worpll. his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. George the Martys in the said County of Middx

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of John Milner and Henry Broadhead Esqrs. two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County (Quorum Unus) bearing Date the Fifth Day of April Instant Ann Taylor Widow of Charles Taylor (but in the said order said to be the widow of George Taylor) and their four Children (Vizt.) Charles aged nine Years and upwards Thos. aged almost seven Years Elizabeth aged three years and John aged about Eight Months were removed from and out of the Parish of St. Giles in the Fields in the said County to the [...] St George the Martyr as the place of last [...] legal settlement.

That there by yor. Petrs. humbly conceive themselves aggrieved and appeal to yor. Worps against the same

Yor. Petrs. therefore humbly pray that Yor. Worps will be pleased to order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St Giles in the Fields in the said County may be ordered to attend this Court some day this present Sessions to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the sd. Court touching the said Appeal

And Yor. Petrs. shall ever pray Etc
