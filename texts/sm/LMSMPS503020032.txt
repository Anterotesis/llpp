To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of William Sayer George Greaves & Thomas Turton[---]

Sheweth That Your Petitioners together with Robert Ballet were Nominated or Appointed Scavengers of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the said County by Thomas Robe and William Booth Esqr two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County by their Warrt. or Order bearing date the 22 day of April last but which was not Served on Your Petitioners till the 29th. of the said Month of April so that the last General Quarter Sessions intervened between the date and Service of the said Warrant or Order, And the said Warrt. or Order was made (as Your Petitioners are informed) not at any Petty Sessions within the division in which the said parish doth lye & whereat Officers are usually appointed and to which the list made by the Parish- -ioners was returned But by the said two Justices seperately each at his own house-That neither of Your Petitioners was at the date or Service of the said Warrant or Order or now is atradesman Living in the said Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell but do all of them reside in the Parish of Saint John Clerkenwell

Wherefore and for diverse other Causes your Petitioners do humbly Appeal to Your Worships from the said Warrant or Order and pray to be releived against the same as to Your Worships shall seem meet, and that a day may be Appointed for hearing this Appeal and that Thomas Ancel who is Vestry Clerk of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell and who served the said Warrant may Attend and be Examined touching the same.

And Your Petitioners shall Ever Pray [...]

Tho: Turton Wm Sayer

George Greaves
