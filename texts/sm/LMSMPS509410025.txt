To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace for the said County assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of The Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Atthallows Barking in the City of London.

Sheweth That by virtue of a certain order or Pass Warrant bearing date the second day of June one thousand seven hundred and ninety seven under the hands and seals of W. Bleamire and A Cumming Esquires Two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum) Thomas Bryant was removed and conveyed from and out of that part of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn which lies above the Bars in the County of Middlesex to the said Parishes Atthallows Barking in the City of London as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Thomas Bryant whereby the Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appear against the said order a Pap Warrant and and Pray that the benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County of Middlesex.

Mr S J Murran [...] for Appellants}
