To the Right Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by virtue of an order of removal under the Hands and of Burford Camper Esquire and John Sherword Esquire Two of his Majestys Justice of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (and being of the Quorum) bearing date Christopher Elverson who was gone from her and her This Children Said Hannah aged Nine Years or thereabouts and Nancy aged This Years of thereabouts were rem and from the Parish of saint Ann of Wapping in the said County of Middlesex to the said parish of saint George and the said Two Last mentioned Justices of the peace in and by the said order of removal Adjudged the Last Legal sett Court of the said Else Elverson and her said Two Children to be in the said parish of saint George

That your Petitioners Asvised and belive the Last Legal Sett Court of the said Else Elverson and her said Two Children is not in the said parish of saint George as by the said orders supposed and therefore they Consider themselves to be Aggreised by the said ordered determination of the said Two Justices and Do Humbly Appeal to your worships against the same

Your Petitioners as well on the part and Behalf of them at and the other Inhabitant and Paristioners of the said parish of saint george Do Humbly may that the said order of [...] said Else Elverson and her said Two Children from the said parish of saint John of wapping to the said parish of saint george may be Quashed And that your Worships would be pleased to make such further Order therein for the Reimbursing and paying your petitioners the Costs and Charge they have been put to in Stain [...] and Providing for the said Else Elverson and her said Two Children in obedient to the said order as to your Worship shall sam Meet

And your Petitioners will own pay etc

Major Might Sollr. for the appellants
