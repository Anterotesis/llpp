To the worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Andrew Hubbard Little Eastcheap in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of John Floud Esqr. and Patrick Colquhoun Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex, (one of them being of the Quorum) bearing date the 20th. Day of March 1793 Ann Lang, Wife of John Lang now at Sea, and her five Children, lawful Issue, namely, Sarah aged 8 Years and upwards, Ann aged 7 Years and upwards, Jane Warren aged 5 Years and upwards John aged 3 Years and upwards, and William Aged 17 Months were removed and conveyed from out of the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Andrew Hubbard, and by the said Pass Warrant or Order the Lawful Settlement of the said Ann Lang and her five Children

is adjudged to be in the Parish of St. Andrew Hubbard Little East cheap in the City of London whereby your Petitioners concerve themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore appeal to your Worships against the said Order and humbly crave such Relief therein as to your Worhips shall seem meet and just and mas much as your Petitioners cannot be ready to have their said Appeal heard at the present Session Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that the benefit of the said Appeal may be saved to them and the hearing and determining the same may be adjourned to the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
