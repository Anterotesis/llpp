Middlesex To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and Steers his Majesty's Justices of the peace assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace hold at the Session House on Clerkenwell Green in and for the County of Middlesex On Monday the twefth day of January in the twenty ninth Year of the Royce of King George the third

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Catherine Durant of Monmouth Street in the Parish of Saint Giles in the fields in the County of Middleson Widow and Dealer in Hats

Humbly Sheweth That your Petitioner was in the twelfth day of September last Convicted before Six Sampson Weight Knight one of his Majesty's Justices assigned to keep the Peace of our Lord the King in and for the said County and also to hear and Determine divers felonies Trespasses and other Misdemeanor done and Committed within the said County upon the Information of Richard King of the Stamp Office Goutler [...] For that your Petitioner late of Monmouth Street in the said County after the first day of October One thousand seven hundred and eighty four mentioned in a Certain Act of Parliament made at Westminister in the twenty fourth Year of his present Majesty's Reign entitled an Act for granting to his Majesty Certain Duties on Licences to be taken out by Persons sending Hats by Retail and also Certain duties on Hats sold under such Licences and for granting Additional duties on all Hats and Caps inported into this Kingdom and within the space of Six Calendar Months next before the Exhibities of the said Information to wit or the thirtieth day of duly in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and eighty eight not regarding the Statute in such Case made and provided did at Monmouth Street After said and [...] to the where the said Justice resided sell to are John Andrews a Certain Steeff Year of the value of nine Shillings of lawful Moncy of Great Britain for the like Sure of Nine Shillings

of like lawful Money then and there paid by the said John Andrews to your Petitioner no Ticket whatsoever market or stamped with way mark or stamp of any Deuom [...] or value being there affaired to the said Hat so sold by your petitioner or to the Lining in the inside of the Crown thereof in any mannee whatsoover Contrary to the form of the Statute in such Case made and Provided Whereby and by force of the Statute in such Case made and Provided your Petitioner hath forfeited the Sum of Ten Pounds of lawful Money of Great Britain One Moiety thereof to the use of his said Majesty and the other Moiety thereof to the said Richard King the said Inprseidut And your Petitioner was on the hearing of the said Information Adjudged by the said Justice to forfeit and pay the Sum of Ten Pounds against which Judgement your petitioner appealed and entered into a recognizance in manner as directed by the said Act to prosecute the said appeal

Your Petitioner therefore thinks herself aggreived by the Judgment of the said Justice and humbly prays the Court will take her Case into Consideration and appoint a time for hearing of this her Petition and appeal

Catherine Durants
