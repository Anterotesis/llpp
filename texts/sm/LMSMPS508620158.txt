To the worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said county assembled

The humble Petition and appeal of William Blake and Christopher Paxton Janners at Chiswick in the county of Middlesex

Sheweth That your Petitioners were convicted by Edward Read Esquire and William Hyde Esquire two of His majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex on the fifth day of April one thousand seven hundred and ninety one And your Petitioners have been adjudged by the said Justices to forfeit the Sum of Twenty pounds of lawful money of Great Britain for and by reason of your Petitioners having taken divers Skins out of the Materials in which the same had been Tanned on the twenty sixth day of January One thousand seven hundred and ninety one without first giving or sending to the proper Officer for the Duties on Hides due notice of the Time of your Petitioner's taking the said skins out of the Materials aforesaid.

That your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices and therefore humbly appeal to your worships against the same and

Chetham Solr. for the appellts.} Your Petitioners humbly pray such Relief in the premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.
