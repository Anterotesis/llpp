To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General [---] Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of Pio Sienesi of the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden in the Said County of Middlesex Pawn Broker

Sheweth

That by a certain Conviction in writing under the Hand and Seal of William Hyde Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the peace For the said County of Middlesex bearing Date the Twenty Fourth Day of September last your Petitioner was is convicted before the said Justice For that he your Petitioner being a Person broker on the Tenth Day of July then last past at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden aforesaid in the County of Middlesex aforesaid did take and Receive of and from and Phineas Andrews a certain Pawn or Pledge that is to say a Silver Table Spoon and did then and there Advance and lend therein the Sum of Ten Shillings and Six pence of Lawful Money of Great Britain

That being such Pawn Broker as aforesaid and not Regarding the Statute in Such Case made and provided No Hearing the Penalty therein contained Did not before your [...] Petitioner had Advanced and Cent the said Sum of Ten Shillings and Six Pence upon the said Person or Pledge Enter or cause to be entered in a Fair and Reqular Manner in a any Book or Books kept for that Purpose a Description of the said Silver Table Spoon which your Petitioner so received in Person and also the said Sum of Money to be advanced and Cent there on with the Day of the Month and Year on which such Silver Table

Spoon was so pawned and pledged and the Name and place of abode of the Person by whom the same was so [...] pawned and Pledged and also the Name and place of abode of the Owner thereof according to the Information of the Person Pewning or Pledging the same according to the Direction of the Statute in such Case made and provided but wholly neglected to make such Entry as aforesaid contrary to the Form of the Statute in such Case made and provided

And you Petitioner was adjudged to pay and For fort For Such Offence the Sum of Ten Pounds Whereby your Petitioner concieves himself aggrieved

[---] Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays that he may be at Liberty to Appeal to the said Conviction And that, a day in this present Session may be appointed for the hearing and determining thereof [...] [...]

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc Pio Sienesi
