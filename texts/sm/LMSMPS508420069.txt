Middlesex-Jane Session 1789

To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petititon of Abraham Seger

Sheweth That by a certain Conviction under the Hand and Seal of William Hyde Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the fourth day of December now last past your Petitioner was Convicted before the said Justice of selling on the Twentyninth day of November last to one Ann Kellenor one Pair of Leather Gloves exceeding the price of one Shilling and four Pence (to wit) of the price of One Shilling and six pence and subject to a Stamp Duty of Threepence with a Stamp or Mark of less Denomination than by the Act is & directed to be affixed being affixed to the said Gloves (to wit) with a Stamp of the Denomination of Two pence contrary to the Statute in that case made and provided and

And your Petitioner was adjudged to forfeit for the same the Sum of Twenty Pounds

That your Petitioner conceiving himself to be aggrieved by such Conviction humbly begs leave to Appeal to this Honorable Court against the same pursuant to a Recognizance entered into by your Petitioner before the said Justice for that purpose and that the Hearing and Determining therefore may be adjourned untill the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County.

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.

Abrm Segen
