To The Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace of the County of Middlesex at their General Sessions of the Peace in and for the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Paul Shadwell in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass or Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of David Wilmot and Charles Digby two of his Majestys Justices of the peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing Date the Second day of December 1773 One Jane Shevels Singlewoman was Removed from the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex aforesaid as the place of her last legal Settlement.

By which said Pass or Order of the said Two Justices your Petitioners and Appellants Apprehend themselves aggreived and do Appeal to this Court against the same.

Therefore your Petitioners and Appellants pray that this Court will appoint a Day next January Sessions for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint John of Wapping and all persons concerned to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of the Court touching the said Appeal And that your Petitioners and Appellants may be delieved in the Premises as this Court shall seem meet.

And your Petitioners and Appellants shall ever pray

Mich Malbon Sol for Appals.} [---]
