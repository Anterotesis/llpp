To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition of Stephen Wicks Apprentice to Benjamin Warner of the Parish of Saint. Mary White Chapple in the said County Butcher.

Sheweth That your Petitioner on the Seventh day of November in the year of our Lord One Thousand Seven Hundred and twenty Eight became bound an Apprentice unto Edwd. Tomkins of the said parish of Saint Mary White Chapple in the County aforesaid Butcher. And on the fourteenth day of September In the Year of our Lord One Thousand Seven hundred and Thirty One was Turned over unto the said Benjamin Warner of the Parish and County aforesaid Butcher with him to serve during the remainder time of has said Apprenticehood from the date of the said Indenture Relation being there unto had may more fully appear during which time the said Benjamin Warner was and is to Teach and Instruct the said Stephen Wicks the Trade Art and Mistery of a Butcher and likewise by said Indenture was to provide for his said Apprentice good and Sufficient wearing Apparel during the said Term, But the said Benjamin Warner hath refused and still doth refuse to provide for his said Apprentice any Manner of Wearing Apparel So that your Petitioner is quite naked and diver stimes your Petitioner is very refused beat and wounded in a Barbarus manner.

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Beggs that your Worshipps may be pleased to order that he be wholy Discharged from his sd. Apprenticehood or Such otherwise as your Worshipps shall seem meet

And your Petitioner shall ever pray and so forth

Stephen Wicks
