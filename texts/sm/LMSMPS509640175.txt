To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in the Present General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Chapel Warden and Overseers of the Poor of the Fithing or Chapelry of Sidmonton on the County of Southampton.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of sd. Henderson and John Rae Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) whereby Sarah therein called the Wife of William Dyer (gone from her) and her two Children Vizt. Charles Aged four Years and and half and Harriet Aged Twelve months were removed and Conveyed from and out of the parish of Hampton to the said Tithing or Chapelry of Sidmonton as the Place of their legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order of the said two Justices Appeal to Your Worships against the same, and humbly Request that the benefit of the said Appeal may be reserved to them and that a day may be appointed in the next General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex for the hearing and determining the same And that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Hampton be then and there ordered to attend to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of the Court touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
