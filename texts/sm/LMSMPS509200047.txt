To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the peace assembled.

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Finchley in the said County

Sheweth That by an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of William Bleamire and Aaron Graham Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof is of the Quorum) bearing date the ninth day of November last Whereby Richard Smith together with his lawful Wife and five Children born in lawful Wedlock to with Richard aged Seventeen Years, Thomas aged seven Years Eleanor aged Three Years, and upwards Elizabeth aged Three Years and Joseph aged Fourteen Weeks were removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Fryer Barne in the said County to the said parish of Finchley as the place of their last legal Settlement

Your petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said order appeal to your Worship against the same and request that the benefit of their said Appeal

may be saved unto them untill the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County and that upon Notice to be given to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Jayern Barnet they and all persons concerend may appear at such General Sessions to hear and abide the Determination of the Court touching the said Appeal

And as in Duty Etc.
