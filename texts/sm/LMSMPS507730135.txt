To the Right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace For the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The Humble Petition of Henry Harrow and Charles Harrow

Sheweth.

That by Indentures of Apprenticeship herewith produced your Petitioner were severally bound an Apprentice to John Pettey. late of the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex Taylor, but now a Prisoner under Execution For Debt, in the Marshalsea Prison For the Term of seven Years, which are not yet expired

That the said John Pettey is so [...] produced by Misfortunes, as to be unable to carry [...] Trade For some time past and to provide Necessaries, and to instruct, or to cause your Petitioners to be instructed in his Trade or Business [...] Taylor, according to the Covenants of the said Indentures of Apprenticeship whereby your Petitioners are left totally destitute.

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray, that this Honourable Court will please to discharge them From their Apprenticeship

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc.

Dan. Gunston Solicitor
