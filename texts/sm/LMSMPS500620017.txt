To the Right Worll: the Justices of Peace for the County of Midds.

The humble Petcion of William Pratt of the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the County of Midds.

Sheweth

That yor. Petr. hath duely served the Offices of Head borough and Constable for the Upper Liberty of St. John streete within the said Parish into wth he was elected, his said Office of Constable ending at Assection day last in the service of wth. Offices yor. Petr. by reason of his dwelling in the High Northern Roade hath been at a greate Charge in Passing disbanded Sonldiers Cripples Women Children and other Passes sent to him by Warrants and is really out of Pockett the Since of Fourteene Pounds and Ten Shillings about such passes their Carriage and Releife Ashe is ready by a faire Accont to make appeare And yor. Petr. hath no way to be reinbursed but by an Order from yor. Worps.

Yor. Petr. therefore being a Tradesman and the want of his money very preindiciall to him doth humbly pray That yor. Worps. will be pleased to order that a Rate may be made by the Churchwardens & Vestry men of the said Parish to reimburse and pay yor. Petr.

And yor. Petr. shall ever pay Etc.

Mallaber
