Middlesex General Sessions

The King a Bullock Esquire

To His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex aforesaid

The Humble Petition of Henry Bullock Esquire Sheweth that by a Summons under the Hand and Seal of John Spiller Esquire One of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County aforesaid bearing date the Eleventh day of April last stating an Information made by John Ramsden against your Petitioner [...] for permitting Thomas Fennett to pass with a Saddle Horse on the Thirty first 9 March last through the private grounds of your Petitioner by which he the said Thomas Fennell did and paying the Toll of the Tumpike Gate of Colnbrook against the form of the Statude

Your Petitioner in Obedience to this Summons attended at the House of the said John Spiller Esquire on the Seventeenth day of the said Month of April and having been Convicted in the penalty of Twenty Shillings

illegally as your Petitioner [---] humbly presumes he your said Petitioner after entiring into the usual Recognizance for his appearance at this Sessions gave Notice to the said John Spiller Esquire on the same Day of your Petitioners inturtion to appeal at the present Sessions against the said Coviction and conceiving that the said John Spiller Esquire [...] would return the Recognizance aforesaid in due time to the present Sessions and that in consequence thereof your Petitioners Appeal would be heard without further Notice as by the Colnbrook Tunpike Act the Notice is directed to be giver to the Treasurer for the time being and your Petition having the honour of being the Treasurer for the Colnbrook Turnpike Road aforesaid could not as he conceived given other Notice Man as aforesaid

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays that as by the said Act Appeals of this nature are directed to be [...] heard and determined at the next general

Sessions for the County of Middlesex and not afterwards and as you Petitioner is now preferred with Evidence to bring on the same he humbly he has that your Worships with be pleased to determine his Appeal to the Conviction aforesaid immediately

And Your Petitioner shall humbly Pray Etc
