Middlesex to wit } To his Majesty's Justices of the Peace at the in General Quarter Sessions of the Peace held at Hicks's Hall in and for the County of Middlesex on Monday the Seventeenth day of October in the Eight year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third now King of Great Britain etc And in the year of our Lord 1768.

The Humble Petition of Appeal of Samuel Simcockes Esquire.

Sheweth That your Petitioner on the Thirty first day of August now last past was Charged upon the Prosecution and Information of Rouquier Cannon before Thomas Miller Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex aforesaid with being Guilty of an Offence pretended to have been Committed by your Petitioner on the Nineteenth Day of July last in the Parish of Saint Clement Danes in the said County by Playing Setting at Staking or punting at a certain Unlawfull Game with Dice called Hazand, against the Form of the Statute in that Case made and provided by means whereof your Petitioner lost and forfeited the Sum of Fifty Pounds of lawfull Money as was pretended.

That the said Thomas Miller Esquire thereupon Adjudged your Petitioner to be Guilty of the said pretended Offence and accordingly Convicted your Petitioner thereof and also Adjudged that your Petitioner should for the said pretended Offence forfeit the said Sum of Fifty Pounds
