To the Worshipfull Chairman and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of Elizabeth French of Lemmon Street Goodmans fields in the said County Widow

Humbly Sheweth That by Indentures dated the 28th. day of November with year of our Lord 1756 One Mary Poulter Spnister Putherself Apprentice to Your Petitioner and with her after the manner of an Apprentice to Serve from the day of the date of that Indenture unto the full end and Term of Seven Years during which Term the said Apprentice her said Mistress faithfully should Serve and her lawful Comands every where gladly do Nor from the Servia of her said Mistress absent herself during all the said Term And your Petitioner did thereby Covenant with the said Mary Poulter that Your Petitioner sho'd and wo'd Teach and Instructor Caussor Procure to be Taught and Instructed in the best way and manner that she- could the said Apprentice in the Art Profession or Science of Musick and Signing and shod. find unto the said Apprentice Sufficient Meat Drink Washing Board- and Lodging and all other Necessaries during the said Term of Seven Years and also in all proper Cloaths Linnen and Wearing Apparel Suitable to such Calling or Science

That although your Petitioner hath well and faithfully performed every thing in the said Indenture contained on her part to be done and performed- And been at a very large Expense thereby Yet her said Apprentice hath not performed her part but hath Absented herself from your petitioner Without any Just Cause or Reason and hath been Seduced and Entiu'd away from your petitioner and she hath been Retained by Mr. Thomas Rosoman of Sadlers Wells and is Employed by him in Signing at Sadlers Wells.

That Sir John Fielding Knt. and Paul Vaillant Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex by Warrant or Certificate under their hands and Seals dated the 15th. day of April instant Ordered that the said Mary Poulter be Discharged of and from her Apprenticeship to your Petr.

That your Petitioner thinks herself Aggrieved by such Determination Order or Warrant of such Justices and therefore humbly Appeals to your Worships therefrom

Your Petr therefore humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to Appoint a time for the said Mary Poulter to Attend this Honble Court to shew Cause why the said Order Warrant or Certificate should not be Quashed and Discharged by your Worships And that your Petr. may have such further and other Relief in the premises as to your Worships shall Seem must

Eliz French

And your Petr shall ever pray
