To the Right Worshipfull his Majties: Justices of the Peace of the County of Middx, At their Genll: Quar Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Peticon of Joseph Sexton.

Sheweth That abt: the begining of September last Your Peticoner was Bound An Apprntice by Indenture to one James Seymour of the Parish of Chiswick in the sd: County of Middx Fisherman, That the said Seymour Your Peticoners Said Master, hath oftentimes Beate and Brinsed him [...] fully, That he hath not Allowed [...] sd Peticoner, necessary Provisions whereby he has been allmost Starved with Hunger, That the said Seymour hath not Instructer Your Peticonr: in his Trade, Doth not Allowe him Cloaths or Soe much as Bed to Lye on, And hath Turned Your Sd: Peticoner out of his House.

Your Peticoner therefore Humbly Prays Your Worships will be Pleased to Discharge him from his Sd: Judrs: of Apprnticeship.

And Your Peticoner Shall ever Pray etc.

Joseph Sexton
