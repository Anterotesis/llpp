To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace held in and for the said County Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Much Baddow in the County of Essex

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of Benjamin Cox and J Fielding Esqrs. two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing date the fifth day of February 1754 Sarah Pavitt Widow of Jacob Sampson Pavitt deceased and their two Children to wit Sarah Aged five Years and Jacob Aged two Years Were Removed from the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the said City Liberty and County to the Parish of Much Baddow aforesaid

By which said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices Your Petitioners and Appellants Apprehend themselves Aggrieved and do Appeal to this Court against the same

Therefore Your Petitioners and Appellants pray that this Court will appoint a Day this present Session for the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Martin and all persons concerned to Attend this Court to hear and abide by the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal and that Your Petitioners and Appellants may be Relieved in the Premisses as this Court Shall seem meet

And Yr. Petrs, Etc.

John Billington Attorney for Appellants
