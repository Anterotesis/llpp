To the Worshipful the Chairman and the rest of the Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at Hicks's Hall in St. John Street in and for the said County at their General Session in the Month of September 1755.

The Humble Petition of John White

Sheweth That your Petitioner being informed that the place of Cryer and Housekeeper at Hicks's Hall in St: John's Street in the said County of Middlesex is in the Disposal of your Worships at your will and pleasure.

And also being informed that Mrs. Susannah Northcote the last appointed Cryer and Housekeeper at Hicks's Hall aforesaid has lately intermarried with one Mr. Robert Strete whereby the said place (at your Worship's pleasure may become vacant

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships that if the said place shall become vacant upon that or any other Account That your Worships will be pleased to appoint your Petitioner to be Cryer and House keeper (in stead of the said Mrs. Northcote) who will carefully and diligently execute the said office of Cryer and Housekeeper And as in Duty bound shall ever pray for your Worships

Jno White
