To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their present General Quarter Sessions of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the Bars in the said County

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of John Willack and R Johnson Esquires two of his majestys Justices of the City and Liberty of Westminster peace for the [...] (one whereof being of the Quorum) hearing date the 8th day of January Instant Jane Alwell Wife of William Alwell (your from her) and her three Children to wit William horn 25th October 1791, Sarah horn 28th April 1793. and John horn 30th May 1796 were removed and conveyed From and out of the parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above bars as the place of this last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order of the said Two Justices appeal to your Worships against the same and request that the Benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto these until the next General Session of the peace to he holden for the said County and that all parties concerned may be then ordered to attend to abide the judgement and determination of your Worships touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners Etc.

Arthur Gliddon Sollr For Apptts
