To the Rt. Honoble: & Rt: Worppll: his Majties Justices of the Peace sitting on the Bench att Hickshall.

The numble Petition & appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlett of Wapping Stepney

Sheweth

That the Church wardens & Overseers of the Poor of St John Wapping. By virtue of a Pass under the hands & Seale. of John Webber Esqr & Robert Constable Esqr two of his Maties Justices of the Peace for this County, Did lately Send to a. One Margret Ovey wife of John Ovey a Soldjer in his Maties Service in Flanders; with Jane and Charles their children Illedging That they belong to our Said Hamlett Whereas in truth they never had any residence nor ever Did Inhabit there

Your Petitonrs Therefore most humbly pray That this Honoble: Bench will be pleased to Grant [...] Order For the Said Churchwardens & Overseers [...] the Poor of St John Wapping afforesaid, To ap [...] and shew cause why they shall not abide the Judgment of this Court touching the settlement of the Said Margret Ovey & her said two children

And yor Peticonr shall ever Pray Etc

Abraham Austen Ch: Wardin
