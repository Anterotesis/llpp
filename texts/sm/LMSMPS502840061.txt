To the Worship full the Chairman and the Rest of the Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at the Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace held for the said County at Hick's hall in St: John's Street on Monday the Third day of July 1732.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Bridget ats Brides London

Sheweth That by Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Richard Manley and Richd. Brown Esqrs: Two of his Majesties of the Peace for the County of Midlesex (Quorum Unus) bearing date the 28th. day of Aprill last past one Francis Watts with Catherine his Wife and Thomas their son Aged about Three Years was Removed from the Parish of St: Giles's without Cripple Gate in ye. County of Midlesex to the said Parish of St. Bridget als Brides London as the Peace of their last Legall Settlement and that this & other hath since such Removal being delivered of another child in [...] the Sd. Pish of St. Brides whereby Your Petitioners think themselves Agreived.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the said Warrant and pray that this Court will Appoynt a time to hear an determine the said Appeal.

And Your Petitioners Shall Pray Etc.

Thomas Harris Vestry Clerk of St: Brides
