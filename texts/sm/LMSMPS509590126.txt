To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Overseers of the Poor of the Precinct of White Friars in the City of London.

Sheweth

That by virtue of an Order of Removal or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Edward Read and John Floud Esquires two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the ninth day of March 1799. Jane the Wife of Thomas Mattenly by the Name of Jane Mattenly and Charles her illegitimate son were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex and delivered to your Petitioners being the Overseers of the Poor of the Precinct of White Friars in the said City of London (the said Justices adjudging that the place of the last legal settlement of the said Jane and Charles her Son was in the said Precinct of White Friars in the said City of London by which said Order of Removal or Pass Warrant Your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioiners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Order of Removal or Pass Warrant and pray such Relief in the premises as to Your Worships shall seem meet and that the benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of th Peace to be holden for the said County
