To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices for the County of Middlesex in Session Sssembled

The Humble Petition of Richard Murthwaite

Sheweth

That your Petitioner was on the 5th. day of January 1799 convicted before Henry James Pye and Joseph More Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex upon the Oath of William Kettle in the Penalty of Five Pounds for demanding and receiving of the said William Kettle one Penny as profit upon Two Shillings leat by your Petitioner upon a Pawn of a pair of Hose otherwise a pair of Stockings to the said William Kettle in the name or under the description of Mr. Roberts one Penny being more than at the rate of One half penny for the loan of any Sum not exceeding the Sum of Two Shillings and six Pence by the Calender Month- including the Current Months in which the said Pawn was received contrary to the Statute Etc. by which Conviction your Petitioner conceives himself agrieved

And therefore your Petitiner appeals to this Court against the same and prays that your Worships will hear him thereon and grant him such Relief as his case requires and that a day may be appointed for the hearing and determining the same

And Your Petitioner will ever pray Etc.

Richd. Murthwaite
