To William Mainwaring Esqr. Chairman and the rest of his Majesties Worshipful Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex

The humble Petition of James Burton of Wardrobe Court Doctors Commons

Sheweth That Your Petitioner is established in the Profession of a Surveyor after having been regularly bred to the same, is of the Age of 24, and hath an increasing family to support: Therefore humbly craves leave of Your Worships, to be admitted a Candidate for the Office of Surveyor (under the Building Act of the 14th. Geo. 3rd.) to the district of St. James and St John Clerkenwell, vacant by the decease of the late Mr: Sutton: Which Office should he be so happy as to be appointed to, Your Petitioner begs leave to assure your Worships, that he will by a faithful and constant attention to the duties thereof, endeavor to render himself worthy sheir Choise.

And, as in duly bound, your Petitioner shall ever Pray.

James Burton

February 1784.
