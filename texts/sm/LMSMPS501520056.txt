To the Rt Worpll his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their general quarter Sessions assembled

The humble petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St Mary Abchurch in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or pass Order under the hands and Seals of two of is Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middx and Liberty of Westmr bearing date the third day of this instant April John Cleevely was removed from the parish of St Margarett Westminster in the said County and Liberty unto the said Parish of St Mary Abchurch London alleadging the said parish to be the place of his last legal Settlement That your Petrs doubt not but to prove That the said John Cleevely never had any legal Settlement in the said parish of St Mary Abchurch whereby they conceive themselves agreived by the said pass order and do humbly appeal to your Worpps therefrom

Your Petr therefore most humbly prays your Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses And that the Churchwardens Overseers of the poor [...] of the said Parish of St Margarett Westminster may appear before you and hear and abide the determination of your Worpps therein

And they Shall ever pray Etc
