To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said on Clerkenwell Green on the Tenth. day of September 1798.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Thomas Hill and James Hills

Sheweth That inconsequence of a Summons issued under the hand and Seal of Cranley Thomas Kerby Esquire One of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County and served on your Petitioner he appeared before the Magistrates at the Public Office in Queen Square Westminster Upon An Information exhibited and prosecuted by William Kettle charging that your Petitioners did at the Parish of Saint Mary le Bone in the said County on the Nineteenth day of June last take and receive of Martha Bunting on redeeming the pledge therein mentioned one penny by way of profit upon three shillings on the thirteenth day of June aforesaid lent upon a pledge of two Handkerchiefs Contrary to the Statue Etc And your Petitioners were upon the hearing of the said Informations Convicted in the Penalty of Five pounds.

That your Petitioners conceive themselves agrieved by such Conviction and are advised to Appeal against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships will be pleased to Permit them to enter their Appeal so that the same may be Heard at this present General Quarter Session of the Peace in and for the said County And that your Worships will appoint a day for hearing and determining the same.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray etc
