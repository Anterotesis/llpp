To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for County of Middlesex Assembled at their General Quarter Session Holden at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in and for the said County

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That William Cooper late of the parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex Mariner being gone into parts beyond the Seas Left his Wife Mary Cooper and one Daughter named Margaret Aged Three Years or thereabouts who are now become Chargeable to the said Parish of Saint John of Wapping and have been Maintained and Supported in the said Parish Workhouse at the Expence of the Inhabitants of the said Parish for the Space of Six Weeks Last past

That the said Mary Cooper is the Daughter of one Walter Graystock of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the said County of Middlesex Gentleman who is a Person of Opulent Fortune and of Sufficient Ability to Maintain his said Daughter Mary Cooper and her said Child but Refuses so to do

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly pray your Worships that the said Walter Graystock may be Compelled by the Order of this Court to Maintain and Support his said Daughter Mary Cooper and

said [...] pursuant to the Statute in that Case made [...] provided And That Your Worships will be pleased to Order the said Walter Graystock and all other Persons Concerned to Attend this Court some Day this Present Session to hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of the Court touching the said Petition And That your Petitioners may have such Relief therein as to Justice and Law Doth Appretain

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray

Etc Fra Hunderson Cha Secks} Churchwardens

Alex Simson James Graham Chas. Bacon} Overseers of the Poor
