To the Worshipfull William Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of William Winder of High Street in the Parish of Saint Mary Matfellon otherwise Whitechapel in the County of Middlesex Pawn broker

Sheweth That by a certain Conviction under the Hand and Seal of William Hyde Esquire one of the Justices of our Lord the King Assigned to keep the Peace of our Said Lord the King in and for the County of Middlesex and also to hear and determine divers Felonies Trespasses and other Misdemeanours committed within the Said County bearing date the Twenty Sixth day of August Last your Petitioner and Appellant was Convicted by the said Justice for that he then using and Exercising the Trade and Business of a Pawn broker did in the High Street aforesaid on the Seventh day of August last Past take and receive from one James Peyles by way of Pawn or Pledge a Pas Spoon and did then and there advance and Lend thereon to the Said James Pegler a certain Sum of Money exceeding Five Shillings to Wit the Sum of Five Shillings and Six Pence of lawful Money of Great Britain yet that your Petitioner and Appellant being Such Pawn broker as aforesaid did not forthwith and before he advanced and Lent the Said Sum of Money upon the aforesaid Pawn or Pledge enter or cause to be Entered in a fair or Regular manner in any Book or Books Kept by your Petitioner for that Purpose a Description of the said Pas Spoon Which your Petitioner so received in Pawn or Pledge and also the Sum of Money to be advanced and Lent thereon with the day of the Month and year and the Name and Place of Abode of the Person by Whom the Same was so pawned or Pledged and the Name and Place of abode of the Carner thereof According to the Information of the Person Pawning or Pledging the Same

According to the Statute in such Case made and Provided but that Your Petitioner and Appellant then and there wholly Neglected to make such Entry as aforesaid Contrary to the Statute Etc

And that your Petitioner and Appellant was by the said Justice adjudged to pay and forfeit the Penalty of Ten pounds for the said Supposed Offence whereby your Petitioner conceives himself aggreived

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeals to this Court against the said Conviction and Prays that Your Worships will Appoint a Day in this present General Quarter Sessions for the Hearing and determining of the same And Your Petitioner will ever Pray Etc

John Bunn Sollr. to the Appellt.
