To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex at the Generall Sessions of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the hand sand Seals of Walter Berry and John Lawton Esquires Two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 18th. Day of October 1757. Eleanor Peterson and Susannah Peterson her Daughter were removed from the Parish of Saint Paul Shadwell in the said County of Middl [...] To the sd. Parish of Saint John of Wapping as the place of their last Legall Settlement

That Your Petitioners Conceive themselves Aggreived by the said Warrant or Order and humbly Appeal against the Same.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that your worships will be pleased to Appoint a Day of this present Sessions that all partys may Attend to hear and abided the Judgmt. and Determination of this Court touching the said appeal

And Your Petr. Shall Ever pray Etc.

Thos: Bennet Nat: King} Church Wardens

William Sibbald Peter Nimmo} Overseers of the Poor

Middlesex At the General Sessions of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King Holden for the County of Middlesex at Hickshall in Saint John Street in the County aforesaid [---] on Monday the second Day of December in the Twenty Fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Second King of Great Britain France and Keland Defender of the Faith

It is Ordered by this Court that the Church Wardens and overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Paul Shadwell in the County of Middlesex have Notice of this Appeal And that they and all persons Concerned Do Attend this Court on Friday next at ten of the Clock in the fore-noon to hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said appeal

By the Court Waller
