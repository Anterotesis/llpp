To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire Chairman and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in their Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition of Richard Sacheverell of Southampton Buildings in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the County of Middlesex Bookbinder,

Sheweth That by Indenture of Apprentiship bearing date the fifteenth day of July 1782 Thomas Harding a Poor Boy of the Parish of Saint Giles Reading in the County of Bucks became bound by the said Indenture for the Term of Seven Years to learn the Art of a Bookbinder in the said Indenture Mentioned,

That on or about the Month of July in the Year 1783 Your Petitioner from the disorderly behaviour of the said Thomas Harden his said Apprentice was forced to Complain to the Majestrates sitting at the Rotation Office Clerkenwell who Committed him to Bridewell, But out of Humanity and from the Perswasion of Your Petitioners friends he forgave him and Received him Back into his Service

That from the Time of his being received into the Service of your Petitioner from Clerkenwell Bridewell he hath behaved in the most outragious manner refusing to do his Lawfull business when Ordered by Your Petitioner and several times stricking his Mistress the wife of Your said Petitioner

That at the General Quarter Session of the Peace held for the County of Middlesex in June last he was Convicted of Stealing a Handkerchief and under went a Punishment for the said Crime and on Monday the 10th day of January Inst. he was again Committed to Bridewell for Assaulting your Petitioner Journeyman and attempting the Life of Your Petitioner Wife

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays that your Worships will be Pleased to Release him from his said Apprentice or grant him such relief as to your Worships shall seem meet and your Petitioner will ever pray Etc.

Rd. Sacheverell
