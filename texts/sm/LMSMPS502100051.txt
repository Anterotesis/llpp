To the Worshipful his Majties Justices of the Peace in Quarter Sessions Assembled in & for the County of Middlesex.

The Humble peticon & Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. George the Martye in the County of Surry

Sheweth That Ann Harding with her Two Children (Vizt.) Grace & Benjamin Harding were by Order under the Hands & Seals of Madt: Hewitt & Wm. Cotesworth Esqr. Two of his Majties Justices of the peace of the Said County of Middlesex bearing Date the 30th. Day of January [...] 1722. removed from the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the Said County of Middlesex to the Said Parish of St. George She Martyr as the place where the Said Ann Harding Grace Harding & Benjamin Harding were last legally Settled

That Your peticoners do humbly conceive that they can make it appear to your Worships that the place of the last legal Settlement of the Said Ann Harding Grace & Benjamine Harding is not in the Said parish of St. George the Martyr

Therefore yor. peticoners humbly Appeal against the Said Order & Pray yor Worships will be pleased to Order the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the Said Parish of St. James Clerkenwell to Attend this Court for the hearing of the Said Appeal Such day as yor. Worships Shall think fit
