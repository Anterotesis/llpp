To the Right Worppll: his Maties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlx.

The humble Peticon of Mathew Deane of the Pish of St Leonard Shoreditch in the sd County Carpenter

Sheweth That your Peticoners Late Father Mathew Deane Deced Served ye Office of Churchwarden for the Sd Pish in the Year of our Lord 1711 in which time your Petrs: sd late Father then disbursed & paid the sume of One hundred Seaventy Seaven pounds &Six pence towards the releife of the poor & putting forth poor Children Apprentices & other Incident Charges relateing to his sd Office Over & above what he reced of & from the Inhabitants of the Sd Pish as will & doth appeare by his Accompts being fairely adjusted & Secled by Severall of the Chiefe & most Substantiall Inhabitants of the Sd Pish & approved of Allowed & Confirmed under the hands & Seals of such two Justices of the Peace for the Sd County as the Statute directs. And that your Petr. hath very often applyed himselfe to the Officers & other Inhabitants of the Sd Pish to be reimbursed the Sd Sume of One hundred Seaventy Seaven Pounds & Six Pence & although Sever [...] Arreares of the Poors Rate for the Sd yeare 1711 within the sd Pish [...] not be collected within the yeare but have since been collected & [...] by the late Churchwarden & Overseer of the Poor of the sd Pish & al [...] a Rate was made about Michelmas last past on purpose to reimburse y [...] Petr: the full Sume of One hundred Seaventy Seaven Pounds & Six Pen [...] but the greatest part of that Rate was Converted to other Uses & Your Petr comd never get one Penny untill the Ninth day of March last past when one of the late Churchwardens paid him thereof Seaventy Seaven Pounds & Six pence but that there now doth & still rema [...] due to your Petr: the Sume of One hundred Pounds & that for want of the Sd Money Yor Petr: [...] Now doth & hath ever Since the Dea [...] of his Sd Late Father to whome he is Administr: paid Six Pounds P Cert P Annu for One hundred pounds which he was forced [...] to Borrow for his Support to manage & Carry on his Buisness

Yor Petr: therefore humbly prayes an Order of this Honrall Court that he may be payed the Sd Sume of One hundred Pounds by such ways & means as to your Worsps shall seem meet.

And Your Petr shall ever pray Etc

Mathew Deane
