To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and overseers of the Poor of the Parish of all Saints in the Town and County of Southampton

Sheweth

That by virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of John Floud and George Storey Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 18th. day of November 1797 Mary Boulton singlewoman was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the said County of Midddlesex to the Parish of All Saints in the Town and County of Southampton aforesaid as the Place of her last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that the benefit of their said Appeal may be received to them and that a day may be appointed in the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex for the Hearing and determining the said Appeal and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch be then and there required to attend to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the Court touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc Duill and Matthews Solictors for the Appellants
