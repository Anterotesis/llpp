William Mainwaring Esqr. Chairman and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex on their General Quarter Session of the peace now Assembled

The Humble Petition of Mary Leigh Widow.

Sheweth That your Petitioner is the Daughter of Thomas Dade late Surveyor under the Act or Parliament for the Parishes of Saint George Middlesex and St. Botolph Aldgate Deceased.

That the said Thomas Dade long before his Death was very Old and Inform and that your Petitioner for some years previous thereto had the Care of his Domestic concerns as well as of those of his Bodily and Mental Infirmities

That your Petitioners Fathers pecuniary circumstances was at the [...] of his Death rather Indigent than Affluent.

That those were a Number of Houses covered in at the time of your Petitioners fathers Death the profits of surveying of which your Petitioner with great Deference Submits he would have best Intitled.

Your Petitioner therefore Humbly Prays that your Worships will be pleased to order the Fees for surveying the Houses as covered in as aforesaid may be paid to her on making a satisfaction to the Gentlemen and present appointed to survey the same or to grant such other relief in the premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.

And your Petitioner as on Duty Bound will ever Pray Etc. Mary Leigh

Dated April 12th. 1791
