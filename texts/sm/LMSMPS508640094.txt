To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Overseers of the Poor of the Precinct of White Fryars in the City of London

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Edw: Read and Wm. Hyde Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace acting in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster in for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 25th. day of June 1791 Sarah Rich Wife of Edward Rich (gone from her) and their four Children to wit, Robert aged Ten Years and upwards Thomas aged Six Years and upwards Lucy aged five Years and Sarah aged Two Years and upwards were removed and Conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Martin in the fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to the said Precinct of White Fryars (in the said Order called White friars in the County of Middlesex as the place of the last Legal Settlement of the said Edward Rich and Sarah his Wife and their four Children to wit Robert Thomas Lucy and Sarah Whereby your Petitioner's conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships to appoint a day in this present Sessions to hear and determine their said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
