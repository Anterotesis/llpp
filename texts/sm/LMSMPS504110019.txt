To the Worshipfull Chairman and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in General Sessions Assembled

The Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Twickenham in the said County of Middlesex

Humbly Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order under the Hands & Seals of John Milner Esqr. and Philip Dyot Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the sd: County of Middx bearing Date the 25th Day of April last Complaint had been made unto them by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St: Giles in the Fields in the County afod: that George Baker and Susan his wife were lately come into the said Parish Endeavoering to Settle there Contrary to Law and it did Appear unto the said Justices that they did Adjudge them likely to become Chargeable to the sd. Parish of St. Giles in the fields mad upon Examination of the said Susan taken upon Oath It further Appeared unto the said Justices that they did likewise adjudge the said parish of Twickenham in the said County was the place of the last legal Settlement of the said George & Susan And did thereby Order them to be Removed and Conveyed from the said Parish of St. Giles in the feilds to the said Parish of Twickenham what was done accordingly

That your Appellants Conceiveing themselves to be Aggreived by the Order of the said two Justices do humbly Appeal there from and pray that your Worships will be Pleased to appoint a Day at this present Session when and where all parties may Attend to hear and Abide the Judgement & Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

And Ye. Appellants (as in Duty bound) shall ever pray Etc.

Wm, Keate Agent for the Appellants
