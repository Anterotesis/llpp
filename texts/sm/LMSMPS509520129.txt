To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green on the Second day of July 1798.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Robert Armstrong

Sheweth That in Consequence of a Summons issued under the hand and Seal of Henry James Pye Esquire one of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County and served on Your Petitioner he appeared before the Magestrates at the Public Office in Queen Square Westminster upon an Information exhibited and prosecuted by James Stallwood charging that your Petitioner did in the Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the said County on the eleventh day of April take and receive of George Winton on redeeming the Pledge therein mentioned one Penny by way of profit upon three Shillings on the Seventh day of April aforesaid lent upon a pledge of A Petticoat contrary to the Statute Etc And your Petitioner was upon the hearing of the said Information convicted in the Penalty of five Pounds.

That Your Petitioner conceives himself agrieved by such Conviction and is advised to Appeal against the same.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to Permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this present General Quarter Session of the Peace in and for the said County And that your Worships will appoint a day for hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
