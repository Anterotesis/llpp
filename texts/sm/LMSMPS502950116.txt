To the Rt: Worspl. His Maties Justices of the Peace at their General Quart Sessions Assembled for the County of Middx

The humble Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. George in the said County

Sheweth That by a Pess Warrant or Order under the hands and Seales of Natheniel Chandler and Ralph Harwood Esqrs two of his Maties Justices of the Peace of the County of Middx (One being of the Quorum) bearing date the Seventh day of September 1733 Martha Jackson Singlewoman was remov'd from the Parish of St. Leonard Shoreditch in the Said County to the Said Parish of St. George (comonly calld St. George in the East) in the Said County as the place of her last Legal Settlement

That yrs Petr think themselves aggrievd by the said Pass Warrant or Order and humbly appeale to this Court aggainst the Sume

Sam Cowper att for the Appellants Therefore humbly pray this Court to Appoint a day in this present General Quartr Sessions that all Parties may them Attend to hear and abide the Judgment and determinacon of the Court [...] this Appeale

And yr. Petrs shall ever Pray Etc
