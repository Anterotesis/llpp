Middlesex To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Gregory by Saint Pauls in the City of London

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order or passer Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Samuel Hawkins and John Staples Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid one of when being of the Quorum) bearing Date the fifth Day of January instant Susannah Cook wife of George Cook who hath deserted her was removed and conveyed out of the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the said County to the said Parish of St Gregory [...] by Saint Pauls in the City of London, at the Place of her last legal Settlement

That you Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant

or Order of the said Two Justices do appeal to your Worships therefrom and humbly Pray that you will Please to hear them in the Premises and Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel may appear before you at a Certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of you Worships touching this Appeal,

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc.
