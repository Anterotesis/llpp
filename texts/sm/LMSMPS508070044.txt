To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Session of the Peace in and for the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth

That by an order of Removal under the hands and Seals of Herbert Mays Esquire and William Blackmore Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace Acting in and for the said County (one Where of being of the quorum) bearing date the first day of February Instant Jane the widow of William Brown deceased and her two Children (vizt) Mary aged Seven years and Susanna aged Two years and upwards were removed from the parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint John of Wapping as the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved by the said order and do Appeal against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that a day may be Appointed of this present Session to hear and Determine the Matter of the said Appeal and that all Parties concerned may then Attend

And your Petitioners shall ever pray etc

S: Wegener Sop, for Appellts
