To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant bearing Date the Third Day of September 1785. under the Hands and Seals of James Fielding and William Hyde Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in for the City and Liberty of Westminster (one whereof being of the Quorum) Elizabeth Bone Wife of George Bone (gone from her) was removed from the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields in the said County to the said Hamlet of Hammersmith as the place of her last legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners apprehending themselves aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant of the said two Justices humbly appeal against the same.

And therefore pray the your Worships will be pleased to Order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor

of the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields aforesaid and all persons concerned to Attend this Court same day next Session to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of the Court touching the premises and that your Petitioner may have such relief therein as to your Worships shall seem meet.

And Your Petrs. will ever pray Etc
