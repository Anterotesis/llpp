To the Worshipful Justices at Hicks's Hall Assembled

The Humble Petition of William Kerley

Sheweth That your Petitioner most humbly Acknowledges the Instness of his Sentence but having been Afflicted with the Rumatizm and been in the Hospital nineteen Weeks and came out of the Hospital only a Week before this Unfortunate Accident happend.

That your Petitioner is a Gentlemans Servant but by means of his illness has made away with what trifle of Money he saved when in Place and if Confined according to his Sentence it will Prove his utter recive as he must part with his Cloaths for a Support having no friend in London to Assist him

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Implores your Worships will be pleased to take his Deplorable Situation into Consideration and Afford him such Relief as in your Great Wisdom shall seem meet.

And your Petitioner as in Duty bound will ever pray.
