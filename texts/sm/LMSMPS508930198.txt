To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex assembled at their General Quarter Session of the Peace began & holden the thirteenth day of January in the thirty fourth year of King George the third

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor the Parish of St Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex and Liberty of Westminster

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Sir W Gibbons and Thomas Wood Junior Esquire[---] two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the second day of November 1793 Elizabeth Rowley Widow was removed from the parish of Sunbury in the said County to the said parish of St Clement Danes as the place of her last legal settlement & delivered to your Petitioners to be provided for by them according to Law Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore Appeal against the said Order and humbly pray relief in the premises And that your Worships will be pleased to order that the benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto them, that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of the peace for the said County of Middlesex And that some day in the same Session may be appointed for that purpose

And Your Petitioners shall every pray Etc
