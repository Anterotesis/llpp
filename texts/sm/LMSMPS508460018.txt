To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace assembled,

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 8th day of May last past Martha Baker widow of William Baker was removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to the said Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Martha Baker whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore Appeal against the said Order and humbly pray Releif in the Premes and that your Worships will be Pleased to Appoint some day in the Present Session for the hearing and determining this their said Appeal

And your Petitioners shal ever Pray Etc

Cox Sollr. for Appellants June 1st, 1789
