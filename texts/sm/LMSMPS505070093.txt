To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the peace at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in and for the said County assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Thomas Southwark in the County of Surry

Sheweth That by Virtue of a pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Thomas Quarrill and Christopher Scott Esqrs. two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (one of the Quorum) bearing date the 27th. day of January 1762. Sarah Juckes Single Woman and her two Children one named William aged two Years next May the other named Sarah aged about Six Weeks (in the said Order said to be) both Bastards born out of Lawful Matrimony were removed from the Parish of Saint Mary Mary White Chapel in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of St. Thomas Southwark in the County of Surry as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby Your Petitioners conecive themselves to be agrieved and against the said Order do appeal.

Yor. Petitioners therefore humbly pray that the benefit of the said appeal may be saved to them and that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned untill the next General Quarter Session of the peace to be holden for the said County.

And Yor Petitioners shall pray Etc.
