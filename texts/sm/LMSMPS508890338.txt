To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the for the County of Middlesex in Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Harry Stoe Esqr an Inhabitant of the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham and County of Middlesex

Sheweth That on or about the fourth day of October 1793 a Rate or Assessment is as made for the Relief of the Poor of the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham in the County of Middlesex and which Rate was allowed by Nathaniel Conant Esquire and Phillip Neve Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said County on the fifth day of the said Month of October

That your Petitioner being aggreived by such Rate craves leave to Appeal therefrom and the grounds of such his Appeal are as follows

First Because your Petitioner in Rated for the Premises which he Occupies beyond the Value of them

Secondly Because Thomas Dean John Horton Thomas Chambers and divers others whose Names are Mentioned in the Book or rate and who severally hold rateable Property in the said Hamlet are not rated at all in respect of such property

Thirdly Because Stephen Randall is not rated in respect of land at or near Shepherds Bush in the said Hamlet supposed to be held by him under the

Trustees for Kensington Parish

Fourthly Because the said Rate is in other Respects partial unjust and Oppresive

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays Your Worships to take the premises into Consideration and that the said Rate on Assessment may be Quashed or that your Petitioner may have such Relief in the premises as to Your Worships shall seem meet And that your Worships will Appoint a day in the present Sessions for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the sd. Hamlet of Hammersmith and all other Persons concerned to Attend to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the court touching the said Appeal

And your Petr Etc

Harry Stoe
