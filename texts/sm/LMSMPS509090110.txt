Middlesex To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in this General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition of Richard Troy of three Cup Yard in the Parish of Saint George the Martyr in the County of Middlesex Farrier

Sheweth That by a certain Order in Writing bearing date the tenth day of march last under the Hands and Seals of Nathaniel Conant and John Scott Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (Riciting that Information and Coal Saint was or the seventh Day of march last made unto John Scott and Phillip Neve Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of this Peace in and for the said County by Elizabeth Tennant of Craven Yard Drury Lane in the Parish of Saint Clement Danes Widow that there was on Christmas Day last due and in arrear to her the said Elizabeth Tennant the Sum of Fifty eight Pounds two Shillings and six pence from Luke Peach of Craven Yard aforesaid Coach Master and Hackneyman for rent of a certain Dwelling House Coach House Stables and Granary in Craven Yard aforesaid but by her to the said John Peach and that the said John Peach had frandulently and clandestinely removed and Conveyed way certain goods and Chattels of him the said John peach not exceeding the Value of fifty Pounds namely of the Value of Parts six pounds to present the said Elizabeth Tennant from distraining the same for the said Arrears of Rent and that Your petitioner and our Arnold Coup land of the Parish of saint Clement Dannis aforesaid Inn Keeper wilfully and know [...] rgly aided and assisted the said John Peach in so fraudulently and Clandestinely receiving and conveying away certain Part of the said Goods and Chattels and in concealing the same And reciting that your Petitioner had been duly sum said and to appear [...] kipecithe to an such the Matter of the said Complaint and Information and that it appearing and being fully served before the

said Justices that Your Petitioner wilfully and two wright aided and assisted the said John Peach in so removing and conveying away Paul of the said Goods and Chattels of the said John Peach So removed as aforesaid not exceeding the Value of fifty Pounds namely five Houses of the Value of forty Pounds and in concealing the same) Your Petitioner was ordered and adjudged to pay the Sum of eighty Pounds of Lawful Money of Great Britain (being doubt the Value of the said five Houses) to the said Elizabeth Tennant or ar before the twenty first Day of the said Month of March Whereby Your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore humbly appeals against the said Order and Prays that the same may be quashed and that the hearing and determining of the said Appeal may he adjourned until the next General Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex

Lord Your Petr. shall ever pray Etc
