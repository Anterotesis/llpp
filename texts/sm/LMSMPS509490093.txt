No. 2

To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex assembled at the General Session of the peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green on the Day of May 1798

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Jeremiah Mangaar of Spital Fields Pawn broker

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the 18th day of May last Convicted in the penalty of Five pounds by Cranley Thomas Kerby Esquires one of the Justices of the peace for the said County upon an Information exhibited and prosecuted by James Stallwood changing That your Petitioner did in the parish of Christ Church in the said County on the Twelfth day of May 1798 take and receive of John Smith the Sum of One penny by way of profit upon three Shillings theretofore to wit on the eleventh day of May aforesaid lent upon a pledge that is to say two pair of Stockings contrary to the Statute Etc.

That your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by such Conviction and is advised to Appeal against the same.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays your Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this present General Session of the Peace in and for the said County and that your Worships will appoint a Day for hearing and determining the same

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc.
