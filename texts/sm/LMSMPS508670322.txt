To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of two Peace for the County of Middlesex at the General Session of two Peace holdens in and for the said County Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Henry Harford of the Parish of Saint Sepulchre in the County of Middlesex Grocer.

Sheweth That your Petitioner thinking himself aggrieved by a certain Rate or Assessment made by two Vestrymen of that part of the Parish of Saint Sepulchre which is in two County of Middlesex on the 7th day of July last past and commencing at Lady try last for the Relief and employment of the Poor in the said Part of the said Parish on the Twenty Ninth day of July now last Past appealed against the said Rate before Joseph Hackney and Charles Inquiet Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace Residing and Acting in and for the said County of Middlesex and thereupon the said Justices afterwards that is to say on the said Twenty Ninth. day of July now last past heard the said Appeal and determined the same by allowing the aforesaid Rate or Assessment.

That Your Petitioner being dissatisfied with the determination of the said Justices on his aforesaid Appeal to them now continues his Appeal against the said Rate or Assessment and also against the determination of the said Justices thereon for that Your Petitioner is charged at the same of Fifty Pounds as and for the Yearly Rent of a certain Messuage or Tenament and Premises in the Occupation of year Petitioner Situate

in Saint John [...] Street in the said Part of the said Parish when your Petitioner ought not to be charged in the said Rate or Assessment at any greater Rent and two Yearly Rent of Thirty Pounds for and in Respect of the said Messuage or Tenement and Premises And for that Your Petitioner is cleansed in and by the said rate and Assessment much more than several other Persons inhabiting in the said Parish in Proportion to the Yearly Rent or Value of the Premises they Respectively Occupy in the said Part of the said Parish are cleansed in and by the said Rate or Assessment in Particular your Petitioner is charged much more that Walter Hattam, George Fillingham, Moses Barrett, John Lorkin, William Griffin and John Willshire are severally charged in and by the said Rate or Assessment in Proportion to the Yearly Rents of the several and Respective Premises for which they and your Petitioner are severally and Respectively charged in the said Rate or Assessment And also for that the said Rate or Assessment is in many Respects Partiall and unequall.

Your Petitioner therefore Humbly Prays that he may be relieved by being Charged in the said Rate or Assessment at the Rate of thirty Pounds a Year instead of the same which your Petitioner now stands Charged with and that the said Rate or Assessment may be amended accordingly or grant him met other Relief as to your Worships shall seem meet And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc.

Septr. 15th 1791

Henry Hurford
