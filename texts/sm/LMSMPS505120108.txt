To the Worshipfull his majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middx at the General Quarter Sessions of the peace assembled

The Humble petition & appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant Or Order under the hands and Seals of James Esdaile and Benjamin Cowley Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 15th. Day of May 1762. Jane Politer widow of John Politer deceased and her Four Children namely Charles aged thirteen years Elizabeth aged five year Sarah aged about three years and William aged about Six months were removed from the parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex to the parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London as the place of their last legal Settlement

That your petitioners Conceive themselves agrieved by the said Warrant or Order and humbly appeal against the same

Your petitioners therefore humbly pray that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day of this present Session to hear & abide the Judgment And determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

And your petrs. shale every pray Etc
