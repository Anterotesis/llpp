Middlesex to wit To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Chairman and the rest of His Majesty's Justices assigned to keep the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex and also to hear and determine divers Felonies Trespasses and other Misdemeanors Committed within the said County in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of James Vandercom

Sheweth That in and by a certain Warrant of Commitment bearing Date the Ninth Day of March last and directed to the Governor of the House of Correction at Clerkenwell or his Deputy under the Hand and Seal of James Pettit Andrews Esquire one of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County Testing housemen petitioner [...] said been that day brought before him the said James Pettit Andrews by John Cridland in the said Warrant Described to be one of the Constables of the Publick Office in Queens Square Westminster apprehended by him the said John Cridland on the twenty Seventh Day of February then and now last in Pitt Street in the Parish of Panecras within the said County and also reciting that your Petitioner and Appellant had been examined by and before the said Justices and that it duly appeared to him the said Justice upon the Oath of John Humpage a Credible Witness that Your Petitioner and Appellant was a Person of evil Fame and a reputed Thief and that your Petitioner and Appellant had not been able to give a Satisfactory account of himself to him the said Justice and of his way of his way of Living and that it had also appeared to the satisfaction of him the said Justices upon the Oath of the said John Cridland that there was just ground to believe that your Petitioner and appellant was in the said Street in the Parish aforesaid in the County aforesaid on the said Twenty Seventh Day of February with Intent to Commit felony on the Persons and Property of HIs Majestys Justices there being It appeal that your Petitioner and appellant was by the said Justices convicted of the Offence aforesaid and that the said Justice adjudged and deemed your Petitioner and appellant for the Cause aforesaid to be a Rogue and Vagrabond within the Intent and meaning of the Statute made [...] in the Seventeenth year of His late Majesty King George the Second Intituled an Act to amend and make more effectual the Laws relating to Rogers and Vagabond and other Houses of Correction whereby your Petitioner and to Houses of Correction whereby your Petitioner and Appellant concernes himself aggrieved

That your Petitioner and appellant on or about the twenty first of march last entered into a Recognizance with two sufficient sureties for his appear once at the present session to try his appeal against the said correction

Your Petitioner therefore humbly appeals to this Court against the said Conviction and prays your Worship will be pleased to appoint some day in the present Session for Hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner will ever pray Etc James Vandercon
