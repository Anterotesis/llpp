To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex at the General Quarter Session of the peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London

Sheweth That By Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Lord Carpenter and Thomas Rea Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty Ninth day of July last past Sarah Elwood the Widow of John Elwood aged about Forty years, And her two Children by her said late Husband deceased (to wit) Elizabeth aged about Six years and Lucy aged about three years were Removed from the parish of Saint George Hanover Square In the County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint Giles Cripplegate in the City of London as the place of their last Legal Settlement

That your petitioners Conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order and humbly appeal against the same

Your petitioners therefore humbly pray That your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day of this present Session That all parties may Attend to hear & abide the Judgment and Determinacon of this Court touching the said appeal

Thos: Ibell Fras Albone} Churchwardens

William Hawksworth Tobias Benton} Overseers
