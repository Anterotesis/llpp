To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and to the rest of the Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Thomas Smith of Great Windmill Street in the Parish of St: James Westminster in the said County of Middlesex Chimney - Sweeper

Sheweth That your Petitioner and Appellant in and by a certain Conviction in writing under the hand and Seal of Robert Baker Esquire one of the Justices of our Sovereign Lord the King assigned to keep the Peace and also to hear and determine divers Felonies Tres- -passes and other Misdemeanors done and committed within the said County of Middlesex acting at the Public Office in Hatton Garden in the said County bearing date the Twenty fourth day of May one thousand seven hundred and Ninety nine was Convicted by the said Robert Baker For that he the said Thomas Smith being a Person exercising the business of a Chimney Sweeper did in the Morning of the Twenty Eighty day of February last past unlawfully cause James Potter his Apprentice Boy to call the Streets before Seven o'Clock in the Morning, the same being between Michaelmas -day and Lady-day against the Statute Etc: And the said Robert Baker did adjudge Your Petitioner and Appellant to forfeit and pay the sum of

[...] of Five Pounds to be distributed as the Law directs Whereby your Petitioner thinketh himself aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Appeals to this Court against the said Conviction And prays that your Worships will appoint a day in this present Session for the hearing and determining of this Your Petitioners Appeal.

17th: June 1799

The Mark of [x] Thomas Smith
