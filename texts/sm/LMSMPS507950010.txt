To William Mainwaring Esquire Chairman of the Sessions of the peace for the County of Middlesex and others his Majesty's Justices Assigned to keep the peace in the said County in their General Session of the peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of Day Collingridge An Apprentice

Humbly Sheweth That your Petitioner was on the Sixteenth day of June in the year of our Lord One thousand Seven hundred and Eighty three bound an Apprentice by Indenture of that date to John Fairbairn of Great Fitchfield Street Oxford Markes in the County of Middlesex Joiner and Cabinet maker for the Term of Seven Years from thence next ensuing -At whose binding out. the Sum of Twenty one pound was given as an apprentice &c.

That your Petitioner has continued in the Service of the said John Fairbairn his Master ever since the date of the said Indenture That about fourt months as his said Master quilled his House in Fitch field Street and look a Public House in Portman mews in which house he hath continued to live ever since, and during which time your Petitioner hath frequently been in want of Employ and of being taught and instructed in the said Art of a Joiner and Pabinct maker and fort want of such instruction from his master he is rendered incapable of getting and Livelyhood and is totally neglected by his said Master or any other person in teaching him in that Business and hath refused to return any part of the premium Paid or to Provide your Petitioner with any other master and your Petitioner did there upon [...] apply to the Majstrates who have bound the master in Recognizance to appear this present Session to Answer the said Complaint

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays your worships will be pleased to take his Case into your Consideration and Appoint a day to near your Petitioner's Complaint and Grant him such Relief as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioner will ever Pray &c
