To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in then General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of Stephen Boone of Sunbury in the County of Middlesex Surgeon and Apothecary

Sheweth That on the thirteenth day of August in the year of our Lord one thousand seven hundred and sixty six one George Smith by the consent and approbation of Samuel Cox and George Chandler Esquires bound himself Apprentice to your Petitioner to serve him from the date of the said Indar [...] for Seven years to Learn the Art and Mistery of Surgeon and At others

That in pursuance of the said Indenteres the said George Smith entered on such Service and continued with your Petitioner her [---] October last about which tune the said George Smith behaved in so outrasions a manner to your Petitioners other Servants that your Petitioner was obliged to deliver up the said George Smith to his Guardians to present worse consequence

That during the time the said George Smith was in your Petitioners Service he always neglected your Petitioners Business and when your Petitioner refremanded the said George Smith for such behaviour he the said Smith would sell your Petitioner it was not in his power to Learn the said Trade or business and therefore refused to give himself any Trouble about the same and was continually quarrelling with your Petitioners Family being fully determined on quitting your Petitioners Service notwithstanding the said Indentures

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships that the George Smith your Petitioners said Apprentice may be discharged for the remainder of the said Term from his said Apprenticeship He by his counsel and Guardians consenting thereto

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc AHancock follr for Nw Boone
