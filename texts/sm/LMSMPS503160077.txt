To The Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Hkeston in the County of Derby

Sheweth That by an order bearing date the Twenty Second day of March in the Year of our Lord One thousand Seven hundred and thirty five under the hands and Seals of George Bourne Esquire and Henry Gough Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex I was Ordered that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Enfield in the County of Middlesex should forthwith Remove and Convey George Bargin and Katherine his wife and Mary Bargin and Jonathan Burgin their two Children from the Said Parish of Enfield to the said Parish of Hkeston in the County of Derby and deliver them unto the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor there

That your Petitioners think themselves Agreived by the said Order and humbly pray to have Such Releife in the Premisses as to your Worships shall seem meet and the nature of their Case requires

And your Petitioners Shall ever pray Etc.

Henry Wilmot Soll for the Appellants
