To the Right Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeale of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Michael Bassishaw London

Sheweth That By Virtue of a Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of John Venner - and - John Tarver Esqrs. Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the Said County of Middlesex (One Thereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Fifteenth day of June One Thousand Seven hundred and thirty three Jane Heath Singlewoman is passed from the Town of new Brentford in the said County to the said Parish of Saint Michael Bassishaw London Alledging the last legal Settlement of the said Jane Heath to be there

Your Peticoners conceiving themselves agreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices doe humbly appeal therefrom [...] unto this Court And humbly pray that Your Worships would appoint Some day the next General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be held for the said County to hear this Appeal And That your Worships will make an Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Town of new Brentford aforesaid and all Partyes concerned in and about the Premisses may then attend hear and abide by the final Determinacon of your Worships

And Your Peticoners Shall ever pray
