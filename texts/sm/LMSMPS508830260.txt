Sessions B R. no. 1434

To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Joseph Cooper an Inhabitant of the Parish of Chiswick in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by a certain Rate or Assesement made for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish of Chiswick in the said County of Middlesex on the Thirty first Day of January One Thousand seven Hundred and Ninety three and a afterwards allowed by two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County and published in the Chwick of the said Parish on the seventeenth day of February One Thousand seven Hundred and ninety three Year Petitioner and many other Inhabitants and Occupiers of Houses and Lands in the said Parish are in and by the said Rate or Assesement charged and Assessed in several Sums of Money for the relief of the Poor of the said Parish.

That at the Time of making the said Rate or Assessement the Reverend James Frebeck was and now is Vicar of the said Parish and the said James Frebeck is rated in and by the said Rate or Assessment at the Sum of One Hundred pounds a Year for his Tythes as Vicar of the said Parish by the Description of The Reverend Mr. Frebeck for Vicorial Tythes when as your Petitioners Apprehends the said James Frebeck ought to be rated in and by the said Rate or Assessment at the Sum of One Hundred and Twenty three pounds a year at the least for his Tythes as Vicar of the said Parish.

That at the Time of making the said Rate or Assessment Jane- Sanbury, Thomas Clifford, Walter Walham, and Samuel Devolle were and now are Inhabitants and Occupiers of Houses in the said Parish but the

said Jane Sansbury, Thomas Clifford, Waltor Walham and Samuel Devolle are not nor is any or either of them by the said Rate or Assessment charged and Assessed in any sum of Money whatsoever for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish.

That Henry Robins named in the said Rate or Assessment was at the Time of making the said Rate or Assessment and now is an Inhabitant and, the Occupier of certain Lands and other Permises which he holds of John Johnson and Jane Sansbury but the said Henry Robins is not in and by the said Rate or Assessment charged in any sum of Money whatsoever for the relief of the Poor of the said Parish for and in respect of the said relief Lands and premises.

That at the Time of making the said Rate or Assessment James Ward and John Plastow named in the said Rate or Assessment were and now are Inhabitants and Occupiers of Houses in the said Parish but the said James Ward and John Plastow are and each of them is charged and Assessed towards the said Relief in Lesser Sums of Money than each of them ought to be in respect to other Inhabitants and Occupiers of Houses and Lands in the said Parish who are charged and Assessed towards he said Relief.

That your Petitioners for the Reasons aforesaid hath material Objections to the said Rate or Assessment and Apprehends himself to be aggrieved thereby and that the said Rate or Assessment is illegal by reason of the manner of making the same and therefore humbly Appeals against the said Rate or Assesssment and

Wm Chetham Solr. for the Appellant } Your Petitioner prays that the said Rate or Assesement may be quashed or that your Petitioner may have such relief in the Premises as to your worships shall seem meet.
