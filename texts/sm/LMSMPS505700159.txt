To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace at Hicks Hall in Saint Johns Street in and for the said County Said Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlett of Kew in the County of Surry

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Thomas Kynaston and Henry Lambe Esqrs. two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Eighth day of March 1766 Samuel Clarke and Jane his wife with his two Children by a former wife therein named and also the Child of the Said Jane by Thomas Simpson her former Husbands also therein named (for nurture) were removed from and out of the Parish of Ealing in the Said County of Middlesex to the said Hamlett of Kew in the County of Surry as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Order and pray that the said Court will be Pleased to Order that Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Ealing and all Persons Concerned do Attend this Court Some day in the next Session of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex To hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal and that the benefit of hearing the Said Appeal at that Sessions may be Saved to your Petitioners And that your Petitioners may be otherwise relieved in the Premises as the said Court Shall see fit

And your Petition Shall ever pray Etc.

John Blake Soll. for the Appellants,
