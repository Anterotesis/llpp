To the Right Worship full his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Sessions at Hicks Hall assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church wardens and Over seers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Paul Covent Garden in the said County.

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of Miles Harper and John Ellis Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace (Quor unus)for the City and Libty of Westmr. as their in is mentioned. bearing- date the Twelfth day of June Instatt William Mellens aged about Forty Seven Years and Ann his Wife were removed from the Parish of St. James Westminister in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of St. Paul Covent Gardens their last Legall place of Settlement.-

Your Petitioner thinking themselves Aggreived by the Judgement of the said Two Justices of the Peace Appeal to this Court against the same and desire to be releived there in

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray, and so forth

Middlesex to witt At the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace of our Lord the King Holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in St. John Street in the said County on Monday to witt the twenty Fifth day of June in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Soveraign Lord George the second King of Great Britain and so Forth.

It is Ordered by this Court that the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. James Westminister in this County have Notice of this Appeal, and that they and all persons Concerned do attend this Court on Friday next at ten of the Clock in the Forenoon to hear and abide the Judgement and Determina- -tion of this court touching the said Appeale.

by the Court Walter

Hughes for the Parish of St. Paul Convent Garden
