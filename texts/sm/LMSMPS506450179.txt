To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Philip Dyot and John Cox Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Eighth day of August 1774 Catherine Moody Singlewoman aged about 24 years was removed from the Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the said County Alledging the said Parish of Saint John of Wapping to be the place of her last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order or Warrant of the said two Justices Do appeal to your Worships therefore and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the Premises And order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields may appear before you at a certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships touching this appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

John Massingarb Richd Martyr} Church Wardens of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping

Jno: Holmes Thos. Eames John Betts P Callaphan} Overseers of the Poor of the same Parish
