To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the [---] Hamlet of Penge in the Parish of Battersea in the County of Surry.

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of P: Colguhoun and Daniel Williams Esquires Two of this Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 31st. day of October 1795 William Wortley and Sarah his Wife and their two Lawful Children Vizt. Rebecca aged 12 Years and upwards and William Aged Six Years or thereabouts were conveyed and Removed from and out of the Parish of Christ Church in the County of Middlesex to the said Hamlet of Penge in the said Parish of Battersea as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said Two Justices and their said Order and pray that your said Worships will be pleased to appoint a day to hear and determine the said appeal.

And Your Petitioners will pray Etc.
