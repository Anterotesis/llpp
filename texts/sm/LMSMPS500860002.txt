Humbly Sheweth

That Yor Peticoner in the Yeare 1701 was Churchwarden of the parish of St. Buttolph without Aldgate in the said County. In which said Office Yor. Peticoner did disburse and Expence for the use of the Poor of the said parish, more than what he had Collected or received the same of Fourteene pounds nine Shillings and Nine pence Which said Account hath been audited and allowed by the Antients of the said parish, By whose report the same hath likewise been allowed of and Confirmed by Robert Bateman & Robert Constable Esqr two of the Maties: Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middx; as by the said Audite and Confirmacon may appeare.

And That Yor: Peticoner hath made frequent Applycacons to John Wright & Edward Walker the prent Chuchwardens of the said parish of St Buttolph without Aldgate in the said County for payment of the sume of Fourteene pounds Nine Shillings & Nine pence So due to Yor: Peticoner as aforesaid Who refuses to pay the same not with standing the Antients have given them a rate of One and Twenty Months As appeares by the Poores Booke To reinburse Yor: Peticoner herein.

Wm. Balch. Sellr Humbly therefore prays Yor: Worshippd (The premisses Considered) to be pleased to appointe a day That the said John Wright and Edward Walker may Attend Yor: Worshipp and shew cause why they should not pay yor: Peticoner the said sume of Fourteene pounds Nine Shillings & Nine pence so due as Aforesaid.

And Yor: Peticoner Shall ever Prays

Bryan: Bentham
