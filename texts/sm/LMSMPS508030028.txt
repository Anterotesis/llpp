To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George Hanover square in the said County.

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass warrant or order under the handsaid seals of W Addington and 9 Reading Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 27th. of June 1785 Lydia Woodrow aged about 37 years singlewoman was removed and conveyed from the Parish of St. Clement Danes in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square as the place of her last legal settlement whereby your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved and Appeal to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioners humbly pray this Court that the benefit of the said Appeal be saved unto them until the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex and that all Persons concerned may then attend to hear and abide the Judgment of this Court touching the said Appeal.

And Your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
