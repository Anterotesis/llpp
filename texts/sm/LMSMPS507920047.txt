To the Worshipfull William Mainwaring Esquires Chairman and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled:

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Anne in the County

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of William Blackmore Esquires and the reverend Herbert Maye Doctor in Divinity two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in for the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 13th. day of December 1784 whereby Joseph Scott was removed and conveyed from and out of Parish of Saint George Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Anne Middlesex as the Place of the last legal settlement of the said Joseph Scott.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant humbly Appeal against the same to your Worships and pray relief in the Premises & that a day in this present Session may be appointed fro the hearing & Determining the said Appeal And Your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc

Jno. Skutt Sol for Appellant Goodmans Filed Jan 10th 178 [...]
