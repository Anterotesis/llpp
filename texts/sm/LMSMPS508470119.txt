Middlesex To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and others his Majesty's Justices of the peace Assembled at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace held at the Sessions House in and for the County of Middlesex on Monday the Sixth day of July in the Twenty ninth year of the Reign of King George the Shir [...]

The humble Petitioner of Charles Simpkin and Ann Orgill of Oxford Street in the parish of Saint Mary le Bone in the County of Middlesex Plumber

Sheweth That your Petitioner were on the 28th. day of May last convicted before John Barnfather and Edward gray Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County on the information of Thomas Rogers Surveyor of the District of Saint Mary le Bone in the said County appointed under an Act of Pariliament made and Passed in the 14th. Year of the Reign of our said Lord the King for erecting or causing to be erested a projection to the front of their House Situate at No. 146 on the North side of Oxford Street aforesaid next to the public Highway in the Parish Saint Mary le Bone aforesaid beyond the limits of ten Inches allowed by the said Act for the projection of Stall Boards to Shop Fronts in Streets 30 Feet wide or more That is to say, two Fees & upward And that the Cornish of the said Front did project beyond the limits of 18 Inches allowed by the said Act for the Covering of Shop fronts in Streets, 30 feet or more that is to say two feet and six inches contrary to the said Act And the said Justices on hearing of the said Information adjudged the said Projection to be a common nuisance against which Judgment your Petitioners appealed and entered into a recognizance in manner as is directed by the said act of 14th. George 3d to Prosecute their Said appeal

Your Petitioners therefore think themselves

aggrieved by the Judgment of the said Justices and humbly pray the Court will take their Case into consideration and appoint a time for the hearing of this their Petition and Appeal

[...] :Smith Solicitor for the said Charles Simpkin and Ann orgill

Middlesex (is wit) At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holden at the new Session House in Clerkenwell Green in and for the said County of Middlesex (by Adjournment) on Tuesday the seventh day of July in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third King of Great Britain etc.

It is Ordered by this Court that John Barnfather and Edward Gray Esquires the Justices within named and also the within named Thomas Rogers have Notice of this Petition and Appeal and that they and all Parties concerned do attend this Court on Thursday next the Ninth day of July Instant at the new Session House on Clerkenwell Green at Ten o'Clock in the Forenoon to hear and abide the Determination of the same Court touching the said appeal

By the Court Selby
