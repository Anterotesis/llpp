To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Session Assembled at Hicks Hall in St. John Street in and for the said County

The humble Petition of William Pentlow and John Turner Keepers of his Majesties Prisons or Goals of New Prison Clerkenwell and Clerkenwell Bridewell in the said County

Most Humbly Sheweth that your Petitioners at the last Quarter Session held for the said County at Hicks Hall aforesaid Your Petitioner then most humbly Presented their Petition unto your Worships Praying for some relief towards the great Expences they are at in Attending the Magistrates with Prisoners on Re Examinations, Attending with Prisonrs Admitted Evidences at Hicks Hall and the Old Bailey and in being Obliged to pay the Window Tax and for Water to Supply the Prisoners in their respective Prisons Etc Your Worships were then pleas'd to Order your Petitionrs. to lay their Petition again before you as this Day And that your Worships would then Consider of and grant us a Sufficient relief as to the paymt. of the said Window Lights and Water and for which your Petitioners as in Duty Bound will for ever pray Etc

Wm. Pentlow Jno. Turner
