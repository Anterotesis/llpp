To the Rt. Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or order Under the Hands and Seals of John Cox and Stephen Clerke Esqr. two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the 19th. day of June 1755 Thomas Hawkesley and his two Children by Alice his late Wife deced Virt. Roger aged Seven Years & a half & upward and Mary Kendall aged three Years & upward were removed from the Parish of St. Giles in the Fields in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westmr. in the said County as the place of their last legal Settlement Whereby Your Petitioners think themselves Aggrieved and appeal to this Court Against the same

Your Petitioner therefore Humbly Pray this Court will Please to appoint a time for hearing & determining the siad Appeal and that all Persons concerned May then Attend.

And Your Petitioner Shall ever Pray Etc.

Morgan Gwynn William Morse:} Overseers of the Poor
