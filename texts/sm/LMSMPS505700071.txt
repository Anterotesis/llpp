To the Worshipfull His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session at Hicks Hall Assembled.

The Humble Petition of John Randall of the parish of Chelsea in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth. That your Petitioner was Appointed High Constable of and for the Division of Hensington in the County of Middlesex within the Hundred of Ossutston at Easter Sessions 1763

That he hath performed the Duties of his Office ever Since to the best of his Ability and Should very Chearfully a Execute the same till your Worships without his Application should please to Nominate Another in his stead but that his further Continuance in the said Office will be detrimental to him in his Business which is that of a Timber Merchant as a the due performance of his Office necessarily hinders him in his own Affairs

That there are Several proper persons for the Service of the said Office in his stead residing in the said Division who have not yet Served, Some of whom are under named for the Approbation of your Worships

That your Petitioners hath Settled and Cleared his Accounts with the Treasurer

Your Petitioner therefore Humbly prays your Worships that you will be pleased to Discharge him from his said Office

Wm. Lambert of Chelsea Carpenter John Clarke, of Hens: Bricklayer Wm. Cox of Hens: Farrier

And he will ever Pray Etc

John Randall
