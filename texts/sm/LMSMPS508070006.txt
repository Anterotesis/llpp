To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace assembled

The humble Petition and appeal of Ann the Wife of William Athill of the parish of Christ Church Spittalfields in the County of Middlesex weaver.

Humbly Sheweth

That by an order or Conviction under the hands and Seals of John Staples and Samuel Hawkins Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the nineteenth day of August 1785 your petitioner was convicted for unlawfully buying and receiving of one Sarah Heath Six ounces Weight of Bengal raw Silk the property of William Hopkins of Goodmans fields in the County of Middlesex Silk Thrower and Intrust [...] with Mary Artis to reiced or Manufacture your petitioner well knowing the said Silk then before to be purloined and Embezzeid

That your petitioner humbly Conceiver herself aggrieved by the said Conviction.

Therefore doth appeal against the Same and humbly prays that the said Conviction may be quashed and that your petitioner may have Such further and other relief as to your worships Shall Seem Meet.

And your petitioner Shall ever pray
