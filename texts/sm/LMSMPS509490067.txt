To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session assembled at the Session House on Clerkenwell Green.

The humble Petition of Freeman Harford and John Vickris Taylor.

Sheweth That your Petitioners were on the Twenty Second day of December one thousand seven hundred and Ninety Seven rated a Sum of Five Hundred and Sixty Seven Pounds for their Brewhouse and Premises situate in the Parish of Saint Anne in the County of Middlesex by the Commissioners asking under a certain Act of Parliament passed in the Twenty Second year of the Reign of Sung George the Third Entituled an Act for paving and freeing from Nuisances and Anneyances the "several Streets Ways and Places within the Parish of Saint Anne in the County of Middlesex and Queen "Street and London Street in the Hamlet of Ratcliffe within the said Parish and for making Ways or "Streets into the Street called Risby's Rope Ground and through or Across Shoulder of Mutton Alley and a "Street called Ropemaker's Fields to Three Colt Street and from the Bridge in the said Street called Risbys Rope Ground into Three Colt Street aforesaid"

That your Petitioners humbly conceive that they are greatly over rated or assessed for their said Premises being charged as above the Sum of Five Hundred and Sixty Seven Pounds which greatly exceeds the Real Value of the said Premises.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the assessment of the said Commissioners and pray That your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day to hear and determine the said Appeal

And your Petitioners as in Duty bound will ever pray Etc

Edward Walter Solicitor for the said Freeman Harford and John Vickers Taylor
