To the Right Worpl. his Maties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx at their General Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble Peticon and Appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. George in the Said County of Middx

Sheweth That by Virtue of W Pass warrant under the hands and Seals of St. Charles Peers Knt. and Stephen Martin Leake Esqr. two of his Maties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx (Quor unus) bearing date the 13th day of July 1732 Henry Wheler Jane his wife and their Son named Henry Wheler aged about 19 years were Sent and delivered by the Church warden and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlet of Poplar and Blackwell to the Parish of St. George above said as the place of their last Legal Settlement

That Your Petrs conceive themselves aggrieved by the Said Pass warrant and Judgement of the said two Justices and do most humbly Appeal to your Worsps there from

And humbly pray your Worsps. will be pleased to hear them in the premisses and Appoint a day in this present General Sessions for hearing the Said Appeal And Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlet of Poplar and Blackwell and all parties concerned may then Appear and hear and abide the determinacon of this Court touching the Said Appeal

And they Shall ever Pray Etc
