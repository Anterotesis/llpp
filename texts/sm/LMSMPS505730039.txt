To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of William Deane and Edm: Byron Esquires two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 19th. day of June 1767 Elizabeth Evers Wife of John Evers (who is now in the Middlesex Hospital) and her two Children by her said Husband (to wit) Frances Aged two Years and Elizabeth Jane aged six Months, were removed from the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of Westminster aforesaid to the said Parish of Saint James as the place of their last legal Settlement Whereby Your Petitioners think themselves Aggrieved and Appeal to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioners therefore Pray this Court will be pleased to appoint a day for the Hearing and Determining of the said Appeal and that all persons concerned may then Attend.

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc. Thos: Thomas Sobr. for Appellts.
