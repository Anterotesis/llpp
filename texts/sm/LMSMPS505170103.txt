Hicks Hall Middlesex} To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace Assembled at their General Sessions of the Peace held as Hicks's Hall in Saint John Street in and for the County of Middlesex on Friday the Tenth Day of December One thousand Seven hundred and Sixty Two

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Hanwell in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by certain Order of Removal bearing date on the Twenty Ninth Day of November One thousand Seven hundred and Sixty Two and made by Subscribed with the Hands and Seals of Henry Lambe and William Godfrey Esquires Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the Said County directed to your Petitioners and To the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor the Parish of Ealing in the County aforesaid Renting that Whereas Complaint had been made by the said Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Ealing unto them the said two Justices of the Peace whereof one was of the Quorum That Elizabeth Wilkinson Single Woman had lately intruded into the said Parish of Ealing there to inhabit as a Parishioner contrary to the Laws relating to the Settlement of the Poor and was there likely to become chargeable if not timely presented And further Renting that Whereas upon due Examination and Enquiry made into the Premisses as well upon the Oath of the said Elizabeth Wilkinson as Otherwise it then Appeared unto them the said Two Justices of the Peace And they Accordingly Adjudged that the said Elizabeth Wilkinso was like to become chargeable unto the said Parish of Ealing And that the last legal place of Settlement of the said Elizabeth Wilkinson was in the said Parish of Hanwell in the said County of Middlesex And by the And by the Said Order they the said Two Justices Ordered and required the said Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Ealing aforesaid that they or some of them should forthwith remove and Convey the said Elizabeth Wilkinson from their said Parish of Ealing to the said Parish of Hanwell and her deliver to your Petitioners or some or one of us By which said Order we were likewise required in his Majesties Name to receive the said Elizabeth Wilkinson into our said Parish and to Provide for her as our own Parishioner or to that Effect as by the said Order when produced will appear Referrence being there unto had

That your Petitioners and Appellants think themselves Aggrieved by the said Order Because the last Legal place of Settlement of the said Elizabeth Wilkinson was [...] in the said Parish of Ealing and not in your Petitioners said Parish of Hanwell

Wherefore your Petitioners and Appellants humbly hope that the said Order of the said Two Justices may be Quashed And that your Worships will appoint a Pay in this present Sessions of the Peace for the Hearing and Determinning of this Appeal or adjourn the same over to a certain Day in your next General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be here held for the said County in the Month of January next

And Your Petitioners and Appellants Shall ever pray Etc

Geo: Pasmore Sollr. for the Appellants
