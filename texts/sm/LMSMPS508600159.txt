To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the peace assembled

The Humble petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Aylesbury in the County of Bucks

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of St George Booth Baronet and Rupert Clarke Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid one whereof being of the Quorum bearing date the fourth Day of this Instant January Thomas Ailward Frances his Wife and their Son William aged about there Quarter of a Year were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Aylesbury in the County of Bucks aforesaid [...] Ailward and his said Wife and Child by which your petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your petitioners therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the said Oder and Adjudication of the said two Justices and in regard the said Order or pass Warrant was not received by your petitioners until Wednesday the Fifth Day of this Instant January and your petitioners therefore could not give Notice of their intention to Appeal in due time for this present Session Your Petitioners therefore pray that their said Appeal may be entered as of this present Session and that the Hearing thereof may be adjourned to the next General Quarter Sessions to be then and there heard at such Day as your Worships shall appoint

And your petitioners shall ever pray Etc
