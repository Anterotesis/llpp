To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in General Sessions assembled The humble Petition of Isaac Seedsman

Sheweth. That your Petitioner by Indenture dated on or about the twenty fourth day of July One thousand seven hundred and Sixty was bound Apprentice to Thomas Pike of Wych Street in the Parish of St. Clement Danes in the County of Middx Carpenter for the Term of seven Years to learn his Art and Mistery

That the said Thomas Pike hath divers and sundry times cruelly beat and bruised your Petitioner, our Sprained your Petitioners left [...] wrist very violently by striking him with a large Piece of wood upon the same and ours pulled your Petitioner violently down by the Hair of his Head against a Bedstead Which nut the same very Ferribly That on the twenty ninth day of March Last the said Thomas Pike struck your Petitioner with an Iron Square on the right Side of his Head and Wounded him thereon and at the same time sprained leg Thumb so that he was Obliged to be under the Care of a Surgeon sometime for Cure

Yor. Petitioner therefore Most humbly Prays that your Worships will be Pleased to discharge your Petitioner from his Apprenticeship to the said Thomas Pike

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc

Isaac Seedsman
