To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled at the General Quarter Sessions of the peace for the said County by Adjournment at the Session house on Clerkenwell Green

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Joshua Charlsworth of Great Russell Street Covent Garden in the County of Middlesex Hosier and Haberdasher

Sheweth

That your Petitioner was on or about the third day of August last Convicted by William Hyde Esquire One of the Justices of the peace for the Said County in the Penalty of the Pounds or an Information said to have been Exhibited by and in the name of one Sarah field before the Said Justice Chargine that your Petitioner did after the first day of August in the year One Thousand seven hundred and Eighty five towith on the Fourth day of February last at Great Russell Street Covent Garden in the Said County Vend Utter and Sell to one Elizabeth Aysbrass One pair of Leather Gloves above the price and Value of one Shilling and four pence and subject to a Stamp duly of Three Pence without the Stamped Ticket by a certain act Parliament directed to be affixed to the Same being Affixed and marked and Stamped with the Stamp as by the Said Act is directed and required contrary to the Statute That Your Petitioner conceives and is advised that he is aggrieved by such conviction & that the same ought not to have been given against him & Your Petitioner is advised to appeal against the Same

Therefore Your Petitioner most humbly prays Your Worships will be pleased to permit him to Eutor his Appeal [...] that the Same may be heard at this General Quarters Sessions of the Peace in and for the said County and that your Worships will appoint a day for having and determining the Same. And your Petitioners shall ever pray J; Charlesworth
