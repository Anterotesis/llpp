To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition and appeal of William Clarke of the parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the said County Carpenter

Sheweth That by an order under the hand and Seal of William Addington Esquire one of His Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the said County bearing date the Sixth day of September 1784 Your Petitioner is ordered to pay or cause to be paid to John Turbett a Journeyman Carpenter the Sum of Seven Shillings as and for Wages stated in the said Order to be due to the said John Turbett from your Petitioner Whereby your petitioner thinks himself aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays that the Benefit of this his Petition and Appeal may be saved to him and that the said John Turbet and all parties concerned may be ordered to attend this Court at the next General Session of the Peace in order to the hearing and determining the merits of this Appeal

And your petitioner shall ever pray Etc

Wm Clarke
