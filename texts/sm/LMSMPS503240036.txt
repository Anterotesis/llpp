The Worshipfull the Chairman and other his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx at their General Quarter Sessions assembled

The Humble Petition of John Miller a Prisoner in Clerkenwell Bridewell

Sheweth That your Petioner did humbly apply to this Honourable Court at the [...] last General Sessions by Petition therein setting forth and Praying that as his time of Confinement was then almost Expired he might be referred to any one or more of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said County to be Baild and Accordingly provided Bail ready for that purpose

That the Court being so much otherwise taken up and Engaged and Your Petitioner haveing no Friends to Forward his Said Petition he was not Examined thereon

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays that as the time of his Confinement according to his Sentence has been Expired for some Considerable time past And that as he is in a very Poor and Miserable Condition thro so long a Confinement your Worships will please to permit his to be bail'd in Court to Marrow Morning or give him such other Releif in the premises as to your Worships shall seem meet

And he will Ever pray Etc

John Miller

Fryday ye 8th day of July 1737.
