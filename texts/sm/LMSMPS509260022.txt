To the worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex at the Quarter Session of the peace assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Clement near Eastcheap in the City of London.

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant or order under the hands & Seals of Ricd Davies and Thomas Barnard two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 27th. day of January 1796 Martha Walker Wife of Matthew Walker and their four Children Martha aged about six Years Margaret aged about four Years Matthew aged about three Years and Hannah aged about Eleven Months were removed from the Parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel in the said County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Clement near Eastcheap in the City of London as the place of their last legal Settlement.

That Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or order and humbly appeal against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a day in the next Session that all parties may attend to hear & abide the Judgment & determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.

for the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Saint Clement near Eastcheap

Thos. Rooke Vestry Clerk
