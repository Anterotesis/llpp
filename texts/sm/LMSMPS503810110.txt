In the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace at Hicks's Hall in the said County now Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Warden and Overseers of the poor of the Liberty of Saffron hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Thomas Smith and Richard Manley Esquires two of his Majesty's of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Thirty first day of July last Elizabeth Drake Wife of Francis Drake a Soldier abroad and their Child William aged twelve Months were removed from the Parish of Saint Margaret in the City of Westminster aforesaid to the said Liberty of Saffron hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Francis Drake Elizabeth his Wife and their Child William Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

[...] hua Cox [...] for the Appellts. Therefore they humbly appeal to this Honourable Court & pray that the said Court will be pleased to Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster and all Persons concerned Do attend this Court some day in the next Session of the Peace for the said County to hear & abide the Judgement and Determi nation of the said Court touching the said Appeal And that the benefit of Hearing the said Appeal may be saved to your Petitioners And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
