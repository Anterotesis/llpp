To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church-Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate in the City of London.

Sheweth That by virtue of an order of Pass warrant under the hands and Seals of The Revd Sir George Booth Baronet and Rupert Clarke Esquire Two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing date the first day of April one Thousand Seven hundred and ninety one Richard Philips his wife and three Children Francis aged Ten Years and ten months Lawrence aged two years and on half of a year and Thomas aged Ten Months were removed and conveyed from and out of The Parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate in the said City of London as the place of the lawful Settlement of the said Richard Philips and Wife and his Children Francis Lawrence and Thomas whereby your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said order or Pass warrant and pray that your worships will be pleased to appoint a day in this present Session for the hearing and determination thereof.

Chetham Solr. for the appellts.}
