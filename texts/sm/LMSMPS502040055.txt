To the Right Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlx in their Generall Quarter Sessions assembled.

The Humble Peticon and appeale of the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Oclave in Silver Street in the City of London

Sheweth, That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seales of Felix Feast and John Pery Esqrs. Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Midlx (Quor unus) hearing Date the Eighth Day of May last past Anne the Wife of Peter Ward with her son Daniel aged about Ten Years were removed From the Parish of St Giles without Cripplegate in the said County of middlx & unto the said Parish of St. Olave in silver street London alledging the said Parish of St Olave in Silver street to be the Place their last legall Settlement.

That your peticoners Conceiving themselves agreived by the said Warrant or order of the said Two Justices Do there fore appeale to your Worps there from, and humble pray that your Worps will be pleased to hear them in the Prmisses, and order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Pooor of the said parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate in the County of Middx aforesaid may appear before you at a certain Day to be by your Worps appointed and here [...] abide the Judgment and Determination of your worps touching this Appeale

And yor.petrs. shall ever pray Etc.
