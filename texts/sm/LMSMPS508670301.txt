To the Right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the church Wardens and Overseers of the Parish of Saint John at Hackney in the said County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of William Quarrill and John Staples two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing date the fifteenth day of February in the year of our Lord One thousand seven hundred and ninety one Margaret the Wife of William Bodman Crockatt who was then Imprisoned in the Marshalsea Prison for Debt was removed from the Parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel in the County of Middlesex unto the said Parish of Saint John at Hackney in the said County of Middlesex alledging the said Parish of Saint John at Hackney to be the place of her last legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justice do therefore appeal to Your Worships therefrom and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the Premises and Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel in the County of Middlesex may appear before you at a certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships touching the Appeal

And Your Petitioners as in Duty bound will ever pray Etc.

Richd. Dann Agent for the Appellants.}
