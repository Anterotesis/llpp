To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex

The Humble Petition of Mary Mickley Widow on behalf of James Mickley her Son a Minor

Sheweth That by Indenture dated the 19th day of Sepr. 1770 the said James Mickley with your Petitioners consent bound himself Apprentice to William Freeman then of Wells Street in the Parish of St Mary le Bone in the County of Middlesex Coach maker for the Term of Seven Years from the date of the said Indenture and that your Petitioner gave the said William Freeman Twenty Pounds an premium with her said Son

That the said Apprentice faithfully served his said Master till the beginning of September last when the said William Freeman was A [...] ted for debt And confined in the Marshalsea Prison until his discharge about a fortnight ago

That your Petitioners Son must have wanted the necessary use of Life during his Mastire confinement had it [...] been for the humanity of your Petitioners Friends

That the said William Freeman is now out of employment and your Petitioner is advised is incapable of [...] ing his Apprentice the Trade of a Coach maker and maintaining him in the manner he ought and taking proper care of his Morals

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships Would be pleased to discharge her said Son from his Apprentiship (and order the said William Freeman to refound so much of the said Premium as will enable your Petitioner to bind her said son to Another Master

And your Petitioner will ever pray Etc

James Mary Mickely

Middx At the General Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the said County (by adjournment on Wednesday the Ninth day of December in the Thirteenth year of the Reign of our sovereign Lord George the Third King of Great Britain Etc

It is Order'd that William Freeman above mentioned have Notice of this [---] petition and that he and all Persons concerned Do attend this Court here on Monday the Twenty first day of this instant December at Nine of the Clock in the Forenoon of the same day to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of the said Court touching the said Petition

By the Court. Butler.
