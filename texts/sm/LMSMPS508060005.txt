Middlesex To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esqr. Chairman and the rest of the Justices in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The Petition and Appeal of Isaac Sansum of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the County of Middlesex

Humbly Sheweth That in Pursuance of An Act of Parliament made and passed in 25th. Years of the reign of his present Majesty "Intituled An Act for Granting to his Majesty certain Duties "on Licenses to be taken out by Persons vending Gloves or "Mittens and also certain Duties on Gloves and Millens "Sold by retail" Your Appellant on the 26th. day of November last was Convicted by John Staples Esqr. One of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in the Penalty of Twenty Pounds for having sold a Pair of Gloves not having a Stamp Affixed there to Contrary to Said Statute As by the Said Conviction Relation being there unto had will more fully and At large Appear.

Your Petitioner Conceiving himself Aggrieved by such Conviction humbly Appeals there from and prays a day may be Appointed for hearing and Determining there of

And Your Appellant will Pray Etc Isaac Sansum
