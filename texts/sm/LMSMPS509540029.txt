To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their present General Quarter Sessions of the peace assembled.

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the the parish of Saint George the Martyr in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That the virtue of an order or pass warrant under the Hands and Seals of Sir Wm. Addington Knt. and Rupt. Clarke Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the said County (one where of being of the Quorum) bearing date the 5th. day of February last Edward Ballard Widower and his three Children vizt. Edward aged upwards of six years Thomas aged upwards of four years and John aged about two years were removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Clement Danes within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex, to the said parish of Saint George the Martyr as the place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said order of the said two Justices appeal to your Worships therefrom, and humbly pray that You will be pleased to hear them in the premises and order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Saint Clement Danes may appear before you on a certain day to be appointed to abide the Judgment and determination of the Court touching the said Appeal.

And Your Petitioners Etc

Arthur Gliddon Soll for Applts
