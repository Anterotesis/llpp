To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General [...] Sessions of the Peace For the said County [...] at the Sessions Houses on Clerkenwell Green.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Richard Murthwaite of Oxford Street in the Parish of Saint Anns Soho in the County of Middlesex Pawnbroker

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the 28th Day of November last Convicted in the Penalty of Five Pounds by William Hyde Esquire One of the Justices of the peace For the said County upon an Information Stating that the same was Exhibited by William Rudd of Sommer's Town in the Parish of Saint Pancras in the said County of Middlesex Painter against Your Petitioner Richard Murthwaite of Oxford Street in the Parish of Saint Anns Soho in the said County of Middlesex Pawnbroker For that Your Petitioner did on the 11th Day of February in the Year of Our Lord 1791. at and in the Parish and County aforesaid in his Shop in Oxford Street in the Parish and County aforesaid Demand Receive and take of and From One John Robb the sum of Two pence of lawful Money of Great Britain as and For and by was of Profit upon a Loan of Eight Shilling's of like lawful Money being an Intermediate Sum Between the Sum of Two Shillings and Six Pence and the Sum of Forty Shilling's then before made by You the the said Richard Murthwaite (to wit) on the Tenth Day of February in the Year 1791 Aforesaid upon a Certain Pawn or Pledge that is to say a Sliver Watch to the said John Webb the said sum of two Pence Being, more than at and after the rate of Four Pence For the Loan of Twenty Shilling's by the Calender Month including the Current Month in which such pawn or Pledge was Redeemed Contrary to the Statute

That Your Petitioner Conceiving himself Aggrieved by such Conviction and being Advised to Appeal against the same

Your Petitioner most humbly Pray's Your Worship's will be pleased to permit Kin to Enter his Appeal so that the Same may by heard at this Present General Sessions of the Peace in and For the said County and that Yours Worship's will Appoint a Day For Hearing and Determining the same

And Your Petitioner will ever Pray &. R Murthwaite
