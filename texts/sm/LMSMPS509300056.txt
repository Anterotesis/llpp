To His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex

The Humble Petition of John Bush of Hounslow in the County of Middlesex Postmaster

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the Twenty fourth Day of June last Past Convicted by John Spiller Esquire one of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County for that he Did on the Twenty third Day of May, last at the Parish of Sarah George Hanover Square in the Said County unlawfully keep use and Employ a certain Carriage for the purpose of conveying passengers for hire to and from different places within the Kingdom of Great Britain to wit to and from London to Hounslow in the said County without being first duly licensed agreable to Act of parliament made in the Twenty fifth year of the reign of His present majesty's Intitled "An act for repealing the duties on " Licences taken out by Persons letting horses for the purpose of " travelting Post and on horses let to hire for travellers post and " by time and on Stage Coaches and for granting other Duties in " lier there of and also Additional Duties on horses let to hire For travelling post and by Time "And Your Petitioner Conceiving himself aggrieved thereby Doth humbly pray to be permitted to appeal from Such Conviction And that you will please to Appoint a Day for that purpose

And your Petitioner Shall Ever pray Etc

John Bush
