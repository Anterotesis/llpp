To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace in their General Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition of William Sexton

Humbly Sheweth That by an Indenture bearing date the 8th. April 1772 one James Hodges son of Richard Hodges late of Sallors Hope in the County of Hereford Labourer did put himself Apprentice to your Petitioner to learn his Art and with him after the manner of an Apprentice to serve from the day of the date thereof unto the full End and Term of seven years and your Petitioner in Consideration of such Service and of the Sum of Â£10 to him in hand paid by Maverly Esqr. Treasurer to the Hereford Society did agree to Teach and Instructed the said James Hodges in his said Art of a Founder and also to find him sufficient meat Drink Lodging and all Necessarys during his said Term that the said James Hodges hath frequently behaved to your Petitioner in a most disobedient Manner that he hath often refused to obey your Petitioners lawfull commands and refused to stay at home at this Possitively ordered and commanded by yr Petitioner so to do that the said James Hodges hath several times deserted your Petitioners service sometimes for a fortnight together and frequently lodges out of your Petitioners House & Your Petitioner has strong suspicion of his being dishonest having frequently lost small sums of Money out of his Bureau

Your Petitioner therefore Prays your Worships would be pleased to make an order and appoint a day for the Attendance of the said James Hodges and that the said James Hodges may be absolutely discharge from your Petitioners service or that your Worships would make such order in the premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.

And your Petitioner will ever pray
