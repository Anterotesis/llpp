To the Right Worshipfull His Maties: Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex

The humble Peticon & Appeal of the Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of the Rolls in the said County

Sheweth That by Warrant render the Hands & Seals of Richard Bealing & Edward Le Neeve Esqr [...] two of His Maties: Justices of the Peace for the County aforesaid (Quon unns) bearing date the twenty fifth day of July 1717 Robert Stokes aged fifty years with his Six Children (Vizt) William aged about twenty two, Hannah aged about nineteen, Robert aged about fifteen, Sarah aged about thirteen, Richard aged about eleven, & Elizabeth aged about Seven years, Were passed from the parish of St. Ann within the Liberty of Westminister in the said County to the said Liberty of the Rolls as the place of their Legall Settlement

Your Peticoner: think themselves aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices do Appeal to this Court and humbly pray that the Church -Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Ann within the Liberty of Westminster & all other persons herein concerned may be Ordered to attend this Court some day this Sessions to hear & abide the Judgment & determination of this Court upon & touching this Appeal

p J Tyson

And your Peticoner shall ever pray Etc
