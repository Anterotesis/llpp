To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace in & for the County of Middlesex in their General [---] Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of Thomas Hancock & William Johnson Surveyors of the Highways of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the Bars in the said County on behalf of themselves and the rest of the Inhabitants of the said Parish and that part thereof called Saint George the Martyr

Sheweth That the Highways belonging to the said Parish are Sofarr out of Order that they cannot be repaired as they ought to be without further Power than what the Laws have hither to directed and Appointed and the Sum of Money necessary to Amend and repair the same cannot be raised without the Assistance of this Court by granting to Your Petitioners on Assessment of Two Pence in the Pound for that Purpose

Yor: Petitioners therefore humbly pray that Your Worships will be pleased to Order an Assessment of Two Pence in the Pound to be made upon the Inhabitants of the said Parish for and towards the amending repairing and putting Order the said Highways

And Yor: Petrs: shall ever pray Etc

Thos. Hancock Wm. Johnson
