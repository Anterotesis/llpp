To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Michas General Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Hales Owen in the County of Salop.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of William Timbrell and William Mantle Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 19th: day of July 1769 Elizabeth Ward the Wife of Daniel Ward with her three Children, Phebe Aged about Eight Years, Jane Aged about five Years, and Edward Aged about three Months were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster aforesaid to the said Parish of Hales Owen (by the name Alce Owen) in the County of Salop as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved and Appeal against the same.

Therefore Your Petitioners humbly pray your Worships to appoint a day in this present Session to hear and determine this their Appeal.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

William Crawley Sollr. for the Appellants York Buildings.
