To The Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint Peter Cornhill in the City of London.

[...] That by Virtue of a certain pass Warrant or Order of Removal bearing Date the twenty fourth Day of November 1773. under the Hands and Seals of Sampson Wright Esqr. and John Barnfather Esqr. two of His Majestys Justices of the peace for the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) Mary Ryley Wife of William Ryley (alledged thereby to be gone from her) and her six Children Namely Thomas aged about twelve years, William aged about ten Years, Bridget aged about Nine Years, Stephen aged about Eight Years, Mary aged about five Years and Elizabeth aged about fourteen Months were removed and conveyed by the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Mary le Strand in the County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint Peter Cornhill as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Ryley and her said six Children whereby Your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved and appeal against the same

[...] am Solicitor Appellants} Your Petitioners therefore pray that the Benefit of this their appeal may be saved unto them and that the Hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County.
