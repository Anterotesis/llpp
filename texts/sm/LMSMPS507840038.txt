To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St Giles in the Fields in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of Thomas Gordon and William Gregson Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the City [...] and Liberty of Westminster in the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty third day of June last Sarah Woodhouse Singlewoman and her two female Bastard Children to wit, Rebecca Aged two Years and Amelia Aged five Months for Nurture were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of St Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster aforesaid to the said Parish of St Giles in the Fields as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Sarah Woodhouse whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication and Order of the said two Justices And Pray that Your Worships will be pleased to appoint a day in this present Session for the hearing and determining the said Appeal

And your Petrs will Pray Etc

Herbt. Robertson Solr. for the Appellants
