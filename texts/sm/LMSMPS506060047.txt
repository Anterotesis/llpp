To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions of the Peace at Hicks's Hall in St. John Street in and for the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden & Ely Rents in the Parish of St. Andrew Holborn in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Nirtue of an Order or Pass Warrant Under the hands and Seals of William Rugge and William Timbrell Esqrs. Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 3d. day of August Last the said William Rugge and William Timbrell did Adjudge the last legal Settlement of Elizabeth Davis Singlewoman to be in the parish of St. Andrew Holborn below the Barrs London in the Liberty of Saffron Hill and by Nirtue of the Same Pass Warrant or Order the said Elizabeth Davis and Frances her Child aged about one month with her for nurture were Removed from the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to the said Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents whereby Your Petitioners Conceive themselves to be Aggrieved

Joshua Cox Sollr [...] for the Appellts. Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court against the said Order and pray that the said Court will be pleased to Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square and all persons concerns Do attend this Court some Day in the Present Session to hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of the said Court Touching the said Appeal and that your Petitioners may be Court Shall see fit

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
