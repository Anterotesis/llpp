To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex at their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Enfield in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order made under the hands and Seals of J Floud and William Brodie Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex bearing date the Eighteenth day of January 1797 Elizabeth Cardiff (an Illeigitimate Child of Mary Cardiff deceased) was removed and Conveyed from the parish of Saint Mary Stoke Newington in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Enfield as the place of her last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioner think themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore appeal against the said Order and humbly pray relief in the premises and that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a day for that purpose

Middlesex to wit At the General Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex on Sunday the thirteenth day of of February in the thirty seventh year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith Etc

It is Ordered that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint Mary Newington in the said County of Middlesex have Notice of the above appeal and that they and all persons concerned do attend on Thursday the sixteenth day of February Instant at nine of the Clock in the forenoon of the same day at the Sessions House Clerkenwell Green to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the said Court touching the said appeal
