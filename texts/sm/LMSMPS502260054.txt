To the Rt. Woreppl his Maties Justices of the Peace at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden at Hicks hall for the County of Middlx

The Humble Peticon and appeal of the Church warden & Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlett of Wapping Stepney in the said County

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of Robert Jackson Esq and Thomas Masters Esqrs now Sr Thomas Masters Knt two of his Maties Justices of the Peace of the Said County (quor unus) bearing [...] the 16th day of February 1724 Mary Jubees Singlewoman [...] from [...] of St. Mary White Chapple in the Said County to the Said Hamlet of Wapping Stepney as the place of the last Legal Settlement

That your Petrs think themselves Agrieved by the Said order and Humbly Appeal to this Honoble. Court agt the Same

Samll Cowper Selt P appellants And prays yr. Worspps would be pleasd to [...] the Church wardens and Overseers of the Po [...] of the Said Parish of [...] and all other persons Concerned may attend this Court Some day this psent General Quarts Sessions to hear And abide the Judge and determinacon of this Court touching the Appeal

And yr. Petr shall ever pray Etc
