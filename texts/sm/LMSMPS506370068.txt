To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Kingston upon Thames in the County of Surry

Sheweth that by Virtue of an Order under the hands and Seals of Henry Lambe and J Miller Esqrs. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Thirteenth day of December one Thousand seven hundred an seventy three John Butler and Mary his Wife together with their three Children Namely then Eldest a Daughter named Mary Aged about four [...] the second a Daughter Named Sarah Aged Two years [...] Third a Son named John aged about seven weeks were removed from the Hamlett of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Kingston upon Thames in the said County of Surry as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your petitioners conceiving themselves agrieved humbly appeal there from

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships that a day may be appointed in this present Session for hearing and determining of the said appeal and to make such order in the premises as to your Worships shall seem Meet And your Petitioners shall ever pray

[...] Tallemach Sollrs: for appellts

John Clarke Hearle} Churchwardens of Kingston

R Porter Thos. Mundy} Overseers of the poor of the same
