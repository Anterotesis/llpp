To the Justices of our Lord the King Assembled at their General Session as of the Peace holding at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in and for the County of Middlesex This 14th. Day of February In the Year of one Lord 1774

The Humble Petition of Thomas Slaughter and Matthew Howlands Parish Churchwarden and Overseer of the Poor of the Parish of Hedgerly in the County of Bucks

Shew unto your Honour That an Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of Jno: Powell and Miller two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace Acting in and for the County of Middlesex bearing Date the Twenty ninth Day of January last past directed to the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlett of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham in the County of Middlesex And to the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Hedgley in the County of Bucks was made Upon Complaint unto the said two Justices (One whereof being of the Quorum) by the Church Warden and Overseers of the Poor of the said Hamlett of Hammersmith Setting forth That two Children Vizt. Molly a Daughter aged Nine Years or thereabout and Zachariah a Son Aged Six Years or there about both lawfull Issues of the Bodies of Zachariah Peacy and Rachael his Wife late of the Parish of Hedgley aforesaid had lately intruded and came into the said Hamlett of Hammersmith and were become Chargeable to the same

Also that they the said Justices upon Examination of the Premises upon Oath, and other Circumstances did adjudge the same to be true and did also adjudge the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Zachariah Peacy Father of the said Molly and Zachariah was in the Parish of Hedgley in the County of Bucks

Therefore. the said two Justices by their said Order of Removal required the said Church Warden and Overseers of the Hamlett of Hammersmith aforesaid on Sight thereof to remove and Convey the said Molly and Zachariah the lawfull Issues of the said Zachariah and Rachael Peacy from and out of their said Hamlett of Hammersmith to the said Parish of Hedgley in the said County of Bucks and them deliver unto the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor there or some or one of them together with the said Order on a true Copy thereof who were thereby required to rece [...] and Provide for the according to Law
