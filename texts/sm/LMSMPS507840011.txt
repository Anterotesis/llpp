To William Mainwaring Esguire and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace assembled in their General Session of the peace at Hicks's Hall on Clerkenwell Green in the County of Middlesex

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Arnold in the County of Nottingham.

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of James Fielding and William Gowan Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex bearing date the twentieth day of March last John aged about twelve Years and William aged upwards of nine Years Children of William Cutts a Soldier by Mary his Wife (gone from him) were removed from and out of the Parish of Saint James's Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to as their last legal Place of Settlement.

That your Petrs conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order and apprehend that the Settlement of the said John abd Williams not in the said Parish of Arnold

Your Ptitioners therefore most humbly pray your Worships that they may be at Liberty to enter the said Order at the present Sessions holden in and for the County of Middlesex and try the Same at the next ensuing Sessions for the said County

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
