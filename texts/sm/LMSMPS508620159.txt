To the worshipful His majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said county assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of William Blake and Christopher Paxton Tanners at Chiswick in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That Your Petitioners were convicted by Edward Read Esquire and William Hyde Esquire two of his majesty's Justices of the Peace for the county of Middlesex on [...] the fifth day of April One thousand seven hundred and ninety one And your Petitioners have been adjudged by the said Justices to forfeit the Sum of Twenty pounds of lawful money of Great Britain for and by reason of your Petitioners having taken divers Skins out of the materials in which the same had been Tanned on the twenty sixth day of January One thousand seven hundred and ninety one without first giving or sending to the proper Officer for the Duties on Hides due notice of the Time of your Petitioners taking the said Skins out of the materials aforesaid

That your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices and therefore humbly appeal to your worships against the same and

Chetham Solr. for the Appellts } Your Petitioners humbly pray such relief in the premises as to your worships shall seem meet.

Middlesex At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex at the Guildhall in King Street Westminster in the said county on Monday the eleventh day of April in the thirty first year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third King of Great Britain Etc.

It is Ordered that Edward Read Esquire and William Hyde Esquire in this appeal named have notice of the said appeal and that they and all Parties concerned Do attend this court at the Session House for the said County on Thursday the fourteenth day of April instant at nine of the clock in the morning to hear and abide the Judgment of the said Court touching the said Appeal

By the Court Selby
