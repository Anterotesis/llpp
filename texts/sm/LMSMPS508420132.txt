To William Manwairing Esquire and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace Acting in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Bromley Saint Leonard in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of John Staples Esquire and the Reverend Herbert Mayo Doctor in Divinity bearing date on or about the Seventh day of January instant whereby John Merritt together with Susannah his wife and their five Children of Lawful Issue Vizt. Thomas Aged ten Years and upwards [...] Sarah Aged eight Years and upwards William Aged five Years and upwards Elizabeth Aged three Years and upwards and Ann Aged Six Months and upwards were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Mary Stratford Bow in the said County to the said Parish of Bromley Saint Leonard as the last place of their legal Settlement

By which Order your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved Do humbly Appeal to this Court against the same and pray that the hearing and determining thereof may be Adjourned until the next General Sessions of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County

And Your Petitioners will ever pray Etc

John Skull Sollr. for the Appellants 17th January 1789
