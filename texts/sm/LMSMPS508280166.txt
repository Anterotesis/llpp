To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace now Assembled.

The Humble Petition of Henry Walker of the Hamlet of Ratcliff in the Parish of Saint Dunstans Stebenheath otherwise Stepney in the said County Sail Cloath Weaver&

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of Charles Digby and Robert Smith Esquires, two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace acting in and for the said County (One where of is of the Quorum) bearing date the seventeenth day of November now last past your Petitioner was adjudg red to be the reputed Father of a Male Bastard Child then lately Born in the Hamlet of Ratcliff in the parish and County aforesaid of the Body of Frances Fitzer Singlewoman and was therein Or dered , as well for the better Relief of the said Ham let of Ratcliff as for the Sustentation and Relief of the said Bastard Child, that your Petitioner Should forthwith upon Notice of the said Order, Pay or Cause to be Paid to the said Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the said Hamlet, [...] some or one of them, the sum of Eleven pounds Seven shillings and Tenpence for and towards the lying of the said Frances Fitzer, and the maintenance of the said Bastard Child to the time of making the said Order and Charges attending the same, and wherein also it is further Ordered that your Petitioner should pay or Cause to be paid to the Churchwarden and overseers of the Poor of the said Hamlet [...] for the wine being or to some or one of them the Sum of Two shillings and six pence weekly and every week for and towards the Keeping [...] -tation and Maintenance of the said Bastard

Child for and during so long time as the said Bastard Child should be Chargeable to the said Hamlet Ratcliff.

Your Petitioner Conceiver himself Aggriev -ed by the Adjudication and order of the said two Justices and therefore humbly Appeals to this Court against the same and prays your Worships will appoint a day in the present Session for the hearing and determining the same. And your Petitioner will ever pray Etc.

Henry Walker

Middlesex At the General Session of the peace of our Lord the King holden at the Session house in and for the said County of Middlesex on Monday the Tenth day of December in the Twenty eight year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third King of Great Britain Etc.

It is Ordered by the Court that the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the Hamlet of Ratcliff in the Parish of Saint Dunstant Stebonheath otherwise Stepney in the County of Middlesex have Notice of the within Appeal and that they and all persons concerned Do attend this Court on Thursday next at nine O Clock in the Forenoon to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

By the Court Selby
