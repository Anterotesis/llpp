To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their present General Quarter Session of the peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of that part of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn which his above the Barsin the said County

Sheweth That by an Order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of J. Floud and William Gascoigne Esqr. two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 29th. day of May 1793. Ann Brathwaite [...] (Widow of William Brathwaite deceased) and her four Children lawful Issue of the said William Brathwaite namely Ann aged Seven years or there abouts, Maria aged Five years, Sophia aged Two Years, and William Aged Three Weeks, were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of St. Matthew Bethnal Green in the said County of Middlesex to the said Part of the said Parish of St. Andrew Holborn as the place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiveing themselves aggrieved by the Orders of the said Two Justices beg leave to Appeal therefrom to [...] your Worships and request that the Benefit of their said appeal [...] may be saved unto them untill the next General Session of the place to be holden for the said County and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said parish of St. Matthew Beth Green may be ordered to attend to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of your Worships thereon

And as in Duty Etc Arthur Gliddon
