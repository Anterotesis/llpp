To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session of the peace assembled.

The humble petition and Appeal of the Churchardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster and County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by virtue of s pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of G. Mercer Jno. Barnfather Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the sixth day of July 1784 John Platt Frances his wife and their four Children namely Frances aged ten years Noah aged eight years, Rebecca aged six years and Vurley aged one year (the two first named Children having gained no settlement in their own right) wore Removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of St. Mary le Bone in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of St. George Hanover square in the said Liberty of Westminster and [---] County aforesaid whereby your petitioners think themselves aggrieved and humbly Appeal to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships will please to grant that the benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determining thereof be adjourned until the next General Session of the peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex and that all persons concerned may be ordered then to attend this Court touching the said Appeal.
