To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Lewis Weltzie an Inhabitant of the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham and County of Middlesex

Sheweth That on or about the fourth day of October 1793 a Rate or assessment was made for the Relief of the Poor of the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the Parish of Fulham in the County of Middlesex And Which rate was allowed by Nathaniel Conant Esqr and Philip Neve Esqr two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said County on the fifth day of the said month of October

That your Petitioners being aggrieved by such Rate raices have to appeal therefrom and the grands of such his appeal are as follows

First Because your Petitioner is rated for the Premises which he Occupies beyond the Value of them

Secondly Because your Petitioner is ever assessed in proportion to the Assessment upon Thomas Winter Esqr and also in proportion to Richard Walmsley Esqr Lady Bailey Sir Clifton Wintringham and divers other persons in respect

respect of the premisses severally occupied by them in the said Hamlet

Thirdly Because Thomas Dean Joseph Poovey John Herton Thomas Chambers and divers others whose names are mentioned in the Book or rate and who severally hold rateable property in the said Hamlet one not rated at all in respect of such Property

Fourthly Because Stephen Randall is not rated in respect Land at or Near Shepherds Bush in this and Hamlet supposed to be held by him under the Trustees for Kensington Parish

Fifthly Because the said Rate is in other respect, partial unjust and Oppresive

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays your Worships to take the Premises into consideration and that the said Rate or assessment may be Quashed or that your Petitioner may have such Relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem meet and that your Worships with appoint a day in the present Session for the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the said Hamlet of Hammersmith and all other persons concerned to attend to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of the Court touching the sd Appeal

And Your Petitioner J Weltzie
