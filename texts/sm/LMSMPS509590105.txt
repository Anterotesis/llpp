To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of Thomas Selly of Enfield in the County of Middlesex Baker

Sheweth

That your Petitioner was convicted on the twenty First day of February last by Humphrey Jackson Esquare one of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County on the complaint of William Launders and John Ball for having unlawfully Sold on the Twentieth day of day of February last Seventeen half Peck Loaves of Wheaten Bread deficient in weight One Hundred and Forty Four Ounces according to two Assize set for such Bread and Your Petitioner was adjudged to pay and forfiet the Sum of Thirty six Pounds for the same

That your Petitioner conceives himself to be Aggrieved by the said Conviction and humbly appeals against the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays Your Worships to appoint a day to hear and determine the said Appeal

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc
