To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the Reverend Sir George Booth Baronet and William Blackborow Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the Said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 12th. day of August 1779 Ann Deseser aged Ten years and Elizabeth Deseser Aged Nine Years Bastards born on the Body of Joyce Deseser were removed and Conveyed from the parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint Luke as the place of their last Legal Settlement Whereby Year Petitioner Conceise themselves to be Aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the a Adjudication and Order of the said Two Justices so for as the same relases to Ann Deveser one of the Pauper mentioned in the said Order and Pray that the Benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden by adjournment for the said County.

And Yoru Petitioners will Ever Pray Etc

Jenings Sollm. for the Appellants 122 Bunhill Row
