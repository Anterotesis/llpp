To His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in their General Session of the Peace held for the said County in the month of September 1781 assembled

The Humble Petition of Samual Newport Keeper of New Prison at Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That at the General Session of the Peace held for this County in the month of December last your Petitioner presented as Petition selling forth various hardships loses and Expences which he then had suffered and incurred in consequence of the number of prisoner's in his Custody since the then late dreadful Riots that your Worships were then so indulgent as to grant your Petitioner Twenty Pounds and to intimate to your Petitioner that if he continued to labour under such circumstances for any longer time your Worships would be pleased to make him some further allowance That your Petitioner hath continued to labour under circumstances of the like kind from that time to the present is within the knowledge of many of Your Worships but should it be doubted your Petitioner is ready to inform your Worships of the particulars of his situation in respect to the extraordinary trouble anxoity and Expence he has suffered and even at present suffers in the management and Government of the very great number of annuity and desperate offenders which have been in the time last abovementioned and now are in his Custody

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly and earnestly prays that your worships will be pleased to take his case into Consideration and grant to him such other recompense as to your Worships shall seem meet

And Your Petitioner as in duty bound will ever pray Etc.

Samuel Newport
