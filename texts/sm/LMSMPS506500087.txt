To The Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace at Hicks Hall in the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of The Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Luke in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Philip Dept and Stephen Clarke Esquires Two of His Majestys Justices of the peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 11th day of January 1775 Sarah Hawthorne Singlewoman aged about twenty five years and her Male Child named thomas aged about three Weeks (for Nurture) were Removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Giles in the fields in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Luke as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Sarah Hawthorne thereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the said Pass Warrant or order of the said 2 Justices and pray Your Worships will please to appoint some day in this present Session for the Hearing and determining the said Appeal and to Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Giles in the fields and all Persons concerned to then attend this Court.

J: Jenings Soler. for Appellants And Your Petitioners Shall ever pray Etc.
