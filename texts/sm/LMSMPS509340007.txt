To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition of John Lunn of New Street Knight to Bridge.

Sheweth That your Petitioner is a Coach master residing in New Street Knights Bridge in the County of Middlesex and employed among other Carriages, a Coach Licenced by the Commissioners for requlating Hackney Coach and Chaire No.487 for which he pays to Government a Duty of £26 per Annum.

That your Petitioner was on or about the 18th day of August last Summoned before John Spiller Esqr. one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said [...] County for keeping and employing a certain Coach as a Public Stage Coach for the purpose of Conveying Passengers for Hire to and from different place within the Kingdom of Great Britain to wit to Piccadilly from Fulham without having as is Alledged first obtained a Licence for that purpose in manner present by the Statute in that Case made and provided.

That it appeared in Evidence before the said Majistrate that the Coach so Complained of for having been worked as a Stage Coach within the Distance Aforesaid was one and the same idential Coach for

which the Commissioners for Licencing and regulating Hackney Coaches and Chains had granted your Petitioner a Licence to work within certain Districts under their Authority or Jurisdiction and for which your Petitioner pay'd the same Duty of £26 per Annum and that such Coach was Worked within such District only.

That the said Majestrate not with standing such Evidence Convicted your Petitioner of the Offence stated in such Summons and ordered your Petitioner to pay the penalty imposed by the said Act when your Petitioner Entered into a Recognizance in Order to appeal against such Conviction.

That your Petitioner Apprehends the said Magistrate hath erred in his Judgment relative to such Conviction and therefore

Your Petitioner prays that he may be heard by himself or his Counsel in the Nature of Appeal against such Conviction in the present General Quarter Session of the Peace.
