To the Worshipful His Majesty's justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of D Wilmot and Chas Friquet Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Ninth day of December 1788 William Loveland, Ann his Wife and then Child lawful Issue Named William of the Age of sixteen Months were removed from the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said William Loveland, Ann his wife and their said Son whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves agrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and then said Order or Pass Warrant and pray that a Day may be appointed by this Court for the Hearing and determining of this Appeal and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch and all persons concerned be ordered to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships in the Premisses And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

John Evans Solor for the Appellants
