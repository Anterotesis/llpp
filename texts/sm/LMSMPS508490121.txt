To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in Session of the peace in and for the said County assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Michael Woodstreet London

Sheweth

That by an order under the hands and seals of Charles Triquet and Thomas Cogan Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty eighth day of July last Thomas Cripple and his four Children namely Mary aged eight years Edmund aged six years and upwards Margaret aged four years and Ann aged upwards of two years were removed from the Parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Michael Woodstreet London as the place of their last legal settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and against the said Order do appeal

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that a short day may be appointed this present session to hear and determine the matter of the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall over pray Etc

Middlesex At the General Session of the Peace four Sovereign Lord the King holder for the County of Middlesex at the Sessions House Clerkenwell Green in the County of Middlesex by adjournment on Monday the seventh day of September in the twenty ninth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith

It is Ordered by this Court that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in this County have Notice of this Appeal And that they and all Parties concerned do attend this Court on Thursday next at nine of the Clock in the Forenoon to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal
