To the Right Worpl his Matys Justices of the peace for the County of Middx at the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of ye Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Dunstan in the East London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of Pass Warrant under the hands & Seals of D'Oyly Michel and John Fuller Esqrs two of his Maties Justices of the peace for the County of Middx (Quor unus) bearing Date the 22 day of April 1727 Catherine the wife of Henry Wright and Mary their daughter aged about three Years were sent & delivered by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Giles without Cripplegate in the sd County of Middx to the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of ye sd parish of St Dunstans in the East London Alledging the sd parish to be the place of their last Legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Warrt and Judgment of the Sd two Justices and do most humbly appeal to Your Worps therefrom

Your Petrs therefore most humbly pray that your Worps will be pleased to hear them in the premisses and appoint a day in this present Sessions for hearing the Sd Appeal & Order that the Church Wardens & Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St Giles Without Cripplegate in the Sd County of Middx & all other parties concerned may then appear & hear & abide the Judgment & determinacon of this Court touching their Sd Appeal

And they shall ever pray Etc
