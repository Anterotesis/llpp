To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace holden in and for the said County assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of West Clendon in the County of Surry

Sheweth That by Order of Removal or Pass Warrant under the Hand and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the sd County of Middlesex bearing Date the nineteenth day of June last Joannah Cooke Widow of George Cooke was removed from the Parish of [---] St. James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to the sd. Parish of West Clendon as the Place of her last legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners do humbly apprehend themselves aggrieved by the Sd. Order of Removal or Pass Warrant and therefore Appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners and Appellants therefore humbly pray that this Court will appoint a Day in the next Session for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. James and all Persons concerned to Attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal And that your Petitioners and Appellants may be relieved in the Premises as to this Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioners and Appellants will ever pray Etc.

July 11th, 1760 John Hall Long Lane Southwk. Solr. for the Appellants
