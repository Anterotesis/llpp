To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Cottingham in the East Riding of the County of York.

Sheweth, That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of John Spinnage and George Wright Esquire's two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the fourth day of September last past Hannah Brown the wife of Joseph Brown was removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster and in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Cottingham as the Place of her last Legal Settlement, whereby your Petitioners Conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Therefore they humbly appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved to your Petitioners and that the Hearing and Determining thereof may be adjourned to the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the County of Middlesex.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc. Robt. Dyneley Sollicitor for the Appellants
