To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Mary Le Bone in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth. That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Stephen Clarke and Saunders Welch Esqrs. Two of His Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing date the 25th. day of February 1760 Mary Haynes Wife of Richard Haynes Silk Dyer (now at Sea) and William their Son aged One Year were removed from the Parish of St. Andrew Holborn above the Bars in the County of Middlesex to the parish of St. Mary Le Bone in the said County as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby Your petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly pray that the benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the peace to be holden for the said County Middlesex

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Thos Huddle Jno Manvell Jos Brockhurst John Roberts} Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor
