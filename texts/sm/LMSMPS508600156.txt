To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Exchange in the City of London

Sheweth That by an Order or pass Warrant bearing date the thirty first day of December 1790 under the hands and seals of John Spiller and T.T Cotton Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) Whereby Jane Stevens Singlewoman was removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Christ Church in the said County of Middlesex To the said Parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Exchange as the place of her last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners apprehending themselves aggrieved by the said Order or pass warrant of the said two Justices Humbly appeal against the same

And therefore pray that your Worships will be pleased to Order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Christ Church in the County of Middlesex aforesaid and all persons concerned to attend this Court same day this present Session to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of this Court touching the premises And that your Petitioners may have such relief therein as to your worships shall seem meet

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
