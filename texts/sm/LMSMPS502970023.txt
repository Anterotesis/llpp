To the Right Worshipfull his Majestyes Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their, Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeale of William Goodall of Saint Mary White Chapple in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That Yor Petitioner is Charged in the Rate or Assessment for and towards the Necessary Releife of the Poor of the said Parish for this Present Yeare One Thousand Seven Hundred and thirty Three in the Sume of Two Shillings and two pence a Month And Your Petitioner Saith that the Rent of his house is but Twenty pounds a Year And the Last Yeare he was Charged and assessed towards the Releife of the said poor no more than One Shilling and a penny per Month which is as much in proportion as Severall of your Peticoners Neighbours pay, who Live in better Houses and have better Comeings in than your Petitioner

Therefore Your Petitioner thinks himself Aggreived and Humbly appeals against the same And prays to be Releived in, the Premes as your Worships Great Wisdom, and Justices Shall Seem meet

And yor. Petr. Shall ever pray Etc Wm. Goodall
