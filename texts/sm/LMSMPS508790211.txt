To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their present General Quarter Sessions of the peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition of Richard Howell

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Rate or Assessment made for the relief of the poor of the Hamlet of Hammersmith in the said County of Middlesex and for other purposes mentioned in the several Acts of Parliament relating to the poor on or about the 20th day of September last and allowed by Two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said County and afterwards duly publish Your petitioner was and is charged in and by such Rate or Assessments with a large and excessive Sum of Money for the Purposes aforesaid and much more than according to the real Value of his capable property within the said Hamlet he should be charged

That your Petitioner Objects to the said Rate or Assessment not only for the reason aforesaid but upon the grounds of the same being partial unfair unequal and unjust for that the Reverend Millwood the Curate of the said Hamlet of Hammersmith who keeps a School in the said Hamlet and occupies a House that used to be rated at Seventy pounds a year is left out and Omitted in the said Rate or Assessment

That Stephen Bland the Sexton of the said Hamlet

(who)

who occupies a House which used to be rated at Thirteen pounds per Annum is also left out and Omitted in the said Rate or Assessment

That William Hallett the Clerk of the Chapel of the said Hamlet who used to be rated for his House at Seven pounds per Annum is likewise left out and omitted in the said Rate or Assessment and

That William Rutter another Occupier of a House in the said Hawlet who used to be rated at Ten Pounds per Annum for the same is also left out and Omitted in the said Rate or Assessment and that those several, Inhabitants and Occupiers of Houses within the said Hamlet were liable to be rated and Assessed towards the relief of the poor of the said Hamlet at the time of making the said Rate or Assessment and for some time before

Your Petitioner Conceiving himself aggrieved by the said Rate or Assessment appeals to your Worships against the same and prays that the Benefit of his said Appeal may he saved unto him and that the bearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Sessions of the peace to be holden for this County and that in the mean Time Notice of his said Appeal be given to the Churchwardens and

Overseers of the Poor of the said Hamlet of Hammersmith they and all Persons concerned may be ordered to attend to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the Court touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioner as Etc.

Arthur Gliddon Sollr. for the Appellant
