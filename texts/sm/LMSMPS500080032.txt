To the Rt Worppfull: their Maties: Justices of the peace for the County of Middx

The humble petion of Charles Whylehead

Sheweth That yor petionr is and was upon the 28th day of November 1690 a prisonr in Finsbury Goale in the Custody of Symon pen keeper of the same for debt and being in noe wise able to pay the same humbly desired the benefitt of the Acts of parliamt made for the reliefe and discharge of poor prisonrs for debt and went before a Justice of the peace to take the Oathes that the Said Acts require in Order to be discharged but the said Keeper neglected to bring yer petionr to the Quart Sesss who is since wth drawen by wth meanes yor petionr is still detained

Yoe petionr therefore humbly prayes to be discharged by this honoble Bench he being Qualified for it

And, your petionr shall ever pray:

Petion of the [...] Whitehead [...]
