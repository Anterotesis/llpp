To the Rt. Worpful his Maties Justices of the peace for the County of [...] Midlx. at their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Peticon & Appeal of the Churchwarden & Overseers of the poor of the Hamblet of Ratcliff in the said County

Sheweth

That by an Order under the Hands & Seals of Stephen Martins Leake & Samuel Jones Esqrs. two of his Maties [---] Justices of the peace for the said County (Quor un) bearing date the fourteenth Day of November last[---] Ann the Widdow of John Street & two Children (vizt) Ann apnd about Two years & Hanah aged about eight Months were Convey'd from the Hamblet of Lymehouse in the said County to our Said Hamblet of Ratcliff as to he Place of their last legall Settlement

your Peticonr thinking themselves [...] there [...] humbly appeale to this Honble [...]

And Pray that you Worsps would be pleased to [...] [...] to Order that the benefit of this appeal may be saved untill then Etc General Sessions of the Peace for this County & that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the sd. Hamblet of Limehouse [---] [...] & all other concern'd may Attend Someday the sd. General Sessions to hear & abide the Judgement & determinacon of this Court touching the said appeal

And your Peticonrs. Shall Ever Pray Etc
