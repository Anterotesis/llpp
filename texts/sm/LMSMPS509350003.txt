To the Worshipfull William Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex assembled at a General Quarter Sessions for the said County

The humble Petition of Robert Simpson

Sheweth. That your Petitioner is a licensed dealer in perfumery residing in Princes Sheet Cavendish Square in the Parish of Saint Marylebone in the County of Middlesex

That your Petitioner on the fourteenth day of January last was convicted before Richard Ford Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County for that your Petitioner on the fourth day of January last did sell to one Elizabeth Dodd a Roll of Pomatum for the have no stamped cover wrapper or label being pasted stuck fastened on affixed to the same contrary to the form of the Statute in that case made and provided and the said Justice did declare that your Petitioner had forfeited the sum of five pounds for the offence aforesaid

That your Petitioner finding himself aggrieved by the Judgment of the said Justice did give security to the account of the said penalty and Costs as by the said Statute is directed and appealed to the next General Quarter Session against the said Conviction

Your Petitioner therefore prays that he may be allowed to prosecute his said appeal before your Worships.

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc
