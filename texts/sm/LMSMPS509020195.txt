To the Worshipful William Manwaring Esqr. and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Church-Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Middlesex in Bread Street in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal bearing date the 8th. day of October 1794 under the hands and Seals of Graham and William Bleamire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex Sarah White the Wife of Thomas White now a prisoner in Newgate and their two Children namely William Aged two Years or thereabouts and James Aged 6 Weeks or thereabouts were removed from the liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Goods and Ely Rents in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Mildred in Bread Street as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Appeal against the said Order of removal and Pray your Worships will be Pleased to Appoint some day in the Present Session for the hearing and Determining thereof

And Your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc

T. Yardly

(Solicitor for the Appellants)
