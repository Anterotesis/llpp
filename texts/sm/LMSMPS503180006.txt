To the Worshipfull the Bench of Justices Mett at a General Quarter Sessions of the Peace held for the County of Middlesex at Hicks' Hall in St John Street[---]

The Humble Petition of William Ellis of South Mimms in the sd County Sheweth.

That Your Petitioner Being Appointed by this Honble Bench Passing Constable for Conveying Vagrants in and through this County or the Year 1734: at the Yearly Salary of Thirty Pounds That he did Convey the sd Vagrants from A lady Day 1734 to A lady Day 1735: That he hath recived fifteen Pounds. for the First Half Year which was Due as Michaelmas 1734: That There was Due to him at a Lady Day 1735 Fifteen Pounds for his Expences and Trouble in Conveying the sd Vagrants He being a very Poor Man Humbly Hopes this Honble Bench: Will Order try shall be paid the sd Sum of Fifteen Pounds

And Your Petitionr shall ever Pray

William Ellis.
