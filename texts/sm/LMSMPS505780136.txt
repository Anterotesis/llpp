To the Worshipful John Hawkins Esqr. and the rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary le bone otherwise Mary bone in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant a order under the Hand and Seal of Stephen Clarke Esqr. one of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said County bearing Date the 3d Day of December 1767 Hannah Brad burn a Child Aged about Eight Years was removed from and out of the Parish of Saint [---] Giles in the Fields in the said County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint Mary le bone as the place of her last legal Settlement whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved and humbly appeal therefrom

Garforth Atty for the Appllants } Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships that a Day may be appointed for the hearing and Determining of the said Appeal and that Your Worships may be pleased to make such Order in the Premisses as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Middlesex At the General Sessions of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the said County on Monday the Seventh Day of December in the 8th. Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third King of Great Britain Etc

It is Ordered by this Court that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Giles on the Fields aforesaid have Notice of this Appeal and that they and all persons concerned do Attend this Court on Thursday the Tenth Day of this Instant Decr. at Nine o' Clock in the Morning to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

By the Court Waller.
