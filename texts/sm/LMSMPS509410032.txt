To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in General in Session of the Peace for the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Margaret Lothbury in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of John Nares and John Staples Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty sixth day of July 1797 Ann Norton Aged ten years and who (as alledged by the said Order) had gained no Settlement in her own Right and John Norton Aged five Years the two Children of Jonathan Norton and Felicia his Wife both deceased were removed and Conveyed form the Parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Margaret Lothbury and delivered to your Petitioners being the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Margaret Lothboury (the said Justices adjudging that the lawfull Settlement of the said Ann Norton and John Norton was in the said Parish of Saint Margaret Lothbury in the said City of London) by which Order of Removal or Pass Warrant Your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Order of Removal or Pass Warrant and Pray that the Benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto them and that

Wm Chetham Solr, for the Appellants } That the hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said County
