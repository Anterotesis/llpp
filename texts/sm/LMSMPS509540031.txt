To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of William Lovell of Whitechapel Road Baker

Sheweth That your Petitioners was convicted on the fourth day of August last by Daniel William Esquire and the Revd Henry Reynett Doctor in Divinity two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County on the Complaint of the Master and Wardens of the Bakers Company for having unlawfully made and exposed to Sale on the second Day of August last one half peck Loaf deficient in Weight nine Ounces and nineteen quarter of a Pock Loaves deficient in Weight fifty six Ounces according to the Assise set for such Bread and your Petitioner was adjudged to pay and forfeit the Sum of nine Pounds fifteen Shillings for the same

That your Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by the said Conviction and humbly appeals against the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships to appoint a Day to hear and determine the said Appeal.

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
