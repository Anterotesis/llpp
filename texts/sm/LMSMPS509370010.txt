To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex assembled at their General Quarter Session of the Peace began and holden the twenty fourth day of April in the thirty seventh year of the reign of King George the third.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West in the City of London.

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order under the hands and Seals of [...] Sir Harry Parker Bart and Millington Buckley [...] Clerk two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the 25th day of March 1797. Maria Hill single Woman was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of St. Marylebone in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of St. Dunstan in the West as the Place of her last legal settlement and your Petitioners were required to provide for her according to Law whereby your Petitioners conceived themselves aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore Appeal against the said Order and humbly pray relief in the Premises and that your Worships will be pleased to appoint some day in this present Session for the hearing and determining this their Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
