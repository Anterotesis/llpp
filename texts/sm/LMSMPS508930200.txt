To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex assembled at their General Quarter Session of the Peace begun and holden the thirteenth day of January in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of King George the third

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex and Liberty of Westminster

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of S P Andrews and St Warner Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster (one whereof [...] therein mentioned as being of the Quorum) bearing date the 27th day of November 1793 Elizabeth Wood singlewoman together with John her Illegitimate Child aged about eight Months for Nurture, were removed from the parish of St. Margaret in the said City of Westminster to the said parish of St Clement Danes as the place of the last legal settlement of the said Elizabeth Wood, and were delivered to your Petitioners to be provided for by them according to Law Whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore Appeal against the said Order land humbly pray relief in the premises And that Your Worships will be pleased to Order that the benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto them, that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of the peace for the said County of Middlesex And that some day in the same General Session may be appointed for that purpose

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
