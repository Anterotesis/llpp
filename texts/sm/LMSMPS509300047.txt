To his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex

The humble Petition of Prince Walker of Hounslow in the County of Middlesex Portmaster

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the Twenty fourth day of June last Past convicted by John Spiller Esquire one of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County for that he did on the Twenty third day of May last past at the parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the Said County unlawfully keep use and Employ a certain Carriage for the purpose of conveying passengers for hire to and from different places within the Kingdom of Great Britain to Wit to and from London to Hounslow aforesaid in the said County without being first duly licensed agreable to an act of parliament passed in the Twenty fifth year of the reign of His present Majesty's Intitled "An act for repealing the daties on beence's taken out by " Persons letting horses for the purpose of travelling portand on " Horses let to hire for travelling port and by time and on stage coaches " and for granting other Duties in liete thereof, and also additional " Duties on chooses let to hire for travelling port and by Time and Your Petitioner Conceiving himself aggrieved thirty doth humbly pray to be permitted to appeal from just Conviction And that you will please to appoint a Day for that purpose

And your Petitioner Shall Ever pray Etc

Prince Walker
