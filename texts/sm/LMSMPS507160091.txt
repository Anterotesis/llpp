To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate London

Sheweth That by an Oder a Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of P. Rowlands Esquire and Jasper Clarke Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the first day of September 1779. Mary May Witness was removal and Concerned from out of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate in the City of London as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary May whereby Your Petitioners Concese themselves to be Aggrieved.

Carter & Simpson Sollrs. for the Appellants} Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships to appoint a day in this Present Session to hear and determine their said Appeal.

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.
