To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall in Saint John Street in the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Clifton in the County of Warwick

Sheweth That by Vertue of a pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of Cadwallader Coker and Benjamin Cowley Esquires two of his majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 25th. day of April last John Chambers (Son of [---] Daniel Chambers and Susanna[---] his wife both deceased) aged about five years were removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Luke in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Clifton as the place of his last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved

Therefore they humbly appeal to this Court against the said Order of the two Justices and pray that the benefit of this their appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and Determining thereof may be Adjourned unto such day in the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be held for this County to hear and determine the same And that your Worships will be pleased to make your Order for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Saint Luke and all Persons concerned do then Attend

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc. Jno. Harris Soll. for the appellants
