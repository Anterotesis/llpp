Middlesex To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of West Ham in the County of Essex

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order under the hands and Seals of Robert Smith and S. D Liptrap Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty fourth Day of March one thousand seven hundred and Eighty nine John Roff and Sarah Smith (in the said Order Called Sarah his Wife) and a Child Named. John Aged about Sixteen Months the Natural Son of the said Sarah were removed from the Hamlet of Mile end old Town in the Parish of Saint Dunstan Stepney. in the said County of Middlesex unto the said Parish of West Ham in the County of Essex as the Place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order. Appeal to this Honourable Court against the same and humbly Pray your Worships to Appoint a Day to hear and Determine the same And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

Chas. Tanner Saml Turner } Churchwardens

Richd Rooke Willm. Layell} Overseers

At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex at the Session House Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex on Monday the Twentieth Day of April one thousand seven hundred and Eighty nine

It is Ordered that the Churchwardens and overseers of the Poor of the Hamlet of Mile end Old Town in the Parish of Saint Dunstan Stepney in the County of Middlesex have Notice of this appeal and that this and all Parties Concerned do Attend this Court on Thursday next at Nine of the Clock in the Forenoon on the Matters of this Petition and Appeal to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal

Middlesex to wit} To William Mainwaring Esquire and others his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace holden at the Session house in and for the said County on Monday the 20th Day of April 1789

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Bray in the Town of Maidenhead in due County of Berks.

Sheweth That by an Order made at the last General Quarter Session of the Peace holden in and for the County of Middlesex on the 12th day of January last setting forth that your Petitioners had exhibited their Petition and Appeal against an Order made on the 14th day of November there last by William Gascoign and G S. Bradshaw Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex That complaint had been made unto them by the Churchwardens and Overseers of Saint Anne within the liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex that Amelia Williams then aged 4 Months had lately intruded and [...] into the said Parish of St Anne and was likely to become chargeable to the same and that they the said Justices upon examination of the Premises upon Oath and other Circumstances did adjudge the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Amelia Williams to be in the Parish of Bray in the Town of Maidenhead in the County of Berks and the said Wm Gascoign and G S Bradshaw did accordingly require the said Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the sd Parish of Saint Anne to remove and convey the said Amelia William from & out of the said Parish of St. Anne to the said Parish of Bray which they had done accordingly whereby your

Petitioners conceived themselves aggrieved and accordingly appealed against the said Order which coming on to be heard at the sd Sessions It was Ordered upon Motion of Counsel made for that purpose for and on behalf of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Ann Westminster that the said Order of the said two Justices be & the same was thereby quashed

That notwithstanding the Court of Quarter Session having quashed the sd Order of the 14th of November last another Order has since been made by Edward Gray & I Barnfather Esqres. two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in & for the County of Middlesex & City and liberty of Westminster bearing date the 19th days February last whereby they have again removed the said Amelia Williams to the said Parish of Bray

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order of the 19th of February last for it does not state that the said Amelia Williams had obtained a new Settlement in the said Parish of Bray subsequent is the last Quarter Sessions when the said Order of 14th of Novr 1788 was quashed or any other special Circumstance to induce the said Edwd Gray and J Barnfather to make such Order of 19th of February as aforesaid

Your Petitioners therefore pray that the said Order of removal made by the sd Edwd Gray & Barnfather of the 19th of February last may be quashed and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St Anne may pay to your Petitioners the Charges of such removal and the Expences of maintaining the said Pauper since She was to removed on the 14th. day of Novr last as aforesaid & also the Costs of this and the former Application

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
