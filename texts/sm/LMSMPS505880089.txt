To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session Assembled

The humble Petition of William Alexander Dodd and George Wanley Bowes

Sheweth That your Petitioners are Surveyors of the Highways within the parish of Hanwell in the County of Middlesex for the present year and have used their best Endeavours for the Repairing and Amending the said ways

That the greatest part of the statute work has been done and the composition Money duly laid out in the said Repairs but that the whole labour and Composition fell very short of the necessary means of compleating the said Business so that your Petitioners have necessarily expended about Thirty six Pounds therein and in compounding with the Turnpik'e Trust and still in disburse about Eighteen pounds thereof

That there being some labour to be performed your Petitioners will be necessarily put to some further expences in digging Gravel and other Things to cooperate in the finishing of the said Repairs

That a Rate of six pence in the pound on the Occupyors or Inhabitants within the said Parish will amount to about the sum of Twenty five pounds

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray are Order of this Honourable Coart for the making of a Rate of six pence in the pound in such manner as shall seem meet according to the form of the Statute in such Case lately provided

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Wm: Alex. Dodd
