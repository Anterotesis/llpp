To the Rt. Worspl. his Majties. Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx.

The Humble Peticon of Nicholas Corrall one of the petty Constables of the parish of Southmims in the said County

Sheweth That at a Court Leet lately held in and for the said parish of Southmims, yor. Petr. was chosen to be one of the petty Constables of the sd parish in the room and place of John Tull: And that your Petr. is regulary Sworn into the said Office accordingly

Yor. Petr. therefore most Humbly Prays yor. Worships would be pleased to appoint him to Pass and Convey [---] the Vagrants through this County at the like Allowance of Forty pounds P Anum as has been made to the Said John Tull & other Constables

And yor. Petr. Shall ever Pray Etc

the mark [x] of the Peticonr. Nichs. Corrall.

Wee whose Names are hereunto Subscribed two of his Maties. Justices of the Peace for the sd County of Middlx residing in or near to the sd parish doe hereby Certifye, that we doe Approve of the Petr. Nichs. Corrall to be appointed to pass the Vagrants And we doe recomend him as a Person well qualifyed for

Craka [...] olds Smith

for that Service: Dated ye 11th. Day of May 1732}
