To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire Chairman of the Court of Quarter Session of the Peace for the County of Middlesex and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County assembled in their General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Cheadle in the County of Stafford

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of John Spiller and T.T. Cotton Esquires Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the day of 1791. Elizabeth Barnes the Widow of John Barnes deceased and her three Children Namely John Aged four years and upwards William Aged three Years and upwards and Catherine Aged one Year and upwards were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Edmonton in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Cheadle in the said County of Stafford as the place of their last legal Settlement. Whereas the said Paupers did not nor did any or either of them ever gain any Settlement in the said Parish of Cheadle

That your Petitioners being aggrieved by such Order and Removal did on the 2d. day of July 1794 give Notice to the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Edmonton that your Petitioners intended at the then next General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County of Middlesex to commence and prosecute an appeal against the said Order and concerning the said Removal, and accordingly your Petitioners do now appeal to this Honorable Court against the said Order and Removal

Your Petitioners therefore humbly

Pray this Honorable Court will please to Appoint a time for hearing and determining the said Appeal and that all parties concerned may then Attend or that your Worships will grant your Petitioners such other Relief in the premises as shall be just

Thomas and Robt. Peake Southampton Buildings, Agents for John Child, Sollicitor for the Appellants }

Middlesex As the General Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at the Sessions House upon Clerkenwell Green in the said County on Monday the Eighteenth day of July in the Thirty first year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third king of Great Britain Etc.

It is Ordered That the Chruchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Edmonton in the said County of Middlesex have Notice of the within Appeal. And that they and all persons Concerned Do attend this Court on Thursday the Twenty first day of July instant at Nine o Clock in the Forenoon of the same day to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal

By the Court
