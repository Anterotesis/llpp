To the Right Worship full his Majesty's of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled in their General Quarter Sessions.

The humble Petition of the Vicar Churchwardens and Inhabitants of the Parish of Ealing all Zealing in the said County Whose Names are subscribed on behalf of themselves and other Inhabitants of the said Parish

Humble Sheweth That the Parochial Church of Ealing in the said County was a Brick & Stone Building of very great an liquity, and by length of time became so very ruinous and Decayed that the Inhabitants of the said Parish durst not assemble therein for Divine Worship, Whereupon a Taber [...] was Erected by the voluntary Contributions of some few of the said Inhabitants [...] Divine worship hath ever since boon perfomed, and an Annual Rent is now [...] Petitioners for the same.

That Great part of the said Church together with the Tower thereof about Five [...] Since fell down and noting now remains Standing but a Small part of one of the Walls thereof So that the said Church and Tower must Unaovidably be Rebuilt.

That the said Parish of Ealing being very Estencive and the Inhabitants thereof very numerous there being about Seven hundred Familys therein and the Town of Old Brent for [...] Which is Cheifly Inhabited by poor Watermen Fisherman and Day Labourers) being pa [...] and lying within the said Parish Your Petitioners are Burthened with a very Numerous [...] for whose Maintenance the Annual Assessments have in some Years amounted to Three Shillings in the Pound, And your Petitioners have lately Erected a Work house for [...] they are Indebted a Considerable Sum, So that Your Petitioners are not able by any [...] Contribution made amongst themselves to raise a Sum Sufficienct to Rebuild the said although they have Used their Utmost Endeavours so to do by getting a subscription an [...] themselves for that purpose but such Subscription in Case the whole moves subscribed [...] be paid, which & very uncertaine will amount to no more than the sum of [...] Hundred and Twenty Pounds, or thereabouts.

That your Petitioners have Employed Diverse able and Experienced Workmen have Carefully viewed the said Church as now in Ruins and made an Estimate of the [...] Rebuilding the same, which upon a Moderate Computation amounts to the Sum of Three [...] Pounds and Upwards, And the said Workmen are now ready in this Court to make [...] truth thereof

That the said Tabernacle in which your Petitioners now assemble for Divine Wo [...] a very slight Building and not likely to stand long, it being only Designed when Built for [...] Continuance and until the said Church could be Rebuilt

Your Petitioners humbly pray your worships will be Please Grant Your Petitioners A Certificate of the truth of the Premisses [...] to be Represented to his Majesty in Order for Obtaining his Grace [...] and favour for the Gaining his Royal Letters Patent for the Colle [...] the Benevolence and Charitable Contributions of his Loving Subjects [...] be Collected in the most speedy and Beneficial manner [...] so good and Pions a Work.

Tho: Lowther Tho Round Churchwarden

And your Petitioners shall Ever Pray Etc
