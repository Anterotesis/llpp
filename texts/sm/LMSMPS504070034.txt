To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the peace for the County of Middle sex at their General Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of John Hillier Apprentice to John Kershaw of the parish of Saint Dunstan Stepney in the County of Middlesex Linemaker

Humbly Sheweth That your petitioner on the twenty eighth day of September One thousand seven hundred and forty nine became bound an Apprentice unto the said John Kershaw with him to serve during the space of seven years from the date of the said Indenture relation being there unto had may more fully appear during which time the said John Kershaw was and is to teach and instruct his said Apprentice in the Art of Line making but hath not

Your petitioner further Sheweth that he hath at divers times been beat and very much brassed and by his said Master his Apprentices or Servants so that he has vomitted blood issuing out of his Nose and mouth for several weeks and without any reason or Provocation whatever and have wanted sufficient necessary's and have also endured many other great hardships

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly pray's that your Worships would be pleased to order that he be wholly discharged from his said Apprenticeship or such other relief as your Worships shall seem meet [...]

And your Petitioner as in duty bound shall ever pray

John Hillier
