To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Woodford in the County of Essex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a pass Warrant or order under the hands and Seals of P. Colquhoun and J. Browne two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (one whereof being of Quorum) bearing date the Thirty first day of May 1794 "William Matthews was removed and Conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Woodford as the place of his last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners are as they Conceived aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal to this Court Against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and Pray that your Worships will be pleased to Appoint a day in this present Session for the hearing and determining their said Appeal

And your Petitioners will pray Etc

J. Dacre Sollr. for the Appellants}

Henry Pools} Churchwardens

Gysbt [...] an [...] vorst Chas Pratt} Overseers
