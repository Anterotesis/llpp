To the Worshipful the Justices of the Peace in the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of John Brettoll Josiah Lister George Bowsked and John Jaques of the Parish of Hornsey in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That a rate or assessment according to a Pound rate after the rate of 1st 3d in the Pound made the 21st day of January 1796 by the Churchwardens and overseers of the poor of the sd Parish of Hornsey upon the inhabitants owners and sanpiers of Houses Cauds Tenements Tithes impropriate and impropriation of tithes in the said Parish touching the relief of the Poor of the said Parish and for other purposes mentioned in the statue made in the 43d year of the reign of the late Quan Elizabeth intilled "An Act for the relief of the Poor"whereby your Petitioners find themselves greatly aggrieved

For that the said rate or assessment hath not been made signed allowed and Published in such manner and form as the Laws made and now in force concerning the making signing allowing and publishing of rates for the relief of the poor of the said parish require

For that by the said rate or assessment Your Petitioners [---] are charged and assessed for Houses Lands Tenements and Premises held and cenfied in the said Parish in a much larger sum than [---] your Petitioners ought to be to Charged and assessed for the same and above the to prepostion at which Lands Tenements and Premises

belonging to William Crane, John Fields, Henry Fountain Richard Child, and Jonathan Heare are charged in the said Parish

For that your Petitioners John Brettell, George Bowsteads and John Jaques and also Edward Gray, John Willan, Philip Booth, and Benjamin Young Parishioners of the said Parish are rated upon a much higher Sum than they pay to there respective Landlows

For that all and every the Inhabitants Owners and Occupiers of houses Lands or Tenements in the said Parish who ought to be rated and assessed to the said rate are not therein rated and assessed

And for that the said Rate or Assessment is in other respects unequal Partial and illegal

Your Petitioners thereof humbly appeal to the Court against the said rate and that Your Worships will be pleased to appoint a day for hearing and determining the said Appeal
