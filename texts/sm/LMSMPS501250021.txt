To the Right Worspll her Matyes Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx

The humble Peticon of Robert Ansell

Sheweth That Yor. petr. did by his Indenture of Apprenticeship bearing date the 21o. September Anno Din 1709 become Apprentice unto one John Rawlins for the term of Seven Years comencing from the day of the date of the said Indenture As thereby may appear

That yor. petrs. said Indenture is not indented according to Law That your petrs. said Master doth very much abuse him and that yor. petr. is not able to learn his trade by reason his said Master is le severe to him That the said Rawlins lives is very ill Reputation Insomuch that name of his Neighbours can give him any Caracter that becomes a Man.

That yor Petr. therefore humbly prays Yor. Worspps to be pleased to order that his said Master may appear tomorrow morning by ten of the Clock before this Honble Bench To Shew cause why Yor. petr. may not be discharged from his sd Apprenticeship

And yor. petr. shall ever pray Etc
