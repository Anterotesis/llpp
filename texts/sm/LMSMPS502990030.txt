To the Rt. Worpll: his Maties: Justices of the peace for the County of Middx in their Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Petition of George Burton.

Sheweth That your Petr. became bound by Indenture of Apprenticehood bearing date the eleventh day of July 1732 to James Chevallier of Old Southampton buildings in the County of Middx Peruke maker for the term of nine Yeares to learn his Art.

That your Petr. in last June Sessions exhibited his humble Peticon to this Court alledging therein that your Petr. was very much beat and abused & prayed to be discharged but on hearing the Sd Peticon was dismist & your Petr. ordered to goe house to his Master & likewise that his Master was Ordered not to give him immoderate Correction,

That your Petrs. Master hath since cruelly & inhumanely beat & Kickt your Petr. ore the Head whereby your Petr. became deaf for upwards of a Fortnight.

That your Petrs. Master is Subject to great Passion in which your Petr. received a hick near your Petrs. Grain whereby your Petr. is became Bursten & severall other Blows and Hicks whereby your Petr. was very much bruised & hurty

That your Petr. having a natural aversion to Choose your Petrs. Mother at the time when your Petrs. became bound did agree wth: his Master that your Petr. shod. be allowed come Butter or told meat for Break fast or supper That your Petr. hath had [...] only [...] Bread & smal Bere for break fast & supper altho your Petrs. Master received wth. your Petr. Twenty Pounds in Money.

Yor Petr. therefore most humbly prays that he may be discharged from his sd Master & that your Petrs. Master may be ordered by this Honble Court to return or pay back to your Petrs. Mother or Friends the Sd. Sume of twenty pounds reced [...] with your Petr. in order to turn over or replace your Petr. to another Master.

And your Petr. shall pray Etc.

George Burton
