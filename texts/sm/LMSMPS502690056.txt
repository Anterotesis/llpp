To the Worshipfull his Maties Justices of the Peace at this present Generall Sessions Assembled

The Peticon of Mary Chapman Spr.

Humbly Sheweth: That your Petr. was put out an Apprentice, by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Anns Within tho Liberty of Westminster to Jane Roxborough the Wife of Stephen Roxborough to learn the Act [---] or trade of a Mantua Maker untill your Petr. came to the age of One and twenty years.

That yr. Petr. hath served her said Mistress upwards of three years, that during the said time yor. Petr. hath at severall times been beat in a very barbarous & Cruell manner both by her Master and Mistris without any provocation whatsoever & that your Petr. hath at severall times wanted both Victualls & drink & other necessarys.

Yor. Petr. therefore humbly prays your Worships that She may be discharged of her Apprenticehood to the said Jane Roxborough her Mistriss & your Petr. as in duty bound shall ever pray.

Mary Chapman
