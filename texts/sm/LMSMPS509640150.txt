To His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Court of General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex on behalf of themselves and other the Inhabitants of said Parish.

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of Ricd Davies and William Forsteen Esqres. Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace Acting in and for the County of Middlesex bearing date the 15th. day of June 1799 one Jane Smith Widow of William Smith deceased and their Two Children Vizt. Thomas and Elizabeth were Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint George in the said County of Middlesex as the lawful Settlement of her the said Jane Smith and her said Two Children

That by virtue of such Pass Warrant or Order of Removal the said Jane Smith and her said two Children have become and are now Chargeable to the Parish of Saint George in the County of Middlesex

Your Petitioners therefore conserving themselves aggrieved by such Pass Warrant or Order of Removal humbly appeal to this Court against the same and pray your Worships to appoint a day for hearing and determining said Appeal and that such relief

may be given and granted to your Petitioners in the Premises us to your Worships shall seem just and reasonable And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Jno. Clement Vestry Clerk of St. George Middx
