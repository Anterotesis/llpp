To the Worshipfull the Chairman and the rest of the Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex att the General Quarter Session's of the Peace held for the said County att Hick's hall in St. John Street on Monday the 11th day of October 1736

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers for the Poor of the parish of St Bridgett otherwise Brides London and the Inhabitants thereof

Sheweth That by Warrant under the hands and Seals of Thomas Clarges and Thomas Rea Esqrs Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Midlesex (One being of the Quorum) bearing date the seventeenth day of September last Elizabeth Neale Widdow and relict of John Neale deceased together with her Two Children (to wit) Elizabeth and Sarah by her said late husband were removed from the parish of St. Mary Le Bone in the said County of Midlesex to the parish of Saint Bridgett otherwise Brides London as the Place of their last legall Settlement Whereby your Petitioners thinck themselves Agreived

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the said Warrant and pray that this Court will Appoint a time to hear and determine the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall pray Etc

Tho: Harris V. X Sol: for St. Brides.
