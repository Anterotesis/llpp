To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green on the Ninth Day of January 1797.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of David Lloyd of Drury Lane in the County of Middlesex Pawnbroker

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the fifth day of January last convicted in the Penalty of Five Pounds by Aaron Graham Esquire One of the Justices of the Peace for the said County upon an Information Exhibited and prosecuted by Thomas Chapman charging That your Petitioner did in Drury Lane in the said County on the Seventeenth day of September last take and receive of Abigail Hill on Redeeming the Pledge thereafter mentioned one penny by way of Profit upon Six pence on the Twelfth day of August last Lent upon a Pledge of a Shawl to the said Abigail Hill contrary to the Statute Etc.

That your Petitioner conceives himself Aggrieved by such Conviction and is advised to Appeal against the same.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays your Worships will be Pleased to Permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be Heard at this present General Quarter Session of the Peace in and for the said County And that your Worships will Appoint a Day for hearing and determining the same.

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray & Etc.
