The Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace [...] Hall in the said County Assembled.

The humble petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. Anne within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of Joseph Girdler and Stephen Clarke Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for he County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Fifth day of June last Margaret Thomas Wife of Henry Thomas who has deserted her was removed and conveyed from the Parish of St. James at Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of st. Anne as the place of her last Legal Settlement whereby your petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved

Therefore they humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and Pray that the Benefit of this Appeal may be saved to your petitioners untill the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for this County And that your Worships will be Pleased to Order that the hearing and determining of the said Appeal be Adjourned untill the said next General Session of the peace And that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St. James Clerkenwell and all person concerned do attend the Court of the said next General Session some day in the said Session to hear and Abide the Judgement and Determination of the same Court touching the said Appeal.

And your Petitioners shall Pray Etc

Samuel Smith John Troller

Peter Denny Jas. Thomas} Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Said Parish of St. Anne
