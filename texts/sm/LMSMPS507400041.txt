Wm.

To the Worshipfull William Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition of Henry Cox one of the Head boroughs for that part of the Parish of St. Sepulchre which is in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the fourth day of May 1780 Sworn into the Office of Head borough for the said part of the said Parish for the Year then ensuing and duly Served the same accordingly

That on the 24 day of April 1781 Matthew Boyle of Cow Cross Street within the said part of the said Parish Brush maker was appointed Head borough in the Room of Your Petitioner for the Year commencing from the fourth day of May instant but for as much as he hath refused or declined to take upon him and be Sworn into the said office Your Petitioner hath been obliged to continue in and do the Duty thereof ever since

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays your Worships that the said Matthew Boyle may be Summoned to appear before Your Worships to shew Cause why he should not take upon him and be Sworn into the Office of Headborough according to his Appointment And that Your Petitioner may be discharged from the same And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc

Harry Cox Headborough
