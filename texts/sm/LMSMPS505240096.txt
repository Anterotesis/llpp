To the Right Worshipful Thomas Lane Esqr. and to the Worshipfull Justices of Peace Assembled together at the Quarter Sessions of the Peace held at Hicks Hall in and for the County of Middx on Monday the 4th. day of July 1763 and to the rest of his Majesties Justice of Peace for the said County of Middx

The humble Petition of John Turner late Keeper of his Majestys Prison Clerkenwell Bridewell

Sheweth That your Worships Petitioner has been keeper of the said Prison for about 2 years [...] past during all which time Your Petitioner humbly hopes he hath behaved himself honestly [...] by strictly obeying all your Worships commands and used the Prisoners committed to his care with [...] humanity and tenderness as the nature of their unhappy circumstances and conditions required & would adm [...]

That your Petitioner having had a large family and but in smal business was with incumbred with Debts [...] before your Petitioner was placed as Keeper of the said Prison and has now a wife and four smal [...] Children unprovided for and being at present out of all manner of imploy or Business they must inevitably become Destitute of the support of life and likely become burthen some to the Parish unless your Worships out of your tender and great goodnesses will be pleased to restore him to your Worships favour by reinstating him into his former post and place of Keeper of the said Prison-And as your Petitioner having a Relation or Friend (if your Petitioner should be so happy as to be reinstated) will immediately advance and pay off all your Petitioners Debts and incumbrances which he is now Incumbred with

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays your Worships in reg [...] to the hardship of his Case and the large family of Children which he now hath that your worships will be pleased to reastablish him and for the further will always keep himself sober and temperate and sincerely and faithfully promise to perform the trust so committed to his Care and Charge in every respect whatsoever as becomes your worships most distiful and obedient Servant

Jno Turner
