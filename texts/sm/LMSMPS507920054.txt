To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace at the [---] Sessions House on Clerkenwell Green in the said County of assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church- Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of The Reverend Sir George Booth Baronet and William Blackborow Esquire two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County bearing date the 11th. day of December last John Bond his Wife and two Children were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint James at Clerkenwell in the County aforesaid to the said Parish of Saint James Westminster as the place of their last legal Settlement whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and pray that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day to hear and determine the said Appeal

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc.

Jas: Dyson Solicitor for the Appellants
