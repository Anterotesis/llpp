Middlesex April Session 1785 }

To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the [...] Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the said County.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrants or Order under the Hands and Seals of Thomas Collins and George Mercer Esquires two of this Majestys Justices of the Pease in and for the County of Middlesex One being of the Quorum Bearing Date the twenty Ninth day of March one Warsant said hundred and Legaley Puse Isaac Pavelly Singleman was removed and conveyat from and out of the Parish of saint Mary to Bond in the said County to the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the County aforesaid as the place of his last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners Conceising & themselves to be Aggriesed by the said Order and Adjudication Humbly Appeal to this Court from the same and pray That the Benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them that the hearing and determining thereof may be Adjourned until the next General Session of the peace to be held in and for the said County That all parties may be then Ordered to attend And that Your Petitioners may have such relief in the premises- as may be then thought meet

And your Petitioners will pray Etc

Jno Skutt Sol for Appellants 9th April 1785
