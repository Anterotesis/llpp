To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Session of Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition & Appeal of Richard Osgood of the Parrish of Fulham in the County of Middx Mallster & Brewer

Sheweth That on the Twenty second day of April last at the House of Mr. John Filks scituate in Covent Garden in the County of Middx Your Petitioner Appeared before Thomas Burdus Esqr & Peter Elers Esqr two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County upon a Summons by them Issued upon a certain Information & Complaint exhibited against Your Petitioner by Edward Whinfield Gent one of the Collectors of his Maty's Revenue of Excise bearing Date the Sixteenth day of April last for the Forfeiture of Thirteen Pounds Twelve Shillings & Six pence therein said to be by Your Petr. incurred for & by reason of his treading & forcing together a Coach of Wetted Barley in order to the making into Malt within three Months before the making the said Information & Complaint against the form of the Statute in that Case made & Provided And that the said two Justices then & there gave Judgment & Sentence against Your Petr for the forfeiture aforesaid upon the Information & Complaint aforesd.

And That Your Petr. conceiving himself aggreived thereby doth humbly Appeal to Your Worships agt. the same.

Your Petr therefore humbly Prays such relief in the Premissed as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petr shall every Pray Etc. Richard Osgood
