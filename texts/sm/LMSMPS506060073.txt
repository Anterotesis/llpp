To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Robert Fladgate of Whembly in the Parish of Harrow on the Hill in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That on or about the nineteenth day of September last, a Second Rate and Assessment of Six Pence in the Pound for this Present Year said Parish of Harrow on the Hill for the Relief of the Poor of the same Parish, which said Rate on or about the Second day of November last was Allowed by Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same County.

That your Petitioner thinks himself much aggrieved by such Rate the same being very Unequal And your Petitioner is Rated more in proportion than the other Inhabitants and Parishoners of the same Parish he being thereby Raised and Advanced from the Sum of One hundred and Fifty two Pounds a Year; to the Sum of Two hundred and Forty Pounds a Year, when at the same time the rest of the Inhabitants and Parishoners are not by this Rate Advanced or Raised from their former Rates, nor are they thereby Assessed and Rated in Proportion to Your Petitioner.

Therefore Your Petitioner Appeals against the same Rate as Unequal and Humbly Prays relief in the Premisses And that this Court will Appoint some day the next General Quarter Session of the Peace for the hearing and Determination of this his said Appeal

And Your Petitioner will ever Pray Etc Robt Mason Solr. for the said Robt. Fladgate.
