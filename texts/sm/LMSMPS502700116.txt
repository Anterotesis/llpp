To the Right Worshipfull his Majestie's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions assembled

The humble Peticon and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the [...] Poor of the Parish of St. Michael Bassishaw London

Sheweth that by Virtue of a Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Anthony Wroth and Valentine Hilder Esqr Two of his Majestie's Justices of the Peace for the said County (Quor unus) bearing Date the 13th August 1730 Henry Dowell Son of Henry Dowell deced by his Wife Mary deced aged about Eight Years is Passed from the Parish of St. Andrew Holbourn above the Barrs in the County aforesaid to the Parish of St. Michael Bassishaw London before mentioned alledging the last legal Settlement of the said Henry Dowell to be there

Your Peticoners conceiving themselves agreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices do humbly appeal therefrom

And humbly Pray that your Worships would appoint a Day this Sessions to hear this Appeall And that your Worships will make an Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Andrew Holbourn above the Barrs and all Parties concerned in and about the Premisses may then attend hear and abide by the finall Determinacon of your Worships

Ant. Pollett Agent for ye Parish of St. Michl. Bassishaw} And Your Peticoners shall ever Pray Etc
