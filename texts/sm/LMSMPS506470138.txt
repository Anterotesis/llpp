To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex.-

The Humble Petition of the Journeymen Weavers in the Plain Velvet Branch in Spitalfields and places adjacent whose Names are hereunto Annexed

Sheweth That on or about the Twenty third day of August One thousand seven hundred and Sixty two certain Wages or Prices for Labour were a Agreed on between the Masters and Journeymen belonging to the said Plain Velvet Branch and that on or about the Sixteenth day of August One thousand seven hundred and Sixty nine certain other Wages or Prices for Labour were agreed on between the said Masters and Journeymen for such Inventions Improvements or Articles as were not comprized or settled in the first above recited Agreement or Prices and that there have since been Invented and now are divers other Articles in the said Branch for which no Provision or Prices are Ascertained or fixed by either of the said Agreements between the said Masters and Journeymen and therefore Disputes do very frequently happen between the said Masters and Journeymen To prevent which for the future Your Petitioners do most humbly Pray your Worships that your Worships will be pleased to regulate order and declare the Wages and Prices of Work in the said Plain Velvet Branch.-

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc.
