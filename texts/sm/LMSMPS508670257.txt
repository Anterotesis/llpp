To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in General Session of the Peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of John Frears of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex Pawn broker

Sheweth That by a certain conviction under the Hand and Seal of William Hyde Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing Date the twenty ninth Day of August one thousand seven hundred and ninety one made on the Information of one Thomas Sambidge Your Petitioner was convicted (as therein alledged) For that your Petitioner then using and exercising the Trade and Business of a Pawn broker did in his Shop Situate in Little Pultney Street in the Parish of Saint James Westminster in the County of Middlesex on the twenty fifth Day of September in the Year of our Lord one thousand seven hundred and ninety take and receive of and from one Mary Pais by way of Pawn or Pledge a Spoon and did then and there advance and lend thereon to the said Mary Pais a certain Sum of Money exceeding Five Shillings (to wit) the Sum of Seven of Seven Shillings and six pence of lawful Money of Great Britain Yet that your Petitioner being such Pawn broker as aforesaid did not (as therein also alledged) forthwith and before your Petitioner advanced an lent the said Sum of Money upon the aforesaid Pawn or Pledge enter or cause to be entered in a fair and regular manner in any Book or Books kept by your Petitioner so that Purpose a Description of the said Spoon which your Petitioner so received in Pawn or Pledge and also the Sum of Money to be advanced and lent theron with the Day of the Month and Year and the Name and Place of abode of the Person by whom the same was so pawned or pledged and the name and place of abode of the owner thereof according to the Information of the Person pawning or pledging the same according to the Statute in such Case made and provided but that your Petitioner (as therein also alledged) then and there wholly neglected to make such Entry as aforesaid

contrary to the Statute in such case made and provided and the said Justice did adjudge your Petitioner to pay and forfeit for the Offence supposed to be Committed by Your Petitioner the Sum of Ten Pounds

That Your Petitioner thinks himself aggrieved by the Judgement of the said Justice and therefore

Chetham Solr. for the Appellant} Your Petitioner doth appeal to your Worships at this Present General Session of the Peace against the Judgement of the said Justice and doth pray such Relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem meet.
