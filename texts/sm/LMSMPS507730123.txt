To the Worship [...] Mainwaring Esqr [...] Rest of His Majestys [...] peace for the County of [...] in their General Quarter [...] of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition of John Bruce Allen

Sheweth That by Indenture of Apprenticeship bearing date the second day of July 1782 your Petitioner became bound to Robert Leech of Clerkenwell Green in the parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the said County, Cabine Maker for the term of seven years from Not date in consideration of the Sum of forty pounds to him the said Robert Leech then paid he covenanted to find your Petitioner good and sufficient meat, drink, and Lodging during the said Terms

That your Petitioner hath performed all the Covenants on his part in the said Indenture contained for him to do and perform But the said Robert Leech is unable to do the like your Petitioners master being now under confinement for debt in the Kings Bench from whence there is not the least probability of his being released

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Worships to discharge him from his said Apprenticeship and to grant him such relief as in nature of his case requires

And as in duty bound

John Bruce Allen
