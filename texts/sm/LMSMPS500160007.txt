To the Right Worll: their Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Midds

The humble Peticon of Wm. Innes James Baker Richard Rains and others Butchers at Smithfields Barrs in the parish of St. Sepulchres in the said County

Sheweth

That yor. Petrs. pay greate Rents for their houses and Shopps and whereas a very ill Customs hath for some time past been made use of in selling meate on the Lords day yor. Petrs in complyance with yor. Works. order for the better observing the Lords day have declined and doe for beare to sell any meate on that day yet never thelesse at Cow Cross and other places pery greate quantities and parcells of meate are sold on the Sundays mornings the people who buy the same fetching itt out of Alleys and private places soe that the Sellers thereof cannot easily bee founds out but the buyers often carry the same in greate parcells although for the prevencon thereof yor. Petrs. keep their shopps open till 12 of the Clock on Saterday nights.

Yor. Petrs. therefore humbly pray that for the better putting yor. said Order in Execucon yor. Worps. wilbee pleased to grannt yor. Warrt. for the apprehending all such persons as shall bee soe found carrying meate on the Lords day that they may answer the Same before yor. Worps.

And yor. Petrs. shall over pray Etc.
