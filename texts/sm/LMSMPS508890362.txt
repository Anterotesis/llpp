To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden & Ely Rents in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the said County

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of P. Colquhourn Esq. and The Revd. H. Reynett [---] Clk two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace (one Whereof being of the Quorum) bearing the 23d day of October Instant William Clark aged about eighteen Months (Son of Christopher Clark who has deserted him) were removed and conveyed from the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the said County to the said Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents as the Place of his last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore appeal against the said Order and humbly Pray relief in the Premises and that the Benefit of their said appeal may be saved unto them until the next General Session of the Peace to be holden in and for the said County

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

Cox Hatton Garden Solir for Appellants
