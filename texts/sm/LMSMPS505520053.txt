To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church- Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Peter Planck and Thomas Balack Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 30th day of July 1765 Mary Might Widow of Henry Might deceased was removed from the Parish of Saint Martin in the fields within the Liberty of Westminster aforesaid to the said Parish of Saint James as the Place of her last legal Settlement Whereby Your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved and Appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore Pray this Court will be Pleased to appoint a day for the hearing and determining of the said Appeal and that all Persons concerned may then attend.

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc Thos: Thomas Sol for Appt.
