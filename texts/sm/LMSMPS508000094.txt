To the Right Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Sessions of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Anthelin in the City of London

Sheweth That by an order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex one being of the Quorum bearing date the first day of June Instant Mary Wellman Singlewoman was removed from the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex aforesaid to the said parish of Saint Anthelin as the place of her last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore conceiving themselves aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices Do humbly appeal to this Court for such Relief in the promises as to your worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

P. Tyrrell Soll. for the Petitioners

Middlesex. At the General Quarter Session of the peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex at the Session house in Clerkenwell Green on Monday the twenty seventh day of June in the twenty fifth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third King of Great Britain Etc

It is Ordered that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster and all Person concerned do Attend the Court at the Session house aforesaid on Thursday the thirteenth day of June Instant at the Hour of nine in the forenoon of the same day (on notice hereof in the meantime to be given to the said Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James aforesaid and all persons concerned) to hear and abide the Determination and Judgment of the said Court touching the said Appeal
