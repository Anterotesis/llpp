To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of John Marlar of Moncken Hadley in the County of Middlesex & Esquire

Sheweth That your petitioner having a house and Field containing about two Acres and an half of Land in the parish of Moncken Hadley in the County of Middlesex for his own residence He hath been assessed by a rate or assessment made the Nineteenth day of July last for relief of the poor of the said parish in the Sums of Two pounds and ten shillings being after the rate of Two shillings in the pound for the yearly value of his said House & Land

That your petitioner conceives the said Rate made upon him to be excessive and unequal For that several House holden Tenants and Occupiers of houses and Land of a greater yearly Rentor Value than his said premisses are rated lower in properties And most if not all the Inhabitants there are greatly under rated in proportion to the Sum of your petitioner is charged

Your petitioner therefore humbly conceives himself aggrieved by the above mentioned Rate or Assessment And doth appeal to this Court against the same and prayeth relief in the premissess Etc

Your petitioner humbly prays your Worships to Grant him an Order for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish and all other persons concerned to attend this Honoble Court on son day in this present Session to shew Cause why the said Rate or Assessment should not be set abide and Quashed and to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of this Court touching the premisses

T Bennett Sollr. for Appellt And your petitioner shall ever pray Etc
