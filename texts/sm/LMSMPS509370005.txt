To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel in the County aforesaid

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Daniel William Esquire and the Reverend Henry Reynell D. D. two of his Majesty Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty eigtith day of February one thousand seven hundred and ninety seven Mary White Singlewoman was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Christchurch in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Mary Whitechapel at the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary White whereby your Petitioner conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore humbly beg Leave to appeal from the said Order and pray that their said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the heaving and determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of the Peace to be field for the said County and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Christchurch Middlesex and all others concerned may be then ordered to attend And that your Petitioners may have [...] eat Relief therein as to the Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioners Will pray Etc.

J: Smith Solicitor for Appellants Great Prescott Street
