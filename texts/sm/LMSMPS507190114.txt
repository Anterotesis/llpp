To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of Saint George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster.

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of G: Howard and John Machin Esquires, two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex One being of the Quorum, bearing Date the fifth day of November 1779. A Female Child not yet Beptized born of the Body of Mary Evans, in or about the Month of June which was in the Year One Thousand, Seven Hundred and Seventy Eight hath lately been left in the [...] Parish of Paddington with intent to be Settled was removed and Conveyed from and out of the said Parish of Paddington in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster as the place of her last legal Settlement; whereby your Petitioners think themselves Aggrieved, and humbly Appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that your Worships will please to grant that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be Saved unto them, and that the hearing and determination thereof be Adjourned until the next General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County and that all Persons Conserned be Ordered to Attend this Court touching the said Appeal.

C: Saxon Soll. for the Inhabitants of St: George's.
