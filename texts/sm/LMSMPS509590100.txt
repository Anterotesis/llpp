To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices for the County of Middlesex in Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of Richard Murthwaite

Sheweth

That your Petitioner was on the 5th dy of January 1799 Convicted before Henry James Pye and Joseph Moses Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex upon the Oath of William Kettle in the penalty of Five pounds for demanding and receiving of the said William Kettle one Penny as Profit upon two Shillings sent by your Petitioner upon a Pawn of a Pair of Hose Otherwise a pair of Stockings to the said William Kettle in the name or under the Description of Mr. Roberts one penny being more than at the late of One halfpenny for the Loan of any Sum not exceeding the sum of two Shillings and six pence by the Calender Months including the Current months in Which the said Paron was received contrary to the Statute etc by Which Conviction your Petitioner conceive himself aggreived

And thereof your Petitioner Appeals to this Court against the same and prays that your Worships will hear him theon and Grant him such Relief as his Cave Requires and that a day may be appointed for the hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner will ever pray Etc

Richd Murthwaite
