To the Worll. his Majties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Peticon of Thomas Woodgate

Sheweth That yor. Peticonr. was on the 19th. of July 1717 bound an Apprentice by Indenture to Valentine Dickins of St. Margarett Westmr. in the County Undertaker for Funeralls for Seven from that time.

That yor Peticrs and Waster hath Absconded from his House for thirteen Months past or more, which is now Intirely Shutt up.

That yor. Peticr. hath had no Employmt. in the Business of an Undertaker which he was to Learn these Six Months past and at any- time Since his Buiding hath had but Very little Instructions therein

That yor Petcr verily beleives his Mars Circumstances are in- So badd a Condicon that it is next to an Impossibility he Should againe return to this Business.

That yor Petict hath taken out a Warrt thereupon from Nath Blackerly Esqr one of his Majties Justices of the Peace for this County and hath Endevred. to have his said Master taken thereupon but he cannot be found

Yor Petrcr therefore Humble Prays yor. Worshipps to make Such Ordr. for the Releife of Yor Peticr. in Orde. to his Discharge or Such other Ordr as to yor Worshipps Shall Seem meet.

And Yor. Peticr Shall ever pray Etc.
