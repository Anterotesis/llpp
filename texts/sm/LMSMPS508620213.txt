To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in the General Quarter Sessions of the peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church= =warden and Overseers of the Poor of that part of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn which his above the Bars in the County aforesaid.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals The saverend for George Booth Baronet and Rupert Clarke Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof is of the Quorum) bearing date the Twelfth day of January Instant George Foster was removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the said County to the said part of the said Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn as the place of his last legal Settlement

your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that the Benefit of their said Appeal may be reserved to them until the next General Session of the peace to be holden for the said County for hearing and determining the same And that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell be then and there required to attend to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of the Court touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Arthur Gliddon Solr for the Appets
