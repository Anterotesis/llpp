To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Royal Exchange in the City of London

Sheweth That by an Order or pass warrant bearing date the 15th day of July 1783 under the hands and seals of David Wilmot and John Spiller Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the peace in and for the said County of Middlesex (One whereof being of the Quorum) whereby Lydia Scott Widow of William Scott deceased were removed from the parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the said County to the said Parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Royal Exchange in the said City as the place of her last legal settlement

That Your Petitioners apprehending themselves aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant of the said two Justices humbly appeal against the same

And therefore pray that your Worships will be pleased to Order the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch aforesaid and all persons conceived to attend this Court some day this present Session to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of the Court touching the premisses And that your Petitioners may have such relief therein on to your Worships shall seem meet
