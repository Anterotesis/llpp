Middlesex To the Worshipfull Wm. Manwaring Esqr. Chairman and the rest of the Justices in the General Quarter Session of the peace assembled

The Potetion & Appeal of Stephen Symmonds

Humbly Sheweth That on or about the Second day of December last your Petitioner was convicted by Robert Smith Esqr. one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex in the Penally of twenty Pounds for that the sd. Stephen Symmonds on the twenty ninth day of November then last past Did sell to one James Douglas one Pair of Gloves of the price of One shilling and two pence & affixed a Ticket there to marked and stamped with a Stamp of less denomination than re directed to be affixed by an Act of Parliament passed in the 25th. Year of his present Majesty's Reign contrary to the sd. Statute As by the sd. Conviction will more fully & at large appear

Your Petitioner conceiving himself aggrieved by such Conviction humbly appeals against the same & prays a Day may be Appointed for the hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner will pray Etc
