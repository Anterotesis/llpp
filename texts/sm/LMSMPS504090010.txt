Middlesex To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition of John Hillier Apprentice to John Kershaw of the Parish of Saint Dunstan Stepney in the County of Middlesex Line Maker

Humbly Sheweth. That Your Petitioner on the Twenty Eighth day of September in the Year of our Lord One Thousand Seven Hundred and Forty Nine became Bound an Apprentice Unto the said John Kershaw with him to Serve dureing the Space of Seven Years from the date of the said Indenture (Relation being thereunto had may More fully and at large Appe [...] ) And dureing which said time the said John Kershaw was and is to Instruct his said Apprentice in the Art of Line making but hath not

Your Petitioner further Sheweth that he hath an diverse times been beat and very much Abused and Bruised by his said Master and his Apprentice and Servants so that he has Vomitted blood Issueing out of his Nose and Mouth, for Several Week's and without any Reason or Provocation whatever, And has Wanted Sufficient Necessary and have Also Endured many other Great [...] to whom Hardships, of which Your said Petioner has Complaind before Complained, but Nothing was done there [...]

Your Petioner therefore most humbly prays that Your Worships would be pleased to Order that he be Wholly Discharged from his said Apprenticeship or have Such other Relief as to Your Worships shall seem Meet

And Your Petitioner as in Duty Bound Shall ever Pray Etc.

John Hillier
