To the Worshippfull Thomas Law Esqr. Chairman and the rest of the Justice of the Peace at the Generall Sessions of the peace of our Lord the King now holden at Hicks Hall in saint Johns Street in and for the county of Middlesex.

The Humble Petition of Daniel Alloth

Humbly Sheweth. That your petitioner by Indenture of Apprentiship bearing date the Thirtyeth day of June in the Year of our Lord One thousand Seven hundred thirty Five was put an Apprentice (by the Charitable Society of Poiton a Province of France) to One Thomas Sorett of the Parish of Saint Gyles in the Feilds in the said county Watchmaker for the Term of seven Years From thence next Ensuing to Learn the Art and Trade of a Watchmaker Since which Your Petitioner hath Faithfully served the said Thomas sorett his said Master who hath Frequently Beat Abused and ill Treated your petitioner in a most [...] barons and Cruell Manner, without any just Provocation given by your Petitioner for the same

Yor. Petitioner therefore Humbly Prays the he May be Discharged from his said Apprentishipp or have such other Releise as to Your Worshipps shall seem Meet

And your Petioner Shall ever Pray Etc Daniel Allotte
