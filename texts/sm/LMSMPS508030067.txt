To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Giles in the Fields in the said County.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of William Coleman and George Byng Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace acting and for the said County (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the fifteenth day of October last, Elizabeth Phipps was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Enfield in the said County to the said Parish of St Giles in the Fields as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Elizabeth Phipps whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court against the adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order And Pray that your Worships will [---] appoint a day in this present Session for the hearing and determining the said Appeal

And your Petitioners will Pray Etc

Herbt: Robertson Solr. for the Appetts.
