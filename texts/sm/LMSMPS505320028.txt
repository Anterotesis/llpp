To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex att their General Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Thomas Paulin Gentleman on behalf of himself and Several Other Householders and Inhabitants of the parish of Fulham on Fulham side in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That the Accounts of Charles Young and John Plase Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Fulham on Fulham side for the year One thousand Seven hundred and Sixty two having been first Verifyed on Oath were allowed. and Confirmed by Thomas Miller Esquire One of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the Said County of Middlesex On the twenty second day of June One thousand Seven hundred and Sixty three

That there are several Extravagant and illegal Disburstments and Overcharges in the said Accounts which your Petitioner Apprehends would be disallowed by this Court and your Petitioner has Several Material Objections to the same Accounts

S Clarke Sollr for Appellt. 15th. Septr. 1763 Your Petitioner therefore humbly Apprehend himself Aggrieved by Such Allowance of the said Accounts and Doth Appeal against the same And Prays that this Court will appoint Some day in the next General Conar Session of the Peace to be held for this County of the said Charles Young and John Place and all Other persons Concerned to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of the same Court touching the said Appeal And that in the mean time the Benefit of the Said Appeal may be Saved to your Petr.

And your Petr. Shall ever Pray Etc.
