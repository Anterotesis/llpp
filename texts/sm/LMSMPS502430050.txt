To the Rt. Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx their Generall Quarter Sessions assembled

The Humble Peticon & [...] appeale at the Church wardens & Overseers of ye. Poor of the Parish of St. Botolph without Bishopsgate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of art Order on Pass Warrantende the Hands & Seals of Sir Daniel Dolins Knt. & Samll. Webb Esqr. Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx (Quor unus) bearing Date 13th: Day of Aprill 1727. Anne Cooper Widow of William Cooper deced was removed from the Parish of St. John at Hackney in the Said County of Middx unto the said Parish of St. Botolph without Bishops gate London, alledging the said Parish of St. Botolph without Bishops gate to be the Place of her last Legall Settlemt.

That Yor. Peticonr Conceiving them Selves agreived by the said Warranter order of the said Two Justices Do there fore appeale to yor. Worships there from, & humbly pray That your Worships will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses, & order, That the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. John at Hackney in the said County of Middx may appear beforeyon at a certain Day by your Worqs to be appointed to hear & abide the Indgment & Determinacon of your Worps toughing this Appeale.

And Yor. Petr. Shall ever pray Etc.
