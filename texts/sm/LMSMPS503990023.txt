To the Right Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden for the said County at Hick's Hall Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Hendon in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That By Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order Under the [...] and Seals of Henry Norris and Lister Selman Esquires two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (One being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty fifth day of August last past Martha Waters (in the said Order called) the Wife of Daniel Waters and three Children (in the said Order called their lawfull Issue) hamely John Aged Twelve Years Richard Aged Nine Years, And Martha Six Years Old were Removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Mary at Stratford Bow in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Hendon as the Place of their last legal Settlement

That Your Petitioner apprehend themselves aggrieved by said Pass Warrant or Order And the Judgment of the said two Justices And Do therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court against the same But in Regard That One of Your Petitioners Material Witnesses (and without whose Testimony Your Petitioners cannot as they are Advised with Safety proceed to Try their said Appeal) is now Absent and Cannot be produced at this present Quarter Sessions of the Peace to give his Testimony on the Behalf of Your Petitioners (and it being in Your Petrs. own delay)

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Pray That This Court will be pleased to Appoint some day [...] the next General Sessions of the Peace to be held for this County, to hear and Determine the matter touching their said Appeal.

And Your Petrs shall Ever Pray Etc

Nicoll Hatton Garden Sollr. for Appellts. 9th. October 1749.
