To the Right Worppll: His Maties: Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled in their genall Quarter Sessions

The humble Peticon & Appeale of the Resident Vice President Trearer & Court of Assistant of the Artillery Company of the City of London & John Smith their Termant

Sheweth That by Order made at the Genall Quarter Sessions of the Peace held for this County by adjornment on the tenth day of October in the year 1715 It was Ordered that the Said Artillery Company should be wholly Discharged from Paying towards the releife of the poor of the Parish of St Giles without Cripplegate in this County [...] [...] in respect [...] of the Said Company ground commonly called the Artillery ground [...] [...]

That Notwithstanding the said Ordered the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Said Parish have rated and assessed the said Company or the said John Smith to the poors rate in the Sume of four pounds & upwards for this present year which your petionrs: are advised they ought not to stand charged therewith

That your Peticoners conceive themselves agrieved with the said Rate or Assessment & humbly Pray that they may be wholly discharged from payment of the said Rate or Assessment. [...] and to be releife in the premisses,

And Yor Petrs: shall ever Pray Etc
