To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal bearing date the 28th. day of January 178 [...] under the Hands and Seals of D. Wit [...] and J: Penteage Esquire two of this Majestys Justices of the Peace [...] in and for the County (one whereof being of the Quorum) Henry Le Vallain aged about Nine years James Le Vallain aged about Six years and an half and Jane Le Vallain aged about Five Years and an half three Children who have gained no legal Settlemant in their even right lawful Issue of John David Robert Le Vallain and of Ann his Wife who have deserted them and left them destitute and unprovided for were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Edmenton the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the sd. John David Robert Le Vallain Haller of the said Henry Le Vallain James Le Vallain and [...] Jane Le Vallain

Your Petitioners Conceives themselves aggrieved by the Adjudication of the said two Justices and therefore humbly Appeal to this Honble. Court agst. the same And pray your Worships will Appoint a day in this Present Session for the hearing and determining the said Appeal

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc

Phi: Jas May Solicetor for the Appellants
