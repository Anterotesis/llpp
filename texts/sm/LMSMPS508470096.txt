To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of Margaret Walton Amelia Brown and Jane Walton

Sheweth That by a certain Conviction under the Hand and Seal of William Hyde Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the Sixteenth day of April instant your Petitioners were convicted of selling on the Eleventh day of March last past to one Elizabeth Wood me pair of Leather Gloves exceeding the Price or value of one shilling and four pence (to wit) of the Price or value of one shilling and eight pence [...] stamp D [...] pence with a stamp or Mark of less Denomination or Value than in and by the Act of Parliament in that Case made and provided is directed to be affixed being affixed to the same (to wit) with a Stamp or mark of the Denomination or Value of one penny contrary to the Statute and your Petitioners were adjudged to forfeit for the same the Sum of twenty pounds

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by such Conviction humbly beg leave to appeal to this Honourable Court against the same pursuant to a Recognizance entered into by your Petitioners before the said Justice for that purpose

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray etc

Margaret Walton Amelia Brown Jane Walton
