To the Right Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex At their Quarter Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Hendon in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth

That By Virtue of a Warrant or Order Under the Hands and Seals of James Fraser and Daniel Gach Esquires Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace For the City and LIberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex (One whereof being Of the Quorum) bearing Date the Sixteenth Day of September- One thousand Seven Hundred and Forty Eight Mary Griffiths in the said Warrant or Order called the wife of Thomas Griffiths was Removed and Conveyed From the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Hendon As the Place of the last Legal Settlement of the said Mary Griffiths.

That Your Petitioners Apprehend themselves Aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of Removal.

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court Against the same, And pray that this Court will Appoint some day in this present Session for the Churchwarden and Overseers Of the Poor of the said parish of Saint Martin in the Fields And All other persons Concerned to Attend this Court to hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners Shall Ever pray Etc

John Nicoll Hatton Garden Sollicitor for Appellants } Exd 10th. October 1748.
