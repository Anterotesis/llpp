To the Worshipful the Chairman and others his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in Quarter Session assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of Dodding Jonathan Bruce of the Parish of Saint George Middlesex in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a certain Order bearing date the sixteenth day of June last made by and under the Hands and Seals of John Staples Esquires and the Reverend Herbert Mayo Doctor in Divinity two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex on the Complaint of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint George Middlesex, the said Justices did adjudge your Petitioner to be the reputed Father of a certain Male Bastard Child of which one Mary Thomas single Woman then before was delivered and which was chargeable to the said Parish.

And for Relief of the said Parish and relief of the said Bastard Child It was ordered by the said Justices that your Petitioner should forthwith pay or cause to be paid the said Churchwardens and Overseers on some or one of them the Sum of Five Pounds sixteen Shillings for and towards the Maintenance of the said Male Bastard Child to the Time of making their Order.

And they did further Order that your Petitioner should likewise pay or cause to be paid to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish for the Time being the Sum of Two Shillings and Six Pence weekly and every Week from the Date of their said Order for and [...] towards the keeping sustentation and Maintenance of the said Bastard Child for and during so long Time as the said Bastard Child shall be chargeable to the said Pairsh.

Wherefore your Petitioner thinks himself aggrieved by the said Adjudication and Order of the said two Justices and humbly appeals against the same and prays Relief in the Premisses Etc.

D J Bruce.
