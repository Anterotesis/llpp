To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the peace assembled

The Humble petition and Appeal of James Dewey

Sheweth That on the sixteenth day of May One thousand seven hundred and Ninety five Your petitioner was Convicted before Patrick Colquhoun Esquire and Daniel Williams Esquire two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex for that Your Petitioner being a Master Weaver in the Silk Manufacture within the said County did on the 22nd. day of April 1795 in the liberty of Norton. Falgate in the said County unlawfully pay to one Samuel Hollowood a Journey man Weaver in the Silk Manufacturer in the County of Middlesex lowet in the parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the said County for certain Work made and manufactured by him the said Samuel Hollowood for Your Petitioner to wit Seventeen Yards and One half of a Yard of Umbrella Silk Serge made with single Silk in the warp and two threads in the Leish and being of the breadth of forty five Inches the Sum of seven pence farthing per Yard for each Yard thereof, the said Sum of seven pence farthing per Yard being a less price than was set had regulated Ordered and declared by the Justices of the peace for the said County at their General Quarter Session of the Peace holden for the said County by adjournmt on the 18th. day of July in the July in the 32nd. Year of the Reign of His present Majesty in pursuance of the Statute in that Case made and provided contrary to the form of the Statute in such case made and provided, and that Your Petitioner was by the said two Justices adjudged to forfeit and pay for the same the Sum of Fifty pounds of lawful Money of Great Britain

That Your Petitioner conceiving himself aggreived by the said Conviction humbly appeals against the same and prays such relief in the premises as to Your Worships shall seem meet Etc. Etc

And Your Petitioner will ever pray Etc.

James Dewey
