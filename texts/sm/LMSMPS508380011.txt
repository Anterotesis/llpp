To His Majesty's Justices for the County of Middlesex at this present General Session of the Peace for the said County assembled

The Humble Petition of Francis Calvart of the Tottenham Court Road in the County of Middlesex Post Master

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on or about the 7th day of October instant convicted in the Penalty Â£10 by William Hyde Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices for the said County for that your Petitioner being a Person licensed to let out. Horses to hire for the purposes travelling Post by the Mile & from Stage to Stage and residing within 5 Miles of the Head Office for Stamps did not nor would on the 23d. & 24th days of July 1788 being the Times appointed for that purpose by the Commissioners for putting in Excution a Certain Act of Parliament made & passed in the 25th Year of the Reign of his present Majesty's Intitled An Act for repealing Duties on Licences taken out by Persons letting Horses for the purposes of travelling Post Etc. (at the Public Notice thereof was given in the Gazette) bring in or deliver to John Sargeant being the Person appointed by the said Commissioners for the purpose of receiving the same at the Office appointed for the reception of the Duty on Post Horses the Accounts that is to say the Stamp Office Weekly Accounts in that Behalf directed in and by the said Act of Parliament for the 4 Weeks ending on the Saturday preceeding such days of delivery but therein failed.

And your Petitioner thinking himself aggrieved thereby humbly begs leave to appeal from such Conviction And that you will please to appoint a Day in the present Session for the that purpose

And your Petitioner will ever pray Etc.
