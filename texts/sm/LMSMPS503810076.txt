The King on the Information of James Winter Against Thomas Goodwin

To the Worshipful Thomas Lane Esqr. and the rest of his Matys Justices of the Peace at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden by Adjournment on Monday the twelfth day of October 1747 at Hicks's Hall in and for the County of Middlesex

The humble Petition and Appeal of Thomas Goodwin

Sheweth

That your Petitioner was on or about the twenty fifth day of July last upon the Information of James Winter Convicted before Henry Broadhead Esquire one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of selling exposing Some Cloths to Sale Contrary to two Acts of Parliamt. the one Made in the fourth and the other Made in the Sevenths year of his late Majesty King George the first And Adjudged to have [---] forfeited Certain Penaltys Mentioned in the Said Conviction

That your Petitioner Apprehends and is Advised that the said Conviction is illegal & wants form

May it please your Worships to Re-hear the said Cause and to grant to your Petitioner Such relief as in your Judgment Shall Seem Adviseable

And your Petitioner Shall ever pray Thos Goodwin.
