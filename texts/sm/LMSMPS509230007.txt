To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Milton next Gravesend in the County of Kent.

Sheweth

That by virtue of an Order of Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Ricd Davies Esquire and Henry Reynett Doctor in Divinity two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex (where of one is of the Quorum) bearing Date the 22d. day of March 1796. James Joseph Grigstone lawful Son of Joseph Grigstone Deceased was removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middx unto the said Parish of Milton next Gravesend in the County of Kent Alledging the said Parish of Milton next Gravesend to be the place of his last legal settlement.

That Your Petitioner conceived themselves of be aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices to therefore Appeal to Your Worships therefrom and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the Premises and Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middx may appear before you at a certain day to be appointed to hear & abide the judgment & determination of your Worships touching the Appeal.

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
