To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Upminster in the County of Essex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hand and Seal of Lister Selman Esqr. One of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex aforesaid bearing Date the 23d. Day of December 1762 Elizabeth Corrial Widow of John Corrial deceased and Children viz. Elizabeth aged Eight Years and Mary aged three years were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Mary Stratford Bow in the same County as Rogues and Vagabonds (viz) wandering and begging there to the said Parish of Upminster as the Place of their last Legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant of the said Justices Do humbly Appeal against the same and pray that the Hearing and Determining thereof may be Adjourned until the next General [...] Session of the Peace to be held in and for the said County of Middlesex And that the Court will be pleased to Appoint a Day in the said next Session for the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Mary Stratford Bow and for the Constable Headborough Tythingman and other Officer of the Peace of the same Parish and all other Persons concerned then to attend to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the then Court touching the said Appeal.

And your Petrs. will pray Etc.

Geo: Fair Attey. for Appellants.
