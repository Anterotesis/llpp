To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace at Hicks's Hall in Saint John Street in and for the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of the Rolls in the said County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of David Wilmot and Charles Digby Esqrs. two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 18th. day of February 1774 Elizabeth Peters was removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch in the County of Middlesex to the Liberty of the Rolls in the said County as the Place of her last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved and against the said Pass Warrant or Order do Appeal

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray this Court will be pleased to appoint some day in this present Session for the hearing and determining the matter. touching the said Appeal And Your Petitioners shall pray Etc.

Middlesex At the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex at Hicks's Hall in Saint John Street in the said County on Tuesday the twelfth day of April in the fourteenth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third King of Great Britain Etc.

It is Ordered that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Leonard Shoreditch have notice of this Appeal And that they and all persons concerned do Attend this Court on Thursday the fourteenth day of April Instant at Nine of the Clock in the forenoon to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of the Court touching the said Appeal

By the Court Butler
