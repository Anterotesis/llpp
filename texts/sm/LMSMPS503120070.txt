To the Right Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace at their Quarterly Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition of William Murphy

Sheweth That yor. Petr. was by Indenture of Apprentice ship bearing date the Thirteenth day of October in the year of our Lord 1729 Bound on Apprentice unto John Hickman Nailor for and during the Term or Space of Seven Years during which time your Petitioners said Master was to find and provide for your Petr. Meat Drink Washing and Lodging and at the end of his said Apprenticeship the said John Hickman was to pay as our Petr. Five Pounds to buy him a sint of Cloaths, which Indenture of Apprenticeship expires the 30th. day of October instand relation being thereunto had more fully and at large is doth and may appear, Not with standing yor. Petr. hath been after times Boat, Brmised and ill treated & hath been refused entrance into the house of his said Master to do his business & learn his trade of a Nailor as an Apprentice ought to do But also refused Provisions & Necessaries according to yr Covenants contained in the said Indenture whereby your Petr, is put to great Hard ships & Diffculties to Maintain himself and also yr. benefit of warning his trade,

Your Petr. therefore most humbly prays that yor Worships would be pleased to order that he may be discharged from his said apprentice ship or such other refeif in yr premisses as to your worships shall seem meet.

And your Petr. shall evers pray Etc
