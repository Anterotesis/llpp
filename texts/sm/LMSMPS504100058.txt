To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace at Hicks's Hall in and for the said County assembled on Tuesday the Sixteenth day of April One Thousand Seven Hundred and Fifty One.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Egham in the County of Surry

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Lord Kinnoul and John Goodchild Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex aforesaid bearing date the fifteenth day of March last for the removing of Richard Cousill with two Children to wit Elizabeth. aged about thirteen years and John Aged about Nine years from the Parish of Teddington in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Egham in the County of Surry the said Richard Cousill and the said two children Elizabeth and John were removed and brought to the said Parish of Egham By which Order and removal your Petitioners think the said Parish of Egham is Aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray that your Worships will be pleased to Appoint a day for hearing their said Appeal and that the said Parish of Egham may have such further and other relief in the premisses as to your Worships shall seem meet And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
