To the Right Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Sessions of the Place assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of parish of Chatham in the County of Kent.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an order of Removal appearing to be under the Hands and Seals of Thomas Lockwood and G. Allcock Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex bearing Date the Seventeenth Day of December last Mary Billings Singlewoman was removed from the Parish of Saint Mary le Bone in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Chatham the said Justices thereby adjudging that to be the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Billings.

That your Petitions conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeal there from.

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that your Worships would be pleased to appoint some Day in his present Session to hear this their Appeal and to make an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint [...] Mary le Bond and all Parties concerned in and about the Premisses may then and there hear and abide the determination of your Worships therein.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Jno. Crowcher Thos.Waring } Churchwardens

John Irwin Abrm: Smith Wm. Wilson Geo: Guy } Overseers of the Poor} of the Parish of Chatham.
