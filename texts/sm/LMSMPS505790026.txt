Middlesex To the Worshipfull the Chairman and other his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said County in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of Henry Phelps

Most Humbly Sheweth That your Petitioner is a Hackney Coachman and on the 17th. of November last between the Hours of five and Six o'Clock in the afternoon your Petitioner took up one Ann Loveridge (as his Fare) opposite the Crown and Anchor Tavern Saint Clements and Carryed her to the 14 Starrs Rosemary Lane where he satt her Down And your Petioner Demanded two Shillings for his said Fare which she refused to pay And your Petitioner thereupon Applyed to George Garrett Esqr. one of the Justices of the Peace for the said County who Issued his Summons for her Appearance on the Day therein mentioned but she not appearing the said Justice Granted his Warrant; On which the said Ann was apprended and taken before the said Justice who Ordered her to pay yr Petr. his said Fare together with a Certain Sum for your Petrs. Expences and Loss of Time the which she refused to do But gave Bail to appear at this Session

That your Petitioner has been at great Expences in the Premes besides his Loss of Time

Therefore most Humbly Prays your Worships to take his Case under your Consideration and relieve him in such Manner as to your Worships may seem meet

And as in Duly Bound he will for ever Pray

Henry Phelps
