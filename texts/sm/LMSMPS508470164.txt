To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the Peace at the Session House Clerkenwell Green in the said County Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of the Revd H. R. Courtenay. D. D. and George Reid Esquires Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the City and Liberty of Westminster (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 1st. day of July 1789 John Banham and his Two Children, Viz Robert George aged Seven Years and Sarah aged upwards of Six Years were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster aforesaid to the said Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggreived.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against Adjudication of the said Two Justices and their said Order & pray that their said Appeal may be saved unto them and that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day to hear and determine the same

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc.

Benjn. Aked Benjn. Capper Robt. Dunkarton William Butler Thos Middleditch Geo. Bride
