Middlesex. To the worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and oversees of the Poor of the parish of saint Mary Whitechapel in the County aforesaid

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hand and Seals of Rice Davies Esquire and the Reverend Henry Reynett D:D two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex aforesaid (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing late the first day of November one thousand seven hundred and ninety Seven Ann Parker Wife of Thomas Parker who hath left her was removed. and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Algate in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish o Saint Mary Whitechapel as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Ann Parker whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly big leave to appeal from the said Order and pray [...] their said appeal may be saved unto theirr and that the heaving and determining there of may be adjourned until the next General Session of the Peace to be held for the said County And that the churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate, [---] and all others concerned may be then ordered to attend And that your Petitioners may have such Relief therein as to the court shall some meet.

and your Petitioners

Will ever pray Etc

I Smith Solicitor for appellants Great Prescott Street
