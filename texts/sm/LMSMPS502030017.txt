To the Right Worspll: his Maties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx Assembled in their Genll. Sessions

The humble Peticon of the Churchwardens and Overseers of ye Poor of the pish of Twickenham in the said County

Sheweth That At a Court Lect lately holden for the Manner of Isleworth Syon in the said County William Glover & John Gill Clandestinely gott themselves Elected into the Office of Headboroughs for the Parish of Twickenham [...] aforesaid & Since have also gott themselves Clandestinely Sworn into the said Office wth. intent to gain a Settlement in the said Parish as appears by the Effects annexed

That the said William Glover, & John Gill are not Parishioners in ye sd Parish of Twickenham but live their only by Virtue of a Certificate from the Churchwardens Etc of St Giles in ye Fields & St. Andw. Holborn in ye sd County And are also Persons of Very mean Circumstances who if they the permitted to Serve the said Office will wth. their Familys in all Likelyhood become a Charge to the said Parish

Yor. Petr. therefore humbly pray that Your Worpps will be pleased to Consider the Premisses & give them such releife therein As to your Great Wisdons Shall Seem meet

And Yor Petr. as in Duty bound Shall ever Pray Etc

Richard Silvester henry Coley
