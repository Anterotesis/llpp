To the Worshipfull His Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeale of Henry Southey

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands of Thomas Wood and Richard Taylor Esquires Two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex (one) being of the Quorum) bearing date the 10th day of November 1784 concerning a Male Bastard Child lately born in the Parish of Laleham in the County of Middlesex of the Body of Sincere Ford Single Woman (as alledged by the said order) Upon the Complaint of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Laleham Your Petitioner is adjudged to be the reputed Father of the said Bastard Child and is thereby ordered to pay to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish the Sum of Two Pounds and six pence for the Lying in of the said Sincere Ford And also the sum of One Shilling and Six pence Weekly for and Towards the maintenance of the said Bastard Child for so long Time as the said Bastard Child shall be Chargeable to the said Parish

Ashfeild Sollr for the Appelant} That Your Petitioner apprehends himself to be aggreived by the said Order and therefore humbly appeals against the same and Prays Relief in the Premises and that the benefit of this Your Petitioners Appeal may be saved unto him and that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned untill the next General Sessions of the Peace for 6 the said County
