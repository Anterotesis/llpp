To the Right Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex In their Gent Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Oversees of the Poor of the Parish of St. Botolph Without Bishopgate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant mides the hands And Seals of John Tall Esqr. and John Lawton Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one Whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Fifth Day of January 1747 Deborah Rickabey Widow Was Passed from the Liberty of Norton Falgate in the County of Middlesex to the Parish of St. Botolph Without Bishopgate London aforesaid Adjudging the Last Legal Place of Settlement of the Said Deborah Ruckabey to be in the said Parish of St. Betolph Without Bishopgate And Your Petr. (acceiring themselves Agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices Do humbly Appeal therefrom and humbly Pray Your Worships

Burr Attorney for Bpsgater Parish} Would appoint some Day this Present General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the Said County to hear this Appeal and that Your Worships Will make an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Liberty of Norton Falgate and All Parties Concerned in and about the Premises may then Attend hear and abide the Final Determination of your Worships

And your Petrs. Shall ever Pray Etc
