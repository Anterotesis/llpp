To the Rt. Worshipfull his Maties Justices of the Peace for ye. County of Middx in their Genll. Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of John Bargean of the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate in the said County

Sheweth That Your Petioner hath lately been nominated and elected one of the Headboroughs for the Liberty called Grubstreet Liberty in the said parish of St. Giles Without Cripplegate Notwithstanding Your Petitioner hath not resided in the said Parish above four Years and is no Tradesman but only a servant to a Gentlemen in the City of London And that there are divers Persons in the said Liberty who have lived longer in the said Parish and ought to serve the said Office before Your Petitioner

Therefore your Petitioner most humbly prays this Court will be pleased to order that the Constable and antient Inhabitants of the said Liberty may attend this Court sometime this present Generall Quarter Sessions to shew cause why Your Petitioner should not be discharged from serving the said Office of Headborough for the said Liberty

And Your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
