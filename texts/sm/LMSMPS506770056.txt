To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session of the peace at the Guild hall at Westminster in the said County assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of Westminister in the said County.

Sheweth

That by virtue of an Order under the hands and seals of John Cox and William Gascoigne Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing Date the Sixth Day of November last Mary Gobby the Wife of George Gobby (in the said Order mentioned and described to be the Wife of John Gobby) gone from her and their two Children namely George aged five years and Susana aged three years were removed and conveyed from the parish of Saint Pancras in the said County to the said Parish of Saint Anne as the place of the last legal settlement of the said John Gobby and Mary his Wife and their said two Children Whereby your petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order And pray that your Worships will please to appoint some Day in this present session for the hearing and determining the said Appeal and to Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Saint Pancras and all Persons concerned doe then Attend this Court.

And Your Petitrs: shall pray Etc

Edward Lane Willm. Roby } Churchwardens

and

Chas. Swindin Richd: Mumby } Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Saint Anne
