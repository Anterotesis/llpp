To the Worshipfull John Hawkins Esqr. one of his Majests Justices of the peace in and for the County of Middlesex and others his Fellows Justices Etc now Assembled at Hicks Hall

The Humble Petition of Barney Dormon an Apprentice

Sheweth That your Petitioner was by Indenture of Apprenticeship bearing date the Twentieth day of October in the Year of our Lord one Thousand seven hundred and sixty Five bound an Apprentice to John Hamersley of Bloomsbury in the County of Middlesex Jeweller for the Term of seven Years and also in Consideration of Ten pounds and Your Petitioners Father was to Find your Petitioner in all manner of Cloaths Juring the said Term of seven Years

That your Petitioners master was about Eight months ago a Prisoner for debt in his Majestyes Prison of the Fleet but got discharged out of the same about six months ago since which he has absconded from his Family and left your Petitioner unprovided for

Therefore your Petitioner most humbly prays your worships to make an Order of this Honourable Court for his said Master to attend the said Court on the First day of next Sessions to shew Cause why he shou'd not be discharged from his said Apprenticeship and that a Copy of this Petition & Order served on Your Petrs. Masters wife be good Service and Your Petr shall ever very

Barny Dormon
