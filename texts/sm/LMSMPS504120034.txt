To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St James within the Liberty of Westmr in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal Under the hands and Seals of Thomas Blencows and William Ashby Esqrs. Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing date the 30th day of May 1751 (one whereof being of the Quorum) Jane the wife of Thomas Southall) as there in Called and Named) was Removed from the Parish of Harlington in the said County to the said Parish of St James as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Thomas Southall and Jane his wife Whereby Your Petitioners think themselves Aggrieved and Appeal to this Court against the same

Therefore humbly Prays this Court will Appoint a time for hearing and Determining the said Appeal and that all Persons Concerned May then Attend

And your Petrs. shall ever Pray Etc Saml. Seddon agent for S' James's
