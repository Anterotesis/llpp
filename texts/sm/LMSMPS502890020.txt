To the Right Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx in their General Sessions assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Great Eversdon in the County of Cambridge

Middx ss. Sheweth That by Warrant or Order dated the 30th. day of December last past and under the hands and Seals of Henry Coltman and William Gould Esqrs. two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said [---] County (Quorum Unus) Mary Foreman Widow was removed from the Parish of Edmonton in the said County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Great Eversdon in the said County of Cambridge under [...] a pretense as your Petitioners an informed of it's being the place of her last legal Settlement

That the said Warrant or Order was not served on or left with or for your Petrs: till the 20th. day of January last past

That your said Petrs: conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order of the said two Justices of the Peace and humbly appeal against the same

Therefore your said Petrs: most humbly pray a day to be appointed sometime this present General Sessions of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex for the hearing and determining this their Petition and Appeal

And your Petrs. shall ever pray Etc

Middx ss. Ad General Quartial Session Pacis Dni Regis tent & Com Middx apnd Hicks Hall in St John Street in Com prd P adjorn die Lune Scilt decimo nono die February Anno regni Dni Georgy scdi nunc Regis Magne Britannie Etc Sexto

It is Ordered by this Court that the hearing & determining the matter of this Appeale be and the same is hereby adjourned untill the next General Sessions of the Peace to be holden for this County, and that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Edmonton in this County (upon notice of this Appeale & Order) and all Persons concerned doe attend the Court of the said next General Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said County by adjornment on Fryday the twenty third day of February instant at ten of the Clock in the forenoon to heare & abide the Judgment & determinacon of the same Court touching the said Appeale

P Cur Walter
