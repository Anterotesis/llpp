Middlesex. To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esqr. and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition of John Beddow of Great Shire Lane in the Parish of Saint Clement Danes Scrivener.

Sheweth. That by a certain Indenture of Apprenticeship bearing date the 28th. day of July 1779 James Keigwin was bound out of the Grey-coat Hospital Westminster to your Petitioner for the Term of Seven Years, two years part thereof are now unexpired

That on or about the 18th day of August last your Petitioner received a Summons from the Worshipful David Walker and another Gentleman in the Commission of the Peace, for your Petitioner to attend them at the Rotation office in Hyde Street Bloomsbury on Friday then next following to answer a Complaint made against Your Petitioner by the said James Keigwin for ill treating him and not instructing him in the art of a Stationer to which Summons Your Petitioner appeared

That by an Order of Adjudication under the Hands and Seals of the said D. Walker and H. Messiter Esqrs. two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex bearing date the 20th day of August last the said Justices did discharge him the said James Keigwin from his said Apprenticeship whereby your Petitioner is greatly aggrived

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays your Worships to set aside the said Order of Adjudication and to order his said Apprentice to return to your Petitioner, or to make such further Order in the Premises as your Worships in your great widsom shall seem meet.

And your Petitioner will ever Pray Etc.
