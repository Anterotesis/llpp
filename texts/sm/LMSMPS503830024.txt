To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace at their Genral Quarter Sessions of the Peace at Hicks's Hall in the said County of Middlesex Assembled on Tuesday the twelfth day of January 1747.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Eten in the County of Buckingham

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the hands and Seals of Christopher Tower and George Tash Esquires too of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex Dated the 17th. day of December 1747 Willilam James and Hannah his Wife together with John their Son about the Age of Ten Years were removed from the Town of the birdge in the said County of Middlesex to the Parish of Eten in the said County of Buckingham as their last Legal Place of Settlement

That the said William James and Hannah his Wife and John their Son not being Parishioners of the said Parish of Eton your Petitioners think themselves Aggreived by the said Order

Yor. Petits. therefore Pray Your Worship that the said Order of the said two Justices may be discharged and that your Petitioners May have Such other Relief in the Premisses as to your Worships shall seem meet [...] [---]

And your Petitrs. shall ever Pray Etc
