To the Worshipful William Mainwaring Esquire and the Rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Sessions assembled.

The humble Petition of William Fallows of Knights bridge in the County of Middlesex Esquire.

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on Wednesday the twelfth Day of December last convicted in the Sum of Twenty Shillings (upon the Information of Mrs. Harriet Kingston) under the Hands and Seals of William Addington[---] Esquires [...] one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said County City and Liberty for causing Hurt and Damage to the Person of the said Mrs. Harriet Kingston upon Friday the seventh day of December last upon the Highway in Fulham Road in the said County by driving your Petitioner Carriage against her contrary to the Statute etc

That so far from your Petitioner's driving his Carriage against the said Harriet Kingston the said Harriet Kingston drove her Carriage in upon your Petitioner's Carriage and thereby caused much Damage to your Petitioners said Carriage

That at the Time of your Petitioner being so convicted finding himself aggrieved entered into a Recognizance before the said William Addington [---] Esquires with [...] Surety [...] to try his Appeal at these present Sessions

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays that the said Conviction [...] may be quashed and that he may be at Liberty to enter and try his Appeal at these present Sessions agreeable to his Recognizance

And your Petr. will ever pray Etc.
