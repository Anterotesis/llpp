To the Rt. Worpll: his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middx in their Generall Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Peticon of Mary Hollyman

Sheweth That your Petr. became bound by Indenture of Apprenticehood bearing date the 23d Day of September in the Year 1728 to Robert Carlisle of the parish of St Andrew Holborn in the County of Middx Cork Cutter to learn his Art & to serve from the Day of the Date above sd. untill yor Peticoner should attain the Age of twenty [---] one yeares or Day of Marriage according to the Statute in that Case made & provided.

That Yor Petrs. said Master doth not provide necessary cloths for your Petr. & but one shift whereby your Petr. is become extreemly louzie.

And that your Petr. hath not sufficient victuals nor drink & that your Petrs lodging hath formerly been on shavings & now is on a Flock Bedd wth:out sheets & only a peice of an old Quilt not sufficient to cover your Petr. or defend from the injury of the cold

Yor Petr. therefore most humbly prays that yor Worps: wou'd pleased to appoint some day this Sessions for your Petrs master to attend [...] [---] this Court to shew cause why your petr should not be discharged from her sd Indenture of Apprenticehood

And your Petr as in duty bound shall pray Etc. Mary Hollyman
