To the Right Worsppl her Maties. Justices of the Peace now sitting at Hickshall for the County of Middx

The humble Peticon of Daniell Dyer of the Hamlett of Wappin Stepney in the said County Victualer.

That yor. Petr. on the sixth day of June 1702 Peticoned yor. Worspps Seting forth that he being Reduced to a mean Condicon and not able to pay four shillings P quater. towards the releift of the Poor of the said Hamlett And Yor. Worspps in Consideracon of the said Peticon ordered the said Peticon to be referred to John Webber Esqr. then one of her Maties Justices of the Peace And to Robt. Constable and Roger Smith Esqrs. two of her Maties Justices of the Peace for this County And Whereas the said John Webber and Robert Constable [...] Examine the said Peticon and it appeared to [...] was [...] did [...] [...] [...] [...] is not able to [...] and not only soe but one William Johnson Marriner Whoes wife hath two roomes in yor. Petr. house is rated 3s P quarter.

Yor. Petr. therefore humbly prays yor. Worspps he being reduced to a mean Condicon as aforesaid and very weake and not able to put on his own Cloaths but what he hath one to help him And having a wife and one Child That yor. Worspps would be pleased to Mitigate the said rate as unto yor. Worspps Shall see in meet

And Yor. Petr. Shall ever Pray Etc.
