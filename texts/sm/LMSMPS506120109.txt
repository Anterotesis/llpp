To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justice of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in General Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Overseers of the poor of the Liberty of Norton Folgate in the said County

Sheweth That by a Warrant or Order dated the Fifteenth day of August last past under the hands and Seals of Cha. Digby and D: Wilmot Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex aforesaid Ann a Bastard Child of Elizabeth Simpson Single woman) Aged about two years and a half was removed from the Parish of Christ Church in the said County of Middlesex to the said Liberty of Norton Folgate as the Place of her legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners [...] conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or order of the said two Justice [...] at a humbly Appeal against the same.

Your Petitioners therefore Pray that your Worships will be pleased to order the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Christ Church and all Persons concerned to attend this Court some day this present Sessions to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of this Court touching the said appeal

And Your Petitioners Etc

9th. Septr. 1771

Robt. Linten Edward Sharman} Overseers of the Liberty of Norton Folgate
