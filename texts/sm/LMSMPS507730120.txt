To the Worshipful William Mainwarnig, Esquire) and the last of His Majesty's justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of Release Assembled

The humble Petition of John Bruce Allen

Sheweth That by Indentunt of Apprentice Ship bearing date the Second day of July 1782 your Petitioner became bound to Robert Such of Clerkenwell Green in the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the Said County Cabinet Maker for the term of Seven years from that date in Consideration of the June of Forty Pounds to him the Said to the Said Luck them Paid he Covenanted to fin [...] your Petitioner good and Sufficientment drink, and Lodging during the Said Term

That your Petitioner hath Performed all the Convenants on his Part in the Said Indenture Contained for him to do and Perform But the Said Robert Luck is enable to do the like your Petitioners Master being now under Confinement for debt in the Things Bench From Whence ther is not the least probability of his being [...]

Your Petitioner therefore humbly Prays your Worships to discharge him from his Said appreticeship and to grant him Such relief as in native of his Case required

And as in duty bound

John Bruce Allen
