To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session assembled

The Humble Petition of John Rennells of Chertsey in the County of Surry

Sheweth That your Petitioner was commanded by a Summons under the hand and seal of John Spiller Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the peace acting in and for the said County of Middlesex to attend on the 22d. day of July last as the hour of Six in the afternoon of the same day at the Office of him the said John Spiller at Brentford Butts in the said County of Middlesex commonly called or known by the name of the Public Office New Brentford to shew cause why he the said John Rennells should not forfeit and pay the Sum of Ten pounds for that he the said John Rennells did on the 16th. day of July then Instant use or cause to be used a certain Carriage called a Coach in a certain Road or Highway leading from London to Chertsey aforesaid to wit at Hammersmith in the said County of Middlesex without

having the Christian name of him the said John Rennells painted there on in large legible Letters Contrary to the Act of Parliament in that case made and provider

That your Petitioner did attend at the time and place aforesaid and shewed as your Petitioner humbly conceives Sufficient cause why he should not be convicted in the said Penalty Not with Standing which the said John Spiller convicted your Petitioner in the penalty or Sum of Five pounds Whereby Your Petitioner conceives himself aggreived and Graves le [...] to appeal thereto

Your Petitioner therefore humbly pray your Worships will be pleased to receive this his Petition of appeal against the said Conviction of the said John Spiller and that your will be pleased to appoint a day in this Session for the hearing and determining the said Appeal and that all parties concerned may then attend.

And your Petitioner shall ever pray
