To the Worshipfull the Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace for the said County of Middlesex

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Stoke Lacey in the County of Hertford

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant bearing Date the twenty Second Day of November last under the hands and Seals of William Hallett and John Rudge Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said County of Middlesex one whereof being of the Quorum Made, upon the Complaint of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Harrow on the hill in the said County of Middlesex, that Sarah Farley Singlewoman and her two Bastard Children Vizt Mary about five years of Age and Charlotte above five Months old had come to Inhabit in the said Parish of Harrow on the hill not having [...] legal Settlement there And that the said Sarah Farley and her two Children were then Chargeable to the said Parish of Harrow on the hill, The said two Justices Did adjudge the same to be true And likewise Adjudged that the lawful Settlement of them the said Sarah Farley and her said two Children was in the said Parish of Stoke Lacey in the said County of Hertford and therefore required the said Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Harrow on the hill to convey the said Sarah Farley and her two Children for nurture. only from and out of the said Parish of Harwes on the hill to the said Parish of Stoke Lacy and them to deliver to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor there and the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Stoke Lacey were by the Same Order required to receive and provide for [...] mas Inhabitants of their said Parish

That the said Sarah Farley and Act said two Children being accordingly concerned by the said Parish of Stoke Lacey your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Adjudication and Order Particularly as Faces concerns the Settlements of the said Children of the said Sarah Farley which you Petitioners humbly apprehend and are advised is not in the said Parish of Stoke Lacey

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said Order Only as for as concerns the Settlement of the said two Children of the said Sarah Farley and pray that the part thereof may be Quatled And that this Honourable Court will be pleased to make such Farther And that this Honourable Court will be pleased to make such further Order in the Premises as to them shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc
