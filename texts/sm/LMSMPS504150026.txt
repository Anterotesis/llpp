To the Worshipfull Thos. Lane Esqr and to the rest of the Justices of this Honble Bench

The Humble Petition of Susannah Bedward

Sheweth That your Petitioner was Assaulted in a Voilent manner on the 21st. of Augt. last by one Brown and on the 24th of the same Month your Petitioner applyed to Mr. Justices Withers for a Wart., ag. the sd. Brown and the same day your Petitioner gave it with Six pence to Henry Spurgin Headborough who Executed the same and instead of going to Justice Wither he look your Petitioner by force to the Sun in Castle street and there threatened your Petition if she did not make it up he would send her to Bridwell nor would Suffer your Petitioner to send for any friends but forced your Petitioner to give the sd. Brown a discharge Your Petitioner most Humbly prays that your Worships will grant an Order that the sd. Spurgin may Attend this Court to Answer your Petitioners Complaint and your Petitioner as in Duty bound Shall ever pray

12th Day of Sept.1751

Susannah. Bedward [x] her Mark

Middx At the General Sessions of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holder for the County of Middx in St. John street in the County if by adjourment on [---] Thursday the Twelfth Day of Sept. in the Twenty fifth Year of the Reign if our Sovereign Lord George the Second King of Great Britain Etc

It is Ordered by this Court that the within named Henry Spargin have Notice of this Petition and Order and that all Parties concerned Do Attend this Court to Morrow at Eleven of the Clock in the forenoon to hear and aside the Judgment and determination of this Court touching the Matters contained is the sd. Petition

by the Court Waller
