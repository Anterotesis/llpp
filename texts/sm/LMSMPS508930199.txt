To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex assembled at their General Quarter Session of the Peace began and holden the thirteenth day of January in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of King George the third

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex and Liberty of Westminster

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Nathaniel Conant and Philip Neve Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof is therein mentioned as being of the Quorum) bearing date the 9th. day of January 1794 William Russell and his Daughter named Mary Ann were removed and conveyed from the parish of St. Giles in the fields in the said County to the said Parish of St. Clement Danes as the place of their last legal settlement & were delivered to your Petitioners to be provided for by them according to Law Whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves agreived

Your, Petitioners therefore Appeal against the said Order and humbly pray relief in the premises And that Your Worships will be pleased to Order that the benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto them, that the hearing & determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex And that some day in the some General Session may be appointed for that Purpose

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
