To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace [...] Hicks's Hall in and for the said County Assembled on monday the fourteenth day of January 1750.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Chepping Wycombe in the County of Backs

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of Henry Trent and William Pickering Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City and Liberty of Westminster in the said County of Middx bearing date the fourth day of this instant January John Pleater his Wife Mary and their two Children St. Mary Aged Trent Years and Jane Aged Twelvel pars were removed from the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster to the said Parish of Chepping wycombe whereby your Petitioners apprehend that the said Parish of Chepping wycombe is Aggreived

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly pray that your Worships will be pleased to Appoint a day for the hearing of their said Appeal and that the said Parish may have Such first her and other Relief in the Premises as to your two Ships shall Seem meet And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

William Tyrell Any for the Appeallants
