To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Assembled at the General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said County on Clerkenwell Green on the tenth day of September 1798.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Thomas Brown.

Sheweth That in Consequence of a Summons issued under the hand and Seal of Henry James Pye Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County said Served on Your Petitioner he appeared before the Magistrates at the Public Office in Queen Square Westminster upon an Information exhibited and prosecuted by William Kettle charging that your Petitioner did at the Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster in the said County on the Nineteenth day of October 1797. take and receive of Martha Bunting on redeeming the pledge therein mentioned one penny by way of profit upon two shillings on the thirteenth day of September 1797. lent upon a pledge of a Blue Apron and Handkerchief contrary to the Statue at And your Petitioner was upon the harring of the said Information convicted in the Penalty of Five pounds.

That your Petitioner conceives himself agreived by Such Conviction and is advised to Appeal against the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays pur Worships will be pleased to permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be heard at this present General Quarter Session of the Peace in and for the said County and that your Worships will appoint a day for hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner shall ever pray etc
