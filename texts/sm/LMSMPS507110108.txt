To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Billingsgate in the City of London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seal of John Sherwood and Jasper Clarke Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Fourth Day of March One thousand seven hundred and seventy nine William Hoskin and his Wife Sarah by whom he has one Child now living named Ann about Four Years of Age were removed from and out of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Botolph Billingsgate by the Description of Saint Botolph in the City of London as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said William Hoskin whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Order or Pass Warrant and pray that your Worships will be pleased to appoint some Day in this present Session for the hearing and determining the said Appeal And to Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint John of Wapping Do attend this Court in to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal.

Wm. Chetham Sollr: for the said Appellants }

And your Petitioners shall ever pray etc.
