To the Right Worshipfull the Chairman and the Best of the worshipfull Justices on the Bench at Hicks Hall

The Humble Pettr. of Mary Heacock Now a prisonr: In the Gate House

Most Humbly Sheweth That your worsps. poor pettr: has Six Small and helpless Children Now liveing in a most Miserable and Deplorable Condition, your pettr: haveing [...] Either Sold or pawnd every thing She had in the world for thier Suport, and haveing Nothing left for thier Further Reliefe must in Course Inevitably perish for want, unless prevented by your worships great Clemencey

May it therefore Please your worps: of your abundant goodness to take the Malloncholly Condition of your poor pettr: and her Distressd, Familey Just worps: Serious Consideration and pleas to order your poor pettr. may be brought to a Speedy tryall or such other Reliefe as in your worps: great Wisdom and goodness Shall Seem Most Expedient, and your pettr wth her Small Familey, will as In Duty bound for Ever Pray Etc
