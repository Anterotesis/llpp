Middlesex To the Right Worshipfull His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the Peace at Hicks Hall Assembled

The Humble Pettition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Wilsdon in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hand and Seals of Wiliam Baker and James Clitherew E [...] two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one Whereof being of the Qurom) bearing date the first day of May 1762 John Roberts and Jane his Wife were lately removed and conveyed from the Parish of Hanwell in the said County of Middlesex to the said parish of Wilsdon as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said John Roberts and his said wife

That your Pettitticoners Apprehend themselves Aggieved by the said pass Warrant or Order and the Judgment of the said two Justices and Do therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the same

Butt in Regard that one your Petitioners Material Witnesses and Without whose Testimony your Petitioners Cannot as they are Advised with Safety proceed To Try their said Appeal) is now absent and cannot be produced at this Session of the Peace to give his Testimony on the behalf of your Petitioners and it being in your Petitioners own Delay

E Clarke Selld For Appellants [...] May 1763 Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that this court will be pleased to Appoint some day in the next Quarter session of the Peace to be Held for this County to Hear and Determine the Matter of the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc
