To the Right Worpll: his Maties Justices of the peace for the County of Middx.

The Humble Peticon of Lewis George Lheureux

Sheweth That your Petr because bound by Indre of Apprenticehood bearing Date the 19th. Day of November in the Year 1729 to John Brodu of the Parish of St. Ann in the Liberty of Westminster Watchmaker for the term of Seven Years to learn his Art.

That your Petrs said Master on or about the twenty fourth of May last absented himself from his dwelling house and in a few Days after your Petrs Masters Wife sold of all their household Goods and as your Petr belives and is inform'd she is gone after her husband and hath left your Petr together with another of his said Masters Apprentices to shift for themselves and that Your Petr is credibly inform'd and belives that his said Master and Mistress are gone to Holland to live there.

Your Petr therefore most humbly prays the Premiss being considered that your Worships would be pleased to discharge your Petr from his said Indenture of Apprenticehood.

And your Petr in Duty bound shall pray Etc

Lewis George Lheureux
