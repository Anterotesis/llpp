To ye worshipfull Bench now Setting att Hicks Hall in St. Johns Street

The Humble Pettison of Thomas Fissher being (brother to George Fisher) the prisoner being in a very sick and in a very weak Condition and never known to be Guilty of such Offence before this Crime which is but Small your petitiner Humbly Craves in behalf of the prisoner that the Sentance may be medigated as you in your wise Discretion shall think Proper

And your petitioner as in Duty bound shall Ever pray

Thomas [x] Fisher

and the prosecuter will be Redg to ye Lame as you Shall think proper To Determin

Robert Teem Prosecuter April ye: 10th: :1730
