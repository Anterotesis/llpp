To the Rt. Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Battersea in the County of Surrey

Sheweth That by an Order Warrant under the hands & Seals of Theodore Smith and Thomas Cotton Esqrs. two of his Maties Justices of the Peace for the said County of Middlesex Dated the Twelfh Day of December 1737 Mary Blake Wife of Henry Blake was removed and Conveyed from the Parish of St. Anns within the Liberty of Westminster County of Middx aforesd. to the said Parish of Battersea in the sd. County of Surrey as the place of her last legal Settlement by which said Order or Warrant the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the sd. parish of Battersea think themselves aggrieved and Do appeal against the same

Yor Petitioners therefore humbly Desire your Worships to make an Order of this Court to appoint some certain Day this present Sessions as your Worships shall think proper for tryall of the Merits of the said Appeal

And yor Petitioners will ever pray Etc

Nicholas: Pether John Driver
