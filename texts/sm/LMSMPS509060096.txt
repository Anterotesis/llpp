To the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex assembled at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace for the said County at the Sessions House on Clerkenwell Green on the twelfth day of January 1795.

The Humble Petition and appeal of John Bond of the Strand in the Parish of Saint Mary le Strand in the County of Middlesex Perfumer

Sheweth That your Petitioner are son or about the Twenty Eighth day of November last convicted by William Bleamire Esquire one of the Justices of the Peace for the said County in the Penalty of Five Pounds on and Information charging that Your Petitioner did on the Eighth day of November last expose to sale and sell to one Catharine Stewart a certain Pot containing Pomatum for the Hair for the price of Six Pence subject to the stamp duty of one Penny without the stamped ticket by a certain act of Parliament directed to be affixed to the same being affixed and marked and stamped with the stamp as by the said Act is directed and required contrary to the Statute

That your. Petitioner conceives himself aggrieved by such conviction and is advised to appeal against the same

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to Permit him to enter his Appeal so that the same may be hear at this General Quarter Sessions of the Peace in and for the Said County and that your Worships will appoint a day for hearing and determining the same

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
