To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex [...] Generall Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled at Hicks's Hall in St John Street in and for the said County.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. Sepulchres in the City of London

Sheweth

That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant bearing date the Eighteenth day of February last under the hands and Seals of Thomas Exelbee Esquire and Richard Farmer Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said County of Middlesex whereof one is of the Quorum Charles Lynn was removed from the Hamlett of Bethnall Green in the said County to the said parish of St Sepulchres London Adjudging the said parish of St Sepulchres to be the last place of the legall Settlement of the said Charges Lynn

That your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order Warrant and Judgement of the said two Justices Humbly Appeal to Your Worships therefrom and pray that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Hamlett of Bethnall Green in the said County may be Ordered to Attend and hear and Abide the Determination of your Worships touching this Appeal

And your Petitioners Shall ever pray Etc

John King Thos: Cromwell} Church Wardens

James Townsend James Greatrex} Overseers of the poor
