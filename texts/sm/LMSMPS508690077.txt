Middx(ss)

To the Worshipfull William Mainwaring Esquire and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting an and for the County of Middlesex in General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of William Bowes Clarke of Coul Street in the Parish of St. Martin in the said County Gentleman

Sheweth That by an Order and the Hands [...] and Seals of Philip Dyot and D Walker Esquiers two of the Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said County bearing date the 13th day of July 1791 Whereby they have adjudged you Petitioner to be the Reputed Father of a Female Bastard Child and have Ordered Your Petitioner to pay certain Sums of Many for Weekley Maintinance and Support of such Bastard Child

That Your Petitioner Conceives himself aggreived by the said Order and appeals to Your Worships against the same and

Humbly prays Your Worships that the said Order may be quashed and that a day may be appointed in the next Session for the Hearing and determining the said Appeal

And Your Petitioner Shall ever Pray
