To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex in their General Session of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Lydia Bulkley of the Town of Uxbridge in the County of Middlesex against such part of the Rate made the 14th. day of July 1784 for the Relief of the poor of the said Town as respects the Messuage or Tenement and premises in which your Petitioner now resides and Dwells.

Sheweth That Mr. Higginson a Merchant in London some time since purchased of the Representation of Mr. Aldridge several Houses in the Town of Uxbridge And after repairing the same let one of such Houses to your Petitioner in which She now lives at £30 P Ann

That the Chapel Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Town of Uxbridge have lately made a Rate for the Relief of the poor thereof and have rated and assessed your Petitioner to such Rate at the Rate and in proportion to the Rent of £50 pr. Ann for her said premises.

That as your petitioner pays a real and bonafide Rent of £30 pr Ann only she conceives She ought not to be rated to the Poors Rate for more than what she pays or in proportion thereto according to the usual mode of rating & Assessing the other Inhabitants of the said Town of Uxbridge

Your Petitioner being aggreived by such Rate or Assessment humbly appeals to this Court against the same or such part thereof as relates to the premes occupied by your Petitioner and humbly prays your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day in this present Session for hearing & Determining of her said Appeal & for granting your Petr. such Relief in the premises as to your worships shall seem meet And that the Chapel wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Town of Uxbridge and all parties concerned on Notice hereof be then and there order'd to attend.

Chrisr. Hill Sollr. for Appellt.
