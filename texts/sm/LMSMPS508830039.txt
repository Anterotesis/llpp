To William Mainwaring Esqr. Chairmen and the other Magistrates for the County of Middlesex

The Humble Petition of James Shields

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the 1st.. day of March last Apprehended and taken unto Custody under the late Police Bill and upon Examination before James Pettit Andrews Esquire the Sitting Magistrate at the Rotation Office Queen Square Westminster [---] was Committed to the House of Correction as a reputed Thief and Vagrant That he put the Bail in Order to lodge an Appear as the present Respious against such Conviction

And Your Petition further humbly saith that he conceived himself aggrieved by such Conviction and humbly prays your Worships to appoint a day for the hearing of this his present Appeal

And Your Petition shall ever pray Etc.

James Shields
