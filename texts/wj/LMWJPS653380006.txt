To the Honble: the Bench of Justices now Sitting at their Quarter Sessions of the Peace Etc

The humble Peticon of John Warner of the Parish of St. Martins in the Fields Salesman

Sheweth That Your Petitioner having the Misfortune of a Dishonest Apprentice who hath Emberled from time to time Sundry Parcels of his Goods to a Considerable value, besides Comitting Several other Notorious Disorders and Fregularylys. Yor. Petitioner thereupon took his said Apprentice before Wm. Come Esqr. who was pleased to Comit him to the house of Correcon for the sd Offence and there he now Continues by the Name of Edward Lomax.

Yor. Petitioner therefore humbly Prays That Yor. honors would be pleased to Cause the sd Edward Lomax to be continued a prisoner as he is without which Yor. Petitioner lyes lyable to Great Dangers from his said apprentice not only in respect of his House and Goods, but even his very Life

And Yor. Petr. Shall ever pray Etc

11. Novr. 1714
