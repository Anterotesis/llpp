To the Worshipfull his Majestyes Justices of the Peace for the City & Liberty of Westmr Assembled at the Michas Quarter Session of the peace held for the City and Liberty aforesaid on Wednesday the 19th: day of October 1757

The Humble Petition of Thomas Higginson

Sheweth That Your Petitioner has taken a Lease of Thomas Panton Esqr of a certain Messuage and Tenement Scituate & being in a Street called James Street near the Hay market for the Term of forty two Yeares from Lady day last past, paying the Yearly Rent of £100, Guineas and has laid out on the, said Premes above £100,

That Your Petitioner will take great Care that no disorders shall be Comitted in the said House And that nothing Contrary to Sobriety decency or good Manners be Exhibited represented or transacted therein and will Conform Strickly to the Letter of the Act of Parliament made for the Lycensing of Publick Entertaining Houses,

Your Petitioner therefore Humbly Prays Your Worships to grant him a Lycense to keep the sd House for Concerts & Musical Entertainment untill the next Michas Quarter Session of the peace to be held for this Liberty

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

{Thomas Higginson John Burnell Samuel Austin, St M [...] Reynold: Higgins, St Mortns Jno Watson, Hd Lane Jno Stone, St [...] Jas Ellison St M [...] Jno Priest Jas Pettro Saml. Boler Dall Cannon John Adams Wm Howell John Brown

We whose hands are hereunto Subscribed being Inhabitants near to your Petitioners House in James Street aforesd. Do request & desire Your Worships to Grant the said petitioner Mr: Higginston a Lycense for the Performance of Concerts and Musical Entertaiments according to the prayer of his petition & beleive the Contents of the said petition to be true

B Stephenson Edwd Owen Simon Lemon Rowland Hozier Jos: Wintor James Dalvy
