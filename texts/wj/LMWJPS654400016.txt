To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City & Liberty of Westmr: in Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition of Jeremiah Johnson

Sheweth That by Indenture of Apprentiship bearing Date the 6th. day of October 1743 your Petitioner bound himself Apprentice for Seven Years to William Bean of the Parish of St. Margaret Westmr. to learn the Business of a Joyner.

That Your Petitioner's said Master about 5 Months ago quitted his Habitation & left your Petitioner unprovided for and where his said Master now is your Petitr. cannot say.

That since the first Year of your Petrs. Apprentiship his said Master hath had no settled Business, nor hath he taken any means or care to Instruct your Petr. in his Business Whereby your Petr. thinks himself greatly Injured And

Therefore humbly Prays this Worshipfull Court to take the Premes into Consideration & Discharge him from his said Indenture of Appreship

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc

Jeremiah Johnson
