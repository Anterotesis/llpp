To the Right Honble Sr. William Stephenson Knt. Lord Mayor, and To the Honourable the Justices of Oyer and Terminer and General Gaol Delivery of Newgate holden for the City of London and County of Middlesex in Session Assembled.

The humble Petition of Abraham Stephens Most humbly Sheweth.

That Your Petitioner was in the Month of February Committed to His Majesty's Gaol of Newgate by Benja. Cowley Esqr. one of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex Charged on Oath of Elizabeth the Wife of Henry Newman and Joseph Cox for receiving from the said Elizabeth an Ounce [...] an half of a Piedmont Silk knowing the same to be Stolen of Which Charge your Petitioner Cons vows of his Innocence doubts not but on his Trial to make the same Evidently Appear to the satisfaction of this Honble. Court.

That this present Session is the third Since his Committed and that last Session in Expectation of being Tryed he had at a Considerable Expence prepared for the Same, that Since his Confinement he had undergone great hardship throt Sickness and poverty having Shipt himself of all. and almost tired his friends in their Support of him. and Strou'd he by any Means, be further detained, his friends may desert him, when he must inevitably perish.

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Implores Your Lordship and this Honble Court, that he may at this present Session make his Innocence appear by a Trial for the above supposed fact. Orbe Bailed or discharged.

Discharged of the same pursuant (as Your Petitioner Humbly Hopes) to the Statute in that Case made and provided.

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray

Abra. Stevens
