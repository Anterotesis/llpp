To the Right Honourable William Bridgeon Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of his Majesty's Justices assigned to deliver his Goal of Newgate of the Prisoners therein being for the City of London and County of Middlesex

The humble Petition of Richard Swift a Prisoner in Newgate

Sheweth That your Petitioner was on the ninth day of March last committed by Sir John Fielding Knt. one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Middx on the Oaths of George Enbank and Thomas Lloyd for buying twenty eight Yards of Cotton and since detained on the Oaths of Thomas Jaques and the said Thomas Lloyd for buying four large Bibles the Property of the said Thomas Jaques and also detained on the Oaths of the said Thomas Jaques and Thomas Lloyd for buying a Quantity of Stone Blue and also detained on the Oath of the said Thomas Lloyd for buying six Dozen of Mould Candles the Property of Mr. Bull the Huntingdon Carrier knowing the Goods aforesaid to have been stolen.

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays That he may be brought to his Trial at this present Sessions for the said supposed Crimes whereof he stands charged or in default thereof that he may be bailed or discharged pursuant to the Statute in such Case made and provided

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc

Richard Tricks
