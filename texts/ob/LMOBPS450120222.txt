To the Right Honourable Thomas Harby Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majestys Justices of Gaol delivery of Newgate holden for the County of Middlesex.

The humble Petition of Samuel Wilson a prisoner in Newgate

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was two Sessions ago committed to prison by Sir John Fielding charged with concealing and imbezzleing his Effect to the amount of Â£20 with an intent to defraud his Creditors.

That Your Petr. is totally innocent of the Crime of which he is charged That Your Petr. prosecutors did not prefer any Bill of Indictmt. at either of the two last Sessions against your Petr. but found means at the first Sessions to continue your Petr. in prison till the last Sessions when the Court on the Application of Your Petr. was pleased to Order that Your Petr. shd. be admitted to Bail but your Petr. being very ill and his Credit totally destroyed by the Prosecution against him he has not been able to induce any of his Friends to because Bail for him.

That this is the third Sessions since your Petr. was first committed and no Bill of Indictment having yet been found against him

Your Petr. therefore most humbly prays that he may either be Tryed or discharged out of Custody this present Session pursuant to the Statute in such Case made and provided

And your Petrs. shall ever pray Etc

Saml. Wilson
