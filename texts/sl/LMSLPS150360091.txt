To the Rt. Honoble Sr. George Merttins Knt. Ld. Mayr of the City of London And the Rt Worll the Rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Bridget als Brides London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant Under the Hands and Seals of Two of his Maties. Justices of the Peace wth in this City (Quor Unus) Bearing Date the Twenty Sixth day of November Anno Dui 1724 Elizabeth Norris the Wife of Francis Norris and Elizabeth their Daughter about Twelve months old were passed & conveyed from the Parish of St. Bartholmew the Great London to the Said Parish of St. Bridgett als Bridges as the Place of their last Legall Settlement

That yor Petitoners do conceive themselves aggreived by the sd. Warrt & Judgment of the Said Two Justices and Do humbly Appeal to you Honor & Worpps [---] against the Same Praying Such Releife in the Permises as to yor Worpps Judgmt Shall Seem just

And they Shall Pray Etc
