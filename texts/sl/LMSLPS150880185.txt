London To the Right Honble the Lord Mayor the Worshipful the Aldermen and other his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the City of London and Liberties thereof at the General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal or pass warrant under the Hands and Seals of Frederick Bull and Richard Clarke Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof One being of the Quorum bearing Date the third of this Instant February George aged thirteen months and upwards the illegitimate Son of Ann Smith a Singlewoman who hath deserted has was removed from the Parish of Saint Bennett Grace Church in the City of London to the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate London as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said George

Your Petitioners conceiving them- selves aggrieved by the said Order of Removal or pass warrant of the said two Justices Do humbly appeal against the same Praying such relief in the Premises as to this Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray
