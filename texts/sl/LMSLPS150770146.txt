To The Right Honourable George Nelson Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in the General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said City Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the third Day of February 1766 Mary James Wife of John James gone from her and whose Settlement cannot be found (as is alledged by the said Order) was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Ann and Agnes within Aldersgate London unto the said Parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street as the Place of her Last Legal Settlement together with her Bastard Child Mary aged four Years for Nurture.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humble appeal to this Honble Court against the same and pray Relief in the Premisses.
