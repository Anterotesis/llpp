To the Right Honourable Sir Crisp Gascoyne Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London And the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the Said City in their General Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of Norton Falgate in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of your Lordship and the Worshipfull Francis Cockayne Esqr, one of the Aldermen of this Honourable City hereunto annexed bearing Date the thirteenth Day of September last Mary Baker Widow of John Baker deceased and their Son Thomas Baker aged about nineteen years, as it said; were removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate London into the said Liberty of Norton Falgate as the place of their last legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners humbly Concieve themselves to be Aggrieved by the said Removal And therefore Appeal to this Honourable Court against the Same

Your Petitioners humbly Pray Such relief in the Premisses as to your Lordship and this Honourable Court shall seem meet.

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray and so forth

Akgder Agent for Appellants
