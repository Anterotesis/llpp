To the Right honble William Beckford Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipful the Aldermen his Bretheren his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Michael Bassishaw London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in [---] for the said City (one being of the Quorum) dated the ninteenth day of April Instant Robert Coulthurst and Mary his Wife were removed from the Parish of Saint Lawrence Jewry in the said City to the said Parish of Saint Michael Bassishaw as the Place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices and therefore humbly appeal against the same and Pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them and the hearing and determination thereof adjourned unto the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City.

And your Petrs. shall ever pray Etc.
