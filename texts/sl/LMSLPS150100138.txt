To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Richard Levett Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. the Rest of his Maties. Justices of the Peace in the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens of the Parish of St. Martins Vintrey London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the Hands and Seales of twoe of his Maties. Justices of the Peace of the City (one being of the Quor) dated 14th. November 1699 One Evan Evans was brought to yor. Petrs. by the Churchwardens of Alhallowes Lumbard Streete upon his Oath That the last legall Settlement was in yor. Petrs. parish where hee lived with Sr. John Sweeteapple as a hired Servt. for a yeare and a Quarter whereas in truth the said Sr. John never was a settled Inhabitant in the said Parish of St. Martins Vintrey nor paid any Scott or Lott therein Only had the use of Vintners hall in the said parish for about three Quarters of a yeare in the tyme of his Sheriffalty And dureing all that tyme kept his house and Trade in Lumbord Street in the parish of Alhallowes Lumbord Streete in the Ward of Langborne where he was then and since hath beene chosen and served a Comon Councill man for the said Ward

Yor. Petrs. humble request is That your Honour and Worpps. will be pleased to hear them on their Appeale in the prmisses And to send the said Evan [...] Evans to the Churchwardens of Alhallowes Lumbord Street where his Master Sr. John Sweeteapple was and is a settled Inhabitant According to the Lawe

And They shall Ever Pray Etc
