To the Right Honoble the Lord Mayor and the Rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Libertys thereof in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Botolph Without Bishopgate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Robert Willmott Esqr. Lord Mayor and Daniel Lambert Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberty's thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 2d. Day of April 1743 Ann Longdon Singlewoman was Removed from the Parish of Saint Nicholaslb/>[---] London to the Parish of St. Botolph Without Bishopsgate London aforesaid Adjudging the last Legal Place of Settlement of the said Ann Longdon to be there [---] your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said Two Justices Do humbly Appeal there from and humbly Pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be Saved unto them and that the same may be Adjourned untill the appr Quarter Session of the Peace to be held for the said City and also to grant your Petitioner such [---] ther [...] Rellese in the Premises as [...] your Worships shall Seem meet [---]

And yr. Petrs. shall ever pray Etc

Burr Attorney for the Parish of Bishopgate
