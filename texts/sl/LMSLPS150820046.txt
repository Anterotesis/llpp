So the Right Honourable Brass crosby Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London, and to the Worshipfull the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Master Wardens and society of Inn holders of the City of London on behalf of themselves and the next of the conenmen of Oats within the said City of London and His Liberties thereof

Sheweth

That for some time past the Common market price of midling English Oat at the new Corn Market in Mark Lane has [...] sded His Sum of Sixteen Shillings the Quarters the Contermaned whereof your Petitioners apprehend must prove very determental to the City of London in general and to your Petitioners in particulars

That your Petitioners are ready to make the breeth hereof appear and to prove the same in such manner as by the Statutes in that behalf is required

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that this Honourable Court will take their Case into Consideration and proceed to examine and determine the Common Market Price of midley English Oats as the same as now and hath been lately bought and sold in the Markets of this City and to Certify the same to his Majesty's chief Offices and Collector of the cusions in such manner as by the Statutes in this behalf is required and directed

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
