To the Right Honoble: Sr. William Ashurst Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Right Worll. the rest of their Maties. Justices of the Peace of the same City

1694 The humble Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of ye Poore of ye Pish of St. Butt wth. out Algate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of A Warrant or Order under the hands & Seales of twoe of their Maties. Justices of the Peace within this City one being of the Quor a newborn female Children the third of May last was sent to yor. Petr. to be Pvided for upon the Oath of one Francis Finney that the said Child was found said in A hand baskett in yor. Petr. said Parish of St. Butt wth. out Aldgate in devonshire Square which divideth the bounds of their said Parish from the Parish of St. Butt Bishoppsgate whereas in truth the posts to which the said gate hangeth And the ground sells on which the said hand baskett was sett laid & found is 12 inches broad and lyeth wholly in the Parish of St. Butt wth. out Bishoppsgate And the said Posts are marked on the inside of them wth. out the said Gate wth. the Markes of the Parish of St. Buttolphs Bishopsgate

Yor. Petrs. therefore humbly Pray yor. Honors. & Worpps. to hear them in the Prmisses and to order the Said Female Child to be sent to the Churchwardens of the Parish of St. Butt Bishoppsgt. in wth. Parish the said Child was laid and found to be by them Pvided for According to the Law.

And they shall Pray Etc.
