To the Right Honble Sr George Thorold Knt Bart Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Right Worpl the rest of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the City of London at their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

1720 The humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the several parishes of St Giles without Criplegate St Botolph without Aldgate St Andrew Wardrobe St Catherine Coleman St Andrew Holborn St Sepulchres St Brides Allhallows on the Wall St Botolph Aldersgate and St Botolph without Bishopsgate.

Sheweth That yours Petitioners are Very much Over burthened with poor and not able to raise Sufficient Sums of money amongst themselves to relieve them notwithstanding their Assessments are greater then heretofore Yet the Charge of the poor is for above their Assessments as the Same upon their Examinacon hath divers times appeared to Several Worthy Aldermen of this City appointed to hear the Same And their poor daily increasing upon their send parishes some of them lying [---] near the Suburbs and others of them in the Suburbs of the City

Their humble therefore unto Your Honr & Worp is that you will be favourable pleased to grunt unto them the Contributions of other parishes within this City for and towards the poor as they have formerly had which are particularly hereunder menconed or such further Allowance as to your Honr & Worps shall seem meet
