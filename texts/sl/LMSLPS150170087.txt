To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Thomas Rawlinson Knt. Lord Major of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. the rest of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace In the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of Alhallowes Lombard Streete London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace within the City of London one being of the Quox Dated the 25th. day of September Anno Dni 1706 One Richard Struguall with his wife and Five Children were brought to yor. Petrs. by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Buttolph Bishoppsgate London To be provided for upon the Oath of the Said Richard Struguall That his last legall Settlement was in yor. Petrs. Parish where hee Lived about 14. Yeares Since (being they unmarried as a hired Servt. with Mr. John Cullum Woollen Draper att 20s. P Annm Mayes Whereas in truth the Said Richard Struguall never lived a whole Yeare with the Said Mr. Cullum his Master as the Law in that Case provides

Yor. Petrs. humbly Pray yor. Honor. & Worpps. to hear them in the prmisses And Quash the Said Warrant made by the Said twoe Justices And Sent the Said Richard Struguall with his wife and Five Children back to the Parish of St. Buttolph Bishoppsgate To be by them provided for and mainteyned untill They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Law

And yor. Petrs. Shall Ever Pray Etc
