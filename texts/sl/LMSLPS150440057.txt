To the Right Honble John Barber Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition of Thomas Nicholson Apprentice to Ryley Lanyon Dancing Master.

Sheweth That Your Petitioner by Indenture bearing Date the third Day of September One thousand seven hundred and twenty eight was according to the Custome of the City of London bound apprentice to his Father James Nicholson Citizen and Sadler Your Petitioner being unacquainted with the Laws of this Realm and the Custome of this City Afterwards to wit the Sixth Day of May One thousand seven hundred and thirty two did bind himself Apprentice to the said Ryley Lanyon for the Term of Seven years which last binding Your Petitioner was enticed and over persusaded to comply with by the said Ryley Lanyon who would frequently meet your Petitioner when from home and use many Arguments with your Petitioner to become his Apprentice since which the said Ryley Lanyon hath immoderately beat and corrected Your Petitioner with an unlawfull Weapon and otherwise misused your Petitioner

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays your Lordship and Worships will discharge him from his said Apprenticeship with the said Ryley Lanyon.

And Your Petitioner shall Ever pray and so forth
