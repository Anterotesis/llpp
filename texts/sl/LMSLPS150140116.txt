To the Right Honoble. Sr. Samuell Dashwood Knt. Lord Major of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. Her Maties. Justices of the Peace In the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poore of the Parish of St. Gregory near St. Paulls London

Sheweth That Deborah Deane wife of John Deane with her Three Children were by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of Twoe of Her Maties an Justices of the Peace (one being of the Quor) dated 7th. September 1703) sent to yor Petrs to be provided for on the Oath of the Said Debora Deane That her Husbands last legall Settlement was in yor Peters Parish where hee was bound an Appntice in by Indenture to one William Gardiner a Cane charge maker with who me he Served a Considerable part of the Terme of his Appntice Shipp Where as in truth the Said John Deane since his Service with William Gardiner in yor. Peters Parish was turned over at the Guild hall of this City by the consent of the Said John Deane & William Gardiner his Master to one Benjamin Tarlton an Inhabitant in Bridewell precinct wth. whome he Served Severall moneths And was the place of his last legall Settlement

Wherefore yor. Peter humbly pray yor. Honor. & Worpps in To hear them in the permisses And send the Said deborah Deane with her Three Children back to the Said Parish of St. Anne Blackfryers from whence Shee came To be by them Pvided for and maintained untill They can Free them Selves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Lawe

And yor. Peter Shall ever Pray Etc
