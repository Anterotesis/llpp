To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Dionis Backchurch London.

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of the Right Honorable John Wilkes Esquire Lord Mayor and Frederick Bull Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof One being of the Quorum bearing date the Twenty ninth day of March 1775. Sarah Harrow a Singlewoman was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the East in the City of London unto the said Parish of Saint Dionis Backchurch as the Place of her last legal Settlement

Your Petitioner conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order, Appeal against the same to this Honorable Court, and pray such Relief in the Premisses as to This Honorable Court shall seem meet.

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.

Robt. Woodmass Josh Barber Church Wardens

James Green Henry Rutt} Overseers
