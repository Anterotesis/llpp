To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the City of London and Liberties thereof in their General Session of the Peace at Guildhall Assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Orders under the Hands and seals of the Worshipfull James Townsend Esquire Lord Mayor of the said City of Aldermen of the said City of London and two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace Acting in and for the said City of London and Liberties thereof (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twentieth Day of October One thousand Seven hundred and Seventy three Edward Wilkinson and Mary his Wife were removed from the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West London to the said Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden as the places of their last legal Settlement Whereby your petitioners think themselves aggrieved and do Appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore pray this Court will be pleased that the benefit of the said Appeal be saved unto your petitioners and that the hearing and Determination thereof be adjourned unto the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden in and for the said City of London at Guildhall aforesaid and also that all persons concerned may be their directed to attend:

And your Petitioners shall pray Etc.

Jno Lane Sollicitor for the Appellants
