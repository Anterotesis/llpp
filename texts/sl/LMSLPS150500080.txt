To the Right Honourable Micajah Perry Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Worshipfull the Justices of the peace of the said City in their Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Egham in the County of Surry.

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City of London (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Fifth day of February 1738 Whereby Grace Burn Wife of Nicholas Burn and her Four Children James Aged Eight years Mary aged five years Elizabeth aged Two Years and Charles aged Two Months were removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate London into our said Parish of Egham in the County of Surry as the Place of their last Legal Settlement

Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant Do humbly Appeal against the same and pray such Relief in the Promises as to this Honourable Court shall Seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
