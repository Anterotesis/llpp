To the Rt. Honourable Sr. Richard Lovett Knt Lord Major of the City of London. And to the Rt Worll the Rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace in the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Peticon of Henry Nath

Sheweth

That Thomas Nath Gor. Petrs. Some by Indenture dated the first of December 1696 was bound Appntice to John Parter or St. Anne Backryers Watchmaker To serve with him for the Terme of Seaven yeares from the date of the said Indenture And received the Sume or Five pounds from Yor. Petr. thereupon And the said John Parter being not Housekeeper but a lodger And not a workeman able to teach the said Thomas the art and Trade of a Watchmaker and being often out of Towne and leaving the said Thomas Nath without any thing for the Subsistence And no worke to doe in his said Trade

His humble cuite unto yor. good Honor and Worpps. wth That you will be pleased to cause the said John Parter to appear before you to shew cause why the said Thomas Nath should not be discharged from the said Indenture of Apprenticeshipp. That soe had may be putt forth to some able workeman To learnt this said Trade for his future livelyhood

And yor. Petr. shall Pray Etc
