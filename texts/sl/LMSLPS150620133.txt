To the Rt. Honoble Francis Cockayne Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the same City in their General Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Anne Castle.

Sheweth That your Petr. at the times of making of the sevl. Rates hereinafter mentd. was and Still is Matron of Christs Hospital which is and for near 200 years last past hath been a House of charity and is and Standeth within the Parish of Christ Church London

That your Petr. for doing her Duty and Service afsd. doth reside & Inhabit in a Certain House or Building part of or belonging to the Govrs. of the said Hospital and Situate in the Parish afsd. And your Petr. so resideing and Inhabiting was by a Rate or Assessment made on the 15th. Day of November 1750 for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish for 13 Weeks Comencing from the 15th. Day of July then last past to the 14th. Day of October exclusive and by another like Rate or Assessment made on the 7th. Day of February 1750 for the like Relief for 13 Weeks Comencing from the 14th. Day of October then last past Rated and Assessed with other Inhabitants of the same Parish towards the said Relief for and in respect of your Petrs. said Residence & Habitation whereby your Petioner Conceiveth herself Aggrived And doth humbly Appeal agt. the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays your Lordship and this Honoble Court to take the Premes into Consideration and to Grant her such Relief therein us to your Lordship and this Honoble Court shall seem meet.

And your Petr. shall ever Pray Etc.
