To the Right Honble Sr George Merttins Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of his Maties Justices of the peace at their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Mildred Poultry London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a pass or Warrt under the hands & Seals of two of his Maties Justices of the peace (Quor unus) bearing Date the 7th day of June Instant Richard Clarke was removed & passed [...] by the Churchwardens & Overseers of ye poor of the Parish of st Margaret Lothbury London to ye parish of St Mildred Poultry London alledging the said parish to be the place of his last Legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Warrt & Judgment of the sd two Justices and do most humbly Appeal to your Honour & Worps therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that your Honour & Worps will be pleased to hear them in the premisses and Order that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the sd parish of St. Margaret Lothbury London may appear before your Honour & Worships & hear & abide the determinacon of the Court touching the Sd Appeal

And they Shall ever pray Etc.
