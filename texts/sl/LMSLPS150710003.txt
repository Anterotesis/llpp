To the Right Honourable Sir Thomas Chitty Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of Oyer & Terminer and Gaol Delivery of Newgate in Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition of James Aldridge

Sheweth That Your Unfortunate Petitioner was committed to the Prison of Woodstreet Compter London upon the eighteenth day of December last, by virtue of the Warrant of the Right Worshipful Robert Alsop Esqr one of the Aldermen of the City of London upon Inspicion of not fully discovering his Estate and Effects under a Comission of Bankrupty agst him and of concealing the same to the Value of twenty Pounds with Intent to defrand his Credrs. As appears by a Copy of the sd Warrant of Comittment annext: And now remains [...] Prisoner in Wood Street Compter for the same

That Your Petitioner is entirely Innocent of the Offence in the said Comittment Specified or any other Felony or Crime Whator.

Wherefore Your Petitioner Prays that he may be tried bailed or discharged at this Present Sessions as Your Lordsp Shall think fit in Pursuance of the Act of Parliament in that behalf

And Your Petitioner Etc
