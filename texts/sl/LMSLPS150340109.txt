To the Right Honoble. Sir Gerard Conyers Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London And to the Right Worpfull the Rest of His Maties Justices of the Peace for the said City & Libties there of in their Generall Quarter Sessions assembled.

The Humble Peticon and Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Michael Bassieshaw London.

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or Warrt under the hands & [...] seals of two of His Maties Justices of the Peace for the said City & Libties thereof (Quon unus) bearing date the twentieth day of June Anno Dm 1723. Elizabeth Davis wife of Edward Davis with Elizabeth their Child aged about three years were removed from the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London to the Parish of St. Michael Bassieshaw London aforesaid alledging according to the Judgmt. of the said Justices the said Parish of St. Michael Bassieshaw to be the Place of their last legal settlement.

That Your Peticoners conceiving themselves aggreived by the said Order or Warrt of the said two Justices Doe humbly appeal to Your Lorp & Worps therefrom And humbly pray they may be heard concerning the Pemisses And that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London may appear before You to hear & abide by the Judgment & Determinacon of Your Lorp & Worps touching this Appeal.

And Your Petitoners shall ever pray Etc.
