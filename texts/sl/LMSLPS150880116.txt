Middlesex To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor The Judges & other the Justices Assigned to Deliver the Goal of Newgate of them Prisoners therein being at their present General Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Prayer of Richard Wiltshire a Prisoner in the said Goal

Sheweth That by a Warrant of Commitment under the Hand and Seal of Sir John Fielding Knight One of the Justice of the Peace for the County of Middlesex bearing Date the 11th. Day of August last Your Petitioner Hands Comitted to the Custody of the Keeper of the said Goal on his Deputy and Charged upon the Oath of Robert Ryland & other for Feloniously Willingly Acting and Assisting in the false Making Forging & Corn forfeiting a Certain Cripping Note for payment at Money purporting to be the Promisory Note of the Governor and Company of the Bank of England for the Payment of twenty Pounds to Thomas Harris Esqr or Bearer on Demand Dated the 5th. Day of May 1773. with Intention to Deft and the Corporation of the Governor and Company of the Bank of England agt. the Statute Etc

That your Petitioner Conceive of his James is Desirous of being Tryed as soon as Possible

Therefore Most Humbly Prays of your Lordships that at this Present Session he may be Tryed Bailed or Discharged

And as in Duty Bound he will for ever Pray

Richd Wiltshire
