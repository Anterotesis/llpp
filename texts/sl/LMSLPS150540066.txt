To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London at their Generall Quarter Sessions assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Stephen Wallbrook in the City of London.

Sheweth That by Vertue of a Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City (whreof one is of the Quorum there is mentioned bearing Date the First Day of December in the year of our Lord One Thousand Seven hundred and Forty Two Sarah Candy Widow and her Son George Aged about Five Years were removed from the Parish of Saint Michael Royall in the said City to the said Parish of Saint Stephen Walbrook as the last legall Place of their Settlement By which your Petitioners humbly conceive themselves aggreived and appeal to this Court against the sd. Pass Order

Your Petitioners therefore pray your Worshipps will be pleased to hear and determine the Matters touching this Appeal and [...] them such Releif in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem just and meet

And yor. Petitrs. shall ever pray Etc.

Wm. Ivatt Agent for the Appellants }
