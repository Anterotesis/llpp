To the Right Honourable Crisp Gascoyne Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Quarter Session assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the hands & Seals of Your Lordship [...] of the City of London and the Worshipful Thos. Rawlinson Esqr Alderman two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty ninth day of June now last past Thomas Budley & Ann his Wife & their two Children Ann aged five years & Christopher aged two Years were removed from the Parish of St. Andrew Holborn London to the Parish of St. John of Wapping aforesaid adjudging the last legal place of Settlement of the sd. Thomas Budley with his said Wife & Children to be in the sd. Parish of St. John of Wapping.

That Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the sd. Order & Judgment of the said two Justices & therefore Do humbly appeal therefrom

Your Petrs: therefore most humbly pray Your Lord & this Hen Courts appoint some Day at this Prson General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said City of London to hear this Appeal And that yor Lp & this Hon Court will be pleased to make an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Andrew Holborn and

all Partys concerned in and about the premisses may then and there hear & abide the final Determination of your

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Thos Thorold Richd Burford} Churchwardens

Blunt Stepn: Taylor} Overseers of the Poor
