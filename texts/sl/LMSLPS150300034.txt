To the Right Honble Sr John Ward Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Right Worpl the rest of his Majesties Justices of the peace at their Genl Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled.

The humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Several Parishes of St Giles witht Cripplegate St Botolph without Aldgate St Andrew Wardrobe St Catherine Coleman St Andrew Holborn St Sepulchres St Brides Allhallows on ye wall St Botolph Aldersgate and St Botolph without Bishopsgate

Sheweth That Your Petitioners are very much Overburthened with poor and not able to raise Sufficient Sums of money amongst themselves to releive them notwithstanding their Assessments are greater then heretofore Yet the Charge of the poor is far above their Assessments as the Same upon their Examinacon hath divers times appeared to Several Worthy Aldermen of this City Appointed to hear the Same And their poor daily increasing upon their Said Parishes Some of them lying near the Suburbs and others of them in the Suburbs of this City.

Their humble Petition therefore unto Your Honour and Worps is that You will be favourably pleased to grant unto them the Contributions of other parishes within this City for and towards the poor as they have formerly had which are particularly hereunder menconed or such farther Allowance as to Your Honour and Worps shall seem meet

And they Shall ever pray Etc



St Giles Cripplegate 90 St Botolph witht Aldgate 50 St Andrew Wardrobe 40 St Catherine Coleman 12 St Andrew Holborn 7 St Sepulchres 44 St Brides 15 Allhallows on ye Wall 12 St Botolph Aldersgate 15 St Botolph Bishopsgate 60
