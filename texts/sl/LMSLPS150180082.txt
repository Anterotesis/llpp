To the Rt. Honoble: Sr. Robert Bedingfeld Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt. Worll. The rest of Her Maties. Justices of ye. Peace In the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Botolph without Aldersgate London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of Her Maties. St Geo of the Peace within the City of London (Quor unus) dated the Fourth day of February 1706. One Hannah Robbins wife of Jasper Robbins now in Ireland with Henrietta Robins their Daughter aged between twoe & three yeares were sent by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Peter in Cornhill London to yor. Petr. to be Pvided for [...] that [...] appeared to the said Justices That the last legall Settlement of the said Jasper Robins the husband of the said Hannah [...] Robins was in yor. Petrs. Parish where he was Apprentice with William Litleboy Taylor And Served him severall Yeares Whereas in truth the said Jasper Robins never lived with the said William Litleboy as an Appntice in yor Petrs. Parish

Yor. Petrs. therefore humbly Pray yor. Honor & Worpps. to hear them in the premisses & Order the Churchwardens & Overseers of St. Peters in Cornhill London to appear here before you To Shew Cause why they should Provide for the said Hanna Robins & her Daughter And to Quash the said Warrant made by the said twoe Justices And to send the said Hanna Robins & Henrietta Robins back to the Churchwardens of St. Peters in Cornhill London To be by them kept and Provided for untill They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof By due Course of Lawe

And They shall Ever Pray Etc

Appeal Butt wth. out Aldersgate
