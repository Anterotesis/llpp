To the Right Honble, Sir Watkin Lewes Knight, Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipful the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City, at their General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of saint Bennet at paul's Wharf, London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City of London bearing Date the twenty second Day of August last past, one of which said Justices being of the Quorum, Sarah the Wife of John Terris and their Three Children, namely, John Kent aged eight Years and upwards, Thomas aged eight Years and upwards and Mary aged Four Years and upwards, were removed from the Parish of Saint Sepulchre, in the said City, to the said Parish of saint Bennet at Pauls Wharf, as the place of their last legal Settlement wehreby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved, and therefore

Pray that this Honourable court will please to take their Case into Consideration, and to hear and determine their said Appeal, and to make such Order therein as shall be just

And your Petitioners shall ever pray etc
