To the Rt. Honrble Sr Robert Beachcroft Knt Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt Worpll the Rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace within the same City in the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

1711 7 Dec The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Martin Vintry London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of her Maties Justices of the Peace within this City (one being of the Quorm) bearing date the Seventeenth Day of December last past one Elizabeth Smith was removed from the Parish of St. Peter the Poor in Bread street. London unto the said Parish of St. Martin Vintry alleadging the said Parish to be the last legal Settlement of John Smith husband of the said Elizabeth who is now in the Maties Service

That your Petitoners doubt not but to prove that the said Elizabeth Smith nor her husband John Smith never had any legal Settlement in the said Parish of St. Martin Vintry whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeal to your Gouland Worpps there from

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray your Honr and Worpp [...] will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and not the prmisses and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Peter the Poor London may appear before you and hear and abide the determination of your honr and Worpps touching this appeale

And your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc

St Martin Vintry agt. St Peter the Poor} Appeale Etc
