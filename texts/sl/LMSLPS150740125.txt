To the Right Honble. William Beckford Esqr. Lord Mayor, the Recorder and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of ye peace for the City of London and Libertys thereof at their General Quarter Session of ye peace Assembled on Monday ye 11th. day of April in the year 1763.

The humble Petition and Appeal of ye Churchwardens & Overseers of ye poor of the parish of St. Mary White Chapel in ye County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a pass Warrant or Order of Removal under ye hands & Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of ye peace for the said City and Libertys thereof (whereof one is of ye Quorum) bearing date the Sixteenth Day of March last past one Mary Davis therein mentioned & described to be a Child about four years of Age & a Bastard and sayd to be lately come to reside in the parish of St. Botolph without Aldersgate London not having obtained any Legal Settlement there and likely to become Chargeable thereto Unless timely prevented, the the said Mary was Removed from the said Parish of St. Botolph to the said parish of St. Mary White Chapel as the place of her last legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners Apprehend themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or pass warrant and therefore do Appeal against the same

Your Petrs. & Appellts. thereupon do humbly pray that this their Appeal may be Adjourned to the next General Quarter Session of ye peace to be holden for the said City and Libertys thereof And that the Respondts. and all other parties concerned may have Notice then and there to attend to bear and abide the Judgmt. & datemt. of ye Court touching the said Appeal

2. Sepr. April Ye. 11th 1763. Billingtons Solr. for Appellts.

And Yr. Petrs. Appellts. will ever pray Etc.
