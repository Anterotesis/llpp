To the Right Woshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Session assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Edmonton in the County of Middx

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of the Right Honorable Robert Willimot Esqr. Mayor of the City of London and Sir Joseph Hankey Knt. one of the Aldermen of the same City two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof one being of the Quorum bearing date the twelfth day of January last whereby Mary Tomkins wife of Henry Tomkins and their Child Charles aged two Years were removed by the Oath of William Haynes from the Parish of St. Botolph Bishops Gate London to the said Parish of Edmonton in the County of Middlesex as the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Tomkins and her Child.

That Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or Order of the said two Justices of the Peace and humbly appeal against the same

Your Petrs. therefore humbly Pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them and [---] to grant Your Petrs. such other Relief in the Premises as to your Worships shall seem [...]

And Your Petrs. shall pray Etc.

Prelleur Sollicitor for the Appellnt.
