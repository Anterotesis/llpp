To the Right Honourable The Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London at the General Sessions of the peace assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint Peter in Cornhill in the City of London.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of a certain Pass Warrant under the Hand and Seal of John Wilkes Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City and one of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and the Liberties thereof bearing Date the twenty eighth Day of April 1774 (thereby alledging that Esther Baker was apprehended in the Parish of Saint Mary Woolchurch Haw in the said City as a Roque and Vagabond wandering abroad and begging and that upon Examination of the said Esther Baker taken upon Oath by the said Justice it did appear that the last legal Settlement of the said Esther Baker was in the said Parish of Saint Peter in Cornhill) She the said Esther Baker was conveyed by Richard Nash one of the Constables in the said City to the said Parish of Saint Peter in Cornhill and was delivered to Your Petitioners to be provided for according to Law.

That Your Petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved by the said pass Warrant and therefore humbly appeal to this Honourable Court against the same and pray that the benefit of this their Appeal may be saved undo them and that the Hearing and Determining thereof may be adjourned unto the next General Quarter Session of the peace to be holden for the said City.

Wm. Chetham Solicitor for the Appellants}
