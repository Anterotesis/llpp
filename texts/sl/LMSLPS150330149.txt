To the Rt. Honrble. Sr William Stewart Knt Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt Worpll: the rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace for the same City in their general quarter Sessions assembled

The humble Petition and appeal of the parish of Tottenham in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of her of his Maties Justices of the peace for the City of London bearing date the ninth day of May last past Frances Bridgforth wife of William Bridgforth with her two Children were removed from the parish of St. Botolph without Aldgate London unto the said Parish of Tottenham as the place of their last legal settlement That your petr conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices do humbly appeal to your Honr and Worpps thereupon [...]

And Pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St Botolph without Aldgate may appear before you to hear and abide the judgment and determination of your Honr and Worpps touching this Appeal

And your Petrs shall ever pray Etc.
