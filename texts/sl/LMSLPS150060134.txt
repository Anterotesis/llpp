To the Right Honoble. Sr. Thomas Lane Knt Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Right Worll. the rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace att the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

Feb 1694/5 The humble Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens' & Overseers of the Poore of Wapping Stepney in the Parish of Stepney in the County of Middx.

Sheweth That Susan Gladwin Singlewoman was by Warrant under the hands & Seales of twoe of their Maties. Justices of the Peace for the said City removed and Conveyed from the Parish of St. Buttolphs Bishopsgate London aforesaid to the Hamlett of Wapping aforesaid Suggesting that shee was last settled there Whereas in trieth the said Susan hath been a hired Servant in Bishopsgate Parish since the Death of Anne Horne

Yor. Petitionrs. conceiveing themselves aggreived doe humbly Appeale from the said Warrant And Pray an Order of this Honoble. Courte that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the said Parish of St. Buttolphs Bishopsgate may attend this Honoble. Courte to have the said Appeale argued this present Sessions.

And Yor. Petr. shall Ever Pray Etc.

4.4.94

Shee lived a Servt. wth. Mr Bowerr in Bishoppsgate steite halfe a yeare att rate of 40s. a yeare time the death of Mr. Horne wch. was last legall settlemt. Warrant Quashed Bishoppsgate keepe Susan Gladwin untill
