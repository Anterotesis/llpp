To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Aldermen of the same City in their General Quarter Session assembled

The humble Petition and appeal of the Commissioners of his Majesty's Revenue of Excise

Sheweth That by a certain Reassessment or Rate made for the present Year on the several Inhabitants and Occupiers of House Lands and Tenements within the Parish of Saint Helen London for and towards the necessary Releif of the Poor of the said Parish at the Rate of three pence in the Pound in pursuance of an Order of Vestry made at a Vestry held ofr the said Parish on the twenty- -third day of February 1775 and of the several Acts of Parliament mentioned relating to the Poor the Excise Office is charged to the said Rate or Assessment the Sum of twenty five pounds

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Rate or Reassessment and therefore appeal against so much thereof as relates to that Part of the Excise Office that is sctuate within the said Parish of St. Helen and pray for such Relief in the Premisses as the Court shall think just

S. Shutz Sollr. for the Appellants
