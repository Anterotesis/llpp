London To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the peace in their Quarter Sessions of the peace at Guildhall in and for the City of London assembled

The humble petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Mary le bone in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the right Honorable John Burnell Esquire Lord Mayor of the said City and Samuel Swain Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the fifth day of January One thousand Seven hundred and Eighty eight Margaret Page mentioned in the said Order to be the Wife of James Page who had deserted her and her Son James aged five years were removed and Conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Andrew in Holborn London unto the said parish of Saint Mary le bone as the Place of the last Legal Settlement of the said Margaret and her said son

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order or Warrant of the said two Justices Do therefore Appeal to your Worships therefrom And humbly pray that your Worships will hear them in the premises touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray

Thos. Martin Solr. for the Appellants No. 3 Newman Street
