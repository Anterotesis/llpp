To the Right Worshipfull his Maty's Justices of the peace for the Citty of London in their Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The hble Pettition and appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the Pish of St. Matthew Friday Street London

Sheweth. That by vertue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands & seals of the Right honble Humphrey Parsons Esqr. Lord Mayor of the Citty of London and St. Richd. Brocas Knt. Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the Citty of London (Quor un) bearind date the Fourteenth day of July last past Stephen Bellingham, Mary his Wife and their Four Children (vizt.) Elizabeth aged about Tenn Years, Mary Eight Years, Martha Six Years and Stephen about Three Years old were removed from the Pish of St. Martin Ludgate in the Citty of London unto the said Pish of Saint Matthew Friday Street London Alledgeing the sd. Pish of St. Matthew Firday Street to be the place of their Lost Legall Settlement.

That yor Petttioners conceiving themselves Agreived by the sd. Warrt. or Order of the said Two Justices doe therefore Appeal to your Worshipps thereof [...] and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the Premes and order that the Church Wardens & over seers of the poor of the said Pish of St. Martin Ludgate London may appear before you at a certain day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of your Worshipps touching this Appeal.

And your pettitioners shall pray Etc.

Thos. Marley
