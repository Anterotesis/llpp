To the Right Honourable Sir Richard Glyn Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London and one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City And To the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City in General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by a certain Pass under the hand and Seal of your Lordship bearing date the Nineteenth day of March One thousand Seven hundred and fifty nine James Harneman was removed from the Parish of Saint Peter le Poor London as a Rogue and Vagabond wandring abroad and Begging unto the said Parish of Saint Luke as the place of his last legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners conceive themselves agrieved by the said Pass and therefore humbly appeal against the Same.

James Barwick Sollicitor for the Appellants,

And your Petitioners Shall ever pray Etc.
