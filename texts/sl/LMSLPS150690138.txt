To the Rt Honble Sir Charles Asgill Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London, and to the Rt. Worshipful the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City & Liberties thereof, in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Diones Backchurch London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands & Seals of Robert Alsop Esqr. and Sir Samuel Fludyer Knt. Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof (One being of the Quorum) bearing Date the fifth Day of October 1758. Thomas Watson was Removed from the Parish of St. Michael Royal London unto the Parish of St. Dionis Backchurch aforesaid Alledging the said Parish of St. Dionis Back- Church to be the Place of his last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of Removal, Do appeal to your Lordship & Workships therefrom, & humbly Pray that the Benefit of this Appeal be saved unto them, and the hearing and determining & thereof be adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be Holden for this City, and that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Michael Royal aforesaid, may then appear before you, to hear to abide the Judgment and Determination of your Lordship & Worships touching this Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall Pray Etc.
