To the Rt. Honble. Sr. Thomas Rawlinson Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. rest of her Maties. Justices of the Peace In the Generall Quarters Sessions Assembled.

The humble Petition & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Martins Cryars London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant dated 9th November 1705 under the hands & Seales of Two of her Maties. Justices of the Peace within the City of London (one being of the (Quor) On Barthls Gunn was sent to Yor. Petr. by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of Alhallowes the Greate London To be provided for upon the Oath of the said Barthls. Gunn That hee lived in Yor Petrs. Parish And there paid Twoe & twenty Pounds P Annum Kent and paid Scott and Lott in the said Parish And hath had noe legall Settlement in any place Since Whereas in truth the said Bartll. Gunn about 23 Years last past went out of yor. Petr. parish And for a Yeare and an halfe before May 1685 dwelt and Inhabited in the said Parish of Allhallowes the greate And hath lived there Even Since

Yor. Petr. humbly pray Yor. Honors. & Worpps. to hear them in the Prmisses & to Order the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Pish of Allhallowes the Greate to appeare here before you To shew cause why they Should not provide for the said Barthll. Gunn And to Send the said Barthll. Gunn to the said Parish of Allhallowes the Greate London To be by them kept & provided for till They ran Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Law

And they Shall Ever Pray Etc
