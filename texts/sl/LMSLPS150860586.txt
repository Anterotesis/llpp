To the Right Honble the Lord Mayor and other his Majestys Justices of the Peace of and for the City of London at their General Quarter Session of the Peace

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Over- seers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Olave Hart Street in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal bearing date the 22d Day of September 1775 under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one being of the Quorum) Richard Rushton therein described to be a Lunatic was removed from the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the said City of London to the Parish of Saint Olave Hart Street the said Justices there by Adjudging the last legal place of Settlement of the said Richard Rushton to be in the said Parish of Saint Olave Hart Street,

That Your Petitioners Conceive themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order and the Judgment of the said Justices and Humbly Appeal therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore most Humbly pray that your Lordship and your worships would be pleased to hear this their Appeal and that the said Order of Removal and Judgment of the said Justices may be Quashed and that your Petitioners may be further and otherwise relieved in and about the premisses as the nature of the Case may require

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
