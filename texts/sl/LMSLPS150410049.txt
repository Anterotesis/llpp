To the Right Honble Sr Richard Brocas Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Worpl his Maties Justices of the Peace for the same City in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble petition and Appeal of John Gray Citizen and Baker of London

Sheweth That Your Petr. follows the employment of a Baker in the Parish of St Giles without Cripplegate London And that on the thirteenth day of June instant your petr. was sumoned before the Right Honble Sr Richd Brocas Knt. Lord Mayor of this City to Answer the Complaint of Thos Foster one of his Lordships Officers for selling two penny loaves of Wheaten Bread which (as the said Thos Foster did then alledge) wanted of their weight seven ounces and six drams Upon whose Oath his Lordship thought fitt to Convict your Petr. of the said Offence.

Your Petr. humbly conceiving himself agreaved by the said Conviction humbly Appeal to this Court for such relief in the Premisses as to this Court shall seem meet.

And Your Petr. shall ever Pray Etc
