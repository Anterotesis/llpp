To the Right Honble Sir Matthew Blakiston Knight Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Session of the Peace for the same City assembled

The humble Petition of Robert Standidge Wharfinger Tenant of Stantons wharf on be half of himself & the Proprietors thereof

Sheweth That by Order made on the Eleventh Day of July 1757 in Pursuance of an Act of Parliament passed in the thirtieth Year of the Reign of His late Majestys Intituled An Act to explain and amend An act made in the Eighteenth Year of His said Majestys Reign to prevent the Misbehaviour of the Drivers of Carts in the Streets of London and Westminster and the Limits of the Weekly Bills of Mortality and for other Purposes in the said Act mentioned The Justices there present did assess and rate that all Good which should be carried from any of the Keys below Bridge and other Places to any of the Wharf in Tooley Street not exceeding Symonds Wharf should be paid for at the Prices therein mentioned

The Station's Wharf is adjoining to Symonds Wharf and is only abt. 70 foot [...] further then the same and Yet Your Petitioners pay Six pence more for every Load of Goods than theses carried to Symond's Wharf

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly pray this Honble Court to alter and amend the first mentioned Order and extend the same to Stanton's Wharf the same being the last Wharf on that Side the River for the loading of Coasting Vessels.

Robert Standidge
