To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesties Worshipfull Justices of the peace for the City of London at the General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Exchange in the said City

Sheweth That by an Order or pass Warrant bearing date the first day of January instant under the hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Robert Peckham Mayor of the City of London and John Boydell Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the said City (One whereof being of the Quorum) William Stennell with Rachael his Wife and their two Children namely Benjamin aged four years and upwards and Mary aged two years and upwards were removed from the Parish of Saint Bottolph without Aldgate in the said City to the said parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Exchange as the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners apprehending themselves aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly appeal against the same

And therefore pray that you will be pleased to order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint Bottolph without Aldgate aforesaid and all persons concerned to attend this Court some day this present Session to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of this Court touching the premisses And that your Petitioner may have such relief therein as to your Worships shall seem meet
