To the Right Honourable Henry Marshall Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London And the Rest of the Worshipfull the Justices of the peace for the said City in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint John the Baptist near Dowgate in London

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Henry Marshall Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and Francis Cokayne Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City and also two of his Majesties Justices Assigned to keep the peace within the said City (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the first day of Aprill one thousand seven hundred and forty five Whereby one Elizabeth Keys Widow of George Keys was removed from and out of the parish of St Martin Vintry in London to the said parish of St John the Baptist near Dowgate in London as the place of her last legall Settlement

Yor Petitioners Apprehending themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly Appeale Against the Same and pray such Relief in the premisses As to this Honourable Court shall think fitt

And yor Petrs: shall pray Etc
