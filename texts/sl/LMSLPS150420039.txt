To the Right Honoble Humphry Parsons Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worpfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City and Libertys in their Generall Quarter Sessions of the Pence Assembled

The Humble Petition of Richard Rotherham Apprentice to Jonathan Spalling A Fan Maker

Sheweth That by Indenture bearing date the Fifth day of September 1726 Your Petitioner was bound to the said Jonathan Spalding a fan maker, whereby he was to Serve his said Master after the Manner of an Apprentice for the Term of Seven Years from the date of the said Indenture Pursueant to the covenants therein Contained, which your Petitioner to the Utmost of his Power hath end endeavoured to discharge with fidelity to his said Master Not withstand which the said Jonathan Spalding hath not allowed unto your said Petitioner Sufficient Necessarys dureing the Sickness Your Petitioner hath been afflicted with since the Comencement of his said Apprentiship or doth he Allow any Apparell Pursueant to the Covenants in the said Indenture, but on the Contrary hath often Immederately beat and Corrected him without any Just Cause

Wherafore Your Petitioner humbly prays your Lordship and Worships will be se good as to Discharge him from his said Master or made such order therein as to your Lordship and Worships shall Seen most meet

And your Petitioner as in duty bound shall ever Pray Etc

Richard Rotheram
