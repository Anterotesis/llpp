To the Worll. his Majesties Justices of the Peace of & for the City of London and Libertys thereof in Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Peticon and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Margaret Westminster in the County of Middx.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant under the hands and seals of Wm. Thompson Esquire Recorder of the said City of London and Sir William Withers Knt. Justices of the Peace of and for the said City and Liberties thereof bearing Date the 28th. Day of March 1715. Philip Jones together with Sarah his Wife and their Three Children Vizt. Elizabeth about Eleven Ann about Seven and phillip Aged about Two Years & a half were removed from and out of the [...] Parish of St. Giles's Cripplegate London aforesd. to the said Parish of St. Margaret Westminster as the place of their Lawfull Settlement.

That Your Petrs. thinking themselves Agreived by the Judgment of the said Two Justices humbly appeal to your Worships against the same.

Therefore humbly pray your Worsps. would be pleas'd to Order that ye Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Sr. Giles's Cripplegate London Attend this Court Some day this Quarter Sessions to hear and abide the final Determinacon of this Court touching this Appeal.

And your Petr. shall ever pray Etc.

April 1715
