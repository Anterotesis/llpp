To the Right Honourable Sir Edward Bellamy Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties therefm their General Quarter Sessions Assembeld

The Humble Petition and appear of John Giles, John Sampkins, Peter Boursett Robert Nicholls, Thomas Pell and Joseph Burston.

Sheweth That your Petitioners being Inhabitants and Householders in the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate London were in an Assessment made by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish the twenty Ninth Day of May 1734. Rated or Assessed for and towards the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish in the Severall Sums of Money therein mentioned which Rater's not only Unequal but also (as your Petitioners are advised) in many respects Illegal and unwarrantable Wherefore your Petitioners Conceive themselves agrieved by the said Sesson Tax as Illegal and unequal said do appeal to your Lordship and Worships therefrom.

Mr James Reads and John Giles Churchwardens of the said Parish Dohereby concent that the said Rate may be Quashed according the Prayer of the said Petition

James Roades John Giles Ch: Wns:

And humbly Pray that the said Rate may be Vacated on that your Petitioners may be otherwise relieved in the Premisses as this Honourable Court shall think fit.

And your Petitioners shall over Pray etc.
