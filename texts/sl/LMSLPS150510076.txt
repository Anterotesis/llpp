To the Right Honble the Lord Mayor and the Rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Botolph without Bishopgate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass-Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said City and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Fifth Day of May 1740 a Female Child Aged about Nine Months Baptized by the Name of Eleanor Kelly was Removed from the parish of St. Olave Hart Street London To the Parish of St. Botolph without Bishopgate London aforesaid Adjudging the Last Legal Settlement of the said Eleanor Kelly to be there And your Petitioners conceiving themselves Agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices Do Humbly appeal therefrom and Humbly pray Such Relief in the premisses as to your Worships Shall seem meet and Convenient

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc
