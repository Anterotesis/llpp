To the Right Honble John Sawbridge Lord Mayor of the City of London And the rest of the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City in their Quarter Sessions of the Peace for the said City assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Edgware in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the Hand and Seal of your Lordship bearing date the Nineteenth day of July last Elizabeth Shillingford and her Three Children to wit Ann Aged five Years Richard Aged Three Years and Samuel Aged One Year were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Lawrence Jewry within the City of London to the aforesaid parish of Edgware aforesaid as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Elizabeth Shillingford.

Your Petitioners therefore conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant humbly appeal against the same and pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal and the hearing and determining thereof may stand untill the next Sessions And for such further Relief in the premises as to this Honble Court shall seem meet.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
