To the Right Honble Sr. John Salter Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London And to the Right Worshipfull the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of William Perkins.

Sheweth That the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Sepulchre London with the Consent of two Justices of the Peace on the 10th. day of July 1736 Did put and Place Your Petr. Apprentice to one Matthias Wilson a Cork Cutter till your Petr. should accomplish his full Age of Twenty four years your Petr. at the time of Such binding being about 11 Years Old.

That your Petrs said Master at the time of Your Petrs. being bound Apprentice was an Inhabitant in the Parish of Christ Church in the County of Surry but afterwards removed into the Parish of St. Andrew Hubbard in the City of London where Your Petr. continued with him for twelve months or thereabouts.

That your Petrs said Master being very much in debt went over to Holland and there settled with his Family and has left your Petr. a Charge upon the Parish of St. Andrew Hubbard where the said Matthias Wilson last lived and where your Petr. Served the last Forty days of his Apprenticeship

Your Petr. being thus left by his said Master without any Person to Instruct him in his Trade and business or to find your Petr. in Cloaths meat Drink Washing and Lodging According to

to the Covenants of the said Indenture of Apprenticeship by which the said Matthias Wilson Covenanted to Instruct your Petr in his Trade and business of a Cork Cutter And also to find him in Meat Drink apparel Washing and Lodging and all other things necessary and fitt for an Apprentice

Your Petr. therefore most humbly Prays this Honourable Court will please to discharge him from his said Apprentice -ship.

And Your Petr. shall Ever Pray Etc.

Wm Perkins
