1694/5 10 January To the Rt. Worspful Sr. Thomas Lane Knt Ld. Mayr. of the City of London & others his Matys. Justices of the peace for the sd. City now sitting at the old Bayly

The humble petion & appeal of the Churchwrdens & Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. Martins in the fields in ye. County of Midx

Sheweth That by warrt undr. ye hands & Seals of Sr. Salathiel Lovell Knt. Recordr. of the sd. City of London & Sr. Tho: Stamp Knt. bearing Date the 10th. of this inst. Janry. 1694. One Sarah Simpson & her son Thomas Simpson aged twenty two months or thereabouts were passed from the parish of St. Giles Criplegate London to the parish of St. Martins in the fields aforesd. upon prtence that Thomas Simpson (husband of the aforesd. Sarah) had his last Legal Setlemt. in the sd. parish of St. Martins in ye fields

That yor. petrs. humbly conceive themselves aggrieved by the sd. pass warrt. for that the sd. Thomas Simpson never had any Legal Setlemt. in the sd. Pish of St. Martins in ye. fields as they doubt not but to make out to yr. worsps

Yr. petrs. therefore humbly pray yr. worsps to ordr. ye Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of [...] St. Giles Criplegate London to attend at a [---] time certein this genrll. quarter Sessions to shew cause why they shoud not receive & provide for the sd. Sarah Simpson & Thomas her son

And yr. petrs. shal ever pray Etc
