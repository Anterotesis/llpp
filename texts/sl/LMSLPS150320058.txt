To the Right Honble Sr John Fryer Bart Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Right Worpl the rest of his Maties Justices of the peace for the City of London at their General Quarter Sessions of the peace assembled

The humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Several Parishes of St Giles without Cripplegate St Botolph without Aldgate St Andrew Wardrobe St Catherine Coleman St Andrew Holborn St Sepulchres St.Bridges Allhallowsen the Wall St Botolph Aldersgate and St Botolph with Bishopsgate London

Sheweth That Your Petitioners are very much Over burthened with Poor & not able to raise Sufficiens Surry of money amongst themselves to releive them notwithstanding their Assessments are greater than heretofore Yet the Charge of the Poor is for above their Assessmts. as the Same upon their Examinacon Saithdwer times appeared to Several Worthy Aldermen of this City Appointed to hear the Same And their poor daily increasing upon their Sd parishes Some of them lying near the Suburbs and other of them in the Subarbs of this City

Ther humble Suit therefore to Your Honour & Worps is that You will be favourably pleased to grant unto them the Contributions of other parishes within this City fer and towards the releif of their poor as they have formerly had which are particularly here under menconed or Such further Allowance as to Your Honour & Worps Shall Seem meet
