To the Rt Honble: William Benn Esqr Lord Mayor of ye. City of London, & to the Worpful ye. rest of His Majesty's Justices of ye Peace of the said City and the Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of ye Poor, of ye. Parish of St Gregory London;

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant, under the Hands and Seals of Your Lordship and Sir John Thompson Knt Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the City of London & of Liberties thereof, (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty Fourth Day of February last past; Elizabeth Benjamin Wife of Benjamin Benjamin (who is Soldier abroad in His Majesty's Service) and their two Children, Thomas aged three Years, and Elizabeth aged Eleven Months; were Removed from the Parish of St Botolph without Aldgate, London, unto the Parish of St Gregory, London, alledging the said Parish of St Gregory, to be the Place of their last legal Settlement.

Cha: Baynbridge Agent for the Appellants } That your Petitioners conceiving themselves Agrieved to your said Warrant or Order of Removal, Do Appeal to your Lordship and Worships therefrom, and humbly pray, that you will be pleased to hear them in the Premisses, and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St Botolph without Aldgate London, may appear before you, to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of Yor Lordship and Worships, touching this Appeal

And your Petitionrs: & Shall pray Etc
