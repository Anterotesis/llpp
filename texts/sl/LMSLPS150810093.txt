To the Right Honourable William Beckford Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesties Justices of Peace at the General Sessions of the Peace for the said City Assembled

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Parish of St. Dunstan in the East in the said City

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Samuel Turner Esqr. late Lord Mayor and Sr. William Stephenson Knt. two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and Liberty's thereof (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 30th. day of October 1769. Mary Wingfield (a Lunatick) Widow of Paul Wingfield deceased was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of St. Mary Abchurch in the City of London to our said Parish of St. Dunstan in the East as the place of her last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved thereby Do humbly appeal against the said Order or Pass Warrant and pray that this Court will be pleased to appoint a day at this present Session of the Peace for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Mary Abchurch and all other Persons concerned to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching this Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

W Merrick Sollicitor for the Appellants
