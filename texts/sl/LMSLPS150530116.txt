To the Right Honoble the Lord Mayor and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Libertys thereof

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Without Bishopsgate in the City of London.

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the hand sand Seals of two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City and Liberty's thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the first day of February 1741 Susanna Smithson Singlewoman Daughter of Alexander Smithson (who is a Native of Ireland and hath no Settlement in England) by Margaret his Wife Deceased was Removed from the Parish of St. Botolph Aldgate London to the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate London Aforesaid Adjudging the Last Legal Settlement of the said Susanna Smithson to be [...] And your Petitioners conceiving themselves Agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices Do Humbly Appeal therefrom And Humbly pray such Relief in the premisses as to Your Lordship And Worships shall seem meet and Conceiving

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
