To the Rt. Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London Assembled in General Quarter Sessions at the Guild Hall of the said City.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Mary Le Bone in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the Br Honble John Blackford, Mayor and Francis Cokayne Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 3d day of July 1750 Mary Joy, Widow of Thomas Joy, deceased, was removed from the Parish of St. Martin Ludgate in the City of London to the said Parish of St. Mary Le Bone in the County of Middlesex, as the place of her last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners thinking themselves aggrieved by the Judgement of the said two Justices of the peace Appeal to this Court against the same, and desire to be relieved therein

And your Petitioners shall ever pray, Etc.

Thos. Huddles Wm. Welton} Churchwardens

Willm. Smith George Squire Overseers of the Poor
