To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary at Lambeth in the County of Surrey

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honorable Thomas Townsend Esquire Lord Mayor and Thomas Hallifax Esquire Alderman two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 16th: Day of October 1773 Sarah Self aged nine Years, and Charlotte Self aged seven Years the Children of Samuel Self, were removed from the Parish of Saint Michael at Queen [...] London into the said Parish of Saint Mary of [...] alledging the said Parish of Saint Mary at Lamb [...] the place of their last Legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners conceive themselves agrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices Do therefore appeal to Your Worships therefrom and humbly pray that You will be pleased to hear them in the Promisses and Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Micahel at Queenhithe London may appear before You at a certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of Your Worships touching the Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

George Hargrave John Jones} Churchwardens

James Taylor} Overseers of the Poor
