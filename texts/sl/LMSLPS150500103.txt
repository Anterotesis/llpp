To the Right Honrble Micajah Perry Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of the Worshipfull ye Justices of the peace at the General Quarter Sessions of the peace now Assembled

The Humble Petition & Appeale of the Church wardens & overseers of the poor of the parish of Alhallows Barkin London

Sheweth That by virtue of an order or Pass warrant under the hands and Seals of Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 30 day of July 1739 whereby Mary Dunstan a Female Bastard Child aged about two years & a half was [...] from & out of the Parish of St. Dunstan in the East London in to our said Parish of Alhallows Barkin London as the place of her & Last Legall Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Conceive themselves much aggrived by the sd. order or Pass warrant & humbly appeal against the same in order to have such relief as your worship's shall seem meett

& your petitioners Shall Ever pray Etc.

Jas. Pannett} Church Wardens

Thos: Cullum Thos. Purne} Overseers of the poor

Hum: Sankey
