To the Right Honble The Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majestys Justices of the peace for the same City in the General Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof one being of the Quorum bearing Date the Seventeenth Day of January 1767 Jane Farr Wife of Edward Farr gone from her (as is alledged by the said Order) and their three Children Sarah aged Fourteen Years Edward aged Nine Years and Hannah aged Seven Years were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London unto the said Parish of St. Stephen in Coleman Street as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Edward Farr Jane his wife and their said three Children.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant humbly appeal against the same and pray Relief in the Premisses.
