To the Right Honble: & Worpll: her Maties: Justices of the peace for the City of London

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Over Seers of the poor of the Parish of Totenham in the County of Middlx

Sheweth That by a Wart. under the hands and [...] coles of Sr. Charles Doncomb Knt. Lord Major of the said City and one of her Maties Justices of the peace for the said City And Etc Thomas Stamp Sent. an other of her Maties Justices of the peace for the said City (Quor Unus) bearing date the Eighteenth day of March last past. Mercy Walton Wife of Edmond Walton and there four Children (to say) Elizabeth aged. fourteen yeares and Mary aged Seaven years Six Moneths and Edmond aged three yeare and Nathaniel aged fourteen Moneths or thereabouts were removed from the Parish of St. Bottolph without Bishopgate London to the Parish of Totenham aforesaid there are alleadging that Mr Henry Walton upon her Oath [...] that Edmond Walton her husband aforesaid several a hyred Servt. by the year with Phillip Raymond a [...] in the Parish of Totenham in the County of Middx and was last legally Settled in the sand Parish of Totenham

That you Peticonrs think them tetar agreived with the Judgment of the saw two Justices of the peace And humbly Appeal against the said Warrant for that yor Peticonly double not bat they well make appear that their legall Settlemt. ward not in the Saw Parish of Totenham

Your Peticonrs therefore humbly pray you Worpp: will be pleased to great in Order that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of St Botolph without Bishopgate London may others this hornble Court this present second to hear and abode the Judgment and final determination of this Court touching this Appeal

And yor Peticoners Shall ever pray Etc
