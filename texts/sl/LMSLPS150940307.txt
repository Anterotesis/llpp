To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Nathaniel Newnham Mayor and John Boydell Esquires two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof one being of the Quorum bearing Date the Second day of October 1783 Mary the Widow of James Hardcastle and her two Children namely Elizabeth Aged Seven Years and upwards and William Aged five Years and upwards were removed and Conveyed from and out of the parish of St Giles Cripplegate in the said City of London unto the said parish of Saint Mary White Chapel as the last legal Settlement of the said Mary whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Agrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal Against the Order and Adjudication of the said two Justices and pray that their said appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and Determination thereof may be Adjourned until the next General Session of the peace to be held for the said City and that all persons concerned may be then Ordered to Attend.

And Your Petitioners will ever pray Etc. by Jno. Skull Sollr. for Appellants. 31st. October 1783
