To the Rt. Honrble Sr Charles Duncombe Knt. Lord Major of the City of London And to the Rt. Worpll the Rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace in the generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble petition of and Appeale of the Churchwardens and overseers of the poore of St. Michael Bassishaw London

Sheweth That one James Condy and Elizabeth his Wife By Virtue of [...] a Warrant under the hands and Seales of Two of her Maties Justices of the peace within the City of London (one being of the Quorm) bearing Date the 15th day of January anne Due 1708 was removed from the parish of St. Andrew holborne London to the said parish of St Michael Bassieshaw Alleadgeing the said parish to be the place of the last legall of the said James Condy and Elizabeth his Wife

That your petitioners doubt not but to prove That the said James Condy and Elizabeth his Wife never had any legall Settlement in the said parish of St. Michael Bassieshaw whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the Judgment of the said Twoe Justices and doe humbly appeale to your Worpps therefrom

Your Petitionrs therefore humbly pray your Honrs and Worpps will be pleased to heare them in ye pemisses and order the Churchwardens and overseers of the poor of the said parish of St. Andrew Holborne to appeare before you and hear and abide the determination of your honr and Worpps touching this appeale

And you Petitionr Shall ever pray Etc
