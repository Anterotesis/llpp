To the Right honble The Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Justices of the Peace for the and City in their Genl. Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Gidding Magna in the County of Huntingdon

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of the Right Honble John Wilkes Esqr. Lord. Mayor of the City of London and Frederick Bull Esqr. one of the Aldermen of the sd. City two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Fourteenth day of Decr. One Thousand Seven hundred and Seventy four Ann Ward widow of William Ward deced and her three Children William Aged four Years Ann Aged Two Years and John Aged Ten Weeks were removed from the Parish of St. Botolph Aldersgate without in the said City of London and on the Twenty fifth day of January then next delivered to one of the Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Gidding Magna as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Ann Ward and her Street Children Whereby your Petitioner Conceive themselves to be Aggreived and against the said Order do Appeal Your Petitioners

Therefore humbly Pray that a day may be Appointed to hear and determine the Matter touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.

Robt Hatfield Junr. Churchwardens

Jonathan Care Robt Hatfield } Overseers of the Poor Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Gidding Magna In the County of [---] Hunting- -don aforesaid.
