To the Right Honble the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesty's Justice of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Saviour Southwark in the County of Surrey

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands & seals of the right Honourable Frederick Bull Lord Mayor and James Esdaile Esquire, two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Fifth day of January last Martha Hamblin Singlewoman was removed from the Parish of Saint Ann in Black Friars London unto the said Parish of Saint Saviour Southwark as the place of her last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves Agrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices Do therefore appeal to your worships therefrom and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the Premises and order that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Ann in Black Friars London may appear before you at a certain day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.

Appeal

Richard Toye Peter Spence} Churchwardens

Peet Broadley James Harvey} Overseers of the Poor.
