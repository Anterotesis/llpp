London To the Right Honourable Sir Thomas Hallifax Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of the Justices in and for the said City in Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West in the Liberty of the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal bearing Date the Third Day of February last past under the Hands and Seals of Frederick Bull and Richard Clark Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and Liberties thereof one being of the Quorum Jane Jones a Singlewoman was removed from the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the Liberty of the said City of London to the said Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West And the said Justices in and by the said Order of Removal adjudged the last Legal Settlement of the said Jane Jones to be in the said Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West.

That your Petitioners Conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order and Adjudicator In as much as they are advised and believe that the Legal Settlement of the said Jane Jones is not (as thereby supposed) in the said Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said Order of Removal and pray that the same may be Quashed And that this Honourable Court will be pleased to make such further Order in the Premises as to them Shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
