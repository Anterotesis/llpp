To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City London in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Margaret Lothbury London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City (One being of the Quorum) bearing date the sixth day of March One Thousand Seven hundred and Seventyone James Mead Rosamond his Wife and their three Children (to wit) John aged four years. James aged two Years and William aged five Months were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate London to the said Parish of Saint Margaret Lothbury London and the place of their last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves Aggrieved thereby do humbly Appeal against the same and Pray that the Hearing and Determining of the said Appeal may be adjourned until the next General Session of the Peace to be held for the said City and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate London and all other Persons concerned may then Attend to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the then Court touching the said Appeal.

Fras. Salt Attorney for Appellts.
