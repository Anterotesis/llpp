To the Right Honble Sr Willm Humfreys Knt & Barrt Lord Mayor of the City of London And to the Right Worpll the Rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Severall Parishes of St. Gile's without Criplegate St. Botolph without Algate St Andrew Wardrobe St Katherine Coleman St Andrew Holbourn, St. Sepulchre St. Brides Allhallows on the And St. Botolph Aldersgate And St. Botolph without Bishopsgate

Sheweth That your Petitioners are Very much over burthened with poor and not able to raise Sufficient Sumes of money amongst themselves to relieve them Notwithstanding their Assessments are greater then heretofore Yet the Charge of the Poor is far above their Assessments as the same upon their Examination hath divers times appeared to several Worthy Alderman of this City appointed to hear the Same And their Poor daily increasing upon them their said Parishes some of them lying near the Suburbs and others of them in the Suburbs of this City

Their humble Petition therefore unto Your good Honr and Worppll is that you will be favourably pleas'd to grant unto them the Contributions of other Parishes within this City for & towards the releiving ye poor as they have formerly had which are particularly here Undermencond or Such farther Allowance as to ye Honr & Worppll shall seem meet
