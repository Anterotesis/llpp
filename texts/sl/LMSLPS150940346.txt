To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City at the Quarter Sessions of the peace of our Lord the King holden for the said City

The humble petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Martin Outwich in the City of London

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order of Removal or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Nathaniel Newnham Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and William plomer Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the City of London (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the second day of April One thousand seven hundred and Eighty three Mary Ann the Widow of William Harding and her Child named John Aged two Years and upwards were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate in the City of London unto the said Parish of Saint Martin Outwich the said Justices thereby adjudging the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Ann Harding and her said Child to be in the said parish of Saint Martin Outwich

That your Petitioners apprehend that the Settlement of the said Mary Ann Harding and her child is not in the said Parish of Saint Martin Outwich

Your Petitioners therefore appeal from the said Order of Removal of the said two Justices and humbly pray that the Hearing of the said Appeal may be adjourned unto the next Sessions of the Peace and that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day in that Session to hear and determine the said Appeal
