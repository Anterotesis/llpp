To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Nathaniel Newnham Mayor and John Hart Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London one being of the Quorum bearing date the Sixth day of February last Mary the Wife of John Boxwell Prisoner in the Kings Bench was removed and Conveyed from and out the Parish of St. Olave Silver Street to the parish of Saint Mary at White Chapelin the County of Middlesex as the last legal Settlement of the said Mary By which Order and Adjudication Your Petitioner conceive themselves to be Agrieved and do hereby Appeal from the same.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that their said Appeal may be saved unto them that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned until thereof General Quarter Session of the Peace to be held for the said City and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Olave Silver Street and all Persons concerned may be them Ordered to attend the Court

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Jno. Skutt Soll, for the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel 1st March 1783
