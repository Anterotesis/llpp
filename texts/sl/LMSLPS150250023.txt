To the Rt Honrble Sr. Samuels Stanier Knt. Lord Major of the City of London and to the Rt. Worpll: the Rest of the Maties Justices of the Peace in the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Severall parishes of St. Giles without Cripplegate St Buttolph without Aldgate, St. Andrew Wardrobe, St Natherine coleman St Andrew Holborn, St Sepulchre St Brides, Allhallows on the Wall, St Buttolph Aldergate, and St Buttolph without Bishopsgate.

Sheweth

That one petitonrs are very much over burtherned with poor and not able to raise sufficient Sumes of Mony amongst themselves to relieve them not with standing their Assesments are greater than heretofore Yet the charge of the poor is farr above their assessments as the same upon their Examination hath divese times appeared to Severall Worthy Aldermen of this City Appointed to hear the same And their poor Dayly increasing upon them their said parishes some of them lying neare the suburbs and others of them in the Suburbs of this City

Their humble petition therefore into good honr and Worpps is that you will be favourably pleased to grant unto them the Contribution of other parished within this City for and towards the releiving the poor as they have formerly had which are particularly hereunder menconed or such further Allowance as to your Honr. and Worpps Shall seem meet
