To the Right Honourable John Wilkes Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipful the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the several Consumers of Barley and Pease within the City of London and the Liberties thereof

Sheweth That for some time past the common Market Prices of middling British Barley and middling British Peace at the new Corn Market in Mark Lane have exceeded the Sum following to wit middling British Barley the Sum of twenty four Shillings the Quarter and middling British Peace the Sum of thirty two Shillings the Quarter the Continuance whereof your Petitioners apprehend will prove very detrimental to the City in General and to your Petitioners is particular

That your Petitioners are ready to make the Truth hereof appear and to prove the same in such manner as by the Statutes in that behalf in required

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that this Honourable Court will take their Case into Consideration and proceed to examine and determine the common Market Prices of middling British Barley and middling British Peace as the same are now and have been lately bought and sold in the Market of this City and to certify the same to his Majesty's Chief Officer and Collector of the Customs in such manner as by the Statutes in this behalf is required and directed

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
