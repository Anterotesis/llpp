London To the Right Honourable Brackley Kennett Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London And the Worshipful the Alderman his Majesty's Justices of the Peace and for the said City and the Liberties thereof at their General Session of the Peace at the Guildhall of the said City assembled

The humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Faith under Saint Paul in the City of London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in a and for the City of London and the Liberties thereof (one whereof being of the Quorum) Dated the Ninth day of August last past Frances Samber a Siglewoman was removed from the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripple gate in the City of London to the Parish of Saint Faith under Saint Paul in the city of London as the Place of her last legal Settlement by which your, Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved

Therefore humble Appeal against the same and Pray such Relief in the Premises as to this Honourable Court shall seem meet.

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray.

J Greenaway Solr. for the Appellants
