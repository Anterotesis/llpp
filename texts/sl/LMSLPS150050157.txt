To the Right Honoble. Sr. Thomas Lane Knt Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll the rest of their Maties. Justices of the Peace att the Generall Quarter Sessions of ye Peace assembled.

1694 28 Nov The humble Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwarden & Overseers of the poore of Alhallowes the greate London

Sheweth That one Mary Idle widdow the late wife of Joseph Idle by virtue of A Warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of their Maties. Justices of the Peace wth. in the City of London one being of the Quor dated the 28th. November last was sent to yor. Petr. to be provided for upon the Suggestion that the said Joseph Idle was a Lodger About Forty dayes together before May 1685 in the said Parish of Alhallowes whereas in truth the said Joseph Idle in the Yeare 1682 dwelt and Inhabited in the Pish of St. Magnus the Martin in the Water house there where he was turn cock for above 6: moneths togather and never had any Manner of legall Settlement in yor. Petrs. said Parish of Alhallowes the greate

Yor. Petr. therefore humbly Pray yor. Honors. and Worpps. to hear them in the Prmisses and to send the said Mary Idle to the said Pish of St. Magnus where the said Joseph Idle her husband was last legally settled to be pvided for an Mainteyned as a poore inhabitant in the said Parish

And they shall Pray Etc

Eliz Cooke Maker oath that Joseph Idle did Lodge in the water house in 1682 Parnell Toegood Robt Standinott
