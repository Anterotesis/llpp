To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor and Worshipful the Aldermen of the City of London, and others his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City, and the Liberties thereof at their General Session of the Peace at Guildhall London Assembled

The humble Petition of the Church: :Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Catherine Cree Church in the City of London

Sheweth That by an Order of Removal bearing date the the Twelfth day of this Instant February One Thousand Seven hundred and Seventy four under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honorable Frederick Bull Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and John Wilkes Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and the Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) Jane Beck a Singlewoman was removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopgate without in the City of London to the said Parish of Saint Catherine Cree Church in the City of London as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Jane Beck

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Order of Removal do therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the same; Praying such relief in the Premisses as to your Worships shall Seem meet

And your Petitioners shall Ever pray Etc

Jms: Fisher Sollicitor for the said Appellants
