To the Right Honble James Townsend Esqr Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Worshipful Justices of the peace in their General Quarter Sessions assembled:

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Martin Ludgate London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City and Libertys thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the sixteenth day of April instant Mary Woodcock Widow of William Woodcock deced and their two Children Elizabeth aged fifteen Years and Mary aged nine Years were removed from the Precinct of Whitefryers London to the said Parish of Saint Martin Ludgate London as the Place of their last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners therefore conceive themselves aggreived by the said Order or Pass Warrant And do humbly appeal against the same And pray such Relief in the Premises as to your Lordship & Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall pray Etc.
