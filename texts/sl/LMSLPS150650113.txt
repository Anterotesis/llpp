To the Right Honble; Thomas Rawlinson Esqr Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace in their General Quarter Session Assembled.

The humble Petition of Thomas Woodman the under keeper of the Prison called the Poultry Compter.

Sheweth That Your petitioners rents in the Parish of Saint Mildred in the Poultry London the Goal comonly called the Poultry Compter and pays the yearly sum of Eighty four pounds for the same.

That this Goal before the late Act to prevent the sale of Spiritous Liquors in Goal was rented at £140. P Annum but by reason of that act it became of less value, and the Landlord, throughly sensible of it reduced the rent to £84., on it is the full, if not more than the Value of it

That your petitioner is in respect of the said Goal overated to the poor of the said Parish in the last Rate made for there Relief

Your Petitionr. therefore humbly Appeales against the said Rate and Assemt. and prays such Relief in the premisses as to Yor. Lop shall seem me

Tho Woodman
