To the Rt. Honrble Sr. Charles Duncombe Knt Lord Major of the City of London And to the Rt Worpll the Rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace in the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and overseers of the poore of the parish of St. Ann Blackfryers London

Sheweth That One Jane Gregory wife of Sampson Gregory the Elder with her four Children Sampson, William Robert and Sarah By Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of Two of her Maties Justices of the peace within the City of London (one being of the Quorn) bearing Date the 19th Day of Aprill last poet was removed from the parish of St. Bartholomew the Great London to the said parish of St Ann Blackfryers Alleadging the said parish to be the place of the last legall Settlement of the said lane Gregory and her said four Children

That you petitioners doubt not but to prove That the said Jane Gregory and her said four Children never had ann legall Settlement in the said parish of St. Ann Blackfryers where by they Conceive themselves aggreived by the Judgment of the said Twoe Justices and doe humbly appeale to your worpps therefrom

Your Petitionr therefore humbly pray your Honrs and Worpps will be pleased to heare them in the pemisses And Order the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poore of the said parish of St. Bartholomew the great To appeare before you And hear and abide the Determination of your Honrs and Worpps touching this appeale

And your Petitioners shall Ever pray Etc
