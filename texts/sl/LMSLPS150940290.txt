To the Right Honble Nathaniel Newnham Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the said City of London in General Session assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Helen in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Nathaniel Newnham and Barnd. Turner Esquires Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 17th day of January last Margaret the Widow of Richard Pragnell was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate London to the said Parish of Saint Helen as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Margaret

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order and therefore appeal against the same and pray relief in the Premises and that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved to them and the Hearing and Determination thereof adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc

Tho. Trundle Soll.
