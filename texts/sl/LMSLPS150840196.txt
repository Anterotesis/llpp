To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the same City at the General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honoble James Townsend Esqr. Lord Mayor and Nathaniel Thomas Esqr. Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Second day of September 1773 Mary Wilson Wife of John Wilson gone from her whose Settlement Can not be found and their Three Children (to wit) John aged Eleven years, Matillda aged three years and William aged One year, Were removed from the Parish of Christ Church, London To the said Parish of saint Giles without Cripplegate London as the place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners Conceive them- selves agrevied by the said Warrant Or Order and Humbly appeal against the same and Pray this Honourable Court to Grant them such relief in the Premises as Shall be thought most Equitable

And your petitioners Shall ever pray Etc.

Thos. Strong Sollr.
