To the right Honrble Sr. George Mertins Knt. Lord Major of the City of London and the rest of [---] his Maties Justices of the peace for the said Citty at their Genall Quarter Sessions now Assembled

The humble peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St James in Dukes place London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order bearing Date the Six and Twentieth day of May last under the hands & Seales of the said Sr George Mertins and Sr. Randolph Knipe Knights Two of his Maties Justices of the peace in and for the said Citty (Quor unus) Edward Simmonds Mary his Wife and three Children Edward aged about seaven years Mary about three years and William about Five months were removed from the parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate to the said parish of Saint James in Dukes place as the place of their last legall Setlement By means of which said Order your petticoners conceive themselves to be Agreived and humbly Appeale therefrom

Your Peticoners therefore pray that your this Court will order the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint [---] Bottolph wth: out Bishopsgate and all other persons concernd to attend this Honrble Court and to abide by & Stand to the determinacon thereof touching the maters herein conteined

And yor Petr shall ever pray Etc.
