To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor & the Rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London at the General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of saint Bartholomew by the Exchange in the said City

Sheweth That by an Order or pass Warrant bearing date the 19th day of April 1783 under the hands and seals of the Right Honourable Nathaniel Newnham Lord Mayor of the City of London and John Hart Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in & for the said City (One whereof being of the Quorum) Whereby Frances the Widow of Thomas Dover Hopkins was removed from the parish of saint Mary Abchurch London to the said Parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Exchange in the said City as the Place of her legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners apprehending themselves aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant of the said two Justices humbly appeal against the same

And therefore pray that you will be pleased to Order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Abchurch afsd. and all persons concerned to attend this Court some day this present Sessions to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of this Court touching the premises And that your Petitioners may have such relief as to your Worships shall seem meet
