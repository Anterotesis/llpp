To the Right Honourable James Townsend Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Worshipful Justices of the Peace in their General Quarter Sessions assembled.

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Martin Ludgate London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Robert Alsop Esquire and Sir James Esdaile, Knight two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty fourth day of October last past Sarah Armson Wife of James Armson gone from her, was removed from the parish of Saint Bartholomew the Great London to the said Parish of Saint Martin Ludgate as the Place of her last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners therefore appeal against the said Order and humbly pray that the said Appeal may be saved to them and that the hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned over until the next General Quarter Sessions of the peace for the said City & Liberties thereof.

And your Petitioners shall pray Etc.
