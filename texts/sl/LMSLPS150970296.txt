To the Right Honble: Thomas Wright Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and the Liberties thereof in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Giles in the Fields in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass arrant or Order under the hands and seals of the said Right Honble. Thomas Wright Esquire and William Newman Esquire two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties, one being of the Quorum, bearing date the 21st. day of July last Moses Cox and (a Woman called in the said Order) his Wife Jane were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London to the said Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Moses whereby your Petitioner conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order And pray that your Worships will he pleased to appoint a day in this present Session for the hearing and determining the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners will pray Etc

Herbt. Robertson Solicitor for the Appellants
