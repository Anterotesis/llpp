1700 To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Richard Levett Knt. Lord Maior of the Citty of London And to the Rt. Worll. the Rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace in the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of Christchurch London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the Hands and Seales of Twoe of the Maties Justices of the Peace within this City (one being of the Quor) Dated the 12. February 1699 One Anne Paine with four Children James William Anne and Mary were brought be the Churchwardens of St. Buttolph Aldersgate To. Yor. Petrs. to be mainteyned upon her Oath That William Paine her husband decd dwelt in Annell Alley in the said Parish of Christchurch by Order of the Churchwarden of the said Parish Where need west 6 moneths Whereas in truth the said Anne was never Marryed unto the said William Paine And that the said 4 Children are Bastards and were Borne in severall Parishes To witt James In Christchurch parish William in St. Buttolph Aldersgate & Anne and Mary in the Parish of St. Gyles without Cripplegate London

Yor. Petrs. Pray They may be heard on their Appeale And that the said Anne may be sent to the said Parish of St. Buttolph Aldersgate And the Children to the Severall Parishes wherein They were burnt To be all provided for untill They can free themselvs of the Charge thereof by one course of Lawe.
