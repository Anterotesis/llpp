To the Right Honoble Sir Gerard Conyers Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London And to the Right Worpfull the Rest of His Maties Justices of the Peace for the said City & the Libties thereof in their Generall Quarter Sessions assembled

The Humble Peticon & Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Gregory by St. Pauls London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Warrt under the hands & seals of Two of His Maties Justices of the Peace for the said City & Libties thereof (Quor unus) bearing date the Sixth day of June 1723. Mary Gulliver Widow was removed from the Parish of St. Andrew by the Wardrobe London to Your Petitioners said Parish alledging the same in the Indgmt. of the said Justices to be the Place of her last legall Settlement.

That Your Peticoners conceiving themselves aggreived by the said Order or Warrt of the said Two Justices Do humbly appeal to Your Lorp & Worps therefrom And humbly pray they may be heard concerning the Premisses And that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Andrew by the Wardrobe London may appear before You to hear & abide by the Judgmt. & Determinacon of Your Lorp & Worps touching this Appeal

And Your Peticoners shall ever pray Etc.
