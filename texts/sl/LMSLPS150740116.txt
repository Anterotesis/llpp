To the Right Honourable William Beckford Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Worshipful the Alderman his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Ann in Black Friar in the same City.

Sheweth That by an Order of Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Francis Cokayne Esquire and Sir Francis Gosling Knight two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty ninth day of July last past [---] John Katesmark Mary his Wife and their Child Mary aged one Year were removed from the parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the in the said City to the said Parish of Saint Ann in Black Friar, as the Place of their last legal settlement.

That Your Petitioners humbly thinking themselves aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices appeal to your Lordship and Worships against the said Order And pray you will be pleased to determine the same and make such Order thereon as to you shall seem meet,

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
