To the Right Honourable John Barber Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City now in their Generall Quarter Sesssions Assembled

The Humble Peticon and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the barrs in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That Josias Disturnell the Elder was by order of Pass warrant dated the fourteenth day of August now last past under the hands and seales of the Right Honourable John Barber Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and Sr. William Billers Knt. and Alderman of the same City two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and the Liberties thereof (one whereof being of the Quorum) removed and conveyed from the parish of Saint Sepulchres in the City of London to the said parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the barrs in the said County of Middlesex as the place of his last legall Settlement

Your Peticoners humbly conceive themselves aggrieved thereby and appeal from the Judgment of the said Justices to the Judgment of this Honourable Court And desire that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Sepulchres London may appear before you to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of this Honourable Court upon and touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall Pray & so forth
