To the Rt, Honoble. Sr. Robert Bedingfeld Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rt. Worll. The rest of Her Maties. Justices of ye Peace In the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St Dunstans Stepney in the County of Middx

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of Twoe of Her Maties Justices of the Peace within the City of London (One being of the Quor) bearing date the 21th. November last One Margery Radley was by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St James Dukes place in London sent to yor. Petrs. to be provided for Upon the Oath of the said Margery Radley That shee Lived an hired Servant by the Yeare for the space of Twoe yeares and upwards with one Mr. Sounds a Cutler over acct. School house Lane in yor. Petrs. Parish And dureing that tyme Received Three Pounds P Annu Wages Whereas in Truth the said Margery Radley Since her Service with Mr. Sound Married one Hugh Radley in the Yeare 1685 and lived with him for the space of 17 yeares in the Parish of St. James Dukes place And hath since his Decease continued in the said Parish as an Inhabitant there

Wherefore yor. Petrs. humbly Pray yor. Honor and Worpps. to heare them in the prmisses And Order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of St. James Dukes place to appeare here before you And to Shew Cause why the said Warrant made by the said twoe Justices should not be Quashed And the said Margery Radley sent to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the said Parish of St. James Dukes places to be by them provided for an maintained untill They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Lawe

And yor. Petrs. shall Ever Pray Etc
