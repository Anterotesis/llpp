To The Right Honourable Thomas Wright Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in the General Quarter Session of the Peace holden for the same City Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of The Reverend William Clements Clerk

Sheweth That Your Petitioner hath been for above Twenty Years past and now is Library Keeper to the President and Fellows of Sion College situate in the Parish of Saint Alphage in the City of London and as such Library Keeper resides in Apartments in the said College and is allowed a certain Yearly Stipend by the said president and Fellows for his Trouble and Attendance as such Library Keeper

That by a certain Rate or Assessment made on or about the Twenty fourth Day of July last for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Alphage Your Petitioner hath been charged in the Sum of Twenty eight Shillings towards the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish for and in Respect of the said Apartments in the said College in which Your Petitioner as such Library Keepers so resides

That Your Petitioner apprehends that as he resides in such Apartments in the said College only as Library Keeper to the said President and Fellows he ought not to be charged towards the Relief of the poor of the said Parish

in any Sum of Money whatsoever and therefore Your Petitioner apprehends himself to be aggrieved by the said Rate or Assessment and that the said Sum of Twenty eight Shillings is illegally charged on him in and by the said Rate or Assessment.

Therefore your Petitioners humbly appeals against the said Rate or Assessment in Respect of the said Sum of Twenty eight Shillings so charge don Yours Petitioner and prays that the said Rate or Assessment may be quashed or that Your Petitioner Petitioner mau be quashed or that Your Petitioner may be discharged from the Payment of the Sum of Twenty eight Shillings or be otherwise relieved in the Premises in such Manner as shall appear just and equitable to this Honourable Court
