To the Right Honble William Beckford Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London And the rest of the Worshipful Justices of the Peace at the General Quarter Session of the Peace now Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Bartholomew the Great London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London bearing date the 30th. day of January 1770. John Turner, Elizabeth his Wife, And their Child George Aged Eight Years were Removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Alphage London into the said Parish of Saint Bartholomew the Great London As the place of their last legal Settlement.

Your Petitrs. therefore Concieve themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant, And humbly Appeal [...] against the Same And Pray that this Honble Court Will be pleased [...] [...] to Make Such Order in the Premisses for their relief As Shall Seem Meet.

And Your Petrs. shall ever Pray.

B Parkes. No 49 Bartho Close Sollr. for the Parish of St. Bartho. the Great Appellants.
