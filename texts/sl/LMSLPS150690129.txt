To the Right Honourable Sir Charles Asgill Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the rest of his Majestye's Justices of the Peace of the said City in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Sir Charles Asgill Knight and Francis Cokayne Esqr. two of his Majestyes Justices of the Peace for the City of London bearing Date the Second day of this Instant December Ann Rogers Wife of Edward Rogers a Sailor in his Majestyes Service (Whose Settlement cannot be found) and their two Children Martha Aged Six years and Lydia Aged three Weeks were Removed and Conveyed fromand out of the Parish of Saint Giles Cripplegate in the said City of London to the said Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex as the Place of the in last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners thinking themselves Aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or Order do therefore humbly Appeal against the Same and Pray your Worshipps to make such Order concerning the Premises as to your Worshipps shall Seem Meet.

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

G. Talmash Attorney for the Appellants.
