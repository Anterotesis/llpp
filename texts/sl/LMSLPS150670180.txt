To the Right Honourable Slingsby Bethell Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Southery in the County of Norfolk

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of your Lordship and William Stephenson Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the third Day of September one thousand seven hundred and Fifty six Mary Brickley Wife of John Brickley said to be a Soldier in his Majestys Service and their three Children Margaret aged ten years Ann aged Four years and John aged two years were Removed and Conveyed out of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London unto the said Parish of Southery in the County of Norfolk as the place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Aggrieved thereby do humbly Appeal against the said order or Pass Warrant and pray such Relief in the premisses as to your Lordship and Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

John Canham Junr.} Churchwardens

John Canham Senr. John Osler } Overseers
