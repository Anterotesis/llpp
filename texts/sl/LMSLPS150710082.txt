To the Right Honourable and Worshipfull the Lord Mayor and others His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of Sir Thomas Chitty Knight Mayor and John Cartwright Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London (one being of the Quorum) bearing late the seventh day of August 1760 John Davis a Child aged Ten Weeks was removed from the Parish of Saint Brides London to the said Parish of Saint James as the place of its last legal Settlement Whereby Your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved and Appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray this Court will please to save the said Appeal and that the hearing and Determining thereof may be adjourned until the next General Session of the Peace to be held for the said City if London And that all person, concerned may be Ordered then to Attend

And Your Petitioner shall Pray Etc

Thos Pickles
