To the Right Honble Thomas Harley Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London, and to the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Session of the Peace in and for the said City assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Margaret Westminster, in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Vagrant Pass, Warrant, or Order of Removal under Your Lordships Hand and Seal bearing Date the 27th, day of August 1768. John Green (therein Mentioned to have been apprehendd in the Parish of St. Magnus in the said City as a Rogue and Vagabond Wandring Abroad, Lodging in the Open Air, and not giving a Good Account of himself) was Removed from the said Parish of Saint Magnus in the said City to the said Parish of St. Margaret Wastmr. in the County of Middlesex as the Place of his last legal Settlement Whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved, and Appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly Pray Your Lordship and this Worshipfull Court this the Benefit of the said Appeal may be Lane unto them, and that the hearing and determining

determining thereof be Adjourned to the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the Said City, and that all Parties concerned may then and there attend.

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

John Shipman Adam Edgar} Church Wardens of St. Margaret Westminster

Rd. Godfree Jos: Askmore} Overseers of the Poor of the Said Parish
