To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate London.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Libertys thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the thirteenth day of November 1755 Winifred Flannegan (called in the said Order Winifred Flannegan Widow of William Flannegan deced) was removed from the Parish of Saint James in Dukes Place London to the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate London aforesaid adjudging the last legal Settlement of the said Winifred Flannegan to be there And Your Petitioners conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices Do humbly appeal there from and pray such Relief in the Premisses as to Your Lordship and Worships shall seem meet and convenient.

And Yr. Petrs. shall ever Pray Etc.

Burr Attorney for Bpsgate Parish.
