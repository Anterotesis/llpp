To the Right Honoble William Bridgen Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Right Worshipful the Recorder and the rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City and the Liberties the roof in their General Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of the Right Honoble. William Bridgen Esquire Lord Mayor; and Sir Matthew Blakiston Knight and Baronet one of the Alderman of the said City, two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City and the Liberties thereof James Stuart aged fourteen Years Susanna Stuart aged Six Years and Jane Stuart Aged Four Years Children of Henry Stuart (who is alleged by the said Order to be a Native of Ireland and to have had no Settlement in England) by Sarah his wife both deced were on Monday the Twenty Third day of July last past removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London into the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate as the Place of their last legal Settlement.

Sep. Sessions 1764 Your Petititrs Concerning themselves Aggrieved by the said Warrant or order humbly Appeal to this Court against the Same and Pray such Relief in the Premes as to this Honoble Court shall Seem Meet

And Your Petrs. Shall ever Pray Etc.
