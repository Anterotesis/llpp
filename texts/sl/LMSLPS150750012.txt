To the Right Honourable William Beckford Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Worshipful the Aldermen his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of Henry Cothery an Inhabitant in the parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street in the said City.

Sheweth. That by a Rate or Assessment for the necessary Relief of the poor of the said Parish of Saint Stephen from the twenty fourth day of June to the twenty ninth day of September both now last past made and signed by the Major part of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish, and allowed and published as the Statutes in such Cases direct, your Petitioner is rated and assessed for and towards the Relief of the poor of the said parish the Sum of one pound five Shillings.

That Your Petitioners humbly apprehending himself aggrieved by the said Rate and Assessment, and that the same is unequal and partial, appeals to your Lordship and Worships against the same, and prays that you would be pleased to quash or amend the same and grant your Petitioner such Relief in the Premisses as to your Lordp and Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitoner shall ever pray Etc.
