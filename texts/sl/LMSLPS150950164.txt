To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of the Close of Sarum in the County of the Wilts (their being no Church wardens of the said Liberty)

Sheweth, That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Sir Watkin Lewes Knight and John Burnell Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Third day of July 1784 Nathaniel Beckley was removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Martin Ludgate in the said City of London to the said Liberty of the Close of Sarum as the place of his last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioner's conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant of the said two Justices do humbly appeal against the same and pray that the hearing and determining of the said Appeal may be Adjourned untill the next Sessions of the peace to be held in and for the said City of London and that the Court will be pleased to appoint a day in the said next Session for the Overseers of the poor of the said Liberty of the said Close of Sarum and all other persons concerned therein to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal.

Mr. Turner Attorney for the Appellants.
