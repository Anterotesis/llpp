To the Rt. Honble the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London in General Quarter Session of the Peace at the Guildhall in and for the said City assembled

The humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Order under the Hands and Seals of The Rt. Honble George Nelson Esqr. Lord Mayor and Sr. John Cartwright Knt. & Alderman two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof (one of the Quorum) bearing date the 9th, day of May 1766, Elizabeth Walker Wife of Edmund Walker (in the said Order said to be gone from her) was removed from the Parish of St. Dunstan in the West in the City of London to the sd Parish of St. Clement Danes as the place of her last legal Settlement whereby Your Petitioners conceive them- selves to be aggrieved and against the said Order do appeal

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray the benefit of the said Appeal may be Saved to them and that the hearing and determining thereof May be Adjourned until the next General [---] Session of the peace to be holden for the said City of London

And Yor. Petrs. shall pray Etc.
