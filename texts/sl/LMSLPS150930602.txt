To the Right Honorable Nathaniel Newnham Esquire Lord Major of the City of London and one of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City and to the rest of the Worshipful his Majesty's Justice's of the peace for the same City in General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble petition and Appeal of the Overseers of the poor of the Liberity of Norton Folgate in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a certain pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of your Lordship and John Boydell Esquire two of his Majesty's Justice's of the Peace for the City of London aforesaid (one whereof is of the Quorum) bearing date the second day of October One Thousand seven hundred and Eighty Three, William Hallier Mary his Wife and their two Children namely William a Lunatic aged Nineteen Years and upwards and John aged nine years and upwards were ordered to be removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopgate London to the Liberty of Norton Folgate aforesaid and the place of their last legal Settlement.

That Your Petitioner's conceive themselves aggrieved by the said pass Warrant or Order and therefore humbly appeal against the same

And your Petitioner's shall ever pray Etc.

Wm. Wilford Jno Humphreys
