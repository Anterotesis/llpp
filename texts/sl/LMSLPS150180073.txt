To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Robert Bedingfeld Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. The rest of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace In the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Bartholomew the Greate London

Sheweth That one Mary Hawinks a Singlewoman by virtue of a Warrant Dated 8th May 1707 under the hands and Seales of twoe of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace within the City of London (one being of the Quor) was brought by the Churchwardens of the Parish of St. Buttolph without Aldersgate to yor. Petrs. to be Provided for upon the Oath of the said Mary That Shee was last legally settled in the said Parish of St. Bartholomew the Greate where about 2 Yeares since Shee lived as an hired Servant for about 2 Yeares with Mr. Wyatt a Baker in Duck Lane in the said Parish And had Obteyned noe other legall settlement since Whereas in truth the said Mary since her Departure from Mr. Wyats Service hath dwelt with one Mr. Willis att Chelsea in Middx as an hired Servt. for the space of one whole yeare and upwards att Â£3. yearely wages And is Settled by Law in the said Parish of Chelsea

Yor. Petrs. humbly Pray That their Appeale may be heard And that the said Mary Hawkins may be sent back to the said Parish of St. Buttolphs Aldersgate To be there provided for by them untill They can Free themselves of the Charge of keeping her by due Course of Lawe

And They shall Pray Etc
