To the Right Honble. Marshe Dickinson Esqr. Lord Mayor Sr. William Moreton Knt. the Recorder and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Libertys thereof at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Mary White Chapel in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a Pass or order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of Thomas Rawlinson and Francis Cokayne Esqrs. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing Date the third day of June last Phillis Whitehead wife of John Whitehead who is a Sailor in his Majesty's Service and their Child named John aged Two Years was removed from the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldersgate in the said City to the said Parish of St. Mary White Chapel as the Place of their last legal Settlement.

That Your Petitioners and Appellants apprehending themselves to be aggrieved by the said order of the said Two Justices Do appeal to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioners and Appellants therefore pray that this Court will appoint a day this present Session for the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Botolph Aldersgate and all other Persons concerned to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment & Determination of the same touching this Appeal and that your Petitioners and Appellants may be relieved as this Court shall see meet

And Your Petitioners & Appellants shall every pray Etc.

Jno. Billington Agent for Appellts. July 7th. 1757.
