To the Right Honble Sr Peter Delme Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of his Maties Justices of the peace for ye sd City at their Genl. Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Nicholas Coleabby London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrt and the hands & Seals of two of his Majties Justices of the peace for the sd City (Quor unus) bearing Date the 6th day of October instant 1724 Prudence the Widdow of Henry Johnson deced with Mary her daughter aged abt Seventeen Years were passed & removed from the parish of St Peters near Pauls Wharf London [---] to the sd parish of St Nicholas Coleabby London Alledging the sd parish to be the place of their [...] present Legal Settlement

That your Petitionrs conceive themselves aggrieved by the sd Warrt & Judgmt of the sd two Justices and do most humbly Appeal to your Honr. and Worps. therefrom

Your Petrs therefore humbly pray that yr Honour & Worps. will be pleased to hear them in ye premisses and order that the Church Wardens & Overseers of the poor of ye sd parish of St Peters near Pauls Wharf may Appear before yr Honour & worps. & hear & abide the determinacon of this Court touching the said Appeal

And they Shall ever pray Etc
