To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City of the General Quarter Session of the peace for the said City assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Ellingham in the County of Southampton

Sheweth That by virtue of of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of Sir Watkin Lewes Knight Mayor of the City of London and Sir Thomas Hallifax Knight one of the Aldermen of the said City two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said and Liberties thereof one being of the Quroum bearing Date the Ninth day of March last Peggy the Wife of James Bronna Sailor and her Son named James aged Twelve Months were removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Bride in the City of London to the said Parish of Ellingham in the said County of Southampton as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby Your petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and humbly appeal against the Same

Therefore your petitioners humbly pray that the Benefit of their said Appeal may be saved unto them and that a Day may be appointed by this Honourable Court for the hearing and Determination thereof And that such Order may be made therein as to this Honourable Court shall Seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
