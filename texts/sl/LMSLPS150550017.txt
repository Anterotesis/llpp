To the Right Honble Robert Westley Esquire Mayor of the City of London and others his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said City at their General Quarter Session of the peace Assembled.

The Humble Peticon of Sarah Booth.

Sheweth , That Your peticoner was put Apprentice by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex to Ann Morgan of the parish of Saint Andrew Holbourne in the City of London Widow (to learn her Art Trade and Mistery that she now useth) by a certain Indenture bearing date the Twelfth day of October last for the Term of Twelve Years as by an Examined Copy of the said Indenture hereunto Annexed doth appear the Original now remaining in the Workhouse of the parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex aforesaid.

That Your Peticoners said Mistress doth unlawfully beat and abuse her and doth not find her with Sufficient Meat Drink and other Necessarys as by Indenture of Apprenticeship she ought and is oblidg'd to do, but on the contrary hath beat and almost Starved your said peticoner to death the she hath not been bound but abt. three Months.

Your Peticoner therefore Humbly prays this Honble Court will take her deplorable Case into Consideration and be pleased to order her to be discharged from her said Apprenticeship or grant her such other releif in the premisses as Your Lordship and Worships shall seem meet and also that her said Mistress may return the Sum of Forty five Shillings back to the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the aforesaid parish of Saint James Clerkenwell she had of them in Consideration of taking your said peticoner Apprentice in order to her being to some other person.

And Your Peticoner (as in Duty bound) shall ever pray Etc
