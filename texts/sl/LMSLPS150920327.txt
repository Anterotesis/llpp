To the Right Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the City of London in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George Southwark in the Country of Surrey.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Stands and Seals of James Townsend and Wm Plomer Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 23rd day of August One Thousand Seven hundred and Eighty One Jane the Widow of John Habin was Removed from the Parish of Saint Edmond the King in the said City of London unto the said Parish of Saint George Southwark alled to the said Parish of Saint George Southwark to be the place of her last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices, do Therefore appeal to your Worships there from and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them on the Premise and order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of saint Edmond the King in the City of London may appear before you to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of Your Worships touching the Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall Ever pray etc

Pra: Vincent Solicitor for the Appelants
