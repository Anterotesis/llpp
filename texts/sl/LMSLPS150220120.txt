To the Rt Honrble Sr Robert Beachcroft Knt Lord Maior of the City of London and the Rt Worpll the rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace in the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St Swithin London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of Two of her Maties Justices of the peace within this City (one being of the Quoru) bearing Date the 25th. Day of September last past One Henry Wilcox with Elizabeth his Wife and Three Children were removed from the parish of Allhallows on London Wall unto the said parish of St Swithin alleadging the said parish of St Swithin to be the place of the last legall settlement of the said Henry Wilcox.

That your Petitioners doubt not but to prove that the said Henry Wilcox never had any legal Settlement in the said Parish of St Swithin whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and to humbly appeal to your Honr: and Worpps therefrom.

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray your Honr. an Worpps. will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Allhallows on London Wall may appear before you and hear and and abide the Determination of your Honr and Worpps touching this Appeale

And your Petitionr shall ever Pray Etc

Peter Way Ch Sanderson} Church Wardens

St Swithin agt Allhallows on ye Wall} Appeale
