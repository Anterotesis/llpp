To the Right Honble Sr Charles Peers Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Right Worpll the rest of his Majestys Justices of the peace in the Generaral Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled

The humble Petition of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the poor of the Severall parishes of St Giles without Cripplegate St Botolph without Algate St Andrew Wardrobe St Katherine Coleman St Andrew Holborn St Sepulchres St Brides Allhallows on the Wall St Botolph Aldersgate and St Botolph without Bishopsgate.

Sheweth That your Petitioners are very much Over burthened with Poor and not able to raise Sufficient Sumes of money amongst themselves to releive them Notwithstanding their Assessments are greater than heretofore. Yet the Charge of the poor is far about their Assessments as the same upon their Examinacon hath divers times appeared to Several Worthy Aldermen of this City appointed to hear the same And their Poor daily encreasing upon them their Said parishes Some of them lying near the Suburbs and others of them in the Suburbs of this City.

Their humble Petition therefore unto your good Honour and Worships that you will be favourably pleas'd to grant unto them the Contributions of other parishes within this City for and towards the releiving the poor as they have formerly had which are Particularly here undermenconed or Such farther Allowance as to Your Honr & Worpps Shall See in meet
