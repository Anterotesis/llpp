To the Rt. Honoble Sr. Robt Beachcroft Knt. Ld. Mayr of the City of London And the Rt Worll. the rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble petition and appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Allhallows the Less London

Sheweth That one Mary Scovell Wido by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands & Seals of two of her Maties Justices of the peace within this City (one being of the Quorn) bearing date the Third day of March last past was removed from the parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London unto the said parish of allhallows the less London alleadging the said parish to be the place of the last legall settlement of the said Mary Scovell

That your Petitioners doubt not but to prove that the said Mary Scovell never had any legall Settlement in the said parish of allhallows the less whereby your petitioners conceive themselves aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeal to your Honr & Worpps therefrom

Your Petitioners most humbly pray your Honr & Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St Giles without Cripplegate may appear before you and hear & abide the determination of your Honr & Worpps touching this Appeals

And your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc

All hallows the less agt St Giles without Cripplegate} Appeal
