To the Rt. Honble: Sr Humphry Edwin Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll his Maties Justices of the Peace of the same City in the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St Peters Cornhill London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands & Seales of twoe of his Maties. Justices of the peace within this City (one being of the Quox) beareing date the 21st day of June 1698 Mary Graves widd were by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the parish of St. Buttolphswithout Bishoppsgate sent to yor. Petrs. to be pvided for upon the Oath of Anne Witherhard Widd That ye last legall Settlement of the said Mary Graves was in Leadenhall streete in yor Petr Parish where her late husband William Graves lived for Severally yeares and followed the Trade of a Frame worke knitter Whereas in truth the said William Graves never was an inhabitant or over had any legall Settlement in your Petrs. Parish to their Knowledge.

Wherefore yor. Petrs humbly Pray yor Honrs. & Worpps. to hear them all in the prmisses and to Order the Churchwardens of the Parish of St. Buttolphs without Bishoppsgate to Appeare here before you And send the said Mary Graves back againe to the Parish of St Buttolphs Bishoppsgate to be by them kept and pvided for till they can free themselves of the Charge thereof by due course of Lawe

And they Shall ever Pray Etc.
