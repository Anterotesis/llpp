To the Right Honble the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City now in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Bridget otherwise Brides in the City of London

Sheweth That Margaret Dawson Singlewoman was by in Order or Pass Warrant Dated the 20th. day of December last 1771. under the hands and Seals of the Right Honourable William Nash Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and J Bird Esqr. and Alderman of the said City Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City and [---] the Libertys thereof one being of the Quorum Removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Sepulchres London to the said Parish of Saint Bridget otherwise Brides London aforesaid as the Place of her last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners and Appellants Conceive themselves Aggrieved thereby Humbly Appeal to the Judgement of this Honourable Court against the same and Pray that this Honourable Court will be Pleased to hear and Determine the said Appeal And that all Persons Concerned may Abide the Judgement and Determination thereof and that your Petitioners may be Releived in the Premisses as this Honourable Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc

A. Bradley Vestry Clerk and Attorney for the Appellants}
