To the Right Honourable Sir William Billers Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London And the rest of the Worpfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the [...] West London

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London (Quorum unus) bearing Date the fourteenth Day of August 1733 Whereby Elizabeth Purton Widow and Relict of Thomas Purton and Mary her Child Aged three Years were removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate London into the parish of Saint Dunstan in the West London as the place of their last legall settlement

Yor Petrs. humbly conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said order or Pass Warrant Appeal Against the same and humbly Crave Such Relief in the Premisses as to this Court shall think Proper

And Yor Petrs; shall ever pray Etc
