To the Right Honrble. Henry Marshall Esqr Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of the Worshipful His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Rumford in the County of Essex

Sheweth That by virtue of An Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands & Seals of the right Honrble Henry Marshall Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London & Richd. Hoare Esqr. two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City bearing Date the twenty Ninth Day of November 1744. Mary Terry wife of Michael Terry Who in the said Order Said to have run away from her was removed from the Parish of Allhallows in the honey Lane to the Parish of Rumford in the County of Essex us the Place of her last legal Setlement.

That Your Petitioner of beleive themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order & humbly appeal against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray they May be relieved against the said Warrant or Order in Such Manner as this Honrble. Court Shall Seem Meet.

And ye. Petrs Shall ever Pray Etc

Thos: Harman Thomas Greson John Lovegrom} Churchwardens

John Gowers John Wood} Overseers
