Appeale Pish of St. Mary Mounthaw agt St. Bennett Pauls Wharfe.

To the Rt. Honble: Sr. Samuell Garrard Baronett Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt. Worpll: the Rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace in ye General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the parish of St. Mary Mounthaw London

Sheweth That one Mary Alderton wife of Anthony Alderton with Two Young Children by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands & seals of Twoe of her Maties Justices of the peace within this City of London (one being of the Quorm) bearing date the Three and Twentieth day of January last past was [...] removed from the Parish of St. Bennett Paulswharfe London unto to the said Parish of St. Mary Mounthaw alleadging the said Parich to be the Place of the lase legall Settlement of the said Mary Alderton

That your Petitioners doubt not to prove that the said Mary Alderton never had any legall settlement in the said Parish of St. [...] Mary Mounthaw whereby they conceive themselved aggreived by the Judgement of the said Two Justices and doe humbly Appeale to your Worpps therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Honr: and Worpps will be pleased to heare them in the prmisses and Order the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the said Parish of St. Bennett Paulswharfe to appeare before you and heare and abide the Determination of your Honr. & Worpps touching this Appeale

And Your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc.
