To the Right Honble: Sr. John Fryer Baront. Lord Mayr. of the City of London, and to the Rt. Worpfull the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City & the Libties thereof in their Genall Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Alhallows Staining London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands & Seales of Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and the Liberties thereof (Quox unus) bearing Date the Twenty fourth Day of January last past Elizabeth Smith Wife of Charles Smith with four Children vizt. Mary aged about Nine years, Elizabeth about Eight years, Richard about five years and Sarah about Two years were removed from the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London unto the Parish of Alhallows Staining London aforesaid, alledging the said Parish of Alhallows Staining to be the Place of their last legall Settlement

That your Petrs. Conceiving themselves Agreived by the said Warrant or Order of the said Two Justices as aforesaid Do humbly Appeale to yor. Lordship & Worps therefrom and humbly pray That yor. Lords & Worps will be pleased to hear them Concerning the Prmisses And that the Churchwarden and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of [...] Giles witht. Cripplegate London may appear [...] before you and hear & abide the Judgment [...] Determinacon of your Lordp & Worps touching this Appeall.

And your Petrs. shall ever pray Etc.
