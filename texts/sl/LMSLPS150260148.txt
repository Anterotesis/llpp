To the right honourable Sr. William Humphries Knight and Baronet Lod Mayor of the City of London & to the right worppfull the rest of his Maties Justices of the peace in the generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Church: wardens & overseers of the poor of the parish of St. Sepulchers London

Sheweth That one Dorothy Wiseback by Vertue of a Warrant under the hands & Seals of two of his Maties Justices of the peace within the City of London (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Sixteenth day of March now last past was removed from the Precinct of White Fryers in the City of London to the parish of St Sepulchres London alleadgeing the said parish is the place of the last Legall Settlement of the said Dorothy Wiseback

That your Petitioners doubt not to prove that the said Dorothy Wiseback had no Legall Settlement in the said parish of St. Sepulchres London whereby they conceive themselves aggrieved by the Judgement of the said two Justices & Do humbly appeal to your Honour & worships thereupon

Your Petitionrs therefore humbly Pray your Honour & Worships will be pleased to hear them in the prmises and Order the Overseers of the poor of the precinct of White Fryers to hear & abide the Determination of your Honour and Worships touching this appeal

And your Peticoner shall ever Pray Etc.

John Ashbridg Ch Warden Walter Bott Ch: Warden
