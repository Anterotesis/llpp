To the Right Honourable William Nash Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipfull the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Aldermanbury London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the sixth Day of June last Richard Archer Mary his Wife and their two Children, to wit John aged two Years, and Thomas aged one Year, were removed from the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate London to the said Parish of Saint Mary Aldermanbury as the Place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the Order of the said two Justices humbly appeal against the same and pray that th [...] honourable Court will please to hear and determine their said Appeal, and make such Order therein as shall be just.

And Your Petitioners shall pray Etc.
