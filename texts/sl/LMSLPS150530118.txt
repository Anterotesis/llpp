To the Right Honble Sir Robert Godschall Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London And to the Right Worshipfull the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Libertys thereof in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition & Appeal of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Andrew Undershaft London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands & Seals of two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City and Libertys thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the first day of this Instant January 1741 Elizabeth Masters, single Woman, was removed from the Parish of Saint Peters Cornhill London the said Parish of St A [...] Undershaft London alledging the said Parish of St Andrew Undershaft to be the place of her last Legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners Conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the sd two Justices Do therefore Appeal to your Lordship & Worships therefrom & humbly pray that your Lordship & Worships will be pleased to save them the benefit of this Appeal over to the next General Quarter Sessions of the peace to be holden for this City & the Libertys thereof And that the Church Wardens & Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of St Peters Cornhill London may then appear before you to hear & abide the Judgmt. & determination of your Lordsp & Wordships touching this Appeal

And your Petrs shall ever pray Etc
