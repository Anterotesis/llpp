To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Samuell Dashwood Knt. Lord Major of the Citty of London And to the Rt. Worll. Her. Maties. Justices of the Peace in the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the County of Middx

Sheweth That one Frances Day Singlewoman by Warrant of twoe Justices of the Peace one being of the Quor were brought by the Churchwardens of St. Sepulchers London to yor Petrs. To be provided for on the Oath of the said Frances That Her last Settlement was in yor. Petrs. Parish of St. James Clerkenwell where shee Lived as a third Servant with Mr. Setchell in St. Jones Court att the rate of Â£3.10s wayes P Annu in the yeare 1695 and hath Since had noe other legall settlement Whereas in Truth the said Frances Day Lived in yor Petrs. Parish about 3 Quarters of a yeare and noe longer

Their Humble Suite unto yor. Lopp. and Worpps. is To heare them in the prmisses And to send the Said Frances Day to the Churchwardens of St. Sepulchers To be provided for (untill They Dan Free themselves of the charge thereof by due Course of Lawe
