To the Right Honrble Humphry Parsons Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the peace at their Generall Quarter Sessions assembled

The Humble Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of ye poor of the parish of St. Catherine Creechurch in the sd. City of London

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order or pass Warrant Under the hands & Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of ye peace for the sd. City (Quor Unus) bearing date the Sixth day of May Anno Dni 1731 Ann Crew Widow was passed from the parish of St. Catherine Coleman London to the sd. parish of St. Catherine Creechurch London as the place of her last legall Settlement

Yor. Peticoners Conceiving themselves aggrieved by the sd. Warrant & Judgment of the sd. two Justices Humbly appeal to ye sd [...] Hnorble Court for Relief in the premisses [---] [...]

And Yor. Peticonrs shall ever pray Etc.
