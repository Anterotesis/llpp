To the Right Honourable Frederick Bull Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipful the Aldermen his Majestys Justices of the of the Peace for the same City at their General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of William Greenwood William Higginson George Hayter, Clement Samuel Strong Thomas Hoskis Littler George Alcock Richard Saubrick John Clempson and John Vasell the Younger.

Sheweth That at the General Quarter Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for the City of London at the Guild hall within the said City on Monday the Eleventh Day of July one thousand seven hundred and fifty seven his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London then and there assembled did in pursuance of an Act of Parliament passed in the thirtieth Year of the Reign of his late Majesty King George the second Intitled an Act to explain and amend an Act made in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of the same King to prevent the Misbehaviour of the Drivers of Carts in the Streets of London and Westminster and the Limits of the Weekly Bills of Mortality and for other Purposes in the said Act mentioned Did unanimously assess and rate the prices for the Carriage of all Goods which should be taken up in the said City of London and carried by any licensed Carts or Car-rooms as well in the said City of London as from the said City of London into the City of Westminster or any other place or places not exceeding the Distance of three Miles from the said City of London and amongst other Things did Ordain and set Town the Rates following to be paid for the Cartage of Goods-to wit-from any of the Wharfs between the Power and London Bridge to Dyers Hall Coal Harbour Steel yard double Hood Warchouse Lawrence Pountney Lane three Cranes Queenhith Queen Street Hill College ill Dowgate Hill and several other places therein mentioned

For a Load of dry Goods-two Shillings and six pence For a small Load-two Shillings

For an half Load-one Shilling and six pence

That your Petitioners are Renters of Warehouses in and near Queen Street wich do not exceed the

Distance from the Keys below the Bridge of Queen Hithe but as Watling Street is mentioned in a subsequent Order of the said Sessions and three Shillings ordered therein to be paid for the Carriage of a Load of Goods thereto and two Shillings and six pence for a small Load and two Shillings for a half Load Your Petitioners have been compelled to pay the last mentioned Rates for the Carriage of their Goods which are unreasonable Prices

That your Petitioners apprehend it would be more reasonable and equitable were the Order first herein before mentioned extended to Queen Street and to all the Hills Lanes and Streets between Queen Street and the Manscon House and where Watling Street is mentioned in the said subsequent Order it may be also therein declared to be Westward of Queen Street.

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that the said first mentioned Order may be extended so as to include Queen Street and all the Hills Lanes and Streets between Queens Street and the Mansion House And in the said other Order where Watling Street is mentioned it may be declared to be Westward of Queen Street and that your Petitioners may have such other Relief in the Premisses as to this Honourable Court shall seem Just.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
