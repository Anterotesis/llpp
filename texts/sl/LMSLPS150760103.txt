To the Right Honble: the Lord Mayor the Recorder and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the peace of the City of London in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace at the Guildhall of the said City Assembled on Monday the fiftenth Day of April one Thousand seven hundred and Sixty five

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Mary White Chapel in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant dated the twenty seventh Day of March last under the Hands and Seals of her of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City Deborah Smith Singlewoman was Removed from the Parish of Saint Mary Aldermary within the said City to the said Parish of St Mary White Chapel in the said County of Middlesex as the place of her last Legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners Apprehend and are advised that the last Legal Settlement of the said Deborah is not in the Parish of St Mary White Chapel

And therefore Apprehend themselves to be aggrieved by the said pass Warrant or Order of Removal and do Appeal to your Worships against the same

Your Petitioners and Appellants therefore humbly pray that Your Worships will to proceed to the hearing of this their Appeal to morrow at Eleven of the Clock, whereof the Respondants have Notice And that this their Appeal may be Allowed with Costs

April 15. 1765

And Your Appellants will pray Etc

Billington. Sol. for Appellts.
