To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Sessions of the Peace at Guildhall in and for the said City assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Peter le Poor in Broad Street London

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of Francis Cokayne Esqr. and Sir Matthew Blakiston Knt. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London one of whom is of the Quorum bearing date the 13th. day of October 1763 Thomas Spencer, Ann his Wife and their three Children Sophia Margaret aged four Years Ann Aged two Years Thomas aged five Months were removed from the Parish of Saint James at Garlickhith London to the said Parish of Saint Peter le Poor in Broad Street London as the Place of their last legal Settlement by which Order your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved

Your Petrs. therefore humbly appeal to this Honble Court And Pray that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint James at Garlickhith London and all Parties concerned may attend the said Court some day in this present Session to hear and abide the Judgment Determination of the said Court touching the said Appeal And that your Petrs. may have such Relief in the Premisses as to the said Court shall seem meet.

And your Petrs. shall ever pray Etc.
