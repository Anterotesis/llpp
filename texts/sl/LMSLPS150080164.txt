To the Right Honoble Sr. Edwd. Clerke Knt Lord Maior of the City of London And the Right Worll. the Rest of his Maties. Justices of the Peace all the Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The humble petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers the Poore of the Parish of St. Brides London

Sheweth That by virtue of A Warrant [...] hands and Seales of twoe of his Maties Justices of the Peace (one being of [...] one Jane Meeres Singlewoman a poore vagrant loose person have [...] inhabitation or lodgeing nor place of abode or settlement but as a vagrant [...] menconed in the warrant) was sent to yor. Petr. by the Oath of one [...] Vandertall her Sister That her last settlement was in the Parish of St. [...] where she lodged att the house of one Pilkington in one of the Alledeading to Shoelane in the Pish of St. Brides att the time of the Death of King Charles the second in the yeare 1684/5 And that the said Jane hath not had any settlement since as she beleives whereas in truth she being Vagrant should have been sent to the place of her birth which was in the Pish of St. Andrew Holbourne London where her Father dwelt and where she was alsoe a servant wth. one Edwd. meered. since that time for seaven yeares together till about two yeares past where she was legally settled as a hired servant.

Yor. Petr. therefore Prays yor. Honors. and Worpps. to hear their Appeale in the Prmisses And to Quash the said Warrant And send her to the Parish of St. Andrew Holborne to be Pvided for being the place of her birth and her last legall settlement as a hired servant

And They shall pray etc.
