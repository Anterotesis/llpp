To the Right Honble Francis Cokayne Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London And the Rest of her Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City [...] in their General Quarter Session of the Peace at Guildhall in for the sd. City assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Warden & Overseers of the Poor of the sd Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the County of of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of the said Lord Mayor and Robt. Alsop Esqrs. two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the sd. City and the Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the 12th. day of Novr. 1750 Moses Thomas Ann his Wife and their two Children Robert aged two years & Bernard Frederick aged Seven Months were removed from the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate London to the sd. Parish of St. Andrew Holborn in the County of Middx as the Place of the last Legal Settlemt. of the said Moses with his said Wife and two Children afsd. Whereby your Petitioners think themselves to be aggrieved

Joshua Cox Sollr for the Appellts: Therefore they Humbly Appeal to this Honble Court And Pray that the sd. Court will be Pleased to Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the sd Parish of St. Botolph without Aldgate and all Persons concerned do attend this Court some day in this present Session to hear & abide the Judgmt. & Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal Etc. And yor. Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.
