To the Right Honble Frederick Bull Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipfull the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the twenty Eighth Day of January last Charles Stark and Mary his Wife were removed from the Parish of Saint Giles Cripplegate within the City of London to the said Parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street as the place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the Order of the said two Justices humbly Appeal against the same And pray that this Honble Court will please to hear and determine the said Appeal and make such Order therein as shall be Just.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
