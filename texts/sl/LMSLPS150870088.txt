To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London at the Quarter Sessions of the Peace of our Lord the King holden for the said City

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Martin Outwich in the City of London

Sheweth That by [...] of an Order of Removal or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Sir Thomas Hallifax Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London and William Lee Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City bearing date the eleventh day of December one thousand seven Hundred and Seventy Six a Woman in the said Order called Rebecca Seager Singlewoman with her three Children named Jonathan, Sarah and Robert for Nurture were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Precinct of White Fryers in the City of London unto the said Parish of Saint Martin Outwich the said Justices thereby adjudging the Place of the last last legal Settlement of the said Rebecca Seager to be in the said Parish of Saint Martin Outwich

That the said Woman called Rebecca Seagor is the lawfull Wife of John Warwick and your Petitioners apprehend that her Settlement is not in the said Parish of Saint Martin Outwich and therefore your Petitioner think themselves aggrieved by the said Order of Removal or Pass Warrant

Your Petitioners therefore appeal from the said Order of Removal of the said two Justices and humbly pray that the Healing of the said Appeal may be adjourned unto the next Sessions of the Peace and that your Worships will be pleased to appoint a Day in that Sessions to hear and determine the said Appeal
