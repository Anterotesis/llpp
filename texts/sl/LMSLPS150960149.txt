To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor of the City of London, and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their general Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Genebel and Ely Rents in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the County of Middlesex,

Sheweth That by an Order under the Hands and Seals of Robert Peckham and Benjamin Hamnet Esquires two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace in and for the said City of London (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the first day of October instant Elizabeth Simmonds Singlewoman (with her two Bastard Children namely William aged three Years and Mary aged Eighteen Months for nurture) was removed and conveyed from the Parish of St. Dunstan in the west in the City of London, to the Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Elizabeth Simmonds Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore appeal against the said Order and humbly pray Relief in the premisses and that your Worships will be pleased to appoint some day in this present Session for the Hearing and determining this their said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Cox, Soll for Appellants.
