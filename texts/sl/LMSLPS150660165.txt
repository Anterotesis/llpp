To the Right Honble Thos: Rawlinson Esqr [...] Lord Mayor and the rest of the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Magdalen Bermondsey in the County of Surry

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Warrant Under the Hands and Seales of Thomas Rawlinson and Francis Cokayne Esquires Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the Said City of London and the Libertys thereof (One being of the Quorum) bearing Date the twenty Seventh day of the February One Thousand Seven hundred and Fifty four William Staples Elizabeth his Wife and their two Children Hannah aged Eight years and Elizabeth Aged five years were removed from out of the parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate London unto the said Parish of Saint Mary Magdalen Bermondsey as the place of their last legal Settlement

yor. Petitionrs Conceiveing themselves Aggreived by the Said Order or Warrant humbly Appeale against the Same unto this Court And pray your Worships to grant them Such Relief in the premisses as shall be thought most equitable And yor. Petr shall ever pray Etc

Jos: Willshire Jn Davy} Church wardens

Charles Coates Jno. Undershell Rob Smith} Overseers of the Poor
