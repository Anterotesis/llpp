To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and the Rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the City of London and the Liberties thereof at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humle Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Trinity Minories In the Liberty of his Majesty's Tower of London.

Sheweth That by an Order of Removal bearing Date the 23d. Day of March last under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Frederick Bull Lord Mayor of the City of London and Robert Peckham Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof, one being of the Quorum Mary the Widow of Christopher Stevens, whose Settlement is Unknown and her Three Children Mary Aged Eleven Years, Thomas Aged Nine Years and James Aged Six Years were Removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Allhallows Barkin in the City of London to the said Parish of Trinity Minories as the Place of the last Legal Settlement of the said Mary Stevens and her Three Children Mary, Thomas, and James,

Your Petitioners therefore Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order of Removal Do Humbly Appeal to this Court against the same Praying such Relief in the Premisses as to this Court shall seem meet

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

Saml Ravenscroft for the Appellants
