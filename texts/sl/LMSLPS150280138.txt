To the Right Honble Sr James Bateman Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London And to the Right Worpll the Rest of his Matys Justices of the peace for the City of London in the General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

8/10 The humble Petition and Appeal of the ChurchWardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Peters [...] near Pauls Wharfe London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Matys Justices of the peace within this City (one being of the Quor) bearing Date the Eighth Day of October Inst Willm Caoke with Ann his wife and three Young Children Ann Willm Sarah Cooke were removed from the Parish of St Bennett at Pauls Wharfe London unto the said Parish of St Peters near Pauls Wharfe Alleadging the sd Parish to be the place of their last Legal Settlement.

That your Petitionrs conceive themselves agrieved by the said Warrant and Judgment of the sd two Justices and do most humbly appeal to your Honour & Worpps therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray Your Honour and Worps will be pleased to hear them in the premisses And that the ChurchWardens & Overseers of the poor of the Sd Pish of St Bennitts at Pauls Wharfe may Appear before you & hear and abide the Determinacon of your Honour & Worps touching this Appeal

And they Shall ever pray Etc
