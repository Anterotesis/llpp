To the Rt Honrble Sr Charles Peers Knt Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt. Worpll: the Rest of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said City in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Margaret Lothbury London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or pass Order under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the City of London (Quorn Unns) [---] bearing date the Eleventh Day of September last past Rebeccah Paine with John Paine her son were removed from the parish of St Mary Staining London unto the said Parish of St. Margarett Lothbury alleadging the said parish to be the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petrs. doubt not but to prove that the said Rebecca Paine never had any legal Settlement in the said parish of St. Margaret Lothbury whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeal to your Worpps therefrom

Your Petrs therefore most humbly pray your Honr and Worpps that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Parish of St. Mary Staining may appeal before you and hear and abide the determination of your Lon [...] & Worpps touching this appeal

And they Shall Ever pray Etc.
