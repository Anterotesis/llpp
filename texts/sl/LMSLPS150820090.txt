To the Right Honourable Brass Crosby Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof in their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Sepulchre London.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of An Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands Seale of the Right Honourable Brass Crosby Esquire Lord Mayor and Sir Richard Glyn Baronet Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace is and for the said City of London and Liberties theresft One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty forth Day of November 1770 Elizabeth Sumner (Wife of Isaac Sumner (who is Patient in Saint Bartholomew Hospital in West Smith field London) and their Two Children Namely Margaret Aged Twenty Months and Mary Aged Seven Months were Removest from the Parish of Saint Ann in Black Fryers London to the said Parish of Saint Sepulchre London as the Place of their Lord Legal Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore Thinking themselves Aggrieved by the said Order of the said Two Justices at the Pence Appeal to this Court against the same, And that the Benefit of this Appeal may 6 Served unto your Petitioners and that the Henning and Determination thereof May be Adjourned untill the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City

And your Petitioners shall Ever Pray.

Toethaker Sollr. for St. Sepulchers London}
