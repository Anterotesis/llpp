To the Right Honble Sr Willm Leiven Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Right Worpll the Rest of his Matys Justices of the peace in the General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Several parishes of St Giles witht Cripplegate St Botolph without Aldgate St Andrew Wardrobe St Katherine Colemen St Andrew Holborn St Sepulchres St Brides Allhallows on the Wall St. Botolph Aldersgate ahd St Botolph without Bishopsgate.

Sheweth That your Petitioners are very much Overburthened with poor and notable to raise Sufficient Sums of money amongst themselves to releive them not withstanding their Assessments are greater then heretofore Yet the Charge of the poor is far above their Assessments as the Same upon their Examination hath divers times appeared to Several Worthy Aldermen of this City appointed to hear the same and their poor dayly encreasing upon their Said parishes some of them lying near the Suburbs and others of them in the Suburbs of this City

Their humble Petition therefore unto your good Honour and Worships is that you will be favourably pleased to Grant unto them the Contributions of other parishes within this City for & towards Releiving the poor as they have formerly had which are particularly hereunder menconed or Such farther Allowance as to your Honr and Worps shall Seen meet
