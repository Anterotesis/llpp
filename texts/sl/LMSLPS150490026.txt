To the Right Honble Sir John Barnard Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London & the rest of the Honble Bench at Justice Hall in the Old Bailey in the Suburbs of the Said City of London

The Humble Prayer of Thomas Crosse

Sheweth That your Supplicant was on the 18th. day of March last taken up on Suspicon of being Concerned in Forging two Several Notes in order to Receive Several Sums of Money from Messrs. Hoare & Co:

That your Supplicant hath Continued in Custody Ever Since the Said 18th. day of March last on the above menconed Charge

Your Supplicant Therefore humbly Prays That he might Either be tried, Bailed, [...] otherwise Discharged out of Custody this Sessions as Shall Seem most meet to this Honble Court

And yor. Supplicant as in Duly bound Shall Ever pray Etc

Thos: Crosse

Entred 18. May 1738.
