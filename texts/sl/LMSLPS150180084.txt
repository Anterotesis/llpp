To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Robert Bedingfeld Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. The Rest of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace In the Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of Christchurch London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace within the City of London (Quor unus) dated the 18th. February 1706 One Susanne Lee Widdow together with her twoe Children were sent by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Olaves Hart Streete London to yor. Petrs. to be provided for upon the Oath of the said Susanne that in her husbands life time shee Lived in St. Martins Le Grand in yor. Petrs. Parish in a house of Twelve Pounds P Annu & continued therein for the Space of twoe yeares & more which was the place of Her last legall settlement Whereas in truth the said Susanne Lee nor her Husband ever was an Inhabitant in yor Petrs. Parish or lived in an house of Â£12. a yeare Nor is St. Martins Le Grand in the Bounds of yor. Petrs. Parish

Yor. Petrs. therefore humbly Pray yor. Honor. & Worpps. to hear them in the Prmisses & Order the Churchwardens & Overseers of St. Olaves Hart Streete London to appear here before You to Shew Cause why they should not Provide for the said Susanne Lee & her twoe Children And to Quash the said Warrant made by the said twoe Justices And send the said Susanne & her Children back to the Churchwardens of St. Olves Hart streete London To be by them kept & provided for untill They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Lawe

And They shall Ever Pray Etc
