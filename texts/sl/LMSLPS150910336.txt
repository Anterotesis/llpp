To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace assembled at the Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden at Guildhall in and for the City of London

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Saviour Southwark in the County of Survey

Sheweth That B: Kenneth Esquire Mayor and Robert Peckham Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and Liberties thereof upon Complaint made unto them by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Martin within Ludgate in the City of London that Margaret the Widow of George Blockley deceased had come to reside in their said Parish of Saint Martin within Ludgate not having obtained any legal Settlement there and was likely to become chargeable thereto did by an Order or Warrant of Removal under their Hands and Seals bearing Date the thirteenth Day of July last order the said Margaret Blockley to be removed and conveyed from the said Parish of Saint Martin within Ludgate unto the said Parish of Saint Saviour as the Place of her last Legal Settlement.

That the said Margaret Blockley by Virtue of the said Order has been removed and conveyed from the said Parish of Saint Martin within Ludgate to the said Parish of Saint Saviour where she now remains chargeable to the same Parish

Your Petitioners apprehending themselves aggrieved by the said Order or Warrant of Removal made by the said two Justices as aforesaid do therefore humbly appeal against the same
