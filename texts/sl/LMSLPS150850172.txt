London (to wit) To the Right Honourbale the Lord Mayor and their Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Chipping Ongar in the County of Essex

Sheweth. That by a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Frederic Bull Esquire Lord Mayor of the said City and Richard Oliver Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City, two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City and Liberties thereof (one of them being of the Quorum) bearing Date the fourth day of July last past, Sarah Travell Aged Thirteen Years Thomas Travell Aged Eleven Years and John Travell Aged Nine Years were Ordered to be Removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Martin in Iron Monger Lane in the said City of London unto the Parish of Chipping Ongar aforesaid as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners Conceive themselves Aggrieved

Your Petitioners Therefore Humbly Appeal to this Court against the said Pass Warrant or Order of the said two Justices
