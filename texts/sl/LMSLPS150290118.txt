To the Rt Honrble Sr William Lewen Knt. Lord Major of the city of London And to the Rt Worpll: the Rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their general quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens, & Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St Stephen in Coleman Street London

Sheweth That by a Pass warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of two of his Maties Justices of the Peace for the City of London (Quor Unus) bearing date the Eighteenth Day of September last past Mary Clarke wife of John Clark with four Children were removed from the Parish of St [...] Giles without Cripplegate London unto the said Parish of St Stephen in Coleman Street alleadging the said Parish to be the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petrs conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said warrant or Order of the said two Justices Do most humbly appear to your Honr and Worpps therefrom And pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of St Giles without Cripplegate may appear before you And hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Honr and Worpps touching this Appeal

And they shall ever pray Etca
