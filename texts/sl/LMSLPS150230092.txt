To ye Rt. Honble. Sr. Robert Beachcroft Lord Major of ye City of London And other ye St. Worshipfull her Masties Justices of ye peace of ye said City at their General Quarter Sessions of ye peace held for the Sd. City.

The Humble Peticon of ye Churchwardens and Overseers of ye poor of ye Parish of St. Saviour Southwark in ye County of Surry

Humbly Sheweth That Mary Harrison ye wife of James Harrison with her three Children were. Since ye holding of ye last Generall Quarter Sessions of ye peace for ye City of London removed by an Order under ye hands and Seals of two of her Majties Justices of ye. peace of ye sd. City from ye parish of St. Anne Blackfryars London to ye parish of St. [...] Saviour Southwark aforsaid as ye place of their last legall settlement gained by ye sd. James Harrison's living as an hired Servt. by ye Year for several Years together with one Mr. Allen a Tayler in the said Parish of St. Saviour Southwark

That your peticoners can make it appear unto this Honble Court that the said James Harrison never gained a Settlemt. by living [...] with ye sd. Mr. Allen as an hired Servant by ye Year in the said parish of St. Saviour Southwark.

Your peticoners therefore do humbly appeal against ye sd. Order And pray that this Honble Court will order ye Churchwardens and Overseers of ye poor of ye Parish of St. Anne Blackfryars London to attend this Court to Show Cause why ye said Order should not be vacated.

And your Peticoners Shall ever pray Etc
