To the Right Honble George Nelson Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London, and the Worshipful the Aldermen his Bretherer. his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Overseers of the poor of the precinct of White Friars in the said City of London

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City (one being of the Quorum) dated the seventh day of February last past. Ann Pinkney. Wife of John Pinkney, gone from her; and their two Children Ann aged five Years, and John aged twelve weeks, were removed, from the parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate in the said City to the said Precinct of White Friars, as the place of their last legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices, and therefore humbly appeal to this honourable Court against the same and

Pray that this Honble Court will be pleased to hear and determine their said appeal and make such Order thereon as shall be just.

And your Petitioners shall pray Etc.
