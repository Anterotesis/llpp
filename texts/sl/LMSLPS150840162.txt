To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor & others his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Session of the Peace for the said City Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Bridget otherwise Brides London

Sheweth That by [...] Order under the Hands and Seals of James Townsend Esqr Lord Mayor & Nathaniel Thomas Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one Whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Third day of February Instant Millicent Hill wife of John Hill a Prisoner for Debt in the Bultry Compter and their three Children to wit William aged 6 years David aged Four years and Thomas aged one year were removed and Conveyed from the Precinct of White Fryers London aforesaid to the said Parish of Saint Bridget otherwise Brides London aforesaid as the Place of their legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners think themselves agrieved and Appeal to this Court againt the same

A. Bradley Solicitor for the Appellants} Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Prays this Honourable Court will Please to grant them such Relief in the Premisses as shall be thought Equitable
