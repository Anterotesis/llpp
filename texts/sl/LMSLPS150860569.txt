To the right Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Sessions of the Pearce Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Church Wardens and overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Andrew Undershaft London

Sheweth That by on order of Pass Warrant under the Hands [...] and Seals of two of his Majesty Justices of the Peace for the said City (ore being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Thirty first Day of January one Thousand Seven Hundred and Seventy five Ann Pettit Wife of Thomas Pettit who has Deserted her was removed from the Parish of Saint Katherine Coleman in the City aforesaid to the said Parish of Saint Andrew Undershaft as the Place of her last Legal Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices Do Humbly Appeal to this Court for such Relief in the Premises as to your Worships Shall see in meet

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.

Saml. Butler Attr. for the Appellants
