To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Dionis Backchurch London.

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of the Right Honourable John Wilkes Esquire Lord Mayor and Samuel Turner Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof. One being of the Quorum, bearing date the fourteenth day of February 1775. Mary Boutflower a Singlewoman, and Mary her Bastard Child aged Eighteen Months along with her for Nurture were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate without in the City of London unto the said Parish of Saint Dionis Backchurch as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Boutflower.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order appeal against the same to this Honourable Court, and pray such Relief in the Premisses as to This Honorable Court shall seem meet.

And Your Petitioners shall every pray Etc.

Rob Woodmass Josh Barby Church Wardens

James Green Henry Rutt} Overseers
