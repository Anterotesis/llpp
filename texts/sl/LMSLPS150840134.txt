To the right Honourable The Lord Mayor and others His Majestys Justices of the peace for the City of London at the General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary Matfelon otherwise Whitechapel in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honourable James Townsend Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and Sr. Robert Ladbroke Knight two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said City of London (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 30th. Day of December 1772 John Siddall Margaret his Wife and their four Children to wit John Aged Seven Years, Elizabeth aged 6 Years, Eleanor aged 4 Years, and William aged 2 Years, were removed from the parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate London to the Parish of Saint Mary at White Chapel in the County of Middlesex as the place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order of Removal do humbly appeal to this Court agt. the same praying such Relief in the Premises as to this Court shall seem meet And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

J: Coverly for the Appellants
