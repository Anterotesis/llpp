To the Right Honrble Thos. Rawlinson Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London To Mr. Recorder and the Rest of the Worshipfull the Alderman of the said City in their Genl. Quarter Session of the Peace in and for the said City Assembled

The Humble Petition of Arthur Hand Citizen and Patten Maker of London

Sheweth That yor. Petr. on Monday the 7th. day of October last at the one of the Pepper Alley in the Borough of Southwark hired a Hackney Coach and went therein to the Artichoak Inn in the said Borough and from there returned [...] to his own house in Abchurch Lane in London aforesaid

That Yor. Petr. for the Hire thereof paid the driver of the said Coach 2s. which he received and took of and from Yor. said Petr.

That Yor. Petr. the next day received a Summery Commanding Yor. Petr. to attend the Lord Mayor at his Mansion house in and for the said City on the Thursday following

That Yor. Petr. in Obedsence there to attended his Lordship who did Adjudge and determine that the said Coach hire amounted to 2s.6d and for non payment thereof be and Yor. Petr. over to the then next following Quarter Session

Yor. Petr. Conceiving himself agrieved thereby Doth humbly appeal agt. the same and prays such relief in the premisses as to Yor. Lordship and Worshipss it shall seem meet

Arthur Hand
