To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor and the rest of the Worshipful Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their Sessions Assembled April 1776

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Frederick Bull and William Plomer Esquires Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Thirtieth day of March One Thousand seven hundred and seventy six Jane Doran the Widow of John Doran was removed and Conveyed from and out of the parish of Allhallows London wall in the City of London to the said parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the County of Middlesex as the place of her last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners thinking themselves aguired by the said Order or pass Warrant of the said two Justices, Appeal to this Court against the same, and desire you will pleasd to appoint a Day for the hearing & determining thereof, and that all Persons Concerned may then attend.

Caleb Athinson Wm Atwilk John Norgrove Jno Tilbury.} Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor.
