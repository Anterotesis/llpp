To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the Bars in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That Sarah the Widow of John King deceased and her Daughter Mary aged about Nine Years were by an Order of Removal or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of The Right Honourable T. Wright Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and Hen: Kitchin Esquire Alderman of the said City Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty ninth Day of November last removed and conveyed from and out of the parish of Saint Michael Bassishaw London to the said Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn above the Bars in the said County of Middlesex as the place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners and Appellants concieving themselves aggrieved thereby humbly appeal to the Judgment of this Honourable Court against the the same and pray that they will be pleased to hear and determine the said Appeal and that all persons concerned may abide the Judgment and Determination thereof and that your Petitioners may be relieved in the Premises as to this Honourable Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
