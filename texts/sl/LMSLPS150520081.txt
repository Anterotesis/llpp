To the Right Honrble Humphry Parsons Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of a London And the rest of the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justice of the Peace in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Clement Eastcheap within the said City of London

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order on Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of this Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Ninth day of December 1740. [...] Richard Lead beater Barbara his Wife and their Two Children Susanna Aged Five years and Elizabeth Aged one Year were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Martin Orgars within the said City of London into the Parish of Saint Clement Eastcheap within the said City of a London the Place of their last legal Setlemt.

Yor Petrs therefore Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant Humbly Appeal Agt. the same And pray such Relief in the Premisses As to this Honrble Court it shall from Meet

And Yor Petrs shall ever Pray Etc

Jno. Finch Wm. Rassell} Churchwardens and Overseer
