To the Rt. Honoble Sr. Robt. Beachcroft Knt. Ld. Mayr. of the City of London & to she Rt. Worll the rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace within the same City in the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

1712 4/1 The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. Mary Woolnoth London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and seals of two of her Maties Justices of the peace within this City (one being of the Quorm) bearing Date the Fourth day of January instant one Mary Harman Wido was removed from the parish of St Giles without Cripplegate London unto the said parish of St Mary Woolnoth alleadging the said parish to be the place of the last legall settlement of the said Mary Harman

That your Petitioners doubt not but to prove that the said Mary Harman never had any legall settlement in the said Parish of St. Mary Woolnoth whereby they conceive themselves' aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices [---] and do humbly Appeal there from

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray your Honr and Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate may appeare before you and hear and abide the determination of your Honr and Worpp therefrom

And your Petitioner Shall ever Pray
