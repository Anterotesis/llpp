To the Right Honourable Thomas Wright, Esquire, Lord Mayor of the City of London, and the Worshipful the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City, at their General Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Michael at Queenhith. London.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrand under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City, (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the twenty Eighth day of July last past Nathan Bingham was removed from the Parish of Saint Martin, Vintry, London to our said Parish of Saint Michael at Queenhith, as the Place of his last legal Settlement, whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

And therefore Pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them, and that the Hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned untill the next general Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden For the said City

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc.
