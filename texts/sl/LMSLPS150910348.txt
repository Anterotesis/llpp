To the Right Honble the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of Brackley Kennett and Richard Clark Esquires two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof bearing Date the 15th. Day of January last Sarah aged thirteen Years Joseph aged Ten Years and William aged Five Years three of the Children of Richard Allen a Soldier in his Majestys Service by Sarah his late Wife deceased were removed from the Precinet of White Fryars in the City of London to the said Parish of Saint James Westminster as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceived themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal to this Court agt. the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and Pray that [...] this Court will appoint some day of in this present Session for the thearing and Determining this Appeal

And Your Petrs. shall ever Pray Etc.
