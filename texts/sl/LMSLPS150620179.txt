To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace Assembled at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace holden of Guildhall in and for the City of London

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. George the Martyr Southwark in the County of Surry

Sheweth That the right honble Francis Cockayne Esqr: Lord Mayor of the City of London and Robert Alsop Esqr. One of the Aldermen of the said City two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City upon Complaint made to them by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. BotolphBishop gate London That Sarah Perry Widow of Robert Perry Deceased was lately come into the said Parish Endeavouring to Settle there contrary to Law Did by an Order or Warrant of Removal Under their hands and Seals Dated the 10th. day of April 1751 Order the said Sarah Perry to be removed and Conveyed from the said Parish of St. Botolph Bishopgate to the said Parish of St. George in Southwark as the place of her last legall Settlement

That the said Sarah Perry by Virtue of the said Order hath been removed and Conveyed from the said Parish of St Botolph Bishopgate to the said Parish of St George Southwark where She now remains chargeable to the same Parish

Your Petitioners Apprehending themselves Aggrieved by the said Orders or Warrant of Removal made by the said two Justices as Aforesaid Do therefore humbly Appeal against the same

Thos: Wakeling tho Andrews} Ch Wardens

Saml Craft Wm Ashmere} Overseers
