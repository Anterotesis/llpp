To the Right Honble James Townsend Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London And the Rest of the Worshipful Justices of the Peace at the General Quarter Session of the Peace held at Guildhall in the said City.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Vedast als Foster London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London bearing Date the tenth day of September 1773. William Paine a Lunatick and his two Children Sarah aged nine Years and Martha aged five Years were removed from the said Parish of St Giles Cripplegate London into the said Parish of St Vedast alias Foster London as the Place of their last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners humbly conceive themselves Aggrieved by so much of the said Order or Pass Warrant as relates to the removal of the said Two Children and humbly Appeal against the same.

And your Petrs. shall ever pray Etc

B. Parkes Agent for the Appellants Bartho. Close No 49.
