To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and other his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace for the said City at Guildhall assembled.

The Humble Petition of Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mildred the Virgin in the Poultry London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Settlement or Removal under the hand and Seals of the Right Honourable his Robert Kite Knight Lord Mayor of the said City and Samuel Turner Esqr. one of the Aldermen of the said City and two of his Majestys Justices of the peace of the said City bearing Date the first Day of April 1767 Thomas Wadsworth was removed from the Parish of Saint Peter Le poor in Broad Street London to the said Parish of Saint Mildred as the place of his last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggreived

Therefore they Humbly appeal to this Honourable Court against the said Order of the said Justices and may this Court to make such order therein as to them shell been meet.

And your Petitioners shell ever pray Etc.

John Farrell Thomas Foster Thos. Curtis} Churchwardens

W Edmond} Overseers
