To the Right Honble Brass Crosby Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Worshipful Justices of the Peace at the General Session of the Peace now Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Bartholomew the Great London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the 28th. day of November 1770 Thomas Johnson Isabella his Wife and their Child Hannah aged three years Were removed from and Out of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London unto the said Parish of St. Bartholomew the Great London as the place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore Conceive themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant and humbly Appeal against the Same And Pray that this Honble Court will be pleased to Adjourn the hearing and determining of the said Appeal until the next general Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City and to make Such Order in the Premisses as shall Seem Meet.

B Parker Vestry Clk. & Agent for the sd Parish
