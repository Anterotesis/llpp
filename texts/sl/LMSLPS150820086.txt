To the Right Honourable Brass Crosby Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City and Liberties thereof in their general Sessions of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the parish of Tenbridge in the County of Kent

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Seventeenth day of January last Elizabeth Barnaby Widow and her two Children to with Ann aged Nine years and Charles aged two years were removed from the Parish of Saint Ann Blackfriars London to the said Parish of Ten bridge the said Justices thereby adjudging that the last legal Settlement of the said Elizabeth Barnaby and Ann and Charles her Children was in the said Parish of Tenbridges

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said Justices and do humbly appeale therefrom but for as much as your Petitioners are not yet prepared to presents this said Appeal

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that your Worships would be pleased to adjourn the hearing of this Appeal to the next General Quarter Session of the peace to be holden for the said City of London and to make such Order therein as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Geo: Punnett Solicitor for the Appellants
