To the Right Honoble. Sr. William Ashurst Knt. Lord of the City of London And to the Right Worll. the rest of their Maties. Justices of the Peace of the same City

The humble Peticon And Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Pish of St. Butt Bishoppgate Lond

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or order under the Hands & Seales of twoe of their Maties. Justices of the Peace wth. in this City (one being of the Quor) one Elizabeth Tate and Susan her Daughter were lately sent to yor. Petr. to be pvided for and mainteyned upon oath made that Josua Tate the husband of the said Elizth. and Father of the said Susan was for above three moneths time before May 1685 settled As a Lodger or Sojourner in the house of one Francis Babbs in Bethlem in the Said Parish of St. Butts where he continued till the time of his death whereas in truth the said Josua Tate did not inhabite in the said Parish of St. Butts for 3 Months as aforesaid but was in truth last legally settled in the Pish of St. Farthes were hee was an Appntic And there had his last legall Settlemt. According to the Law As yor. Petrs. can fully prove before you.
