London ss. July Session 1767.} To the Right Honourable Sr Robert Kite Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Right Worshipful the Reorder and the rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City and the Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of a Warrant or order under the hands and Seals of the Right Honoble. Sir Robert Kite Knt. Lord Mayor and Sir Charles Asgill Knt & Baront two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace of the said City and the Liberties thereof bearing Date the Second Day of July instant Mary Gibson wife of Henry Gibson (who is alleged by the said order to be gone from her) and their Child Katherine Mary aged five months were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldersgate London into the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate aforesaid as the Place of their last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or order humbly appeal to this Court against the same and Pray such relief in the Premisses as this honourable Court shall Seem meet and that the benefit of their said Petition & appeal and the hearing and determining thereof be Saved unto them untill the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City and the Liberties thereof

Messieurs Clara & Bampfield Sollr. for the Appellants}

And yr Petrs. Shall ever Pray Etc
