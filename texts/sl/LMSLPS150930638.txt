To the Right Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London At their Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Stephen Walbrook his City of London.

Sheweth That by virtue of Pass Order under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing date the 27th. day of February 1782 Susannah Rooke a Singlewoman was removed from the Parish of Saint Nicholas Coleaboy in the said City [...] then Walbrook in the said City as the last Legal Place of her Settlement by which your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and appeal to this Court against the said Pass Order

Your Petitioners therefore pray your Worship will be pleased to hear and determine the matters touching their appeal and give them such relief in the premisses as to your Worship shall seem Meet.

And your Petr's shall ever pray Etc.
