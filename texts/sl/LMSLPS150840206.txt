To the Right Honourable James Townsend Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City and Liberties thereof in their General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Peter Le Poor in Broad Street London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of Your Lordship and Nathaniel Thomas Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof bearing date the Eighth day of March last past Francis Wakeling Singleman was removed from the Parish of Saint Sepulchres London to the said Parish of Saint Peter le Poor the said Justices by their said Order adjudging the last legal place of Settlement of the said Francis Wakeling to be in the said Parish of Saint Peter le Poor

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggreived by the said Order and humbly appeal therefrom

Your Petitionerss therefore most Humbly pray that your Worships would be pleased to appoint a day for hearing this Appeal at the present Sessions and to make such order therein as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall every pray Etc.
