To the Rt. Honble Sir William Calvert Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Worshipfull the rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace for the same City & the Libtys thereof in their General Quar. Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition of John James the Son of John James of the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the County of Middx Labourer

Sheweth That on the 1st. day of February 1747 your Petr. by a foreign Indenture was bound Apprentice to Peter Hulett of the Parish of St. Bartholomew the Great London Picture frame maker for the Term of Seven Years, at which time your Petitioner was under the Age of Fourteen Years.

That when your Petr. was bound to said Hulett the Sum of fifteen Pounds was paid to him by the Executor of Daniel Taintwrier Esqr. deceased being part of the Charity Money by him left to put out poor Children of the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell Apprentices.

That your Petr. Served said Hulett for the Space of Twelve Months but during that time said Hulett not only unmercifully beat Knock'd down Kick'd and Stampt on your Petr. to the Danger of his Life but Suffered and permitted a Woman who lived with him as his life frequently during that time to beat and abuse your Petr. Kick him and break his Head, but kept such a House as your Petr. might have perished for want of food had he not been relieved by the Neighbours and his Parents nor find him Sufficient Apparel nor had the said Hulett work in the said Business where by to Employ your Petr. two Days in the Week so that it was Impossible for the said Hulett to Instruct your Petr. in the said Trade so as to get his Livelyhood thereby.

Your Petr. therefore most humbly Prays Your Lordship and this Honourable Court to take his Case into Consideration and that the may be Discharged from his said apprenticeship and have such forth and other Relief in the Premisses as to your Lord and this Honourable Court Shall Seem Meet.

And your Petr. shall ever Pray etc.

John James
