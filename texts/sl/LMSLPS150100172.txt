To the Rt. Honble the Lord Major, and other his Maties Justices of the Peace at the Sessions of peace holden for the City of London, and Liberties thereof

The humble Petition of Peter Ward and John Sandford Churchwardens of the Parish of St. Buttolph without Aldergate.

Most humbly Sheweth. That Isaac and Peter Dove the Children of Richard Dove by Sarah his wife deceased are by Warrant under the hands and Seales of the Rt. Honble. the Lord Mayor and Sr. Francis Child two of the Justices of the Peace for the said City passed to the Parish aforesaid from St. Martin Outwich where they last dwelt, when in truth the said Richard nor any from him gave the sd. Parish of St. Buttolph any Notice of his intention to inhabitt there, nor ever tooke any notice of him as an Inhabitant, he having resided there onely four Monthes.

And further your Petitioners most humbly shew that Mr. Isaac Dighton marryed the Grandmother of the said Children, and holds a Considerable Estate by her, who is board to appeare here and abide the Judgment of this Court, and they are advised they are either to be passed back to the said Parish from whence they Came, or to their said Grandmother who by Law ought to take Care of them.

Yor. Petrs. therefore humbly pray they may be heard by their Conncill touching the premisses, and may be Releived therein as to your Wisdomes shall seeme meet.

And they shall ever pray Etc.
