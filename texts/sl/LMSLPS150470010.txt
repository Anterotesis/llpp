To the Right Honourable Sir Edward Bellamy Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the said City in General Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of Solomon Myers of the Parish of St Buttolph without Bishopsgate

Sheweth That your Petitioner about the Fifteenth day of March one thousand seven hundred and thirty three was put an Apprentice by Foreign Indenture to Abraham Levi the younger of London Diamond Cutter to Serve from thence for the Space of Five Years

That your Petitioner hath faithfully Served the said Levi for the Space of Two years and behaved and Demeaned himself in all things a [...] an honest apprentice, But the said Levi hath not allowed to your Petitioner Sufficient Meat Drink washing & Lodging nor taught and Instrusted your Petr in the said Trade of a Diamond Cutter as by his Agreement he is Obliged to do And hath Several times beat your Petr. very immediately without any Just occasion

Therefore your Petitioner humbly Prays that for the Causes aforesd he may be Discharged from his said Apprenticeship, and have such other Relief as to this Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc

Solomon Mayors
