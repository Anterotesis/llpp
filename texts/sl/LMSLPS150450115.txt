To the Right Honoble the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesties Justices for the Citty of London

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of the Right Honoble Sir William Billers Knight Lord Mayor of the Citty of London and Sir Robert (Bay his Knight two of his Majesties, Justices of the Peace for the said Citty and liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty fourth day of June 1734. Anne Newey Singlewoman was removed from the Parish of Saint Buttolph without Bishopgate London to the said Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Anne Newey

That Your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggreived by the said Warrant or Order and humbly Appeal against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that your Lordship and Worships will be pleased to Appoint a day of this present Sessions that all parties may Attend to hear and abide the Judgment and determinacon of this Court for and touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners (as in duty bound) shall ever pray
