To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary at Hill London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Robert Peckham Esquire Lord Mayor of the said City of London and John Hart Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City & one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Tenth day of January in the Year of our Lord 1784 Christiana the Widow of Patrick Norton was removed and conveyed by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Alhallows Barkin London from and out of the said Parish to the said Parish of Saint Mary at Hill as the Place of her Last legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly appeal against the same to your Worships and pray Relief in the Premisses and that a Day may be appointed for the hearing and determining the said Appeal.
