20 Nov April 1695

To the Right Honble: Sr Thomas Lane Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London & to the rest of the Justices of the Peace for the said City

The Humble Peticon & Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the pish of St Giles in the Feilds in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That On the seven & twentieth day of March last past Richard Clarke son of Henry Clarke was passed by warrant under the hands & seals of Sr Salathiel Lovell & Sr Edward Clarke Knts two of his Maties: Justices of the peace for the said City of London from the Pish of St. Sepulchers London to the said pish of St Giles in the feilds alleadging that the last [...] legall settlement of the said Richard Clarke was wth: his father Henry Clarke in the said pish of St Giles in the Feilds

Your Peticonr thinking themselves aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices Do appeal to this Honble: Bench And humbly pray (the prmises considered) that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the pish of St Sepulchers London may attend this Honble: Bench to hear & abide the finall Judgment & determination thereof touching this Appeal

And yor Peticonr (as in duty bound) shall evr pray Etc

8:6:95 Appeale disallowed
