To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Edwd. Clerke Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. the rest of his Maties. Justices of the Peace all The Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the parish of St. Mary Magdalens Old Fishstreete London

Sheweth That one Ellen Whitton wth. her 5 Children by Warrant under the Hands & Seales of two of his Maties. Justices of the Peace within this City (one of the Quox) Dated 18th. June 1697 were brought and left by the Churchwardens of St. Gregoryes wth. yor. Pets to be provided for on the Oath of one Samuel Walsh That William Whitton her late husband served one Aron Watts as an Appntice in yor. Petr. said Parish of St. Mary Magdalens Whereas in truth the said Aron Watts was a settled Inhitant in the said Parish of St. Gregoryes before May 1685 & the said William Whitton lived with him there as an Appntice above a yeare And that about Xpmas 1685 the said Aron Watts privately tooke a house in yor. Petr. parish of St. Mary Magdalens under ten pounds a yeare wth. out giveing any notice thereof According to the Law And the said William Whitton did continue with the said Aron Watts his Master thereabout ten monethes and then went away from him And that afterwardes the said Aron Watts returned to the said Parish of St Gregoryes where he abode three yeares dureing ye terme of his Indentures And never was a Settled Inhabitant in St. [...] Mary Magdalens according to the Law

Yor. Petrs. humble suite unto yor. Honors. & Worpps.: is That you wilbe pleased to hear them in the Prmisses And to Quash the said Warrt. made by the said two Justices And send the said Ellen Whitton & her Children Barke to the said Parish of St. Gregoryes where the said Aron Watts was legally settled And where the said William Whitton dwelt with him for above a yeare together as his Appntice.

And they shall Pray etc.
