To the Right Honble. Sir Crisp Gascoyne Knight Lord Mayor and the rest of the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the City of London.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Michael Bassishaw London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Robert Alsop Esqr. and John Blackford Esqr. two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London (one whereof is of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty Eighth day of October last Thomas Clark aged about twenty Years was removed from and out of the parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate London to the said Parish of Saint Michel Bassishaw as the Place of his last legal Settlement.

The Appellants Thick themselves aggrieved by the Said Order and therefore humbly appeal against the same and pray Your Lordship and Worships to grant them such Relief in the Premisses as Shall be thought most Equitable

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.
