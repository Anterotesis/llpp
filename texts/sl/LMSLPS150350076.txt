To the Rt Honble Sr Peter Delme Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt Worpll. the rest of his Maties Justices of the peace for the said City in their general quarter Sessions assembled

The humble Petition of Margarett Brackley Widdow

Sheweth That by an Indenture of Apprenticeship bearing date the Eighteenth day of April anno Dui 1720 Arthur Whale a poor child of the parish of St Brides in this City, was put and placed forth an Apprentice by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish unto Samuel Brackley of the Parish of St. Martin Ludgate Victualler to serve untill he the said Apprentice shall attain untill his full age of Four and Twenty years as by the said Indentures more at large may appear That Your Petitioners said husband Samuel Brackley is since deceased and the said Arthur Whale the Apprentice doth now greatly misbehave himself towards her by frequently insulting and abuseing here absenting her service night and day, purloining and imbezleing her Goods, and abuseing her customers in such Ende manner that she is greatly damaged thereby and many other undemeaners and Enormities.

Wherefore and for that the said Apprentice Doth not doe his duty to your Petitioner his Mistress she most humbly prays your Honr and Worpps will be pleased to hear her in the Prmisses and Discharge the said Apprentice from your Petr. or otherwise to deal with him as to your Honr and Worpps shall seeme meet

And your Petr. shall ever Pray Etc.
