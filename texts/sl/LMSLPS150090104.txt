To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Humfry Edwin Knt Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. the rest of his Maties. Justices of the Peace att the Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of Allhallows on ye wall London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under the Hands & Seales of two of his Maties. Justices of the Peace within this City one being of the Quox Dated the second Day of May 1698 Edmund Elizabeth William John & Jane Towes the Children of Edmund and Sarah Towes decd Were by the Churchwardens of the Parish of St. Stephen Coleman Streete brought to yor. Petr. to be pvided for on the Oath of Benjamin Johnson That the last legall Settlement of the said Edmund Towes decd was in yor. Petr. Parish where hee Dwell in a house of Twelve pounds rent P Annm And there Dyed about four yeares past Whereas in truth since the Death of the said Edmund Towes The said Elizth. Sarah his Widdow removed & Dwell in the said Parish of St. Stephen Coleman- -streete for about two yeares before he Death And was rated & assessed to pay to the Poore of the said Parish And likewise to the Scavenger As by the Parish Bookes may Appeare And alsoe kept a Chandlers Shopp in the said Parish

Yor. Petr. therefore pray yor. Honor. & Worpps. To order the said Churchwardens of St. Stephen Coleman Streete to appeare here before you And to hear them all in the prmisses And to Quash the said Warrt. And send the said Five Children back to the Parish of St. Stephen Colemanstreete To be pvided for According to the Law

And They Shall Pray Etc
