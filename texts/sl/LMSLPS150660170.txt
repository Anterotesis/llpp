To the Right Honourable Stephen Theodore Janssen Esqr. Lord Mayor Of the City of London and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Anthony Vulgarly Called Saint Antholin in the City of London

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London / One whereof being of the Quorum) Bearing Date the Sixteenth day of May 1755 Benjamin Bayley was removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Pancras in Soper Lane London into the said Parish of Saint Anthony Vulgarly Called Saint Antholin in the said City of London

Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said two Justices Do humbly Appeal against the [...] Pray such Releif in the Premisses as to this Court shall Seem meet

Fra Salt. Appellts. Attry.
