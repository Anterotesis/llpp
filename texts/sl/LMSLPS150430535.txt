To the Right Worshippfull his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the Citty of London in their general Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The hble pettition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Peter Cheap London.

Sheweth That by vertue of an Order or pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Francis Child Esqr, Lord Mayor of the Citty of London and Six Richard Hopkins Knt. Two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the Citty of London (Quor Un) bearing date the 31st. day of March Anno Dom 1732 John Willoughby Sarah his Wife and their Two Children (vizt.) John aged about Four years and George Aged about Eight Moneths were lately [...] removed from the Pish of Allhallows on the Wall London unto the said parish of Saint Peter Cheap London Alledgeing the said parish of Saint Peter Cheape to be the place of their last Legall Settlement.

That your pettitioners conceiving themselves agreived by the said Warrant or Order of the said Two Justices Doe therefore Appeal to your Worshipps therefrom and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the premes and Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of Allhallows on the Wall London may appear before you at a certains day to be Appointed to hear and abide the Judgemt & determination of your Worspps touching this Appeale

And your pettiotonars shall pray Etc.
