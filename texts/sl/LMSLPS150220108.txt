To the Rt Honrble Sr Gilbert Heathcote Knt Lord Major of the City of London and to the Rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace in the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the parish of St Michael Royal London

Sheweth That one Mary Clay Widdow of John Clay with her four Children Ann, Alice, Mary and Elizabeth by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of her Maties Justices of the peace within this City (one being of the Quoru) bearing Date the fourth Day of May last past was removed from the parish of Allhallows the Great London unto the said Parish of St Michael Royal alleadging the said parish to be the Place of the last legal Settlement of the Said Mary Clay

That your Petitioners doubt not but to prove That the said Mary Clay never had any legal Settlement in the said Parish of St Michael Royal whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeal to your Honr & Worpps there from

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Honr & Worpps to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the said Parish of Allhallowes the Great may appear before you and hear and abide the Determination of your Honr & Worpps touching this Appeal

And your Petitioners Shall Ever Pray Etca
