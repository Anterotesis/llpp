To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate without in the City of London

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of The Right Honourable John Wilkes Esquire Lord Mayor of the said City and Nathaniel Thomas Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 28th. day of March 1775. Thomas Keble Joanna his Wife And their two Children Sarah Aged Six Years and Elizabeth aged three Years were removed and Conveyed from out of the Parish of Saint Giles Crippelgate without in the said City of London to the said Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate without London as the place of their Last Legal Settlement whereby year Petitioners Conceive themselves to be Aggrived.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray your worships to appoint a day to hear and determine their said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
