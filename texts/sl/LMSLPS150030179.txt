To the Right Honoble. Sr. Tho. Stampe Knt. Ld. Maior of the City of London And to the Right Worll. the Justices of the peace wth. in ye said City in the General Quarter Sessions assembled

The humbly Peticon of ye Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Pish of St. Mary Woolnoth London

Sheweth That one Sarah a female child aged about twoe yeares The Father & Mother being unknowne were by the Churchwardens of St. Michaell Cornhill by virtue of a warrant under the hands & seales of twoe of their Maties. Justices of the peace brought to & left wth. yor. Petr. upon a Suggestion That the said Sarahs last legall settlement was in yor Petr. Pish whereas in truth the said Sarah passing through Cardinals capp Ally in yor. Petr. Pish in the late greate Frost one Mrs. Drayner seeing the child And Itt craveing for Drinke shee releived itt whereupon the said Sarah went away of itts owne accord into the Pish of St. Michaell Cornhill and sett downe att Mr. Watford doore there And after twoe houres was taken upp by their officers and kept by them about three weekes & since sent to yor. Petr go aforesaid

Yor. Petrs. humble suite to yor. Honor. & worpps. is That you wilbe pleased to order the Churchwardens of St Michaell Cornhill to appeare before vor. And to heare their appeale in the prmisses And to order the said Sarah to be sent to the said Pish of St. Michaells To be pvided for by them according to Lawe.

And They shall pray Etc
