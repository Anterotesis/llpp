To the Rt. Honble: Sir Robert Gods shall Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London & to the Rt. Worshipfull the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City & ye Liberties thereof, in their General Quarter Session assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwarderns & Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Botolph without [...]

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Warrant under the Hands and Seals of your Lordship and Robert Westley Esqr. Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Libties thereof (One being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Sixth Day of November last past, Elizabeth Hawkins, aged Six years, George Hawkins aged five Years and William Hawkins aged One Year, Children of John Hawkins by Elizabeth his Wife were Removed from the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London, unto the Parish of St. Botolph without Aldersgate London aforesaid alleadging the said Parish of St. Botolph without Aldersgate, to be the Place of the last legall Settlement of the said three Children

Cha Baynbridge Agent for the Appellants } That Your Petitioners conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Warrant or Order of Removall as aforesaid, Do Appeal to your Lordp and Worships therefrom, And humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear ym. concerng. the Premisses and that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of Yr. Said Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London may appear before you, to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Lordp and Worps touching this Appeal.

And your Petitioners shall pray Etc
