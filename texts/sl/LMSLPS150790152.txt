London To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Session of the Peace at Guildhall Assembled:

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church- Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seal of the Worshipfull Robert Alsop Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City of London and one of his Majesty Justices of the Peace for the said City bearing Date the fourth day of May instant Margaret Parsons a Rogue and Vagabond was conveyed from the Parish of Saint Bridge in the said City of London to the said Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden as the Peace of her last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved And against the said Pass Warrant do Appeal to the County

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly Pray that your Worships will be pleased to appoint some Day in this Present Session for the hearing and determining of the said Appeal And to Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Saint Bridget and also persons concerned do then Attend this Court

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

J:Lane Sollicitor for the Appellants
