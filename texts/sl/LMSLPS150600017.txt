To the Right Honourable Sr. William Colseat Knt. Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Session Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of Thomas and Paul Corbell of the Parish of St. Martin Vintry in London Sugar Refiners & Co partners

Sheweth That by a Rate or Assessment made upon the Inhabitants, of the said Parish for the Relief of the Poor thereof for one half year commencing from Lady day 1748 & ending at Michas last your Petrs. are Rated and assessed much more in the Pound than any other Inhabitant or Inhabitants of the aforesaid Parish and for non payment thereof the late Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish made a Distress of your Petrs. Goods & have since made Sale thereof for which reason your Petrs. apprehend they are unequally & unreasonably Rated and Assessed & much aggrieved as well by the said Rate and Assessment as by the said Distress And therefore humbly appeal against the same and pray such Relief in the Premisses as to your Worships shall seem meet

Tho. Corbett Paul Corbett
