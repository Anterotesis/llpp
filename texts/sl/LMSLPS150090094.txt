To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Humfry Edwin Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. ye rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace att the Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Butt wthout. Algate London.

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrt. under the Hands & Seales of twoe of his Maties. Justices of the Peace within this City (one being of the Quox) bearing date the sixeteene day of June 1698 David Price with John Price his son were by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Sepulchers London Sent to yor. Petr. to be provided for upon the Oath of the said David Price that his last legall settlemt. was in yor. Petr. Parish where he was a housekeeper in a Tenemt. worth Tenn pounds p Annm & where the said John Price his son was born And alsoe paid to the poore and other Parish Dutyes Whereas in Truth the said David Price Dwelt in the Parish of St. Sepulchers about Eight yeares last past were he hath been a Penconer divers yeares And recd from the Overseers nine pence p weeke pencon And had & Did wear a brasse badge As a Penconer of the said Parish of St Sepulchers

Wherefore yor. Petr. humbly prayes yor. Honors. & Worpps to hear them in the prmisses And to order the Churchwardens of the Parish of St. Sepulchers London to Appeare here before you And to send the said David Price with John his sound back again to the said Parish of St. Sepulchers London to be by them kept and pvided for untill they can Free themselves of the charge thereof by Due course of Law Where the said David Price hath had his last legall settlement.

And They Shall Pray Etc
