To the Rt Honrble Sr Richard Hoare Knt Lord Maior of the City of London and also to the Right Worpll: the Rest of her Majesty's Justices of the Peace in the general quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Andrew Holborn London

1712 23/10

Sheweth That one Elizabeth Harper Widdow with her Five Children by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of her Maties Justices of the peace (within this City one being of Quoru) bearing date the three and twentieth day of October last past was removed from the parish of of St Bridgett als Brides London unto the said parish of St Andrew Holborn London alleadging the said parish to be the place of the last legall Settlement of the said Elizabeth Harper

That your Petitioners doubt not but to prove that the Said Elizabeth Harper never had any legall Settlement in the said parish of St Andrew Holborn whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the Said two Justices And do humbly appeal to your Honr and Worpps therefrom

Your petitioners therefore most humbly pray your Honr and Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St Bridgett als Brides may appear before you and hear and abide the determination of Your Honr: and Worpps touching this appeal

And your petitioners Shall Ever pray Etc
