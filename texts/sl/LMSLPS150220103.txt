To the Rt Honrble Sr Gilbert Heathcote Knt Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt Worpll the Rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace in the General Quarter Sessions assembled.

The humble Petition and appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St Helen near Bishopsgate London

Sheweth That one Ann Jones Singlewoman by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of her Maties Justices of the Peace within this City (one being of the Quoru) bearing Date the Fourth Day of May last past was removed from the Parish of Allhallows the Great London into the said Parish of St Helens alleadging the said parish to be the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Ann Jones

That your Petitioners doubt not but to prove that the said Ann Jones never had any legal Settlement in the said parish of St Helens whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the Judgment of the said two Justices and doe humbly Appeal to your Honr and Worpps thereof com

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Honr & Worpps to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poore of the said Parish of All hallows the Great may appear before you and hear and [---] abide the Determination of your Honr & Worpps touching this Appeale

And your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc

Appeale adjorned to next 2 Sessions: if Susanna Lloyd Anne Jones supposed Mistresse happen to dye in them term in the Appeal to be allowed by consent And costs to be allowed to Appellts

Appeal allowed
