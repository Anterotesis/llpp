To The Right Honourable William Bridger Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City in the General Quarter Session of the peace for the said City assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Mary at Marlborough in the County of Wilts.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Francis Cokayne Esqr. and Sir Charles Asgill Knight and Baronet two of the Alderman of the City of London and two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the same City and the Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Eighteenth Day of February last past Hannah Johnson Wife of John Johnson who is gone from her (as is alledged in the said Order or Pass Warrant and their four Children (that is to say) James aged Eight Years, John aged Seven Years, Christopher aged four Years and Ann aged two years were removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Olave in Silver Street London to the said parish of Saint Mary at Marlborough as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said John Johnson Hannah his Wife and their said four Children.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly appeal to this Honourable Court against the same and pray Relief in the premisses.
