To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesties Worshipfull Justices of the Peace for the City of London at the General [...] Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Royal Exchange in the City of London

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass warrant bearing date the 20th. day of November last past under the hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Branckley Kenneth Lord Mayor of the said City and John Hart Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the said City (One whereof being of the Quorum) Whereby Frances Boardman a Singlewoman was removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate London to the said Parish of Saint Bartholomew by the Royal Exchange in the said City as the Place of her last legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners Apprehending themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or pass warrant of the said two Justices humbly appeal against the same

And therefore pray that you will be pleased to order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate London aforesaid And all persons concerned to attend this Court some day this present Session to hear and abide the Judgement and determination of this Court touching the premises And that Your Petitioners may have such relief therein as to Your Worships shall seem meet

And Your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc.
