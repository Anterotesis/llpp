To the Right Honoble. Sr. Thomas Rawlinson Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt. Worll. The rest of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace In the Genral Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petiton and Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of Deptford In Kent

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and seales of Twoe of Her Maties Justices of the Peace within the City of London (Quox unus) dated the 25th. day of May Ano Din 1706 One Mary Hatton Widdow was Sent by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St Barthll. Exchange London to yor. Petrs. to be provided for upon the Oath of the Said Mary Hatton That Her husband Joseph Hatton lived in Yor. Petr. Parish Severall Yeares And was a hired Servant by the Yeare And Served Mr. Fownos dwelling in Deptford Whereas in Truth the Said Joseph Hatton never was a hired Servt. to the said Mr. Fownes only a writer in Mr. Fownes & Fire [...] by the day for 13d a day which was Allowed by the Queen And the Said Mary Hon hath Since the Decease of her husband been Releived by the Churchwardens of St. Barthll, Exchange where hee was severall Yeares a Settled Inhabitant

Yor. Petr. humbly Pray Yor. Honor. & Worpps. to hear them in the Prmisses And Order the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Barthll. Exchange London To appear here before You To shew Cause why they should not Provide for the Said Mary Hatton And alsoe to send the said Mary Hatton to the said Parish of St. Barthll. Exchange To be by them kept and provided for till They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Law.

And Yor. Petr. Shall Ever Pray Etc
