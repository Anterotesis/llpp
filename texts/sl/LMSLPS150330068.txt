To the Right Honble Sr Willm Stewart Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Right Worpll the rest of his Maties Justices of the peace for the City of London at their General Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of several Parishes of St Giles without Cripplegate St Botolph without Aldgate St Andrew Wardrobe St Catherine Coleman St Andrew Holborne St Sepulchres St Brides St Botolph Aldersgate and St Botolph without Bishopsgate London

Sheweth That Your Petitioners are very much over burthened with poor & not able to raise Sufficient Sumes of Mony amongst themselves to releive them not withstanding their Assessments are greater then heretofore yet the Charge of the poor is far above their Assessmts as the same upon their Examinacon hath divers times appeared to several worthy Aldermen of this City appointed to hear the Same And their poor daily increasing upon their sd parishes some of them lying near the Suburbs & others of them in the Suburbs of this City

Their Humble Suit therefore to your Honour & Worps is that you will be favourably pleased to grant unto them the Contributions of other parishes within this City for and towards the releif of their poor as they have formerly had which are particularly hereunder menconed or such further Allowance as to Your Honour & Worps Shall seem meet.

St Giles without Cripplegate £90 St Botolph without Aldgate £50 St Andrew Wardrobe £40 St Catherine Coleman £12 St Andrew J Colborne £7 St Sepulchres £44 St Brides £15 St Botolph Aldersgate £15 St Botolph Bishopsgate £60

And they shall ever Pray Etc.
