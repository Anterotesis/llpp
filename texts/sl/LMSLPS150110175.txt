To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Richard Levett Knt. Lord Mayor of the Citty of London And to the Rt. Worll. the Rest of his Matyes. Justices of the Peace In the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poore of the parish of St. Anne Blacefryers London

Sheweth

That one Thomas Crosly by virtue of a warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of the Maties. Justices of the peace one being of the Quor dated 24th. July 1700 was brought to yor. Petrs. by the Churchwardens of the parish of St. Andrewes Wardrobe on the Oath of the said Thomas Crosly That the last legall Settlement was [...] in the said parish of St. Anne Blackfryers Whereas in Tenth the said Thomas Crosley hath not been legally a Settled Inhabitant in the said parish of St. Anne Blackfryers Since May 1685

Yor. Petrs. therefore pray That your honour and Worpps. would Order the said Churchwardens of St. Andrewes Wardrobt to Appeare before you And to heard them in the prmises to And to Send the said Thomas Crosly to the said Churchwardens of St. Andrewes Wardrobe To be pvided for untill They Pann Free themselves of the Charge thereof by one Course of Lant

And yor. Petrs. Shall Ever pray Etc
