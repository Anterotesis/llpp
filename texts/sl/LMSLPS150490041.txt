To the Right Honble: Sir John Barnard Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of the honourable Bench at Justices hall in the old Bailey in the Suburbs of the said City

The Humble prayer of Jonathan Thomas a Prisoner in Newgate

Sheweth That your Supplicant was on the fourth day of August last past taken up & committed on Suspicon of Diministring the Gold Coyn of this Kingdom Called Guineas and hath ever Since continued a prisoner for the Same

Your Supplicant Therefore knowing himself Innocent of the fact humbly prays that he might not be detained longer in Custody to the ruin of himself and family, but that he might be Either tried Bailed, or discharged this Sessions as shall Seem most Meet to this Honble Court

And Your Supplicant as in duty bound Will ever pray

Jonathan Thomas

Dated the 6th: of September 1738 being the first day of this Session
