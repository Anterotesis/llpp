To the Rt. Honr:ble Sr James Bateman Knt Lord Maior of the City of London And also to the Rt Worpll: the rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City in their general quarter Sessions assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseer of the Poor of the Parish of St Christopher London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Pass order under the hands & Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London (one being of the Quorn) bearing Date the 26th Day of October last past James Martin was removed from out of the parish of St Bartholomew by the Exchange London into the Said parish of St Christopher Alleadging the said Parish to be the Place of his last legal Setlement

That your Petr. doubt not but to prove That the said James Martin is not legally settled in the said parish, of St Christophers whereby they conceive themselves aggrieved by the said warrant & Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeal to your Honr and Worpps therefrom

Your Petrs therefore most humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Said Parish of St Bartholomew [...] near the Exchange may appear before you and hear and abide the Determination of your Honr and Worpps touching this Appeal

And they shall ever pray Etc
