To the Right Honourable Brass Crosby Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the rest of his Majestys Justices of the peace in and for the said City in their General Quarter Session of the peace assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a Pass warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Brass Crosby Esquire and Thomas Hallifax Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the peace in and for the said City of London bearing date the Ninth day of March one thousand seven hundred and seventy one Ann Wife of John Walton (therein said to be gone from her) and their three Children to Wit William Aged five years Elizabeth aged two Years and George aged six Weeks were removed from the parish of Saint Bridget Vulgarly called Saint Brides to the said Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell as the Place of their las legal Settlement by which your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved and humbly Appeal therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray your Worships that the benefit of their said Appeal may be said to them & that a day may be Appointed for the hearing and determining such Appeal and that your Worships would be pleased to make such Order in the Premisses as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

W Johnston Sol. for the Appellts.
