To the Right Honble Sr. Gerard Conyers Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Right Worpl the rest of his Maties Justices of the peace for the City of London at their General Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and overseers of the poor of the Several parishers of St. Giles without Cripplegate St. Botolph without Aldgate St. Andrew Wardrobe St. Catherine Coleman St. Andrew Holbourn St. Sepulchers St. Brides alhallows on the Wall St. Botolph Aldersgate and St. Botolph without Bishopsgate London

Sheweth That Your Petitioners are very much Over burthened with poor & not able to raise Sufficient since of mony amongst themselves to relieve them notwithstanding their assements are greater than heretofore Yet the Charge of the poor is par above their assessments as the same upon Examinacon hath divers times appeared to serveral Worthy Aldemen of this City appointed to hear the same And their poor daily increasing upon their said parishes some of them lying near the Suburbs and others of them in the Suburbs of this City

Their humble Suit therefore to Your Honour & Worps is that You will be favourably pleased to grant unto them the Contributions of the other parishes within this City for and towards the relief of their poor as they have formerly had which are perticularly hereunder menconed or Such further Allowance as to your Honour & Worps shall seem meet
