To the Right Honoble Sir John Williams Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Right Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City at the Generall Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled

The humble Petition and appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Sir William Thomson Knight Recorder and Sir Richard Brocas Knight Alderman two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and the Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty Ninth day of June 1736 Elizabeth Coppinger Widow of Henry Coppinger deced and her three Children Elizabeth Coppinger Aged Six years and upwards Henry and Mary Twins aged about eight Months were removed from the Parish of Saint Buttolph without Aldgate London to the said parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London as the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggreived by the said Warrant or Order and humbly Appeale against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Pray that they may be releived in the premises in such Manner as to this Honoble Court Shall seem meet

And your Petitioners as in duty bound shall ever pray Etc.

Hen Bridgen Ch Ward Wm. Sells Overseer
