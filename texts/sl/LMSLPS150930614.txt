To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justice of the peace in and for the said City in their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary at Hill London

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Williamss Comer Esquire Lord Mayor of the said City of London and John Hart Esquire two of his Majesty's Justice of the Peace for the said City bearing date the thirty first day of January One Thousand Seven hundred and eighty two Jane the Widow of Robert Walls having then lately come to reside in the Parish of Allhallows Barkin not having obtained any legal Settlement there It was Adjudged by the said Magistrate Ripon due proof made before then that the parish of Saint Mary at Hill was the place of the last legal Settlement is the said Jane Walls to which Parish She was accordingly removed.

Your Petitioner's conceiving themselves So be greatly Aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant humbly appeal to your Worship and pray relief in the premisses and that a day may be Appointed for heaving and determining the said Appeal

Lon Jul 82
