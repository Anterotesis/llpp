To the Right Honble. Robert Westley Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their Quarter Session of the Peace for the said City Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Katherine Coleman London

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands & Seals of Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the first day of November last Jane Dickenson Widow of Robert Dickenson deceas'd was remov'd and convey'd from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate London to the aforesaid Parish of Saint Katherine Coleman London as the Place of her last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant Humbly Appeal against the same and Pray such Relief in the Premises as to this Honble. Court it shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Thos: Harrison Bright Whilton Nicho Lynch
