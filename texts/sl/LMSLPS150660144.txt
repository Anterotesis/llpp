To the Right Honourable Stephen Theodore Janssen Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Fulham in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Stephen Theodore Janssen Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and William Alexander Esquire one of the Aldermen of the same City of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and the Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the eighth day of May last past Mary Balldrew Singlewoman was Removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Peter le Poor in Broad Street London aforesaid to the said Parish of Fulham as the Place of her last legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners apprehend themselves aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or Order and the Judgment of the said two Justices and do therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioner therefore humbly pray that this Court will be pleased to appoint same day in this Present General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to Hear and Determine the Matter touching their said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall Ever pray [...] John Nicoll Sollr. for Appellts: Hatton Garden 16. June 1755.
