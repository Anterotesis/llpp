To the Right Honble William Plomer Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Worshipful the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City in General Quarter Session of the Peace holden for the said City Assembled

The Humble Petition of Cornelins Donovan of London Citizen and

Sheweth That in this present Hilary Term your Petitioner obtained Judgment in His Majestys Court of Kings Bench at Westminster againt Thomas Wright Esqr. and Evan Pugh Esqr. two of the Inhabitants of the City of London for the Sum of One thousand four hundred and sixty seven pounds for Damages which your Petitioner had sustained as well by reason of the unlawful riotous and tumultuous assembly of divers Persons to the Number of twelve and more at the Parish of Allhallows London Wall in the Ward of Broad Street in London aforesaid and there unlawfully feloniously and with force wholly demolishing a Dwelling house of your Petitioner situate and being in the Parish and Ward aforesaid together with divers Goods and Chattles of Your Petitioner where with the said Dwelling house was furnished And also for your Petitioners Costs and Charges by him about his Suit in that behalf expended And his Majestys Writ of Fieri facias on the said Judgment hath been delivered to the Sheriffs of the said City

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays this Honourable Court will be pleased to order the said Damages so recovered by your Petitioner to be raised and levied on the Inhabitants of the said City and paid to your Petitioners according to the form of the Statute in such Case made and provided
