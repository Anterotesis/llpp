To the Right Honorable Brackley Kennett Esquire Mayor of of the City of London And the Worshipfull the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City and the Liberties thereof at their General Quarter Session of the Peace at the Guildhall of the said City Assembled.

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Faith under Saint Paul in the City of London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal. under the hands and Seals of Brackley Kennett Esquire Mayor of the City of London and John Hart Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City of London and the Liberties thereof (one whereof being of the Quorum) Dated the thirtieth day of May One thousand Seven Hundred and Eighty Alphonso Gilbert was removed from the Parish of Saint Martin Ludgate in the City of London to the Parish of Saint Faith under Saint Paul in the said City as the place of his last legal Settlement by which your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved:

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal Against the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal and pray such relief in the Premises as to this Honorable Court shall seem meet.

And your Petitioners will ever Pray.

John Greenway Soliciter for the Appellants.
