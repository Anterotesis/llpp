To the Right Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London at their General Quarter Sessions assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Ethelburga in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a pass Warrant or order under the Seals of two of his majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing date the twenty Second day of May last past George Staines aged Eight Years and upwards Margaret Staines aged five years and upward and Ann Staines aged two years and upwards the lawfull Children of George Staines deceased by Margaret his Wife (who [...] lately deserted them) were Removed from the Parish of Alhallows London Wall London to the said Parish of Saint Ethelburga London as the last legal place of their Settlement By which your Petitioner's Conceive themselves aggrieved and appeal to this Court against the said Pass Order

Ste: Toulmin Agent for the Appellants Your Petitioners therefore pray your Worships will be pleased to hear and determine the Matters touching this Appeal & give them such relief in the premisses as to your Worships shall seem just and meet

And Yor Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
