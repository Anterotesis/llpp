To the Right Honourable Sir Samuel Fludyer knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and others his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City of London At their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled on Monday the 7th. December 1761

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Christ Church in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of William Alexander Esquire and Sir Matthew Blakiston Knt. two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City of London bearing date the Eighteenth day of November now last past John Ingall Aged Seventy Seven Years was removed from and out of the said Parish of the Saint Andrew in Holborn to the said Parish of Christ Church Middlesex Alledging the last legal Settlement of the said John Ingall to be in the said Parish of Christ Church

Therefore your said Petitioners Conceiving themselves Agrieved by the said Order of the said two Justices Do humbly Appeal to this Court against the same Praying such releif in the Premisses As to this Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioners Etc.

William Carter attorney for Appelts.
