[...] To the Right Honourable Sr. John Fryer Barrt. Lord Mayor of the City of London And others the Rt. Worshipful his Majties Justices of the peace of the Said City at their General Quarter Session of the peace held for the Said City.

The humble peitocn and Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. Mary Newington in the County of Surry.

Sheweth That Katherine Perkins wife of John Perkins was by an Order or Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Majties Justices of of the Peace for the City of London bearing Date the Second Day of February 1720 removed from the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London to the parish of St. Mary Newington Aforesaid as the place of her last legal Settlement.

That Yor. Peticoners do humbly conceive that they can Make it Appear unto this Honble. Court that the place of the last legal Settlement of the Said Katherine Perkins is not in the Said parish of Newington

Yor. Peticoners therefore do humbly Appeal against the Said Order And pray that this Honbl. Court will Order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London to Attend this Court to Shew cause why the Said Order Should not be Vacated.

And Yor. Peticoners Shall Ever Pray Etc.
