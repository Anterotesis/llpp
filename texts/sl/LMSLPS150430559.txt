To the Rt. Worsppll: his Majtys: Justices of the Peace for the City of London, and the Liberty's thereof.

The Humble Peticon and Appeal of the Churchwardens adn overseers of the Poor of the Parish of East Greenwich in the County of Kent

Sheweth: That by a warrant or Order dated th 28th: day of November last under th hands and Seals of the Honble: John Barber Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London, and Sr. Richard, Brocas Knt. two of his Majtys: Justices of the Peace for the said City, and Liberty's thereof (Quor un) John Osborn and Anne his wife, and her two Children were Conveyed from the Parish of St. Andrew Holborn London, to the said Parish [...] of East Greenwich, as the place of the last legall Settlement of the said John Osborn and Anne his wife, and her said Children

And that your Peticonrs: conceive, they are aggreived by the said order, of the said two Justices; and do Appeal against the same, to this Court

Your Peticonrs: therefore humbly pray, your Worships will please, to hear the Appeal, and that all parties may attend to abide the Judgement and Determinacon of this Court, touching the same.

And your Peticonrs: shall ever pray

George Wigrell} Churchwarden

John Ainslie Tho: Brice} Overfeers of the poor
