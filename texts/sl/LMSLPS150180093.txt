To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Robert Bedingfeld Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt. Worll. The rest of Her Maties. Justices of ye Peace In the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of Casehorton In the County of Surry

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of Her Maties. Justices of the peace within the City of London One being of the Quor bearing date the 26th. October last One Richard Scrugnall with his Wife and Five Children was by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St Buttolph without Bishoppsgate in London Sent to yor. Petr to be provided for upon the Oath of the said Richard Strugnall That hee lived an hired Servant by the Yeare for the Space of One whole Yeare and Upwards with one Mr. Edward Carleton In yor. Petr. Parish Whereas in Truth the said Mr. [---] Richard Strungnall never was a legally Settled Inhabitant in yor Petrs. Parish

Wherefore yor. Petrs. humbly Pray yor. Honor. & Worpps. to heare them in the prmisses And Order the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the said Parish of St. Buttolph wth. out Bishoppsgate London to Appeare here before You And to Shew cause why the said Warrant made by the said twoe Justices should not be Quashed And the said Richard Strugnall with his wife and 5 Children sent to the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the said Parish of St. Buttolph without Bishoppsgate To be by them provided for and maintained untill They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due course of Law

And yor. Petrs. shall ever Pray Etc
