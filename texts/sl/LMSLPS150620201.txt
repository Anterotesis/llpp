To the Right Honorable Francis Cokayne Esqr Lord Mayor Richard Adams Esqr Recorder and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London and Liberty's thereof in General quarter Sessions assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church- -Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St Matthew Bethnal Green in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by A pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of the said Lord Mayor and Thomas Rawlingson Esqr two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City and Libertys (whereof one is of the Quorum) bearing date the Second day of February 1750 Elizabeth Ashton widow of James Ashton deceased and their four Children Jane aged Seventeen Years Ann Aged nine years Susanna aged Six years and George William aged four years were removed from the parish of St. Botolph Bishopsgate London to the sd. parish of Saint Matthew as the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petr. and Appellts. apprehending themselves agrieved by the sd. Order of the said two Justices do appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petrs. and Appellts. therefore pray that this Court will appoint a Day this present Session for the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Botolph Bishopsgate and all persons concerned to attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgment and determinacon of the same touching this Appeal And that your Petrs. and Appellts. may be relieved as this Court shall see meet.

And ye Petrs. Etc Robt:Browne Wm Wright} Church warden

R: Simons Joseph Eustace} Overseer of the poor
