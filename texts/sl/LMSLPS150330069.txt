To the Rt Honrble Sr William Stewart Knt Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt Worll: the rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace for the said City in their general quarter Sessions assembled.

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Allhallows on the Wall London

Sheweth That the Poor of the said Parish being grown very numerous your Petitioners for many years past have been allowed twelve pounds by orders of this Court to be paid by the several Parishes herein named by the following Proportions Vizt St George Botolph Lane Six pounds, St Martin Orgars Four pounds and St Alban Wood Street Forty Shillings That at the general quarter Sessions of the Peace holden for this City in the month of July last the said Sum of Twelve pounds was ordered to be paid to your Petrs as usual for releif of the Poor of the said Parish of Allhallows but the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the abovemenconed Parish of St Alban Wood Street then appealing against the said Order the matters in difference was then referred to three of the Aldermen of this City to examine into the prmisses Upon which referrence no Determination being yet made your Petrs have not reced any part of the said Twelve pounds altho the other two parishes are willing to contribute and pay as usual

Your Petrs therefore most humbly pray your Honr and Worpps will be pleased to order the payment of the said Twelve pounds for this prsent year and also the said Twelve pounds now in arrear and unpaid, for that the Poor of your Petrs said Parish more and more encreasing

And yor Petrs Shall ever pray Etca.
