To the Right Honourable William Nash Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the rest of the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Andrew Hubbard in the City of London

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or Vagrant Pass under the Hand and Seal of The Right Honourable William Nash Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London bearing date the Twenty sixth day of August last Christian Solomon Wife of John Solomon a Sailor in His Majesty's Service and their Child Rachel Aged Three Months [...] Conveyed from the parish of Saint Michael Cornhill London to the said Parish of Saint Andrew Hubbard, it appearing to his Lordship that the last legal Settlement of the said John Solomon was in the said Parish of Saint Andrew Hubbard

That your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Vagrant Pass, and humbly Appeal therefrom

Your Petitioners Therefore most humbly pray that your Worships would be pleased to Adjourned Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden in and for the said City of London and to make such Order therein as to your Worships shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.
