To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof, at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by the Order of the Right Honourable Nathaniel Newnham Esquire Mayor of the City of London and John Hart Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City, two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City and Liberties thereof under their Hands and Seals made the seventeenth day of July in the year of our Lord One Thousand Seven Hundred and Eighty three concerning a Male Bastard Child named Jacob aged three Years and seven Months or thereabouts born in the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the said City of the Body of Hannah Lyon Singlewoman Reciting that Whereas it appeared unto them the said Nathaniel Newnham Esquire Mayor of the said City and John Hart Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said city two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City and Liberties thereof as well upon the Complaint of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of saint James Dukes Place in the said City as upon the Oath of the said Hannah Lyon Singlewoman, that she the said Hannah Lyon was delivered of her said Male Bastard Child Named Jacob aged three years and seven Months or thereabouts in the Parish Saint Botolph Aldgate in the said City, out of lawful Matrimoney, and that on or about the second day of December One Thousand Seven hundred and Seventy nine she the said Hannah Lyon was removed by the Order of two Magistrates for the said City together, with her said Male Bastard Child for Nuture from the said Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate unto the said Parish of Saint James Dukes Place as the then and now lawful Settlement of her the said Hannah Lyon And that she the said Hannah Lyon and her said Bastard Child had ever since been and then were chargeable there to and

James Dukes Place to the time of making that their Order And they did thereby further Order that the said Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the said City and their Successors forth time being should likewise pay or cause to be paid to the said Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint James Dukes Place, and their Successors for the time being or some or one of them, the further Sum of Two Shillings Weekly and every Week from the date thereof for and towards the support and maintenance of the said Bastard Child during so long that the should remain with his said Mother as a Nurse Child in and at the Expence of the said Parish of Saint James Dukes Place.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order of the said Nathaniel Newnham Esquire and John Hart Esquire the two Justices afore named Do therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the same, praying such relief in the Premises as to this Court shall seem meet

And Your Petitioners shall Ever Pray Etc.
