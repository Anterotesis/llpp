To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the same City at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Sir William Plomer Knight and John Wilkes Esqr. Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Sixth Day of May One thousand Seven Hundred and Eighty Six Mary Groves a Singlewoman was removed from the parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street London to the said Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London as the place of her last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceive themselves agrieved by the said Warrant or Order and humbly appeal against the Same and pray this Honble Court to grant them such Relief in the premises as shall be thought most Equitable

And your Petitioners shall ever pray
