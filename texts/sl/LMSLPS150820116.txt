To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London at the General Quarter Session of the Peace holden for the said City assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Tottenham in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of HIs Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London and Liberties therefore being of the Quorum bearing Date the twentieth Day of February 1771 Two Children to wit William Tickle aged six years and Elizabeth Tickle aged one your and upwards Children of John Tickle deceased by Elizabeth his Wife who hath deserted them were removed and conveyed by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate London from and out of their said parish and unto the said Parish of Tottenham and delivered unto your Petitioners (the said Justices adjudging the last legal Settlement of the said two Children to be in the said Parish of Tottenham)

Wm. Chetham Attorney for the Appellants Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices humbly appeal to his Honourable Court against the said Order or pass Warrant and pray Relief in the premises and that this their Appeal may be hand and determined at this present Session.
