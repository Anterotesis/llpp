To the Right Honorable. Sr Wm. Ashurst Knt. Ld. Maior of the City of London & other their Maties. Justices of the Peace of the same City att the Genall Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled:

The humble Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Martins Ludgate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands & Seales of twoe of their Maties. Justices of the Peace wth. in this City (one being of the Quor.) Sara Brookebanck was brought & delivered to yor. Petr. to be pvided for upon the Oath of the said Sarah That she was last legally Settled in yor. said Petr. Pish with one Mr. Morrice widdow in the old Bailey as an hired Servant for Fifty Shillings wages yearly Whereas in truth the said Sarah was hired by the said Mrs. Morrice for a yeare att such wages If they both Liked one another att a moneths end But the said Sarah continued wch. Mrs. Morrice only for a fortnight And they left her service And went away from her And soe had noe settlement wth. yor. Petr. According to Law.

Their humble suite to yor. good Honors. & Worshipps is That you wilbe pleased to order the Churchwardens of St. Gyles Cripplegt. To appeare before you And to heare yor. Peticoner in the Prmisses And to order the Churchwardens of St. Gyles Cripplegate to Keepe the said Sara Brookebanck Till they can Free themselves [...] of the charge thereof by due course of Law.

And they shall pray Etc
