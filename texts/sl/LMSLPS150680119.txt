To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor & the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London & the Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph wihtout Bishopsgate London.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Libertys thereof (One being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Seventeenth day of August 1757 John Cox Dorothy (called in the said Order Elizabeth) his Wife and their three Children John aged Seven Years Charles aged four Years and William aged five Months were removed from the parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate London to the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate London aforesaid adjudging the last legal Settlement of the said John Cox Elizabeth his Wife and their said three Children to be there And Your Petitioners conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices Do humbly appeal therefrom and pray such Relief in the Premisses as to Your Lordship and Worships shall seem meet and Convenient.

Burr Attorney for Bpsgate Pish.}

And Yr. Petrs. shall ever pray Etc.
