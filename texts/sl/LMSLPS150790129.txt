To the Right Honble The Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in the General Quarter Session of the Peace for the same City assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Chapel Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Precinct of Bridewell London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and the Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the fourth Day of July One Thousand Seven Hundred and Sixty Seven Ann Middleton Wife of Robert Middleton gone from her whose Settlement could not be found (as alledged in the said Order) was removed and conveyed by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London from and out of their said Parish unto the said Precinct of Bridewell as the Place of her last legal Settlement [...]

Your Petitioners, conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly appeal to this Honourable Court against the same and pray Relief in the Premisses and that the Benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto then and that the Hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned unto the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City.
