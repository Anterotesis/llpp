To the Right Honourable George Nelson Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the County of Middx.

Sheweth That David Williams an Inhabitant of the same Parish is lately become Poor and unable to maintain himself so that he is now in the workhouse and kept at the Charge of the same Parish being Old and unable to work

That Edward Williams of Fleet Lane near the Fleet market London undertaker son of the said David Williams is of sufficient Ability to maintain him

That your Petitioners said Parish is heavily burthened with a great number of Poor Old Blind Lane and Impotent Persons

Whereby your Petitioners humbly pray that the said Edward Williams may be ordered and obliged at his own Charges to relieve and maintain for his said poor Father according to such Rate, as to your Lordship and this Honble Court shall seem meet to Assess

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc

B. Eliz. C.2 S.7.
