Guild Hall London February Session 1786 To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor of the City of London the Aldermen and other his Fellows Justices of the Peace for the said City in General Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Chruch- Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a Warrant or Order of Removal bearing date the 11th. Day of February Instant under the Hands and Seals of William Pickett and John Wilkes Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City of London and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) Margaret Montress Singlewoman and her three illegitimate Children, William aged Seven Years and Upwards Robert aged Four Years and upwards and Ann aged Seven Months and Upwards were Removed and Conveyed from and out of Parish of Saint Giles witout Cripplegate London to the said Parish of Saint Matthew Bethnal Green as the place of their Last Legal Settlement.

Your Petitioners Apprehending themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or Order of Removal of the said Two Justices Humbly Appeal to this Court against the same and pray that your Worships will be Pleased (as your Petitioners did on the 16th. Day of February Instant give Notice unto the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate purporting their Intention of this Appeal) to proceed upon this their Appeal and give such Relief in the premises as to Your Worships shall seem Meet

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

J Cadogan Robt. Wrightson} Church Wardens

Jonar. Sunderson Wm. Beverly} Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Matthew Bethmal Green
