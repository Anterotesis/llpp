To the Right Honourable Sir Edward Bellamy knight Lord mayor of the City of London and to the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City in their General Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Burrell Blythe an Inhabitant of the parish of Christ Church London

Sheweth. That your Petitioner is an Inhabitant of the said parish of Christ Church and an occupyer of one house within the said parish the Rent and Taxes of which are paid by your Petitioner who for the Year One Thousand Seven hundred and Thirty there hath been rated by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the said parish in much larger Sumes of money than he ought to have been towards the Releif of the poor of the said parish which said Rate or Assessment your Petitioner humbly Conceives in an many respects Unequall Arbitrary and Illegall and Particularly for that diverse Inhabitants and Occupyers of houses within the said parish some of which pay as large and others much larger Rents than your Petitioner whose Circumstances Do exceed or at least Equall your Petitioners have for that year been Rated in much less sumes of money than your Petitioner And your Petitioner further Sheweth that the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said parish for the said year One Thousand Seven hundred and Thirty three have made up their Amounts for the said year and procured the same to be Allowed Nothwithstanding many Charges and disbursements are therein contained which Are contrary to Law and ought not to have been allowed by reason whereof great Sumes of money which ought to have been applyed to the Releif of the poor of the said parish are made use of and disburst for other purposes then by Law they ought to be and thereby the Charge upon the Inhabitants of the said parish And particularly upon your petitioner is Greatly Increased And the Rate thereby become much more burden some And as your Petitioner Conceives illegall by reason of such Unwarantable overcharge

Your Petitioner therefore conceiving himself greatly Aggreived by the Rate or Assessment So made by the Churchwardens And Overseers of the said Parish for and towards the necessary Releife of the poor of the said parish Humbly prays that the said Rate may be Quashed And the said Accounts disallowed And that your Petitioner may be Releived in the premisses in such manner as to this Court shall Seem just and fitting

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
