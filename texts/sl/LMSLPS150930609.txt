To the Right Honourable the London Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City in their Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary at Hill London

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Sir Thomas Hallifax Knight and John Hart Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City bearing date the Thirteenth day of September One Thousand and Seven Hundred and Eighty two Alice Buck Singlewoman having then lately come to reside in the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopgate not having detained any legal Settlement there It was adjudged by the said Magistrates upon due proof made before them that the Parish of Saint Mary at Hill was the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Alice Buck to which Parish she was accordingly removed

Your Petitioner's conceiving themselves to be greatly aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly Appeal to your Worship and pray who in the premises and that a day may be appointed for hearing and determining the said Appeal
