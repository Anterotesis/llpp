To the Right Honourable The Lord Mayor and other His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in the General Session of the Peace for the sane City assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of saint Martin Outwish London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City of London and the Liberties therefore being of the Quarum bearing Date the sixth Day of May 1769 Thomas Guy was removed and conveyed from this parish of Saint Helen near Bishopsgate London unto the said parish of Saint Martin Outwith as the place of his last legal Settlement by which Your petitioners apprehend themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal against the said Order or Pass Warrant and humbly pray that the Benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto Your Petitioners and that the Hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned unto the next General Quarter Session of the peace to be holden for the said City of London.
