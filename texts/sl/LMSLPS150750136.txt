To the Right Honble Sr.Wm.Stephenson Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City at their Genr. Sessions assembled on Monday the 10th. day of December 1764.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church- wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal dated the twenty Second day day of October last under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honble William Bridgen Esquire then Lord Mayor of the said City of London and Sr. John Cartwright Knt. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the said City (One being of the Quorum) Sarah Garland Widow was removed from the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the said City of London to the said Parish of St. John of Wapping in the said County of Middlesex as the place of her last legal Settlement together within her Male Bastard Child John aged Six Months for Nurture.

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order by Removal and do humbly Appeal therefrom That your Petitioners have given Notice to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Botolph Aldgate of this their Appeal

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray this Court that they may be heard at this Prest. Sessions of the Peace touching their said Appeal And that all Parties concerned may abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touch [...] the same, And your Petrs. shall ever pray Etc.

Richd. Hassall Henry Wardall} Churchwardens

Matthew Dickson Thos Wright} Overseers of ye Poor
