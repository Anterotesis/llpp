To the Right Honorable Samuel Plumbs Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London And the Worshipfull the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City and Laborties thereof at their General Quarter Session of the Peace at the Guildhall of the said City Assembled

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Faith under Saint Court in the City of London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of the Right Honorable Samuel Plumbs Esquire Lord Mayor of the said City of London and Evan Pugh Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City and Liberties thereof (one whereof being of the Quorum) Dated the Ninth day of June One thousand Seven Hundred and Seventy nine Mary Williams Widow was removed from the Parish of Christ Church London to the Parish of Saint Faith under Saint Paul in the City of London as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Williams by which you Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appe [...] against the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal and pray such relief in the premises ask this Honorable Court it shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

John Green away Solicitor for the Appellants.

Exeter Court Strand
