To the Right Honourable William Bridgen Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majest's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of the Right Honourable William Bridgen Esquire and Sir Matthew Blackiston Baronet two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and Liberty thereof one being of the Quorum bearing Date the first day of March last James Collins Sarah his Wife and their four Children Eleanor aged Nineteen Years Katherine aged Eleven years James aged Seven years and Jane aged ten Months were removed from the Parish of Saint Bridget Vulgarly Saint Brides London to the said Parish of Saint Anne as the Place of their last legal Settlement Where by Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be Aggrieved

Therefore they humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and Pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto Your Petitioners untill the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the City of London and the Liberties thereof And that the hearing and Determining of the said Appeal be Adjourned to the said next Session of the Peace

And Your Petitioners Shall ever Pray Etc.

Edwd Scarlett Thos Chamberlans John Jaguier Osborn Thornton} Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St Anne
