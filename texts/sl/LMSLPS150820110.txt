To the Right Honble. the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the sd. City now in their general Quarter Session of the peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George the Martyr in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That Sarah Compton and Seven Years and upwards Daughter of Henry Compton gone from her where Settlement sd. not be found was by Order or Pass Warrant Dated the 17th June last 1771 under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honble. Brass Crosby Esqr Lord Mayor of the City of London and Sir Richard Glyn Knight & Alderman of the City Two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and the Liberties thereof, one being of the Quorum, removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London to the said Parish of Saint George the Martyr in the County of Middlesex as the Place of her last legal Settlement

Your Petrs. & Appllts. conceive themselves aggrieved thereby humbly appeal to the Judgement of this Honble. Court against the same, & pray that this Honble Court will be pleased to hear and determine the said Appeal And that all persons concerned may abide the Judgment & Determination thereof And that ye. Petrs. may be releived in the Premises as this Honble Court shall seem meet

And Yor. Petrs. shall ever pray Etc

Thos. Wade Jur Atty for the Appellants
