To the Right Honourable Nathaniel Newnham Esquire, Lord Mayor of the City of London, and the Worshipful the Alderman his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City, at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Michael Royal, London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order, or Pass Warrant, under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of t he Peace for the said City one whereof being of the Quorum, bearing date the tenth day of January Instant Henry Eve and [---] Sarah his Wife were removed from the Parish of Saint Gregory by Saint Paul's within the said City of London, to the said Parish of Saint Michael Royal, as the Place of their last legal Settlement whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore beg leave to appeal against the said Order of the said two Justices, and that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them, and that the Hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned untill the next General Session of the Peace to be holden For the said City.

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc.
