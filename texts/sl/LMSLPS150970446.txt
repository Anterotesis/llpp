To the Right Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the City of London in their General Quarter Session assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George Southwark in the County Surrey

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honorable Thomas Wright Esquire Mayor and Henry Kitchen Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and liberties thereof one being of the Quorum bearing date the 23d day of November last Ann Penny the Wife but called in the said Order the Widow of William Penny was removed from the Parish of Saint Bartholomew the Great London unto the said Parish of St. George Southwark in the County of Surrey alledging the said Parish of Saint George Southwark to be the place of her last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or Warrant of the said two Justices do therefore Appeale to your Worships therefrom and humbly Pray that you will pleased to hear them in the Premises and Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Bartholomew the Great London may appear before you at a certain day to be appointed to have and abide the Judgment and determination of your Worships touching the said Appeal

And your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

Vincent Solicitor for the Appelllants
