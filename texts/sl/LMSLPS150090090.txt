To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Humpfry Edwin Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And the Rt. Worll. the Rest of his Maties. Justices of the Peace att the Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The humble peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poore of the Parish of St. Brides London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands & Seales of twoe of his Maties Justices of the Peace of the City (one being of the Quox) Dated the 17th. day of August Anno Dm 1698 One Thomas Pritchard wth. Susanna his wife & John James Peter Richard & Susanna his Children were by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the parish of James Dukes place London sent to yor. Petr. to be pvided for upon the Oath of the said Thomas Pritchard that his last legall Settlement was in yor. Petrs. Parish where he served as an Appntice wth one Thomas Pare Silkedyer for the space of seaven yeares & hath had not other settlement elsewhere whereas intruth the said Thomas Pritchard wth. his wife & Children hath for above nine yeares now last past lived in the said Parish of St. James Dukes place And hath been releived by the Churchwardens of the said Parish from tyme to tyme wth. Bread coles & money As as Inhabitant of their parish

yor. Petr. therefore Pray yor. Honors. & Worpps. to hear them all in the prmisses and Order the said Churchwardens of Sr James Dukes place to Appear here before you And alsoe to send the said Thomas Pritchard wth. his wife & children to the Churchwardens of the said Parish of St. James Dukes place to be by them kept & pvided for untill they can free themselves of the house thereof by due course of Lawe
