To the Rt. Honrble Sr. William Humfreys Knt. & Barntt Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt Worpll: the Rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace within the same City in the general quarter Sessions assembled.

The humble petition and appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Lawrence in the old Jewry London

Sheweth That one Elenore Harris by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesties of the peace within this City (one being of the Quor) bearing date the fourteenth day of October last past was and delivered [...] the Churchwarden & Overseers of the poor of St. Andrew Holborne London unto the parish of St. Lawrence London alleadging the said Parish to be the place of her last legal Settlement

That your Petitionr. doubt not but to prove that the said Elenor Harris never had any legal Settlement in the said parish of St Lawrence whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeal to your Honr & Worpps therefrom.

Your Petitr. therefore most humbly prays your Honr & Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmissed and that the Churchward- -ens & Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St Andrew Holborn may appear before you and hear and abide the determination of of your Honr & Worpps touching this appeales

And your Petrs Shall ever pray Etc.
