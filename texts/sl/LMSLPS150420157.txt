The Humble petition of Thomas Thompson now a prisoner in Newgate To the Honble Sr Jew Thompson Rocorder of the Citty of London and the rest of the Justices [...]

Sheweth That your peticoner was Convicted before you Honr in November Last for a Conspiracy and hath Undergone his punishment heretofore but was Ordered by the Court [---] to give Security for his good behaviour for a Twelve months afterwards which we Humbly pray may be now Taken in Court his Bail being ready and the prosecute having notice Thereof

Your petioner therefore most Humbly prays Your peticoner away be Admitted to prett in Bail [...] for his good behaviour for a Twelve [...] and your Peticoner as in Duty bound Shall ever pray Etc
