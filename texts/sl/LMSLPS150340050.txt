To the Rnt. Honble: Sr. Gerrard Conyers Kt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rt. Worshipfull the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the same City in their Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Peticon and Appeal of John Jeffryes

Sheweth That Your Petitioners is Deputy Keeper of the Poultry Compter Situate in the Parish of St. Mildred in the Poultry in this City and for his own Private use and as a dwelling for himself Family he rents a House contiguous to the said Compter for which he pays after the rate of Eighty pounds for And rent But now so it is May it please your Honr. Worsps that the Church Wardens & Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of St. Mildred in the Poultry hard for some Years last part and for the last Year 1722 Rated the said John Jeffeyes the Yearly some of Eight Pounds [...] the Relise of the poor of the said parish wch. is more in Proportion by the Yearly Summer of five pounds than the rest of the Inhabitants of the said Parish do pay for House of the same Rent of Eighty Pounds P Ann

Wherefore and for that Yr. petitioner Humbly Conceives himself aggresived by the said Rater Assembler made for the said Year 1722 doth most humbly appeal to Yr. Honr. of Worpps therefrom

And prays that Yr Honr. Worpps will be pleased to hear him the Premisses and Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers the Poor of the said Parish may Appear before You to hear and also the Judgement of Determination of Yr. Honr. of Worpps touching this Appeal

And Your Petitioner as in Duty bound Shall Ever Pray Etc

John Jefferys
