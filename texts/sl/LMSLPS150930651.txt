To the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justice's of the Peace for the City & London in their General Session of the Peace at Guildhall Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardnes and Overseers of the Poor of their Parish of Saint Martin in the fields in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of the Worshipfull William Plomer Lord Mayor of the said City and Barnard Turner Esquire one other of the Alder- men of the said City of London (one where of being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty Ninth Day of January One Thousand Seven Hundred and [...] Two, Elizabeth the Widow of Richard [...] the said [...] from the Parish of Saint Martin Vintry in the said City of London to the & Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields as the Place of her last Legal Settlement Where by your Petitioner's conceive themselves Aggrieved and do Appeal to their Count against the same

Yours Petitioner's therefore pray this Court will be Pleased to Appoint a Day in these Present Sessions for the hearing and determining the said Appeal And that all Persons concerned may then be directed to Attend

Josh. Hall Soll. for the Appellants.
