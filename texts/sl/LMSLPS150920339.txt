To the right Honorable Sir Watkin Lewes Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London And the Worshipful the Alderman his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City and Liberties thereof as their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Faith under Saint Paul in the City of London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of Sir Watkin Lewes Knight Mayor of the said City of London and Sir James Esdaile Knight one of the Aldermen of the said City two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City and Liberties thereof (one whereof being of the Quorum) Dated the Sixth day of March last past Mary Brown a Singlewoman and her two illegitemate Children namely Mary aged five Years and William Aged one Year for Nurture were removed from the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West in the City of London to the Parish of Saint Faith under Saint Paul in the said City as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Brown by which your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal against the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal and Pray such relief in the Premises as to this Honorable Court shall seem meet

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

J. Greenaway Sol. for the appellants 23d April 1781.
