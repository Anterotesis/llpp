To the Right Honble. the Lord Mayor the Worshipfull the Recorder of the City of London and the rest of the Justices of Oyer & Terminer & Gaol Delivery of Newgate for the Sd. City.

The Humble Petition of James Mould

Sheweth That Your Petitioner was on or about the Ninth day of July last past comitted to prison or Suspition of being concerned, along with others, not as yet taken in pensoning of one Edward Stone, a Custom House Officer.

That Your Petitioner has a Family & is very Poor having had but very litle, besides the Gaol allowance to support him, during the long Series of the Time of his Imprisontis

That Your Petitioner has been drove to great Expenses in Subpenaing a great Number of Witnesses (most of them being Sea faring men) to be present & give Evidence this Present Sessions at his Tryal.

Therefore your Petitioner humbly begs that he may be tryed. bailed or discharged or otherwise relieved this present Sessions in such manner as to you, in your Wisdom shall be thought proper

And Your Petitioner shall ever Pray Etc.

James Mould

Mr. Theed retained in the Cause
