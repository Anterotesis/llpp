To the Right Honourable Sir Charles Asgill Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City in their General Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the said Lord Mayor and Francis Cokayne Esqr. two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twelfth day of November last Edward Somerton Elizabeth his wife and their Child Elizabeth aged one Year were removed and Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Brides in the said City to the said Parish of St. Giles in the Fields as the place of their last Legall Settlement.

Your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved by the said Order or pass Warrant of the said two Justices of the peace, Appeal to this Court against the same and humbly pray that your worships will be pleased to appoint a day for the hearing and Determining of the said Appeal.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray. Etc

Humphy Elmes Attorney for the Appellts.

Saml Dailley Willm. Stable} Churchwardens

Saml Lavington Jno Baggett} Overseers of the poor
