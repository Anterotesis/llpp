The Humble Peticon of James Chambers.

13:12:1714

Pets benefican P Libtat Subdits

To the Rt Honble. Sr Samuel Stainer Knt. Lord Major of the City of London, and to the rest of the Honble. Bench of Justices Sitting at Justices Hall in the Old Baily.

The Humble Petition of James Chambers A Prisoner in Newgate.

Sheweth That yor Petr. was comitted above Eighteen Years agoe upon Suspicion of High Treason, and hath been detained there ever since a Prisoner, without being brought to his Tryall, althor he hath severall times Entered his pray Etc in this Court as the Act of Parliament directs.

Yor Petr therefore most humbly prayes that he may be either tryed this Sessions or otherwise discharged, as this Honble. Court shall think fitt.

And yor Petrs. as in duly bound shall ever pray Etc.
