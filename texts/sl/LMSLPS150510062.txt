To the Right Honrble Humphry Parsons Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Alhallows Broad street within the said City of London

Sheweth That by Vertue of an order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Third day of November 1740. [---] Mary Brown Widow was Removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Peter Cornhill within the said City of London into the Parish of Alhallows Broadstreet within the said City of London as the Place of her last legal Settlement

Yor Petrs. therefore Conceiving themselves Aggreived by the said Order or Pass Warrant Humbly Appeal Against the same And Pray such Releif in the Premisses As to this Honrble Court it shall seem meet

And yor Petrs shall ever Pray Etc

John Johnson John Stephens} Churchwardens

Jas. Budget [...] John Rowe} [...] Overseers
