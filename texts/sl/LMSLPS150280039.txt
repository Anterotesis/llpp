To the Right Honble Sr James Bateman Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Right Worpll the Rest of his Matys Justices of the peace in the General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Several parishes of St. Giles without Cripplegate St. Botolph without Aldgate St Andrew Wardrobe St Katherine Coleman St Andrew Holborn St Sepulchres St. Brides Allhallows on the Wall St Botolph Aldersgate at St Botolph without Bishopsgate.

Sheweth That Your Petitioners are very much overburthened with poor and not able to Raise sufficient Sums of money amongst themselves to releive them Notwithstanding their Assessments are greater than heretofore Yet the Charge of the poor is far above their assessments as the Same upon their Examinacon hath divers times appeared to Several Worthy Aldermen of this City appointed to hear the Same And their poor daily increasing upon them their Said parishes Some of them lying near the Suburbs and others of them in the Suburbs of this City

Their humble Petition therefore unto Your good Honour and Worships is that You will be favourably pleas'd to grant unto them the Contributions to other parish within this City for and towards the releiving the poor as they have for merly had which are particularly hereunder menconed or such farther Allowance as to Your Honr. & Worps Shall Seem meet
