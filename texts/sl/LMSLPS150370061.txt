To the [...] Humble Sr. Francis Forbes Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of his Majesties Justies of the Peace at then General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

24/6 The humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Anne Blackfry London

Sheweth That by virtue of a pass Warrant under the hands & tall of tow of his Mities Justies of the peace for the City of London (Quor unus) bearing Date the 21t. day of June Last 1726 Elizabeth Wheally wife of Thomas wheathey the Younger and their three children, Thomas John, & Charles were sent & Delivered by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Bridge ate Brides London unto the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Anne Blackfryers London alledging the said Parish to be the pleace of their last Legal settlement.

That your Petitioners Conceive themselves aggrived by the said warrant & Judgmt of the said two Justies and do most humbly appeal to your Honour and Worships therefrom.

Your Petitioners therefore must humbly pray that your Honour & Worps will he pleased to hear them in ye premisses and Order that the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor ye Parish of St. Bridge late buly may appear before your Honb & worps hear & Abide ye determinacon of the Court touching the sd Appeal

And Your Petrs shall ever pray etc.
