To the Right Honourable John Barber Esquire [...] Lordmayor of the City of London and the rest of his majestys Justices of the peace for the said City in Quarter Sessions Assembled At the Guildhall of the said city

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Christ Church in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by pass Warrant or order under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honble. John Barber Esqr Lord Mayor of the said City and Sir William Bilcers Kt Two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City of London[---] Quory unus bearing Date the Twenty Sixth Day of September now last past Benjamin Wilson was removed and Conveyed from the parish of Saint Bottolphs Bishopsgate London to the said parish of Christ Church by the name of the parish of Christ Church in Spittle fields in the County of Middlesex as the Last legal place of Settlement of the said Benjamin Wilson

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves Aggreived by the said Warrant or order of the said two Justices Humbly Appeal to this Court against the same and pray that this court would appoint Some Day this present Quarter Sessions for the Churchwardens and overseers of he poor of the said parish of saint Bottolphs Bishopsgate London and all other Parties concerned to attend to Hear and abide the Judgmetn and Determinacon of this court touching the said Appeal

And your Petr shall ever pray et

Abr Davis attorney Spittle fds for appellants
