To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London the Recorder and other his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of removal under the Hands and Seals of Sir Thomas Hallifax Knight the Lord Mayor of the said City and Frederick Bull Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof (one of them being of the Quorum) bearing Date on the Ninth day of September last past William Urton Ann his Wife and Thomas their Son Aged Eight Years and upwards were removed from the Parish of Saint Bennett Fink in the said City to the said Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell

That your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order of the said two Justices and therefore humbly Appeal to this Honourable Court against the same

And Pray this Honourable Court will please to appoint a Day for the hearing and determining the said Appeal and to make such Order therein as shall be just

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
