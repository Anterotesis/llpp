To the Right Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweh That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Sir Richard Glyn Bart: Lord Mayor of the said City of London and F: Cokayne Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one whereof is of the Quorum) bearing date the 14th. day of July 1759 Mary Nevill was removed from the Parish of Saint Dunstan in the West London to the said Parish of Saint James as the Place of her last legal Settlement; Whereby Your Petitioner think them selves Aggrieved And therefore Appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Pray this Court, that the benefit of the said Appeal may be Saved unto them until the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City of London And that ale persons concerned may then Attend To hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the same Court touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.

Johns Aldertons Mattw: Fairless Church Wardens of the said parish of Saint James

Charles Wale John Warner Overseers of the same Parish.
