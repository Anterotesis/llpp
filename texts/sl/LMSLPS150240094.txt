To the Right Honrble. Sr. Richard Hoare Knt Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt Worpll: the Rest of her Majesty's Justices of the Peace in the General Quarter Sessions assembled

1712 11/12

The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Bartholomew the less London

Sheweth That One Mary Croome Widdow by virtue of a warrant under the hands and Seals of two of her Majesty's Justices of the peace within this City one being of the Quoru) bearing Date the Eleventh Day of December last past was removed from the precinct of Whitefryers London unto the said parish of St Bartholomew the less alleadging the said parish to be the place of the last legall settlement of the said Mary Croome

That your Petitioners doubt not but to prove that the Said Mary Croome never had any legall Settlement in the Said parish of St Bartholomew the Less whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeal to your Honr: and Worpps therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray your Honr. and Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Overseers of the said precinct of White Fryers may appear before you and hear and abide the determination of your Honr: and Worpps touching this Appeale

And your Petitionrs: Shall ever Pray Etc
