To the Rt. Honourable Marshe Dickenson Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London & the rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for said City in their General Session of the Peace at Guild Hall in & for the said City Assembled

The humble Petition & appeal of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of Saffron hill Hatton Garden & Ely Rents in the Parish of St. Andrew Holborn in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands & Seals of the sd. Lord Mayor & Slingsby Bethell Esquires two of his Ma- -jestys Justices of the peace for the sd. City & the Libertys thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 15 Day of November 1756 Mary Everett Singlewoman was removed from the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London to the sd. Parish of St. Andrew Holborn in the County of Middlesex as the Place of her last Legal Setlement of the Said Mary Everett whereby Your Petitioner thinks themselves aggrieved

Joshua Cox Sollr. for the Appellts. Therefore they Humbly appeal to this Honourable Court and Pray that the said Court will be Pleased to Order the Church Wardens & Overseers of the Poor of the sd. Parish of saint Giles without Cripplegate London and all Persons concerned to attend this Court some Day in the next ensuing Session of the Peace to be holden in & for the said City of London to hear and abide the Judgement & Determination of this Court touching the sd appeal and that the Benefit of hearing the sd. appeal may be in the [...] mean time Stayed to Your Petitioners

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc
