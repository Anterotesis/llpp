To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City now in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition & Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Gabriel Fenchurch Street in the City of London

Sheweth That William Vaughan and Sarah his Wife wereby order or Pass Warrant Dated the 13th. day of December 1783 under the hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Robert Peckham Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and Richard Clarke Esqr. and Alderman of the said City Two of his Majestys of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof one being of the Quorum Removed & Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Bridget otherwise Birdes London to the said Parish of Saint Gabriel Fenchurch Street London aforesaid as the place of their last settlement

Your Petitioners & Appellants Conceive themselves Aggrieved thereby appeal to the Judgment of this Honourable Court against the same and pray that this Honourable Court will be pleased to hear & Determine the said Appeal And that all Persons Concerned may abide the Judgement and Determination thereof And that your Petitioners may be Relieved in the Premises as this Honourable Court shall seem meet

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

A: Bradley Atty for the Appellants}
