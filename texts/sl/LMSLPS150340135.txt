1723 11/4 To the Right Honoble Sr. Gerard Conyers Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London & to the Worpfull the rest of His Majestys Justices of ye. Peace for the said City & the Libties thereof in their General Quarter Sessions assembled.

The Humble Petition & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St Mary Woolnoth London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under ye. Hands and Seales of Two of His Majtys Justices of the Peace for the Said City of London & Libties thereof Quor unus) bearing Date the Eleventh day of their Instant April, Thomas Reason with Anne his Wife were removed from the Parish of St Bartholomew the Great London to the Parish pf St Mary Woolnoth London aforesaid alledging that of St. Mary Woolnoth to be the Place of their last legall Settlement

That your Peticoners concerning them selves agrieved by the said Order or Warrant of the said Two Justices Do humbly appeale to your Lordship and Worships therefrom And humbly pray That your Lordship and Worps will be pleased to hear them concering the Prmisses And that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St Bartholomew the Great London may appear before you & hear of abide the Judgment & Determination of your Lordp & Worps touching this Appeale

And yor. Petrs. shall ever pray Etc.
