To the Right Honoble. James Townsend Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Right Worshipful the Recorder and the rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the Said City and the Liberties thereof in their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition & appeal of the Churchwardens and overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Vagrant Warrant or Order under the hand and Seal of the Right honoble. James Townsend Esquire Lord Mayor one of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the Said City & the Liberties thereof bearing Date the Third day of September instant Ann Harrell Singlewoman who is alleged by the said Order to have been apprehended in the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate London as a Vagabond was removed and Conveyed from thence to the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in the County of Middlesex at the Place of her last legal Settlement

That Your Petitionrs Conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant or order do humbly appeal to this Court against the same and Pray such Relief in the Premies as to this honoble court Shall seem meet And Your Petrs shall ever Pray Etc

clare & Bampfield Sollr. for the appellts. 6. Sep: 1773
