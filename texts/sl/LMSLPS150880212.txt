To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Session of the Peace at Guildhall Assembled

The Humble Petition of The Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of The Parish of Saint Luke in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Frederick Bull and William Plomer Esqr. Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London, and Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the 11th day of March 1777. Ann Denn the Widow of Basil Denn deceased was Removed and Conveyed from The Parish of Saint Sepulchre in the City of London to the said Parish of Saint Luke as [...] ce of her last legal Settlement Whereby Your Petitioners conceive themsel [...] to be Aggrieved and do humbly Appeal against the said Order or Pass Warrant

Your Petitioners humbly Request that the Benefit of their said Appeal may be Saved unto them until the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City of London

And Your Petitioners will ever pray Etc Etc Etc

J. Jenings Attorney for the Appellts.
