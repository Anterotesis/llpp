To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majestys Justice of the Peace for the City of London and the Libertys thereof in their General Quarters Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petiton and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and [...] Libertys thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Ninth day of May One thousand seven hundred and sixty one Mary Feild Wife of William Feild was removed from the Parish of Saint Martin Outwich London to the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate London aforesaid adjudging the last legal Settlement of the said William Field and Mary his Wife to be there And Your Petioners conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Order and Judgement of the said two Justices Do humbly appeal thereform and pray such Releif in the Premisses as to Your Lordship and Worship shall seem meet and Convenient.

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Jeyes Attorney for the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate.
