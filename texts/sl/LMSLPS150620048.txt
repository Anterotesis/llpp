To the Right Honourable Francis Cokayne Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City in their General Quarter Session assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of William Sewell Citizen and Fruiterer of London and Mich [...] Bolt Citizen and Goldsmith of London.

Sheweth That your Petitioners have taken to farm of the Mayor & Comonalty and Citizens of the City of London one of the City Markets called Newgate Market and now jointly hold the same by vertue of a Leave to them thereof made.

And that by a Rate or Assessment made for the Relief of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Faith under Saint Paul's London for one year Commencing from Easter Day 1750. Your Petitioners were rated and assessed the Sum of Sixty five Pounds (to be paid at thirteen Monthly Payments) for and in respect of their tenure of the said Market, or of such part thereof as lyeth within the same Parish.

Your Petitioners Apprehending the said Rate or Assessmt. to be Excessive and very unequal and unreasonable and the said sum of 65 pounds to be much more than they ought to be Rates or Charged with Conceive them- selves to be aggrieved thereby And do humbly Appeal against the same

And Pray such Relief in the Premisses as to your Lordship and this Honble Court shall seem meet.

Leadbeater Soll. for the Appellants
