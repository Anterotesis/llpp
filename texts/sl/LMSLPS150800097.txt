To the Right Honourable Samuel Turner Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue a pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Samuel Turner and Richard Peers Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London and the Liberty thereof one being of the Quorum bearing date the Twenty eight Day of June last Margaret Webb Wife of Henry Webb gone from her and their Child Charlotte aged about two Years were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint Bartholomew the less London to the said Parish of Saint Anne as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Henry Webb Margaret his Wife and their said Child Whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved

Therefore they humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Order and Pray that Your Worships will be pleased to hear and Determine the said Appeal according to Law and Justice

And Your Petitioners Shall pray Etc S Grantham Benjn. Dorsett Peter Balchin Peter Mernices} Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of Saint Anne
