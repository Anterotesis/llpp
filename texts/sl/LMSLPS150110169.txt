To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Richard Levett Knt. Lord Majors the Citty of London And to the Rt. Worll. the Rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace att the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Mary Magdalens Old Fish Streete London

Sheweth

That by virtue a Warrant under the Hands and Seales of Twoe of his Maties Justices of the Peace within this Citty One being of the Quor dated the 26th. March 1700 One William Fry was brought by the Churchwardens of St. Buttolph Bishoppsgate To Yor. Petrs. to be mainteyned upon his Oath That his last legall Settlement was in Yor. Petrs. Parish where hee served as an Appntice wth. one William Andrewes Vintner untill 1656. hee having been a Soldier and in his Maties. service ever Since untill 1690 since wth. to me hee hath been an Inmate in St. Buttolph And that hee had noe other legall Settlement in yor. Petrs. parish wth. Appeared to be 46 Yeares since hee was a Servant in yor. Petrs. parish And is unknowne to yor. Petrs. And by Acknowledgement hath been in the Parish of St. Buttolphs what hee hath paid to the Constable Scavenger and for Trophyes and hath been releived by them as a poore Inhabitant due hath had divers Certificates under the Hands of the minister Churchwardens & Antient Inhabitants of St. Buttolphs Owning him to be an Inhabitant of their said Parish As by the Same may Appeare

Yor. Petrs. humbly Pray to be heard on their Appeale And to said the said William Fry unknowne to yor. Petrs. to the said Parish of St. Buttolphs Bishopsgate where he hath lived many yeares one been releived And had Certificates from them as aforesaid.

And they Shall Pray Etc
