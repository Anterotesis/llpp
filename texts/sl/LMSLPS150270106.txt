To the Right Honble Sr Charles Peers Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London And to the Right Worpll the rest of his Majties. Justices of the peace with Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St Bridgett als Brides London.

Sheweth That by Vertue of a Warrant under the hands & Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the peace (Quox unus) bearing Date the Thirteenth day of March last One Hester Willmott with Wooly Wilmott her daughter about Seven Years of Age were sent and delivered by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Sepulchres London unto the sd parish of St Bridgett als Brides Alleadging the Sd pish to be the place of this last Legall Settlement

That your Petitioners therefore conceive themselves agriev'd by the Sd Warrant and Judgement of the sd two Justices and do most humbly Appeal to your Worps therefrom

Your Petitioners most humbly pray Your Honour and Worps will be pleasd to hear them in the premisses and Order that the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Sd pish of St Sepulchres may appear before You and hear and abide the Determinacon of ye honour and Worrpl touching this Appeal

And they Shall ever pray Etc.
