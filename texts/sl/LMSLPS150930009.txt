To The Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London And to the rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City at their general Quarter Session of the peace for the said City

The Humble Petition of John Anderton

Sheweth That your Petitioner is an Inhabitant of the parish of Saint Leonard Eastcheap in the City of London

That by a rate of Assessment on the several occupiers of Houses Lands & in the said Parish of Saint Leonard Eastcheap made and assessed the 7th day of July 1780 for and towards the necessary releif of the poor of the said parish and for other purposes in the several Acts of Parliament for the releif of the poor mentioned for the year beginning at the Feast of Easter 1780 and ending at the same Feast 1781 Your Petitioner is lated and assessed to pay to the releif of the said poor one shilling and three pence per wich Weekly and every Week during the said year

That your Petitioner is a day labour in [...] Man and Rents only part of a small house in about in the said Parish at the Annual Sum of Ten Guineas

That your Petitioner humbly conceives he is aggreived by the said Rate or Assessment inasmuch that the same is not an equal and equitable Rate according to the several Rents and the Property of the respective Inhabitants of the said Parish

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly prays Your Worships will Quash or set asside the said rate or Assessment so far as the same relates to your Petitioner And grant your Petitioner such further and other releif therein as to your Worships shall seen just

Jas. Hill Sollr: 15 Octr. 1781.

And your Petitioner will ever pray Etc.

John Anderton
