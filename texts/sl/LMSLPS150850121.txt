To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipfull the Aldermen and Recorder of the said City at the General Sessions of the Peace in and for the said City Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseer of the Parish of Saint Michael Woodstreet London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or order under the hands and Seals of Sir Charles Asgill Knight and Baronet and Nathaniel Thomas Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Seventh day of May 1773 Francis Dambry Susanna his Wife and their Child Mary Aged Eight Years Were Removed from the Parish of Saint Ann Within Aldersgate London to the Parish of Saint Michael Wood Street London as the Place of their last Legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves Aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or Order and humbly Appeal against the same

And your Petitioners humbly pray such relief in the Premisses as to your Lordship and Worships shall seem just

And your Petitioners as in duty bound shall Ever pray.
