To the Right Honoble the Lord Mayor & the rest of the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London at the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace for the same City Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Mayor Commonalty and Citizens of the City of London Governors of the House of the Poor commonly called St. Bartholomew Hospital near West smithfield London of the Foundation of King Henry the Eight.

Sheweth That St. Bartholomews Hospital situate in [...] Parish of St. Bartholomew the less is of Antient & Royal Foundation Granted & Established by King Henry the 8th for the Relief of Sick Lame Wounded & diseased Poor, wherein some Thousands of Poor objects are Annually Relieved and Cured

That soon after the great Fine of London, Houses & Shops were made in the Buildings within the Cloisters & Precinct of the Hospital, for the Convenicnce of the Inhabitants of London (then in great distress for Houses) who Occupying the same as Tenants to the Hospital for their own benefit Became Rateable & chargeable to the Poors Rates.

That about the Year 1730 some of the Antient Buildings & Wards of the Hospital & some of the sd Houses & Shops (having become reuinous) were pulled down & since which by the Donation of Governors & other Benefactors, four large Pyles of Buildings have been Progressively elected & are now used for the Hospital, one of which Pyles or Wings contains a Hall Compting House & other Rooms wch are used for Meetings of the Governors Receiving Rents, Examining & Admissions of patients, & the other three Pyles of Buildings are used for Wards for the patients & their Nurses only.

That one of the sd. Pyles or Building of the Hospital being finished about the year 1758 the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor & inhabitants of the Parish of St. Bartholomew the less by a Rate Intitled a Rate or Assessment on the Inhabitants & occupiers of Lands Houses Shops Sheds Tenements & Ward houses within the Parish of St. Bartholomew the less London made & Assessed by them the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor & Inhabitants of the sd. Parish whose names are thereunto subscribed pursuant to the several Acts of parliament made in that behalf for three Months for or towards the relief of the poor of the sd. Parish as their necessity shall require have Assessed & Rated Your Petitioners for such Wards of the sd. Hospital at & ofter the Rate of £20. a year for & towards the Relief of the poor of the sd. Parish as followeth.. that is to say "The Mayor Commonalty & Citizens of the City of London Governors of the House of the poor, commonly called St. Bartholomews Hospital

near Westsmithfield London, of the Foundation of King Henry the 8th. For Lands, on which stood Tenements & Houses by them pulled down & demolished the Tenants [---] whereof were used to be charged in the Poors Rate 8s/4d Weekly. £1.13.4 Monthly £5 quarterly: That the Parish have continued such Rate upon such Wards for the poors ever since to the detriment & loss of the Charity.

That about the Year An Elaboratory also hath been Erected for the Service of the Hospital only upon Ground within the Liberty & Precinct thereof which the sd, Churchwardens Overseers & Inhabitants have Assessed after the Rate of £2.18 a Year as followeth that is to say the Mayor Commonalty & Citizens of the City of London Governors of the House of the poor commonly called St Bartholomews Hospital near Westsmithfield London, of the Foundation of King Henry the 8th. for their Elaboratory where sevl. Houses stood which were used to be charged in the poors Rate Weekly1s [...] Monthly 4s.. Quarterly 12s. which Rate hath been continued & now continues to be rated to the detriment & loss of the Charity.

That all the sd. 4 Pyles of Buildings being finished in 1766, the Governors pulled down 19 Old Houses & Shops (part of the sd. Antient Buildings) to make an Area to the Hospital for the benefit of the patients which is a void anoccupied space of Grand & about 66 Yards by 60 for which the sd. Parish Officers have Assessed & rated the sd. Governors after the rate of £41.5 a year-as followeth that is to say "The Mayor Commonally & Citizens of the City of London Governors "of the House of the Poor commonly called St. Bartholomews Hospital near "Westsmithfield London of the Foundation of King Henry the 8th. for Land "on which stood Tenements & Houses (within the Quadrangle of the sd "Hospital beside such as were before by them pulled down & demolished) "by them pulled down & demolished the Tenants whereof were used to be "charged in the Poors Rate 17s & 2d [...] .2 Weekly £3.8.9 Monthly £10.6.3 "quarterly". and which sd. Sums of £20. £22.8 & £41.5 making in all £63.13 a year (over & above what is charged upon their respective Officers residing in the Hospital) was for the Year 1767 & now continues to be Assessed & rated on the sd. Governors in respect of the Premes.

That your Petitioners are Adviced & humbly Insist that Your Petitioners servts. or Poor Patients Inhabitants or occupiers of the sd. premes are not liable nor ought to be charged any thing to the Poors Rate.. in respect of the sd. Hospital Elaboratory or void space of Ground.

Therefore your petitioners humbly Appeal from such rate & Assessment, in regard to the sd. above

mentd. Sums of Money and pray Such Redress and relief in the Premisses, as to this Court Shall Seeing meet

And shall ever Pray
