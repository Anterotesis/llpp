To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. William Withers Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. The rest of Her Maties. Justices of ye. Peace In the General Quartr. Sessions Assembled

The humble Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Georges in Southwark in the County of Surry

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace within the City of London one being of the Quox Dated the 8th. day of November Anno Din 1707. One Edward Skepper was by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Sepulchers London sent to yor Petrs. to be provided for & maintained upon the Oath of the said Edward Skepper that he was last legally settled in yor. Petr. Parish where hee Lived for the space of three moneths or thereabouts in the Year of our Lord 1684 in Blackman Streete in the Said Parish Whereas Truth the said Edward Skepper lived a Servant 2 yeares about 18 Yeares agoe in the beginning of the Reigne of the late King William in the Parish of Defert in Flinchshire in Wales

Wherefore yor. Petrs. humbly Pray yor Honor. & Worpps. To hear them in the prmisses And Quash the said Warrt. made by the said twoe Justices And Order the said Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the said Parish of St. Sepulchers to appear here before you to Shew Cause why They should not Provide for and mainteyne Edward Skepper And after send the said Edward Skepper to the said Parish of St. Sepulchers to be by them Provided for and mainteyned untill They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Lawe

And They shall Ever Pray Etc
