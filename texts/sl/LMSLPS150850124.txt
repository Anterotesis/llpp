To the Right Honble Frederick Bull Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipfull the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Sessions of the Peace assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one whereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the twenty first Day of January last Mary Smith Singlewoman with her female Bastard Child aged three Years and a half for Nurture, was removed from the Parish of Saint Sepulchre within the City of London to the said Parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street as the place of her last legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices and therefore humbly appeal to this Honourable Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them And that the Hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned untill the next General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said City

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
