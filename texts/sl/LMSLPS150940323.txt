To the Right. Honorable the Lord Mayor of the city of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said city now in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Bridget otherwise Brides in the City of London.

Sheweth That Richard Humphries was by order or Pass Warrant dated the 20th. day of August 1783 under the Hands and seals of the Right Honorable Nathaniel Newnham Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and Sir William Plomer Knight and Alderman of the said City two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof one being of the Quorum removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Sepulchre London to the said Parish of Saint Bridget otherwise Brides London aforesaid as the Place of his last Legal Settlement.

A. Bradley Attorney for the Appellants} Your Petitioners & Appellants conceiving themselves aggrieved thereby humbly appeal to the Judgment of this Honorable Court against the same and pray that this Honorable Court will be pleased to hear and determine the said Appeal And that all persons concerned may abide the Judgment and Determination thereof And that your Petitioners may be relieved in the Premises as to this Honorable Court shall seem meet.

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray.
