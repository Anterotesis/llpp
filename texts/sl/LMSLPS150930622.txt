To the Right Honle. Sir William Plomer Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof, in their General Quarter session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Sir William Plomer Knight and Thomas Wright Esquires, two of His Majestys Justices of the peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof (One being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty Seventh day of May 1782, Mary Mitchell Singlewomen was removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Gregory London to the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex, whereby, Your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved, and humbly Appeal to this Court against the same.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that your Worships will be pleased to grant that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determination thereof be Adjourned until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City and Liberties thereof and that all Persons concerned therein may be ordered then to attend this Court touching the said Appeal.

Wm. Saxon Solbr. for Appellts.
