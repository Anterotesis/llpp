To the Right Honble the Lord Mayor of the City of London and others his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City in Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of John Bottomley

Sheweth That Sr. Francis Gosling knt. one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said City on the 20th. day of March last Past made an Order in the Words following that in to say To Mr. John Bottomley of the City of London Pawnbroker Whereas it Appears to me Sir Francis Gosling knt. one of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said City on the Oath of Elizabeth Winspeare the Wife of William Winspeare of Black Friars London Porter that within two years last Past she Pawned with you the said John Bottomley a silk Camblet suit of Boys Cloath to wit a Coat Waistcoat and a Pair of Breeches in the whole of the value of Eighteen Shillings for the sum of Five Shillings And Whereas it Appears to me the said Justice as well on the Oath of the said Elizabeth Winspeare as of Catherine Burn that on the sixteenth of this Instant March she tendered to you the said John Bottomley the sum of nine Shillings in Order to take thereout the said sum of Five Shillings and the Interest and Charges for Warehouse Room Agreed upon which Amounted to Two Shillings and three Pence or thereabouts being after the Rate of one Penny Half penny for each month from the time of lending the said Five Shillings And Whereas it duly Appears to me that you the said John Bottomley have refused to Accept the Money so as aforesaid tendered to you Now on hearing of the said Matter in the Presence of you the said John Bottomley I do Order and direct you the said John Bottomley forthwith to deliver up to the said Elizabeth Winspeare the said Suit of Boys

so by her Pawned to you as aforesaid Given under my Hand at the Guildhall of the said City this twentieth day of March in the year of our Lord 1765

Fras. Gosling

That your Petitioner is Adivsed that he hath just Cause to Appeal from the said Order and that the same ought to be Quashed

Your Petitioner therefore most humbly Prays your Lordship to take the Premisses into Consideration and to Order that the matter of the said Appeal may be forthwith heard and determined by your Lordship

And your Petitioner shall ever Pray
