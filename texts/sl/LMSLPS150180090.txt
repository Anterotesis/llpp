To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Thomas Rawlinson Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt. Worll. The Rest of Her Maties. Justices of ye. Peace In the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Austins London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace within the City of London One being of the Quor Dated the Sixth day of August Anno Din 1706. One Martha Quicklebrough with her twoe Children named Martha and Mary were brought to yor. Petrs. by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Gyles without Cripplegate London To be Provided for upon the Oath of the said Martha Quicklebrough That her husbands last legall Settlement was in yor. Petrs. Parish where hee lived one Yeare as an hired Servt. with Thomas Jennal in the Old Exchange a Diamond Cutter Whereas in truth the said Joseph Quicklebrough before his supposed Marriage with the said Martha was married to one Susanne Valence whoe is now liveing And by whome hee hath Severall Children Audris the only lawfull wife of the said Joseph Whereby the prtended Marriage with the said Martha is Void

Yor. Petrs. humbly Pray yor. Honor. & Worpps. to hear them in the prmisses And Quash the said Warrant made by the said twoe Justices And send the said Martha with her twoe Children back to the Parish of St. Gyles Cripplegate To be by them provided for and mainteyned untill They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Lawe

And yor. Petrs. shall Ever Pray Etc

Appeal of St. Austins agt. Cripplegt.
