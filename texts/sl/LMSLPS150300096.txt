To the Rt Honrble Sr John Ward Knt Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt Worpll The Rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City in their general quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Dionis Backchurch London

Sheweth That one Mary Walker wife of William Walker by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of two of his Maties Justices of the Peace for the City of London (Quorm Unus) bearing Date the Eighth Day of January instant was removed from the parish of Allhallows the less in the said City of London unto the said Parish of St. Dionis Backchurch alleadging the said Parish to be the Place of her last legal Settlement

That your Petrs Doubt not but to prove that the said Mary Walker hath not any legal Settlement in the said parish of St. Dionis Backchurch whereby they conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices And do humbly Appeal to your Honr and Worpps therefrom

Your Petrs therefore most humbly pray's your Honr and Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens, & overseers of the said Parish of Allhallows the less may appear before you and hear and abide the judgment & determination of your Honr and Worpps therefrom touching this Appeal

And they Shall ever pray Etc
