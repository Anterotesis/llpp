To the Right Honourable Sir Edward Bellamy Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City in their General Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Samuel Patison an Inhabitant of the Parish of Christ Church London

Sheweth That your Petitioner is an Inhabitant of the said Parish of Christ Church and an Occupyer of one House within the said parish the Rent and Taxes of which are paid by your Petitioner who for the year 1788 hath been rated by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the said parish in much Larger Sums of money that he ought to have been towards the releif of the poor of the said parish which said Rate or Assessment your Petitioner Humbly Conceives is in many respects unequall Arbitary and illegall and particularly for that diverse Inhabitants and Occupiers of Houses within the said parish some of which pay as Large and others much Larger Rents than your Petitioner whose Circumstances do Exceed or at least Equall your your Petitioners have for that year been rated in which less sums of money than your Petitioner And your Petitioner Farther Sheweth that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said parish for the said Year 1733 have made up their Accounts for the said year and procused the same to be Allowed notwithstanding many Charges and disbursements are therein continued which are contrary to Law and ought not to be Allowed by reason whereof great Sums of money which ought to have been applyed to the releif of the poor of the said Parish are made use of and disburst for other purposes than by Law they ought to be and thereby the Charge upon the Inhabitants of the said Parish and particularly upon your Petitioner is Greatly Increased and the Rate theirby become much more burden some and as your Petitioner Conceives Illegall by reason of such the warrantable overcharge.

Your Petitioner therefore conceiving himself greatly aggrieved by the Rate or Assessment so made by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the said parish for and towards the necessary Releif of the poor of the said parish Humbly prays that the said rate may be quashed and the said Accounts disallowed and that your Petitioner may be releived in the premisses in such manner as to this Court shall seen Just and Fitting

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.

The Appel. Occupys one House in Warwick Lane in the Sd. parish and pays for ye Same P Ann 20:0:0 And is assessed to the poor weekly 0:1:6
