To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City in their General Quarter Session of the peace for the same City Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Master, Governors and Commonalty of the Mystery of Barbers of London

Sheweth That Your Petitioners by a certain Rate or Assessment made for the Relief of the poor of the parish of Saint Olave Silver Street London on or about the thirtyeth day of September now last past (by the Description of the Worshipful Company of Barbers) are Assessed and Charged towards the said Relief in the Sum of Nine pounds four Shillings for and in Respect of their Hall and premisses situate in Monckwell Street for one Quarter of a Year from the twenty fourth day of June 1773 to the 29th: day of September following

That Your Petitioners are advised that the said Rate or Assessment so made on Your Petitioners is excessive and unequal And Your Petitioners find themselves aggrieved thereby And therefore humbly Appeal to this Honorable Court against the said Rate or Assessment And pray Relief in the Premisses And that this Honorable Court will be pleased to hear and Determine this their Petition and Appeal at this present General Quarter Session of the Peace

James Mary & Sollicitor for the Appellants
