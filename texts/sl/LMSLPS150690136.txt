To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and the rest of the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London & the liberties thereof at the General Quarter Session of the peace holden at the Guildhall in and for the said City & liberties Assembled an Monday the Fourth Day of December 1758.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant or Order the hands & Seals of Thomas Rawlinson & William Alexander two of his Majesties of the peace for the said City one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Second day of November 1758. Charles Martin and Elizabeth his Wife were removed from the Parish of St. Botolph without Aldersgate London to the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London as the Place of their last legall Settlement

That your Petitioners Conceive themselves agrieved by the said Warrant or Order and humbly Appeal against the same

Your Petrs. therefore Humbly pray your Lordship and Worships Judgmt. & Determination touching the said Appeal and to be Relieved in the premises and that the said Appeal may be allowed

And your Petrs. shall ever pray Etc

Thomas Stagg Sollr. for the said Appellants
