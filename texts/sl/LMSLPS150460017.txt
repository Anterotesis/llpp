To the Right Honble Sir Edwd. Bellamy Kt Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City in their General Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled

The Humble petition and Appeal of Samll Patison an Inhabitant of the Parish of Christ Church London

Sheweth That your Petitioner is an Inhabitant of the said parish of Christ Church and Occupier of one House within the said parish the rents and taxes of wch. are paid your Petitioner who for the year 1734 hath been rated by the Church Wardens and overseers of the said parish in much Larger sums of money than he ought to have been towards the releife of the poor of the said parish which said Rate or Assessment your Petitioner Humbly conceives is in many respects unequall Arbitary and Illegall & Pticularly that diverse Inhabitants and Occupyers of Houses within the said Parish some of wch. pay as Large and others much Larger rents than your Petitioner whose Circumstances do Exceed or at Least Equall your Petitioners have for that year been rated in much less sums of money than your Petitioner and your Petitioner further Sheweth that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish for the said Year 1734 have made up their Accounts for the said Year and procured the same to be Allowed notwithstanding many Charges and Disbursments and therein contained which me contrary to Law and ought not be have been allowed by reason whereof great Sums of money which out to have been applyed to the releife of the poor of the said parish are made use of and disburst for other purposes than by Law they ought to be and thereby the Charge upon the Inhabitants of the said parish and particularly upon your Petitioner is greatly Increased and the rate thereby become much more burdensome and as your Petitioner conceives illegall by reason of which unwarrantable Overcharge

The Appl. Occupies one House in Warwick lane in the said parish and pays for the same to ann 20:0:0 And is Assessed to the poor weekly 0:1:6

{ Your Petitioner therefore conceiving himself greatly aggreived by the rate or Assessment so made by the Church Wardens and Overseers of the said parish for and towards the necessary releife of the poor of the said parish humbly prays that the said rate may be quashed and the said Accounts disallowed And that your Petitioner may be releived in the premisses in such manner as to this Court shall seem just and fitting

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.
