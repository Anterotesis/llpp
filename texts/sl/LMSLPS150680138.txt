To the Rt Hon Marshe Dickinson Esqr Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the Same City in their General Quarter Session Assembled

The Humble Petition & Appeal of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Romford in the County of Essex.

Sheweth That by an Order or pass Warrt. under the hands & Seals of the Rt Worshipful Francis Cokayne Esqr & Slingsby Bethell Esqr two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London & the [...] thereof (one of them being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 22d. Day of June last past Ann the Wife of Thomas Hawkins therein mentioned to be a Sailor in his Majties Service and their two Children Ann aged 4 Years & John Aged 2 years were afterward vizt. on the 7th. Instant & not before removed from the Parish of St Alphage in London into the sd. Parish of Romford as the place of the last legal Settlemt. of the sd. Thos. Hawkins Ann his Wife & their said two Children.

That Your Petitioners humbly Conceive themselves aggrieved by the Premes And therefore Appeal to this honourable Court And pray such releif in the same as shall Seen meet

And for-as-much as Your Petitioners have not been enabled (by reason of the Shortness of the time Since Such removal) to give due Notice of this Appeal and prepare themselves to have the Same Determined at this present Session they humbly pray the benefit thereof may not be lost And also that it may stand Adjourned until the next Genl Quarter Session then to be heard.

Gapper Attorney.

And your petitioners Shall pray Etc.
