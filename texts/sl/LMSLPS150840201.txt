To the Right Honourable Frederick Bull Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Worshipful Justices of the Peace at the General Session of the Peace now assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Bartholomew the Great London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Nineteenth day of November One thousand seven hundred and seventy three Sarah Holland Widow of Stephen Holland and her two Children (to wit) Mary aged fourteen Years and upwards and Frances aged Ten Years [...] removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn London into the said Parish of Saint Bartholomew the Great London as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Sarah Holland

Your Petitioners therefore Conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant and humbly Appeal [...] the same

And your Prtitioners shall ever pray
