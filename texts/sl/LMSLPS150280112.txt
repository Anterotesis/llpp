To the Rt Honrble Sr James Bateman Knt Lord Maior of the City of London And Also to the Rt Worpll: the Rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City in their general quarter Sessions Assembled

8/4 The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St Buttolph without Aldgae London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of two of his Maties Justices of the Peace for the City (one being of the Quorn) bearing date the Eighth Day of Aprill instant John Blackhall and Ann Blackhall his wife were removed from the Parish of St Stephen in Coleman: Street London unto the said Parish of St Buttolph without Aldgate alleadging the said Parish to be the place of their last legall Settlement.

That Your Petitionrs: Doubt not but to prove that the said John Blackhall and his wife hath not any legal Settlement in the said parish of St Buttolph without Aldgate whereby they conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices do humbly appeal to your Honr and Worpps therefrom

Your Petitionrs therefore most humbly pray your Honr and Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses And that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the said parish may appear before you and hear and abide the Determination of your Honr and Worpps touching this Appeal.

And they Shall Ever pray Etc.
