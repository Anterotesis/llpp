To the Right Honoble the Lord Mayor and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Libertys thereof

The Humble petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of Order or Passwarrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Libertys thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 22d. Day of March 1743 ann Cork Wife of Charles Cork and their two Children Mary Aged Nine Years and James Aged One Year Were Removed from the Parish of St. Botolph Aldersgate London To the Parish of St. Botolph Bishopgate London Aforesaid Adjudging the Last Legal Settlement of the said Ann Cork and her two Children to be there and Your Sd [...] Conceiving themselves Agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices Do humbly Appeal there from and humbly pray that the benefitt of the said Appeal be Saved unto them and that the hearing and Determining thereof be Adjourned to the Next General Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said City of London and that Your Lordship and Worships Will made an Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of St. Botolph Aldersgate and All Persons Concerned Do Attend the Court of the said next Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said City to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of the same Court touching the said Appeal

Burr Attorney for Bpsgate Parish-}

And yr. Peter. Shall ever pray Etc
