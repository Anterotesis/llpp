To the Right Honourable John Wilkes Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipful the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldersgate without in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one wehereof being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Eighth Day of March last David Tuke (a Lunatic) Susanna his Wife and their three Children to wit John aged nine Years William aged seven Years and Thomas aged five Years (which said John and William have generally gained no Settlement for themselves) were removed from the Parish of Alhallows on the Wall in the said City of London to the said Parish of Saint Botolph Aldergate without as the Place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the Order of the said two Justices humbly appeal against the same and pray that this Honourable Court will please to order that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them And that the hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned unto the next General Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said City

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
