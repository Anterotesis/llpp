To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London Mr. Recorder and the rest of the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition of George Selby

Sheweth That on the Twentieth day of March in the sixth Year of his present Majesty's Reign your Petitioner was Convicted before George Nelson Esquire then and still Lord Mayor of the City of London and then and Still one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London for neglecting to place or put up in a Conspicuous manner on or ever his form and in a large fair and legible Character wrote or printed a true and particular Account of the Quantity of Codfish which he had for Sale in Billingsgate Market on Tuesday the 18th. Instant before he exposed the same for Sale And is Adjudged to pay and forfeit the Sum of five pounds

Your Petitioners conceiving himself Aggrieved thereby Doth humbly Appeal against the said Conviction as being illegal and Prays such Relief in the Premises as to your Lordship and Worships shall seem meet.

Fras. Salt Attry for Appellt.
