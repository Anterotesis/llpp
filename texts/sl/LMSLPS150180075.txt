To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Robert Bedingfeld Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt. Worll. The rest of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace In the Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of Christchurch London

Sheweth That one William Jenkins Anne his wife & Anne & Henry their Sonne & Daughter of virtue of a Warrant Dated 19th. June 1707. under the hands & Seales of twoe of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace within the City of London (one being of the Quor) was brought by the Churchwardens of the Parish of St Bartholomew the lesse to yor. Petr. to be provided for on the Oath of the said William Jenkins That hee had lived in the Parish of Christchurch with Mr. Greene a Surgeon for the Space of Twoe yeares and upwards as a hired Servt att Â£7. P Annu Wages Whereas in Truth the said William Jenkins hath been a Marryed man above 25. yeares past & dwelt in the Pish of St. Bartholomew the lesse long before his Comeing into the Service of Mr. Greene And that his wife and Family have constantly dwelt in the said Parish of St Bartholomew the lesse for 25. yeares past where hee hath alsoe Resided Excepting his twoe yeares Service with Mr. Greene as aforesaid

Yor. Petrs. humbly pray That their Appeale may be heard And that the Said William Jenkins with his wife and Children may be Sent back to the said Parish of St. Bartholomew the lesse To be there provided for by them being there setled according to the Lawe
