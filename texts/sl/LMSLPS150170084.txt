To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Thomas Rawlinson Knt. Lord Major of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. The rest of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace In the Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Johns Wapping In the County of Middx

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of Her Maties. Justices of the Peace within the City of London (One being of the Quox) Dated the 10th. day of October Anno Dni 1706. One Margery Radland late wife of Hugh Radland were brought to yor. Petrs. by the Churchwardens & Overseers of [...] the Poore of the Parish of St. James Dukes place London To be Provided for upon the Oath of the Said Margery That her husbands last legall Settlement was in yor. Petrs. Parish where her husband Lodged att the house of one Mr. Murray in Cock Alley on the 15th. day of November 1685 (being the day they were Married) And for above twoe monethes after At which tyme the Said Hugh Radland went to Sea Whereas in truth the Said Hugh Radland was not an Inhabitant in yor. Petrs. Parish by the Space of 40 dayes before May 1685. And soe by the Law never had any legal Settlement in yor. Petrs. Said Parish

Yor Petrs. humbly Pray yor. Honor. & Worpps. to hear them in the prmisses And Quash the Said Warrant made by the Said twoe Justices And send the Said Margery Radland back to the Parish of St. James Dukes place To be by them Provided for and mainteyned untill They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Lawe.

And yor. Petrs. Shall Ever Pray Etc.
