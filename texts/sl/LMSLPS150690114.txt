To the Right Honourable Sir Richard Glyn Knt. Lord Mayor and the rest of the Honourable Commissioners of Oyer and Terminer and Goal Delivery of Newgate holden for the City of London in Session Assembled

The Humble Petition of Robert Bridges.

Sheweth That Your Petr. was on the 17th. day of October last Committed to Newgate on the Oath of John Curd and charged with robbing John Lawley Esqr. on the highway of a hat a pair of Silver Buckles and a pair of Shoes for which Supposed offence Your Petitioner hoped to have been Tryed at the last Session. but the Indictment not being found tile the Adjournment of the same Session holden at Guildhall the 2d, Novr. following Your Petr. hath remained in Newgate ever Since.

Yor. Petr. therefore most humbly prays Your Lordship & this Honble. Court that he may at this present Session be Tried Bailed or Discharged of the offence aforesaid according to the Statute Etc.

And Your Petr. hall ever pray Robert Bridges 6 Decr. 1758
