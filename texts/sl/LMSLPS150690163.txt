To the right honourable Sir Charles Asgill Knight Lord mayor of the City of London And is the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City at their General Quarter Sessions assembled the Eleventh day of January One thousand Seven hundred and fifty eight

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant dated the twenty sixth day of November last past under the hand and Seal of Francis Cockayne Esquire one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace of the said City Isabella Ford therein said to be the Wife of James Ford gone from her was removed from the Parish of Saint Mary Wolnoth London to the said Parish of Saint John of Wapping as the Place of her last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant and Appeal against the same

That your Petitioners have given Notice to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Mary Woolnoth of this their Appeal

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray this Court that they may be heard at this present General Quarter Sessions of the Peace touching their said Appeal And that all parties concerned may attend and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the Same

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

Thos. Cotton Attorney for the Appellants Red Lyon Street Wapping

James Bishop H Batley} Churchwardens

John Beatson Wm Walker} Overseers of the Poor
