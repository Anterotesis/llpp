To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in General Quarter Session of the Peace at the Guildhall in the said City assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the hands and Seals of John Sawbridge Esquire Mayor and John Wilkes Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London and Liberty thereof one being of the Quorum bearing Date the Nineteenth Day of December last A Woman and Child in the said Order called and described by the Names and Descriptions of Elizabeth Coward the Widow of George Coward deceased and George her Son aged ten years were removed and conveyed from the Parish of Saint Giles Cripplegate without London to the said Parish of Saint Anne (in the said Order called and described by the name of the Parish of Saint Anne Soho in the County of Middlesex) as the Place of their last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Adjudication of the said two Justices and their said Eeder And pray that the benefit of this Appeal may be saved to your Petitioners and that the hearing and determining thereof may be adjourned to the next general Session of the peace to be taken for the said City and the Liberty thereof

And Your Petitr shall pray Etc

Joseph Poley Wm Simpson} Churchwardens and

Ja Marshall Still Wilson} Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Anne
