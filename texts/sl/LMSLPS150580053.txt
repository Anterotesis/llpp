To the Right Worshipfull the Justices of the Peace in and for the City of London in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Benedict Vulgarly Bennet Fink London

Sheweth That by an Order or Warrant made under the Hands and Seals of The Right Honourable William Benn Esquire Lord Mayor of the said City of London and the Right Worshipfull Francis Cockayene Esquire two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said City bearing Date the Twenty Sixth day of November 1746 James Chaplin Ann his Wife and their two Children James aged five years and Benjamin aged two Years were removed from the Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldergate London to the said Parish of Saint Benedict as the Place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves aggreived by the said Removal humbly appeale against the said Order or Warrant And pray such Relief in the Premes as this Honourable Court shall think just and reasonable And that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Botolph without Aldersgate and all other Partys concerned may then attend hear and abide the final Determination of Your Worships

And Your Petitioners shall Pray Etc

J Alexander Sollr. for the Appellants
