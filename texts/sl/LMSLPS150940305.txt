To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties therefore at their General Session of the Peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the said City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue a Pass Warrant or Order of Removed under the Hands and Seals of [---] Nathaniel Newnham Esquire (late Lord Mayor) and John Hart Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof one being of the Quorum bearing date the fourth day of November One Thousand Seven hundred and Eighty three Frances the Wife of Samuel Israel who hath deserted his was removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint James Dukes Place in the said City of London to the said Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the said City as the last legal Settlement of the said Frances

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal of the said two Justices Do therefore humbly appeal to this Court against the same Praying such Relief in the premises as to this Court shall seem meet.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Jno. Fisher & son Sollicitors for the Appellants
