To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and others his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof at their General Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of Saint George in the County of Middx

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace (to wit) Brass Crosby Esquire and Thomas Hallifax Esquire (one being of the Quorum) for the City of London aforesaid bearing Dated the Fourth day of this instant December Ann Wilkinson Wife of Jonathan Wilkinson (who had Deserted her and was not to be found) and her Three Children Thomas Aged Four Years Joseph Aged Two Years and an Half and Charles Aged Ten Months was Removed from the parish of Saint Augustin in the City of London to the said parish of Saint George in the said County of Middlesex, And the said last mentioned Two Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and Liberties thereof in and by the said Order of Removal Adjudged the last Legal Settlement of the said Ann Wilkinson and her Three Children to be in the said parish of Saint George

That your Petitioners are Advised and Believe that the last Legal Settlement of the said Ann Wilkinson and her Three Children is not in the said parish of Saint George as by the said Order is Supposed and therefore they Conceive themselves to be Aggrieved by the said Order and Determination of the said Two Justices and Do Humbly appeal to your Worships against the same

Your Petitioners therefore as well on the part and behalf of themselves as of the Inhabitants and Parishioners of the said parish of Saint George Do Humbly Pray that the said Order of Removal of the said Ann Wilkinson and her Three Children from the said Parish of Saint Augustin to the said parish of Saint George may be Quashed And that you Worships Would please to make, such further order therein for the Reimbursing and Paying your Petitioners as well the Costs and Expences they have been put to in Maintaining and Providing for the said Wilkinson and her said Three Children in Obedience to the said Order As also the Costs and Charges they have or may be put to in Prosecuting this then Appeal against the said Order And that your Petitioners may be further and otherwise Relieved in the premises as to your Worships shall seem Meet

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc,

Major Wright Sollr for the Appellants
