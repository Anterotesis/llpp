To the Right Honourable Sir William Billers Knight Lord Mayor and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London at the Quarter Sessions assembled

The Humble Petition and appeal of Joseph Wichs and William Howes Substantiall Householders[---] Inhabitants and Churchwardens of the parish of Allhallows on the Wall London

Sheweth That the said Parish of allhallows on the Wall London now is and for Severall years last past hath been Scituate divided and Extended intot he Severall Wards of Broadstreet, Bishopsgate Aldgate and Lymestreet in the said City of London and that there now is and for Severall years last past there hath been a Distinct Alderman for oath of the said Wards that John Kellaway Richard Beckington Francis Holford and Charles Pole have been Appointed Joint Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish and now Act as Such under a- ioynt appointment or Warrant Under the hands & Seals of Sir Gerrard Conyers and Sir Richard Hopkins Knights, Micajah Perry and Robert Godschall Esqrs. Aldermen of the said Severall Wards

Your Petitioners humbly apprehending themselves to be Aggrieved by the said Joint Appointment or Warrant and conceiveing the Same to be Illegall do humbly Appeale from the Same and pray that the said Loyut appointment or Warrant way be Quashed and that they may be otherwise releived as to your Lordship and Worships Shall Seen meet

And yor. peticonr. shall ever pray Etc

Joseph Wilks Willm Howes
