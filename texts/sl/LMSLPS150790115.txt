To the Right Honorable Thomas Harley Lord Mayor of the City of London And he Rest of the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Martin Vintrey in the said City.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the second Day of February instant Patrick Doyle Elizabeth [---] his Wife and their Three Children, to wit Mary Aged four Years Catherine Aged Three Years and Elizabeth aged fifteen, Months were removed and Conveyed out of the Parish of the Holy Trinity within the said City of London in the Parish of Saint Martins Vintrey aforesaid as the Place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly Appeal against the same And Pray that he Benefit of the said Appeal and the Hearing determining thereof may Stand adjourned [...] until the next General Quarter Sessions, and for Such further Relief in the Premisses as to this Honorably Court it. shall Seem meet.

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc.
