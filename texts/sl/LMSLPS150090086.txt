To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Humpfry Edwin: Knt Lord Maior of the City of London And the Rt. Worll. the Rest of his Maties. Justices of the Peace att the Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled

The humble peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Mary Magdalens Oldfishstreete London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the Hands and Seales of twoe of his Maties Justices of the Peace of this City (one being of the Quox) Dated the 15th day of September Anno Dm 1698 One Anne Lane was by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Gyles without Cripplegate London sent to yor. Petrs. to be provided for upon her Oath That her last legall Settlement was in yor. Petrs. Parish where shee served as an Appntice wth one John Cooke and Mary his wife for the space of nyne moneths & hath had noe other settlemt. elswhere since whereas in truth the said Anne had not continued wth. the said Jno. Cooke in the said Parish of St. Mary Magdalens as a servant for the space of one whole yeare As appeares by the said Warrant

Yor: Petrs. therefore humbly pray yor: Honors & Worpps to hear the mall in the prmisses and Order the said Churchwardens of St. Gyles wthout. Cripplegate to Appear here before you And alsoe to send the said Anne Lane to the Churchwardens of the said Parish of St. Gyles wthout Cripplegate to be by them kept & provided for untill they can Free themselves of the charge thereof by due course of Lawe

And They shall Pray Etc
