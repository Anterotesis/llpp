To the Right Honoble. Sr. Richard Levett Knt. Lord Mayor of the Citty of London And to the Rt. Worll the rest of his Maties. Justices of the Peace in the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poore of the parish of St. Buttolph without Aldgate London

Sheweth

That one last Eldred Kingstone widdow by virtue of a warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe of his Maties Justices of the peace one being of the Quox dated 30 July 1700 was brought to yor. Petrs by the Churchwardens of the parish of St. Buttolph without Bishopp gate on the Oath of the said [...] Eldred That her last legall Settlement was in the said parish of St. Buttolph without Aldgate her late husband having Lived there before King Charles the Second Death and over Since untill They came into the said parish of St. Buttolph Bishoppgate whereas in truth the said East Eldred Kingston nor her husband were Ever legally Settled in the said parish of St. Buttolph Aldgate

Yor. Petrs. therefore pray that yor. Honour and Worpps would Order the said Chruchwardens of St. Buttolph Bishoppsgate to appeare before you And to heare them in the prmisses And to send the Said East Eldred Kingstone to the Churchwardens of St. Buttolph Bishoppsgate To be pvided for untill They Cann Free themselves of the Charge thereof by Due Course of Lawe

And yor. Petrs. Shall pray Etc
