To the Worshipful His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Session of the peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Reginald Dennison for and on the behalf of the Master Wardens and society of the art and Mistery of Apothecaries of the City of London

Sheweth That on or about the 17th. day of June last the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Ann Black Fryars London Did make an Assessment on the Inhabitants of the said parish for the Necessary relief of the poor thereof for one Quarter of a Year commencing at Easter Day last

That in and by such Rate or Assessment Your Petitioner is rated at the sum of three Pounds sixteen Shillings and six pence for the Hall belonging to the said Master Wardens and society and also at the Sum of Five pounds six Shillings and three pence for the Capital stock employed in Trade carried on in the Name of the said society for the benefit of several of the Members thereof

That your Petitioner Apprehending the said Assessment to be illegal and unequal and finding himself or the said Society aggrieved thereby Doth appeal to this Court against the same and humbly prays that he may be heard by Counsel against the said Assessment and that the same so far as it respects the Hall and Capital Stock of the said society may be Quashed or that your Petitioner may have such other relief as to your Worships shall seem meet and that you Will be pleased to appoint a Day for the Traverse and Determining of his said Appeal

And Your Petitioner will ever Pray Etc
