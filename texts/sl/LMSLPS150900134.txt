To the Right Honourable Samuel Plumbe Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London & the Worshipful the Alderman his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Allhallows London Wall

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant dated the 4th day of June last under the hands and Seals of the Right Honourable Samuel Plumbe Esquire Lord Mayor and James Esdaile two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City, Hannah the Wife of Thomas Stewart who hath deserted her, and her two Children Vizt Mary Aged two years and Susannah Aged two months was Conveyed from the Parish of Saint Gile's without Cripplegate London to the said Parish of Allhallows London Wall as the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners Conceived themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant of the said two Justices have Appealed against the same.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that this Court will be pleased to hear and determine the said Appeal and make such Order therein as shall be just.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
