To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the [...] County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of An Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and seals of the Right. Honourable Thomas Harley Esqr Lord Mayor of the City of London and Sir Francis Gosling Knight two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London and Liberties thereof are whereof being of the Quorum bearing date the Twentyeth day of July last Elizabeth Mills was removed from the Parish of Saint Ann in Black Friars London to the Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel in the said County Alledging the said Parish of Saint Mary White Chapel to be the Place of her last Legal Settlement.

That Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Warrant of the said Two Justices Do. Appeal to your Warships therefrom And Humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the Premisses and Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Ann in Black Friars May appear before You at a Certain day to be Appointed to hear and Abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships touching this Appeal

And Your Petr. shall ever pray Etc

Saml Hawkins Wm Hamilton Church Wardens of the Parish of St. Mary White Chapel

William Scullard John Coope George Crosby Major Rohde} Overseers of the Poor of the same Parish
