To the Rt. Honble: Sir John Williams Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London & to the Worpfull the rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the Said City & the Libties thereof in their Genll. Quarter Session assembled.

The humble Petition & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Mary Woolnoth in the City of London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands & Seales of your Lordship & Sir Henry Hankey Knt. Two of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City the Libties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Twenty Sixth day of January last past, Sarah the Wife of Charles Woodhouse & two Daughters, to wit, Sarah aged ten Years & Martin aged five years or thereabouts, were removed from the Parish of St Christopher London unto the Parish of St. Mary Woolnoth London aforesaid, alledging the said Parish of St. Mary Woolnoth to be the Place of their last legall Settlement

That Yor. Petr. Conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Warrant or Order Do appeale to yor. Lordship & Worps therefrom, and humbly Pray yr you Will be pleased to hear them in the Prmisses & that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Christopher [...] London aforesaid may appear before you to hear & abide the Judgmt. & Determination of Yor. Lordp & Worps touching this Appeale

Baynbridge Agent for the Appellants.

And Yor. Petrs. Shall pray Etc.
