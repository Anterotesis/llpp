To the Right Honoble. Sr. Edwd. Clerke Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. the rest of his Maties. Justices of the Peace at the Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The humble petition & Appeale of the Churchwardens of the Parish of St. Michaell Royall London

Sheweth That one Barbara Clerke Singlewoman by Virtue of A Warrant under the Hands & Seales of two of his Maties Justices of the Peace wth. in this City one being of the Quor dated 18th. June 1697 was brought & left by the Churchwardens of Alhallowes Barkin wth. yor. Petrs. to be provided for upon her Oath that her last legall settlemt. was in their said Parish of St. Michaells where shee was a hired Servant for a years wth. one Mr. Ward of Colledge hill there whereas in truth the said Barbara lived two yeares in the said Parish of Alhallowes [---] whence shee was brought And but seaven monethes wth. the said Mr. Ward in yor. Petrs said Parish of St. Michaells

Yor. Petr. therefore humbly pray yor. Honors. & Worpps To hear them in the Prmisses And to Quash the said Warrant & send the said Barbara to the Churchwardens of Athallowes Barking where shee dwelt two whole yeares And was the Place of her last settlement According to Law

And shee shall Pray Etc.
