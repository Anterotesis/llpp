To the Rt. Honoble: Sr Richard Levett Knt. Lord Mayor of the Citty of London And to the Rest of his Maties. Justices in the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poore of the parish of St. Faiths London

Sheweth

That one Anne Salter wife of Samuell Salter (whoe is runn away) by virtue of a warrant under the hands and Scales of Twoe of his Maties Justices of the peace was brought by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poore of the parish of St. Sepulchers London to your Petr. upon the Oath of Davis Tunis That the last legall Settlement of the Said Samuell Salter was in the said parish of St. Faithes where hee Lived with Lawrence Salter his Father a Victualler in Joy Lane untill about 5 years Since and Since hath had noe Legall Settlement Whereas in Truth the said Lawrence Salter the Father Since his going away out of St. Faithes Parish dwelt in the parish of St. Sepulcher where hoe paid to the Church and poore And where the said Samuell his Some Lived with him And had Served as An Appntice with [---] one in the said parish

Yor. Petrs therefore pray That yor. Honr. and Worpps would Order the said Churchwardens of St. Sepulchers to Appear before you And to heare them in the prmisses And to Send the said Samuell Salter to the said Churchwardens of St. Sepuchers To be by them & ordered for and in aniteyned untill They Came Free themselves of the Charge therof by Due Court of Lane

And yor. Petr. Shall pray Etc.
