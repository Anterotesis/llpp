To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London Mr. Recorder and the rest of the Worshipfull His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition of Edward Peirce

Sheweth That on the fourteenth day of March in the sixth year of his present Majesty's Reign your Petitioner by the Name of Edward Price was Convicted Before George Nelson Esquire then and still Lord Mayor of the City of London and then and Still one of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London for buying at Wapping Old Stairs within the Weekly Bills of Mortality Three Thousand One hundred Salt Fish brought there to be sold on the first day of March 1766 and afterwards dividing the same in Shares amongst Sundry Fishmongers And is Adjudged to pay and forfeit the Sum of Twenty Pounds

Your Petitioners conceiving himself Aggrieved thereby Doth humbly Appeal against the said Conviction as being illegal and Prays such Relief in the Premises as to your Lordship and Worships shall seem meet.

Fras. Salt Attry for Appellt.
