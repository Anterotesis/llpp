To The Right Honourable The Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City in heir General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Margaret in new Fish Street in the City of London.

Sheweths. That by Virtue of a certain Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Frederick Bull Esquire Mayor of the City of London and John Wilkes Esquire one of the Aldermen of the said City two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties there of one being of he Quorum bearing Date the fourth Day of June 1774. Ann Warwick was removed and delivered by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Magnus by London Bridge in the said City anto the said Parish of Saint Margaret in new Fish Street as the Place of her last legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners apprehending themselves to be aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly appeal against the same and pray Relief in the Premisses and that the Benefit of this their Appeal may be saved unto them and that the Hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned unto the next General Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City.

Chetham Sollr. for the Appellants}

3 19-
