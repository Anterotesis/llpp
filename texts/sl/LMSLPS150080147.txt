To the Right Honoble. Sr. Edwd. Clerke Knt. Ld. Maior of the City of London And the Rt. Worll. the Rest of his Maties. Justices of the Peace att the Generall Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assembled.

The humble petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens of the Parish of Christchurch London

Sheweth That our William Paine with his wife and three Children by Virtue of A Warrant under the Hands & Seales of Two of his Maties. Justices of the Peace in London (one being of the Quor) were sent to yor. Petrs. from from the pish St. Gyles Criplegate London To be pvided for on the Oath of the said William That hee was legally settled in the said parish of Christchurch where hee lived Two yeares and three moneths and on his own Account was called forth and Didwatch there whereas in truth the said Wm. Paine was only A lodger in Swann Alley And was never taken notice of or entred in any books or Assessmt. as an inhabitant or charged wth. any Tape or rate to the Church or Poore or to watch or ward wth. in the said Parish of Christchurch.

Yor. Petr. humble suite to yor. Honors. and Worpps is to heare this in the prmisses And to Quash the said order made by the said Two Justices And send the said William Paine to the Churchwardens of St. Gyles Cripplegate To be pvided & mainteyned untill they can Free themselves thereof [...] Law

And they shall Pray
