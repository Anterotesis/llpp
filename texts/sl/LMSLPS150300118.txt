To the Rt. Honble Sr. William Lowen Knt Lord Maior of the [...] of London and to the Rt. Worpll the rest of his Maties Justices of the peace for the said City in their general quarter Sessions assembled

The humble petition Appeal of the Churchwardens & overseers of the poor of the Parish of St Margarett Lothbury London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a pass warrant or order under the hands seals of the of his Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London (Quorm Unus) bearing Date the Sixth Day of Angust last past Matthew Woodhouse with Susannah his wife and two Children Susannah and John were removed from the Parish of St. Clemens Eastcheap London unto the said Parish of St. Margarett Lothbury Alleadging she said parish to be the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petr. conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said warrant and Judgement of the said two Justices Do humbly Appeal to your Honr and Worpps therefrom and pray [...] you will be pleased to hear them in the primisses and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the said Parish of St. Clement Eastcheap may appear before you and hear and abide the Determination of Your Honr and Worpps touching this appeal

And they shall ever pray Etc
