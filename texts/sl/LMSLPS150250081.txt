To the Right Honrble Sr Samuel Stainer Knt Lord Major of the City of London and to the Right Worpll the Rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace within the same City in the General Quarter Sessions Assembled

15/8 1714 The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. James Clerkenwell in the County of Middx

Sheweth That one Sarah Bolten wife of John Bolten With her two Children John and George by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the peace within this City (one being of the Quorn) bearing Date the Eighteenth Day of August last past was removed from the parish of St. Sepulchre London unto the said parish of St James Clerkenwell alledging the said parish to be the place of their last legall Settlement

That your Petitionr do humbly conceive themselves aggreived by the said Judgment of the said two Justices and do appeal to your Honr and Worpps therefrom

Your Petition therefore most humbly pray your Honr Worpp will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwardens Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St. Sepulchre may Appeare before you and hear abide then determination of your Honr Worpp touching this Appeale

And they Shall Ever Pray Etc
