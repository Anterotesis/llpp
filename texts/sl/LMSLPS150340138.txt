Londn: ss April 2o. 1723 To the Rt: Humble his Majties: Justices of the Peace for the City of London in Quarter Sessions Assembled:

The Humble Peticon of Tho: Read to the Parish of Chipping Onger in the County of Essex

Sheweth 22/4 That Your: Petrs: dwells in the parish of Chipping Onger in the County of Essex in which Parish by his Industry he hath always Supported himself and Wife and one Child

That Yors: Petitioners wife being with Child, and near the time of her Delivery the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Chipping Onger refuse to suffer Yor:Petr: any Longer to dwell in the said Parish, exept Yor: Petr: bring them a Certificate from the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish where he Acquired his last Legal Settlement, And have Obtained a Warrant to remove them his Wife (now near the time of her Delivery) and Child out of their said parish, which may tend to the ruing of Yor: Petr: and Poor Family he being unable to gitt a Livelihood for them in the Parish where his Settlement is, [---] which at Chipping Onger he is Capable of doing if he could continue in Peace there

That Yor: Petr: Humbly conceives the Place of his last Legal Settlement is in the Parish of St:Peter Le Poor in the City of London, he living as a Menial or Hired Servant with Sr John Bull above the last Two Years before his Marriage and was Married dureing the time he was his Servant, but the Yor: Petr: hath (to his great expence and loss of business) severall times waited on the Churchwardens and Overseess of the Poor of the said Parish of St: Peter Le Poor for a Certificate to prevent the danger of removing his Wife in her Condicon & there utter ruing they that ChurchWardens & Overseers of the Poor refuse to give him any

Yos: Petr: therefore humbly Prays this Honble: Court will Please to Order the said Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St: Peter Le Poor to give him a Certificate that he may be enabled to Support his family and prevent the damage he may susteyne by being removed from his present aboad, Or that he may be otherwise Retried as they in that great Wisdom shall see meet

And Yor: Petr: as in duty found shall ever Pray Etc
