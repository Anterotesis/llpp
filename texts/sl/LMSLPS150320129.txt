To the Right honourable Sr. John Fryer Barrt. Lord Mayor of the City of London and Others the Right Worshipful his Majties Justices of the peace of the said City of their General Quarter Sessions of the peace held for the Said City.

The humble peticon & Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Mary Rotherhith in the County of Surry.

Sheweth That Thomas Ward with his three Children were by an Order or Warrant under the hands & Seals of two of his Majties Justices of the peace for the City of London bearing Date the Sixteenth day of February 1720. removed from the Parish of St. Bottolph without Bishopgate London to the parish of St. Mary at Rotherhith aforesaid as the place of their last legal Settlemt.

That Yor. Peticoners do humbly conceive that they can Make it appear unto this Honourble. Court that the place of the last Legal Settlement of the Said Thomas Ward and his three Children [...] is not in the Said Parish of Rotherhith

Yor. Peticoners therefore do humbly appeal agt. the Said Order and pray that this Honble. Court will Order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Bottolph without Bishopgate London to Attend this Court to Shew cause why the Said Order Should not be Vacated

And yor. Peticoners shall Ever Pray Etc.
