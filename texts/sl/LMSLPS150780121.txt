To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London & the rest of his Matys Justices of the peace for the same City at the General Quarter Session of the peace now assembled

The Humble petition and appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London

Sheweth That by an Order under the hands and Seals of Sir Robert Kite Knight Lord Mayor and Francis Cokayne Esquire two of his majesties Justices of the peace for the said City and liberties thereof (One being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Seventh day of March one thousand Seven hundred and Sixty Seven Sarah Dean Widow of John Dean deceased whose Settlement Could not be found was Removed from the Parish of Alhallows the Great London to the parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London as the place of her last legal settlement

Your petitioners think themselves aggrived by the said Order and therefore humbly appeal against the same and pray this Honourable Court to Grant them such Relief in the Premises as shall be thought most Equitable

And your Petr. shall ever pray

Thos. Stagg Sollr for Cripplegate Parish
