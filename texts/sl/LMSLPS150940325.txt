To the Right Honorable & Nathaniel Newnham Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipful the Aldermen, his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City at their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Benedict vulgarly Bennett Fink in the said City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the hands and Seals of John Wilkes Robert Peckham Esquires two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London bearing date the Sixth day of August last past Elizabeth the Wife of Robert Freckingham and their four children to wit Mary aged seven Years and upwards Robert aged five Years and upwards Julian aged four Years and a half and William aged 2 Years were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman Street in the said City to the said Parish of Saint Benedict regularly Bennett Fink as the Place of their last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray, that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved into them, and that the hearing and Determination thereof may be adjourned unto the next General Quarter Session of the peace to be holden for the said City,

And Your Petrs. shall ever Pray Etc.
