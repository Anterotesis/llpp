To [---] the Right Honble the Lord Mayor & the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Session of the Peace aforesaid

The humble petition of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Parish the holy Trinity in the Town of Guildford in the County of Surry

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands & Seals of the Right Honble John Sawbridge Esqr. & Frederick Bull Esqr. two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London & Liberties thereof one being of the Quorum bearing date the 23d day of March 1776 Jane Crow the wife of Wm Crow & her two twin Children James & Mary aged thirteen Months were removed & conveyed out of the Parish of St. Sepulchre in the sd City of London unto the sd Parish of the Holy Trinity in the Town of Guildford in the County of Surry as the place of their last legal [...] Settlement

That your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved by the sd. Order & have appealed therefrom

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that you will be pleased to appoint a day of hearing & determining the Merits of the sd. Appeals

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
