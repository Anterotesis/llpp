To the Right Honble Sr John Ward Knt Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the rest of his Majtys Justices of the peace at the General Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. Pancras London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Matys Justices of the peace within the City of London (Quorn unus) bearing Date the Eighteenth day of March last past Mary Gibbs wife of George Gibbs with two Children were Sent and Delivered by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Parish of Allhallows Barking London unto the Sd Parish of St. Pancras London Alledging the Sd Parish to be the place of their last Legal Settlement

That Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved by the Sd Warrant and Judgment and do most humbly Appeal to Your Honr and Worpl therefrom

Most humbly pray Your Honl and Worps will be pleased to hear them in the premisses And Order that the Church Wardens and Overseers of ye poor of ye sd Pish of Allhallows Barking may Appear before You And hear and abide the Determination of Yr Honr and Worps touching the sd Appeal

And they Shall ever Pray Etc
