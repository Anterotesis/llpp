To the Right Honble Stephen Theodore Janssen Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the same City. in their General Quarter Session assembled.

The humble Petition of Thomas Woodman the Under keeper of the Prison called the Poultry Compter.

Sheweth That Your petitioner rents in the parish of Saint Mildred in the Poultry London the Gaol commonly Called the Poultry Compter and the House wherein Your petitioner dwells and pays the Yearly Sum of Eighty four pounds for the wholes

That the said Goal and the Dwelling House before the late Act of Parliament to prevent the Sale of Spirituous Liquors in Goals were rented at the Sum of £140 P Annum but by Reason of that Act the said Goal and Dwelling House become of less Value, and the Land lord, thoroughly sensible of it, reduced the Rent to £84, and which is the full, if not more than the, Value of it.

That Your Petitioner is in Respect of the said Goal and Dwelling House overrated in and by the last Rate and Assessment made for the Relief of the Poor of the said Parish.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly appeals against the said Rate and Assessment and prays such Relief in the premisses as to your Lordship shall seem meet

Tho. Woodman
