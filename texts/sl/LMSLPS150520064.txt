To the Right Honble Daniel Lambert Esquire Mayor of the City of London and To the Rest of the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City in their General Quarter Session of the Peace in and for the said City Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Bridget otherwise Saint Brides within the said City

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majestys Justice of the Peace for the said City (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Ninth day of July in the Year of our Lord one thousand Seven hundred and Forty one Sarah Hall Wife of Joseph Hall and their two Children Elizabeth aged Seven Years and Sarah aged three Years were removed and Conveyed from and Out of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate in London into the said Parish of St. Bridget otherwise Saint Brides in London aforesaid as the Place of their last Legal Settlement

Your Petitioners therefore Conceiving themselves Aggrieved by the said Order or Past Warrant Humbly Appeal against the same and Pray such releif in the Premisses as to this Honourable Court shall Seem meet

And yor. Petrs. shall Pray Etc
