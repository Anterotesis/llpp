To the Right Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London as their General [---] Session Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Stephen in Walbrook in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Order under the Hands and Seals of Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London (whereof One is of the Quorum) bearing date the Thirtieth day of April last past Sarah Spencer Widow of John Spencer deceased And their two Children Ann aged Eleven years and Mary aged for years were removed from the Parish of Saint Margaret in Lothbury London to the said Parish of Saint Stephen in Walbrook London as the last legal Place of their Settlement By Which Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and appeal to this Court against the said Pass Order

D. Webb Agent for the Appellants Walbrooks 14th May 1765 Your Petitioners therefore Pray your Worships will be pleased to hear and Determine the Matters touching this Appeal and give them such Releife in the premisses as to your Worships shall seem Just and Meet

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray
