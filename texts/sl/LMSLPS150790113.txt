To The Right Honorable Thomas Harley Esquire Lord Mayor And to the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof in General Quarter Session of the Peace at the Guildhall in and for the said City Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of a pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of The Right Honorable Thomas Harley Esquire Lord Mayor and Sir Charles Asgill Baronet two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof (one of the Quorum) bearing date the Ninth day of December 1767, ann Murphy Widow was removed from the Parish of Saint Andrew by the Wardrobe London to the Parish of Saint Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex as the Place of her last legal Settlement, whereby Your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved, and against the said Pass Warrant or Order do Appeal

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Wm. Massey Wm:Seager} Churchwardens

M Wingrove Willm: Maning} Overseers of the Poor
