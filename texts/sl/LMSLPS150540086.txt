To the right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Worshipful the Aldermen his Brethren his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace assembled.

The humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Trinity London.

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of your Lord Stick and the worshipful Robert Westly Esquire, two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 7th Day of Decr. 1742 George Farecloth aged 13 Years and Robert Farecloth aged 11 Years were removed from the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopgate London unto the said Parish of Trinity London as the Place of their last legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners humbly conceive themselves agrieved

Therefore your Petitioners humbly appeal to this honourable Court against the Order of the said two Justices and humbly pray they may be heard in the Premisses and have such Relief therein as shall seem most meet and that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the sd. Parish of St. Botolph Bishopgate London may be injoyned to abide by the Judgment and Determination of your Lordship and Worships touching the said Appeal.

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

John Grant Humphrey Spencer} Church Warderns

Jon Lumley Thos. Robinson} Overseers
