To ye. Rt. Honble: Robert Willimott Esqr. Lord Mayor of ye. City of London, and to the Worshipfull ye rest of His Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City & the Liberties thereof, in their Generall Quarter Session assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of ye. Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Christchurch London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Your Lordship and Edward Gibbon Esqr. Two of His Majesty's of ye. Peace for the City of London, and the Liberties thereof (One being of the Quorum) bearing Date the Second Day of May last past, Charles Allen, Mary his Wife, and their four Children, James aged Nine Years, Mary aged Seven Years, Lydia aged three Years, and Charles aged Six Months, were Removed from the [...] Precinet of White Fryers London, unto the Parish of Christchurch London, Alledging the said Parish of Christchurch to be the Place of their last legall Settlement.

Baynbridge for Appellts. That Your Petitioners Conceiving themselves Agrieved by teh said Warrant or Order of Removall, Do therefore Appeal to Your Lordp & Worps therefrom, and humbly pray, that the Benefit of this Appeal [...] be saved unto them, and the hearing and determining thereof, be Adjourned, untill the next Generall Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City of London & the Liberties thereof, And that the Overseers of the Poor of the said Precinct of White Fryers London, may then appear before You, to hear and abide ye. Judgment & Determination of your Lordship and Worships touching this Appeal.

And Your Petitionrs. Shall pray Etc.
