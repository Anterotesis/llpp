To the Right Honble Samuel Plumbe Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the rest of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City of London in their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace (by Adjournment) Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Son of the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Frederick Bull and William Plomer Esquires Two of His Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof one being of the Quorum bearing Date the Eighteenth day of October 1779 Sarah Allen aged Thirteen years Joseph Allen aged Eleven years and William Allen aged three years the three Children of Richard Allen or Soldier in the Surrey Militia were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Precinct of White Friers London to the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Westminster whereby your Petitioners think themselves aggrieved and humbly Appeal to this Court against the same

Your Petitioners therefore humbly pray that your Worship will pleased to grant that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them and that the hearing and determination thereof be Adjourned until the next General Sessions of the Peace to be holden for the said City and Liberties there of and that all Persons concerned be Ordered then to attend this Court touching the said Appeal

John Barnard Jas Geo: Martin Thos. Tho Brithwaite Wm Clarke} Churchwardens and Overseers
