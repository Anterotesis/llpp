To the Right Honrble Robert Willimott Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majestys Justices of Goal Delivery of Newgate in their Session of Goal Delivery of Newgate for the said City at Justies hall in the Old Bailey Within the suburbs of the said City Assembled

The Humble Petition of Stephen Parker a Prisoner Confined in his Majesty's last of Newgate of Felony

That Your Petioner on the Twenty Fifth Day of April 1743 was Committed a Prisoner to the Gaol of Wood street Compter in London for taking of Alexander Patten a Guinea and helping him to a Watch and stock buckle Which was Stole from him and not Apprehending the Thief

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays That he May at this Present Session of Goal Delivery of Newgate holden for the City of London aforesaid on a Tursday This Nineteenth Day of May 1742 be either Tryed Bailed or Discharged

And yor Petitioner (as in Duty Bound) shall ever pray Etc

The Mark of Stephen [x] Parker
