To the Rt. Honble Sr Charles Duncombe Knt Lord Major of the Citty of London And to ye Worpll the Rest of her Maties Justices of the Peace in the generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeale of the Churchwardens and overseers of the poore of the parish Trinity in London

Sheweth That one Robert Fuller and Elizabeth his wife By Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and water of Twoe her Maties Justices of the peace within the City of London (one being of the Quor) bearing Date 6th Day of Aprill 1709 was removed from the parish of St. Michael of Queen hith London to the said parish of Trinity Meadgeing the said parish to be the place of the last legall Settlement of the said Robert Fuller & Elizabeth his Wife

That yor Petition doubt not but to prove That the said [...] Robert Fuller & Elizabeth his Wife never had any legall Settlement in the said parish of Trinity whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the Judgment of the said Two Justices And doe humbly Appeale to your Worrps therefrom

Your Petitionr therefore humbly pray yor Honrs and Worps will be pleased to heare them in prmisses And Order the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poore of the said parish of St. Michael Queenhith to appeare before you And he are and abide the Determination of your Honr: and Worpps touching this Appeale

And yor: Petitionr: shall ever pray Etc
