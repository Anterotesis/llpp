To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Thomas Rawlinson Knt. Lord Major of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. The rest of Her Maties Justices of the Peace In the Generall Quarter Sessions Assembled

The humble Peticon & Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Antholins London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seales of twoe other Maties Justices of the Peace within the City of London (One being of the Quox) Dated the First day of August Anno Dni 1706. One Elizabeth Ballard late wife of Henry Ballard Decd were brought to yor. Petrs. (to be provided for) by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Gyles without London upon the Oath of one Mary Langley That the last legall Settlement of the Said Henry Ballard was in yor. Petrs. Parish where hee lived and Served as Appntice by Indenture to one Edwards in Cobbs Court in Budge Rowe Barber Whereas in truth the Said Henry Ballard was only turned over to the Said Edwards under colour to Obteyne his Freedome of the City After which hee tooke a house in Bread Streete Ward where hee was last legally a Setled Inhabitant in the Parish of Alhallowes Bread Streete And that his last legall Settlement was not in yor. Petr. Parish

Yor. Petrs. humbly Pray yor. Honor. and Worpps. to hear them in the prmisses And Quash the Said Warrant made by the Said twoe Justices And send the Said Elizabeth Ballard back to the Parish of St. Gyles to be by them Provided for and mainteyned untill They can Free themselves of the Charge thereof by due Course of Lawe

And Yor. Petrs. Shall Ever Pray Etc.
