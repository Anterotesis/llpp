To the Right Honourable John Wilkes Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Worshipful the Aldermen his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the same City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the several Consumers of Wheat Barley and Oals within the City of London and the Liberties thereof

Sheweth That for some time past the common Market prices of middling What middling British Barley and middling British Oals at the new Corn Market in Mark Lane have exceeded the Sums following to wit middling British Wheat the Sum of forty Eight Shillings the Quarter middling British Barley the Sum of twenty four Shillings the Quarter and middling British Oats the Sum of Sixteen Shillings the Quarters the Continuance whereof your Petitioners apprehend will prove very detrimental to the City in General and to your Petitioners in particular

That your Petitioners are ready to make the Truth hereof appear and to prove the same in such manner as the Statutes in that behalf is required

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that this Honourable Court will take their Case into Consideration and proceed to examined and determined the common Market Prices of middling British Wheat middling British Barley and middling British Oats as the same are now and have been lately bought and sold in the Markets of this City and to certify the same to his Majesty's Chief Officer and Collector of the Customs in such manner as by the Statutes in this behalf is required and directed

And your Petitioners will ever pray Etc.

Robt. Holders Sol
