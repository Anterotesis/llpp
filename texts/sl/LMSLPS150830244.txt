To the Worshipful his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London at the General Quarter Session of the Peace holden for the said City Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Nicholas Olave London

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order of Removal or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof One being of the Quorum bearing Date the Sixteenth day of March 1772. Eleanor Ellison Widow of Christopher Ellison Deceased and their four Children to wit. Christopher Aged about Seventeen years, Robert Aged Eleven Years. Eleanor Aged Eight Years and Sarah Aged Six years were removed and Conveyed by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of the Holy Trinity London from and out of their said Parish unto the said Parish of Saint Nicholas Olave and delivered unto your Petitioners (the said Justices Adjudging the last legal Settlement of the said Eleanor Ellison and her said four Children to be in the said Parish of Saint Nicholas Olave)

James Farrer, Attorney for the Appellants. Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be aggrieved by the Judgment of the said two Justices humbly Appeal to this Honourable Court against the said Order or pass Warrant and pray Relief in the Premises and that their Appeal may be heard and determined at their present Session
