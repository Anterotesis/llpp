To the Worshipful his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Overseers of the Poor of the Liberty of Saffron hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents in the Parish of Saint Andrew Holborn in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by on Order under the Hands and Seals of the Right Honble John Wilkes Esqr. Lord Mayor, and Frederick Bull Esqr. one of the Aldermen of the said City of London bearing date the 28th. day of January last Mary Tate Widow of John Tate was removed from the Parish of Saint Giles Cripplegate without in the said City to the said Liberty of Saffron Hill Hatton Garden and Ely Rents as the place of the last legal Settlemt. of the sd. Mary Tate wherely your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved and against the said Order do appeal.

Your Petitioners therefore most humbly pray that the Benefit of their said appeal may be saved unto them until the next General Quarter Session of the Peace to be holden for the said City of London And that your Worships will be pleased to appoint some Day in the said next Sessions for the Hearing and determining of their said Appeal.

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Cox. Sollr. for Appellants

ex
