To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the City of London at their General Sessions here Assembled

The humble Petition of Abraham Harrison

Sheweth That your Petitioner was bound an Apprentice to Godfrey Harris and John Harris of the City of London Partners and Cornfactors by a foreign Indenture bearing Date the 26th. Day of January 1732 for Seven Years from the Date thereof.

That yor Petitioner's Mother paid to his said [...] Masters the Sum of Two hundred Pounds as Consideration money with him as an Apprentice & for his Instruction in the said Trade and Mystery of a Cornfactor.

That your Petir. did serve his said Masters from the Date of the said Indenture until the Day of May now last past when his said Masters turned your Petitr. away from their Service And altho yor Petr. hath since tendred & offered himself & is willing to serve out the Remr. of his time his said Masters have refused & still do refuse to receive or further to in street him as an Apprentice

Wherefore Yor Petr humbly prays Yor Worship's would be pleased to order his said Masters to attend this Honble Court to shew Cause why yor Petitionr should not be discharged from his said Apprenticeship or that yor Petitioner be otherwise relieved in the Premisses as to your Worhsips shall seem meet

And yor. Petr. shall ever pray Etc.
