To the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the City of London assembled at their general Sessions holden at Guildhall London in and for the said City

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex.

Sheweth That by a Pass Warrant or order of removal bearing date the Sixth Day of November one Thousand Seven Hundred and Seventy one under the Hands and Seals of the Honourable Brass Crosby then Lord Mayor and Thomas Hallifax Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the City of London (one being of the Quorum) Eleanor Torrie Widow of James Torrie deceased whose Settlement cannot be found and their two Children to wit Eleanor aged [---] Two Years and upwards and Ann Aged One year were removed from the Parish of Saint Alphage London to the Parish of Saint John of Wapping as the Place of their last Legal Settlement.

That your Petitioners apprehending them- selves aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or order of Removal do Humbly appeal against the same:

Your Petitioners therefore Humbly Pray your Worships will be pleased to order the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Alphage London and all other Persons concerned to attend this Court some Day the next Sessions to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of the Court touching the said Appeal, and that your Petitioners may have such relief therein as to Justice and Law doth Appertain:

And your Petitioners will ever Pray Etc

Jno: Hodgson [...] Bmungler} Church Wardens

Josh. Clark Thos Willis} Overseers.
