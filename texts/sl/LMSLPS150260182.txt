To the Rt Honrble Sr William Humfryes Knt & Baronett Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt Worpll: the rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City did the general quarter Sessions assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Buttolph without Bishopsgate London

Sheweth That one Charles Matthews and Ruth his wife by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said City of London (one being of the Quor) bearing date the tenth day of September last past was removed from the parish of St Vedast als Foster London unto the Parish of St. Buttolph without Bishopsgate London alleadging the said parish to be the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitionrs doubt not but to prove that the said Charles Matthews and Ruth his wife never had any legal Settlement in the said Parish of St Buttolph without Bishopsgate whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the said warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and do most humbly appeal to your Honr & Worpps therefrom.

Your Petitionrs therefore most humbly prays your Honr and Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwarden and Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St Vedaft als Fester may appear before you and hear and abide the determination of your Honr and Worpps touching this appeal

And your Petitionrs Shall ever Pray Etc.
