To the Right Honrble Robert Alsop Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London And to the rest of his Majesty's & Justices of the peace for the Said City at their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Michael Bassishaw London.

Sheweth That by a Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Sir Robert Ladbroke and Sir Joseph Hankey Knights two of His Majesty's Justices of the peace for the said City (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Second Day of May one thousand Seven hundred and fifty two One Jane Hill Widow of Samuel Hill deceased and her four Children (Vizt.) James aged about 8 Years Richard Seven Years Thomas four Years and Anne two Years or thereabouts were & removed from the Parish of Saint Stephen Coleman street to the Said Parish of Saint Michael Bassishaw as the place of the Settlement of the said Jane Hill and her Said four Children.

By which said Warrant or Order Your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved and humbly appeal to this Court against the Same praying Such Relief in the Prmisses us to this Court shall Seem meet

And your Petitioners, as in Duty bound will pray.

Gardner Sollr. for the Appellants.
