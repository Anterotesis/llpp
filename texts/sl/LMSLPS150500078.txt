To the Rt. Honrble the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the Citty of London in Quarter Sessions Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint John of Wapping in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Vertue of a Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of the Rt. Honrble the Lord Mayor and Sr. John Salter Knt. two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said Citty of London bearing date the Ninth day of Aprill 1739. Frances Cook Widow of Stephen Cook Deceas'd and her Three Children Susan (therein said to be) Two years, Stephen aged Four years and Henry aged Fifteen Months were Removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Ann in Blackfryers in the said Citty to the Said Parish of their John of Wapping as the place of their last legall Settlement

Yor. Petrs. humbly Conceiving themselves Aggreived by the said Order of Removeall and Appeare to this Court against the same and pray that the same may be heard this present Quarter Sessions and that all Persons Conceived may Attend to hear and abide the Judgement and Determination of this Court tour King the said Appeal

And Shall Pray Etc

Edwd. Staple Jno. Hale} Churchwardens

John Smith Robt. Walker} Overseers of the Poor
