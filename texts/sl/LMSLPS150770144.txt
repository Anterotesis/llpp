To the Right Honourable George Nelson Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London & the rest of His Majestys Justices of the Peace of the said City in their General Quarter Session Assembled.

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Sepulchre in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Sir Samuel Hudyer Knight & Baronet and Sir Henry Banks [---] Knight Two of His Majestys Justices of the peace for the City of London (one whereof being of the Quorum bearing date the 26th. day of February 1766. Susanna Cross Singlewoman was removed from the Parish of St. Sepulchre in the City of London to the Parish of St. Sepulchre in said County of Middlesex whereby Your Petitioner conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioner therefore Appeal against the said Order or Pass Warrant and humbly pray that the Benefit of the said Appeal may be reserved untill the next General Session of the peace & to be releived in the Premisses as to your Lordship and this Honourable Court shall seem meet

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.
