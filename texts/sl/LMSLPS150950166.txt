To the Right Honorable the Lord Mayor and others of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof in General Quarter Sessions of the Peace at the Guild Hall of the said City Assembled.

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church- Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish and Town of Hungerford in the Country of Berks.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an Order under the Hands and Seals of your Lordshipp and John Hart Esquire two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the City of London and Liberties thereof bearing Date the Twenty Eighth day of June last Catherine Cook aged Five Years and upwards and Ann Cook aged there Years and upwards (being the Daughters of James Cook who hath deserted there) were removed and Conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Giles's without Cripplegate to the said Parish and Town of Hungerford at the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Catherine and Ann Cook whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Your Petitioners therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the Ajudication of the said Two Justices and their said Order And Pray that the Benefit of this Appeal may be saved to the Petitioners and that the hearing and Determining thereof be adjourned to the next General Session of the Peace to be holden in and for the said City and Liberties.

And your Petitioners shall pray Etc.
