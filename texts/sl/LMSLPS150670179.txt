To the Right Honble the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majestie's Justices of the Peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof in their General Quarter Sessions Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate London.

Sheweth. That by Virtue of an Order of Removal under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London & Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the 12th. day of April 1756 Dorcas Blanchard Widow was removed from the Parish of Saint Sepulchres London to the Parish of Saint Botolph without Bishopsgate London aforesaid Adjudging the last legal Settlement of the said Dorcas Blanchard to be there And Your Petitioners conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Order and Judgment of the said two Justices Do humbly appeal there from And pray such Relief in the Premisses as to Your Lordship and Worships shall seem meet and Convenient.

And Yr. Petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Burr Attorney for Bpsgate Parish.
