To the Right Honrble St. Samuel Stainer Knt Lord Major of the City of London and to the Right Worpll the Rest of her Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said city in the general quarter Sessions assembled

The humble Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St. Martin Ironmonger lane London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of her majesties Justices of the peace within this [...] one being of the Quorn) bearing Date the Fourth Day of February last past One Judith Davis with her two Children Hanna and Susanna were Sent and delivered to the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poore of the said parish of St Martin Iron monger Lane London alleadging the said parish to be the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petitioners doubt not but to prove that the said Judith Davis never had any legal settlement in the said Parish whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeale to your Honr and Worpps therefrom

Your Petitionr: therefore most humbly pray your Honr and Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmisses and that the Churchwarden and Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Stephen Wallbrooke London do appeare before you and hear and abide the Determination of your Honr and Worpps touching this Appeale

And your Petitionr. Shall ever Pray Etc.
