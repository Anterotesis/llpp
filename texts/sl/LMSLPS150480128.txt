June 1737 To the Right Honourable Sir John Thompson Knight Lord Mayor of the Citty of London and to his Worshipfull Breth hea [...] the Justices of the Peace for the Same Citty.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Christopher London.

Sheweth. That by a Pass Warrant or Order made by your Lordship and the Worshipful Daniel Lambert Esqr on the Seventeenth day of June last Jane Marshall widow of Christopher Marshal deced a Poor Person) was Removed out of the Parish of Saint Margaret in Lothbury London to the said Parish of Saint Christopher and now remains in Chargeable to the said Parish.

That Your Petr. apprehend they are agrieved by Such pass Warrant or Order in Regard that (as they are advized) the said Jane Marshall or her husband Christopher Marshall never obtained any legall Setlement in the said Parish of Saint Christopher.

For which Reason your Petrs. humbly Appeal from the said Pass Warrant or Order and Pray the same may be discharged and that Your Lordship and Worships will Grant your Petrs. such Relief in the Premisses as to your Lopp and Worships Shall Seem meet.

And Yor: Petrs. Shall ever Pray Etc. Tho: Bosely Edward Lawrence John Clarke
