To the Right Honourable The Lord Mayor and the rest of the Worshippfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by Vertue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of Robert Alsop and Thomas Rawlinson Esqrs. two of his Majestyes Justices of the Peace for the City of London (one whereof is of the Quorum) bearing Date the thirty first Day of July last Past Elizabeth Clark Wife of Samuel Clark who is a Soldier abroad in his Majestyes Service (as in Alledged in the said Order) and their two Children Valentine Aged four Years and James Aged two Years were Removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Giles without Cripplegate London to the said Parish of Saint James Clerkenwell in the said County of Middlesex as the Place of their last legall Settlement

The Appellants think themselves aggrieved by the said Order and therefore humbly Appeal against the same and Pray Your Worshipps to Grant them such Relief in the Premisses as shall be thought most Equitable.

And the Petitioners will ever Pray Etc

G. Talmask Attorney from the Appellants}
