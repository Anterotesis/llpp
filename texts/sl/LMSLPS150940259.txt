To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace in and for the said City in their General Session of the Peace assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of St. Mary at Hill London.

Sheweth That by Virtue of an order or pass Warrant under the Hands and Seals of Nathaniel Newnham Esqr. Lord Mayor of the said City of London and Richard Clash Esqr. two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City bearing Date the twenty Second say of the February one thousand seven hundred and Eighty three Thomas Bye and Elizabeth his Wife [...] having then lately come to reside in the Parish of St. Giles Cripplegate not having obtained say legal Settlement there It was adjudged by the said Majestys upon due saw of made before them that the said Parish of St. Mary at Hill was the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Thomas to which parish he and the said Elizabeth were accordingly removed

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to the greatly aggrieved by the said News or pass Warrant humbly appeal to your Worships & pray Relief in the Premisses and that a day may be appointed for touching and determining this Appeal.
