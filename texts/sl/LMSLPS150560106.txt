To the Right Honourable Henry Marshall Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of the Worshipfull the Aldermen his Brethren in their Generall Quarter Session Assembled.

The Humble Petition and appeal of the Church- -Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate London

Sheweth That by Vertue of an Order or Pass under the hands and Seals of Two of his Majesties Justices of the Peace in and for the said City of London (One being of the Quorum) bearing date the sixth day of July One thousand seven hundred and forty five one Ann Heaviside wife of Thomas Heaviside (who is a Soldier abroad in his Majesties service and hath not any legall settlement in England as she [...] believes or knows of) and their two Children Margaret aged three years and John aged Eleven Months were removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Botolph without [---]Bishopgate in London into the parish of Saint Botolph without Aldgate in London as the place of their last legall settlement.

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves agrieved by the said order or Pass warrant humble appeal against the same and pray such relief in the premisses as to the Court shall seem Just

And your Petitioners shall pray Etc

Will Salt Soldr for Appllants
