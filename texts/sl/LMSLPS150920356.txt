To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the said City at their General Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of Bromley in the County of Kent

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order under the Hands and Seals of The Right Honourable Sir Watkin Lewis Knight and George Hayley Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and the Liberties thereof bearing Date the fourteenth Day of November last Mary the Wife of William Mathews a Sailor in his Majesty's Service and their three Children namely Mary Ann aged ten Years William aged seven Years and Margaret aged two Years were removed from and out of the Parish of Saint Andrew Hubbard in the said City to the said Parish of Bromley as the Place of their legal Settlement Whereby your Petitioners conceive themselves to be aggrieved.

Therefore humbly pray your Warships to receive their Appeal against the said Order and to appoint a Day in the next General or Quarter Sessions to hear & determine the same.
