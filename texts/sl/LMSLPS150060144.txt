6 June 1695

To the Rt. Honoble. Sr. Thomas Lane Knt. Lord Maior of the City of London And to the Rt. Worll. the rest of his Maties Justices of the Peace att the Generall Quarter Sessions of ye Peace Assembled.

The humble Peticon and Appeale of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poore of the Parish of St. Gyles wth. out Cripplegate London

Sheweth That by virtue of a Warrant under the Hands and Seales of twoe of his Maties. Justices of the Peace wth. in this City (one being of the Quor) Dated 6th. June 1695 one Margaret Axtell late wife of Richard Axtell decd and William and Anne their Children were sent to yor. Petr. to be pvided for upon the Oath of Elizth. Osman That the said Richard Axtell served his Appnticeshipp wth. and Samuel Osborne Founder in yor. Petr. said pish being admidged his last legall Settlemt. Whereas in truth after the expiration of the said Appnticeshipp to witt in February 1684 The said Richard Axtell tooke a lodgeing in the hone of one John Mason in the Parish of St. Brides in which Parish hee marryed and there inhabited for about ten yeares following and there Dyed As yor. Petr. can Prove in this behalfe

Yor. Petr. humble since unto yor. Honor and Warrnts. in That yor. will be pleased to Order the said Churchwardens of St. Brides to appeare before you And to heare them all in the prmisses And to send the said Margaret and her twoe Children to the said Parish of St. Brides where the said Richard Axtell was last setled an Inhabitant To be pvided for According to the Law

And They shall Pray Etc.
