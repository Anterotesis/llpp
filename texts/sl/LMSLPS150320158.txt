To the Right Honble Sr John Fryer Bar Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the rest of his Maties Justices of the peace at the General Quarter Sessions of ye peace Assembled

The humble Petition & Appeal of the Church Wardens & Overseers of the poor of the parish of St Mary Wool [---] church London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Maties Justices of the peace for the City of London (Quor unus) bearing Date the third day of October last John Dixon and Catherine his wife were sent and Delivered by the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Anne in Black fryers London to the said parish of St. Mary Woolchurch London alledging the said parish to be the place of their last Legal Settlement.

That your Peticoners conceive themselves agrieved by the said Warrt: and Judgemt: of the said two Justices and Doe most humbly Appeal to your Honr. & Worps therefrom.

Your Petrs: therefore most humbly pray your honr. & Worps. will be pleased to hear them in the premisses & Order that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the said parish of St. Anne in Black fryers may appear before you & hear and abide the Determinacon of this Court touching the said Appeal

And they shall ever pray Etc.
