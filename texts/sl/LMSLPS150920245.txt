To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace for the said City Assembled

The Humble Petition of James Malo of the Parish of Saint Stephen Coleman Street in the said City of Londn Weaver

Sheweth That your Petitioner did in Michaelmas Term last Commence his Action in his Majesty's Court of Kings Bench at Westminster against Thomas Wright Evan Pugh and Robert Pukham Esquires three of the Inhabitants of the said City of London for Recovery of the Damages sustained by Your Petitioner by reason and on Account of the unlawful riotous and tumultuous Assembly of divers Persons to the Number of twelve and more at the said City of London in the Parish of Saint Stephen Coleman Street in the Ward of Coleman Street on the seventh day of June in the year of our Lord One thousand seven hundred and eighty and then and there unlawfully feloniously and with force demolishing as well wholly as in part a Dwelling House and divers Outhouses and other Buildings and Premises thereunto adjoining and divers goods and Chattels therein kept and deposited belonging to your Petitioner in the said City of London

That your Petitioner having declared in such Action the Defendants Pleaded thereto and the Cause being thereupon at Issue the same came on to be and was Tried before Mr. Justice Buller and a special Jury at the sittings after the last Hilary Term at the Guildhall in and for the said City of London when after a long Tryal a Verdict was found for your Petitioner for the Sum of Three tho [...] six hundred and sixty seven pounds and six pence being the Amount of the Damages so by Your Petitioner sustained on the Account and in the manner aforesaid besides your Petitioner's Costs of Suit

That your Petitioner hath since the Tryal of the said Cause and since the said Verdict was found as aforesaid got his Costs

Charges and Expences of the said Action and Tryal duly and in the usual manner Taxed by the Proper Officer of the said Court of Kings Berick and which said Costs have been Taxed and Setled at [...] Sum of Ninety two pounds nine shillings and six pence

That your Petitioner did on the seventh day of July last past duly obtain and sign final Judgment in the said Action in the said Court of King's Bench against the said Thomas Wright Evan Pugh and Robert Peckham the Defendants therein for the said two Sums of Three thousand six hundred and sixty seven pounds and six pe [...] nd Ninety two pounds nine shillings and six pence making together the Sum of Three thousand seven hundred and fifty nine pounds ten shillings being the whole Amount of the Damages and Costs so by your Petitioner sustained in the manner and on the Occasion aforesaid which said Final Judgment now remains entered up of Record and in full force in the said Court.

That your Petitioner hath lately on his said Judgment so obtained as aforesaid Sued out his Majesty's Writ of Levari Facias in the said Court of Kings Bench against the said Defendants in the said Action for the raising and levying of his Your Petitioner's said Damages and Costs and hath pursuant to the Statute or Statutes in such Case made and Provided delivered the said Writ to the Sheriffs in such Case City of London to be by them presented and delivered to your Lordship a [...] Worships in Order that your Petitioner's said Damages and Costs may b [...] raised levied and Collected on the Goods and Chattels of the Inhabitants within the said City according to the Directions contained in the said Statute or Statutes

Your Petitioner the Plaintiff in the said Action therefore humbly requests and intreats your Lordship and Your Worships that [...] will immediately cause the said Sum of Three thousand seven hundred and fifty nine pounds ten shillings to be raised levied and Collected on the Inhabitants of the said City of London and to be afterwards paid to your Petitioner the said Plaintiff he being the Person intitled thereto and that the same Sum may be raised levied Collected and paid in such manner and form and by such Ways and means as are mentioned and expressed in and by and as shall be pursuant to the

Statute or Statutes in such Case made and provided and particularly the several Statutes made in the Twenty seventh year of the Reign of Queen Elizabeth the first year of the Reign of King George the first the eighth year of the Reign of king George the second and the Twenty second year of the Reign of King George the second And that you will be pleased to made Issue. and grant such Precept and Precepts Order and Orders of Sessions for the purposes aforesaid as to Your Lordship and Worships shall seem meet and proper

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc James Malo
