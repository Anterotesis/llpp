To the Right Honble the Lord Mayor of the City London and the Rest of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the same City in the general Quarter Sessions of the peace for the same City Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Church Wardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of saint Benedict vulgarily called Bennet Fink London

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of the Right Honble Sir William Stephenson Knt. Mayor of the City of London and Francis Cokaye Esqr one of the Aldermen of the said City two of his Majestys Justices of the peace for the City of London and the Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing Date the third Day of October instant Mary Newton and her Male Bastard Child aged five weeks for Nurture were removed from and out of the Parish of St. Giles Cripplegate London to the said Parish of Saint Benedict vulgarly Bennet Fink as the place of the last legal Settlement of the said Mary Newton

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves to be Aggrieved by the said Order or Pass Warrant humbly Appeal against the same to this Honble Court And pray relief in the premises And that benefit of the said Appeal may be saved unto them And that the hearing and Determination thereof may be Adjourned intil the next general Session of the peace to be holden for this City and the Liberties thereof

And your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc
