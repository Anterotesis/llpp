To the Worshipfull his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the City of London in their General Quarter Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Richmond in the County of Surry

Sheweth that by Virtue of an Order or Pass Warrant under the hand and seal of the Right Honourable Frederic Bull Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London bearing date the twenty first day of January In the Year of our Lord one Thousand seven hundred and Seventy four Ann Stayner was removed from the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate without unto the said Parish of Richmond in the County of Surry aforesaid Alledging the said Parish to be the Place of the said Ann Stayner's last legal settlement

That your petitioners conceiving themselves agrieved by the said Warrant or Order of the said Justice Do therefore appeal to your Worships therefrom and Humbly pray that you will please to hear them in the premises and order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Bishopsgate without appear before you at a Certain Day to be appointed to hear and abide the Judgment and determination of your Worships touching this appeal and your petitioners shall ever pray Etc.

Jemmett and Tallemach Sollicitors for Appellts.}
