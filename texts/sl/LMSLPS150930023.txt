To the Right Honourable William Plomer Esquire, Lord Mayor of the City of London, and the Worshipful the Aldermen his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City, at their General Quarter Sessions of the Peace Assemble.

The Humble Petition of Henry West, an Inhabitant of the Parish of Saint Mildred in the Poultry, London.

Sheweth That by a Rate and Assessment made for one whole Year, for the said Parish of Saint Mildred in the Poultry, commencing the twenty Fifth Day of March last past, and ending the twenty Fifth Day of March now next ensuing, your Petitioner was [...] freed and assessed the Sum of ten Pounds and Four Shillings, as his share and Proportion towards the said Rate

That your Petitioner humbly conceives the said Charge and Assessment of ten Pounds and Four Shillings, to be illegally, partially, and unequally assessed upon him, and considerably more than by Law he ought to pay.

Your Petitioner therefore begs leave to appeal to this Honourable Court against the said Rate and Assessment And Prays that this Honourable Court will please to take his Case into Consideration, and to grant him such Relief as in their Wisdom shall seem just and reasonable

And your Petitioner shall ever pray Etc.

Dan. Gunston Solicitor
