Guild hall London 23d February 1784

To the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of the City of London and other his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberties thereof in General Quarter Session assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the poor of the parish of Chelveston in the County of Northampton

Sheweth That by virtue of an Order of removal under the hands and Seals of your Lordship and of Nathaniel Newnham Esquire two of has Majesty's Justices of the peace for the City of London aforesaid and the Liberties thereof (one being of the Quorum) bearing date the Twenty Eighth day of January One thousand Seven hundred and Eighty four; Thomas Barfield with his two Children by Sarah his late wife, namely; John aged Seven years and upwards and Thomas aged four years and upwards; were removed from the parish of Saint Gabriel [...] Fenchurch Street London to be said parish of Chelveston, as the place, of the last legal settlement, of the said Thomas Barfield; by which order, your Petitioners conceive themselves aggrieved

Your Petitioners therefore humbly appeal, to this Court, against the Adjudication of the said Two Justices and against their said Order & pray that your Worships will be pleased to Grant them such relief in the premises as to your Worships shall seen meet

And your Petitioners Etc
