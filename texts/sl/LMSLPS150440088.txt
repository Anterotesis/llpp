To the Right Honble. John Barber Esqr. Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the worshipful his Majestys Justices of the peace for the said City in their General Quarter Sessions of the peace Assembled.

The Humble Petition and Appeal of Thomas Corbett and Paul Corbett

Sheweth, That your Petitioners are Partners and Inhabitants of the Parish of St. Martin Vintry London where they carry on the Trade or Business of Sugar Refiners and are rated towards the Relief of the poor by the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish in a Rate called the Poors Rate In which Assessment your Petitioners Conceive they are unequally rated and assessed several of the Inhabitants of the said parish being rated less in proportion to your Petitioners for the Rents of their Houses and Stock in Trade The Names of those Your Petitioners conceive are undercharged in the said Rate or Assessment are Sr. Henry Blunt and Company Sugar Bakers William Belitha Esqr. and Company Sugar Bakers and Philip Fruchard Woodmonger and Dealer in Coals

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Rate or Assessment humbly appeal to this Honble Court for Relief
