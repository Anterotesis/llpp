1746-7 To the Right Honourable William Benn Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and to the Rest of the Worshipfull the Aldermen his Brethren in their Generall Session of Gaol Delivery of Newgate for the City of London Assembled.

The humble Petition of Humphry Francis a Prisoner in Newgate.

Sheweth That Your Petitioner on the Twenty Eighth day of July last was Committed a Prisoner to the Poultry Compter for stealing a Letter at the Post Office with Half a Guinea in it and in the same Compter Remained a Prisoner untill Fryday the fourth Day of this Instant September when he was Removed to the Gaol of Newgate Charged with the same Offence.

Your Petitioner therefore humbly prays that at this Present Session of Gaol Delivery of Newgate he may be Tryed Bailed or Discharged

And Your Petitioner shall Pray Etc.

Humphry Francis
