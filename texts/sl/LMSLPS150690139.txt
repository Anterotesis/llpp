To the Right Honourable Sir Charles Asgill Knight Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same City in their General Quarter Sessions assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Mildred in the Poultry London

Sheweth That by a Warrant or order dated the Sixteenth day of June 1758 under the hands and Seals of the right Honourable Sir Charles Asgill Knight Lord Mayor of the said City and Slingsby Bethell Esquire Two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said City of London and Liberties thereof One being of the Quorum Hannah Edghill Wife of John Edghill who is gone from her and their two Children Elizabeth aged four Years and William aged about Two years were removed and Conveyed out of the Parish of Saint Stephen in Coleman street London to the Parish of Saint Mildred in the Poultry aforesaid

By which Warrant or Order your Petitioners Conceive themselves Aggreived and humbly Appeal to this Court against the same Praying such Relief in the premisses as to this Court shall seem meet

And Your Petitioners as in Duty bound will pray

Henry Swaysland W Edmond} Churchwardens

Jonas Cockerton Thos. Curtis} Overseers
