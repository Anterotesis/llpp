To the Right Honble Sr. Charles Asgill Knt. Lord Mayor of the City of London. Sr. William Moreton Knt. Recorder And the rest of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the said City and Liberty's thereof at the General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of the Parish of St. Mary White Chapel in the County of Middlesex

Sheweth That by a Vagrant Pass or warrant under the Hand and Seal of William Alexander Esqr. one of the Alderman of the City of London aforesaid Directed to John Elthorp of the London Work house and requiring him to Convey Elizabeth Alexander Single woman (Apprehended as a Rogue and Vagabond in the Parish of St. Botolph Aldgate London) to the said Parish of St. Mary White Chapel in the County of Middlesex as the Place of her last legal Settlement

That the said Elizabeth Alexander hath been accordingly Removed in pursuance of the said Pass or Warrant to the said Parish of St. Mary White Chapel

That your Petitioners and Appellts. apprehended themselves Agrieved by the said Pass or Warrant and humbly Appeal against the same

Your Petitioners and Appellts. therefore humbly pray the Court will proceed to hear their said Appeal against the said warrt. and Grant them such Relief as to your worships shall seem Just in and touching the premises, And that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the poor of said Parish of St Botolph Aldate may (upon having had notice for that purpose) attend this Court to hear and abide the Judgmt. and determination of the Court touching the said Appeal

And Yr. petr. will ever pray Etc

June ye. 26th. 1758 Jno. Billington Solr. for Appellts.
