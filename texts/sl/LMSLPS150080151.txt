To the Right honoble: Sir Edwd. Clarke Knt: Lord Maior of the Citty of London, And to the Right Worll. the Rest of his Majties: Justices of the Peace in the Generall Quarter Sessions assembled.

The Petition and Appeale of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poore of ye Parish of St. John Wapping in the County of Midd.

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Warrant under ye Hands and Seales of twos of his Majties: Justices of the peace of the City of London dated 5o. October 1697 (one being of the Quor) Margarett Bentley Widdow was sent to your Petitionrs. to be provided for upon the Oath of Sarah her Daughter, That the said Margarett was an Housekeeper in the said Parish of St. John Wapping in Midd where she paid or was assessed all Dutyes to that Parish, and hath had no Settlement since Whereas in truth the said Margarett did never dwell or in habit in the said parish of St. John Wapping or ever was assessed, to pay any dutyes wth. in the same Parish,

This humble Suite unto your good Honour, and Worpps. is That you wilbe pleased to heare them in ye premisses, and to Quash the said Order or Warrant, made by the said two Justices, And send the said Margarett To the Churchwardens of St. Andrew Holborne London To be provided for & maintained, from whome she was brought to your Petitionrs.

And they shall pray Etc.
