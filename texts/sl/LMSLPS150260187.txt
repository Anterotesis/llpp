To the Rt. Honrble Sr. William Humfreys Knt & Baronett Lord Maior of the City of London and to the Rt Worpll: the Rest of his Majesties Justices of the Peace for the said City in the General quarter Sessions assembled.

The humble Petition and appeal of the Churchwardens & Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Margaret Lothbury London

Sheweth That Thomas Phelps with Margaret his wife and Thomas their Son by Virtue of a Warrant under the hands and Seals of two of his Majesties Justices of the peace for the said City (one being of the Quor) bearing date the twenty third day of September last past was removed from the parish of [...] St. Olave in the old Jewry London unto the said Parish of St Margarett Lothbury alleadging the said Parish to be the place of their last legal Settlement

That your Petr. doubt not but to prove that the said Thomas Phelps never had any legal Settlement in the said parish of St Margaret Lothbury whereby they conceive themselves aggreived by the said Warrant and Judgment of the said two Justices and do humbly appeal to your Honr and Worpps therefrom.

Your Petitionrs therefore most humbly pray's you Honr and Worpps will be pleased to hear them in the prmissed and that the Churchwardens & Overseers of the poor of the said parish of St Olave in the old Jewry may appear before you and hear and abide the determination of your Honr and Worpps touching this appeal

And your Petitioners Shall ever pray Etc.
